# Piece: The Tools
## The biography of a plot holder, read before they speak.

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment
- **Persona:** [The Serious Cultivator](position_the_serious_cultivator.md)

## Place
[The Plot](place_the_plot.md).
Leaning against the shed
or laid out on the path
or in the hand.
They travel with the persona.
They do not stay in the shed
when the work is happening.

## Load Bearing
Without them the plot cannot be tended.
But the specific tools -
not the generic tools -
are load-bearing in a different way.
The condition of a person's tools
is the first thing read
by anyone who knows how to read it.
The serious cultivator's spade:
clean, sharp, the handle smooth
from years of use.
The new tenant's spade:
still has the sticker on it.
Remove the specific tools
and the plot holder
has no biography
visible from the neighbouring plot.

## Apparent
A spade. A fork. A hoe.
Possibly a trowel.
The condition varies enormously.
Some have been sharpened this season.
Some have been sharpened
every season for twenty years.
Some have been sharpened once,
at the point of purchase,
and not since.
The handles tell the most -
whether they have been held
ten thousand times
or ten.

## Yearbook
Every plot they have ever tended.
The spade that came from a father's shed
after the father died -
still sharper than anything bought new.
The trowel that was lost
for three weeks in 2019
and found under the gooseberries.
The fork with the repaired handle
from the year the original handle
snapped during the second week
of the worst bindweed summer
anyone on the site
can remember.
Each one has been used
until it fits the hand
that uses it.
That fit is not transferable.
