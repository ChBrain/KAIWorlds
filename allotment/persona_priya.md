# Persona: Priya
## Title: [New Tenant](position_the_new_tenant.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment

Software engineer. Two children.
Plot 11. Eight months.
She put her name on the waiting list
during lockdown
and forgot about it
until the letter arrived.

## Projection
A compact, quick-moving woman
who arrives with a bag
that contains more than she will use
and leaves with muddy hands
and less anxiety than she arrived with,
which she has not yet connected
to the plot.
She has a second version of the laminated plan
on her phone.
The phone plan has been updated
more recently than the laminated plan.
Neither of them bears much resemblance
to what is actually growing.
She talks to the plants.
She is not sure why she does this.
She is fairly sure it doesn't work.
She does it anyway.

## Action
Try the thing.
Research it first - briefly, thoroughly,
in the forty-five minutes after the children
are in bed on a Tuesday -
then try it.
The research is correct.
The plot has not read the research.
Adjust. Try again.
Ask Sandra on Plot 10 something
she probably already knows the answer to
and receive an answer
that is more useful
than anything she found online.
File this under:
research has limits,
come back to.

## Shadow
She came here because
a vague idea about vegetables
and fresh air
and doing something with her hands
felt like the right answer
to a question she had not quite formed.
The question was:
what is the thing that is mine,
in a life where most things
are shared or managed or optimised
for someone else's benefit?
The plot is hers.
Hers in a way that the house is not -
the house is the mortgage's -
and the job is not -
the job is the product's -
and the children are not -
the children are the future's.
The plot does not belong to anyone
who can take it back
for quarterly review.
She has not named this
as the reason she is here.
She thinks she is here
because of the tomatoes.
The tomatoes were a disaster.
She is still here.

## Tell
She stays twenty minutes longer
than she planned.
Every visit.
