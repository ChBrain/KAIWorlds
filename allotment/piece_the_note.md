# Piece: The Note
## The thing that was said without being said.

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment
- **Process:** [process_the_boundary_dispute](process_the_boundary_dispute.md)

## Place
The gate of [The Plot](place_the_plot.md).
Or the noticeboard.
Or, in serious cases,
hand-delivered to the committee.

## Load Bearing
Without it the boundary dispute
has no written record
and no escalation mechanism.
The allotment communicates in writing
when face-to-face communication
would require one of the parties
to look directly at the other party
while saying the thing,
which is not possible
given the current state of relations.
The note makes direct confrontation
available without requiring
direct confrontation.
Remove it and the grievance
must either be spoken aloud
or filed in perpetuity under
probably nothing.
Neither option is preferred.
The note is the third way.

## Apparent
A piece of paper.
Or a card.
Folded once.
Written by hand,
which means the handwriting
can be identified
even when the note is unsigned.
Some notes are signed.
Signed notes are either
very confident or very angry.
The tone is always measured.
The British note is not a vehicle
for expressed emotion.
It is a vehicle for
extremely precise observation
delivered in language
that cannot be accused of
being unreasonable.
The unreasonableness is
in the precision.

## Yearbook
Every boundary incident
the site has officially recorded
and several it hasn't.
The note about the bonfire smoke in 2019.
The note about the shed
that was possibly four inches over the line.
The note that was
not technically threatening
and was interpreted as threatening
and resulted in a meeting
with three committee members present
as per the constitution.
The note that resolved nothing
but established a position
that has not been formally abandoned.
The notes that were never replied to.
The replies that were also notes.
The site's diplomatic history,
filed in the folder in the committee room
under Correspondence.
