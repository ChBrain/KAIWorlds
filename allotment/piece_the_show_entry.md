# Piece: The Show Entry
## Eleven months carried to a table in August.

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment
- **Process:** [process_the_show](process_the_show.md)

## Place
[The Show Bench](place_the_show_bench.md).
Between ten in the morning
and two in the afternoon
in August.
Before that: the plot.
After that: eaten,
or displayed briefly,
or quietly composted
depending on the result.

## Load Bearing
Without it the Show has no content.
Without it the Tending process
has no external measure -
the season accumulates
but nothing names
what the accumulation produced
in relation to anyone else's accumulation.
The show entry is the moment
when private effort
becomes public fact.
Remove it and the plot holders
tend in isolation forever.
Keep it and everyone discovers,
on one afternoon in August,
exactly where they stand.

## Apparent
A vegetable.
Specifically: the best one.
The one selected from
all the others of its kind
on this plot this season
as the best representative
of what was possible here.
It is placed on a plate
or a board or a piece of paper
according to the schedule's requirements.
A label with the plot number.
No name.
The entry is anonymous.
The plot number is known to everyone.

## Yearbook
Everything grown on this plot
since the first Show entry was made.
The year the beans were extraordinary.
The year the beans failed entirely.
The eleven consecutive wins
in the runner bean category
and what they cost -
not in effort, which was considerable,
but in the quality
of the relationship with Plot 6
which has not been the same
since 2018 and may not recover.
Every entry that placed.
Every entry that did not.
The judge who gave first prize
to a carrot that was technically
larger but clearly inferior
in every other respect.
The year the Decorative Gourd category
was introduced against everyone's wishes.
The produce does not remember any of this.
The plot holder does.
