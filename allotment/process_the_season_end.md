# Process: The Season End
## The reckoning that nobody asked for.

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment
- **Place:** [The Plot](place_the_plot.md)

## Initiated by
It is October.
The first frost has been.
The courgettes, which survived everything
including neglect, drought,
and the structural ambivalence
of their tender,
have finally succumbed.
This is a relief.
The plot must be cleared.
Spent plants pulled.
Beds turned.
The shed checked for anything
that should not overwinter.
The season is over.
It produced what it produced.

## Direction
Toward the empty bed.
Not the harvest - the bed after.
The cleared ground.
The turned soil.
The specific quality of a plot
at the end of October
which is: waiting.
The plot does not know
it is waiting.
The person does.

## Lever
What was planned pressing against
what happened.
The carrying force:
the original seed catalogue,
the laminated plan,
the intentions of March -
now measured against
the actuality of October.
The courgettes won again.
The carrots forked.
The beans were either extraordinary
or entirely absent.
The tomatoes - let us not discuss the tomatoes.
Something ate the brassicas.
Something always eats the brassicas.
The resistance: the person
who must now decide
what the season means
and whether they are coming back.
They are coming back.
They know they are coming back.
They are deciding anyway.

## Echo
The plot is clear.
The beds are turned.
The shed is locked.
The person has decided
they are coming back.
They have also decided
several things they will do differently.
These decisions are made
every October.
They are partially kept.
The plot neither knows
nor cares about the decisions.
The plot will be here in March.
[process_the_winter_plot](process_the_winter_plot.md) begins.
[process_the_agm](process_the_agm.md) is approaching.
