# Position: The Wild Plotter
## The philosophy and the bindweed, arriving simultaneously.

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment
- **Process:** [process_the_tending](process_the_tending.md)
- **Process:** [process_the_boundary_dispute](process_the_boundary_dispute.md)
- **Place:** [The Plot](place_the_plot.md)

## Has
The coherent alternative position.
It exists before this persona arrived -
in the books, the podcasts,
the research that shows
the conventional allotment
is ecologically impoverished
and that working with natural processes
produces more diverse and resilient results.
The position is not wrong.
The position is also
not fully in control
of what happens
when you implement it
on 250 square metres
adjacent to twelve other plots
whose holders did not agree
to the experiment.

## Orders
Tend differently.
Not less - differently.
The position does not tend toward bare soil,
straight rows, chemical intervention.
It tends toward habitat,
companion planting,
the kind of productive disorder
that looks, from the path,
like something got away from someone.
The position does not supply
a visible distinction
between intentional wildness
and simple overwhelm.
This is the position's structural problem.
It does not acknowledge this.

## Loses
The legibility.
They were certain that the philosophy -
explained clearly,
evidenced correctly -
would be readable from the path.
It is not readable from the path.
From the path it reads as:
that plot again.
The committee has the constitution.
The constitution says weed-free and tidy.
The position does not define weed.
Neither does the constitution.
Both parties have noticed this.
Only one party considers it an advantage.

## Drives
On the loss of legibility -
explains.
The mycorrhizal networks.
The companion planting rationale.
The data on pollinator populations
in managed versus unmanaged plots.
The explanation is correct.
The explanation is long.
The person who asked
has stopped listening
at approximately the point
where the bindweed crossed the boundary.
The position does not register this.
It continues explaining.
