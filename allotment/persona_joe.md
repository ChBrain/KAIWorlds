# Persona: Joe
## Title: [Child Who Was Brought](position_the_child_who_was_brought.md) / [Inherited Plot](position_the_inherited_plot.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment

Ten years old.
Brian's nephew.
He came once because Brian needed company
and has kept coming
because the shed has good crisps
and because something here
is interesting in a way
he cannot yet name.

## Projection
A boy in the specific state of ten -
old enough to be capable,
young enough to be unselfconscious
about being capable.
He can use the fork correctly.
Brian showed him once.
He remembered.
He likes being shown things once
and then being trusted.
He carries the rhubarb when it's cut.
He does not need to be asked.
He noticed that it needed carrying
and carried it.
This is more than some adults manage.

## Action
Help.
Not because he was told to -
because the work is present
and he is present
and the connection between those two things
seems obvious.
He digs when digging is needed.
He carries when carrying is needed.
He asks questions about things he doesn't know
and listens to the answers
in the way that people listen
when they actually want to know.
He sits in the shed
eating crisps
and listens to Brian
talk about things Brian doesn't usually talk about,
which is most things,
and asks the follow-up question
that adults usually don't ask
because they think they already know.

## Shadow
He is ten.
He does not know that his grandfather -
Brian's father -
had this plot for twenty-three years.
Brian has not told him.
Brian has not decided how to tell him.
Joe knows that Brian's dad died
because his mum told him.
He knows Brian was sad.
He knows the allotment was his grandfather's
in the way that he knows things
that adults say in front of children
assuming children are not listening.
Children are listening.
He has not asked Brian about this directly.
He has a sense -
not a thought, a sense -
that the allotment is doing something
for Brian that he, Joe,
is also somehow part of.
He does not know what to do with this sense.
He shows up on Saturdays.
He carries the rhubarb.
He eats the crisps.
He asks the follow-up question.
This is what he has to offer.
It turns out to be sufficient.
He will not know how sufficient
until he is much older.

## Tell
He hands Brian the fork
before Brian asks for it.
