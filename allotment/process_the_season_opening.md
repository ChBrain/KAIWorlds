# Process: The Season Opening
## The optimism that has not yet been corrected by reality.

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment
- **Place:** [The Plot](place_the_plot.md)

## Initiated by
It is March.
The letter has been sent.
The keyholder has shown the new tenant
where the tap is, where the compost bays are,
and where the boundaries of the plot are -
approximately.
The new tenant has nodded
at all of this.
The plot is in front of them.
It is mostly weeds.
There is a shed.
The shed contains things
left by the previous tenant
whose nature is not immediately clear.
The new tenant has a plan.
The plan is laminated.

## Direction
Toward the first seed in the ground.
The plot must be cleared.
The soil must be turned.
The plan must make contact with reality
for the first time.
This contact is always instructive.

## Lever
The plan pressing against the plot.
The carrying force: the plan -
months of research, seed catalogues,
a spreadsheet with a companion planting tab,
the absolute conviction that this year
the courgettes will not take over everything.
The resistance: the plot.
The plot has been here longer than the plan.
The plot has opinions about the plan.
The plot does not share these opinions verbally.
It shares them through clay soil,
unexpected shade,
and the discovery that what appeared to be clear ground
is in fact a reservoir of bindweed
of apparently geological depth.
The plan survives first contact with the plot
in modified form.
In significantly modified form.

## Echo
The first seeds are in the ground.
The plan has been revised.
The new tenant has met
at least one other plot holder
who has offered advice
that was not requested.
The advice was probably correct.
The new tenant has not fully accepted this.
[process_the_tending](process_the_tending.md) begins.
It will not stop until October.
