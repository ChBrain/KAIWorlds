# Piece: The Shed Contents
## The archaeology of everyone who was here before.

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment
- **Place:** [The Shed](place_the_shed.md)

## Place
[The Shed](place_the_shed.md).
On the shelves, on the floor,
on the hook by the door.
Some of it in use.
Some of it waiting to be useful.
Some of it from a previous tenant
whose reasons are no longer available.

## Load Bearing
Without it the shed is empty
and the shed is never empty.
The shed contents are the plot's memory -
not the plot holder's memory,
but the memory of the plot itself,
accumulated across multiple tenancies
and never fully cleared.
Remove them and the inherited plot
has nothing to inherit.
The serious cultivator
has no physical record
of what was tried before
and whether it worked.
The shed contents are
the site's archaeology,
accessible without a trowel,
largely unexamined.

## Apparent
On the shelves:
old seed packets, most past their use-by date.
Canes in various stages of decomposition.
A sprayer with something dried in the bottom.
Three trowels - one belonging to this tenant,
one whose provenance is unclear,
one that is definitely from before.
The kettle. The two mugs.
On the floor:
a bag of something
that was probably compost
and is now just a bag.
Netting, folded once correctly
and then not again.
On the hook by the door:
a jacket that belongs to the current tenant.
A hat that does not.

## Yearbook
Everything left behind
by everyone who was here before.
The pot of WD-40 that has been here
since before the current tenant's predecessor.
The hand-lettered sign
that says PLEASE SHUT THE DOOR
in capitals
that suggest a previous incident
of significant proportion.
The single seed packet
from a variety
that has not been commercially available
since approximately 2009
and which the current tenant
has not planted because they are
keeping it in case.
In case of what
has not been determined.
