# Piece: The Bindweed
## The thing that crosses the boundary regardless of what was decided.

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment
- **Process:** [process_the_boundary_dispute](process_the_boundary_dispute.md)

## Place
[The Plot](place_the_plot.md).
Plot 6, primarily.
Also Plot 10, increasingly.
Also the boundary between them,
where the [Boundary Marker](piece_the_boundary_marker.md)
is approximately.
The bindweed does not know
where the boundary marker is.
The bindweed does not know
what a boundary is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the boundary dispute
is hypothetical.
With it the boundary dispute
has physical evidence
that requires no interpretation.
The bindweed is in the soil of Plot 10.
The bindweed came from Plot 6.
These two facts are not in dispute.
What is in dispute is
what follows from them
and who is responsible
and what *managed wildness* means
in a legal context
and whether the constitution's phrase
*weed-free and tidy*
applies to a plant
that some ecologists consider
a native species of significant value.
Remove the bindweed and these questions
do not arise.
They have arisen.
The bindweed is not going anywhere.

## Apparent
White flowers. Heart-shaped leaves.
Climbs anything.
Roots that go down further than anything else on the site.
Cut it at the surface: it returns.
Pull it up: it returns from the fragments.
Ignore it: it crosses the boundary
and begins the same process
on the adjacent plot.
From above it is, genuinely,
quite beautiful.
From ground level, in Plot 10,
in late June, it is something else.

## Yearbook
Arrived on Plot 6
in the second year of the current tenancy.
Or was always in the soil of Plot 6
and surfaced when the ground
stopped being regularly turned.
Both explanations are available.
Only one party accepts each.
Crossed into Plot 10 in 2021.
[A note](piece_the_note.md) was sent.
A reply was received explaining
managed wildness.
The bindweed continued.
Is present in the soil of Plot 10
at a depth that suggests
it has been there for approximately
three years.
Has been managed.
Has not been eliminated.
Will not be eliminated
without addressing the source.
Both parties are aware of this.
The committee has the relevant clause.
The committee is currently
defining the word *weed*.
This is taking longer than expected.
