# Place: The Plot
## The earth that has been here longer than anyone on the site.

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment

## Shown
- Approximately 250 square metres of earth.
  The earth is the same earth it always was.
  What has been grown in it, composted into it,
  dug into it, neglected in it -
  all of this is in it.
  It does not show this on the surface.
- Beds. The arrangement varies.
  Some are raised. Some are flat.
  Some are in the arrangement
  of the previous tenant.
  The current tenant has modified them.
  Partially.
- [The Shed](place_the_shed.md).
  At the back or the side.
  Containing [The Shed Contents](piece_the_shed_contents.md).
- The paths between beds.
  Their tidiness is a statement.
  About what kind of plot holder this is.
  Read immediately by anyone who knows how to read it.
- The boundary edge.
  Where [The Boundary Marker](piece_the_boundary_marker.md) is
  or is approximately.
  The beds nearest the boundary
  are always the tidiest.
- The tap connection.
  Shared with adjacent plots
  or accessed from the standpipe.
  Queue implications in dry July.
- The gate. Padlocked or latched.
  A place to leave [The Note](piece_the_note.md)
  when the person is not present.
  They are always checked on arrival.

## Holds
- [The Tools](piece_the_tools.md) - in use or against the shed
- [The Boundary Marker](piece_the_boundary_marker.md) - approximately
- Whatever is currently growing -
  or failing to grow -
  or refusing to stop growing
  despite all reasonable efforts

## Offers
- The work. To whoever shows up.
  The plot does not distinguish
  between a good week
  and a bad week for showing up.
  It requires the same attention regardless.
- The evidence of the previous tenant.
  The perennials they planted.
  The soil they amended.
  The decisions that are now permanent features.
- The view of the neighbouring plot.
  Unavoidable.
  Instructive.
  Occasionally motivating.
  Occasionally demoralising.
  Always present.

## Withheld
- What the soil actually wants.
  The plot responds to what is done to it
  but not always in the way intended.
  The relationship between
  what is planted
  and what grows
  is not fully predictable
  after thirty years
  and was not predictable
  at the beginning.
  The plot keeps this to itself.
  It reveals it in June.
