# Position: The Inherited Plot
## Here because someone else was here first.

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment
- **Process:** [process_the_season_opening](process_the_season_opening.md)
- **Process:** [process_the_winter_plot](process_the_winter_plot.md)
- **Place:** [The Plot](place_the_plot.md)

## Has
The plot and the reason for the plot
arriving as one thing.
The person died, or moved, or gave it up -
and this persona is here
because of that,
carrying the tenancy
and the implicit question
of what the tenancy means now
that the person who held it
is no longer holding it.
The position has a history
it did not make.
The shed contains things
it did not put there.
The perennials come back every year
because someone else planted them.

## Orders
Decide.
The position does not specify
what the decision is -
only that one is required
and has been required
since the moment the plot
was transferred.
Keep it or give it up.
Tend it as it was or make it yours.
Honour the history or dismantle it.
The position does not offer
unlimited time to decide.
The weeds are not waiting.

## Loses
The clarity about why they are here.
They were certain
they knew why they took the plot -
grief, or duty, or genuine interest,
or the vague sense
that this is what the person would have wanted.
The first season takes the certainty.
They are still here.
They cannot explain precisely why
in terms that would satisfy
someone asking directly.
The plot has become something
they did not plan for it to become.
The person who planted the perennials
is in it somewhere.
The position cannot separate
tending the plot
from the question of who the tending is for.

## Drives
On the loss of clarity -
comes back anyway.
The perennials come up.
Something about this
is sufficient.
They have stopped trying to explain it
in terms that would make sense to others.
The plot makes sense
on its own terms.
The position runs on that.
