# Place: The Path
## Where private plots become a public world.

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment

## Shown
- Grass or gravel or compacted earth,
  depending on the season and the site.
  Wide enough for a wheelbarrow.
  Wider in theory than in practice
  when two wheelbarrows meet.
- It runs the length of the site.
  Every plot is accessible from it.
  Every person on the site
  passes through it
  to get anywhere.
  There is no route to any plot
  that does not involve the path.
- The water standpipe.
  On the path. Shared.
  The queue at the standpipe in dry July
  is the site's most concentrated
  social event outside the AGM.
- The view of every plot simultaneously.
  From the path
  the whole site is readable.
  Which plots are tended.
  Which are not.
  Which have improved since last week.
  Which have deteriorated
  in a way that suggests
  something has happened
  that has nothing to do with gardening.

## Holds
- The traffic of the site.
  Every person. Every wheelbarrow.
  Every bag of compost.
  Every conversation that begins
  as two people happen
  to be walking in the same direction.

## Offers
- The encounter.
  Unavoidable.
  The path does not permit
  the kind of privacy the plot offers.
  On the path, everyone is visible.
  Everyone is potentially
  in conversation.
  The nod that replaces the conversation.
  The pause that becomes a conversation.
  The information that travels
  faster along the path
  than it does through
  any official channel.
- The view of the whole.
  The single place on the site
  from which the entire allotment
  can be read at once.
  Used by the serious cultivator
  to assess the competition.
  Used by the committee member
  to identify compliance issues.
  Used by the new tenant
  to understand what this place is
  before they understand what their plot is.

## Withheld
- What is noticed and not mentioned.
  The path is a public place.
  Things are seen on the path
  that are not said on the path.
  The plot that has not been touched
  in three weeks.
  The plot holder who looks
  like something has happened.
  The information travels
  from the path to the shed
  and is discussed there.
  The path sees everything.
  The path says nothing.
  The shed says it instead.
