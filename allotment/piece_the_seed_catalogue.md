# Piece: The Seed Catalogue
## The plot as it will be, in January, before the earth has an opinion.

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment

## Place
Where the plot holder is.
In winter: the kitchen table,
the armchair, the Sunday morning.
It does not live on the plot.
It belongs to the season
before the season.

## Load Bearing
Without it the waiting list
has no object for its optimism.
The winter plot has no forward motion.
The new tenant has no plan
to be corrected by reality.
The serious cultivator
has no annual reckoning
with what was tried last year
and what will be tried differently.
Remove the catalogue and the world
loses its winter engine -
the specific quality of wanting something
that has not yet been tested.

## Apparent
A printed catalogue.
Pages slightly soft from handling.
Corners turned down
on the varieties being considered.
Pencil marks in the margins.
Circles. Question marks.
The notation system
varies by person
and is legible only to them.
Some pages are unchanged
from last year.
Some pages have been
heavily reconsidered.
The tomato pages
are always heavily reconsidered.

## Yearbook
Every January since the plot was taken.
Every variety that was ordered
and grew as expected.
Every variety that was ordered
and did not.
The catalogue for the year
the courgettes won
has three courgette varieties circled.
The catalogue for the following year
has none.
The record of a person's relationship
with their own optimism,
revised annually,
never fully corrected.
