# Persona: Brian
## Title: [Inherited Plot](position_the_inherited_plot.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment

His father had Plot 9
for twenty-three years.
His father died in February.
Brian took the tenancy in March
because it seemed wrong to let it go.

## Projection
A solid, quiet man
who arrived at the plot in March
with no plan
and no experience
and the specific look
of someone who is doing something
they have not decided to do.
He has the [tools](piece_the_tools.md) his father used.
The spade is sharper than his father
ever needed to tell him.
He has not planted anything
he chose himself.
Everything on the plot
was chosen by someone else.
He tends it anyway.

## Action
Come back.
Not every week at first -
there was no particular reason
and several reasonable excuses.
Then every week,
because the rhubarb needed cutting
and nobody else would do it.
Now every Saturday,
without deciding.
The rhubarb gets made into crumble
because that is what his father made
and he does not know
what else to do with rhubarb
and has not investigated.

## Shadow
He is here because it seemed wrong
to let the plot go.
This is true.
It is also not the whole truth.
He does not know the whole truth.
He knows that on the plot
he is in a place
his father was every Saturday
for twenty-three years,
and that the soil under his hands
is the same soil
that was under his father's hands,
and that the rhubarb
comes up the same way
it came up when his father was here
and will come up the same way
when Brian is no longer here either.
He has not sat with this directly.
He has sat with it obliquely,
on the upturned crate in the shed,
with a cup of tea his father
would have made differently
and that Brian makes the same way
without noticing.
He is not grieving at the allotment.
He is doing something
that does not have a name
that is in the same vicinity as grieving
and is also not quite that.
The plot holds this without naming it.
Brian is grateful for this.
He could not tell you why.

## Tell
He cuts the rhubarb
at the same time of year
his father cut it.
