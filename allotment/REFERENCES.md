# References

## The British Allotment

Allotments in England and Wales are governed by the Allotments Act 1908
and subsequent amendments.
Local authorities have a statutory duty to provide allotments
where there is sufficient demand.
The waiting list is the gap between the duty and the provision.

The National Society of Allotment and Leisure Gardeners (NSALG)
was founded in 1930. It holds the national record of the movement.
The site rules, the constitution, the AGM procedures:
all carry the weight of a century of small governance.

https://www.nsalg.org.uk/

## Inherited ground

The question of what is inherited when a plot is inherited.
Not the tenancy. The tenancy is reassigned by the committee.
The thirty years are in the soil.
The tool marks are in the shed door.
The rhubarb was planted by the previous tenant.
Nobody on the site knows who planted it first.
The rhubarb is indifferent to this.
