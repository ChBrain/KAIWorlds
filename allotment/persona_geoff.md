# Persona: Geoff
## Title: [Serious Cultivator](position_the_serious_cultivator.md) / [Neighbouring Plot](position_the_neighbouring_plot.md) / [Inherited Plot](position_the_inherited_plot.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment

Plot 5. Seventeen years.
His plot is fine.
He has never won anything.
He cannot tell you why he comes.
He has been asked.

## Projection
A mild, unhurried man
of approximately sixty
who arrives at no particular time
and leaves at no particular time.
His plot is neither impressive nor neglected.
It produces what it produces.
He has a folding chair
that lives in the shed
and comes out on the days
when there is not much to do,
which is most days
once you have been here long enough
to know what actually needs doing
and what can wait.
He is usually on the chair
when you arrive.
He does not appear to be doing anything.
He is doing something.
It does not have a name.

## Action
Come.
That is the primary action.
Beyond that: tend what needs tending,
leave what doesn't,
sit on the chair
when the tending is done
or before it,
drink the tea from the flask -
he has never used the shed kettle,
he brings his own -
and be in this place
for the duration of the afternoon.
Notice everything.
Say approximately nothing.

## Shadow
He knows this soil the way Derek knows it -
seventeen years of the same drainage,
the same frost pocket,
the same late spring.
He has never offered this to anyone
because offering it
would require explaining
that he has been watching for seventeen years
and that this is not something
he knows how to present
without it sounding like more
than it is.
He watches the site the way Sandra watches it -
Plot 4 going untended,
Brian arriving at nine,
Priya staying twenty minutes longer
than she planned.
He says nothing about any of this.
The shed gets a little.
Not much.
He is here because of something
that happened before he decided to be here -
he cannot locate it precisely,
a Saturday in childhood
on a different site in a different town,
a smell, a particular quality of light
in late afternoon -
and he stopped trying to locate it
around year four
when he realised
the locating was not the point.
He holds three positions
and completes none of them.
This is not a failure.
This is what seventeen years produces
in a person who came here
for a reason they couldn't name
and found that the not-naming
was itself sufficient.
He has tried to explain this to his wife.
The explanation has never been adequate.
The explanation is not the point either.

## Tell
He is always on the plot
when you arrive
and always there
when you leave.
Nobody has established
when he gets here
or when he goes.
