# Piece: The Boundary Marker
## The line that is not where both parties believe it is.

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment
- **Process:** [process_the_boundary_dispute](process_the_boundary_dispute.md)

## Place
Between [The Plot](place_the_plot.md)
and the adjacent plot.
Precisely where it is
depends on who you ask.

## Load Bearing
Without it the plots have no legal definition.
Without it the boundary dispute
has no object -
the grievance requires
something physical to be about,
even when it isn't about the physical thing.
The boundary marker is what
the boundary dispute is officially about.
Its presence is therefore load-bearing
in both the legal sense
and the dramatic sense.
Remove it and the dispute
has to name what it is actually about.
Neither party wants that.

## Apparent
A post. Or a stake.
Or a line of stones placed by a previous tenant.
Or a row of bricks
that was definitely not there
when the current tenant arrived.
Or a path that both parties
use as the boundary
because the original marker
disappeared sometime between 2015 and now
and nobody has officially replaced it.
The marker's position is known.
Both parties know its position.
They know it differently.

## Yearbook
Originally placed when the site was surveyed.
Moved once - officially -
in 1994 when the paths were resurfaced.
Possibly moved unofficially
on at least two other occasions,
neither of which can be proven
and both of which are known about.
Present at every boundary dispute
the site has recorded
and several it hasn't.
It has never resolved anything.
It has provided the occasion
for everything.
