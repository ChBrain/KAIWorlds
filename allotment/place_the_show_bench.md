# Place: The Show Bench
## One afternoon in August when private effort becomes public fact.

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment
- **Process:** [process_the_show](process_the_show.md)

## Shown
- A long table or series of tables
  in the site's hall or main building.
  Set up on the morning of the show.
  Taken down at four.
- Sections marked by category:
  Runner Beans. Courgettes. Tomatoes.
  Onions. Potatoes. Soft Fruit.
  Decorative Gourd - the new category,
  still slightly controversial.
- The entries. Arranged by plot number.
  Anonymous to the judge.
  Not anonymous to anyone else.
  Everyone knows whose beans are whose.
  Nobody says this.
- The judging cards. Face down
  between eleven and two.
  Face up at two.
  The period between eleven and two
  during which nobody is permitted in the hall
  has a specific quality of suspended time.
  Plot holders stand outside.
  Conversations happen.
  The conversations are about
  everything except the show.
- The judge's table at the far end.
  The judge arrives from another site.
  The judge does not know the history.
  This is the entire point of the judge.

## Holds
- [The Show Entry](piece_the_show_entry.md) - all entries,
  from all plots, for the duration of the show

## Offers
- The verdict. To everyone simultaneously.
  At two o'clock.
  Private effort made public fact.
  Eleven months compressed
  into a first, second, third,
  and - crucially - the absence
  of any of those.
- The specific distance
  between the person and their entry.
  The entry is on the bench.
  The person is by the far wall.
  The bench holds the thing they made.
  The thing they made
  is now being looked at
  by people who did not make it.
  This distance is the show's mechanism.
  The person learns something
  about themselves from it
  every year.
  They do not always enjoy the lesson.

## Withheld
- What the judge actually thought.
  The judging cards record the decision.
  They do not record the deliberation.
  Whether the second-place runner bean
  was genuinely inferior
  or whether the judge
  simply preferred a different style
  is information that does not travel
  from the judge's table
  to the plot holder by the far wall.
  This gap has sustained
  at least three ongoing disagreements
  on this site alone.
