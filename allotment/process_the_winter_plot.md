# Process: The Winter Plot
## The tending when there is nothing to tend.

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment
- **Place:** [The Plot](place_the_plot.md)

## Initiated by
It is November.
The plot is empty.
There is nothing that urgently requires
the person's presence.
The person comes anyway.
Not every week - the urgency is gone -
but regularly, in the specific way
of someone checking on something
that doesn't need checking on.

## Direction
Toward March.
That is the only direction.
The winter plot does not grow toward anything.
It rests.
The person rests with it,
in so far as a person can rest
in a place that is cold
and muddy
and contains a shed
with a broken gutter
that should really be fixed before spring.

## Lever
The absence of task
pressing against
the person who needs one.
The carrying force:
the plot - empty, patient,
making no demands,
generating no urgency,
requiring nothing except
to be left alone until March.
The resistance:
the person - who came here
to grow things
and is now in a field
in November
looking at mud
and finding this
more sustaining than they can explain
to anyone who asks.
The winter plot does something to the person
that the growing season cannot do
because the growing season is too busy.
The winter plot is what the shed is for.

## Echo
It is still winter.
Or it is March.
The plot is the same plot.
The person has changed
in the specific way
that sitting with something empty
changes a person,
which is: slightly.
Incrementally.
Without drama.
The seed catalogues have arrived.
The laminated plan is being revised.
The decisions made in October
are being partially kept.
[process_the_season_opening](process_the_season_opening.md)
is two months away.
The person is already looking forward to it
in a way they would not
have predicted in July
when the courgettes were winning.
