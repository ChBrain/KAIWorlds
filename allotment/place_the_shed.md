# Place: The Shed
## Between the plot and the world. Where nothing urgent is happening.

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment

## Shown
- A small structure at the back or side of the plot.
  Wood, mostly. Corrugated roof.
  The roof leaks in one place
  that has been known about
  for at least two seasons.
- Shelves along one wall.
  Holding [The Shed Contents](piece_the_shed_contents.md).
- A hook by the door.
  A jacket. Sometimes a hat
  that belongs to someone
  who is no longer on the site.
- The kettle. On a shelf or a box.
  It requires an extension lead
  or a battery pack.
  The question of power
  was resolved years ago
  by whoever resolved it.
  The current tenant
  benefits from the resolution
  without knowing the full history.
- Two mugs. A jar of instant coffee.
  A jar of teabags that has been here
  longer than the current tenant.
- The door. It sticks in damp weather.
  Everyone who uses the shed knows this.
  It is not fixed.
  It is managed.

## Holds
- [The Shed Contents](piece_the_shed_contents.md)
- The kettle and its associated infrastructure
- The specific quality of a small enclosed space
  where it is acceptable to sit down
  and do nothing
  for a duration
  that would be difficult to justify
  on the plot itself

## Offers
- The tea. To whoever needs a reason
  to stop working without admitting
  they need to stop working.
- The chair - or the upturned crate,
  or the low shelf
  that functions as a seat.
  The shed always has somewhere to sit.
  It is never comfortable enough
  to stay too long.
  This is correct.
- The conversation that is not possible
  on the plot.
  The shed has no neighbouring plots.
  What is said in the shed
  is said in the shed.
  The site does not have
  an official mechanism for this.
  The shed is the unofficial mechanism.

## Withheld
- What has been said here.
  The shed has no minutes.
  What is decided in the shed
  in November
  over bad instant coffee
  between two people
  who have been here long enough
  to be honest with each other
  is not recorded anywhere.
  It is the most important
  governance structure on the site.
  Nobody has named it as such.
