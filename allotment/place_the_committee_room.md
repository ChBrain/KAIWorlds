# Place: The Committee Room
## Where the written rules meet the situation the written rules weren't written for.

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment
- **Process:** [process_the_agm](process_the_agm.md)
- **Process:** [process_the_boundary_dispute](process_the_boundary_dispute.md)

## Shown
- A room in the site's main building
  or the back room of the shed block
  or the village hall hired for the AGM.
  The room changes. The dynamic doesn't.
- A table. Enough chairs for the committee
  plus two extra for members of the public
  who attend under their right to observe.
  Both extra chairs are sometimes occupied.
  This changes the atmosphere considerably.
- The folder.
  The constitution, the tenancy agreements,
  the minutes of previous meetings.
  On the table or on the shelf.
  Present at all formal proceedings.
  Consulted at moments of impasse.
  Occasionally resolved by.
- A noticeboard.
  The rota. The site rules.
  A laminated sheet about bonfires
  that everyone has read
  and nobody agrees on.
  A photograph from a previous summer show.
- The chair's position.
  At the head of the table.
  The chair has been in this position
  for eleven years.
  The chair is very tired.

## Holds
- The folder - containing the constitution,
  the tenancy agreements,
  the minutes,
  and [The Note](piece_the_note.md) correspondence
  filed under Correspondence

## Offers
- The formal process.
  To whoever needs their grievance
  to be a matter of record
  rather than a matter of feeling.
  The committee room does not resolve feelings.
  It resolves - or defers -
  matters of record.
- The three committee members.
  Required by the constitution
  for any disciplinary matter.
  They are always present.
  They do not always agree.
  The constitution anticipated this.

## Withheld
- Whether the outcome was actually fair.
  The committee room produces
  decisions that are procedurally correct.
  Whether procedurally correct
  and actually fair
  are the same thing
  in a given instance
  is a question the room
  does not address.
  Both parties leave the room
  with the decision.
  At least one of them
  also leaves with this question.
  Sometimes both.
