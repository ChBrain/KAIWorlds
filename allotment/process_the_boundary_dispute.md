# Process: The Boundary Dispute
## The matter that is not about the boundary.

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment
- **Place:** [The Plot](place_the_plot.md)

## Initiated by
Something has crossed the boundary.
Bindweed. A root system.
A shed that is - and this will need to be measured -
possibly four inches over the line.
Or a bonfire whose smoke
drifted in a direction
that a reasonable person
would have anticipated.
Or nothing has crossed the boundary
but the boundary itself is disputed -
the original markers having been
repositioned at some point
between 1987 and now
by someone who is no longer on the site
and whose reasons are therefore
unavailable for examination.

## Direction
Toward resolution -
which on an allotment site
means toward a state
in which both parties
have agreed to disagree
in a way that can be maintained indefinitely
without either party
having to acknowledge
what the dispute is actually about.
The boundary is not what the dispute is about.
The dispute is about something older.

## Lever
The stated grievance
pressing against
the actual grievance.
The carrying force:
the thing that crossed the boundary -
the bindweed, the smoke, the shed,
the four inches -
which is specific and measurable
and can be brought to the committee.
The resistance:
the actual grievance -
which predates the boundary incident
by anywhere from one to thirty years
and involves a tone of voice,
or a comment at the AGM in 2017,
or the perception that some people
on this site think they own it
just because they've been here longest.
The committee will address the boundary.
The committee does not have jurisdiction
over the actual grievance.
Nobody does.
It accumulates instead.

## Echo
The boundary matter has been
resolved, deferred, or escalated to the committee.
The actual grievance
has been filed under the boundary matter
where it will remain
until the next incident
provides a new boundary matter
to file it under.
Both parties continue to tend their plots.
They tend them very deliberately
without looking up
when the other party is present.
This requires considerable skill.
Both have become excellent at it.
