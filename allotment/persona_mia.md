# Persona: Mia
## Title: [Child Who Was Brought](position_the_child_who_was_brought.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment

Five years old.
Priya's daughter.
She comes because her mother comes.
She does not know
this is a reason.

## Projection
Small. Usually muddy
from approximately four minutes
after arrival.
She has her own trowel -
it was a present,
orange handle,
her name written on it
in permanent marker
because things go missing.
She has a patch.
A small bed at the near end of Plot 11
that Priya marked out
and said: *that bit's yours.*
She has planted things in it.
Some of them have grown.
She is not sure which ones she planted
and which ones arrived.
She has not distinguished between them.
Both are hers.

## Action
Dig.
Not productively -
the digging does not have
a destination.
The digging is the point.
Also: find worms and report them
to whoever is closest.
Also: eat the strawberries
before anyone mentions the Show.
Also: sit in the shed
on the big crate
and swing her feet
and ask questions
that don't require answers.
Also: be at the allotment.
Just that.
Be in this place.

## Shadow
She does not have a shadow yet.
She is five.
What she has instead
is the receiving -
the smell of the turned earth,
the weight of the orange trowel,
the particular quality
of her mother on this plot,
which is different
from her mother anywhere else
and which Mia cannot name
but registers in the way
that five-year-olds register things -
completely, without filing.
She is accumulating
what she will not know
she accumulated
for thirty years.
On an unremarkable Tuesday
when she is thirty-five
she will put her name
on a waiting list
and not be entirely sure why
and it will be this -
the smell, the trowel,
the specific way her mother
looked at the plot and exhaled,
as though she had arrived
somewhere she had been trying
to get to all week.

## Tell
She finds a worm.
She brings it to Priya.
