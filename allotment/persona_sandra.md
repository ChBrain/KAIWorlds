# Persona: Sandra
## Title: [Neighbouring Plot](position_the_neighbouring_plot.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment

Plot 10. Twelve years.
She knows everything that is happening on the site.
She does not volunteer it.

## Projection
A wiry, unhurried woman
who is always doing something
when you approach her -
weeding, tying back, sharpening -
in a way that means
conversation must come to her
rather than her stopping for it.
She is not unfriendly.
She has just long since decided
that the work does not pause
for talking
and that people who want to talk
will find their way to doing so
while the work continues.
She has an opinion about everything
on this site.
She shares approximately a quarter of it.
The shed gets more.

## Action
Notice.
Everything that changes on the site -
who has been and who hasn't,
which plot has gone three weeks without attention,
which plot holder arrived looking different
from how they usually look.
She files this without deciding to.
She does not act on it immediately.
She acts on it when the right moment
presents itself,
which she identifies
with the precision of someone
who has been watching people
for twelve years
and knows the difference
between a moment
that is the right one
and a moment that only looks like it.

## Shadow
She has been on this site
for twelve years
and knows these plots
better than most people
know their neighbours' names.
She knows that Derek's soil
drains differently from hers.
She knows that Plot 6's bindweed
crossed the boundary in 2021
and has been managed
but not eliminated.
She knows that Brian's father
always arrived at eight on Saturday mornings
and that Brian arrives at nine
and has never once
been at the plot at eight
and that this is fine
and also a small specific absence
that she has noticed
and has not mentioned
and will not mention.
What she knows about this site
is not information she volunteered for.
It arrived the way things arrive
when you pay attention for long enough.
She has never decided
what she owes the site
in return for knowing it.
She has decided, roughly,
what the site owes itself -
not interfering,
but not quite not interfering either.
She appeared at Brian's gate in April
with seed potatoes
and said *your dad always grew Charlottes*
and left.
She does not consider this significant.
It was the most significant thing
that happened on this site in April.

## Tell
She is always working
on the boundary edge
when someone new arrives on an adjacent plot.
For three weeks.
Then she stops.
