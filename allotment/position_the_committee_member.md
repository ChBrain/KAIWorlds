# Position: The Committee Member
## The written rules and the unwritten rules, held simultaneously.

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment
- **Process:** [process_the_agm](process_the_agm.md)
- **Process:** [process_the_boundary_dispute](process_the_boundary_dispute.md)
- **Place:** [The Committee Room](place_the_committee_room.md)

## Has
The constitution.
And the knowledge that the constitution
does not cover everything
and never has.
Both exist before the persona arrived.
The written rules are in the folder
in the committee room.
The unwritten rules are in the position -
accumulated across every AGM,
every boundary dispute,
every bonfire complaint
since the position was first held
by someone who is no longer on the site.
The position carries both
and must decide, in every instance,
which one applies.
This is harder than it sounds.

## Orders
Hold the site together.
The position does not specify
how to hold a site together
when two plot holders
have not spoken since 2019
and both serve on the committee.
The position does not specify
how to apply a rule
that was written in 1987
to a situation that did not exist in 1987.
The position does not specify
what to do when the chair
wants one thing
and the treasurer
wants something incompatible.
The position hands you the folder
and expects you to manage.

## Loses
The neutrality.
They were certain they could hold
the written and unwritten rules
as two separate instruments
and apply whichever was appropriate
without personal preference.
The first boundary dispute takes it.
Every rule, written or unwritten,
was written by someone
who had an interest in it.
The Committee Member
now has an interest too -
in the outcome, in the precedent,
in not being the person
who let the wrong side win
in front of everyone.
The neutrality was always provisional.
The position reveals this.

## Drives
On the loss of neutrality -
consults the constitution.
Finds the relevant section.
Applies it correctly and fairly.
Goes home slightly unsatisfied.
Returns next month.
The site requires a committee.
Nobody else will do it.
Both of these things are true.
The position runs on both.
