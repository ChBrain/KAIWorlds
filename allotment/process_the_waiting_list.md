# Process: The Waiting List
## The years spent wanting something before you know what it is.

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment
- **Place:** [The Council Office](place_the_council_office.md)

## Initiated by
Someone has put their name on the list.
They do not know exactly why.
A vague idea about growing things.
A sense that this would be good for them
in a way they cannot specify.
The list is managed by the council.
The council is not in a hurry.
The average wait is four years.
Some people have been waiting since 2009.
They have not withdrawn their names.
They are not entirely sure
what they are waiting for anymore.
They are still waiting.

## Direction
Toward the letter.
The council letter that arrives
on an unremarkable Tuesday
informing the applicant
that a plot has become available
and would they like to proceed.
The direction is entirely passive -
the applicant does nothing
except remain willing.
The plot comes to them
or it doesn't.
Time passes either way.

## Lever
The idea of the plot
pressing against
the reality of the waiting.
The carrying force:
the original impulse - the magazine article,
the retirement, the health scare,
the neighbour's tomatoes,
whatever it was that made the person
put their name on a list
for something they have never done.
The resistance: four years.
During which the idea of the plot
becomes more perfect
with each passing season
because it has not yet had to be real.
The waiting list produces
the most enthusiastic allotment holders
on the site.
They arrive with plans.
The earth receives the plans
without comment.

## Echo
The letter has arrived.
The plot is available.
The applicant says yes
before they have thought carefully about
what yes means in terms of Saturdays.
[process_the_season_opening](process_the_season_opening.md) can begin.
