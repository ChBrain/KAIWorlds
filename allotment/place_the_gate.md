# Place: The Gate
## Where the person who has a job becomes the person who has a plot.

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment

## Shown
- A gate. Metal or wood.
  Padlocked, or combination,
  or on a simple latch
  if the site is trusting.
- The noticeboard beside it.
  The site rules.
  The committee contact details.
  A notice about the AGM
  that has been there since October.
  The rota for the communal areas.
  [A Note](piece_the_note.md), sometimes,
  addressed to no one
  or to everyone
  or to the person
  who has been leaving the tap running.
- The view through it.
  The plots. The paths.
  The shed block.
  And beyond it all:
  whoever is on their plot today,
  which tells you
  without walking further in
  something about
  what kind of Saturday this will be.
- The view behind it.
  The street. The car.
  The thing that has to be done later.
  The week that was.

## Holds
- The key, or the combination,
  which is changed periodically
  and announced on the noticeboard
  in advance.
  Some members find out
  after the fact.
  This has been the subject
  of a note.

## Offers
- The crossing.
  One side: the week, the job,
  the screen, the commute,
  the decision that has been pending
  since Tuesday.
  Other side: the plot.
  The gap between those two things
  is the width of a gate.
  The gate does not enforce the crossing.
  The crossing happens anyway.
  Most plot holders notice it.
  Some of them came here
  specifically for it.

## Withheld
- Whether the crossing works today.
  The gate cannot guarantee
  that the week stays on its side.
  Most of the time it does.
  Some Saturdays the thing
  that has been pending since Tuesday
  comes in through the gate
  and sits at the end of the plot
  and waits.
  The plot does not help with this.
  The plot requires attention.
  The pending thing also requires attention.
  The gate offered the crossing.
  It cannot do the rest.
