# Piece: The Rhubarb
## The thing that comes back.

## Owner
- **Project:** Allotment
- **Persona:** [Brian](persona_brian.md)

## Place
[The Plot](place_the_plot.md).
Where it has always been.
Plot 9, the bed nearest the shed.
Not planted this year.
Not planted by the current tenant.
It simply came up.
As it always does.
As it always will.

## Load Bearing
Without it Brian's plot
is a plot he chose.
With it the plot chose him -
or rather the rhubarb chose him,
and the plot came with it,
and neither of them
made the choice consciously.
Remove the rhubarb and the Inherited Plot position
loses its physical mechanism -
the thing that returns
regardless of what the tenant intends
or whether the tenant intends anything at all.

## Apparent
A perennial. Broad leaves, red stalks.
Arrived before the current tenant.
Will remain after.
Cut annually, at the right time.
The right time is known
the way things are known
that were taught without being taught -
by watching, once,
and then by the body knowing.
It makes crumble.
That is what it makes.
That is sufficient.

## Yearbook
Planted by the previous tenant -
the man who had this plot for twenty-three years
and whose fork is now in the shed
and whose son cuts the rhubarb
at the same time of year
and makes crumble with the same recipe
and has not decided
whether this is grief
or something in the same vicinity
that does not require a name.
Before that: another tenant.
Before that: someone else.
The rhubarb does not distinguish
between them.
It comes up the same way every year.
The plot has had it
longer than anyone currently on the site
has been anywhere.
