# Piece: The Errand

## Owner
- **Project:** Oh, April
- **Position:** [The Carrier](position_carrier.md)

## Place
[The Route](place_route.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it [Placement](process_placement.md) has a destination.
A destination means the [Carrier](position_carrier.md) can arrive and be done.
The Errand removes arrival as a possibility  - 
the prank is the journey, not the endpoint.
Remove it and the [Carrier](position_carrier.md)'s position loses its defining quality:
the one who travels toward something that is not there.

## Apparent
An instruction. Verbal or written.
A destination named with confidence.
A task described as simple, specific, completable.
Go here. Find this. Ask for that.
None of it exists.
The instruction does not mention this.

## Yearbook
In Scotland it has been called *Hunting the Gowk*  - 
the cuckoo hunt, the fool's errand  - 
where the victim is sent on a series of tasks
each one passing them on to the next
with a sealed note that reads *dinnae laugh, dinnae smile,
hunt the gowk another mile.*
In workshops it has been a long weight,
a left-handed screwdriver,
a tin of striped paint,
a bubble for a spirit level.
The apprentice sent to fetch something unfetchable
while the workshop watches.
The errand is never about the destination.
It is about what the traveller does
when they begin to suspect
there is no destination.
Do they keep going?
Turn back?
Open the note?
The errand is patient.
It has sent many people down [The Route](place_route.md)
toward something that was never there.
It is possible you have been on it.
The destination felt very specific at the time.
