# Piece: The Announcement

## Owner
- **Project:** Oh, April
- **Position:** [The Designer](position_designer.md)

## Place
[The Street](place_street.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it the [Designer](position_designer.md) has no vessel.
The universal prank requires institutional authority as its surface  - 
the form that signals *this requires no suspicion.*
Remove it and the [Designer](position_designer.md)'s prank must be aimed at one person.
The scale collapses from a town to a single [Target](position_target.md).
The hypothesis loses its widest proof.

## Apparent
Printed. Broadcast. Published.
In the voice of an institution that has earned trust
over many years of telling the truth.
Formatted correctly.
Sourced apparently.
No visible seams.

## Yearbook
It has been a Swiss spaghetti harvest
reported with complete seriousness
on a respected news programme
watched by millions who called in afterwards
to ask how to grow their own.
It has been a digital clock installed in a famous tower.
A bell purchased and renamed.
A planet alignment that would briefly reduce gravity.
A left-handed version of a standard product.
A new service launched on the first of April
that everyone assumed was a joke
and turned out to be real.
Each time it wore the clothes of institutional authority.
Each time authority was the surface
and something else was the payload.
Each time the gap between what the audience believed
and what was true
revealed something about the audience
the audience did not know about itself.
You read announcements with a certain trust.
You know that.
The announcement knows it too.
