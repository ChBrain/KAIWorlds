# References

## Restoration Conservation

Conservation-restoration - the professional practice of examination, documentation, treatment, and preventive care of cultural heritage material. Governed by standards including the ICOM-CC guidelines and the American Institute for Conservation Code of Ethics.

The condition report - standard documentation produced by a conservator-restorer prior to treatment. Records the physical state of an object: material composition, damage, previous interventions, and proposed treatment. The primary instrument for decision-making about intervention.

Raking light - oblique illumination applied across a surface at a low angle (typically 5-45 degrees) to reveal surface texture, impasto, abrasion, previous restoration, and crack structure invisible under direct light. Standard examination technique in paintings conservation.

Consolidation - the stabilisation of deteriorating or detaching material before further treatment can proceed. Precondition for examination of fragile surfaces.

The withholding - the ethical core of conservation practice: minimum intervention. The rule that what is irreversible cannot be corrected. The decision not to act is as considered as the decision to act.
