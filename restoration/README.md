## What this world is

A studio. A treatment room. A single morning moment.
The work chain and the body chain run simultaneously.

One persona. Five pieces. Three places. Four positions. Nine processes.
This world does not repair. It reads.

Vera stands at the angle the raking light requires - the longest sightline across whatever surface is present. Before the history is offered, she asks one question. She has done this for twenty years. The gloves went on after the accident. They have not come off since.
The hands carry three chains at once: examination has its instrument, the patient surface has its subject, the check has its engine. The work chain and the body chain occupy the same moment. Vera does not choose between them.

## The studio

Three places structure the world.

**The Studio** - where the work happens. A surface is present or about to be. The raking light is positioned or will be. Examination runs before intervention. Consolidation before treatment.

**The Treatment Room** - clinical space, after the accident. The hands are the patient surface here. The treating clinician holds the condition report that differs from what the restorer holds. The gap between the two assessments runs through the recovery.

**The Moment of Commitment** - the half-second before the gloves close each morning. The hands briefly present, not touching each other, not knowing where to go. The check runs. Then the gloves close and the work begins.

---

## The persona

**Vera** *(The Restorer)* - stands at an angle. Not the centre, not the wall. The position from which the longest sightline crosses whatever surface is present. The gloves are on. She does not move toward a surface - the distance closes gradually. She does not know she is inside a consolidation she built.

---

## The pieces

**The Hands** - load-bearing. Three chains run through them simultaneously: the work chain, the injury chain, the body chain. Remove them and examination has no instrument, the patient surface has no subject, the check has no engine.

**The Gloves** - pale grey, close-fitted, seams at the knuckle line. Applied each morning after the accident. The right hand moves to cover the left when anyone looks at the gloves.

**The Raking Light** - positioned at forty-five degrees across the surface. Finds crack structure before the eye does. The angle Vera has been at since the raking light position became automatic.

**The Scar** - the surface of the right hand, damaged. Located and updated each morning in the check. The boundary is known. The reading continues.

**The Condition Report** - the complete record of what the examination found. Every layer named, every damage mapped, every previous intervention identified. The restorer's condition report and the clinical record do not agree.

---

## The processes

**Examination** - the trained reading in the restorer, running before the decision to examine. Direction: every layer named. The prepared surface resists: previous consolidation applied so this reading cannot proceed.

**Intervention** - what follows from the finding. Direction: toward the minimum necessary. The withholding runs alongside.

**Consolidation** - stabilising before treatment. The precondition for examination to run again.

**The Finding** - what the raking light surfaces. The name for what was not visible before the angle.

**The Check** - the body chain's engine. The right hand reading the left. The left reading the right. The boundary of the scar located and updated. Running every morning since the damage.

**The Accident** - the event that produced the scar, the gloves, the check. It runs once. Its echo runs through every subsequent process.

**The Treatment** - clinical care of the hands in the treatment room. Direction: functional recovery. The clinical measure: the hands work. Sensation sufficient. Movement sufficient.

**The Recovery** - what follows the clinical threshold. The hands past discharge. The gap between what the clinical record holds and what the restorer holds.

**The Withholding** - the decision not to intervene. Not absence of response. The deliberate halt before the irreversible action.
