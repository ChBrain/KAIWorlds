# Role: Tori
## Title: The One Who Throws

## Owner
**Project:** Judo

**Role:** Tori

The technique is theirs to execute.
They commit first.
There is no throw without commitment.

## Projection
Already moving when you arrive.
Not rushing — committed.
The difference is that rushing
has not yet decided.
Commitment has.
The body is already past the point of return
before the mind finishes the thought.

## Action
Enter. Break the balance.
Find the moment when the force is fully extended
and give way.
Redirect what arrives.
The throw happens at the intersection
of their momentum and your stillness.
You are the stillness.
They go over.

## Shadow
The throw only works
if you give something up first.
Your back. Your position. Your balance.
Tori cannot throw without becoming
temporarily vulnerable.
The technique requires the exposure.
This is not a flaw in the technique.
This is the technique.
Tori has not fully understood this yet.
They are still trying to throw
without the exposure.
The throws that work
are the ones where they stop trying.

## Tell
They turn their back.
That is when the throw begins.
