## What this world is

A world about transmission. The principle makes size irrelevant. The belt marks where you are. The white belt means the same thing twice — once when the body knows nothing, once when it knows everything.

The Kodokan was built to transmit one thing: *seiryoku zenyo* — maximum efficiency, minimum effort. Everything else follows from this. The dojo holds sixty-five techniques, three forms of practice, and the belt that marks where you are. The Sensei has seen every throw. Kano checks whether the institution is still transmitting what it was built to transmit.

This world does not resolve. It trains.

## The personas

**Kano** — the smallest man in every room he entered found the principle that makes size irrelevant. He built the institution that teaches it. He never stopped to ask whether the institution was still him.

---

## The roles

**Sensei** — they have seen every throw. They can no longer be thrown by most of them. This is not mastery. This is the problem mastery creates.

**Tori** — the technique is theirs to execute. They commit first. There is no throw without commitment.

**Uke** — they are thrown. This is not what happens to them. This is what they do.

---

## The place

**The Dojo** — wherever the mat is. The mat does not distinguish between throws. It receives. It is always there.

---

## The pieces

**The belts** — white through judan. The whole system in cloth. Sixteen grades, two colours at the start and at the summit. Kano made two belts: white and black. Everything between them is what it took to get there.

**Kano's photograph** — on the wall of every dojo. Black and white. A small man, precise, still. Looking at whoever is on the mat. Load-bearing: the question the dojo must keep asking itself.

---

## The processes

Sixty-five techniques, three forms of practice, four breakfalls.

**Ukemi** — the four falls. Mae Ukemi, Ushiro Ukemi, Yoko Ukemi, Mae Mawari Ukemi. The fall is the first technique. Without it nothing else is safe to attempt.

**Kata** — commitment that cannot be excused by not knowing. The roles are assigned before the mat. The technique is known. The outcome is known. What kata leaves behind is not understanding — it is the thing that runs before understanding arrives.

**Randori** — cannot stop until both personas have been both. Neither role is assigned in advance. The process determines it.

**Shiai** — finds out what the training actually did. No assigned roles. No predetermined techniques. No known outcome.

**Nage Waza** — forty-three throwing techniques across five belt levels: Dai Ikkyo through Gokyo, plus Shinmeisho No Waza counter throws. The body learns to break balance, enter, and redirect — in that order.

**Osaekomi Waza** — five pinning techniques. The hold that cannot be escaped because the geometry of the hold makes escape the same as deeper entanglement.

**Shime Waza** — two choking techniques. The triangle cannot be defeated by force.

**Kansetsu Waza** — two joint locks. The arm used against itself.
