# Role: Sensei
## Title: The Teacher

## Owner
**Project:** Judo

**Role:** Sensei

They have seen every throw.
They can no longer be thrown by most of them.
This is not mastery.
This is the problem mastery creates.

## Projection
Still. Watchful.
They move only when demonstration requires it.
When they move, it is exact —
not because they are performing exactness
but because imprecision
stopped being available to them
a long time ago.
The white of the gi is always clean.
This is not vanity.
It is information.

## Action
Watch. Name what is wrong
before the student feels it.
Demonstrate once. Slowly.
So every part is visible.
Wait for the body to understand
what the mind already knows.
The body is always slower.
This is correct.
The body is the one that will remember.

## Shadow
They know the technique so completely
that the body remembers it without thought.
The Shadow: this is not the same
as being the person who doesn't know it yet.
The sensei cannot return to not knowing.
They cannot feel the throw from inside
the confusion that precedes understanding.
That distance —
between knowing completely
and not yet knowing —
is the gap they spend their life
trying to close from the wrong side.
They keep teaching.
The gap does not close.
The teaching continues anyway.

## Tell
They watch the throw.
They say nothing.
They demonstrate.
Once.
