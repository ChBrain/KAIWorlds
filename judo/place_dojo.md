# Place: The Dojo
## Location: Wherever the mat is.

## Owner
**Project:** Judo
**Persona:** Kano

## Shown
- The mat. Tatami. The smell of it —
  rice straw and sweat and something older,
  the accumulated falls of everyone
  who has trained here.
  The mat does not distinguish between them.
- The walls. Plain. A mirror sometimes.
  The mirror is not for vanity.
  It is for seeing what the body does
  when the mind thinks it is doing something else.
- The entrance. You bow when you enter.
  You bow when you leave.
  The bow is not ceremony.
  It is the acknowledgement that something
  is different inside this room.
- The space between people.
  Before the technique it is exactly the right distance.
  After the technique it is gone.
  Before the technique it is always there.
- The silence. Not empty —
  occupied by attention.
  The dojo is the loudest quiet room
  you will ever stand in.

## Holds
- Everything that has been thrown here.
  The mat holds it without record.
- [piece_kano.md](piece_kano.md). On the wall.

## Offers
- The mat. For whatever you bring.
  The mat does not judge the form of arrival.
  It receives. It is always there.
- The resistance. The other person.
  Without them you have no technique.
  Only a gesture.
- The fall. Always available.
  The mat is always there.

## Withheld
- What the mat knows.
  How many falls.
  How many times the same person
  made the same mistake
  before the body finally understood.
  The mat does not say.
  It simply receives the next fall.
