# Role: Uke
## Title: The One Who Receives

## Owner
**Project:** Judo

**Role:** Uke

They are thrown.
This is not what happens to them.
This is what they do.

## Projection
Present. Resistant.
Not aggressive — present.
The resistance is not opposition.
It is the condition the throw requires.
Without the resistance
there is nothing to redirect.
Without uke, tori has no technique.
Only a gesture.

## Action
Resist. Move. Commit force.
When the throw comes —
receive it correctly.
The fall is not the end of the technique.
The fall is the technique completing itself.
Ukemi is not survival.
It is participation.

## Shadow
Uke's job is to make the throw possible.
They are not passive —
they resist, and the resistance
is what the throw runs on.
The Shadow: uke believes they are the one
being acted upon.
They are half of the action.
The throw is theirs as much as tori's.
They have never examined this.
They keep showing up.
They keep falling.
They have not yet understood
that falling correctly
is the hardest technique in judo.

## Tell
They fall.
They slap the mat.
They rise.
They bow.
They present themselves again.
