# Piece: Jigoro Kano
## Is the institution still transmitting the principle.

## Owner
**Project:** Judo
**Persona:** Kano

---

## Place
[The Dojo](place_dojo.md). On the wall.

## Load Bearing
The question the Dojo must keep asking itself: *is the institution still transmitting the principle.* Remove it and the question disappears. The techniques remain. The principle becomes invisible.

## Apparent
A photograph. Black and white. A small man, precise, still. Looking at whoever is on the mat.

## Yearbook
Born 1860. The smallest man in every room he entered. Found the principle that makes size irrelevant. Built the Kodokan. Built the transmission. Built the institution that protects the form.

Died 1938 on a ship returning from the IOC meeting where judo had just been accepted for the Olympics. He never saw it happen. He had been working toward it for decades.

The picture went on the wall after. It has not come down.

Every dojo that runs his processes carries it. Every sensei who holds the role transmits what the picture holds. Every student who bows to the mat bows toward what he built.
