# References

## Jigoro Kano (1860–1938)

Born in Mikage, Hyogo Prefecture, Japan. Educator, athlete, founder of judo.
Studied jujutsu from 1877. Founded the Kodokan in 1882. Developed judo as
a system of physical, mental, and moral education. First Asian member of the
International Olympic Committee (1909). Died at sea, 4 May 1938, returning
from the IOC meeting in Cairo where judo had been accepted for the Olympics.

**The works this world is built on:**

*Kodokan Judo* — Jigoro Kano
First published 1986 by Kodansha International, Tokyo.
The definitive technical account of judo as Kano systematised it.
Recommended edition: Kodansha paperback.

*Mind Over Muscle: Writings from the Founder of Judo* — Jigoro Kano
Edited by Naoki Murata. Published 2005 by Kodansha International, Tokyo.
Kano's essays on the principles beneath the techniques.

## The formal classification

*Kodokan Gokyo no Waza* — Kodokan Institute, Tokyo
The official classification of judo throwing techniques, revised 1920.
The canonical five groups that structure the nage waza in this world.

## The institution

The Kodokan Judo Institute, founded 1882, Tokyo.
