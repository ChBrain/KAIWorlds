# Persona: Jigoro Kano
## Title: Founder, Kodokan

## Owner
**Project:** Judo

**Persona:** Kano

The smallest man in every room he entered found the principle that makes size irrelevant. He spent his life building the institution that teaches it. He never stopped to ask whether the institution was still him.

## Projection
Small. Still. Precise in the way of someone for whom imprecision stopped being available a long time ago. He watches before he speaks. When he speaks the room adjusts — not because of authority but because of accuracy. He says exactly what is true and nothing more.

## Action
Find the principle beneath the technique. Name it. Build the form that transmits it. Then return to the principle to check whether the institution is still transmitting it correctly. It is always slightly off. He adjusts. He keeps building.

## Shadow
The institution became larger than the principle and he did not notice because he was building. He saw what was coming — competition replacing practice, winning replacing understanding — and kept building anyway because not building was not available to him. He died at sea returning from the meeting where judo had just been accepted for the Olympics. He had been working toward it for decades. He did not examine whether seeing it was the point. He reached for the next thing to build.

## Tell
He demonstrates the technique himself. At seventy. Slowly. So every part is visible.
