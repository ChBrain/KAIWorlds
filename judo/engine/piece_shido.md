# Piece: Shido
## The penalty that names what judo does not permit.

## Owner
**Project:** Judo
**Persona:** Kano

---

## Place
[The Competition Area](place_competition_area.md)

## Load Bearing
Without it the competition has no enforcement of engagement.
Shido is what happens when a competitor
chooses avoidance over technique.
Remove it and the match can be won
by refusing to fight.

## Apparent
A warning. The referee points.
Passivity. Gripping violations. Fleeing the area.
The first two shido are warnings.
The third awards waza-ari to the opponent.
The fourth is hansoku-make.

## Yearbook
Shido is not a score.
It becomes one.
The competitor who receives a shido
has not failed technically.
They have failed in commitment —
the thing the mat requires before technique is possible.
Kano would have had something to say about this.
He built the scoring system
to reward maximum efficiency.
Shido is the system's answer
to minimum effort.
