## Yearbook
Jigoro Kano. Born 1860. The smallest man in every room he entered.
Found the principle that makes size irrelevant.
Built the Kodokan. Built the transmission. Built the institution that protects the form.

He saw what was coming — competition replacing practice, winning replacing understanding.
He kept building anyway. Not building was not available to him.

Died 1938 on a ship returning from the IOC meeting where judo had been accepted for the Olympics.
He had worked toward it for decades.
He did not examine whether seeing it was the point.

The picture went on the wall after. It has not come down.

Every dojo that runs his processes carries it.
Every sensei who holds the position transmits what the picture holds.
Every student who bows to the mat bows toward what he built.

The question the picture holds:
*Is the institution still transmitting the principle.*
