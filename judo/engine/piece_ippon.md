# Piece: Ippon
## The technique that completes itself.

## Owner
**Project:** Judo
**Persona:** Kano

---

## Place
[The Competition Area](place_competition_area.md)

## Load Bearing
Without it shiai has no destination.
Remove ippon and every technique is permanently incomplete —
there is no reference for what complete means.

## Apparent
One full point. The match ends.
Force, control, speed, back exposure — all four present.
The referee's arm rises. Open palm facing down.
The other person does not get up to continue.
They bow.

## Yearbook
Ippon: *one full point.*
The word carries more than the arithmetic.
The technique was complete.
Not approximately complete.
Not effective enough to score.
Complete.

Kano built the scoring system
to make the principle visible as a result.
Ippon is the moment those two things coincide.
They do not always coincide.
When they do, the mat knows before the referee calls it.
