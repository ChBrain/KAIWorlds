# Process: Referee
## The body that speaks for the mat.

## Owner
**Project:** Judo
**Persona:** Kano

---

## Initiates
The bow. The competitors at their marks.
*Hajime.*
The match is live.

## Direction
The referee moves with the match.
Watches. Names. Controls.
Every call is a gesture before it is a word.

*Hajime* — begin.
*Matte* — stop. Return to marks.
*Sono-mama* — freeze. The position holds.
*Yoshi* — continue from the freeze.
*Osaekomi* — the hold is valid. The clock starts.
*Toketa* — the hold is broken. The clock stops.
*Yuko* — the smallest score. The arm extends sideways.
*Waza-ari* — half point. Two fingers raised.
*Ippon* — full point. The arm rises. Open palm down. The match ends.
*Shido* — penalty. The finger points.
*Hansoku-make* — disqualification. The match ends without a technique.
*Soremade* — that is all. Time has ended.

## Lever
The vocabulary.
Remove any single call and the match loses
the ability to name what just happened.
The gesture is not ceremony.
It is the scoring system made visible.

## Echo
The score on the board.
The competitor who won.
The technique that was named — or the one that wasn't.
The mat does not hold this.
The record does.
