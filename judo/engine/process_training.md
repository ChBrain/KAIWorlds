# Process: Training
## The sensei directs. The body receives.

## Owner
**Project:** Judo
**Persona:** Kano

---

## Initiates
The session opens.
The sensei has decided what the body needs today.
The students do not know yet.
They are on the mat. That is enough.

## Direction
The sensei shows. Once.
The student does.
The sensei corrects what is wrong
before the student has named it wrong.
The student does again.
The correction is not instruction.
It is the gap made visible
so the body can close it.

## Lever
The sensei's precision.
Approximate correction produces approximate technique.
The lever is the exactness of what is named —
not the effort of the student,
which is already present.

## Echo
The body is slightly different from when it arrived.
Not the mind — the body.
It does not know yet what it learned.
It will know when the technique is needed
and is already moving.
