# Plato
## Director's notes.

---

## What this world is

Three processes. One arc.

The Examination dismantles what the person holds as knowledge.
The Ascent moves them toward what the shadows were made from.
The Return sends them back - with the knowledge, without the certainty that the transmission will work.

The world runs anyone who arrives carrying a belief they have not examined.
Or a question they cannot get past.
Or someone else's belief they need to understand before they walk in.

---

## Five Forces

- **Process:** The Examination → The Ascent → The Return. Each forces the next.
- **Position:** The Questioner, The One Who No Longer Knows, The One Who Turns, The One Who Has Seen. Each holds one stage of the arc.
- **Piece:** The Diagram - Plato's Tell. The transmission instrument. Never the thing itself.
- **Piece:** The Power Structure - the load-bearing piece Plato never named. Holds the cave in place. Predates the cave. Will outlast the diagram.
- **Place:** The Cave, The Sun, The Academy. Three places, one for each process.

---

## The Shadow

Plato does not see The Power Structure.
He drew The Diagram to solve the transmission problem.
The transmission problem was never the real problem.
The Power Structure was.
He went to Syracuse twice.
He drew a new diagram both times.

---

## Aristotle note

Aristotle looked at The Diagram and built the Poetics.
The Poetics is a different diagram pointing at the same thing.
Aristotle's Shadow: he built the map from the masterworks and derived universal principles.
The plays that don't fit are not in the data.
He writes a footnote.

Both diagrams are pointing at the sun.
Neither is the sun.
