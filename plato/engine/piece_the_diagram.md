# Piece: The Diagram

## Owner
- **Project:** Plato
- **Persona:** [Plato](persona_plato.md)

## Place
[The Academy](place_the_academy.md).
On the wall.
Already there when the session begins.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and [The Return](process_the_return.md) has no instrument.
The one who has seen the sun
has no way to point at what they saw.
The transmission requires a surface.
The diagram is that surface.
Without it the knowledge stays inside the one who carries it
and dies when they do.

## Apparent
Lines on a surface.
The thing the lines point at is not on the surface.
Plato knows this.
He draws it anyway.
The diagram is never the thing.
It is the direction the thing is in.

## Yearbook
First drawn in Athens.
Redrawn in Syracuse.
The diagram did not change between the first trip and the second.
The Republic did not change after Syracuse.
When the argument fails to hold -
a new diagram appears.
The diagram is Plato's Tell.
It has been on the wall of every room he has entered
since Socrates was killed.
It will be on the wall after he is gone.
Aristotle will look at it and build the Poetics.
The Poetics is a different diagram pointing at the same thing.
