# References

## Plato (c.428-348 BCE)

Philosopher. Student of Socrates. Founder of the Academy in Athens.
The Allegory of the Cave appears in Book VII of the Republic - his account of knowledge, ignorance, and the cost of returning to the people who have not left.
Written circa 375 BCE.

**The works this world is built on:**

*The Republic* - Plato
Circa 375 BCE. Translation consulted: Penguin Classics edition, translated by Desmond Lee (2007).
The primary source for The Cave, The Ascent, The Diagram of the Divided Line, and the philosopher-king problem.

*The Meno* - Plato
Circa 385 BCE.
The primary source for the examination method: the moment when the interlocutor discovers they do not know what they believed they knew.

## Secondary sources

*The Open Society and Its Enemies, Vol. 1: The Spell of Plato* - Karl Popper
Published 1945, Routledge, London.
Critique of the political implications of Platonic philosophy.
Source for The Power Structure piece: the argument that the philosopher-king solution is a closed system that protects its own authority.

*Plato* - R.M. Hare
Published 1982, Oxford University Press.
Overview of the dialogues and their structural relationship.
