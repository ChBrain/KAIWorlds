# Piece: The First Measurement
## The number that makes everything after it legible.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** Johannes Krey

---

## Place
[The Station](place_the_station.md).
On the deck of the research vessel.
30 April 1957. Morning.

## Load Bearing
Without it the paradox has no proof.
The two lines on the graph —
nutrients declining, oxygen still falling —
require sixty-seven years of monthly measurements
to become visible.
The first measurement is the first point
on both lines.
Remove it and the record has no beginning.
The paradox cannot be named
without the record.
The record cannot begin without this number.
Every scientific paper, every policy decision,
every RECOVER project deliverable,
every cross-border monitoring protocol,
every conversation in every world
of the Baltic Sea architecture —
all of it runs on the record
that starts with this number.
The most consequential single act
in the entire timeline
is a man on a cold deck in April
writing down a temperature.

## Apparent
A number. Several numbers.
Temperature: the water at each depth.
Salinity: the salt content at each depth.
Oxygen: the dissolved oxygen at each depth.
Nutrients: nitrogen and phosphorus at each depth.
Written in Krey's hand in a field notebook
on the deck of the research vessel.
Later entered into the institutional record.
Later digitised.
Later published.
Later cited.
The handwriting is no longer visible.
The number is.

## Yearbook
30 April 1957.
The first entry.
The record begins.

Monthly entries: 1957 to 1975.
A gap: 1976 to 1978. The record resumes.
Another gap: 1983 to 1985. The record resumes.

Late 1980s: the nutrients begin to decline.
The nitrogen reduction policy is working.
The oxygen continues to fall.
The paradox is accumulating in the data.
Nobody has named it yet.

2014: Lennartz et al., *Biogeosciences*.
The first full statistical analysis
of the complete record — nine parameters,
fifty-six years of data.
The paradox is named.
Doing the right thing is no longer enough.
The First Measurement is cited.

2016: permanent observatory installed at the station.
Continuous data for the first time.

August 2019: the observatory disappears.
Shore cable torn. Station gone.
Not found.
Five years of continuous data
never collected.
The gap is in the record.
Permanent.

2024: von Jackowski et al., *Scientific Reports*.
Warming confirmed as the primary driver.
The First Measurement is cited again.

November 2024: new observatory deployed.
The continuous record resumes.

The First Measurement is now sixty-seven years old.
It is still the first point on the graph.
It is still the beginning of the proof.
Krey died in 1988.
He did not see the paradox named.
He saw the record accumulate.
That was what he came for.
