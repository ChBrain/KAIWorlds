# Place: The Ice
## Location: The Baltic basin. ~10,000 BCE.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** The Hunter

## Shown
- Tundra. Low, treeless, wind-scoured.
  The basin is visible as a depression — lower ground,
  meltwater pooling in the hollows.
  Not a lake yet. Not a sea.
  A place where water is beginning to remember
  it is water.
- The ice edge to the north. The horizon is bright
  where the ice sheet reflects the sky.
  The reindeer move toward it.
  The Hunter moves with them.
- The rivers. Meltwater channels running south and west.
  The Hunter knows every crossing.
  The crossings shift year by year.
  He notes this as: the ice is moving.
- The flint outcrops. Where the glacier retreated,
  the flint is exposed. The Hunter knows
  which outcrops produce the best flint.
  Some are already underwater.
- The smell: cold, clean, the absence of anything rotting.
  In this climate, nothing rots.
  Everything is held.
- The silence. Wind and birds.
  No trees to break the wind.
  No sea to make the constant sound the sea makes.
  The basin is quiet the way only an absence is quiet.

## Holds
- [The Flint](piece_the_flint.md).
  In the exposed glacial deposits.
  The technology that makes this world survivable.

## Offers
- The crossing. In winter, the basin floor is frozen solid.
  The Hunter walks across what will be the Kiel Bight
  without knowing it will become the Kiel Bight.
  The crossing is available for as long as the winter holds.
- The reindeer migration route.
  North in summer. South in winter.
  The Hunter's calendar is this route.
  Available as long as the reindeer run it.

## Withheld
- What the basin will become.
  The Hunter standing at the edge of the depression
  in 10,000 BCE cannot see the Kiel Bight.
  He cannot see the Ertebølle middens,
  the Viking Schlei, the Boknis Eck station,
  the dead zone forming in August.
  The basin holds all of this in its future.
  It does not announce it.
  It is simply low ground where water pools.
