# Persona: The Restorer
## Title: Ecosystem Manager — Western Baltic, ~2045

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** The Restorer

She made the decision knowing the recovery would not be a return, and she made it anyway.

## Projection
A woman at the monitoring station.
Not on the deck of FS LITTORINA —
at the screen, where the data arrives continuously
from the observatory that has been running
without interruption since 2024.
The second line on the graph
is moving in the right direction.
She is watching it move.
She does not look satisfied.
She looks like someone watching
a very slow thing that was nearly not possible.

## Action
Hold the decisions that were made
against the pressure to revise them —
the agricultural lobby, the fishing industry,
the governance cycles that want to reduce
the cost of compliance.
The decisions are made. They are not permanent.
They require defending every budget cycle.
The Restorer defends them.
This is the work now.
Not discovery. Maintenance of the conditions
that allow the recovery to continue.
The recovery is not self-sustaining yet.
It requires the decisions to keep being made.

## Shadow
The recovery is real.
The loss is also real.
The eelgrass is at three metres
where it was at four metres in 1000 CE.
The cod are rebuilding
toward the model's target,
not toward the pre-industrial density.
The harbour porpoise is more frequent,
not abundant.
The Restorer knows the difference
between what the recovery produced
and what the original was.
She was told this would happen —
the scientists said:
recovery is possible but not return.
She believed them.
She made the decision with full knowledge
of what it would and would not produce.
The Shadow is that knowing the difference
does not make the difference smaller.
She looks at the three-metre eelgrass
and knows it used to be four.
The metre is gone.
The three metres are here because of her.
Both are true every time she looks.

## Tell
She pulls up the trend line.
The second one — oxygen.
It is moving upward.
She watches it without speaking.
Then she says: it's working.
She does not say: it's enough.
