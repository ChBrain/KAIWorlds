# Piece: The Tile
## The infrastructure that runs without anyone deciding.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** The Farmer

---

## Place
[The Fjord](place_the_fjord.md).
In the ground beneath the field.
Four kilometres from the shore.

## Load Bearing
Without it the wet corner floods in winter
and the field is unproductive in the margins.
With it the field drains, the yield improves,
the family is fed, the farm is viable.
All of this is correct.
It is also true that without it
the nitrogen loading of the western Baltic
runs through the wetland filter —
slow, diffuse, partially absorbed.
With it the filter is bypassed.
The loading is direct and continuous.
Both things are what the tile does.
The tile does not choose between them.
It drains. That is what tiles do.
Remove it and the field floods.
Keep it and the fjord loads.
There is no version of this piece
that does only one thing.

## Apparent
Ceramic. Cylindrical. Fifteen centimetres long,
seven centimetres in diameter.
Laid end to end in a trench
sixty centimetres below the field surface.
Perforated along its length —
the water enters through the holes,
follows the gradient, exits at the ditch.
The Farmer who laid it
will never see it again.
It is buried.
It runs in the dark.

## Yearbook
Laid ~1790, Schleswig-Holstein.
The wet corner drained. The field extended.
The first yield from the new ground:
rye, improved over the previous year.
The Farmer notes the improvement.
He does not note the tile.
The tile is infrastructure, not event.

The tile drains every year.
Every year the nitrogen follows it
to the Møllebæk to the inlet to the Bight.

1909: Haber-Bosch. Synthetic nitrogen
becomes available. The application rates
on the same fields increase.
The same tile carries the increased load.
The tile does not know about Haber-Bosch.
It drains.

1957: Boknis Eck station begins recording.
The nitrogen in the water column
is the accumulation of the tile's work
since 1790 and before.
The record begins after the loading began.
The tile has been running for a hundred and sixty years
before anyone starts measuring what it carries.

2026: the Farmer's descendant
walks the same field boundary.
Checks the same tile outfall after rain.
Notes the drainage is working.
Goes back to the field.
The tile is still running.
It will run until someone takes it out.
No one has taken it out.
