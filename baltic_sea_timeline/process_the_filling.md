# Process: The Filling
## The basin receiving what the ice released.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** The Fisher

## Initiates
The ice retreats. The meltwater has nowhere to go
but into the basin. The land is still rising —
isostatic rebound, the crust recovering —
but the water rises faster than the land.
The basin fills. The freshwater world
establishes itself completely:
pike, perch, freshwater molluscs,
reed beds at the shore, birch and pine
advancing behind the retreating ice.
The Fisher arrives at a shore that is fully itself.
Nothing is missing. Nothing is transitional.
The lake is the world.

## Direction
Toward fullness. The freshwater system
builds its complete ecology.
Species fill every available position.
The shore becomes inhabited — not seasonally,
as the Hunter moved through the tundra,
but permanently. The midden accumulates.
The same shore. The same meals.
The same record, generation after generation.
The Filling produces completeness.
That is the problem the next process will solve
by ending it.

## Lever
The freshwater threshold.
The lake is sealed from the salt sea
by the land bridge at what will become
the Danish Straits. The land bridge
is being eroded by the rising sea level
on the other side.
The lever is the height of that threshold —
when it drops below sea level,
The Salt initiates.
The Filling cannot prevent this.
It continues filling until the moment
it is no longer filling with freshwater.

## Echo
A complete freshwater world.
Shore settlements. Shell middens.
A people who have never tasted salt.
A land bridge that is getting lower every year.
The Lake is whole.
The Salt is coming.
Neither the lake nor the Fisher
can see both of these things at once.
