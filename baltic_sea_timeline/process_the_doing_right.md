# Process: The Doing Right
## The correct response to a problem that has already changed.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** Johannes Krey

## Initiates
The monitoring establishes the baseline.
The record accumulates. The nitrogen loading
continues through the postwar agricultural intensification —
Haber-Bosch fertilisers at scale, the tile network
now carrying synthetic nitrogen as well as organic.
The oxygen in the deep basins begins to decline.
The eelgrass begins retreating from its deeper extent.
The evidence accumulates in the record
alongside the accumulation of the damage.
The scientists read the record.
They name the problem: eutrophication.
They recommend the solution: reduce the nitrogen.
The policy is made. The reduction begins.
Late 1980s.
This is the right thing.
It is the correct identification of the correct cause
and the correct application of the correct remedy.
The Doing Right initiates.

## Direction
Toward recovery. The nitrogen in the water column
begins to decline. The measurements confirm this.
The scientists and the policymakers are doing
what the record told them to do.
The direction is correct.
The destination does not arrive.
The oxygen continues to decline.
Not because the nitrogen reduction failed.
Because the system changed while the solution
was being prepared.
The warming deepened the thermocline.
The thermocline sealed the deep water longer.
The deep water ran out of oxygen faster.
The bacteria worked more efficiently in warmer water.
The internal phosphorus load fed the next bloom.
The right solution was applied
to a problem that had acquired a second driver
that the solution was not designed for.
The direction is correct.
The direction is no longer sufficient.

## Lever
The second driver. Warming.
It was always present in the data.
It was not named as the primary driver
until 2024 — sixty-seven years after
the monitoring began.
Remove the second driver and the nutrient reduction
restores the oxygen. That is the counterfactual.
Keep it and the correct action produces
an incorrect result, not because the action was wrong
but because the system continued to change
while the action was being taken.
The lever is the thing the solution
was not built to address.

## Echo
The Paradox. Two lines on a graph.
Nutrients declining. Oxygen still falling.
Both in the same record.
Both true.
The Station holds both — and the Station
is The Now before The Now knows it is The Now.
The Deciding initiates from the shore
the Scientist stands on when she pulls up the graph.
The Station is the instrument.
The Now is where the instrument is read.
