# Piece: The Anaerobic Loop
## The process that predates oxygen and is still running underneath everything.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** The Bacterium

---

## Place
[The Ancient Water](place_the_ancient_water.md).
Also: the dead zone at Boknis Eck. 25 metres depth. August.
Both simultaneously. The place has not changed.
Only the water above it has.

## Load Bearing
Without it the dead zone is an environmental problem.
With it the dead zone is the oldest process on Earth
reasserting itself in conditions that have briefly —
in geological terms — allowed something else
to run on top of it.
Remove it and the Paradox is about nitrogen and warming.
Keep it and the Paradox is about the thin margin
between the world that supports complex life
and the world that was here before complex life
and will be here after it.
The Anaerobic Loop is what makes the branch point
not just important but — at this scale — the most
recent in a series of moments across 3.8 billion years
where the margin either held or didn't.
Most of those moments nobody named.
This one has a name.
This one has a Scientist pointing at a graph.
This one has a RECOVER project.
This one has a Tourist at the waterline.
The Loop does not know about any of them.
It runs.

## Apparent
Not visible. Detectable by instruments
measuring hydrogen sulfide in the porewater,
oxygen depletion rates in the water column,
methane flux from the sediment.
The Scientist's instruments detect it every August.
The readings: unambiguous.
The process: running at maximum efficiency.
The efficiency is not new.
The conditions that allow it to run this long
into the summer — that is new.
Or rather: newly returned.

## Yearbook
3.8 billion years ago.
The first anaerobic metabolism.
The organic compounds consumed.
The hydrogen sulfide produced.
The process replicates with the organism.
The process spreads.

2.4 billion years ago: the Great Oxidation Event.
Photosynthetic organisms produce oxygen
as a metabolic waste product.
Oxygen accumulates in the atmosphere.
For the anaerobic organisms: catastrophe.
Most die. Some retreat to environments
without oxygen — deep sediments,
hydrothermal vents, anoxic basins.
The Anaerobic Loop does not stop.
It continues in the margins.

The margins have been expanding and contracting
for 2.4 billion years.
Every anoxic basin, every deep dead zone,
every stratified lake in summer —
the Loop runs in all of them.
It has never needed to do anything
except run when the conditions allow.

The conditions at Boknis Eck, August 2026:
oxygen below 2mg per litre at 25 metres.
The thermocline sealed since April.
The bloom sinking, decomposing.
The Loop running at maximum efficiency.
The dead zone: the margin, expanded.

The instruments record it.
The Scientist names it.
The Branch Point holds it.

The Loop does not know it is being named.
It runs.
It has always run.
It will run after the Scientist,
after the Baltic,
after everything in this timeline
has become sediment.

The last entry in this Yearbook
has not been written yet.
It will not be written by anyone
who was present at the first entry.

Except one.
He was present at the first entry.
He will be present at the last.
He is present at every entry between them.
He is the only continuous witness
in the entire timeline.
He does not interfere.
He observes.
He finds the question of whether
the Branch Point will hold
to be, on reflection,
the most interesting question
he has encountered
in a very long time.

He does not say this to anyone.
He is simply there.
As he has always been.
As he will always be.
The Loop runs.
He watches.
