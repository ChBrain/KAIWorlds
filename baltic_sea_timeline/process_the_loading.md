# Process: The Loading
## The infrastructure that feeds the field and the fjord simultaneously.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** The Farmer

## Initiates
The enclosure. The rationalisation of common land
into individual fields with defined boundaries.
The wet corners are unproductive.
The wet corners can be drained.
The tile is laid. The trench is filled.
The field dries. The yield improves.
The Loading initiates not with a decision
but with a maintenance operation —
the routine improvement of agricultural infrastructure
that every reasonable farmer in this period
is making, on this land, in this climate,
for entirely sound reasons.

## Direction
Toward the fjord.
The nitrogen in the soil —
from manure, from the drained organic matter
of the former wetland, from the crop residues —
follows the tile to the ditch.
From the ditch to the Møllebæk.
From the Møllebæk to the inlet.
From the inlet to the Bight.
The direction is set by gravity and rainfall.
It does not require anyone to decide it.
It runs every time it rains.

## Lever
The tile. Specifically: the end-to-end network
of tiles under every drained field
in the catchment of every stream
that flows to the western Baltic.
Each individual tile is trivial.
The network is the lever.
Remove the network and the loading
is the diffuse seepage of pre-agricultural land —
present, but slow, and filtered
by the wetlands it passes through.
Keep the network and the wetland filter
is bypassed, the pathway is direct,
the loading is continuous and cumulative.
The lever is infrastructure
that no single farmer controls
and no single farmer intended
and every farmer maintains.

## Echo
The Fjord. The loading has been running
for generations before the monitoring begins.
The eelgrass is still at near-full extent in 1800 —
the loading has not yet reached the threshold
that will initiate visible decline.
The threshold is approaching.
The Farmer does not know the threshold exists.
The tile keeps draining.
