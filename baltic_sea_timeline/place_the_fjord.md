# Place: The Fjord
## Location: The Flensburg Fjord and the Schlei. ~1800 CE.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** The Farmer

## Shown
- The inlet. The same water that was The Bight
  eight thousand years ago —
  now named, bordered, farmed at its edges.
  The Schlei: fifty kilometres of sheltered water,
  the Naturpark's boundary not yet drawn,
  the monitoring station not yet imagined.
- The agricultural landscape behind the shore.
  Fields enclosed. Boundaries marked.
  The wet corners in the process of being drained.
  The smell of turned earth and manure
  that has been here since the enclosures.
- The eelgrass. Still visible from a boat
  in clear water — the beds at near-full extent,
  four metres deep in the shallows,
  covering the basin floor the way
  the Witness saw them beginning to form
  six thousand years ago.
  The Farmer does not look at the eelgrass.
  He looks at the field.
- The Møllebæk. Running at the field boundary —
  the small stream that connects the tile drain
  to the inlet. After rain it runs brown,
  carrying the topsoil from the drained field.
  The Farmer notes the brown water
  as: the tile is draining well.
- The fish. Still abundant.
  Cod in the channels, herring in autumn,
  flounder in the shallows.
  The Farmer's wife fishes here twice a week.
  The fish have always been here.
  They will always be here.

## Holds
- [The Tile](piece_the_tile.md).
  In the ground beneath the field.
  Running continuously.

## Offers
- The fish. Still at pre-industrial abundance.
  Available at the shore without equipment —
  the flounder in the shallows, the eel in the reeds.
- The reed beds. For thatching, for weaving.
  The Farmer's barn roof is Schlei reed.
  Has been for three generations.
- The crossing. By boat in summer, on foot in winter
  when the shallow bays freeze.
  The Hunter's winter crossing, eight thousand years
  later, still available in cold years.

## Withheld
- The loading. The nitrogen from this field
  is in this water right now, after last week's rain.
  The Møllebæk carried it.
  It is invisible — dissolved, diluted,
  undetectable without instruments
  that do not yet exist.
  The Farmer standing at the shore
  sees clear water and abundant fish.
  Both things are true.
  The loading is also true.
  All three are present simultaneously.
  Only one is visible.
- The threshold. The eelgrass will begin
  retreating in living memory of people
  not yet born. The threshold between
  the loading the ecosystem can absorb
  and the loading that initiates visible decline
  is close. Not yet crossed.
  The Fjord holds it in the sediment —
  the chemistry is changing, layer by layer,
  year by year, invisibly.
  The monitoring that would see this
  does not begin for a hundred and fifty years.
