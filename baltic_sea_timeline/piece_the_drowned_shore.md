# Piece: The Drowned Shore
## The world that is still there, unreachable.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** The Witness

---

## Place
[The Bight](place_the_bight.md).
Underwater. Two to eight metres below the surface,
depending on the location.
The submerged Mesolithic settlements
of the western Baltic coast.

## Load Bearing
Without it the lost world is simply lost —
gone, irretrievable, available only as absence.
With it the lost world is present —
physically present, under the water,
holding the evidence of what was here.
The Drowned Shore is not metaphor.
It is the actual lakeshore, the actual midden,
the actual rise above the waterline
where the Fisher ate for generations —
now two metres below the surface of the Bight.
Remove it and the Pre-Salt world
has no physical address.
Keep it and the address exists —
it simply requires diving to reach.
The knowledge that the old world is still there
is what makes the Witness's testimony
something other than delusion.
She is not misremembering a world that vanished.
She is remembering a world that is underwater.

## Apparent
Visible from the surface on clear days —
the Witness can see the shape of the old rise
through the water, when the light is right
and the water is not turbid.
The midden: a darker mass at two metres depth.
The reed bed stumps: still in the sediment.
The flint scatter from the Hunter's fire:
still on the basin floor,
exactly where it was left.
It has been eight thousand years.
The flint does not know this.

## Yearbook
The lakeshore. ~7,000 BCE.
The eating place of the settlement.
The accumulation of generations.

The Salt arrives. ~6,000 BCE.
The sea level rises.
The shore is inundated.
The salt water covers the midden.
The anaerobic conditions preserve it.
The world goes underwater intact.

Submerged site archaeology: beginning in the
twentieth century CE. Divers in the western Baltic
find the Mesolithic settlements — the stone tools,
the midden layers, the fish weirs preserved in the sediment.
The Drowned Shore is found.
It was there the entire time.
It was waiting for someone to learn
to look in the right direction.

The Boknis Eck scientists lower their water samplers
above the Drowned Shore every month.
They are measuring the water column
above a world they do not know is below them.
Both records are running simultaneously.
Neither knows about the other.
