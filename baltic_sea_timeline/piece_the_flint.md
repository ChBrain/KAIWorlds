# Piece: The Flint
## The technology that makes the Holding survivable.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** The Hunter

---

## Place
[The Ice](place_the_ice.md).
The exposed glacial deposits at the basin edge.

## Load Bearing
Without it the Hunter has no fire, no cut meat,
no scraped hide, no hafted point.
The flint is not a weapon. It is the material
that makes warmth, food and shelter possible
in a world where nothing grows and everything freezes.
Remove it and the Hunter cannot survive the Holding.
Keep it and the Hunter survives the Holding
and cannot survive the world the Holding becomes —
not because the flint fails,
but because the flint does not teach him
what the new world requires.
The technology outlasts the world it was built for.
The Hunter does not.

## Apparent
Grey-brown. Sharp where knapped.
The edges are the point — literally and structurally.
The Hunter carries several.
The best ones are from the outcrop
that will be underwater in eight hundred years.
He does not know this.
He knows: this outcrop produces the best edge.
He returns to it every year.

## Yearbook
Knapped on the shore of the basin,
~10,000 BCE.
The outcrop: exposed by the retreating glacier.
Available for as long as the basin stays dry.

The same flint technology will be used
by the Ertebølle Fisher at the lake shore
three thousand years later.
The Fisher will not know where the outcrops were.
The outcrops will be underwater.
The technology survived. The source did not.
