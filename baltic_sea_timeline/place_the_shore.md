# Place: The Shore
## Location: The Schlei and the western Kiel Bight. ~1000 CE. Haithabu.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** The Merchant

## Shown
- The Schlei. Fifty kilometres of sheltered water
  from the Kiel Bight to the isthmus at Haithabu.
  The ships move in both directions.
  Westward: Frankish goods, wine, cloth.
  Eastward: furs, amber, slaves, herring.
  The herring is the cargo that never stops moving.
- Haithabu at the western end of the Schlei.
  A semicircular embankment. Inside: workshops,
  longhouses, the quay. The smell of fish, salt,
  woodsmoke, hides, human density.
  One of the largest settlements in northern Europe.
  The whole structure is built around the fact
  that the Schlei connects two seas.
- The eelgrass. Visible from the quay
  in clear water — the beds extending to four metres,
  covering the basin floor in the shallows.
  The Merchant walks past it every day
  on the way to the boat.
  It is simply what the bottom looks like.
  He has no reason to look at it.
- The cod in the channels. In winter,
  the boats go out before dawn.
  The cod are where they have always been.
  The Merchant knows the coordinates
  the way the Hunter knew the flint outcrops.
  He does not think about whether they will be
  there next year. They have always been there.
- The herring run. Autumn.
  The surface of the Bight darkens with the shoals.
  Every boat goes out. Every barrel is filled.
  The run lasts three weeks.
  It has lasted three weeks every autumn
  in living memory.

## Holds
- [The Herring Barrel](piece_the_herring_barrel.md).
  On the quay. Full. Waiting for the ship.

## Offers
- The trade route. The Schlei from Bight to isthmus.
  Available to any ship that can navigate it.
  The route predates Haithabu and will outlast it.
- The herring run. Three weeks every autumn.
  The abundance is real. Available at scale.
  The first time the sea has been harvested
  at a scale beyond the local.
- The quay. The point of transfer
  between the sea and the market.
  The place where the herring becomes a commodity.

## Withheld
- The stock level. The Merchant does not have
  this concept. The herring come every autumn.
  That is the whole of what is known.
  How many herring there are,
  whether the number is changing,
  what would happen if the run did not come —
  these questions do not exist.
  The sea does not offer a ledger.
  It offers a run.
  The run has never failed.
- The eelgrass at full extent.
  It is here. The Merchant does not see it
  because he has no reason to look.
  It will not be at full extent
  when the monitoring begins
  a thousand years from now.
  The difference between these two states
  is one of the central facts
  the Stewardship world is trying to name.
  The Merchant is standing above the evidence.
  He is looking at the barrel.
