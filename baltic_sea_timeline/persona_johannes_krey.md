# Persona: Johannes Krey
## Title: Professor of Planktology — Institut für Meereskunde, Kiel. 1957.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** Johannes Krey

He started the record, and he did not live to see what it would say.

## Projection
A man on the deck of a research vessel.
April, cold. He is not cold —
he is attending to the instruments
with the focused calm of someone
who has prepared for this morning
and is now executing the preparation.
The cable is running correctly.
The bottles will come up full.
The measurements will be precise.
He is doing what he came to do.
He is very good at it.

## Action
Lower the sampler. Record the depth.
Bring up the bottles. Measure on deck.
Enter the numbers. Note the date.
Return next month. Do it again.
Build the record that the sea cannot build for itself —
the long-term account of what is happening
in a water column that has no memory
beyond what the sediment holds,
and the sediment is not talking yet.
This is the work. The work is continuous.
The work is the point.

## Shadow
He is standing above the pre-loading baseline
without knowing it is there.
The sediment below the station holds
a hundred and fifty years of loading —
the Tile draining since 1790,
the Haber-Bosch nitrogen since 1909.
It also holds the centuries before the loading,
the record of the Fjord before the Farmer,
before the Merchant, before the border,
before Haithabu.
Krey is measuring from now forward.
The record he is building is the most precise
account of the western Baltic
that has ever existed.
It begins after the damage began.
He does not know the damage began before him.
He is establishing the baseline.
The baseline is already compromised.
He cannot know this.
The instruments were not here before.
This morning they are here.
This morning is where the record begins.

## Tell
He watches the first bottles come up.
He checks the seal on each one —
not because he doubts the equipment,
but because precision is the habit
and the habit does not require a reason.
He checks. The seals are good.
He nods once.
He writes down the time.
