# Place: The Bight
## Location: The western Baltic. ~6,000 BCE. The Littorina Sea.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** The Witness

## Shown
- Saltwater. The smell is new —
  the particular mineral sharpness
  that did not exist here before.
  The Witness smells it before they see
  any other change. The smell arrives first.
- The reed beds: dying. The freshwater reeds
  cannot survive the salinity.
  They brown at the edges.
  They retreat to the river mouths
  where fresh water still dilutes the salt.
- The fish: wrong. Pike and perch
  are gone from the open water.
  Something else is moving in the shallows —
  the Witness does not have a name for it.
  It is herring. The herring have never been
  in this water before. They do not know this.
  They are simply following the salt.
- The old shore: underwater.
  The rise above the waterline where the midden was —
  the eating place of the settlement —
  is now two metres below the surface.
  The Witness can see it on clear days,
  looking down through the water.
  The midden is still there.
  It is unreachable.
- The eelgrass: beginning.
  In the shallows where the salt and the light
  and the sediment meet at the right depth —
  the first eelgrass beds are establishing.
  The Witness does not know what they are yet.
  They are simply: plants in the water
  that were not there before.
  They will take three hundred years to reach
  full extent. The Witness will not live
  to see them complete.

## Holds
- [The Drowned Shore](piece_the_drowned_shore.md).
  Underwater. Still present.
  Unreachable from the surface.

## Offers
- The new water. Salt, cold, clear.
  The herring in the shallows.
  The saltwater molluscs beginning to arrive.
  A different world, complete in itself,
  available to whoever can learn
  to read the new chemistry.
- The depth. The basin is deeper than the lake was
  because the sea level is higher.
  More water. More pressure.
  More of everything the sea will become.

## Withheld
- What the shore felt like before the salt.
  The Witness knows. Nobody else does.
  The knowledge is untransmittable —
  the next generation is born into this water.
  They have no referent for freshwater.
  When the Witness says: it was different,
  the child looks at the water and sees
  what water is. This is water.
  It was always water.
  What the Witness holds will die with them.
- The eelgrass at full extent.
  It is beginning now, in the shallows.
  In three hundred years it will cover
  the basin floor to four metres depth.
  The Witness is standing above it
  without knowing it is there.
