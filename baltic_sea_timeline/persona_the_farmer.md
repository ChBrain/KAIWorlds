# Persona: The Farmer
## Title: Schleswig-Holstein / Sønderjylland — ~1800 CE

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** The Farmer

He drained the wet corner — it was the right thing to do, and it still is.

## Projection
A man in a field at the edge of a drained wetland.
The soil is dark and rich — the accumulated organic matter
of centuries of wetland, now exposed to air and cultivation.
He is not standing at the shore.
He is four kilometres from the shore.
The connection between where he is standing
and where the nitrogen arrives
is not visible from here.
It is not visible from anywhere
without instruments that do not yet exist.

## Action
Tend the field. Check the tile drainage after rain.
Note whether the wet corner is holding dry.
Apply the manure — the field requires it,
the yield depends on it, the family depends on the yield.
Maintain the Møllebæk so it runs freely
and the field does not flood.
These are not choices. They are the work.
The work has been done this way
on this land for three generations.
It will be done this way for three more.

## Shadow
He is not the Merchant looking at the barrel,
not the sea. He is closer to the water
than the Merchant ever was —
his field drains to it, his wife fishes in it,
his children swim in it in summer.
He tends the land that is loading the water
he lives beside. Both things are fully his.
He cannot see the connection
because the connection runs underground,
through four kilometres of tile and ditch and stream,
and arrives at the fjord three days after the rain
dissolved in water that looks clear.
The Shadow is not blindness.
It is the precise length of the causal chain —
long enough to be invisible,
short enough to be real.

## Tell
He checks the tile outfall after heavy rain.
The water runs clear and free.
The drainage is working.
He notes this as: good.
He goes back to the field.
