# Process: The Holding
## The world locked before it could become itself.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** The Hunter

## Initiates
The Weichselian glaciation. The ice sheet covers Scandinavia,
the Baltic basin, most of northern Europe.
The land is depressed under the weight.
The basin that will become the sea
is a tundra depression with meltwater pooling at its edges.
The world is calibrated to this.
Every organism, every migration route, every skill
the Hunter carries — built for the Holding.
The Holding has lasted longer than any human memory.
It will end without announcing it.

## Direction
Toward release. The ice retreats north.
The land begins to rise — isostatic rebound,
the crust recovering from the weight of the ice.
Meltwater accumulates in the basin.
The reindeer follow the retreating cold northward.
The Hunter follows the reindeer.
The direction is north. Always north.
The Holding releases its pressure slowly enough
that no single generation feels the world changing.
They feel it as: the good hunting is further north this year.

## Lever
The weight of the ice on the land.
Remove it and the land rises,
the basin fills, the world the Hunter knows
becomes a lake, then a sea, then a different world entirely.
The lever is the ice itself —
the thing that makes the Holding possible
is the thing whose removal ends it.

## Echo
The basin begins to fill with meltwater.
The Lake initiates.
The Hunter is still moving north.
He does not know the basin behind him
is becoming something that will swallow
the shore his children's children will stand on.
