# Process: The Taking
## The sea named as resource. The first harvest with a ledger.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** The Merchant

## Initiates
The herring run. The cod hold the channels.
The eelgrass beds are at full extent.
The sea is at the peak of what it will ever be
in human terms — the full ecology
of the Littorina Sea, four thousand years
of saltwater species filling every position.
A trader arrives at the Schlei
and sees not a world but a supply chain.
The Taking does not initiate with malice.
It initiates with a barrel and a price.

## Direction
Toward the ledger. The herring is caught,
salted, barrelled, priced, traded.
The barrel makes the local abundance
into an international commodity.
Distance is introduced between the catching
and the eating. The person who eats
the salted herring in Cologne
has no relationship with the Bight
that produced it. The Bight has become
a place that produces something
for somewhere else.
This is the direction.
Not extraction yet — the volumes are sustainable.
But the orientation is set.
The sea is now facing in the direction of use.

## Lever
The barrel. The salt. The trade route.
Remove them and the herring is eaten locally —
the sea provides for the people at its shore,
as it has always provided.
Keep them and the herring is a commodity —
its value now determined by markets
that have no knowledge of the Bight's condition.
The lever is the distance between the catch
and the price. That distance will only grow.

## Echo
The Shore. The Bight inhabited and working.
The Schlei a trade route. Haithabu at its western end.
The first systematic harvest of the western Baltic.
The sea at full extent providing for the first time
at a scale beyond the local.
The eelgrass still at full depth.
The cod still filling the channels.
The oxygen in August still above the threshold.
The abundance is real.
The orientation toward extraction is set.
The Loading is four hundred years away.
It will not feel like a continuation of this.
It will feel like something new.
It is not something new.
