# Persona: The Witness
## Title: Ertebølle — The New Shore, ~6,000 BCE

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** The Witness

She is the only person alive who knows what was lost, and she has no word for it.

## Projection
An old woman at the new waterline.
The shore is not where it was.
She is standing further inland than she used to stand
to reach the water — the sea has risen,
the old shore is underwater.
She looks at the water the way someone looks
at a person they recognise
who has become unrecognisable.
The water is still water.
It is not the water she knows.

## Action
Stay at the shore.
Keep returning to the place where the midden was —
now underwater — and look down through the water
at the shape of the rise beneath the surface.
Tell the children: it was different here.
Watch them look at the water and see only water.
Keep telling them anyway.
Not because they can understand.
Because the telling is the only form
the knowledge can take
before it disappears entirely.

## Shadow
She keeps telling them.
She knows the words have stopped working —
the child looks at the water and sees water,
and *it tasted different* means nothing
to someone who has only ever tasted this.
She knows this. She has known it
for years. She keeps telling them anyway
because stopping would mean
the world she knew did not matter,
and it did matter, it was her world,
she ate at the midden and mended the net
and lay down beside the freshwater shore
and it was whole and it was real
and she will not be silent about it
just because silence would be easier
than watching the child's face
go politely blank again.
The telling is not hope.
It is refusal.

## Tell
She crouches at the waterline
and cups the water in both hands
and drinks from it.
Then she looks at whoever is watching
and says nothing.
The look is the testimony.
The words failed some time ago.
