# Persona: The Fisher
## Title: Ertebølle — The Lakeshore, ~7,000 BCE

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** The Fisher

She has never tasted salt.
A woman at the waterline. Knee-deep.
The net in her hands is the work of weeks —
every knot a decision, the mesh size
calibrated to the fish she is catching.
She reads the water the way the Hunter
read the tundra: completely, precisely,
in the language of this specific place.
She is not preparing for a different world.
There is no different world.

## Action
Read the lake. Set the net where the fish are.
Know which shore is sheltered from which wind.
Know where the pike holds in summer
and where it moves in winter.
Return to the midden shore.
Eat. Leave the shells.
Come back tomorrow.
The repetition is not habit. It is knowledge —
the accumulated proof that this shore provides.

## Shadow
The completeness is real.
The lake gives what it holds without withholding.
This is not a temporary abundance —
the Fisher has no framework for temporary abundance
because every abundance she has known
has been renewed the following season.
The world has always refilled.
The completeness is the condition
that makes the ending invisible.
She cannot prepare for the Salt
because preparation requires knowing
that what you have is not permanent.
She knows no impermanent world.
Everything she knows has always been here.

## Tell
She tastes the water before setting the net.
She has done this every day since she could walk.
The water is always the same.
One morning it will not be.
She will taste it.
She will have no word for what she tastes.
