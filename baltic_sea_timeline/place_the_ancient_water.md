# Place: The Ancient Water
## Location: The early ocean. ~3.8 billion years ago.
## Also: the dead zone at Boknis Eck, 25 metres depth, August.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** The Bacterium

## Shown
- Water. No shoreline visible — the continents
  are not yet in their current configuration.
  The ocean covers most of the surface.
  The colour: not blue. Grey-green, turbid,
  rich with dissolved minerals and organic compounds.
  No oxygen in the water column.
  No oxygen in the atmosphere above it.
  Nothing requiring oxygen has yet evolved.
- The hydrothermal vents on the seafloor.
  Hot, mineral-rich water rising through the crust.
  The chemistry that makes the first metabolism possible
  is concentrated here.
  The vents do not know they are the origin of everything.
  They are simply hot.
- The organic compounds. In the water column,
  on the seafloor, accumulating.
  Not alive. The precursors of life.
  The substrate the first metabolism will run on.
  Available. Waiting for nothing.
  Chemistry does not wait.
- The silence. Not the silence of absence —
  the silence of a world that has no ears
  and no sound-producing organisms.
  The ocean moves. The vents hiss.
  No organism hears it.
- And simultaneously, accessible from this place
  by going forward 3.8 billion years
  and 25 metres down:
  The dead zone at Boknis Eck. August.
  The same absence of oxygen.
  The same hydrogen sulfide in the porewater.
  The same anaerobic metabolism running
  at maximum efficiency.
  The same silence in the water column
  below the threshold everything else needs to survive.
  The Ancient Water is not only then.
  It is also now.
  Below the thermocline.
  Every August.
  Getting longer every decade.

## Holds
- [The Anaerobic Loop](piece_the_anaerobic_loop.md).
  Running. Has been running for 3.8 billion years.
  Present here and at Boknis Eck simultaneously.

## Offers
- The scale. The place that makes the Baltic
  legible as what it actually is:
  a thin film of complex life
  on the surface of a very old process
  that does not know the complex life is there.
- The baseline beneath the baseline.
  The Boknis Eck station measures from 1957.
  The sediment below it holds the pre-loading baseline.
  The Ancient Water holds the pre-oxygen baseline —
  the state the water returns to
  when the conditions that support complex life
  are removed long enough.
  Available to whoever asks
  what the dead zone actually is.

## Withheld
- When the thin margin becomes permanent.
  Not philosophically — practically.
  At what rate of warming,
  at what duration of stratification,
  does the dead zone stop being seasonal
  and become the permanent condition?
  That question has a scientific threshold.
  The instruments at Boknis Eck
  are measuring toward it every August.
  The Scientist knows the question exists.
  She does not know if she will see
  the threshold crossed before it is too late
  to name it as a crossing rather than
  a new baseline.
  The Ancient Water holds this threshold.
  It does not announce it.
  It simply runs at whatever rate
  the conditions allow,
  and the conditions are allowing more
  every decade,
  and the instruments record this,
  and the record is in the archive,
  and the archive is in the zip,
  and the zip is in the world,
  and the world is not yet decided.
