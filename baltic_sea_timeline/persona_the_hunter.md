# Persona: The Hunter
## Title: Mesolithic — The Baltic Basin, ~10,000 BCE

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** The Hunter

The world he was built for is ending without telling him.

## Projection
A man in the tundra. Dressed in scraped hide.
Moving. Always moving — the stillness of the Hunter
is the stillness before the throw, not the stillness of rest.
He reads the ground the way another person reads a face.
Every depression, every drift pattern, every absence of birds
is information. He is the most precise reader
of this landscape that will ever exist.
The landscape is about to change
into something his precision cannot read.

## Action
Follow the reindeer. Read the ground.
Find the flint outcrops where the glacier exposed them.
Cross the basin in winter when it is frozen.
Return south in autumn.
Note that the good crossings are further north this year.
Note it as practical information.
Do not name it as a trend.

## Shadow
Every skill he has was built for the Holding.
The Holding is ending. He cannot see this
because the ending looks identical to the world continuing —
the ice is simply further north than last year,
as it was last year, as it will be next year.
His children will stand on a shore
he will walk across in winter.
His grandchildren will not know
there was ever a winter crossing.
The precision that makes him extraordinary
in this world is the precision that will fail
in the next one — because it was built
to read a world that is about to stop existing.

## Tell
He notes the crossing is further north this year.
He adjusts his route.
He does not tell anyone.
It is not yet a thing that requires telling.
