# Piece: The Returned Eelgrass
## The recovery that is not a return.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** The Restorer

---

## Place
[The Future — Branch A](place_the_future_branch_a.md).
The shallows. Three metres depth.

## Load Bearing
Without it Branch A has better numbers
but no visible evidence.
The Returned Eelgrass is the piece
that makes the recovery felt rather than measured —
the thing a person can see from a boat
in clear water and know that something changed.
Remove it and Branch A is a graph.
Keep it and Branch A is a shore
where something came back.
Not everything. Something.
The difference between those two statements
is the whole weight of the branch point.

## Apparent
Green ribbon leaves, thirty to eighty centimetres long,
in beds at three metres depth —
one metre shallower than the Viking-era extent,
two metres deeper than the 2026 extent.
Visible from a boat on a clear day.
The Restorer looks at it
the way the Witness looked at the drowned midden —
holding two states simultaneously,
knowing the depth it used to reach,
seeing the depth it has reached.
Both present at once.

## Yearbook
The first eelgrass bed in this timeline:
~5,700 BCE. The Salt has entered.
The conditions are right.
The first beds establish in the shallows.
Three hundred years to full extent.
Four metres depth.
Established for seven thousand years.

The decline: 1970s.
Turbidity increasing.
The light reaching the bottom diminishing.
The depth limit retreating upward.
Four metres to three metres to two metres
to one metre. By 2026: one metre.
Seventy-five percent of the 1900 extent.

The decision: 2028. Ecosystem-based management.
The nutrient reduction deepened.
The cod recovering. The zooplankton recovering.
The bloom season shorter. The turbidity lower.
The light reaching the bottom again.

The return: slow. Ten years of monitoring
before the trend is unambiguous.
The beds at two metres by 2035.
At three metres by 2043.
The Restorer watching the depth gauge
the way Krey watched the oxygen reading —
looking for the number that confirms
the work was worth it.

It reaches three metres.
The instruments confirm it.
The Restorer pulls up the graph.
The trend line is moving upward.
She watches it without speaking.
Then she says: it's working.
She does not say: it's enough.
She knows it is not enough.
She knows it is what there is.
Both things are in the Returned Eelgrass.
The piece holds them without resolving them.
