# Process: The Salt
## The threshold that made everything before it unreachable.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Timeline
**Persona:** The Witness

## Initiates
The land bridge at the Danish Straits
erodes below sea level.
The North Sea has been rising since the glaciation ended.
It has been pressing against the threshold for centuries.
The threshold drops one centimetre.
Then another.
Then the salt water finds the gap
and the gap widens and the process
is no longer stoppable by any force
available to the people standing at the shore.
It initiates without announcement.
The Fisher tastes the difference.
She has no word for it.

## Direction
Inward. The salt water moves into the basin
that has been freshwater for two thousand years.
It does not move as a wave.
It moves as chemistry — the salinity rising
slowly enough that no single morning
feels like catastrophe,
quickly enough that within a human lifetime
the lake is a sea.
The freshwater species cannot adapt.
They retreat to rivers and lakes.
The saltwater species follow the salt in.
The world reorganises around a new chemistry
it has never held before.

## Lever
The salinity threshold.
Every organism in the Ancylus Lake
has a salinity tolerance.
Below it: survival.
Above it: death or retreat.
The lever is not dramatic.
It is chemical. The water reaches
the organism's threshold and the organism
responds as it was built to respond.
The catastrophe is the accumulation
of millions of individual thresholds
being crossed in the same direction
across the same decades.

## Echo
The Littorina Sea — the early Baltic,
brackish, saltwater species establishing.
The eelgrass beds forming for the first time.
The cod arriving.
The freshwater shore drowned
under the rising sea level.
The midden preserved under the salt water
that ended the world that built it.
The Bight begins.
