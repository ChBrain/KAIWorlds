# Piece: Empty Chair

## Owner
- **Project:** Grimm
- **Place:** [The Workroom](place_workroom.md)

## Place

The [Workroom](place_workroom.md), Berlin. The Empty Chair sits at the second desk, where [Wilhelm Grimm](persona_wilhelm_grimm.md) wrote from 1840 until his death in December 1859. After December 1859 it is empty. It remains at the desk.

## Load Bearing

Remove the Empty Chair and the [Enduring](process_enduring.md) process loses its sharpest instance. The [Hearth](position_hearth.md) maintains the configuration of the [Household](place_household.md); the Empty Chair maintains the configuration of the [Workroom](place_workroom.md). Both are forms of the same operation. Remove the chair and Jacob's four years of work after Wilhelm's death become a different kind of event: a widower returning to labor. With the chair they become the continuation of a configuration that has lost a body without losing its shape.

Further: remove the Empty Chair and the [Workroom](place_workroom.md) revises itself. With one writer and one desk the room becomes an ordinary study. The room's identity depends on the two-desk configuration. The configuration depends on the presence of two chairs, even when one is empty.

## Apparent

A wooden chair pushed in against the second desk. Straight back. Worn seat. The wear pattern on the seat is asymmetrical: heavier on the right, lighter on the left, because Wilhelm sat slightly turned toward his brother's desk. The cushion is still the cushion Wilhelm used. It has not been replaced.

On the second desk: the [Manuscript](piece_manuscript.md) open to the page Wilhelm was working on when he stopped. The inkwell at the top right, still half full. A pen on the blotter. The papers in their stacks.

## Yearbook

The chair enters the Workroom in 1840 when the brothers move to Berlin and set up the two-desk configuration for the third time (Kassel 1806-29, Göttingen 1829-37, Kassel 1837-40, Berlin 1840-). Wilhelm sits in it from 1840 until December 1859.

After Wilhelm's death the chair is not moved. Jacob does not rearrange the Workroom. He does not turn his own desk to face the window. He sits at his desk as he always sat, and the second desk remains in its place. For four years Jacob works in a room configured for two writers in which only one writes. No letter from this period mentions the chair. No visitor comments on it in their memoirs.

September 1863: Jacob dies at his desk. [Dorothea Wild-Grimm](persona_dorothea_wild.md) enters the Workroom that evening. She sees both chairs empty. She closes the door. She does not remove either chair during the four years she survives Jacob. She does not enter the Workroom again except to clean.

After her death in 1867 the sons inherit the house. The Workroom is eventually dismantled. The chairs and desks are distributed to various archives and museums. Wilhelm's chair is in the Grimm Museum in Kassel now, behind glass, on a plaque that names it as Wilhelm's chair. The plaque does not name the four years.
