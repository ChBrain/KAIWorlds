# Persona: Jacob Grimm
## Title: The Systematizer

## Owner
- **Project:** Grimm

**Persona:** Jacob Grimm

Born Hanau 1785. Died Berlin 1863, at the letter F. He did not marry. He lived in his brother's household for thirty-four years. He wrote on one side of a desk that was flush against another desk. The other desk was Wilhelm's. When Wilhelm died, Jacob did not move the desks and did not remove the chair.

## Projection

Present tense. He is at the desk. The light comes from the left because his desk is set for a right-handed writer. The inkwell is at the top right. Papers stack in three heaps: the current letter of the dictionary, letters received awaiting answer, and a third heap he does not name even to himself, which contains the unsorted. The [Card File](piece_card_file.md) is in vertical columns against the wall to his right.

He does not look up when someone enters. He finishes the word he is writing. Then he looks up. Then he returns to the word. He has done this for forty years.

## Action

He writes an entry for the Deutsches Wörterbuch. He takes a word (today: *Fleisch*) and assembles every attested use across six centuries of German literature, drawing citations from the [Card File](piece_card_file.md). He is assembling, not deciding. He believes he is assembling, not deciding. Each citation gets a place. Each sense gets a sub-entry. The entry grows until it is correct. This is the work of [Ordering](process_ordering.md).

The test: if he adds a sentence that he composed himself, he has slipped out of the slot. If he adds a sentence that was written by someone else and he located it and placed it, he is in the slot. He records. He does not compose.

## Shadow

He does not see that the order he serves is an order he made. The twenty-four letters he inherited from Latin are not the order of his language. The alphabetical arrangement is a decision that looks like a discovery. When he places *Fleisch* next to *Fleiß* he has made a claim that the two are more related to each other than *Fleisch* is to *Leib* or *Brot*. He would deny this with his life. He is denying it with his life.

He will die at F. He does not know this, but he knows he is not going to finish. He has known this since 1846. He has continued anyway because the project is not a book to be completed but an act to be sustained. What he does not see is that the sustaining has become the meaning, and the completing has become impossible not because time is short but because the project he described is not the project he is doing. He is writing German. He believed he was recording it.

## Tell

He sharpens his pen before every sitting. He sharpens it again mid-sitting when the letters begin to lose their edges. He does not write with a worn nib. The sharpening is not a ritual. It is a refusal of approximation at the smallest scale, repeated hundreds of times a day. You can hear him from the next room.
