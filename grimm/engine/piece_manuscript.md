# Piece: Manuscript

## Owner
- **Project:** Grimm
- **Persona:** [Wilhelm Grimm](persona_wilhelm_grimm.md)

## Place

Where [Wilhelm Grimm](persona_wilhelm_grimm.md) is. The Manuscript travels with him from Kassel to Göttingen to Kassel again to Berlin, always on the desk he is using, always within reach of his pen.

## Load Bearing

Remove the Manuscript and the [Smoothing](process_smoothing.md) process has no object. The [Smoother](position_smoother.md) is defined by the document they revise. Without the document the position dissolves.

Further: remove the Manuscript and Wilhelm's claim to be a scribe cannot be tested. The Manuscript is the body of evidence that proves its own framing false. Remove it and the claim stands unchallenged, which is a different world.

Remove the Manuscript and the [Second Preface](piece_second_preface.md) has nothing to introduce. The Preface is a frame around the Manuscript. The frame without the object is not a frame.

## Apparent

A thick folder on Wilhelm's desk. Inside it, loose quires and bound copies of the Kinder- und Hausmärchen in every edition so far. First edition 1812, second 1819, third 1837, fourth 1840, fifth 1843, sixth 1850, seventh 1857. Each is a different book. Each presents itself as the same book.

The folder also contains working copies annotated in three colors of ink from three decades. The margins are denser than the type. Alternative wordings. Phrases struck through and rewritten. Small arrows indicating words that should move. Marginal notes: *clearer, softer, too dark for children, check second version*.

## Yearbook

Begun in 1807 when Jacob and Wilhelm started collecting tales from Kassel-area informants. First published in 1812 by the Realschulbuchhandlung, Berlin. Second edition 1819 adds the Second Preface, which constructs Dorothea Viehmann as a peasant woman.

Between 1812 and 1857 the Manuscript receives seven editions' worth of revisions. The first edition Little Red Cap has the girl die in the wolf's stomach with her grandmother. The second edition adds the hunter who cuts them out. The third edition softens the hunter's violence. The fourth adds a second wolf to be drowned in a bucket of sausage water. The fifth normalizes the German. The sixth adjusts a diminutive. The seventh is the one that enters the cultural memory.

The Manuscript survives Wilhelm's death in 1859. Jacob does not revise it. Jacob does not publish a further edition. The seventh stands as the last. After Wilhelm's death the Manuscript is archived. In the twentieth century Heinz Rölleke publishes the Ölenberg fragments and the 1812 first edition as corrective texts. Readers of the corrective texts are shocked by the darkness of the early tales. The shock measures the success of the [Smoothing](process_smoothing.md) that the Manuscript hosted.

The Manuscript is in the Kassel archive now. It receives no further entries. It continues to be read, which is a different kind of entry.
