# Process: Ordering

## Owner
- **Project:** Grimm

## Initiated by

A [Systematizer](position_systematizer.md) has accumulated sufficient material to require arrangement. The [Card File](piece_card_file.md) is beginning to fill. A set of inherited conventions (an alphabet, a grammatical scheme) is available and feels natural. The first decision to place one item next to another has been made without being recognized as a decision.

## Direction

Toward a complete arrangement in which every item has its place. The arrangement is understood by the systematizer as the natural order of the domain made visible. The direction is one: place, place again, place until every item has been placed. Completion is not reachable, but the direction does not depend on reaching it.

## Lever

Carrying force: the conviction that the order is discovered rather than imposed. Every placement reinforces the claim. The alphabet appears to be the nature of the lexicon. The grammatical scheme appears to be the structure of the language. The placements accumulate into an ontology, and the ontology becomes invisible by being taken as given.

Resistance: the domain's compatibility with alternative orders. A lexicon could be ordered by meaning rather than alphabet, by frequency rather than letter, by chronology rather than form. The resistance is the silent presence of the orders not chosen. The systematizer does not perceive them as options. The resistance does not prevent the ordering. It means the ordering was always a decision, and the invisibility of the decision is the measure of the ordering's success.

## Echo

[Enduring](process_enduring.md) inherits a [Card File](piece_card_file.md) that has become a method, a [Systematizer](position_systematizer.md) who will die inside the arrangement, and a discipline that now reads its own object through a frame produced by the arrangement rather than discovered in it. What changed: the domain that had no visible order before the ordering began now has an order that will be read as natural for generations to come.
