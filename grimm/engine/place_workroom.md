# Place: The Workroom

## Owner
- **Project:** Grimm

## Shown

Two desks flush against each other, long edge to long edge. [Jacob Grimm](persona_jacob_grimm.md)'s faces the window. [Wilhelm Grimm](persona_wilhelm_grimm.md)'s faces the wall. The flush joint means that when Jacob looks up from his work, he sees the back of his brother's head; when Wilhelm looks up, he sees the wall.

Each desk has an inkwell at the top right corner. Each has a candle for evening work. Paper is stacked in three piles on each desk. The books belonging to the desk sit on a low shelf behind the chair, within arm's reach if the writer leans back.

The light comes from one window. It serves Jacob. Wilhelm works with borrowed light, reflected off the far wall, supplemented by his candle even in the mornings of winter.

## Holds

- [Manuscript](piece_manuscript.md): on Wilhelm's desk, open to the page he is working on.
- [Card File](piece_card_file.md): in wooden boxes arranged in vertical columns against the wall beside Jacob's desk.
- [Empty Chair](piece_empty_chair.md): at the second desk, after December 1859.

## Offers

A shared silence that serves two kinds of work. The [Ordering](process_ordering.md) that Jacob performs at his desk. The [Smoothing](process_smoothing.md) that Wilhelm performs at his. Each kind of work requires the other's presence and is interrupted by the other's absence. When Jacob travels to Vienna in 1813 for the Congress, Wilhelm cannot work. When Wilhelm is ill for months, Jacob works less and recovers less ground than he should.

The room offers the thing that neither brother could articulate: that the work requires witness, not conversation. Witness. The presence of another person doing the same kind of thing at the same time in the same room.

## Withheld

The room does not hold the fact that the configuration is unusual. No visitor sees two desks back to back and remarks on it, because the brothers have been doing it since they were students in Marburg in 1803. By the time they are professors, the arrangement is invisible. By the time they are in Berlin, it is furniture.

The room does not hold the question of what happens when one of them stops writing. It will hold that question in December 1859. Until then it does not.

The room travels. It was in Kassel from 1806 to 1829. It was in Göttingen from 1829 to 1837, briefly, before the expulsion. It was in Kassel again from 1837 to 1840. It has been in Berlin since 1840. The furniture is not the same furniture in every city. The configuration is the same configuration. The room is not a room. The room is a geometry that can be rebuilt anywhere with two desks and a wall.
