# Persona: Wilhelm Grimm
## Title: The Smoother

## Owner
- **Project:** Grimm

**Persona:** Wilhelm Grimm

Born Hanau 1786, fifteen months after Jacob. Died Berlin 1859. Married Dorothea Wild in 1825 at the age of thirty-nine. Four children. Lived with his brother Jacob under the same roof from the year of his birth until the year of his death, excepting only three years of separation during university. Chronically ill from childhood onward with heart trouble. He worked at the second desk.

## Projection

He sits at the second desk in the [Workroom](place_workroom.md). The desks are flush. Jacob's right shoulder is nearly at his left shoulder. He does not turn to speak. He speaks across his own desk, forward, as if to the wall, and Jacob hears him and answers across his own desk also forward. This has been the case for thirty years. It is not an avoidance. It is the configuration that lets both men work.

He has the [Manuscript](piece_manuscript.md) open. It is a tale he wrote down in 1812. He is reading it now in 1843 for the fifth revision. He strikes out a word. He writes another above it. The new word is gentler, more pious, more German in a way he cannot name but recognizes. He does not consider what he is doing. He considers only whether the new word is better. It always is.

## Action

He reads aloud a line from the current tale. "And the wolf ate the grandmother whole." He pauses. He strikes *ate* and writes *swallowed*. He strikes *whole* and writes *with one gulp*. He reads again. "And the wolf swallowed the grandmother with one gulp." He nods. He writes a note in the margin for the printer. This is the work of [Smoothing](process_smoothing.md).

The test: if he believes he has improved the tale, he is in the slot. If he believes he has changed it, he is not in the slot. He does not change tales. He releases what was always in them.

## Shadow

He does not see that he is the author. He believes he is the scribe who removes the distortions that time and the mouths of many tellers introduced into pure folk speech. He believes that under the variation there is a tale, and that his work is to reveal it. The tale under the tale.

He does not see that there is no tale under the tale. There are only the tellings. The tale he is revealing is the tale he is composing. He composes it over fifty years, and the composing looks to him like polishing, because in each revision the tale becomes more like the thing he thought it always was. He is sculpting by addition a figure that did not exist until he sculpted it. He will die believing he released the German tale into its true form.

He will also die before he finishes. The seventh edition of 1857 is not his stopping point. It is only the last his heart allowed.

## Tell

When he finds a word that he has just replaced once before in a previous edition, he replaces it again, differently. He does not remember that he has already replaced it. He does not recognize his own editing from the time before. The tale he is smoothing is also smoothing him, and he cannot tell any more where his hand has been. A phrase arrives three editions into the future in a form that would shock the young Wilhelm who first wrote it down. The young Wilhelm is not around to object.
