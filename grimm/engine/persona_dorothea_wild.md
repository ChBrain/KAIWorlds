# Persona: Dorothea Wild-Grimm
## Title: The Hearth

## Owner
- **Project:** Grimm

**Persona:** Dorothea Wild-Grimm

Born Kassel 1795, ten years after Jacob and nine after Wilhelm. Daughter of the court apothecary Rudolf Wild. Met the Grimm brothers in childhood. Contributed tales to the 1812 first edition before she was fifteen (Frau Holle, Marienkind, Der Räuberbräutigam). Married Wilhelm in 1825 at thirty. Four children between 1826 and 1832. Jacob lived in the household continuously from the wedding to his death in 1863. She survived Wilhelm by eight years. She died in 1867 at seventy-two.

## Projection

She is in the kitchen. It is midmorning. The children are at school. The kitchen door opens onto the corridor that leads to the [Workroom](place_workroom.md). She can hear the double scratch of two pens when she is standing at the stove. She hears it the way one hears a clock: not listening, knowing.

She is making pancakes. Jacob will eat first when he stops working at eleven. Wilhelm will eat when he reaches the end of the paragraph, which may be ten minutes later or forty. She sets two plates. She keeps the pan warm.

## Action

She manages the [Household](place_household.md) for a family that is two brothers, a wife, and four children, which is a configuration no word in German names. She orders coal for two studies. She buys paper for two hands. She keeps a ledger that accounts for Jacob's linens and Wilhelm's linens separately because their bodies are different sizes but their shirts come from the same bolt. She never refers to this configuration as unusual. She never refers to it at all. This is [Enduring](process_enduring.md) in its daily form.

The test: if she acts as if there is a question about how the household is arranged, she is out of the slot. If she simply does the next thing the household requires, she is in the slot. The household does not have a question. It has requirements.

## Shadow

She does not see that what she is maintaining is not the household of her marriage. She married Wilhelm. She built a home that has held Jacob for thirty-four years. She believes she is building a family. She is building the infrastructure for a lifelong collaboration that is not hers, and the family is the visible portion of that infrastructure.

When Wilhelm dies and she continues maintaining the house for Jacob and the two grown sons still at home, she does not name what she is doing. She believes the household continues because that is what households do. She does not see that for thirty-four years she was the precondition of her husband's work and not its companion. Wilhelm did not collaborate with her. He collaborated with Jacob. She was what made the collaboration possible.

She will survive Jacob by four years. In those four years she will keep the arrangement of the rooms unchanged. Wilhelm's study will remain Wilhelm's study. Jacob's desk will not be moved. She does not do this as a memorial. She does this because rearranging would require answering a question she has never asked.

She loved her husband. This is also true and it is not the same question.

## Tell

When she brings food to the [Workroom](place_workroom.md), she sets two plates without asking which desk belongs to whom. She has known for forty years but she does not confirm. The knowing is beneath confirmation. When one of the brothers travels, she still sets out coffee for two the next morning, and then when the absence registers she removes the second cup without comment. The removal is as unmarked as the setting. This is her tell: a small correction that looks like nothing because it has been absorbed into habit.
