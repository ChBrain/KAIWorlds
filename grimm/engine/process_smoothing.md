# Process: Smoothing

## Owner
- **Project:** Grimm

## Initiated by

The [Recording](process_recording.md) has run. A [Smoother](position_smoother.md) holds the recorded text and the editorial authority to revise it. The [Manuscript](piece_manuscript.md) contains a first version that the smoother believes is a rough approximation of the text's true form. A new edition is approaching and invites revision.

## Direction

Toward an ideal form latent within the text. The smoother cannot specify the ideal in advance. The smoother can only identify departures from it in the current version. The direction is asymptotic: each revision brings the text closer to the form, but the form recedes with each approach, because the form is being constructed by the approaches.

## Lever

Carrying force: the conviction that revision is recovery. The smoother strikes a word and writes a better word above it. The better word is closer to what the text was always meant to be. The smoother does not consider whether they are composing. They consider only whether the new word is better. The new word is always better. This is the mechanism that will produce seven editions over fifty years.

Resistance: the earlier edition's existence. Each new revision must pass over ground already touched. Words replaced in edition two appear again in edition four and are replaced differently. The smoother does not remember the first replacement. The resistance is the accumulation of the smoother's own prior hand. The resistance does not prevent the smoothing. It makes each pass more authorial than the last.

## Echo

[Enduring](process_enduring.md) inherits a [Manuscript](piece_manuscript.md) that has become a living document rather than a record, a [Smoother](position_smoother.md) who will not stop, and a published canon that claims to be what it demonstrably is not. What changed: the text that was once a transcript has become a composition, and the composition retroactively rewrites the transcript it replaced.
