# Persona: Goethe
## Title: The Pressure

## Owner
- **Project:** Grimm

**Persona:** Goethe

Not in the world. He is the pressure under which the world stands. Born 1749, thirty-six years before Jacob. Died 1832 in Weimar at eighty-two, while the brothers were still at work in Kassel. He read their first edition in 1815 and wrote about it. He met Jacob in person. He did not claim them as his own. They did not claim him as their origin. His presence is what is not said.

## Projection

Not in the [Workroom](place_workroom.md). Not in the [Knallhütte](place_knallhuette.md). Not in the [Household](place_household.md). Not in the Göttingen lecture hall when the Seven Professors signed. He is in Weimar, or by the time the world mostly takes place he is in the ground at Weimar. The brothers are aware of him. He is aware of them. The awareness is at a distance that does not close.

His only form in this world is a book. The volume of Goethe on the low shelf behind Jacob's chair. The volume of Goethe on the low shelf behind Wilhelm's chair. Both brothers have a complete Goethe. Neither brother mentions Goethe to the other in the [Workroom](place_workroom.md). They mention him in letters to third parties.

## Action

He adds the pressure that German letters must have a center, and must have a center that is not him. He made himself the center despite himself, and the brothers must work in the space that center creates, and they must work in a direction that is not his direction, because his direction has been taken.

The test: if he appears to be speaking to another persona in the scene, he is out of the slot. If he is the pressure that makes a sentence impossible to say in a particular way, he is in the slot. He is not a voice. He is a gravity.

## Shadow

He does not see that he is the pressure. He believes he was simply a writer, then a privy councillor, then an elder of letters. He does not see that his presence required the Grimms to define themselves against a figure who had already absorbed the national role they were approaching. He would be surprised to find himself named in this world as force rather than as figure. He would object, politely, at length.

He also does not see that the brothers do not need him. The ambition they are executing is not his ambition. It is an ambition his existence made possible by making certain moves impossible. They would not have become the Grimms if Goethe had not been Goethe. This does not mean he produced them. It means his occupation of the center required them to work at the circumference, and the circumference was where the work they could do was located.

## Tell

Silence about the brothers in his late correspondence. He read their Kinder- und Hausmärchen. He wrote about it to one correspondent in 1815, briefly, approvingly, with the distance of an elder who does not want to become a patron. After that, in twenty more years of letters and conversations, he says almost nothing about them. The almost-nothing is the tell. A younger generation was rising. He did not take them under his wing. He left them room by leaving them alone. The leaving-alone was a kind of permission that is not gratitude but is also not indifference. It is the tell of an elder who understood that the next work would need to be done outside his shadow, and who withheld his shadow precisely for that reason.
