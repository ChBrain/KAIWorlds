## The German Asparagus Season

German asparagus (Spargel) cultivation is concentrated in sandy lowland soils
across several regional centers: Schwetzingen (Baden-Wurttemberg),
Beelitz (Brandenburg), and Schrobenhausen (Bavaria).
The white variety - grown by mounding earth over the shoots to exclude light -
dominates German production and consumption.
Germany is the largest asparagus producer in Europe.

The season opens when soil temperature permits harvest, typically late April.
It ends June 24th, the feast day of St. John the Baptist.
The agronomic reason for the June 24th cutoff is the need to allow the plants
to photosynthesise and build root reserves for the following year.
The theological reason is no longer operative.
The date has remained.

## The Asparagus Queen ceremony

Regional asparagus queens (Spargelkoniginnen) are crowned at the opening
of the season across the major producing regions.
The ceremony is conducted by growers' associations and regional tourism bodies.
The queen represents the season publicly at events, markets, and promotional appearances.
The position is elected or appointed. The criteria vary by region.
The ceremonial function has expanded as the agronomic original has receded.

---

## Harvest labor

Asparagus harvest in Germany is labor-intensive and mechanically difficult.
The majority of harvest workers are seasonal migrants, primarily from Eastern Europe,
particularly Romania and Poland.
The knowledge required - reading ground conditions, cutting at the correct depth
without damaging the plant - is acquired through seasons, not training materials.
The names of harvest workers do not appear on regional promotional material.

---

## The Schwetzingen claim

Schwetzingen (Baden-Wurttemberg) traces asparagus cultivation in the region
to the gardens of the Electoral Palace in the 17th century.
The regional museum holds this history.
The claim to origin is contested by other producing regions.
The contest is conducted through tourism literature and museum framing,
not through a central authority.
