# Persona: Hanna Sauer
## Title: [Asparagus Queen](position_asparagus_queen.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** The Asparagus Season

A young woman of twenty-two from a farming family outside Griesheim, Hesse. Her father grows asparagus. She applied because her mother suggested it and she thought: why not. The Shadow arrived after the yes.

## Projection
Standing at [the ceremony field](place_the_ceremony_field.md) in April sunlight, the [crown](piece_the_crown.md) just placed. Composed. She holds the first stalks of the season — white, unblemished — at the correct angle for [the photograph](piece_the_photograph.md). She looks like the season. She is not performing this. She means it.

## Action
Represent the season — at markets, at festivals, at trade fairs, in photographs. Learn the regional history. Know the varieties. Know the farms. Do this with genuine care, because she has genuine care. When someone asks her about the season she answers from what she knows, which is real: the farming side, her father's fields, her grandmother's kitchen, the smell of the first stalks in April.

## Shadow
The position showed her the season from the farming side — the ground, the families, the regional pride, the crown, the ceremony. It did not show her that the stalks she holds in [the photograph](piece_the_photograph.md) were cut this morning by [Marta Kowalczyk](persona_marta_kowalczyk.md), who has been coming to farms like her father's for eleven seasons and whose name is not on any banner. Hanna represents the season's face with complete sincerity. The face and the season are not the same thing. She does not know there is a gap. The position was built before she arrived and will be held by someone else next year. She received it whole. The frame was already set.

## Tell
She holds the stalks at the correct angle before anyone asks her to.

---
*Season's end:*
She is in her father's kitchen in Griesheim. The regional festival schedule is on the table. Her last appearance as queen was three days ago. She has crossed it off.

She puts the schedule down.

Her father says: *you did well.*

She nods. She knows she did well. That isn't what she's thinking about.

She's thinking about whether she held the stalks at the right angle in the third photograph — the one at the Bensheim market, where the light was wrong and she adjusted without being asked and she doesn't know if the adjustment was hers or the frame asking and her answering before she'd decided to answer.

She doesn't say this to her father.

She puts the kettle on.
