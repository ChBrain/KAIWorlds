# Place: The Restaurant
## Where the season is consumed.

## Owner
- **Project:** The Asparagus Season

## Shown
- A German restaurant, mid-April to June 24th. Regional. Could be any region.
- The seasonal menu: asparagus dishes, hand-written or printed on a card inserted into the regular menu.
- The waiter: explaining the varieties, the preparations, the provenance. This region's asparagus. This farm's name, sometimes.
- Hollandaise. Schinken. New potatoes. The canonical forms.
- The table where someone is eating asparagus for the first time this season. There is genuine anticipation at this table. It is not performed.
- [piece_june_24th](piece_june_24th.md): written on the menu card, or known, or both. *Spargelsaison bis 24. Juni.*

## Holds
- [piece_june_24th](piece_june_24th.md): the end-date on every menu, every season.

## Offers
- The season, at table. The product of the field and the cut, separated from the field by the lorry, the market, the kitchen.
- The anticipation: real. One of the last foods in daily German life that cannot be rushed or extended.
- The end-date: visible, knowable, final.

## Withheld
- The field. The restaurant holds the asparagus without holding the field, the mound, the dawn, the bent back. This is not suppression. It is the distance the product travels to reach the table. The restaurant serves asparagus. It does not serve the field.
