# Piece: June 24th
## The date that outlived its reason.

## Owner
- **Project:** The Asparagus Season
- **Position:** [The Saint](position_the_saint.md)

## Place
The calendar. Every calendar. Every restaurant's seasonal menu. Every farm's planning document.

## Load Bearing
Without it the season has no wall. [process_the_end](process_the_end.md) does not run. The Hypothesis collapses — there is no demonstration that a date can outlive its reason and become more itself in the surviving.

## Apparent
A date. June 24th. Written or printed. On a calendar, a chalkboard, a typed menu. Sometimes marked. Sometimes simply known. The saint's name is attached in ecclesiastical calendars. In agricultural practice, the saint's name is not mentioned. The date is mentioned.

## Yearbook
Johannistag — the feast of John the Baptist. The agronomic reason arrived first: the plant needs the summer to rebuild energy. The saint's feast provided the date. The theological reason is almost entirely forgotten. The date is not. Every farmer knows it. Every restaurant crosses asparagus off the menu on June 24th. The saint is not consulted. The date holds without him. This is the piece's entire history and its entire present condition.
