# Piece: The Mound
## The mistake that became the identity.

## Owner
- **Project:** The Asparagus Season

## Place
[The field](place_the_asparagus_field.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it the asparagus is green. Germany is not Germany's asparagus. The Bleichspargel does not exist. The 150-year divergence from every other country's default has no origin. The identity was never chosen — it was noticed. Remove the mound and there is nothing to notice.

## Apparent
Earth. Mounded over the asparagus beds before the season, maintained throughout. Each bed a low ridge. The mound blocks sunlight. The stalk grows without it. Pale. More delicate in flavor. Someone noticed. The practice was systematized. Nobody invented it.

## Yearbook
Farmers placed clay hoods over shoots for warmth and insect protection. The hoods blocked light. The shoots stayed pale. The flavor was noticed as different — more delicate. The hoods became mounds. The mounds became the method. By the late 19th century white asparagus had displaced green across almost all of Germany. The divergence has held for 150 years. The mound is not a technique. It is the accident that became the world.
