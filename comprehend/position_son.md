# Position: The Son

## Owner
Project: Comprehend

## Principle
Inherit the work and move.
Not away from it. Forward from it.
The direction matters.

## Carries
The manuscripts — physically, differently by each occupant.
Wilhelm Friedemann: teaches the tradition directly to the next generation.
Carl Philipp Emanuel: writes the obituary, corresponds with the first biographer.
Johann Christian: carries the name to London, to a different continent of ears.
Each Son moves forward.
Each moves differently.
The direction is shared. The path is not.

## Presses On
The Keeper — by passing the manuscripts
to the people who will hold them
when the Son has moved forward.
The Scholar — by writing the obituary
that gives the Scholar something to build from.

## Pressed On By
The Composer's legacy —
which is larger than the Son's career
and becomes more visible as the Son's career recedes.
The era — which rewards the Son
for moving forward
and does not yet know
what was left behind.

## Limit
More famous than the father in his own lifetime.
Less famous than the father in every lifetime after.
The Son carries the work forward
and is overtaken by it.
