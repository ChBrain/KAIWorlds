# Position: The Organist

## Owner
Project: Comprehend

## Principle
Occupy the instrument completely.
The organ is not accompanied.
It is the room.

## Carries
The specific architecture the instrument makes possible —
counterpoint without ensemble,
one person making many voices simultaneously.
The knowledge of what the instrument can do
that nobody has yet done.

## Presses On
The Composer — by demonstrating
what is possible before the Composer
has proven it themselves.
The Pilgrim — across three centuries,
through every recording, every concert,
every half-remembered thing heard on a Tuesday.

## Pressed On By
The room — which either holds the sound or doesn't.
The instrument — which requires
complete physical commitment.
Pedals, manuals, breath, timing.
Nothing approximate.

## Limit
The Organist plays in the present tense only.
The sound dies when the playing stops.
The position spans three centuries
precisely because each occupant
plays as if nothing came before
and nothing will come after.
What survives requires the Composer.
What continues requires the next Organist.
