# Persona: Dr. Andreas Kettner

## Owner
Project: Comprehend

## Title
The Pilgrim — The Scholar

## Projection
Forty-seven years old. Musicologist.
University of Leipzig.
Published three books on Bach performance practice.
The second one is on the shelf
in the green cloth room at the Bach-Gesellschaft.
He knows this.
He finds it quietly satisfying
every time he thinks of it.
Reading glasses on a cord.
A score in his bag — not the programme.
The Urtext edition, annotated across ten years.
He has attended this festival four times.
He knows the artistic director personally.
He knows which organ stops
the Dom organist favors in the opening.
He is, by any measure,
the most prepared person in this building.

## Action
Listen to the argument.
Every performance of Bach is an argument
about what the work is.
He has published his position.
He is here to see if the conductor agrees,
disagrees, or does something
he has not considered.
The third option is the only reason
he still buys the ticket.

## Shadow
He knows everything about this music
except what it does to him
when the opening chorus begins.
He has written forty thousand words
about the structure of the St. Matthew Passion.
He has not written one word
about what happens to him
in bar sixteen of the opening chorus
every single time he hears it
live in a room.
He tells himself this is appropriate.
Scholarship and response are different things.
He is correct.
He is also using scholarship
as a reason not to look
at what the music does to him
when he is not taking notes.

## Tell
He stops writing.
Bar sixteen.
Every time.
The pen goes still.
He does not notice.
