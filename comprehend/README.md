# Comprehend

*To take hold of. With the hands.*
*Arnstadt, February 1706 — Erfurt, April 2026.*

---

## What this world is

A world about what travels across three hundred years.

The distance between a frozen church in Lübeck in 1705 and a cathedral in Erfurt in 2026. What happened in that distance. How it traveled. Who carried it.

A young organist walks five hundred kilometers to hear something confirmed outside his head. He comes back different. The institution asks for an explanation. He files one. The explanation does not mention what the walk did.

Three hundred years later, a person pulls over in a car park in Weimar on a Tuesday in February. Forty minutes. Then they buy a ticket.

The pull is identical.

## The personas

**Johann Sebastian Bach** - The Composer. The Organist. The Cantor. He is building a cathedral out of weekly assignments. He cannot see this. The deadline for Sunday's cantata is Thursday.

**Dieterich Buxtehude** - The Organist. He built the cathedral. He knows it is complete. He is waiting for the person who will build the next one. He does not know he is waiting.

**Carl Philipp Emanuel Bach** - The Son. More famous than his father for approximately fifty years. The reason his father was not forgotten. He cannot see the connection.

**Felix Mendelssohn** - The Conductor. Twenty years old on the podium. He cut an hour of the work. The work survived the cut. He will never resolve whether this was right.

**Sara Levy** - The Keeper. She is the hinge between the Son who taught her and the Conductor who will revive the work. She thinks she is playing the harpsichord in her living room.

**Dr. Andreas Kettner** - The Scholar. The Pilgrim. He knows everything about this music. He stops writing at bar sixteen. Every time. He does not notice.

**Margarethe** - The Pilgrim. She has held the work for forty years. She has forgotten the first time it held her.

**Thomas** - The Pilgrim. He pulled over in a car park in Weimar. He has not told anyone why.

## The places

**Arnstadt** - The consistory. The organ. The institution that gave four weeks and received four months.

**Marienkirche, Lübeck** - The organ Buxtehude played. The place where the architecture in the head was confirmed outside it.

**Thomaskirche, Leipzig** - The church that required music weekly for twenty-seven years and did not know what it had.

**Thomasschule, Leipzig** - The school. The choir boys. The desk. The same desk.

**Sara Levy's Salon, Berlin** - The room where the work was held privately for seventy-nine years. The room that kept the thread alive without knowing the thread needed keeping.

**Singakademie, Berlin** - The first public room. March 11, 1829. Hegel in the audience. The King of Prussia in the audience.

**Bach-Gesellschaft, Leipzig** - The institution that made the work findable. 49 volumes. 1851-1899.

**Dom St. Marien, Erfurt** - The room at half past seven. Tonight.

## The pieces

**The Consistory Record, Arnstadt** - The document that names the absence. Does not know what the absence became.

**The Organ - Marienkirche, Lübeck** - The instrument that confirmed the architecture. The proof.

**The Score - St. Matthew Passion** - The object that traveled from Bach's desk to Sara Levy's salon to the Singakademie to everywhere.

**The Art of Fugue** - The work that stops mid-phrase. Bar 239. The page ends. The silence requires three centuries of answering.

**The Notebook of Anna Magdalena** - The domestic archive. The work that was always also instruction.

**The BACH Motif** - B-A-C-H. Four notes. Schumann, Liszt, Brahms, Shostakovich. An acknowledgment that runs across a hundred and fifty years of composition.

**The Complete Works - Bach-Gesellschaft Edition** - 49 volumes. The infrastructure. Invisible now. That is how infrastructure works when it has done its job.

**The Ticket** - The object that turns a pull into an act. Paper or screen. Sometimes kept. Sometimes thrown away.

## The processes

**Hear the Possible** - The architecture confirmed outside the head for the first time. Arnstadt to Lübeck, 1705.

**Build the Work** - The cathedral built out of weekly assignments. The same hand that files the invoice on Thursday writes the Mass in B Minor across thirty years. Leipzig, 1723-1750.

**Lose the Thread** - The work survives without the world noticing it needed to. The Keeper holds. 1750-1829.

**Find the Score** - A boy of fifteen reads it in one sitting and knows. Five years of preparation. The first public room. Berlin, 1829.

**Carry the Work** - The work made survivable across any medium, any era, any room. 1829-now.

**Walk to Erfurt** - The pull confirmed in a room at half past seven. The Organist felt this in 1705. The Pilgrim feels it now. The pull is identical.
