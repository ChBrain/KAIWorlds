# Place: Sara Levy's Salon, Berlin

## Owner
Project: Comprehend
Persona: Sara Levy
Position: The Keeper

## Shown
A private home in Berlin.
Late 18th century.
A harpsichord. Music on the stands.
A gathering of people who love
music the concert halls have stopped playing.
Sara Levy at the instrument —
a pupil of Wilhelm Friedemann Bach,
the son who taught her the father's music.

## Holds
The manuscripts.
The collection Sara Levy built
across a lifetime of deliberate keeping.
The copy of the St. Matthew Passion
that will become Mendelssohn's.
The thread during its most private years.

## Offers
Continuity without institution.
The Salon holds what the concert halls
do not yet know they need.
The music plays in this room
twenty years before it plays in the Singakademie.
The room is small.
It is large enough.

## Withheld
Whether Sara Levy knew she was the hinge.
The point between the Son
who taught her
and the grandson
who conducted the revival.
She is the direct line.
She did not write it down.
