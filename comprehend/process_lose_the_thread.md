# Process: Lose the Thread
## The work survives without the world noticing it needed to.

## Owner
Project: Comprehend
Position: The Son
Position: The Keeper
Piece: The Score — St. Matthew Passion
Piece: The Art of Fugue
Place: Sara Levy's Salon, Berlin
Persona: Carl Philipp Emanuel Bach

## Initiates
The Composer is dead.
The objects are in the room.
The Sons have careers of their own.
The style has changed.
Counterpoint is old-fashioned.
The new music is leaner, more elegant, more fashionable.
Nobody has thrown anything away yet.

## Direction
Toward the edge of disappearance.
Not deliberately. The Sons move forward
because forward is where their work is.
The Keeper holds in two ways simultaneously.
Sara Levy holds without knowing that holding is the work —
it is simply what she loves.
Zelter holds with full knowledge of what he is doing
and does it anyway, against the era's indifference.
Both are the Keeper.
Neither receives institutional recognition for it.

## Lever
The gap between what the work is
and what the era can hear.
Carrying force: the Keeper's love —
which requires no institutional support,
no public recognition,
and no awareness that it is load-bearing.
The Keeper does not know she is the Lever.
That is constitutive.
Remove that ignorance: the holding becomes performance.
Remove the Keeper entirely: the score wraps cheese
and nobody picks it up.
Resistance: fashion, time, the preference of the living
for music written by the living.

## Echo
The manuscripts have survived
without the world noticing they needed to.
The Son wrote the obituary.
The Keeper built the collection.
A boy in Berlin will be given a score
by his grandmother
who got it from her sister
who got it from the Son
who got it from the desk in Leipzig.
What was held in private
is about to enter a public room.
