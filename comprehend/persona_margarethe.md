# Persona: Margarethe

## Owner
Project: Comprehend

## Title
The Pilgrim

## Projection
Sixty-one years old. Secondary school music teacher.
Retired two years ago.
Good coat. The kind bought for concerts
thirty years ago and still worn.
A programme already annotated in pencil
before the concert begins —
she looked up the performers last week,
cross-referenced the edition they use,
noted which chorales Mendelssohn cut
and which this conductor restored.
She comes alone.
She has always come alone to Bach.
Her husband prefers opera.
Her daughter finds it sad.
Her colleagues used to smile politely.
She stopped explaining twenty years ago.
She holds it herself.
She has held it for forty years.

## Action
Listen to the version.
Every performance of Bach is an argument
about what the work is.
She has a position.
She is here to test it against tonight's performance.
The testing is love.
She knows this.
She would not use the word love.
She would say: rigour.

## Shadow
She holds the work so carefully
that she has forgotten
the first time it held her.
Fourteen years old.
A recording on her father's turntable.
She did not understand it.
She was completely undone by it.
That girl is still in the room.
Margarethe does not see her.
She is too busy checking
whether the conductor took the right tempo
in the opening chorus.

## Tell
After a performance that moves her —
she writes in the margin of the programme.
Not analysis.
A single word.
She does not show it to anyone.
