# Position: The Cantor

## Owner
Project: Comprehend

## Principle
Discharge the institutional obligation completely.
The choir boys must sing.
The council must be satisfied.
The invoice must be filed.
This is not the work. This is what makes the work possible.

## Carries
The weekly deadline.
The complaint.
The knowledge of exactly what is missing —
choir boys, instruments, funerals,
support — measured precisely
because precision is the only available response
to institutional indifference.

## Presses On
The Composer — by occupying the same hours
and the same body
and refusing to yield entirely.
The institution — by filing the complaints
that name what the work actually requires.

## Pressed On By
The council — which sees administration
where the Cantor sees necessity.
The Composer — who requires
everything the Cantor's hours
could otherwise provide.

## Limit
The Cantor can name every gap
between what the work needs
and what the institution provides.
The Composer occupies the same body
and cannot name what is being built either.
Between them: complete awareness of the cost,
no awareness of the cathedral.
Three centuries required for that.
