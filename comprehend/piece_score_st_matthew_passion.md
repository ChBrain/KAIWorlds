## Yearbook
1727: performed at Thomaskirche, Leipzig.
1750: Bach dies. The score remains.
1750–1823: held privately.
Passes from Bach's desk
to Carl Philipp Emanuel
to the Berlin Sing-Akademie collection.
1823: Bella Salomon gives a copy to her fifteen-year-old grandson.
1829: Mendelssohn conducts it at the Singakademie.
First public performance in a century.
Hegel is in the audience.
The King of Prussia is in the audience.
1841: Mendelssohn conducts it again, closer to the original.
1854: first performance in England.
1882: first unabridged performance.
Now: performed every year in churches and concert halls
on every continent.
Mendelssohn described it as "the greatest of all Christian works."
The regard has not diminished since.
