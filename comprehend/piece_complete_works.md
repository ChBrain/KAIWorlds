# Piece: The Complete Works — Bach-Gesellschaft Edition

## Owner
Project: Comprehend
Place: Bach-Gesellschaft, Leipzig

## Place
Bach-Gesellschaft, Leipzig.
Then: every library, conservatory, and music school
that holds a copy.
Which is all of them.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Performer has no authoritative score.
The Scholar has no standard text.
The Conductor edits in the dark.
The Pilgrim attends a concert
built on guesswork and oral tradition.

## Apparent
49 volumes. Green cloth binding.
Each volume: one category of work —
cantatas, passions, keyboard music, chamber music.
The handwriting of Bach visible in facsimile throughout.
A scholarly apparatus in German
that grows more precise with each volume.
Published across 48 years.
The editors change. The project does not.

## Yearbook
1850: the Bach-Gesellschaft founded in Leipzig
on the centenary of Bach's death.
The founding purpose: collect, edit, publish
every known work.
1851: first volume published.
1899: 49th and final volume published.
Complete. Or as complete as known work allows.
1950: the Neue Bach-Ausgabe begins —
a second complete edition,
more historically informed,
correcting what the first edition
could not yet know.
Now: both editions in libraries everywhere.
The digital edition online.
The work findable by anyone
with a connection and a question.
The Scholar's project
has no final volume anymore.
