# Process: Carry the Work
## The work made survivable across any medium, any era, any room.

## Owner
Project: Comprehend
Position: The Scholar
Position: The Performer
Position: The Conductor
Piece: The Complete Works — Bach-Gesellschaft Edition
Piece: The BACH Motif
Place: Bach-Gesellschaft, Leipzig

## Initiates
The Singakademie concert has happened.
The thread is pulled taut.
The Scholar begins writing.
The Society begins collecting.
The Performer begins playing.
The work enters institutions
for the first time since Leipzig.
1829 to now.

## Direction
Toward every room simultaneously.
First the Scholar makes the work findable —
49 volumes, complete works, 1851–1899.
Then the Performer makes it playable on anything.
The work stops belonging to one city,
one era, one instrument, one tradition.
The BACH Motif appears in Schumann, Liszt, Brahms,
Shostakovich — composers writing
the four notes as an act of acknowledgment
across a hundred and fifty years.
The work is no longer survivable.
It has survived.

## Lever
The gap between the work existing in one public room
and the work existing in all rooms.
Carrying force: the institutions —
the Society, the biography, the editions,
the conservatories, the recordings.
These are not enthusiasm. They are infrastructure.
Remove the infrastructure: the work remains
a sensation from 1829 that nobody followed up.
Resistance: the scale of the complete works,
the changing performance practice,
the question of authenticity that follows
every era's attempt to play music
written for a different room.

## Echo
49 volumes. Complete works. 1851–1899.
Every known cantata, passion, fugue, suite, concerto.
The Pilgrim's concert exists
because someone published the score
that someone collected
from the desk of the Son
who inherited it from the room in Leipzig.
The infrastructure is invisible now.
That is how infrastructure works
when it has done its job.
