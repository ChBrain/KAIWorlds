# Position: The Scholar

## Owner
Project: Comprehend

## Principle
Name what the work is.
Make it findable.
Findability precedes everything else.

## Carries
The biography — Forkel, 1802.
The complete edition — Bach-Gesellschaft, 1851–1899.
The catalogue — Schmieder, BWV, 1950.
Every known work assigned a number and a location.
The argument — that this work is
what the Scholar says it is,
made in public,
before the public agrees.

## Presses On
The Conductor — by producing the score
the Conductor needs before the Conductor knows they need it.
The Performer — by establishing
what is authentic and what is editorial.
The Pilgrim — by ensuring the concert
exists to attend.

## Pressed On By
The work itself — which is larger
than any single Scholar's capacity
to name completely.
The era — which must be persuaded
before it will fund the naming.

## Limit
The Scholar names the work
after the work has already done
most of what it will do.
Forkel writes in 1802.
The St. Matthew Passion was performed in 1727.
The naming is always late.
It is still necessary.
