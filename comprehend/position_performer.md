# Position: The Performer

## Owner
Project: Comprehend

## Principle
Make the work audible in this room, tonight.
Not last night's room. Not the room it was written for.
This room. These ears.

## Carries
The score — as written.
The editorial tradition — accumulated since 1829.
The instrument — which may or may not be
what the work was written for.
The knowledge of the gap between the score
and what the room can hear.

## Presses On
The Pilgrim — directly.
The sound reaches them before anything else does.
The Conductor — by making the Conductor's decisions
audible or inaudible in practice.

## Pressed On By
The score — which specifies exactly.
The Conductor — who has edited.
The Scholar — who has an opinion
about what the editing cost.
The Pilgrim — who has come a long way
and does not know what they are about to hear.

## Limit
The Performer makes the work live
in the present tense only.
Like the Organist.
Unlike the Organist: the Performer
always plays someone else's architecture.
The obligation is to the score
and to the room simultaneously.
When they conflict: decide.
Tonight.
