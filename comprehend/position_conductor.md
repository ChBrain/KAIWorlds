# Position: The Conductor

## Owner
Project: Comprehend

## Principle
Return the work to the condition it was always in —
heard by people in a room together.
Not restoration. Return.

## Carries
The score — made available by the Keeper and the Scholar.
The forces — orchestra, choir, the assembled room.
The editorial decision —
what to cut, what to keep,
how much the era can hear
without losing it entirely.

## Presses On
The Performer — by making the performance possible.
The Pilgrim — by creating the room
the Pilgrim will one day enter.
The Scholar — by demonstrating publicly
what the Scholar has been arguing privately.

## Pressed On By
The score — which was written for different forces,
a different room, a different era.
The audience — which can only hear
what it has been prepared to hear.
The Keeper — whose seventy-nine years of holding
now require the Conductor
to justify them in public.

## Limit
Every editorial decision that makes the work
accessible to the present era
is also a distance from the work as written.
Mendelssohn cut an hour.
The work survived the cut.
The question of what was lost
arrives later, with the Scholar.
