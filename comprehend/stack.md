# Stack

## Owner
Project: Comprehend

## Context
Arnstadt, February 1706. A young organist walks five hundred kilometers to hear Dieterich Buxtehude play. The institution gave him four weeks. He stayed four months. He came back different.

Erfurt, April 2026. A person hears something on a phone in a van on a Tuesday. They pull over. Forty minutes later they look up where to hear it live.

The distance between those two moments is this world.

## Positions

**The Organist** - Occupy the instrument completely. The organ is not accompanied. It is the room.
**The Composer** - Build the work the position requires. Then build the work the position does not know it requires.
**The Cantor** - Fulfill the weekly obligation. File the invoice. Attend the funeral.
**The Son** - Inherit the work and move. Not away from it. Forward from it.
**The Keeper** - Hold the work when the world is not looking. Not as duty. As love.
**The Conductor** - Return the work to the condition it was always in - heard by people in a room together.
**The Scholar** - Name what the work is. Make it findable. Findability precedes everything else.
**The Performer** - Make the work audible in this room, tonight.
**The Pilgrim** - Go toward the thing. Before you have words for what it is.

## Processes

**Hear the Possible** - The architecture confirmed outside the head for the first time. Arnstadt to Lübeck, 1705.
**Build the Work** - The cathedral built out of weekly assignments. Leipzig, 1723-1750.
**Lose the Thread** - The work survives without the world noticing it needed to. 1750-1829.
**Find the Score** - The work returned to the condition it was always in. Berlin, 1829.
**Carry the Work** - The work made survivable across any medium, any era, any room. 1829-now.
**Walk to Erfurt** - The pull confirmed in a room at half past seven. Now.
