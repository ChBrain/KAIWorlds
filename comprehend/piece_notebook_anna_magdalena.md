# Piece: The Notebook of Anna Magdalena

## Owner
Project: Comprehend
Persona: Johann Sebastian Bach
Persona: Anna Magdalena Bach

## Place
Where Anna Magdalena is.
Then: where the archive holds it.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the domestic interior of Process 2 disappears.
The Composer becomes the Cantor without remainder.
The work inside the household —
the teaching, the love, the daily music —
has no object.

## Apparent
Small bound notebook. Bach's hand and Anna Magdalena's.
Music written for her to study and to play.
Minuets, polonaises, chorales, songs.
Some pieces by Bach. Some by others.
Some by the sons. Some by Anna Magdalena herself.
The handwriting changes throughout.
The book was used. It shows.

## Yearbook
1722: Bach begins the first notebook for Anna Magdalena
shortly after their marriage.
1725: a second notebook. Larger. More ambitious.
It contains what may be the most famous pieces
attributed to Bach —
including a minuet long believed to be his
that scholars later attributed to Christian Petzold.
The attribution does not diminish the notebook.
It widens it — the Keeper of the household
collected music worth keeping
regardless of who wrote it.
Anna Magdalena outlives Bach by ten years.
She dies in poverty in Leipzig, 1760.
The notebooks survive her.
They are in the archive now.
Facsimiles are printed. Recordings made.
The book that was used in a room in Leipzig
is now used in rooms everywhere.
