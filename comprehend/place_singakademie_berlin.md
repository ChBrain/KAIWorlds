# Place: Singakademie, Berlin

## Owner
Project: Comprehend
Piece: The Score — St. Matthew Passion
Persona: Felix Mendelssohn

## Shown
A concert hall north of the Unter den Linden, Berlin.
Known today as the Maxim Gorky Theater.
March 11, 1829.
158 singers. Full orchestra.
A sold-out house.
Hegel in the audience.
The King of Prussia in the audience.
A twenty-year-old on the podium.

## Holds
The moment Process 4 becomes audible.
The first public performance of the St. Matthew Passion
in a century.
The second survival of the Score.

## Offers
The public room.
The work existed privately for seventy-nine years.
The Singakademie is the first room
large enough and willing enough
to hold it publicly again.
The thread pulled taut.

## Withheld
What the audience heard
compared to what Bach wrote.
Mendelssohn cut ten arias, seven choruses.
The work that filled the Singakademie
was shorter and louder and more Romantic
than the work that filled the Thomaskirche.
The Singakademie does not record this.
The Scholar will note it later.
The Pilgrim will not know unless they ask.
