# Process: Build the Work
## The cathedral built out of weekly assignments.

## Owner
Project: Comprehend
Position: The Composer
Position: The Cantor
Piece: The Score — St. Matthew Passion
Piece: The Art of Fugue
Piece: The Notebook of Anna Magdalena
Place: Thomaskirche, Leipzig
Place: Thomasschule, Leipzig
Persona: Johann Sebastian Bach

## Initiates
The Composer arrives in Leipzig, 1723.
The Cantor position is waiting.
The church requires music weekly.
The school requires the choir boys to sing Latin.
The Composer applied for this position.
Told them exactly what he was.
The institution heard: Cantor.
That was enough for them.

## Direction
Toward permanence.
The same hand that files the invoice on Thursday
writes the Mass in B Minor across thirty years.
The Cantor and the Composer are not separate operations.
They are simultaneous. In the same room. At the same desk.
The institution sees the Cantor.
The work accumulates regardless.

## Lever
The weekly deadline meeting the architecture that requires forty years.
Remove the deadline: the Composer drifts.
Remove the architecture: the Cantor produces adequate music and nothing more.
Carrying force: the confirmed possibility from Process 1.
Resistance: the council, the choir boys who cannot sing,
the funerals insufficiently numerous,
the princess who was an amusa,
the institution that does not know what it has hired
and would not have hired it if it did.

## Echo
The work exists on paper.
In the room: cantatas, Passions, the Mass in B Minor,
the Notebook of Anna Magdalena,
the Art of Fugue on the desk — last page, mid-phrase, silence.
Whether the silence is death or completion the manuscript does not say.
The Composer is gone.
The Cantor position is vacated.
The objects remain.
The Sons are alive.
The Keeper is not yet looking.
The world has moved on to other music.
