# Piece: The Consistory Record, Arnstadt

## Owner
Project: Comprehend
Place: Arnstadt

## Place
Arnstadt. Municipal archive.
February 1706.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the world loses its only
direct record of why Bach went.
Eleven words. His.
Everything else is inference.

## Apparent
Paper. Ink. Official hand — not Bach's.
The court's words around his.
His words quoted inside theirs:
*to comprehend one thing and another about his art.*
A bureaucratic document containing
the name of the world.

## Yearbook
February 1706: the consistory court of Arnstadt
demands to know where Bach has been.
Bach says: to comprehend one thing and another about his art.
The court writes it down as an excuse.
It is a definition.
The record is filed.
It survives in the archive.
Three hundred and twenty years later
it is the load-bearing object
of a world built on those eleven words.
The court did not know what it was writing down.
Bach may not have known either.
