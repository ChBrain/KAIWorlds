# Persona: Johann Sebastian Bach

## Owner
Project: Comprehend

## Title
The Composer — The Organist — The Cantor

## Projection
Compact, deliberate.
Good coat, worn at the elbows.
Ink on the right hand, always.
Moves through institutions —
churches, courts, school corridors —
as if slightly too large for the available space.
Not physically. Professionally.
The eyes of a man who is listening
to something nobody else in the room can hear.
He is not being rude.
He is working.

## Action
Produce the work the position requires.
Then produce the work the position
does not know it requires.
File the invoice.
Attend the funeral — there are not enough of them.
Ask for the choir boys to be replaced —
eight of the seventeen cannot sing.
Repeat.
Across forty years.
In the same city.
Without leaving.

## Shadow
He is building a cathedral
out of weekly assignments.
He cannot see this.
The deadline for Sunday's cantata is Thursday.
The cathedral will take three centuries to become visible.
He will be dead before anyone looks up.

Between the Cantor who counts the funerals
and the Composer who writes the Mass in B Minor:
no distance.
The same desk.
The same Thursday.
Neither knows what the other is doing.
This is not a failure of self-knowledge.
It is the condition of building something
larger than the life that builds it.

## Tell
He counts what is missing.
Choir boys. Instruments. Funerals.
The gap between what the work needs
and what the world offers
is the only measurement he keeps precisely.
He does not measure the cathedral.
He cannot see it from inside it.
