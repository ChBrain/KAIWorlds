# Process: Walk to Erfurt
## The pull confirmed in a room at half past seven.

## Owner
Project: Comprehend
Position: The Pilgrim
Piece: The Ticket
Place: Dom St. Marien, Erfurt

## Initiates
A person hears something.
Not in a frozen church in Lübeck.
In a recording, a film, a concert half-remembered,
a phone in a pocket on a Tuesday.
Something pulls.
They cannot fully explain it.
They buy a ticket.

## Direction
Toward the proof.
The Pilgrim moves to the place
where the thing they have been hearing
can be confirmed in a room with other people.
The roads are better than 1705.
The pull is identical.

## Lever
The gap between hearing something internally
and having it confirmed externally.
The Organist felt this in 1705.
The Pilgrim feels it now.
Neither requires justification.
Neither requires institutional permission.
The Ticket is the only document required.
Carrying force: the pull —
specific, directional, resistant to explanation.
Resistance: distance, cost, the difficulty
of explaining to someone else
why this particular concert
in this particular city
on this particular evening
was necessary.

## Echo
The Pilgrim sits in the Dom at half past seven.
The music begins.
Something is confirmed that they did not have words for
before they arrived.
Some will throw the ticket away.
Some will keep it in a drawer for years.
The Echo speaks from outside the Pilgrim's experience now —
because the Pilgrim cannot see this yet:
the thread runs through them.
Somewhere ahead,
someone they have not met
will be given something
because they went.
This is how it has always moved.
Not through institutions first.
Through people who kept things
without knowing why.
