# Piece: The Art of Fugue

## Owner
Project: Comprehend
Persona: Johann Sebastian Bach

## Place
Bach's desk, Leipzig — last seen here.
Bach-Gesellschaft, Leipzig — published here.
Everywhere — performed here, argued over here.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Composer's final statement
disappears mid-sentence.
The silence at the end of the work
is the most load-bearing silence in this world.
Without this piece the Composer simply stops.
With it: the Composer stops in a way
that requires three centuries of answering.

## Apparent
Manuscript. Bach's hand deteriorating toward the end.
A series of contrapuntal pieces
building toward a final fugue
on the subject B-A-C-H.
The final fugue breaks off.
Mid-phrase. Bar 239.
The page ends.

## Yearbook
1740s: Bach works on the collection across his final decade.
1749: still working. Eyesight failing.
March 1750: surgery by the English oculist John Taylor.
The surgery fails. Bach goes blind.
July 28, 1750: Bach dies.
On the desk: the Art of Fugue, unfinished.
Or finished. The manuscript does not say.
1751: published posthumously by Carl Philipp Emanuel.
A chorale prelude added at the end
by Carl Philipp Emanuel —
a piece Bach dictated blind in his final days:
*Before Thy Throne, My God, I Stand.*
Whether this was intended as the ending
nobody knows.
It is what we have.
The fugue at bar 239.
The chorale after.
Silence.
