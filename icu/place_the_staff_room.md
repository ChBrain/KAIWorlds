# Place: The Staff Room
## Location: Off the unit. Between the clinical world and the outside world.

## Owner
- **Project:** ICU

## Shown
- A small room that was not designed for what it is used for.
  The chairs do not match.
  The table has a scratch along one edge
  that has been there for years.
- A kettle. Always recently used or recently emptied.
  The sound of it boiling
  is the specific sound of a person
  deciding to stay for five more minutes.
- Lockers along one wall.
  Coats inside them. Bags.
  The outside world, held in metal boxes,
  waiting for the end of the shift.
- A noticeboard.
  Rotas. A staff wellbeing leaflet
  that has been there long enough
  to have curled at the corners.
  A photograph from a leaving party, two years old.
  Three people in the photograph
  no longer work on this unit.
- A window. It shows a car park
  or a wall or a roof.
  It shows outside.
  That is its function.
- The door to [The ICU Unit](place_the_icu_unit.md).
  Never fully closed.
  The unit can be heard through it.
  The unit can always be heard.

## Holds
- The things said here that are not said on the unit.
  Not filed. Not charted.
  Said once, to one person,
  in the four minutes between shifts
  or the ten minutes after a difficult night.
  Or not said.
  Both happen here.

## Offers
- The kettle. To whoever needs
  something to do with their hands
  that is not clinical.
- The chair in the corner.
  The one that is slightly further
  from the door than the others.
  The experienced nurses know
  which nurse needs that chair tonight
  before the nurse knows it themselves.
- The four minutes.
  The shift has ended.
  The next thing has not started.
  The four minutes are the only time
  in this building
  that belongs entirely to the person
  holding them.
  The unit can still be heard.
  It is heard differently from here.

## Withheld
- What is carried out.
  The staff room does not process
  what the nurses bring into it.
  It offers the kettle and the chair
  and the four minutes.
  What goes home with the nurse
  after those four minutes
  is not the room's work.
  The room does not track this.
  Nobody tracks this.
  That is the gap the room holds
  without being able to name it.
