# Position: The Sedated Patient
## The one the room is built around who cannot participate in it.

## Owner
- **Project:** ICU
- **Process:** [process_the_sedation](process_the_sedation.md)
- **Place:** [The ICU Room](place_the_icu_room.md)

## Has
The body in the bed.
Everything the room does
is done to it or for it
or because of it.
The position is the reason
every other position in this world exists.
It exists before any persona arrives —
the bed has held others before this person
and will hold others after.

## Orders
Receive.
The position does not ask whether the patient
is ready to receive what the room sends.
The room sends it regardless.
The ventilator breathes.
The nurses work.
The family speaks or does not speak.
The monitor reads the body continuously.
The position does not offer
any of this to the patient for their consideration.
It simply runs.

## Loses
The ability to refuse.
They were certain — in the life before this —
that they had agency over what entered them.
What was said to them.
Who touched them.
What decisions were made about them.
The position takes all of it.
Every decision in this room
is made without them.
Every word spoken at this bedside
is spoken to someone
who cannot confirm they heard it.

## Drives
On the loss of agency —
the body does what it can.
Sometimes it responds to a familiar voice.
Sometimes it moves toward a hand.
These are not decisions.
They are the body finding
the smallest possible version
of the agency that was taken.
