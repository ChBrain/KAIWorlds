# Piece: The Phone
## The thing that connects this room to every room that isn't it.

## Owner
- **Project:** ICU
- **Place:** [The ICU Room](place_the_icu_room.md)

## Place
Where [The One Who Manages](position_the_one_who_manages.md) is.
In a pocket or a hand.
Never put down for long.

## Load Bearing
Without it the world outside
does not know what is happening here.
Without it [process_the_call](process_the_call.md)
has no mechanism.
The phone is the bridge
between the room and everything outside it
the people who couldn't come,
the people who don't know yet,
the people who need updates
at regular intervals
because the not-knowing
is its own kind of vigil.
Remove the phone and the room
becomes sealed.
That is not always worse.
Sometimes the room needs to be sealed.
The phone prevents this.

## Apparent
A screen. Always slightly too bright
for this room at this hour.
Notifications arriving continuously
people who have heard,
people who are asking,
people who want to help
in ways that require response.
The battery dying at the wrong moment.
The charger plugged into the wall
beside the [Chair](piece_the_chair.md).
The phone face-down sometimes,
when the room asks for something
the phone cannot give.
Face-up again within minutes.

## Yearbook
It received [The Call](process_the_call.md)
that started everything.
It has been used to update twelve people
and then update them again
when the update changed.
It has been used to look up
what the numbers on the [Monitor](piece_the_monitor.md) mean.
It has been used to book a hotel
near the hospital
and then not check out on the day
the booking said.
It has been held by someone
who needed to call a person
they had not spoken to in years
because this was the moment
that ended the years of not speaking,
and they sat in the corridor
for ten minutes holding it
before they made the call.
It has been left in a coat pocket
for one hour by someone
who decided the room needed more
than the phone could allow.
One hour was all they managed.
The phone came back out.
It always does.
