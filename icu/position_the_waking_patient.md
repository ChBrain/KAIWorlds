# Position: The Waking Patient
## The one arriving back into a room that ran without them.

## Owner
- **Project:** ICU
- **Process:** [process_the_waking](process_the_waking.md)
- **Place:** [The ICU Room](place_the_icu_room.md)

## Has
The gap.
Days or weeks of the room
continuing without them.
It exists before consciousness returns.
The gap was accumulating
while they were under.
They arrive into it
without knowing its shape
or its size
or what it contains.

## Orders
Orient.
What day is it.
What happened.
How long.
The position does not wait
for the patient to be ready
to receive these answers.
The room has them.
The room gives them
in whatever order
the questions arrive.

## Loses
The before.
They were certain, in the last moment
they remember, that they were
still in the life they had been living.
The waking takes that certainty.
The life continued without them.
The people in this room
have been changed by something
the patient did not experience.
They are being welcomed back
by people who are different
from the people who said goodbye.
The patient does not know this yet.
The faces are familiar.
What the faces have been through
is not.

## Drives
On the loss of the before
asks the question underneath
all the clinical ones.
Usually not aloud.
Sometimes aloud, once,
in the night when the room is quiet:
*am I still the same person.*
The position does not answer this.
Nobody in the room answers this.
The question stays in the air
and eventually the room
moves on around it.
