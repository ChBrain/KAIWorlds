# Piece: The Ventilator
## The thing that breathes when the patient cannot.

## Owner
- **Project:** ICU
- **Place:** [The ICU Room](place_the_icu_room.md)

## Place
[The ICU Room](place_the_icu_room.md).
Beside the [Bed](piece_the_bed.md).
Connected to the patient by a tube.
The tube goes into the throat.
This is the condition of the room.

## Load Bearing
Without it the patient who cannot breathe
does not breathe.
Without it the [Sedated Patient](position_the_sedated_patient.md)
position cannot run
the sedation that keeps the patient
from pulling out the tube
exists because the tube exists.
The tube exists because the ventilator exists.
Remove the ventilator and the room
becomes a different kind of room entirely.
It is the machine that makes
the vigil possible.
It is also the machine that the family
must eventually decide about.

## Apparent
A machine on a stand.
Grey or white. Larger than expected.
Tubes running from it to the patient.
A bellows movement, the machine
breathing in, breathing out,
in, out, regularly, mechanically,
at a rate set by the clinical team
not by the patient's body.
A screen showing numbers and waveforms.
The sound: rhythmic, present,
the room's breath not the patient's.
When it alarms the room stops.
When it is turned off
the silence is a specific kind of silence
that everyone in the room
will remember for the rest of their lives.

## Yearbook
It has breathed for people who recovered
and went home and forgot
what it felt like to have a machine
breathe for them.
It has breathed for people
who did not recover.
It has run through nights
when only the nurse was in the room
the family asked to go home, to sleep,
to take care of themselves
and the machine breathed steadily
into the empty chair.
It has been the thing
that a family member heard from the corridor
before they came in
that rhythm, that sound,
telling them the person was still here.
It has been disconnected.
More than once.
By more than one set of hands.
Always with care.
Always with the nurses present.
The room is never the same
after the sound stops.
