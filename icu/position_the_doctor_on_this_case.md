# Position: The Doctor On This Case
## The clinician for whom this patient has stopped being only a case.

## Owner
- **Project:** ICU
- **Process:** [process_the_morning_shift](process_the_morning_shift.md)
- **Process:** [process_the_night_shift](process_the_night_shift.md)
- **Place:** [The ICU Unit](place_the_icu_unit.md)

## Has
The accumulated clinical knowledge
of one patient across multiple days.
It exists before this persona arrived
built from every round,
every blood result,
every conversation with the family,
every small change in the chart
that only makes sense
in the context of the days before it.
The position knows this patient
in a way the rounding doctor cannot
and the incoming nurse has not yet.
It is the most complete clinical picture
in the room.

## Orders
Hold the clinical standard.
The position does not order
the persona to become involved.
It orders them to be present
with complete knowledge
and make decisions based on it.
The involvement arrives anyway.
The position does not prevent this.
It does not have a mechanism for preventing it.

## Loses
The clinical distance.
They were certain they could hold
complete knowledge of a patient
without the knowledge becoming personal.
The number of days takes it.
Day one: a clinical picture.
Day five: a clinical picture
with a family attached to it.
Day ten: a clinical picture
that they think about
on the drive home.
The caring is still clinical.
The clinical is no longer only clinical.
The position has no name for this.
It is not in any protocol.
It is the most common thing
that happens to the doctors
who stay on a case long enough.

## Drives
On the loss of clinical distance
stays longer than the round requires.
Answers questions the rounding doctor
did not have time to answer.
Comes back in the evening
when the schedule does not require it.
Does not document why.
