# Piece: The Bed
## The thing the world runs on.

## Owner
- **Project:** ICU
- **Place:** [The ICU Room](place_the_icu_room.md)

## Place
[The ICU Room](place_the_icu_room.md).
Centre. Always centre.
Everything in the room
is arranged around it.

## Load Bearing
Without it there is no ICU room.
Without it there is no vigil.
Without it the family has no place to gather,
no monitor to watch,
no chair to sit beside.
The bed is the reason
every person in this world
is in this room.
Remove it and the world has no engine.
It does not move.
It does not speak.
It holds the person
the room is built around.

## Apparent
A hospital bed. Raised. Adjustable.
Rails on the sides.
White sheets changed by the nurses
with a care that is practical and also tender.
Tubes. Lines. The ventilator if there is one —
its rhythm the room's rhythm,
not the patient's anymore.
The person in it is the person they were
and also something else now —
something that requires all of this
to continue.
Both are true simultaneously.
The bed holds both without resolving them.

## Yearbook
It has held people who left it walking.
It has held people who did not leave it at all.
It has held people while their families
sat beside it for days
and said everything
and said nothing
and held the hand in it
and let go of the hand in it.
Every vigil this room has ever held
was held beside this bed
or one exactly like it.
The bed does not distinguish.
It holds whoever is placed in it
with the same white sheets
and the same rails
and the same tubes
if tubes are needed.
It has been the last thing
many people ever saw.
It has been the first thing
many people saw when they woke.
The sheets are always clean.
That is all it promises.
