# Piece: The ICU Diary
## The things that cannot be said aloud, written down anyway.

## Owner
- **Project:** ICU
- **Place:** [The ICU Room](place_the_icu_room.md)

## Place
[The ICU Room](place_the_icu_room.md).
On the small table beside the [Bed](piece_the_bed.md).
With a pen beside it.
The pen is always beside it.

## Load Bearing
Without it the things that cannot be said aloud
have nowhere to go.
The room is full of things
that cannot be said aloud
to the patient, because they may not hear;
to each other, because the hearing
would cost too much.
The diary receives them.
It does not require the writer
to say them to anyone.
It holds them until the patient
can read them, if they can
or holds them after.
Remove the diary and the room
keeps everything inside the people.
That is not sustainable.
That is not its function.

## Apparent
A notebook. Plain cover usually.
Sometimes brought by a nurse
who has seen what a diary does
for the families who keep one.
The handwriting changes
depending on who wrote the entry
different people, different days,
different hours of the night
when the corridor was quiet
and someone needed to say something
to the person in the bed
without disturbing the room.
Some entries are long.
Some are one line.
Some are a name, written,
because the writer needed
to write the name
to confirm the person was still real.

## Yearbook
It has received things
that had never been said in thirty years
of knowing someone.
It has received ordinary things
*the weather today, the dog is fine,
your sister called*
because ordinary things were
the only anchor available.
It has been read by a patient
who did not know anyone had written in it
and who sat with it for a long time
after reading.
It has been kept after.
It has been given to children
who were too young to be at the bedside
so they could write something
to their person from home.
It has been the place
where someone wrote *I forgive you*
before they were ready to say it.
Whether they were ever ready to say it
is not recorded.
The diary holds the writing.
What happened after is elsewhere.
