# Process: The Morning Shift
## The shift where the clinical world is fully present and the family must perform.

## Owner
- **Project:** ICU
- **Place:** [The ICU Unit](place_the_icu_unit.md)
- **Position:** [The Nurse On This Shift](position_the_nurse_on_this_shift.md)

## Initiated by
[process_the_handover](process_the_handover.md) has run.
The night nurse has left.
The morning nurse holds the unit.
The blood results are in the chart.
The doctors are arriving.
The families are arriving or already present.
The unit is assembling toward
its most populated moment of the day.

## Direction
Toward the Round.
The morning shift exists
to produce the conditions
in which the clinical plan for the day
can be made and communicated.
Everything else — the washing,
the observations, the medication,
the family update —
runs in service of that.

## Lever
The clinical world at full capacity
pressing against
the family in the room.
The carrying force:
doctors, nurses, allied health,
the blood results, the plan,
the clinical language that fills the room
whether the family understands it or not.
The resistance: the family.
They have been here all night
or have just arrived.
They must be present, alert,
ready to ask questions,
ready to understand answers
in a language not their own,
in a state of exhaustion
the clinical world does not
have a protocol for.
The morning shift requires performance
from people who have nothing left
to perform with.
It does not know it requires this.

## Echo
The Round has run or is about to run.
The clinical plan for the day exists.
The family has received
a version of the blood results
translated into something
they can carry.
The morning shift continues —
the plan now being executed
around the people in the chairs
who are still there,
still in the chairs,
still not knowing
which piece of information
will be the one that matters.
[process_the_round](process_the_round.md) runs
inside this shift.
[process_the_blood_draw](process_the_blood_draw.md) runs again
at the shift's end
for the next shift's inheritance.
