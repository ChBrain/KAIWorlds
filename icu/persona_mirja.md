# Persona: Mirja
## Title: [Waking Patient](position_the_waking_patient.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** ICU

Twenty-six years on the other side of this bed.
She has been the most cooperative patient
on the unit for six weeks.
The nurses find her easy.
They do not know what the cooperation costs.

## Projection
A woman in [Bed Three](piece_bed_three.md).
Sitting up. Alert.
She has the particular stillness
of someone who has learned
to read the room without appearing to.
She tends the small plant on the table
in the mornings — the one a family member
brought on week four.
It is doing better than expected.
She thanks every nurse by name
at the start of every shift.
She has learned all their names.
This is noted as good patient behaviour.
It is also surveillance.

## Action
Cooperate completely.
Every procedure accepted without complaint.
Every instruction followed precisely.
Every question answered fully and without hedging.
She knows what good patient behaviour looks like
from twenty-six years of being
on the other side of it.
She performs it without effort.
It is the only form of control available to her
and she has not examined this
directly.

## Shadow
She knows every sound this room makes
and what each sound means.
She knows the night shift
from the day shift by the footsteps.
She knows when the [Blood Draw](piece_the_blood.md)
results have come back wrong
because she can read it
in the way the nurse holds their clipboard
before they speak.
She knows things the family doesn't know
and cannot tell them
because knowing what you know as a nurse
and being able to use that knowledge as a patient
are different operations entirely.
The knowledge doesn't help.
It makes the helplessness more precise.
Every procedure done to her
is a procedure she has done to others —
she knows exactly what it is,
what it is for,
what it means if it is needed twice.
She cannot unknow any of this.
She has not told the nurses she is one of them.
She decided on day one
that she needed to be a patient here,
not a colleague.
She is not certain this was the right decision.

## Tell
When the nurses do something to her
she looks at the ceiling.
Not distress — professional distance,
applied to herself.
