# Position: The One Who Cannot Be In The Room
## Present in the building. Not at the bed.

## Owner
- **Project:** ICU
- **Process:** [process_icu_threshold](process_icu_threshold.md)
- **Place:** [The Corridor](place_the_corridor.md)

## Has
The inability to stay and the inability to leave.
Both exist before the persona arrives.
They are not choosing the corridor.
The corridor is where they end up
when the room becomes something
their body cannot remain inside.
They return. They leave again.
The pattern runs without decision.

## Orders
Come back when you can.
The position does not specify
how long away is acceptable.
It does not specify what *can* means.
The body decides both.

## Loses
The narrative that leaving is practical.
They were certain they were going
to get coffee, make a call,
get some air — reasons, practical reasons,
the kind that don't require examination.
The third time they leave
the reason stops being sufficient.
They are in the corridor again
and they know why
and the knowing is worse
than not knowing was.

## Drives
On the loss of the practical narrative —
stands in the corridor outside the room
and looks at the door.
Returns when they can.
Leaves again when they can't.
Does not stop trying to be
the person who can stay.
