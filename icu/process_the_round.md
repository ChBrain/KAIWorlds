# Process: The Round
## The clinical language that fills the room whether the family understands it or not.

## Owner
- **Project:** ICU
- **Process:** [process_the_morning_shift](process_the_morning_shift.md)
- **Place:** [The ICU Unit](place_the_icu_unit.md)
- **Position:** [The Nurse On This Shift](position_the_nurse_on_this_shift.md)

## Initiated by
The morning shift is running.
The [Blood Draw](process_the_blood_draw.md) results are in the chart.
The clinical team is assembled
doctors, nurses, allied health.
The family is present or has been called.
The team moves from room to room.
This room is next.

## Direction
Toward the clinical plan for the day.
What the body needs.
What will be done.
What the numbers mean
and what will be adjusted because of them.
The Round is a clinical mechanism.
The family is adjacent to it.
Their presence is encouraged.
Their understanding is not guaranteed.

## Lever
The clinical picture
pressing against
the family's capacity to receive it.
The carrying force:
the team, the chart, the results,
the clinical language
precise, efficient, not designed
for people who have been awake
for thirty-six hours.
The resistance: the family.
They are present.
They are trying.
They understand some of what is said
and not other parts
and cannot always tell which is which.
They have questions.
The Round has a schedule.
The questions that fit the schedule
get answered.
The questions that don't
get carried home
and typed into a search engine at midnight
or asked of the nurse
in a corridor later
in a different form
than they were first shaped.

## Echo
The clinical plan exists.
The family has received
what the Round could give them.
The nurse carries the rest
the questions that came after,
the thing the family member said
when the team had already moved on.
The Round is complete.
The [Morning Shift](process_the_morning_shift.md)
executes the plan.
[process_the_procedure](process_the_procedure.md)
runs if the plan requires it.
