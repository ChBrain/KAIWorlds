# Process: The Procedure
## The family expelled. The body worked on. The room changed when they return.

## Owner
- **Project:** ICU
- **Place:** [The ICU Unit](place_the_icu_unit.md)
- **Position:** [The Nurse On This Shift](position_the_nurse_on_this_shift.md)

## Initiated by
The clinical plan requires something
that cannot be done with the family present.
The [Nurse On This Shift](position_the_nurse_on_this_shift.md)
asks the family to wait outside.
The ask is polite.
It is not a question.
The family goes to [The Corridor](place_the_corridor.md).
The door closes.

## Direction
Toward the procedure complete.
The clinical team works.
The family waits.
The direction is clinical — toward the body
needing something done to it
and the thing being done.
The family's direction
is simply: away, until called back.

## Lever
The clinical necessity
pressing against
the family's need to be present.
The carrying force: the procedure —
it requires space, sterility,
the specific conditions
under which clinical work
can be done correctly.
The resistance: the family
who must leave the room
containing the person they love
and stand in a corridor
not knowing what is happening
to that person right now.
They were told it was necessary.
They were not told what necessary feels like
from the corridor side.
The door does not tell them anything.
The sounds that come through it
are not informative.
They wait.

## Echo
The procedure is complete.
The family is called back.
The room has changed —
a new line, a new position,
something adjusted, something added.
The family enters a room
that has been worked on
in their absence.
They did not see what happened.
They will not be told every detail.
The change is simply present
and they accommodate it
the way they have accommodated
every other change in this room —
by continuing to be here.
