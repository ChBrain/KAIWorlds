# Piece: The Chair
## The thing that makes the staying possible and visible.

## Owner
- **Project:** ICU
- **Place:** [The ICU Room](place_the_icu_room.md)

## Place
[The ICU Room](place_the_icu_room.md).
Beside the [Bed](piece_the_bed.md).
Closer than any other furniture.
The distance between the chair and the bed
is the distance between
the person who is here
and the person in it.

## Load Bearing
Without it the staying has no form.
You can stand beside the bed.
People do. For the first hour,
sometimes the second.
But the vigil requires the chair.
The chair is what transforms
presence into endurance.
It is also what makes the staying visible
to the nurses, to the other family,
to the person in the bed
if they can see it.
The empty chair is also load-bearing.
When the chair is empty
the room knows someone has left.

## Apparent
Plastic, usually. Or vinyl.
The kind of chair that does not invite sleep
but permits it.
Armrests worn at the edges.
The seat slightly compressed
from continuous use.
Someone before this vigil
sat in this chair for a long time.
Someone before them did too.
The chair holds the shape
of everyone who has stayed.
A pillow from home, sometimes,
placed on the seat.
A coat folded over the back.
These are information.

## Yearbook
It has been slept in and wept in
and sat in for hours without moving.
It has been moved closer to the bed
by people who needed to hold a hand
without standing.
It has been moved to the wall
by nurses who needed access
and moved back after.
It has been the place where
someone received the news
that the news had changed
better, or worse
and sat very still
while their mind caught up.
It has been left with a coat on it
by someone who stepped out for coffee
and needed the coat to say
*I am coming back.*
The coat always meant that.
The nurses always understood.
