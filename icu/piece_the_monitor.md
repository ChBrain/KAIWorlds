# Piece: The Monitor
## The thing that speaks when the patient cannot.

## Owner
- **Project:** ICU
- **Place:** [The ICU Room](place_the_icu_room.md)

## Place
[The ICU Room](place_the_icu_room.md).
Above and behind the [Bed](piece_the_bed.md).
Visible from the [Chair](piece_the_chair.md).
Visible from the door.
Visible from everywhere in the room
because that is its function.

## Load Bearing
Without it the vigil has no language.
The patient cannot report their own condition.
The monitor reports it for them —
in numbers, in lines, in the specific sound
of a heart that is still beating.
Remove the monitor and the family
has no information except what they can see
with their own eyes,
which is not enough
and also too much.
The monitor mediates between
what is happening inside the patient
and what the people in the room
are able to receive.
It does not do this gently.

## Apparent
A screen. Numbers in different colours.
A line tracing the heart —
the green line that the room
learns to read without wanting to.
The sound: regular, metronomic,
present in every silence.
When it changes, everyone hears it.
When it alarms, the room stops.
The nurses read it differently
than the family reads it.
Both are reading it continuously.
Neither can stop.

## Yearbook
It has been watched for hours
by people who did not understand
what they were watching
and watched anyway.
It has been the first thing
people looked at when they entered the room
before they looked at the person in the bed.
It has been the thing that alarmed
in the night when only one person was there
and that person learned in that moment
what kind of person they were.
It has been looked at on a phone —
the numbers, what do the numbers mean,
typed into a search engine at 3am
in the corridor outside —
by The One Who Manages,
who needed to know
and could not stop needing to know.
The monitor has been right and wrong
and right again.
It has continued running
after the person in the bed
no longer needed it to.
It was turned off then.
The silence after was different
from any silence before it.
