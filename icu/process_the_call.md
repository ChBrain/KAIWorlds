# Process: The Call
## The gap between what was said and what was heard.

## Owner
- **Project:** ICU
- **Place:** [The ICU Unit](place_the_icu_unit.md)
- **Piece:** [The Phone](piece_the_phone.md)

## Initiated by
Something has happened to the patient.
Someone must be told.
The [Phone](piece_the_phone.md) is the mechanism.
The person receiving the call
is somewhere else entirely
in a meeting, in a car, making dinner,
living the life they were living
before this call existed.
The call ends that life
and begins another one
in the same moment.

## Direction
Toward the family in the room.
The call is not the destination.
The call is the force
that moves people from wherever they were
to the bedside.
What they heard on the call
travels with them.
It arrives at the bedside before they do.

## Lever
What was said pressing against
what was understood.
The carrying force: the words used
*critical, serious, you should come*
clinical language that does not carry
the weight of what it means.
The resistance: what the person receiving the call
needed the words to mean
in order to survive the drive.
The gap between those two things
is what arrives at the bedside.
It does not close when the person enters the room.
The room makes it larger.

## Echo
The person is in transit or has arrived.
They are carrying a version of events
that is partly accurate
and partly what they needed to believe
to get here.
The admission will correct it.
The correction is its own event.
[process_the_admission](process_the_admission.md)
receives what the call sent.
