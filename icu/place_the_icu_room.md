# Place: The ICU Room
## Location: The intensive care unit. The room the world has contracted to.

## Owner
- **Project:** ICU
- **Place:** [The ICU Unit](place_the_icu_unit.md)

## Shown
- A room smaller than it should be
  for everything it contains.
- The bed. One per room.
  An instance of [The Bed](piece_the_bed.md).
  Centre. Always centre.
  Rails up or down depending on the patient.
  White sheets. The person in it.
- [The Monitor](piece_the_monitor.md).
  Above and behind the bed.
  Its sound: present in every silence.
  The green line that the room learns to read.
- [The Chair](piece_the_chair.md). Beside the bed.
  Closer than anything else.
  A pillow from home on it, sometimes.
  A coat over the back, sometimes.
- [The ICU Diary](piece_the_icu_diary.md).
  On the small table. Pen beside it.
- [The Thing Brought From Home](piece_the_thing_from_home.md).
  On the windowsill or the table.
  The only object that belongs to the person in the bed.
- The window. Small. Unreliable light.
  It shows sky or a wall or nothing useful.
  It does not tell you what time it is outside.
- The door to the corridor.
  Always present. Never locked.
  It admits cold air when it opens.
  The corridor is visible through it
  but feels like a different world.
- The nurses' station beyond the glass.
  Visible. The nurses move there
  with a purposefulness that the family
  cannot always read.
- [The Ventilator](piece_the_ventilator.md) if the patient needs it.
  Beside the bed. Connected.
  The room's rhythm, not the patient's.

## Holds
- The bed assigned to this room
  an instance of [The Bed](piece_the_bed.md)
- [The Chair](piece_the_chair.md)
- [The Monitor](piece_the_monitor.md)
- [The Ventilator](piece_the_ventilator.md) when present
- [The ICU Diary](piece_the_icu_diary.md)
- [The Thing Brought From Home](piece_the_thing_from_home.md)

## Offers
- [The Chair](piece_the_chair.md). To whoever will sit in it and stay.
- [The ICU Diary](piece_the_icu_diary.md). To whoever needs to write something they cannot say.
- [The Thing Brought From Home](piece_the_thing_from_home.md). To whoever needs to hold something real.
- The door. To whoever needs to leave.
  It does not require explanation.

## Withheld
- Whether the person in the bed
  can hear what is said to them.
  The nurses say: assume they can.
  The room does not confirm this.
  The room holds the question open
  for the duration of every vigil
  and releases it only when
  the vigil is over.
  By then it is too late
  to use the answer.
