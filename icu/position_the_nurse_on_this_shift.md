# Position: The Nurse On This Shift
## The one who has been in this room with other families doing this same thing.

## Owner
- **Project:** ICU
- **Process:** [process_the_morning_shift](process_the_morning_shift.md)
- **Process:** [process_the_evening_shift](process_the_evening_shift.md)
- **Process:** [process_the_night_shift](process_the_night_shift.md)
- **Place:** [The ICU Unit](place_the_icu_unit.md)

## Has
The knowledge of how this usually goes.
It exists before this family arrived
and before this patient arrived.
The position carries the weight
of every previous vigil
this nurse has been present for.
The family cannot see this weight.
That is correct. That is the job.

## Orders
Care for the patient.
Care for the family.
Do not let either see
what the other costs.
The position does not acknowledge
that these are three separate demands
dressed as one.

## Loses
The neutrality of gratitude.
They were certain they cared for families
because it was right.
They did. That was true.
But somewhere in the accumulated vigils
they began collecting moments of gratitude
a family's thank you, a hand briefly held,
the specific look of relief
when they explain something clearly
and using those moments as fuel
for the next shift.
The caring is real.
The collection is also real.
They cannot separate them now.
They have not tried.
The shift requires both.

## Drives
On the loss of neutral caring
finds the family member
who most needs something explained
and explains it.
Clearly. Carefully.
Stays a moment longer than required.
Receives the gratitude.
Files it for later.
Returns to the patient.
