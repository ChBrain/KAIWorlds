# Process: The Goals of Care Conversation
## The family asked to speak for someone who cannot speak.

## Owner
- **Project:** ICU
- **Process:** [process_the_morning_shift](process_the_morning_shift.md)
- **Process:** [process_the_night_shift](process_the_night_shift.md)
- **Place:** [The ICU Room](place_the_icu_room.md)
- **Position:** [The Doctor On This Case](position_the_doctor_on_this_case.md)

## Initiated by
The clinical picture has reached
a point that requires a conversation
it has been approaching for some time.
The [Doctor On This Case](position_the_doctor_on_this_case.md)
has requested the family present.
The [One Who Manages](position_the_one_who_manages.md)
has made the calls.
The family is assembled —
or as assembled as it can be.
The door to [The ICU Room](place_the_icu_room.md) is closed.
The [Monitor](piece_the_monitor.md) is running.
The [Bed](piece_the_bed.md) holds the patient.
The room holds everyone.

## Direction
Toward the family understanding
what the clinical world already knows
and being asked what to do with it.
Not toward a decision —
toward the conditions
in which a decision can be made.
The conversation does not force the decision.
It makes the decision unavoidable.

## Lever
The clinical reality
pressing against
the family's knowledge of who this person was.
The carrying force:
what the [Blood](piece_the_blood.md) has been reporting,
what the [Chart](piece_the_chart.md) carries,
what the clinical team has been reading
in the numbers every morning —
named now, clearly, without softening,
because the moment for softening has passed.
The resistance:
every image the family has
of this person before this room.
Their voice. Their opinions.
What they said once about hospitals,
about machines, about this exact situation —
or did not say, which is its own weight.
The family arrived for a round.
They are now in a different conversation.
The transition between those two things
is not announced.
It becomes visible only when
the clinical language shifts register —
when the doctor stops discussing
what is being done
and begins discussing
what might stop being done.
The family may not know
the register has changed
until they are already inside it.
The family is being asked
to speak for someone
who cannot speak
and who may not have known
they would need to be spoken for.
What this person would have wanted
is not in any file.
It is in the people in this room
who loved them.
It is the only place it exists.

## Echo
The conversation has happened.
What was decided — or not yet decided —
is now in the room.
The clinical team has a direction.
The family has said something
they cannot unsay.
The room holds that for a moment —
the clinical team still present,
the family still present,
the [Monitor](piece_the_monitor.md) still running,
the [Bed](piece_the_bed.md) still occupied —
before anyone moves toward the next thing.
That moment has no name in any protocol.
It is the weight of the irreversible
before it becomes the past.
The [ICU Diary](piece_the_icu_diary.md) may receive
what cannot be said aloud
after the clinical team has left.
The room continues.
The [Monitor](piece_the_monitor.md) runs.
What comes next depends on
what was decided.
Both directions — continued treatment
and withdrawal — are held
by the same architecture.
The world does not distinguish between them.
It holds whatever the room requires.
