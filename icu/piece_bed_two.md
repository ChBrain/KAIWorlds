# Piece: Bed Two
## The bed where the waking is happening and the family is holding something back.

## Owner
- **Project:** ICU
- **Place:** [The ICU Room](place_the_icu_room.md)

## Place
[The ICU Room](place_the_icu_room.md).
Centre of the room.
The nurses pass it most frequently
it requires more attention now
that the patient is waking.

## Load Bearing
Without it the [Waking Patient](position_the_waking_patient.md)
position has no place to surface into.
The gap, seventeen days of the room
running without the patient
is anchored here.
The [ICU Diary](piece_the_icu_diary.md) sits on its table.
The family knows what is in it.
The patient does not know it exists yet.
Remove this bed and the gap
has no physical location
it becomes something that happened
somewhere general,
and the architecture cannot work
with somewhere general.

## Apparent
A hospital bed. Rails down now on one side
the patient can move more.
The [Ventilator](piece_the_ventilator.md) has been removed.
There is a water cup with a straw.
The photograph is still on the windowsill.
The chair pulled close.
The [ICU Diary](piece_the_icu_diary.md) on the table.

## Yearbook
It has been the whole world
of one person for seventeen days.
The ceiling they could not see
for the first days, sedated, under.
The ceiling they began to see
as the sedation lifted.
The light they tracked
as orientation returned
is it day, is it night,
what direction does the window face.
The hands on their arms
the lines, the draws, the checks.
The first time their own hands
responded to something.
The weight and temperature
of the rails.
The specific sound of their own breathing
when the [Ventilator](piece_the_ventilator.md) was removed
their breath, not the machine's,
uncertain and theirs.
This is what the bed held.
The patient's world as it rebuilt itself
from the ceiling down.

## Withheld
What it holds when it is empty.
The bed cleaned, remade, reset
identical to how it was
before this patient arrived.
The room does not mark the difference.
The unit continues.
Another patient will come
or has already come.
The bed does not distinguish between them.
What was the entire world of one person
is now a bed.
That transition has no duration.
It happens between one thing and the next.
The bed holds that
without being able to say so.
