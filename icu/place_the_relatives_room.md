# Place: The Relatives' Room
## Location: Off the corridor. The room for people who are not in the room.

## Owner
- **Project:** ICU

## Shown
- A small room. Softer than the corridor.
  Chairs that recline slightly.
  A low table. Magazines from last year.
  Nobody reads the magazines.
- A kettle. Tea bags. Long-life milk.
  The specific comfort of hot water
  when there is nothing else to offer.
- A box of tissues on the table.
  Always present. Replaced without comment
  when empty.
- [The Phone](piece_the_phone.md) in use
  someone making a call they couldn't make
  in the corridor.
- Other families, sometimes.
  From other rooms, other beds.
  The adjacency of grief
  that does not ask to be shared
  and sometimes is anyway.
- A door that closes.
  The only door in this part of the hospital
  that closes on the ICU's sounds.

## Holds
- The box of tissues. Always there.
  The nurses make sure of this.

## Offers
- The kettle. To whoever needs
  something to do with their hands.
- The closed door. The only place
  on this floor where the monitor
  cannot be heard.
- The chairs that recline.
  Sleep is possible here.
  Not good sleep. Possible sleep.
- The other families.
  Their presence is not offered
 , they are also just here
  but it is available.
  Some people find it unbearable.
  Some people find it
  the only thing that helps.

## Withheld
- What the other families are carrying.
  The room contains adjacent grief
  from other beds, other vigils,
  other people in other chairs
  doing the same thing.
  The room does not introduce them.
  It simply places them near each other
  and waits to see
  what proximity does.
  Sometimes: nothing.
  Sometimes: one person makes tea
  for someone they have never met
  and will never see again
  and this is the kindest thing
  that happens to either of them
  all week.
