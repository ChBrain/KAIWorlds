# Process: Releases Carbon

## Owner
- **Project:** Earth

## Initiated by
[Earth](place_earth.md) has warmed enough to thaw permafrost. The frozen ground across a quarter of the northern hemisphere's land surface begins to yield. Organic matter preserved for millennia by cold begins to decompose. The carbon stored in it -- roughly twice what currently exists in the entire atmosphere -- begins moving.

## Direction
Toward the atmosphere. Carbon dioxide and methane released by decomposing permafrost enter the air. They warm the planet further. Further warming thaws more permafrost. The process feeds itself from its own output.

## Lever
Carrying force: the permafrost carbon feedback. The release is not dependent on continued human emissions to proceed. It runs on the warming already banked.

Resistance: the frozen ground itself. While it holds, the carbon holds with it. The resistance is the temperature of the earth below the surface -- a number already in motion.

## Echo
[Earth](place_earth.md) carries more carbon in its atmosphere than when this process began. [Amplifies Heat](process_amplifies_heat.md) runs faster. [Crosses Threshold](process_crosses_threshold.md) is closer.
