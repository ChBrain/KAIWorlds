# Piece: The Threshold

## Owner
- **Project:** Earth
- **Process:** [Crosses Threshold](process_crosses_threshold.md)

## Place
[Earth](place_earth.md). Embedded in the system. Not marked.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and [Crosses Threshold](process_crosses_threshold.md) has no hinge. [Amplifies Heat](process_amplifies_heat.md) becomes gradual change without a point of no return. [Releases Carbon](process_releases_carbon.md) and [Collapses Current](process_collapses_current.md) lose their trigger. The tragedy of this world requires that a line exists which, once crossed, ends the argument.

## Apparent
A number on an instrument. A temperature reading. A parts-per-million concentration. A salinity measurement in a column of seawater. A degree of ice loss recorded by a satellite. Present in [The Data](piece_the_data.md) as one value among thousands. Not marked differently from the values before it or after it.

## Yearbook
1.5 degrees Celsius named as the boundary by the Paris Agreement, 2015. Scientists have since identified at least five major tipping elements already at risk below this level. Warm-water coral reefs declared in long-term decline as of Earth Day 2026. The West Antarctic ice sheet assessed as possibly already past its tipping point. The AMOC at its slowest in 1,600 years. Each threshold has a different location. Each has the same structure: approach is measurable, crossing is quiet, consequence arrives late and large.
