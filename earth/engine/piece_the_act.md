# Piece: The Act

## Owner
- **Project:** Earth
- **Process:** [Acts Once](process_acts_once.md)
- **Persona:** [Actor](position_actor.md)

## Place
Where [Actor](position_actor.md) was when they took it. Not where they are now.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and [Acts Once](process_acts_once.md) produces intention without consequence. The world needs one thing that is genuinely irreversible on the human side -- not just on the planetary side. [The Threshold](piece_the_threshold.md) is [Earth](place_earth.md)'s irreversible moment. The Act is the human one. Without it the human processes produce only procedure and knowledge. Nothing enters the world that was not already in it.

## Apparent
A document signed. A switch thrown. A transmission sent. A quantity released or withheld. Physical at the moment of taking -- a body at a specific location performing a specific action. Present tense: already complete. The object or signal that carried it is still in the world. [Earth](place_earth.md) received it.

## Yearbook
No single entry. The Act is a position in a structure, not a historical event. Every version of it shares the same property: it was taken by one person, at one moment, without certainty of outcome, inside a system that would not return the answer on a human schedule. Some acts of this kind have been named later: Rachel Carson publishing Silent Spring, 1962. The technician who shut down the Soviet nuclear warning system in 1983 rather than report a false alarm. Most have not been named. The piece runs regardless.
