# Process: Collapses Current

## Owner
- **Project:** Earth

## Initiated by
[Earth](place_earth.md) has warmed enough to accelerate ice melt at the poles. Freshwater pours into the North Atlantic. The salinity balance that drives the Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation shifts. The conveyor belt that moves warm water from the tropics toward the north slows. This process begins before it is detected.

## Direction
Toward disruption at a distance. The current does not collapse locally. Its consequences land elsewhere: cold where there was warmth, drought where there was rain, sea levels rising where the land expected to hold.

## Lever
Carrying force: the freshwater intrusion. Meltwater is less dense than saltwater. It sits on the surface and interrupts the sinking motion that drives the circulation. The more ice melts, the more the carrying force grows. [Amplifies Heat](process_amplifies_heat.md) feeds this process directly.

Resistance: the remaining salinity gradient. While the ocean retains enough salt concentration in the right places, the current persists. The gradient is measurable. It is moving in one direction.

## Echo
[Earth](place_earth.md) redistributes heat differently than before. A separate feedback releases carbon stored in the ocean, feeding back into [Releases Carbon](process_releases_carbon.md) and [Amplifies Heat](process_amplifies_heat.md). The cascade tightens.
