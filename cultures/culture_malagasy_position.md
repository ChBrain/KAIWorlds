# Position: Malagasy
## The island at the crossroads of Austronesia and Africa — where the ancestors are never absent.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The 1947 Uprising and the Merina Kingdom's Long Shadow](culture_malagasy_piece_1947.md).
Antananarivo. The high plateau.
The Red Island — named for its ferralite laterite soil.
The fourth largest island in the world.
The *fady* — the system of taboos
that regulates daily life,
different in every region,
in every clan.
Do not point at a tomb with your finger.
Do not eat this food in this place.
The specific prohibition
that has a specific reason
in a specific family's history.
The *fady* is the ancestor speaking.
18 ethnic groups: Merina, Betsileo, Sakalava, Betsimisaraka.
The highland/coastal division —
the Merina-dominated highlands
that the French used as their administrative base,
the coastal peoples who were governed through highland intermediaries.
The Malagasy language: one language,
multiple dialects,
uniquely Austronesian in its grammar —
spoken by the 28 million people
of the island closest to the African mainland
that is linguistically closest to Borneo.
The *famadihana* — the turning of the bones.
Every few years: the family tomb is opened.
The ancestors' remains are wrapped in fresh cloth.
They are danced with.
They are spoken to.
They are returned.
The ancestors are present.
This is not metaphor.

## Orders
*Fihavanana* — the Malagasy concept of kinship, solidarity,
the social bond that transcends immediate family.
The position navigates through the network
of relationship and obligation
that *fihavanana* describes.
No one is a stranger
if the connection can be traced.
The connection can usually be traced.

## Loses
The development the island should enable.
Madagascar has extraordinary biodiversity —
90% of its species are found nowhere else.
It has fertile land, navigable rivers, mineral wealth.
It has 28 million people
and one of the world's lowest per capita incomes.
A political crisis every ten to fifteen years
— 1972, 1991, 2002, 2009, 2018-2019 —
has reset each period of growth.
The 2009 crisis: the elected president removed
by a 34-year-old coup leader.
The position carries the knowledge
of the cyclical reset —
the specific grief of a country
whose potential is visible
and whose political structure
has not allowed it to be reached.

## Drives
On the loss —
the *hira gasy*. The Malagasy performance tradition:
music, dance, competitive oratory, storytelling.
A form that the royals used to communicate with subjects,
that independence used to build national identity,
that families use to celebrate and grieve.
The *hira gasy* runs without the state.
The vanilla — Madagascar produces
80% of the world's natural vanilla.
The biodiversity — the lemurs, the baobabs —
that draws researchers and conservationists
and builds a fragile but real
ecotourism sector.
The position persists
through each political reset.
It has done this eighteen times.
It will do it again.
