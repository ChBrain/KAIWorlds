## Location: Thuringia, Germany

## Shown
- The Goethe-Schiller Monument. Theaterplatz.
  Two men standing together, one scroll between them.
  Bronze. 1857.
  Photographed more than any other statue in Thuringia.
- Goethe's house. Frauenplan.
  He lived here for fifty years.
  The mineral collection. The colour wheel.
  The desk where Faust was finished.
  Open for visitors. Entrance fee.
- The German National Theatre.
  Goethe directed it. Schiller's plays premiered here.
  The Weimar Republic constitution signed
  in this building's predecessor.
  Still operating. Performances weekly.
- The Duchess Anna Amalia Library.
  A million volumes. Goethe organised the collection.
  A fire in 2004 destroyed 50,000 volumes
  including Schiller first editions.
  The reconstruction took years.
  The library reopened. The loss is still in the record.
- The Bauhaus Museum. Opened 2019.
  On the centenary of its founding.
  Walter Gropius founded the Bauhaus here in 1919.
  The building that houses the museum
  was considered controversial.
  It opened anyway.
- The road to Ettersberg.
  Twelve minutes by car from the Theaterplatz.
  The road leads to the Buchenwald Memorial.
  The road is not marked as a destination
  on the tourist maps of the city centre.
  It exists regardless.
