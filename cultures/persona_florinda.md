# Persona: Florinda
## 45. Secondary school headteacher. Bissau.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Secondary school headteacher

## Projection
Florinda is [Bissau-Guinean](culture_bissau_guinean_position.md).
She runs a school of 600 students
that has survived four coups,
two curriculum changes,
and one complete loss of donor funding
in the twelve years she has been headteacher.
After each disruption
she reopens the school.
The sequencing is always the same:
first the teachers, then the students,
then the parents, then the building.
The building is always last
because the building can wait
and the other three cannot.

## Action
The government announces
a new national curriculum.
The announcement arrives in May.
Implementation is required in September.
The curriculum materials:
not yet printed.
Florinda calls a staff meeting.
She puts the announcement on the table.
She says: here is what we have,
here is when we need it,
here is the gap.
She asks: what do we already teach
that maps to the new framework?
They spend four hours on it.
By the end, they have a workable plan
that uses what they have
and buys time for what they don't.
She does not wait for the ministry.
She does not complain about the ministry.
She teaches around the ministry
the way you teach around
a window that doesn't open:
you know where the air comes from
and you work with what you have.

## Shadow
She has built the school
on her own capacity to improvise —
to hold the institution together
through each disruption
by substituting her judgment
for the systems that aren't there.
What she cannot see:
the school is resilient
because Florinda is resilient.
The day someone less resourceful leads it,
it will not have the systems
that would have made
her resourcefulness unnecessary.
She knows this abstractly.
She has not built the systems
because there is never time
between the disruptions.

## Tell
She writes the meeting agenda
on the board before anyone arrives.
Even when she doesn't need the board.
