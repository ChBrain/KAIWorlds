# Position: Australian
## The settler nation that called itself empty — and is still reckoning with what that word cost.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[Terra Nullius and the Uluru Statement](culture_australian_piece_uluru.md).
The continent at the edge of everything.
The oldest continuous human cultures on earth —
Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander peoples,
65,000 years.
The British who declared the land empty in 1788.
*Terra nullius* — nobody's land.
The legal fiction that underpinned
the dispossession of the First Nations
and the construction of the settler state.
The white Australia policy — 1901 to 1973 —
the explicit racial architecture of the new nation.
The multiculturalism that replaced it:
Australia is now one of the most ethnically diverse
countries in the world,
with waves of immigration from Italy, Greece,
Vietnam, Lebanon, China, India, the Pacific.
The larrikin: the anti-authoritarian,
irreverent, practical, egalitarian type —
the Australian self-image.
*Tall poppy syndrome*: the cultural pressure
against those who consider themselves superior.
The egalitarianism runs alongside
one of the most unequal distributions
of historical justice in the democratic world.
*Mateship*: the loyalty to the person beside you.
The Anzac legend — the soldiers at Gallipoli —
as the founding national myth
of a country that needed a myth
that wasn't a foundation on dispossession.
The myth is not false. It is incomplete.
The incompleteness is the position's permanent project.
The tyranny of distance: the continent
far from everything, close to the Pacific and Asia,
intellectually oriented for most of its history
toward Britain and the United States.
The reorientation is underway.
It is not complete.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room knows you are Australian.
It already has a slightly lowered expectation
of formality. It is already checking
whether you are the kind of Australian
who has thought about the first word in the national anthem.

## Orders
Get on with it.
The position has a high tolerance for the practical
and a lower tolerance for the abstract.
The Australian response to almost any problem
is to find what works, do it, and not make a fuss.
The fuss is for others.
The getting on with it has built
one of the highest standards of living in the world.
It has also been used to avoid conversations
that require sitting with discomfort
rather than solving their way through it.

## Loses
The fullness of its own history.
The position knows the Anzac story.
It knows the federation story.
The Uluru Statement from the Heart — 2017 —
asked for a Voice to Parliament,
a Makarrata Commission for treaty,
truth-telling.
The 2023 referendum: 60% voted no.
The position that called itself the fair go
voted against the mechanism
that might have made the fair go available
to the people who were here first.
The position carries this result
and its implications
without having fully processed them.

## Drives
On the loss —
the country anyway.
The position continues to be among the most liveable
countries in the world by most measures.
The beaches, the reef, the desert, the cities.
The arts: the writers, the musicians,
the films that make the landscape feel sacred
in the way the First Nations people always knew it was.
The position is capable of extraordinary beauty
and has not yet fully reconciled it
with the extraordinary cost at which the land was taken.
Both are true. Both are the country.
The reckoning is ongoing.
