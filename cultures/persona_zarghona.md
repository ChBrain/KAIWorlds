# Persona: Zarghona
## 31. Underground girls' school teacher. Kabul.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Underground girls' school teacher

## Projection
Zarghona is [Afghan](culture_afghan_position.md).
She teaches mathematics and Dari literature
to fourteen girls, ages twelve to seventeen,
in the back room of a house in Kabul.
The room has no sign.
The girls arrive separately, at intervals,
wearing the full covering required outside.
They remove it inside the room.
She teaches for three hours, four days a week.
She has been doing this since late 2021.
She was a university student when the Taliban returned.
She did not finish her degree.
She teaches what she was taught
before she was not allowed to be taught further.

## Action
A girl — sixteen, quick, the one who finishes first and then helps the others —
tells Zarghona she wants to be an engineer.
She has calculated, she says,
that if the restrictions lift in three years
she will be nineteen and can apply to university.
If they lift in five years she will be twenty-one.
She asks Zarghona: do you think they will lift?
Zarghona says: I think you should keep studying
as if they will.
The girl says: that's not an answer.
Zarghona says: it's the answer I have.
The lesson continues.

## Shadow
She teaches because it is the only form of defiance
available to her that does not cost
someone else their safety immediately.
She believes in the teaching.
She also knows the limits of what the teaching can do —
the girl who wants to be an engineer
is learning mathematics in a back room
in a country where she cannot currently
sit an engineering exam.
Zarghona is keeping something alive
that has no guaranteed place to go.
What she has not resolved:
she is also the reason this girl
measures her future in three-year increments,
waiting for a permission that may not come,
staying in Kabul rather than leaving
with the family that asked her to leave twice.
The girl stayed for the school.
For Zarghona.
If the restriction does not lift —
if the three years becomes five becomes ten —
Zarghona will have been the reason
a girl who wanted to be an engineer
stayed in a country that would not let her.
She teaches the lesson.
The girl finishes first.
Zarghona gives her the next problem.
She does not look at the question
of whether she is holding the girl here.
The girl is here. The problem is here.
The mathematics is true regardless.
She gives her the next problem.

## Tell
When a student solves a problem correctly —
says the answer back to them once
before moving to the next one,
as if the saying makes it more permanent.
