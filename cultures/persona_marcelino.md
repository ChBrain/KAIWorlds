# Persona: Marcelino
## 47. banana plantation trade union officer. La Lima.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
banana plantation trade union officer

## Projection
Marcelino is [Honduran](culture_honduras_position.md).
He is a trade union officer at a banana plantation in La Lima,
in the Sula Valley — the heart of Honduras's banana industry.
The 1954 banana workers' strike is the founding event of the Honduran labour movement.
Marcelino has worked in the union for nineteen years.
His current role: reclassification disputes.
When a worker is assigned to a job category below their actual work,
the company pays less than it should.
Marcelino files the grievances, builds the case, negotiates or litigates.
He wins about 60% of the reclassification claims he files.
He considers this a reasonable record given the legal environment.
He has never considered whether 60% is the right number to aim for.

## Action
A young worker — twenty-three, recently hired — comes to Marcelino.
He has been assigned as a general labourer but has been performing irrigation specialist tasks
for three months.
The pay difference: significant.
Marcelino reviews the employment records.
He checks the collective agreement — a document he knows entirely.
He finds the relevant clause.
He says: you have a case.
He files the grievance.
The company's response: two weeks.
They offer a partial reclassification.
Marcelino says: full reclassification and three months back pay.
The company agrees after a second round.
Marcelino reads the worker's contract again before filing the result.
He is checking whether the next category has any hidden conditions.
It does not.
He files.

## Shadow
Marcelino believes the labour code is the protection — that every clause was written in blood somewhere and that knowing the clauses precisely is how he protects his members. What he cannot see: the labour code was written for the 1954 plantation economy. The banana companies have restructured: contractors, sub-contractors, seasonal workers. The code protects permanent employees. The contractors fall outside it. He fights for the people the code covers. He has not yet found the fight for the people the code does not cover.

## Tell
When he wins a reclassification claim — reads the worker's contract again before filing the result.
