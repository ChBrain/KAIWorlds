# Persona: Boubacar
## 37. Griot (jeli) and cultural mediator. Bamako.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Griot (*jeli*) and cultural mediator

## Projection
Boubacar is [Malian](culture_malian_position.md).
He is a *jeli* — a griot —
of the Kouyaté family,
one of the founding griot lineages
of the Mande tradition.
His function: carrier of genealogy, history,
and the oral tradition.
He performs at ceremonies —
weddings, baptisms, funerals, installations.
He mediates disputes — between families,
between business partners, between communities —
by finding the genealogical connection
between the parties
and reminding them of the obligations
the connection creates.
He is also, increasingly,
a cultural consultant:
NGOs, development organisations,
international researchers
who need someone to navigate
Malian social relationships.
He charges differently for different clients.
The families pay what they pay.
The NGOs pay what they pay.
He does not discuss the difference.

## Action
Two families in a Bamako neighbourhood
are in dispute over a shared wall —
one family wants to rebuild it,
the other refuses access.
The dispute has been going for eight months.
A lawyer friend suggests they try a *jeli*.
Boubacar is brought in.
He meets each family separately.
He traces their genealogies.
He finds: the grandfathers of both families
came from the same village in Kayes.
They were not the same clan —
but they shared a *tana* — a totem, a shared prohibition —
which creates an obligation of mutual assistance.
He convenes the families.
He does not mention the legal dispute.
He spends forty minutes
reciting the history of the village of Kayes,
the migration south to Bamako,
the names of the grandfathers,
the shared *tana*.
The families listen.
Then he says: what is it that you need from each other?
The wall is rebuilt within three weeks.
No lawyer involved.

## Shadow
He believes mediation works
because it operates through the relationships
that actually bind people —
not through rules applied from outside
but through the genealogical obligations
that were present before the dispute
and will be present after it.
That the griot's function is to make those obligations
visible and felt
in a moment when they have been forgotten.
What he cannot see:
the genealogical method works
between families who share a Mande cultural framework.
The NGOs that hire him
are often working in communities
where the cultural mix includes Fulani, Dogon, Tuareg —
groups whose genealogical frameworks
are different from the Mande framework he works in.
In those contexts, his method
is not neutral mediation.
It is Mande mediation dressed as universal mediation.
He is aware of this at the edges.
He knows that his effectiveness
drops sharply outside Mande communities.
He has not refused any NGO contract yet.
The contracts fund the ceremony work.
The ceremony work is the core.
He is using one to subsidise the other.
The boundary between them
has been blurring.

## Tell
When entering a room where he will mediate —
greets every person present
by name, even if he doesn't know them.
He will have learned the names beforehand.
The greeting is the first act of mediation.
