# Piece: The Condominium and Kastom
## Seventy-four years of being governed by two countries at once — and the traditional system that outlasted both.

## Owner
- **Place:** [Port Vila](culture_vanuatuan_place_port_vila.md)

## Place
Port Vila. Efate island.
The harbour.
The French boulangerie.
The British-style pub.
The kastom market.
All three within five minutes of each other.
The Condominium's ghost
in the streetscape.

## Load Bearing
Remove the Condominium
and the New Hebrides is a single-colonial-power
Pacific territory with the usual trajectory.
With it: the specifically absurd
double-colonial structure
that made it impossible for either power
to fully impose their system —
because the other power was there too,
and the ni-Vanuatu could play them off each other,
and neither could justify
the full administrative capacity
to govern what the other would also govern.
The result: kastom survived
more intact than in most Pacific territories
because neither colonial power
fully penetrated the village.
The Nagriamel movement is the counter-piece:
Jimmy Stevens, 1970s,
the Santo separatist movement
backed by American libertarians
(the Phoenix Foundation, James Watt)
who wanted to create a tax haven
on the island of Santo.
The independent Vanuatu government
and a Papua New Guinean military force
suppressed it in 1980 —
the first military action
of an independent Pacific state.
The kastom land claims underlying Nagriamel —
the legitimate grievance
beneath the libertarian project —
were never fully resolved.
The land question: still present.
Independence 1980 — jointly granted by Britain and France,
the only joint decolonisation in history.
Bislama as the national language:
the creole that allows
138 languages to govern together.

## Apparent
1606: Pedro Fernández de Quirós.
Spanish expedition. Claims for Spain. Leaves.
1774: Cook. Names them New Hebrides.
1800s: sandalwood traders, blackbirders.
Labour trade to Queensland sugar plantations.
Depopulation.
1880s: French and British settlers.
Competition.
1906: Anglo-French Condominium.
Jointly governed. The "Pandemonium" as residents called it.
1940s: WWII. US forces in large numbers.
Santo as major US base.
The cargo cult: John Frum movement on Tanna.
Awaiting the American goods.
Still active.
1971: Walter Lini, first independence leader.
1974: Vanuaaku Pati founded.
1977: kastom land movement intensifies.
1980: independence. Republic of Vanuatu.
Nagriamel rebellion on Santo suppressed.
Lini: first PM.
2015: Cyclone Pam. Category 5. One of the most powerful
cyclones ever recorded in the Southern Hemisphere.
Direct hit on Port Vila.
Vanuatu rebuilt.
Kastom knowledge used in rebuilding.
2018: Ambae volcano. Two evacuations.
Entire island population moved.
The country that keeps being tested
and keeps passing.
