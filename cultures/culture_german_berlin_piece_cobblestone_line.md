# Piece: The Cobblestone Line
## The wall is gone. Its path is marked in the ground. You can walk across it without noticing. Or you can follow it.

## Owner
- **Place:** Berlin

## Place
Berlin. The city centre.
The line runs through Mitte, through Prenzlauer Berg,
through Kreuzberg, through Wedding.
Brass cobblestones set into the pavement.
Two rows. The width of the wall.
Interrupted by intersections, by buildings,
by the places where the wall
went through the middle of a block
and the block was later demolished.
The line is not always continuous.
The wall was not always continuous either.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Berlin position loses its ground truth.
The wall divided Berlin for 28 years.
It was built in 1961 and fell in 1989.
It divided families, streets, a city, a country, a continent.
Its line is still in the cobblestones
because Berlin decided
that the wall should not disappear
into the pavement unmarked.
The cobblestone line is the decision
to remember while living on top of the memory.
The position carries both:
the city that was divided
and the city that marked the division
and then built on it.
Remove the line and Berlin becomes
a city that has processed and moved on.
The line is the evidence
that Berlin has not moved on.
It has built forward.
These are not the same thing.

## Apparent
The Berliner Mauerweg: the official path.
160 kilometres.
Follows the entire course of the wall.
Completed 2006.
You can walk it in a week.
You can cycle it in a weekend.
Most Berliners have not done either.
The East Side Gallery: 1.3 kilometres
of preserved wall on the Spree.
Painted by artists in 1990.
The largest open-air gallery in the world.
Tourists photograph it every day.
The Mauer im Kopf: the wall in the head.
The phrase coined after reunification.
The psychological division
that persisted after the physical one.
Surveys in 2004: 25% of West Germans
wished the wall were back.
12% of East Germans.
The cobblestone line is there.
The Mauer im Kopf is also there.
Both are real.

## Yearbook
13 August 1961: the wall begins to be built.
The GDR seals the border overnight.
Families wake up on different sides.
1961–1989: 140 kilometres of wall.
A city divided.
155 people killed trying to cross.
Perhaps more.
9 November 1989: the wall opens.
The announcement: a bureaucratic mistake.
The crowds: unstoppable.
The wall: breached.
3 October 1990: reunification.
East and West Berlin: one city.
1991: the Bundestag votes to move the capital
from Bonn back to Berlin.
1999: the government arrives.
Berlin is the capital again.
2002: construction of the Berliner Mauerweg begins.
2006: completed.
The line in the cobblestones.
The city built on both sides of it.
The cranes still building.
Berlin is always finishing something.
