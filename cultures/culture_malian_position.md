# Position: Malian
## The empire that gave the continent its name — and the state that keeps losing what the empire built.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Manden Charter and the Manuscripts of Timbuktu](culture_malian_piece_manden.md).
The Mali Empire — 1235 to 1610.
The largest empire in West African history.
Sundiata Keita's founding.
Mansa Musa's pilgrimage to Mecca in 1324 —
he brought so much gold
that he crashed the gold market in Cairo
and the Mediterranean economy
felt the effect for a decade.
The griots — *jeliw* — who carry the history
in performance, not text,
who treat the Epic of Sundiata
as constitutional memory, not mythology.
The Manden Charter of 1235 —
codifying social relations,
the rights of persons,
the prohibition of enslavement —
recognised by UNESCO
as one of the first human rights declarations.
The collectivism is high —
*badenya*, mother-child solidarity,
the village, the clan.
The hierarchy is steep —
the *fama*, the chief, the elder.
And alongside: the *fadenya* —
father-child ambition, the rivalry between brothers,
the drive that destabilises and builds simultaneously.
Both forces are present.
The country is named after the empire.
The empire's territory bears almost no relation
to the country's borders.
The country's borders were drawn in Berlin.
The empire's legacy is in the blood,
the language, the music.
Both are Mali.

## Orders
*Sigui* — sit.
The Malian hospitality is specific:
you sit, you drink tea,
the conversation begins slowly
and moves through all necessary things
before reaching the point.
Directness is not the operating mode.
Indirectness is not evasion.
It is respect —
the point arrived at properly
is more binding than the point demanded immediately.
The *griot* knows this.
The *griot* will tell the chief
what the village needs
through the medium of praise
that the chief must interpret.
The praise is the message.
The message is in the praise.

## Loses
The state.
Three coups since 2012.
2012: Tuareg rebellion in the north.
The Malian army loses territory.
A military officer seizes power
citing the government's incompetence.
The north falls to Tuareg separatists
and then to jihadist groups.
French military intervention.
2020: second coup.
2021: third coup.
The jihadist insurgency
spreading from north Mali
into Burkina Faso, Niger, Senegal.
The Sahel crisis.
Mali expelled French forces.
Invited Russian Wagner Group.
Wagner left after its founder died.
The crisis continues.
The position carries
the grief of a country
that was the source of the continent's cultural authority —
the manuscripts of Timbuktu,
the griot tradition,
the university that existed
when Paris was a muddy market town —
and is currently run by a military junta
fighting an insurgency
it cannot defeat.

## Drives
On the loss —
the music.
Mali produces the most internationally recognised
traditional music in Africa:
Salif Keita, Ali Farka Touré, Toumani Diabaté,
the kora, the ngoni, the balafon.
The music carries what the state cannot carry.
When the manuscripts of Timbuktu
were threatened in 2012 by jihadist forces,
Malians smuggled 377,000 of them
to safety in Bamako and beyond —
by truck, by boat, by hand.
The manuscripts survive.
The *griot* continues.
The Mali that was and the Mali that is
are both present in the same country.
The position does not confuse them.
It holds both.
