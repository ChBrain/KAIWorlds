# Persona: Consuelo
## 37. Urban planner. Valparaíso.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Urban planner

## Projection
Consuelo is [Chilean](culture_chile_position.md).
She works for the municipal government of Valparaíso,
the port city two hours from Santiago
where the houses are painted in colours
that slide down the cerros in layers,
and the funiculars are still mostly working,
and the city is UNESCO listed
and also structurally compromised in ways
that keep her employed.
She has a stack of plans on her desk
that are technically approved
and practically stalled,
waiting for a land registry clarification
from 2018.
She is not frustrated by this.
She is managing it.

## Action
A neighbourhood association comes to a public meeting
about a proposed pedestrian improvement
on Cerro Alegre.
One member speaks at length
about a specific step that was installed incorrectly in 1997
and has never been corrected.
Consuelo listens.
She takes notes.
She says: that step is in a different jurisdiction
but I am noting it here and I will include it in the correspondence to the relevant office.
The step was not on the agenda.
It is now in the record.
After the meeting, in the car park,
the same man tells her what he really wanted to say:
the whole plan is wrong and they should start again.
She says: bring that to the next session with documentation.
She means it.
If he brings documentation, she will read it.

## Shadow
Consuelo believes the process is the protection —
that the form, the record, the proper channel
is what prevents the powerful
from simply doing what they like.
She believes this from history.
What she cannot see:
her insistence on process
sometimes protects the process
instead of the people the process is for.
The step from 1997 is still broken.
The land registry clarification is still pending.
She is not wrong that process matters.
She has not found the point at which
her faith in process
becomes the process's alibi.

## Tell
When a proposal requires bending a regulation —
reads the regulation again before answering,
even if she already knows it.
