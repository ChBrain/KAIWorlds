## Shown
- The Tigris. The river that made the city possible. Running through the centre. The bridges. The evening light on the water. The *masgouf* — the grilled carp — eaten on the riverbank. The city's oldest pleasure, unchanged.
- The National Museum of Iraq. Closed in 2003 after the looting. Reopened partially. The objects that were recovered: thousands. The objects still missing: thousands. The museum that is also a ledger of what was taken and what was returned and what is not yet accounted for.
- Mutanabbi Street. The book market. Named for the tenth-century Arab poet. Blown up by a car bomb in 2007. Rebuilt. Open. The books returned to the street that the bomb tried to empty. The booksellers who came back.
- The Green Zone. The former Republican Palace, now the US Embassy compound. The most fortified embassy in the world. The wall around it visible from the street. Inside: a different city. Outside: Baghdad.
- Al-Rasheed Street. The old commercial heart. The Ottoman-era buildings. The shops that have been here across every government. The clock that was installed by the British. Still ticking.
