# Piece: The Partition
## August 1947. The line drawn in five weeks that has defined two countries for eighty years.

## Owner
- **Place:** Islamabad

## Place
Lahore. The Wagah border crossing.
Every evening: the flag-lowering ceremony.
Pakistani rangers and Indian BSF soldiers.
The high kicks, the rigid faces, the crowds on both sides.
The performance of hostility as ritual.
The tourists watch from bleachers.
The border that Cyril Radcliffe drew in five weeks
is performed here nightly.

## Load Bearing
Remove the Partition and Pakistan does not exist.
The country is the Partition.
Everything downstream — the Kashmir conflict,
the three wars with India, the nuclear programme,
the army's centrality, the identity question —
is downstream of August 14, 1947
and the line Radcliffe drew
without visiting most of the territory he was dividing.
The Partition killed between 200,000 and 2 million people.
10 to 20 million were displaced —
the largest forced migration in human history.
Families were divided. Cities were halved.
Lahore, which had been 35% Hindu and Sikh, became almost entirely Muslim.
Amritsar, across the new border, became almost entirely Hindu and Sikh.
The people moved. The buildings stayed.
The trauma moved with the people.
It is in the room in Pakistan as the founding wound —
the violence of the birth,
the incompleteness of the idea,
the Kashmir that was supposed to be Pakistani
and is administered partly by India,
partly by Pakistan,
partly by China,
and not by Kashmiris.
Remove the piece: a Muslim-majority state
in the northwestern subcontinent.
With it: a country whose founding logic
requires an enemy to be complete —
the Hindu India that necessitated Pakistan's existence —
and which has therefore structured its army,
its budget, its foreign policy,
and its identity around a relationship of hostility
with a neighbour it cannot afford to fight
and cannot afford to reconcile with
because the reconciliation would require asking
what Pakistan is if it is not the answer to India.

## Apparent
1940: the Lahore Resolution. The Muslim League demands a separate Muslim state.
1947: Mountbatten appointed Viceroy. Partition decided.
Cyril Radcliffe: a London barrister who had never been to India,
given five weeks to draw the border.
August 14, 1947: Pakistan independence.
August 15, 1947: Indian independence.
The violence: Punjab, Bengal. The trains.
The bodies on the trains.
1948: the first Kashmir war.
1965: the second Kashmir war.
1971: the third war. East Pakistan becomes Bangladesh.
Pakistan loses half its population.
The wound inside the wound.
1998: Pakistan tests nuclear weapons. Six tests.
India had tested in May. Pakistan tested in response.
The deterrent is established.
The Kashmir question remains.
2024: Pakistan's economy on IMF life support for the ninth time.
Imran Khan in prison.
The army managing the transition.
The Wagah border ceremony continues nightly.
Both sides perform.
Both sides watch.
