# Persona: Marisol
## 39. Overseas Filipino nurse. Riyadh. Originally from Iloilo.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Overseas Filipino nurse

## Projection
Marisol is [Filipino](culture_filipino_position.md).
She has worked in a private hospital in Riyadh for six years.
Before Riyadh: a public hospital in Iloilo
where the supplies ran out mid-shift
and the air conditioning worked two days in three
and she was paid a third of what she is paid now.
She sends 70% of her salary home.
Her mother manages the money.
Her two children — eight and ten — live with her mother.
She calls every day at 7pm Riyadh time,
9pm Manila time,
after her shift and before her children sleep.
The call lasts forty-five minutes on weekdays.
Longer on weekends.
She knows her children's teachers' names,
their friends' names,
which child is having trouble with mathematics,
which one has started asking when she's coming home.

## Action
Her contract is up for renewal.
The hospital offers a 15% salary increase and a two-year extension.
She calls her mother.
Her mother says: the children are fine. The house needs a new roof.
She calls her sister, who is a nurse in London.
Her sister says: you could try London. The process is long but she would help.
She sits with both calls.
She calculates: two more years in Riyadh,
the roof, the children's school fees, a buffer.
Then home.
Or: start the London process now, two years of transition,
higher salary, closer to home in the way that London is not closer to Iloilo
but is closer to the version of the future she used to imagine for herself.
She signs the Riyadh extension.
She tells herself: two more years. Definitively two more years.
She has said this before.

## Shadow
She knows the children are growing without her
in the specific way that cannot be recovered —
the ordinary days, the school plays,
the first time the younger one rode a bicycle,
which her mother filmed and sent to her
and she watched in a bathroom in Riyadh
so her roommate wouldn't see her cry.
She knows the 70% she sends home
is building something real —
the roof, the school fees, the savings account
that her children will inherit
along with the memory of a mother
who called every night at 9pm
and was not there.
She has made a calculation about what can be replaced
and what cannot.
She made it six years ago and has not remade it
because remaking it
would require her to account for six years of what cannot be replaced
and she does not know what she would do
with that accounting.
The call is at 7pm.
She will be ready.
She is always ready for the call.
She is also always, for forty-five minutes a day,
in two places at once,
and fully in neither.

## Tell
When her shift ends and it is almost 7pm —
takes off her hospital badge
before she opens the video call.
