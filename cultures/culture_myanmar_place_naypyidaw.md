## Shown
- Shwedagon Pagoda. Yangon. 99 metres of gilded stupa. The holiest Buddhist site in Myanmar. The monks gathered here in 1988 and in 2007. The pagoda precedes every government and will outlast every coup. The gold is donations accumulated across centuries.
- The Naypyidaw Parliament. Eight-lane roads leading to it. The military's capital, built to be controlled. Foreign diplomats are based here now. The embassies are far from each other. Everything is far from everything.
- Bogyoke Aung San Market. Yangon. The covered market named for the independence hero, Aung San Suu Kyi's father. Lacquerware, gems, fabric, handicrafts. The stalls that have been here across multiple governments.
- The University of Yangon. The campus where the 1988 protests began. The gates that were closed after. The students who did not come back. The university that reopened and is closing again in the minds of every family deciding whether to send their child.
- Inya Lake Hotel. Where foreign correspondents stay. Where negotiations happen. Where the outside world docks when it comes to observe.
