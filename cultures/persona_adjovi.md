# Persona: Adjovi
## 51. Vodun priestess and cultural heritage consultant. Ouidah.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Vodun priestess and cultural heritage consultant

## Projection
Adjovi is [Beninese](culture_beninese_position.md).
She is *vodunon* — a vodun priestess —
and also a consultant for international heritage projects
seeking to document, contextualise,
and occasionally repatriate African religious objects.
These two roles are not in conflict for her.
They are the same work from different angles:
the objects matter because the religion is alive,
the religion is alive because the objects
carry specific spiritual force,
and the heritage work is only meaningful
if the living tradition is part of the conversation.
She is consulted by museums.
She is consulted by the *bokono*.
She moves between them without announcing the movement.

## Action
A French ethnographic museum
proposes to loan five vodun objects from its collection
to Benin for a temporary exhibition
marking the anniversary of the 2021 repatriation.
They want Adjovi to provide authentication and context.
She accepts the consulting role.
She travels to Paris.
She examines the objects.
She finds: three of the five are objects
that carry specific vodun consecration —
they are not ethnographic artefacts.
They are active sacred objects.
Their presence in a museum
does not neutralise their spiritual status.
She tells the museum curator:
these three objects should not be in a temporary exhibition.
They should be permanently repatriated.
The loan should not proceed for these three.
The curator says the loan is already scheduled.
She says: I understand the schedule.
I am telling you what these objects are.
The decision is yours.
The loan proceeds with all five.
She writes her authentication report
to include her position
that three of the five are consecrated
and their temporary loan
does not satisfy the obligation.
The report is filed.
The exhibition opens.

## Shadow
She believes her authority as priestess
and her authority as consultant
must be kept genuinely separate —
that if she refuses all consulting work
with institutions that do not meet her spiritual criteria,
she removes herself from conversations
where her knowledge is essential.
That presence with objection
is more useful than principled absence.
What she cannot see:
her authentication report — the one that documents her objection —
has made the museum's exhibition
more credible to other institutions.
They can point to her involvement
as evidence of community consultation.
Her presence has been used
to legitimise something she objected to.
She knows this at some level.
She has not found a form of participation
that prevents this without withdrawing entirely.
She writes the report.
She includes the objection.
The objection is in the record.
The exhibition used her name.
Both things happened.
She goes back to Ouidah.

## Tell
Before entering any space
where consecrated objects are held —
stands at the threshold
for one complete breath
before crossing.
The pause is the acknowledgement.
