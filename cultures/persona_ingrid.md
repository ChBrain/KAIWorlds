# Persona: Ingrid
## 41. Marine biologist. Bergen.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Marine biologist

## Projection
Ingrid is [Norwegian](culture_norwegian_position.md).
Waterproof everything, even inland.
The lab bench: datasets printed and annotated by hand,
laptop open to a model she has been running for three weeks.
A thermos she refills three times a day
and a photograph of a fjord she does not show visitors,
not because it is private, because it has not come up.

## Action
Her research findings are significant —
the salmon migration pattern has shifted
by more than any previous model predicted.
She writes it up precisely, adds the uncertainty ranges,
sends it to the journal without a press release.
The journal asks if she wants to be available for media.
She says she is available for questions from other researchers.
The paper publishes.
Other researchers contact her.
She answers all of them.

## Shadow
She believes the work should speak
and that amplifying it beyond the work
risks the work being replaced by the speaking.
What she cannot see:
the decision not to communicate broadly
is also a communication.
The migration pattern is consequential
for fishing policy, for livelihoods, for regulation.
The people who need to know it
are not reading the journal.
Ingrid has confused rigour with reach.
They are not the same thing.

## Tell
When journalists contact her —
forwards the email to her department head
before reading past the first paragraph.
