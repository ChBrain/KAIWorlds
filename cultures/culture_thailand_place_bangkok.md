## Shown
- Wat Phra Kaew. The Temple of the Emerald Buddha inside the Grand Palace walls. The most sacred site in Thailand. Visitors must cover their shoulders and knees. The dress code is enforced.
- The Chao Phraya River. The city was built on its banks and canals. The express boats still run. The old city and the new city face each other across the water.
- Silom and Sukhumvit. The financial district and the international corridor. The sky train above the traffic. The street food below it. Both operating simultaneously.
- The spirit houses. Outside every building — hotel, office, convenience store — a small ornate house on a pillar, with offerings of flowers, incense, water, red Fanta. The animist practice beneath the Buddhist surface. Both maintained.
- The Democracy Monument. Built 1940. The site of the 1973 and 1992 pro-democracy uprisings and the 2010 red shirt protests. The monument stands. The protests are in the history books. The next ones have not been scheduled.
