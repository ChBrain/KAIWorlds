# Piece: Sturmfest und erdverwachsen
## Storm-proof and rooted in the earth. The words the state chose for itself.

## Owner
- **Place:** Hanover

## Place
Hanover. The Niedersächsisches Landesmuseum.
The Herrenhäuser Gärten.
The largest urban forest in Europe: the Eilenriede.
A green city on the Leine
that most people pass through on the way to somewhere else.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Lower Saxony position loses its self-description.
*Sturmfest und erdverwachsen* comes from the Niedersachsenlied,
the state anthem.
It is the phrase the position chose
when it needed to name what it was.
Storm-proof: the North Sea coast, the weather,
the Saxon resistance to Charlemagne,
the refusal to be knocked over.
Rooted in the earth: the landscape,
the Lüneburg Heath, the farmland,
the Baroque gardens of the Hanoverians,
the Volkswagen in Wolfsburg
which is the largest car factory in the world
and employs 70,000 people in a city of 130,000.
The piece is load-bearing because
the phrase names something true about the position:
it is a position that holds
through weather, through history,
through being passed between powers
and coming out still itself.

## Apparent
The Niedersachsenlied. Written 1926.
The second stanza: *Wir sind das Volk der Niedersachsen,*
*sturmfest und erdverwachsen.*
We are the people of Lower Saxony,
storm-proof and rooted in the earth.
The Volkswagen factory. Wolfsburg.
Founded 1938 by the Nazi state.
Now: 70,000 employees.
The largest employer in Lower Saxony.
The company that makes the car
that most of the world first associated
with German engineering.
The Hanoverian kings: George I of Britain, 1714.
The King of Britain was the Elector of Hanover.
The personal union lasted until 1837.
The British king's horse-guards still wear
the Hanoverian white horse.
The Lüneburg Heath: 7,400 square kilometres.
Purple heather in August.
The largest heathland in Germany.
The Frisian coast: the Wadden Sea.
UNESCO World Heritage.
The tides expose the sea floor twice a day.
You can walk on the bottom of the North Sea.
This is also Lower Saxony.

## Yearbook
772: Charlemagne begins his thirty-year campaign
against the Saxons.
The Saxons resist.
804: the Saxons converted by treaty and force.
The resistance is noted in the record.
1235: the Duchy of Brunswick-Lüneburg established.
The foundation of what becomes Hanover.
1714: George Louis, Elector of Hanover,
becomes King of Great Britain.
The Hanoverians on the British throne
until 1901.
1866: Prussia annexes Hanover.
The Hanoverians do not forget this.
Some have not forgiven it.
1946: Lower Saxony established
by the British military government.
A merger of four territories
that had never been one state.
The identity was constructed.
It held.
1938: Volkswagen founded in Wolfsburg.
1974: Gerhard Schröder born in Lower Saxony.
Later Federal Chancellor.
His path smoothed by Lower Saxon corporatism.
The state produces chancellors
and keeps its forests.
