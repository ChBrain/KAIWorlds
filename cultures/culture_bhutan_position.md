# Position: Bhutanese
## The country that measured happiness — and the question of who was being measured.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[Gross National Happiness](culture_bhutan_piece_gnh.md).
The Vajrayana Buddhism that runs through everything —
the monasteries, the *dzong* fortresses,
the prayer flags on every pass,
the *driglam namzha* — the code of etiquette
that governs dress, behaviour, posture
in official contexts.
The position was formed in a small Himalayan kingdom
between India and China
that chose, deliberately,
to develop slowly and on its own terms.
The Wangchuck dynasty has governed since 1907.
The transition to constitutional monarchy in 2008
was initiated by the king,
not demanded by the people.
The position is not restless in the way
of positions that fought for what they have.
It was given the form.
It is still learning what the form means.
The hierarchy is real and gentle in its presentation:
the king is beloved,
the monks are respected,
the elder is deferred to.
The face the position shows
is serene and considered.
What is underneath the face
is the question the GNH does not always measure.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room is calm.
The calm is also work.

## Orders
Preserve.
The Bhutanese position does not optimise for growth.
It optimises for continuity —
of the culture, the language, the Buddhist practice,
the environment.
60% of the land is constitutionally protected forest.
The television arrived in 1999.
The internet arrived with the television.
The position is still deciding what arrived with them.

## Loses
The margin.
They were certain the careful development —
the controlled tourism, the high tariffs,
the deliberate pace —
would protect the culture
from what fast development does to cultures.
The protection is real.
It is also maintained by limiting
who can come and who can stay
and who belongs and who does not.
The Lhotshampa — the Nepali-speaking southerners —
were 25% of the population.
In the late 1980s and early 1990s,
citizenship criteria were applied
in ways that produced mass statelessness.
100,000 people expelled to refugee camps in Nepal.
The happiness was being measured
for a population that had been made smaller.
The position does not speak of this directly.
It is present in the room
as the thing that is not in the GNH survey.

## Drives
On the loss of the margin —
holds the form.
The *driglam namzha*. The forest cover. The prayer flags.
The position holds what can be held
and does not examine too closely
what was discarded to hold it.
It is building the constitutional monarchy.
It is developing the economy carefully.
It is sending its students abroad
and hoping enough of them come back.
It is still deciding what it is
when it is not being looked at
by people who came to see the happiness.
