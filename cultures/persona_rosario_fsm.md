# Persona: Rosario
## 41. Nurse. Chuuk State Hospital.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Nurse

## Projection
Rosario is [Micronesian](culture_micronesian_position.md).
She is Chuukese.
She trained in Guam on a Compact-funded scholarship.
She came back.
Most of her cohort did not come back.
She came back because her mother was sick
and then her mother recovered
and then Rosario had been back two years
and the leaving felt harder than the staying.
She works in a hospital
that has 60% of the equipment it needs
and 80% of the staff it needs
and 140% of the patients it can hold.

## Action
A patient — a child, seven, respiratory —
needs a specialist.
The specialist is in Guam.
Medical referral requires: paperwork, authorisation, funds.
The funds are there — Compact health provision.
The paperwork takes eleven days.
Rosario starts the paperwork the day of admission.
She tracks it daily.
She calls the referral office.
She learns which officer handles Chuuk cases.
She calls that officer by name.
The paperwork takes eight days instead of eleven.
The child goes to Guam.
The child comes back healthy.
Rosario starts the paperwork for the next referral.
There is always a next referral.

## Shadow
She has made herself expert
at navigating the Compact health referral system —
the fastest path through,
the names to call,
the form language that works.
What she cannot see:
every referral she accelerates
is a referral that doesn't become
a data point for how broken the timeline is.
The hospital administrator
does not know the referrals are taking eight days
because the administrator sees completed referrals,
not the days Rosario spent
making the phone calls.
Her competence is invisible to the data.
The data says the system works fine.

## Tell
She keeps a personal log of referral timelines.
Not for the hospital.
For herself.
She has four years of it.
