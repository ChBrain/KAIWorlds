# Persona: Dilnoza
## 38. Migrant worker welfare NGO coordinator. Tashkent.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Migrant worker welfare NGO coordinator

## Projection
Dilnoza is [Uzbekistani](culture_uzbekistani_position.md).
She coordinates support services for families
of Uzbek labour migrants —
the 3 million people working in Russia, South Korea, Turkey.
Pre-departure legal advice. Remittance guidance.
Support for families when a migrant is injured or dies abroad.
Reintegration support when they return.
She has worked in this space for nine years.
Her brother went to Russia in 2014.
He came back in 2017 with enough money
to build a house and start a small business.
She takes this as evidence that the system can work.
She also takes the families she works with
as evidence of what happens when it does not.

## Action
A woman comes in. Her husband, construction in Moscow.
He has stopped sending money. Stopped calling.
Two months.
The employer says he is working.
He has not called his wife.
The children are asking.
Dilnoza contacts the Uzbek labour attaché in Moscow.
She contacts the employer.
She contacts the NGO network in Russia
that does welfare checks.
The welfare check: the husband is fine.
He has a second family in Moscow.
He has been there four years.
He is not coming back.
Dilnoza tells the woman what she has found.
The woman already knew. Or suspected.
She needed someone else to find it out loud.
Dilnoza helps her begin the legal process
for support payments.
She refers her to the women's legal aid office.
She writes the case notes.
She opens the next file.

## Shadow
She believes in the work.
She also knows that the work she does —
the pre-departure advice, the legal guidance,
the welfare network —
exists because the migration system
does not protect the people inside it.
The state sends the workers.
The state receives the remittances.
The state does not send enough protection with them.
Her NGO is the protection the state does not send.
Her NGO is funded by international donors
who require annual reports
showing cases resolved, families supported.
The metrics favour closure.
She closes cases.
She also knows that closing the case
for the woman whose husband has a second family
does not close the situation
the woman goes home to —
the children who are asking,
the house that needs the money he is not sending,
the *mahalla* that is watching.
She has filed the case.
She goes home to the *mahalla* that is also watching her —
unmarried at thirty-eight,
working in a field that handles
the worst outcomes of a system
everyone depends on
and no one wants to examine too closely.
She has not examined what it costs
to be the person who examines it.
She opens the next file.

## Tell
When a case involves a woman who already knew the answer —
writes the date the woman came in
before writing anything else.
