# Piece: La Transición
## The negotiated democracy that Spain chose instead of revenge.

## Owner
- **Place:** Madrid

## Place
Madrid. The Cortes Generales.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and Spanish democracy is a rupture.
With it, Spanish democracy is a negotiation with the past -
which is more durable.
The left did not prosecute. The right did not resist. Both agreed to begin.

## Apparent
The Cortes Generales, Madrid.
The Pactos de la Moncloa. October 1977. All major parties signing.
The Constitution ratified December 1978. 88% in favour.
February 23, 1981: Lieutenant Colonel Tejero seizes the Cortes. Armed.

## Yearbook
1936–1939: the Spanish Civil War.
Franco wins. 500,000 dead.
1939–1975: 36 years of dictatorship.
November 20, 1975: Franco dies.
November 22: Juan Carlos I crowned.
June 1977: first democratic elections since 1936.
1978: the Constitution.
1981: the coup attempt. February 23.
Lieutenant Colonel Tejero seizes the Cortes.
The King broadcasts. The army stands down.
The Transition holds.
1982: the Socialists win. First left government since the Republic.
The right accepts the result.
The Transition confirmed.
1986: EU membership.
2004–present: ongoing debate about historical memory.
The amnesty law contested.
The Transition's terms renegotiated generationally.
The structure it built: still standing.
