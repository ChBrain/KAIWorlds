# Persona: Zied
## 35. Constitutional law professor and podcast host. Tunis.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Constitutional law professor and podcast host

## Projection
Zied is [Tunisian](culture_tunisian_position.md).
He teaches constitutional law at the University of Tunis
and hosts a podcast — *Dustour* (Constitution) —
that reaches about 40,000 listeners per episode.
He explains constitutional developments
in Tunisian Arabic, without jargon,
to people who did not study law.
He has been doing this since 2017,
when he realised his students could explain
the 2014 constitution to him
but could not explain it to their parents.
Since 2021 the podcast has become
something else: a running public record
of what is being changed,
what is being lost,
what the 2022 constitution does
that the 2014 constitution prevented.
He does it in plain language.
His listener numbers doubled after 2021.
He does not consider this good news.

## Action
A government official contacts the university
asking that Zied's course syllabus
be reviewed for "balance" —
specifically the sections comparing
the 2014 and 2022 constitutions.
The request is informal. Not a directive. A request.
The department head asks Zied to review it himself.
Zied reviews his syllabus.
He finds nothing unbalanced.
He submits the same syllabus.
He tells the department head what he has done.
He records an episode of the podcast
explaining, in general terms,
that constitutional law education
requires teaching multiple constitutional texts comparatively.
He does not mention the request.
He does not need to.
His listeners understand.

## Shadow
He believes transparency is protection —
that doing his work publicly
and rigorously
creates a cost for interference
that pure private resistance does not.
That 40,000 listeners
are a structural fact
that changes the calculation
for anyone considering silencing him.
What he cannot see:
the 40,000 listeners are also his exposure.
The visibility that protects him
is the same visibility
that makes him a legible target.
The colleagues who are less visible —
who teach the same things
but without the podcast —
face less attention.
He has chosen visibility as strategy.
He is probably right that it works.
He does not know
what happens the day it stops working.
He has not prepared for that.
He records the next episode.

## Tell
Before recording each episode —
reads the specific constitutional article
he will discuss that day
out loud, to himself,
one time,
before switching on the microphone.
