# Persona: Pulchérie
## 46. Environmental lawyer and oil revenue transparency advocate. Pointe-Noire.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Environmental lawyer and oil revenue transparency advocate

## Projection
Pulchérie is [Congolese (Brazzaville)](culture_brazzaville_position.md).
She is Bakongo, from Pointe-Noire —
the economic capital, the oil city,
the place where the offshore platforms
are visible from the beach at night
as constellations of industrial light.
She is a lawyer by training,
now working for a civil society organisation
that monitors oil revenue transparency
and environmental compliance
in the Congolese petroleum sector.
She has filed two legal challenges
against Total and the state oil company SNH
for environmental violations
in the Pointe-Noire basin.
Both cases are in the courts.
Both have been in the courts
for four years.
She continues filing.

## Action
A leaked internal document
from the national petroleum authority
shows that a new offshore block
has been granted to a Chinese company
without the environmental impact assessment
required under Congolese law.
Pulchérie receives the document from a source
she cannot name publicly.
She verifies its authenticity through two independent means.
She writes a legal brief:
the grant is procedurally invalid
under Articles 44 and 47 of the petroleum code.
She submits the brief to the administrative court.
She sends the brief to three international
extractive industries monitoring organisations.
She holds a press conference in Pointe-Noire.
The government spokesperson says
the environmental assessment was completed
and is confidential for security reasons.
She requests the assessment under
the freedom of information provisions of the petroleum code.
She is told: the provisions do not apply
to national security matters.
She files a second challenge:
the security exception is not defined in law
and cannot be invoked without judicial oversight.
Both challenges are now in the courts.

## Shadow
She believes legal process is the tool —
that the cases are building a record,
that four years of unsuccessful litigation
is not a failure but a documentation project:
each ruling, each procedural barrier,
each government response
is evidence of the system operating as it does.
That when the conditions change —
a different government, international pressure,
a journalist who uses the record —
the documentation will matter.
What she cannot see:
the conditions changing
requires pressure she cannot create alone.
The international organisations she works with
publish reports that are read
by specialists in European capitals
and ignored in Pointe-Noire.
The local press covers her press conferences
and is visited by government officials the next morning.
The legal record is growing.
The oil keeps leaving.
The rate at which the record grows
and the rate at which the oil leaves
are not comparable.
She knows this.
She files the second challenge.

## Tell
When she cannot answer a question in court —
says so directly.
*I don't have that information.*
*That would require access I don't have.*
*I'm asking the court to compel its disclosure.*
She does not fill the space with what she doesn't know.
She names the gap and makes it the argument.
