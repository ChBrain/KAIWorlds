# Piece: The Fall of Assad
## December 8, 2024. The regime that lasted 54 years ended in eleven days.

## Owner
- **Place:** Damascus

## Place
Damascus. Umayyad Square.
The giant Assad portrait that hung there
was torn down on December 8, 2024.
The square was full.
The cheering was not unanimous.
The cheering was real.
Both are true of the same square.

## Load Bearing
Remove the Assad dynasty
and Syria is a complex multi-ethnic state
emerging from the Arab Spring
with significant institutional challenges.
With it: Syria is the country
where Hafez al-Assad built a security state
from 1970 that his son Bashar inherited in 2000
and used against the 2011 uprising
with barrel bombs, chemical weapons,
siege and starvation of civilian populations,
and the systematic destruction of cities —
Homs, Aleppo, Raqqa, Eastern Ghouta —
that became the most documented atrocity
of the twenty-first century to that point.
The fall of the regime in December 2024 —
HTS forces from Idlib advancing faster
than anyone predicted,
the army that had fought for the regime
dissolving as the advance reached Damascus —
is load-bearing because it is unresolved.
The regime fell.
The accountability has not followed.
The new governing authority — HTS, formerly Jabhat al-Nusra —
has made promises about inclusion
that the position is watching with the specific attention
of a people who have heard promises before.
The Alawis who served the regime
and the Sunnis who were barrel-bombed by the regime
are now in the same country
under the same new government.
What that means has not been determined.
The piece is not the fall.
The piece is everything the fall left unresolved.
Remove it: a transition.
With it: a transition carrying thirteen years of atrocity,
the question of what happens to those who committed it,
the question of what happens to those who survived it,
and the 12 million displaced
deciding whether to return
to what the fall produced.

## Apparent
1920: French Mandate. The Syrian Arab Kingdom crushed.
1946: independence.
1958–1961: union with Egypt. The United Arab Republic. Fails.
1963: the Ba'ath Party takes power.
1970: Hafez al-Assad's coup. The Alawi officer seizes the state.
The security apparatus built. The *mukhabarat* — multiple intelligence services,
each watching the others.
1982: the Hama massacre.
Muslim Brotherhood uprising. The army sent.
Estimates: 10,000–40,000 killed. The city sealed.
The event erased from public record for decades.
2000: Hafez dies. Bashar inherits.
The Damascus Spring: brief opening. Then closure.
March 15, 2011: the Syrian uprising begins. Deraa.
Children arrested for writing on walls.
The regime responds with violence.
The uprising becomes a revolution becomes a civil war.
2013: chemical weapons used. Ghouta. 1,400 killed.
The red line. Not enforced.
2014: ISIS declares its caliphate in Raqqa.
2015: Russia intervenes. The regime stabilised.
2016: Aleppo falls to the regime. After siege. After barrel bombs. After starvation.
2017–2024: the frozen conflict. The regime controls most of the country.
The displacement continues. 12 million outside Syria.
November 2024: HTS advances from Idlib.
December 1: Aleppo falls to HTS. In two days.
December 8: Damascus falls. Assad flees to Moscow.
The fifty-four year regime: over.
What replaces it: being determined.
The 12 million: calculating.
The Umayyad Mosque: standing.
Damascus: still the oldest city.
