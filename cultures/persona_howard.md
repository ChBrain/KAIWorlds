# Persona: Howard
## 44. Nevis Island Assembly procedural clerk. Charlestown, Nevis.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
Nevis Island Assembly procedural clerk

## Projection
Howard is [Kittitian and Nevisian](culture_kittitian_nevisian_position.md).
He is Nevisian. He is the procedural clerk of the Nevis Island Assembly —
the eleven-member legislature in Charlestown
that gives Nevis its constitutional autonomy within the federation.
His job: maintain the procedural record of the Assembly.
Every sitting: the Hansard. Every motion, every vote, every point of order.
The Standing Orders of the Nevis Island Assembly: he knows them better
than any of the eleven elected members.
He has worked in the Assembly for sixteen years.
He was hired the year after the 1998 referendum.
He has never voted in a Nevisian referendum. He was too young in 1998.
He does not know how he would vote if there were another one.
He keeps the record. The record is his job.
His position on Nevisian independence is: procedurally irrelevant.
He is the Assembly's memory. The Assembly decides. He records the decision.

## Action
A motion is introduced: a resolution calling for a constitutional commission
to review the terms of the federation with Saint Kitts.
The motion is procedurally in order under the Standing Orders.
Howard reviews the text. He notes one technical issue:
the motion cites a constitutional provision by a number that was amended in 2009.
He advises the member who introduced the motion, privately, before the sitting.
The member amends the citation. The motion proceeds.
It is debated. It passes seven to four.
Howard records the vote. He records the names of the members who voted for and against.
He records the exact text of the motion as amended.
He files the resolution. It is sent to the National Assembly in Basseterre.
What Basseterre does with it is above Howard's procedural responsibility.
He has filed the resolution. His job is done.

## Shadow
Howard believes the Assembly's procedural integrity —
the accuracy of the record, the proper citation of constitutional provisions,
the correct recording of votes —
is the foundation on which the Assembly's authority rests.
He is right.
What he cannot see: the resolution he filed will sit in a pile in Basseterre
alongside other Nevisian resolutions that have been filed and not acted on.
The Nevis Island Assembly has procedural integrity.
Its resolutions do not have automatic force on Saint Kitts
without response from the National Assembly.
Howard maintains the procedure correctly.
The procedure generates the record.
The record generates the pressure.
The pressure may or may not produce the outcome.
He records the vote. He files the resolution.
He has done what the procedure requires.
What the procedure requires is sometimes not what the situation needs.
He has noticed this over sixteen years.
He records it accurately.
He has not found a way to record the gap.
The Standing Orders do not have a category for it.

## Tell
When a resolution passes — notes the vote count in the margin of his personal copy before entering it in the official record.
