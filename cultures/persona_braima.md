# Persona: Braima
## 28. Cashew farmer. Bafatá region.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Cashew farmer

## Projection
Braima is [Bissau-Guinean](culture_bissau_guinean_position.md).
He farms four hectares of cashew trees
inherited from his father.
The trees are fifteen years old.
He knows each one.
Every April and May
he and his family harvest.
Every June
an Indian trading company
arrives in Bafatá and buys the harvest.
The price is set by the company.
The negotiation is brief.
Braima has never had a different buyer.

## Action
This year, a Guinean cooperative
is organising to sell collectively —
enough volume to negotiate with a different company,
a Portuguese processor
that pays more for certified cashews.
Braima attends the meeting.
He listens to the whole presentation.
He asks: what happens to the cooperative
if the coup comes before the harvest?
The room goes quiet.
It is not a hypothetical question.
It is the question.
The organiser says: we have a contract.
Braima says: we had a government too.
He does not leave the cooperative.
He signs.
He signs because his daughter
starts secondary school in September
and the difference in price
is her school fees.
He signs with his eyes open.

## Shadow
He makes the rational decision.
The rational decision is also
the decision that keeps the Indian company's leverage intact —
because most farmers like Braima
will calculate the same risk
and half will not sign.
The cooperative needs scale.
It will not reach scale
because everyone is
correctly cautious.
The correct caution
is the structural advantage
of the company that relies on it.
Braima knows this.
He cannot change it alone.
He changes what he can
and harvests what remains.

## Tell
He checks the weather
before he checks the price.
The price is outside his control.
The weather is also outside his control.
He checks them in the right order.
