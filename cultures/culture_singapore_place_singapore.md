## Shown
- The Padang. The field. The colonial buildings: the Supreme Court, City Hall, the Cricket Club. The National Day Parade assembles here. The country performs itself annually on this ground.
- Marina Bay Sands. The three towers, the boat on top, the infinity pool. The skyline argument: we built this from nothing in sixty years.
- The HDB estates. 80% of Singaporeans live in public housing built by the Housing Development Board. The flats are owned, not rented. The void decks at the base: weddings, funerals, community. The policy that housed a nation.
- Changi Airport. Terminal 4, the automated check-in. The Jewel — the waterfall in the transit hub. The airport as national statement. Consistently first in the world. The ranking is tracked.
- The hawker centres. The food courts that are also the social commons. Chicken rice, laksa, roti prata, char kway teow. UNESCO intangible heritage. The place where the meritocracy pauses and eats together.
