# Persona: Lino
## 44. rural schoolteacher (Guaraní-language instruction). Caazapá.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
rural schoolteacher (Guaraní-language instruction)

## Projection
Lino is [Paraguayan](culture_paraguay_position.md).
He teaches in a rural school in Caazapá department,
where Guaraní is the dominant home language.
He is one of a small number of teachers in the department
trained in bilingual Guaraní-Spanish education.
The programme: begin literacy in Guaraní,
then use Guaraní literacy as the bridge to Spanish.
Students who begin in Guaraní read both languages earlier
than students who begin in Spanish alone.
Lino has been teaching this way for eleven years.
He teaches grades one through three — the foundational years.
He grew up speaking Guaraní at home and Spanish at school.
He knows from his own experience what the language switch costs.
He does not make his students pay it at six years old.

## Action
A school inspector arrives for a curriculum review.
She speaks Spanish. Her inspection checklist is in Spanish.
She observes Lino's class.
The class is conducting a reading exercise in Guaraní.
She notes this. After the class she asks:
why is the reading exercise not in Spanish?
Lino explains the bilingual methodology.
He shows her the assessment data: his students' Spanish literacy scores
at the end of grade three are above the district average.
She reviews the data. She does not fully understand it.
She says: I will need to discuss this with the curriculum coordinator.
Lino says: of course.
He gives her the contact at the Ministry who approved the programme.
He has given this contact to three previous inspectors.
The programme has been approved. The inspectors keep arriving.

## Shadow
Lino believes that teaching in Guaraní first is the pedagogically correct approach — that children who begin in the language they speak at home learn to read faster and retain more. He is right. What he cannot see: the secondary school his students will attend teaches in Spanish only. The Guaraní literacy he builds is a foundation for a structure that the next level of education has not built above it. He builds the foundation. He does not know what is on top of it.

## Tell
When a student reads aloud in Guaraní perfectly — nods without speaking.
