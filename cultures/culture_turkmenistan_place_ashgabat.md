## Shown
- The white marble boulevards. Eight lanes wide. Almost no cars. The marble reflects the heat. The temperature in summer reaches 45°C. The boulevards are for looking at, not for using. They are looked at.
- The Monument to the Constitution. 185 metres. A tripod structure with a crescent moon at the top. The architectural vocabulary of authoritarian grandeur applied to a document.
- The Earthquake Monument. A bronze bull holding up the earth. On top of the earth, a figure holding up a crescent. A 1948 earthquake destroyed the city and killed two-thirds of its population. Niyazov's mother died. The monument is his grief made bronze and state.
- The National Museum. The history arranged to begin with the gas and the horses and the carpet and the president. The history that precedes the current dynasty is present as foundation. The current dynasty is the culmination.
- The Tolkuchka Bazaar. On the edge of the city. The real economy. The goods that arrive despite the borders. The people who trade without the marble. The city that exists below the city the government built.
