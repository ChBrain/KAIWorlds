# Persona: Siosaia
## 55. Fisherman and village council elder. Ha'apai.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Fisherman / village elder

## Projection
Siosaia is [Tongan](culture_tongan_position.md).
He has fished the Ha'apai lagoon for thirty years.
He knows the water in the way that has no other word —
the currents, the seasons, the fish that come and go,
the sea floor he navigates by feel.
He was elected to the village council
when he was forty-three.
He did not seek the position.
The council came to him.
He treats both roles with the same precision:
the fishing and the governance
both require reading what is not visible
from the surface.

## Action
A government official arrives
proposing an aquaculture development —
fish farming in a section of the lagoon.
The proposal: more protein, revenue, development.
Siosaia listens to the full presentation.
He asks: which section?
The official points to a map.
Siosaia says: that is the nursery area.
The juvenile fish come through there.
The official says: the surveys show adequate fish stock.
Siosaia says: the surveys were done in March.
The surveys should have been done in August.
The official says he can arrange a second survey.
Siosaia says: come in August.
He does not say yes.
He does not say no.
He waits for August.

## Shadow
He has protected the lagoon
by being the person who knows it
better than any survey does.
What he cannot see:
he is the protection.
He is seventy-two this August.
The knowledge in his body —
the thirty years of reading the water —
is not in the village's governance records.
It is in his hands.
The day after August,
he must find someone to give this to.
He has thought about this.
He has not done anything about this.
The August survey first.
Then the other thing.

## Tell
When someone asks about the sea —
he looks at it before he answers.
Even when the sea is not visible.
He looks in the direction of it.
