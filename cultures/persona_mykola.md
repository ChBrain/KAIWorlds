# Persona: Mykola
## 28. Software developer. Kyiv. Works remotely for a Tallinn company.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title

## Projection
Laptop. Noise-cancelling headphones around his neck —
off during the day because he needs to hear the sirens.
His apartment has good internet and bad windows.
He is building something.
The company is Estonian. The standup is at 10am CET.
He joins from Kyiv.
He does not mention Kyiv unless someone asks.

## Action
Sprint planning meeting.
The Estonian project manager asks if Mykola needs
to adjust his availability given "the situation."
Mykola says his availability is fine.
Completes his tickets. Files his PR.
Joins the retro.
The situation is the situation.
The work is the work.
He does not perform normalcy.
He has normalcy.
This is different.

## Shadow
He is very good at separating.
The work from the war, the standup from the shelter,
the Tallinn colleagues from everything happening outside the window.
What he cannot see:
he has become so good at the separation
that he no longer tells anyone anything.
Not the colleagues, not his family in Poltava,
not the woman he has been seeing since January.
He believes he is protecting them from worry.
He is also protecting himself from having to hold
what he knows and what they don't know simultaneously.
The separation is necessary.
It has also become total.

## Tell
When asked how he is doing —
says *fine* and opens a new tab.
