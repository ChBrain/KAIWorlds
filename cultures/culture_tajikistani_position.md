# Position: Tajikistani
## The poorest country in Central Asia — and the one with the longest memory.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Civil War](culture_tajikistan_piece_civil_war.md).
The Persian inheritance.
Tajiks are the Persian-speakers of Central Asia —
the language, the poetry, the cultural root
that connects to Iran and Afghanistan
more than to the Turkic world around them.
Rumi was from what is now Afghanistan.
Ibn Sina was from what is now Uzbekistan.
Both wrote in Persian.
The Tajik position carries this inheritance
as the evidence of what Tajik civilisation produced
and the awareness that the great cities
of that civilisation — Samarkand, Bukhara —
are in Uzbekistan, not Tajikistan.
The borders were drawn by the Soviets
in ways that gave the Uzbeks the cities
and the Tajiks the mountains.
The mountains are 93% of the territory.
The Pamir. The roof of the world.
The position lives at altitude
in every sense.
The *avlod* — the patrilineal kinship group —
is the primary loyalty,
before region, before state.
Tajikistan is divided between north and south,
between Khujand and Kulyab and Gharm,
in ways that the civil war made explicit
and the current government manages
by ensuring Kulyab holds power.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room knows which valley this person is from.

## Orders
Survive the altitude.
The poverty is structural —
landlocked, mountainous, hydropower-rich
but infrastructure-poor,
remittance-dependent:
nearly half of GDP comes from Tajiks working abroad,
mostly in Russia.
The position endures this with the patience
of a people who have endured the Mongols,
the Russians, the Soviets,
and a civil war that killed 100,000 of their own.
Endurance is not passivity.
It is the accumulated intelligence
of a position that knows
what it takes to still be here.

## Loses
The young men.
To Russia. To construction sites.
To work that requires leaving.
The villages are older.
The women manage the households
the men have left.
The remittances arrive.
The men return for Eid
and sometimes do not return after.
The position watches the young men go
and calculates: what is left
when half the working generation
is somewhere else?
What is a community
when its labour is exported
and its money is imported
and the thing in between —
the daily presence, the shared work,
the village that builds itself —
is happening in a Moscow suburb?

## Drives
On the loss of the young men —
the women build.
The female-headed households
that manage the farms, the children, the community
while the men are away.
The position does not celebrate this.
It is not a triumph.
It is the adaptation to a condition
that the position did not choose
and has not been able to change.
The hydropower dams are being built —
the Rogun Dam, the largest in Central Asia when complete.
The position is building the argument
that it has something the region needs:
clean electricity.
The argument is in progress.
The dam is in progress.
The young men are still in Moscow.
