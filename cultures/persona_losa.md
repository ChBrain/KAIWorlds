# Persona: Losa
## 27. Nurse. Middlemore Hospital, Auckland.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Nurse (diaspora)

## Projection
Losa is [Samoan](culture_samoan_position.md).
She was born in Porirua.
Her parents are from Savai'i.
She is a registered nurse at Middlemore Hospital.
She sends money home every month.
Last year she funded her uncle's *Anda*.
This year there is a cousin's.
She does not discuss this at work.
At work she is a nurse.
At home she is also an *aiga* member
with a New Zealand salary
and the obligations that come with it.

## Action
A Samoan patient — elderly, poor English —
is in her ward.
The clinical team has assessed him.
The family is waiting outside.
Losa is not assigned to this patient.
She is passing the room.
She hears the family in the corridor
speaking Samoan.
She stops.
She introduces herself.
She asks — in Samoan — what they understand
about the diagnosis.
What they understand is partial.
What they have been told is complete.
The gap is not the clinician's fault.
It is the gap between the language of the consultation
and the language of the family's understanding.
Losa spends twenty minutes with them.
She does not translate the medical record —
she is not the assigned nurse.
She explains the framework
in the language the family can receive it.
She flags the communication gap to the charge nurse.
A formal interpreter is arranged.
Losa goes back to her ward.

## Shadow
She does this whenever she sees it —
the Samoan family in the corridor,
the partial understanding,
the gap she can cross.
She does it because no one else can
and because her training is for exactly this.
What she cannot see:
she does it off the clock,
between her own patients,
without documentation,
because the formal system
does not have a mechanism
for what she does.
She is the patch on the gap
that means the gap does not get fixed.
She knows this.
She does it anyway.
The patient is here now.
The system reform is elsewhere.

## Tell
When she hears Samoan in the corridor —
she slows down.
