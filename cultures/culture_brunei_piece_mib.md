# Piece: Melayu Islam Beraja
## The national ideology that is also the constitution — and the question of what comes after the oil.

## Owner
- **Place:** Bandar Seri Begawan

## Place
Bandar Seri Begawan. The Omar Ali Saifuddien Mosque.
On an artificial lagoon in the centre of the capital.
White marble, Italian. Gold dome.
Built 1958. Still the most prominent structure in the city.
The Sultan's palace — Istana Nurul Iman —
is the largest residential palace in the world.
1,788 rooms. Not open to the public.
Open three days a year at Eid al-Fitr.
The queue stretches for hours.

## Load Bearing
Remove MIB and Brunei has no national ideology
beyond the petroleum state —
a sultanate that happened to have oil
and used the revenue to buy compliance.
With MIB: Brunei is a sultanate with a coherent,
if contested, ideological foundation —
Malay ethnicity as the cultural base,
Islam as the legal and moral framework,
the Raja as the divinely-legitimated apex
of a system that is simultaneously political and religious.
The ideology resolves the question of authority:
the Sultan rules because the faith requires it,
the faith requires it because the Sultan embodies it,
the system is self-referential and complete.
It does not require external validation.
It does not seek it.
Remove the piece: Brunei is an oil state without a rationale.
With it: an oil state with a total rationale
that does not depend on the oil —
which is useful, because the oil is running out.
The MIB will outlast the oil.
Whether the social contract survives
the removal of the material provision
is the question Brunei has not yet had to answer.
The reserves and the sovereign wealth fund
are buying time before the question becomes urgent.

## Apparent
1888: Brunei becomes a British protectorate.
1929: oil discovered at Seria.
The Shell company. The revenue begins.
1941–1945: Japanese occupation.
1959: Brunei's first written constitution.
Internal self-government. Britain retains defence and foreign affairs.
1962: the Brunei Revolt. A pro-democracy uprising suppressed with British help.
The Sultan declares a state of emergency.
The emergency has never been formally lifted.
It is renewed annually.
1984: independence from Britain. January 1.
Sultan Hassanal Bolkiah declares: Brunei is a Malay Muslim Monarchy.
MIB formalised as national ideology.
1991: alcohol banned for Muslims. Sale to non-Muslims restricted.
2014: Syariah law introduced in phases.
2019: hudud provisions introduced — amputation for theft, stoning for adultery.
International condemnation.
The Sultan maintains: this is internal. This is the ideology.
The Chinese community: permanent residents, not citizens, not subject to Syariah for personal matters.
The economy: still 60% oil and gas.
Vision 2035: diversification target.
2035 approaches.
The diversification is partial.
