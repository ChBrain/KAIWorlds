# Piece: The Folkhemmet
## The people's home — the idea Sweden built itself around.

## Owner
- **Place:** Stockholm

## Place
Stockholm. The Riksdag.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and Swedish consensus loses its structural logic.
The folkhemmet is not a welfare policy — it is the operating mode
of a society that decided everyone would be included in the home
and built institutions to make that true.

## Apparent
Municipal housing blocks in every Swedish city.
Universal healthcare signs in every hospital.
480 days of parental leave per child, available to either parent.
The IKEA catalogue — functional, identical price for everyone.

## Yearbook
1928: Hansson's speech. The concept named.
1932: the Social Democrats win power.
They hold it, with brief interruptions, for 44 years.
The folkhemmet built during that time.
1976: first non-Social Democrat government since 1932.
The folkhemmet stays. The institutions are too embedded to remove.
1994: Sweden joins the EU.
The folkhemmet adjusts. It does not dissolve.
2006–2021: centre-right governments.
The folkhemmet adjusts further.
The welfare state remains the largest in Europe per capita.
2022: Sweden joins NATO.
The security model changes.
The domestic model does not.
The consensus that built the folkhemmet
is still building something.
