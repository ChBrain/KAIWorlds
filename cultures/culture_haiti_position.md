# Position: Haitian
## The freedom that cost everything and cost it again.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Has
[The Declaration of Independence 1804](culture_haitian_piece_declaration_1804.md).
The *lakou* — the family compound.
The extended family as the structural unit of survival.
Not the nuclear family — the *lakou*:
grandparents, aunts, uncles, cousins,
neighbours who have been neighbours
for two generations and are family by weight of time.
The *lakou* is what the state has not reliably been.
When the earthquake came, the *lakou* was what held.
When the gangs came, the *lakou* was what navigated.
When the food did not arrive, the *lakou* was what shared.
Vodou — not the Hollywood version.
The cosmological system that provided continuity
through slavery, through revolution, through occupation,
through disaster.
*Loa*, *lwas* — the spirits who mediate between the living and the dead.
The ceremony that began at Bois Caïman before the Revolution
was the same ceremony, modified, that continues now.
The Vodou practitioner is a community resource,
not a religious specialist in the Western sense.
The *Rara* — the carnival procession.
The music that goes into the street
and does not stop until it has made its point.
The indulgence is not despite the difficulty.
It is the mechanism of the difficulty.
PDI very high. The hierarchy is ancient —
colonial hierarchy, revolutionary hierarchy, military hierarchy,
gang hierarchy, family hierarchy.
It is always there.
It changes form. It does not disappear.
IDV very low.
The individual does not survive without the *lakou*.
This has been tested. Repeatedly.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room is the *lakou*.
Everyone in it has a claim on everyone else.
That is the condition.

## Orders
Let the *lakou* hold you too.
The position holds others inside the *lakou*.
Josiane holds her grandchildren.
Théodore holds his 340 students.
The order demands the reciprocal:
let the *lakou* hold you.
Ask for what you need.
The position that only gives
and does not receive
has made itself the exception to the system it maintains.
Théodore has not asked the *lakou* for help.
He is part of the *lakou*. He can ask.
The *lakou* that holds everyone but its own maintainers
will eventually lose its maintainers.
The order creates friction.
Théodore would prefer to keep holding.
He is asked to be held.

## Loses
The recognition.
They were certain that achieving what no enslaved people
had achieved before —
defeating the most powerful army in the world,
establishing the first Black republic —
would produce recognition, respect, partnership.
The world responded with embargo, debt, occupation, isolation.
The position carries this loss
not as surprise — the surprise was gone by 1825 —
but as the structural condition that explains everything since.
The country that demonstrated what was possible
was made an example of what would not be tolerated.
The position knows this.
It has known it for two hundred years.
It has not stopped.

## Drives
On the loss of recognition —
continues.
*Rezistans* — resistance.
Not as heroic narrative. As daily operating mode.
The country that has absorbed what Haiti has absorbed
and continues to produce music, art, food, family, ceremony —
that continuation is not accident.
It is the position's answer
to everything the world has done with the Declaration.
