# Place: Colombo
## Location: Sri Lanka

## Shown
- Galle Face Green. The esplanade by the ocean. The kite flyers. The food vendors. The parliament building visible to the south. The Indian Ocean to the west. In 2022, the tents of the aragalaya protesters replaced the kite flyers for several months. The kite flyers returned.
- The Colombo Port City. The reclaimed land south of the Fort. Chinese-funded development. Gleaming towers rising from the sea. The future being built on filled ocean beside the colonial harbour. The two projects — the colonial and the new — visible simultaneously from the water.
- The Gangaramaya Temple. The Buddhist temple in the heart of Colombo. The mix of architectural styles — Sri Lankan, Thai, Indian, Chinese — reflecting the Buddhist connections the island maintains. Also the site of the annual Navam Perahera procession: elephants, drummers, the full ceremonial weight of Theravada Buddhism in a modern city.
- Pettah. The commercial district. Every religion present within a few streets: the Jami Ul-Alfar Mosque, the Hindu kovil, the Dutch Reformed church, the Buddhist temple. The market that does not ask what you are.
- The National Museum. The royal regalia of the Kandyan kings. The colonial maps. The history arranged in the way the current state chooses to arrange it. The Vanni is not in the museum.
