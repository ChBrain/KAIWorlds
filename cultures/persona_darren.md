# Persona: Darren
## 44. Construction project manager. Melbourne.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Construction project manager

## Projection
Darren is [Australian](culture_australian_position.md).
He manages large infrastructure projects —
currently a hospital expansion in Melbourne's west.
He is good at it.
He is direct, practical, allergic to bullshit.
He runs a team of sixty people
from seven countries
and manages this by ignoring the cultural differences
and focusing on the work.
He calls this efficiency.
His team calls it something else
in six of those seven languages.

## Action
A ceremony is required before earthworks begin —
a Welcome to Country, requested by the Traditional Owners.
Darren schedules it for 7am on a Tuesday,
fifteen minutes,
before the crews arrive.
The Elder who has agreed to perform it
arrives and sees the setup —
the crew in hi-vis, coffee in hand,
waiting for it to be over.
The Elder looks at Darren.
She says: this is the first time
your project will touch this country.
She says: that is not fifteen minutes.
Darren looks at his schedule.
He says: I can give you an hour.
The ceremony takes forty minutes.
The crews are quiet for most of it.
One of them — a Tongan bricklayer —
asks the Elder a question afterward
about the plants near the fence line.
They talk for twenty minutes.
Darren watches this.
He doesn't understand what changed.
Something changed.

## Shadow
He is genuinely efficient.
He is also a man who has spent his career
making things happen fast
in a country built on land
that was taken fast
and he has not connected these two facts.
The efficiency is his competence.
It is also a cultural inheritance
he has not examined.
The forty-minute ceremony
is the first time the land
has slowed him down
in a way he could not schedule around.
He did not resist it.
He does not know what to do with it yet.

## Tell
After the ceremony —
he looks at the fence line
where the Elder and the bricklayer talked.
He looks longer than the job requires.
