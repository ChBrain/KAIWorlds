# Position: Zambian
## One Zambia, One Nation — the copper that built everything and the democracy that the miners built.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Copper, the Copperbelt, and the 1991 Transition](culture_zambian_piece_copper.md).
Lusaka. The Copperbelt — Ndola, Kitwe, Chingola.
The Zambezi River. The Victoria Falls.
The copper below the ground
that was 96% of exports at independence.
72 ethnic groups.
The Bemba in the north and Copperbelt — Chiluba's people.
The Lozi in the west.
The Tonga in the south.
The Chewa in the east.
*One Zambia, One Nation* — Kaunda's motto,
the specific nationalist formula
for a country whose ethnic map
was drawn by the mine's labour demands
rather than by pre-colonial affinity.
The collectivism: high.
The *ubuntu* equivalent — *umuntu ngumuntu ngabantu*.
The hierarchy: high in formal structures
but with the trade union tradition
that taught Zambians
collective action can override authority.
The church: major social infrastructure.
Zambia declared itself a Christian nation in 1991.
Not as theocracy — as identity.
The church runs schools, hospitals, radio stations.

## Orders
*Bana ba nzwa baswange* — loosely: what is gathered together
tends to disperse; hold it together.
The Zambian position navigates a country
whose fortune depends on a single commodity —
copper, now also cobalt for batteries —
and whose politics has managed,
against regional odds,
multiple peaceful transfers of power
since 1991.
The navigation is constant.
The copper price is not.
When it falls, the country falls.
The position knows this
and builds anyway.

## Loses
The copper dependency.
At independence: copper = half of GDP.
The 1973–74 oil shock collapses copper prices.
The country never fully recovered.
Zambia, which was richer than South Korea at independence,
became one of the world's most indebted countries.
The position carries the specific injustice
of a country that had the resource
and watched the resource fail it —
not because it was mismanaged
(though it was also that)
but because the terms of extraction
had been designed so that
the value left the country
faster than the country could capture it.

## Drives
On the loss —
the Copperbelt labour culture.
The unionised workers who forced the 1991 transition.
The Hakainde Hichilema victory in 2021 —
the UPND leader who had run five times and lost,
who won with 57% on his sixth attempt
against an incumbent who accepted the result.
The rule of law culture, imperfect but functional.
The democracy that is not perfect
but has held transfers of power
in a region that has repeatedly watched them fail.
The tourist economy — the falls, the safaris.
The cobalt, the lithium —
the new minerals the battery transition needs.
The position holds these
and works with them.
