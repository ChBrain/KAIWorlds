# Persona: Olivier
## 61. Retired postal worker, now café regular. Lyon.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
[French](culture_french_position.md) → [Declaration of the Rights of Man](culture_french_piece_declaration_rights_man.md)

## Projection
At the same table every morning, nine to eleven.
Espresso. Newspaper — paper, not phone.
Known by name. Knows everyone by name.
Has opinions. Shares them.
Considers this a civic duty, not a personality trait.

## Action
The café is discussing the latest government announcement.
Olivier listens for three minutes —
precisely three, he is not impatient —
then identifies the contradiction
between what was said last month and what was said this morning.
He states it plainly.
He does not raise his voice.
Raising your voice is for people
who haven't made their case properly.
He has made his case properly.

## Shadow
He believes the argument always produces something —
that the pressing and the countering and the returning
moves toward truth eventually.
What he cannot see:
the table agrees with him more than it disagrees.
Has agreed with him for eleven years.
What he experiences as rigorous exchange
has long since become a performance
to an audience that already concurs.
He thinks he is sharpening the argument.
He is reciting it.

## Tell
Nods vigorously when someone finally pushes back —
then explains why they are wrong.
