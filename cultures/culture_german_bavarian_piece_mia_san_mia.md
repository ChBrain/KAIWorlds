# Piece: Mia san mia
## We are who we are. Not an explanation. A position.

## Owner
- **Place:** Munich

## Place
Munich. The Marienplatz.
The Neues Rathaus. The Glockenspiel.
Also: the Hofbräuhaus, three minutes away.
Also: the Allianz Arena, eleven kilometres north.
The same three words on the wall of FC Bayern's dressing room.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Bavarian position loses its ground.
The claim is not pride in the ordinary sense.
It is the refusal to explain.
Other German states exist within Germany.
Bavaria exists within Germany
and within itself simultaneously.
*Mia san mia* is the sentence
that does not require the listener's agreement
to be true.

## Apparent
Three words in Bavarian dialect.
Standard German: *Wir sind wir.*
Not quite: the dialect carries something
standard German cannot.
*Mia san mia* is not *Wir sind wir.*
The grammar is the same.
The weight is different.
On the wall of FC Bayern Munich's dressing room
since Uli Hoeneß put it there.
Before that: in the mouths of grandmothers
who could not tell you when they first heard it.
The Tracht worn to the Oktoberfest -
not as costume, as clothing.
The Lederhosen of a man who wears them
every September,
as his father did,
as his father did.
The Freistaat - Free State - a title
that carries no legal weight
and every cultural weight.

## Yearbook
1806: Bavaria becomes a kingdom.
The Wittelsbach dynasty has ruled for over six centuries.
The kingdom lasts until 1918.
The Freistaat declared on the same day
as the German Republic.
Bayern before Germany.
1871: Bavaria joins the German Empire
under Prussian dominance.
The Bavarians do not forget
that they joined.
The joining was a choice.
Choices can be made differently.
1923: Hitler's Beer Hall Putsch, Munich.
Bavaria carries this.
It does not always face it directly.
1933–1945: Dachau, first concentration camp,
sixteen kilometres from Munich.
The reckoning is uneven.
1949: Bavaria signs the Basic Law
with a formal objection to its terms.
Signs anyway.
The objection is still recorded.
1957: Franz Josef Strauß becomes CSU chairman.
Bavaria's political dominance of its own affairs
becomes structural.
The CSU governs Bavaria for all but three years
from 1957 to the present.
2006: Munich hosts the World Cup.
The Allianz Arena glows red.
*Mia san mia* does not require a world cup
to be true.
The world cup confirms what was already settled.
