# Place: Canberra / Uluru
## Location: Australia

## Owner

## Shown
- Parliament House. Canberra.
  Built into Capital Hill.
  The Aboriginal mosaic at the entrance —
  *Meeting Place* by Michael Tjakamarra Nelson.
  The building that contains both
  the Uluru Statement and its rejection
  in the same institution.
  The flag of Australia flying above it.
  The Aboriginal flag not flying above it.
  Both flags present in the country.
  One flag on the building.
- Uluru. The Northern Territory.
  The sandstone rises 348 metres from the plain.
  The Anangu have been here for at least 10,000 years.
  The rock changes colour at dawn and dusk —
  orange, red, purple.
  The tourists photograph the colour.
  The Anangu photograph nothing.
  The sacred sites on the rock are not to be photographed
  and this request is now posted
  at the entry to the national park.
  The climbing ban since 2019.
  The tourists who climbed in the last days
  before the ban took effect.
  The Anangu who watched them.
- The National Museum of Australia. Canberra.
  The exhibits on the Stolen Generations.
  The personal testimony.
  The objects.
  The museum that tries to hold
  the full history in the same building
  as the founding celebration.
  The attempt is genuine.
  The full history does not fit in any building.
- The beach. Any beach.
  The surf lifesavers in their red and yellow.
  The barbeque.
  The cricket.
  The country that is also this —
  the lived warmth and the physical ease
  that is genuinely part of the Australian experience
  and is not cancelled by the history
  and does not cancel it.
  Both are the country.
  The beach is real.
  The history is real.
  They occupy the same land.
