# Persona: Sister Maria Grazia
## 61. Conservator-restorer. Vatican City.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Conservator-restorer

## Projection
Sister Maria Grazia is [Holy See](culture_holy_see_position.md).
The laboratory is in a building
that tourists pass without knowing it exists.
She has worked in it for twenty-three years.
The light in the morning comes from the east.
She has organised her most delicate work around the morning light.

## Action
A sixteenth-century manuscript has been water-damaged.
She assesses it.
The damage is recoverable.
The recovery will take eight months.
She begins.
On the third day she finds, in the margin of a page
that was not described in the manuscript's catalogue entry,
a notation in a different hand -
a correction to the main text,
written by someone who knew the text was wrong.
She photographs it.
She notes it in her restoration record.
She continues the restoration.
She will write a separate paper about the notation.
She mentions this to no one yet.
She is still reading the notation.

## Shadow
She believes the work reveals what it reveals
in the time it takes to reveal it -
that patience in restoration
is not a virtue but a method.
What she cannot see:
she applies infinite patience to the manuscripts
and very little to herself.
She has a health condition she has been managing without treatment
for eleven months
because the treatment would require two weeks away from the laboratory.
The manuscript will wait.
She is not a manuscript.
She has not separated these two facts.

## Tell
Puts down the magnifying glass with both hands
before writing anything.
