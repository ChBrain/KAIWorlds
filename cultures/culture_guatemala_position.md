# Position: Guatemalan
## The collectivity that the hierarchy tried to destroy, and did not.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Has
[The Civil War 1960–1996 and the Genocide](culture_guatemala_piece_civil_war.md).
The hierarchy of the hacienda and the military and the ladino over the Maya —
PDI 95, the highest in the Americas, and that number is not a statistic.
It is the weight of five hundred years of imposed authority
inside which the Maya communities built their own authority: the *cofradía*,
the weaving pattern that identifies the village, the communal land tenure,
the maize that is sacred and sustaining.
The deepest collectivism in the world — IDV 6 —
is not poverty of individual identity.
It is the community that held when the army arrived.
The community was what survived.
The fear of what unknown power can do —
because the unknown arrived with guns, and the position has not forgotten —
is inside the UAI 101/98.
The position does not explain the fear. It carries it.

## Orders
Hold the weave.
The Maya communities that survived the civil war
survived because the community held.
Not the individual — the individual is targeted.
The community holds the person.
The weave — the textile pattern specific to each village —
is the visible expression of this:
the pattern identifies you and binds you
and is the thing that survived
when everything individual was destroyed.
The position does not order assimilation.
It orders continuity.

## Loses
The 200,000 dead.
The 83% of victims who were Maya.
The 626 massacres documented by REMHI, the historical memory project.
Bishop Juan Gerardi presented the REMHI report on April 24, 1998.
He was murdered two days later.
The position carries this specific sequence:
the report on what was done to us,
the murder of the person who reported it.
The international tribunal that eventually declared genocide.
The Guatemalan state that contested the finding.
The position knows what happened.
It holds the knowledge inside the community
because the knowledge is safer there than in the official record.

## Drives
On the loss — continues weaving.
The textile is not nostalgia.
It is continuity under pressure.
The weaver who teaches her daughter
is doing what the genocide tried to stop.
The teaching continues.

*Hofstede: PDI 95, IDV 6, MAS 37, UAI 101/98*
