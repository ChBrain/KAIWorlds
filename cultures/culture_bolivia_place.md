# Place: La Paz / Sucre
## Location: Bolivia (two capitals)

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Shown
- La Paz: the seat of government. 3,640 metres above sea level. The highest capital city in the world. The air is thin. Visitors notice. Residents do not.
- The Mercado de las Brujas. The Witches' Market in La Paz. *Ch'uño* — freeze-dried potatoes, a technology 2,000 years old. Llama foetuses for offering to the *Pachamama*. Herbs. Amulets. The market is not tourism. It is supply.
- El Alto. The city above La Paz, 4,150 metres. Largely Aymara. The fastest-growing city in Bolivia in the 1990s and 2000s as rural populations urbanised. The *cholets* — the multi-storey buildings with Andean baroque facades and rooftop event halls, built by Aymara architects in a style that deliberately synthesises indigenous and modern. The aesthetic is intentional. The statement is intentional.
- Sucre: the constitutional capital. Colonial. White buildings. The Casa de la Libertad where independence was declared, August 6, 1825. Where the 2009 constitution was also debated and signed.
- The Salar de Uyuni. The largest salt flat in the world. 10,582 square kilometres of white. The world's largest lithium reserve underneath. The sky reflects in the wet season. Both facts are the country.
