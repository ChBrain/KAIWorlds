# Persona: Inés
## 39. Environmental lawyer. Quito.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Environmental lawyer

## Projection
Inés is [Ecuadorian](culture_ecuador_position.md).
She works for a small NGO that takes cases under Article 71 —
the rights of nature provision —
before Ecuadorian courts.
She has been doing this since 2012.
The year the Yasuní decision was not yet made.
She has handled sixteen Article 71 cases.
Four resulted in injunctions that temporarily halted extractive activities.
Two of those four are still in force.
Two were overturned on appeal.
Twelve resulted in no relief.
She tracks these numbers.
She does not publicise them.
She publicises the four.
The four matter. The twelve also matter.
She knows both sets of numbers are the truth.

## Action
A river in the Oriente — the Amazonian region — is named as plaintiff in a case.
This is standard Article 71 procedure: the river has rights, a human representative brings the case on behalf of the river.
Inés is the lawyer.
The defendant is a mining company operating upstream.
She argues: the river's right to regenerate has been violated by sedimentation from the mining operations.
The evidence is water quality data. Photographs. Community testimony.
The judge asks: how do I assess the river's testimony?
Inés says: the river's testimony is the water quality data.
The river speaks through its condition.
The judge accepts this framing.
The injunction is granted.
The mining company appeals.
The injunction is under appeal.
Inés opens the next case.

## Shadow
Inés believes that each case won
expands the jurisprudence — builds the body of law
that makes the next case more winnable.
She is right.
What she cannot see:
the twelve cases that produced no relief
also built jurisprudence.
They established the limits of what Article 71 can currently do.
Each lost case was also precedent.
The body of law being built
includes the boundaries of the right
as clearly as it includes its successes.
Inés reads the four successful cases carefully.
She reads the twelve less carefully.
The twelve know something the four do not.
She has not yet fully read what the twelve know.

## Tell
When asked how many cases she has won —
says "four" before she says "out of sixteen."
