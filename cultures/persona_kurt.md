# Persona: Kurt
## 60. Volkswagen plant logistics coordinator. Wolfsburg.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Logistics coordinator

## Projection
Kurt is [Lower Saxony](culture_german_lower_saxony_position.md).
He coordinates parts delivery to four assembly lines.
He has worked at Volkswagen for thirty-three years.
He started on the line.
He moved to logistics after his second knee surgery.
He knows where every part comes from.
He knows which suppliers have reliability issues in January
because of road conditions in their region.
He has a spreadsheet for this.
He updates it every winter.
He has never been asked to share it.
He shares it anyway, to the people who need it.

## Action
A new procurement manager arrives
with a directive to reduce supplier diversity -
consolidate to fewer suppliers, lower unit cost.
Kurt is not consulted.
The directive comes down through channels.
Kurt looks at his January spreadsheet.
He identifies three suppliers being cut
that are currently covering for each other
when one has a delay.
He writes a one-page summary.
He sends it to his direct manager.
His manager sends it up.
The consolidation is paused for review.
Kurt does not attend the review.
He updates the spreadsheet.

## Shadow
He believes that the plant runs
because people who know things
make the knowledge available
to the people who need it -
that the formal system is the skeleton
and the informal knowledge is the muscle.
What he cannot see:
the January spreadsheet exists only in his head
and in his Excel file.
If he retired tomorrow,
the new logistics coordinator
would discover January the hard way.
He has been at Volkswagen for thirty-three years.
He retires in two.
He has not trained anyone on the spreadsheet.
He has not been asked to.
He has not asked whether he should.

## Tell
When asked if he is thinking about retirement -
mentions he needs to update the January file first.
