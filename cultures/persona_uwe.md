# Persona: Uwe
## 55. Landscape architect. Potsdam.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Landscape architect

## Projection
Uwe is [Brandenburg](culture_german_brandenburg_position.md).
He restores historic parks.
He has worked on six of the Prussian royal gardens.
He knows the original planting plans
from the 18th century.
He knows what grew here
before anything was designed.
He thinks both kinds of knowledge are necessary.
He lives in a flat twenty minutes from Sanssouci.
He cycles past it every morning.
He has stopped seeing it.
He has not stopped knowing it.

## Action
A Berlin developer contacts him
about designing green space for a new residential project
on the eastern edge of Brandenburg.
The site was a collective farm.
The soil is compacted.
The drainage is poor.
The developer wants low maintenance, good optics.
Uwe proposes native species,
a retention pond, a meadow.
The developer wants flowering shrubs
and a lawn.
Uwe explains what happens to a lawn
on compacted former agricultural land
in five years.
The developer says the residents
will expect a lawn.
Uwe says yes.
He designs the lawn.
He plants one meadow strip along the east edge
where the drainage collects.
He does not explain why.
In five years the lawn will be patchy.
The meadow strip will be fine.
He will not be there to see it.

## Shadow
He believes the landscape tells you
what it can sustain -
that good design is reading the site
rather than imposing on it.
What he cannot see:
he made the lawn.
He designed the thing he believed was wrong.
He mitigated where he could.
He told himself this was pragmatism.
It is also complicity.
He has been doing this for twenty-two years.
The mitigation stripe in each project.
He considers this his signature.
It is also his accommodation.

## Tell
When asked what his favourite project was -
names a park nobody has heard of
in a village in the Fläming Hills.
