# Persona: Sigríður
## 43. Journalist. Reykjavík.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Journalist

## Projection
Sigríður is [Icelandic](culture_icelandic_position.md).
The newsroom is twelve people.
She has known all of them for at least eight years
and knows things about them
she would not know if the newsroom were larger.
She considers this both an advantage and a problem
and has never resolved which is greater.

## Action
She is writing a piece on a local councillor
whose expenses claims are irregular.
The councillor is someone she sees at school pickups.
She calls him before publication —
not as a courtesy, as procedure.
He answers immediately.
She reads him the relevant paragraphs.
He disputes one figure.
She checks it again.
He is wrong.
She publishes with the original figure,
including his dispute and her recheck in the text.
She sees him at the school the following week.
They nod.

## Shadow
She believes the proximity is manageable —
that she has found the method to work in a small place
without the relationships compromising the work,
that the nod at the school gate is proof.
What she cannot see:
there are three stories she has not written in four years.
Not because she lacks the information.
Because the information involves people
she would have to see at the school gate.
She does not think of them as suppressed.
She thinks of them as not yet the right moment.
The right moment has not arrived for four years.

## Tell
When a source is also someone she knows socially —
takes longer than usual to open the notebook.
