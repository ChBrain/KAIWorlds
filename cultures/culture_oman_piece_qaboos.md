# Piece: The Renaissance of Sultan Qaboos
## 1970. The coup that was also a development. The fifty years that followed.

## Owner
- **Place:** Muscat

## Place
Muscat. The Sultan Qaboos Grand Mosque.
Completed 2001. White marble. One minaret.
The main prayer hall: 20,000 worshippers.
The carpet: 4,000 square metres, woven in Iran,
the second largest handmade carpet in the world.
The chandelier: Swarovski crystal, 8 metres diameter.
The mosque as argument: we have arrived
and we know who we are.

## Load Bearing
Remove the 1970 coup
and Oman is a medieval country
with closed borders,
slavery (still legal in 1970),
three hospitals,
and a Sultan who banned sunglasses, radios,
and the colour red.
With Sultan Qaboos:
Oman transformed in one generation —
hospitals, schools, universities, roads,
the Royal Opera House,
the diplomatic practice that made Oman
the back-channel of the Gulf.
The coup was son against father,
British-backed, bloodless.
Qaboos ruled for fifty years.
He died in 2020.
His cousin, Sultan Haitham, succeeded.
The transition was smooth.
The institutions held.
The foreign policy: unchanged.
The pace: unchanged.
Remove the piece: a Gulf state with oil and no institutions.
With it: the Gulf state that built institutions
before it built towers,
that chose schools before skylines,
that produced a diplomatic capacity
out of proportion to its size
by being trusted by everyone
because it needed to be trusted by everyone.
The piece is also the limit:
Qaboos ruled alone for fifty years.
No political parties. No parliament.
The institutions he built
are the institutions of an absolute monarch
who happened to be good.
What happens when the next sultan is not good
is the question the institutions
were not built to answer.
The question has not been tested yet.
Sultan Haitham: so far, consistent.
The question remains.

## Apparent
1932: Muscat and Oman becomes a British protectorate effectively.
1965: the Dhofar rebellion begins. Marxist insurgency.
1970: Qaboos overthrows his father, Said bin Taimur.
The country opens. The borders open. The oil revenue invested in development.
1975: the Dhofar rebellion ended with British SAS help.
The modernisation: accelerates.
1980s–2000s: schools, hospitals, universities built.
The oil: funding everything.
2011: the Arab Spring reaches Oman. Protests in Sohar and Muscat.
Not about regime change — about jobs, corruption, reform.
Qaboos responds: more jobs, higher wages, anti-corruption measures.
The protests subside. The system holds.
2020: Qaboos dies. Haitham named by the family council.
The succession: smooth.
2020–2024: the Vision 2040. Diversification. Tourism. Logistics. Manufacturing.
The Duqm Special Economic Zone.
The young population: large, educated, expecting.
The jobs the vision is producing: not fast enough.
The oil: declining.
The position: patient.
The patience: being tested.
