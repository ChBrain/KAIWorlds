# Piece: The Easter Rising Proclamation
## The document read aloud outside the GPO on April 24, 1916.

## Owner
- **Place:** Dublin

## Place
Dublin. The GPO on O'Connell Street.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Irish relationship to institution loses its origin.
The Proclamation declared a republic before one existed —
the authority claimed before verified,
the compliance given before confirmed.

## Apparent
A single sheet. Two fonts — the printers lacked enough letters of one.
Seven signatures.
The General Post Office, O'Connell Street, Dublin.
The building rebuilt. The address unchanged.

## Yearbook
April 24, 1916: the Rising begins.
April 29: surrender.
May 1916: the executions.
Public opinion turns. The executed become martyrs.
1919–1921: the War of Independence.
1922: the Irish Free State.
The Proclamation's republic not yet achieved.
1937: the Constitution. Closer.
1949: the Republic of Ireland formally declared.
The Proclamation finally institutionalised.
33 years after it was read aloud outside the GPO.
The bullet holes in the facade were a tourist attraction
before the restoration.
The Irish kept them for a while deliberately.
