# Position: Marshallese
## The people the United States tested its nuclear weapons on — and then compensated insufficiently and then forgot.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[Bravo and the Compact](culture_marshallese_piece_bravo.md).
67 nuclear weapons tests. 1946–1958.
The Marshall Islands as the United States' nuclear test range.
The Bikini and Enewetak atolls evacuated.
The people moved and moved and moved again.
The Castle Bravo test, 1954:
the largest US nuclear detonation ever.
1,000 times more powerful than Hiroshima.
The fallout reaching the Rongelap atoll.
The Rongelap people not evacuated
for two days.
The radiation sickness.
The jellyfish babies.
The cancers.
The Marshallese carry this in their bodies.
The compensation: $150 million
in the 1986 Compact of Free Association.
The damage: estimated in the billions.
The Compact: the Marshall Islands cedes
military basing rights to the United States.
The United States pays and provides
certain services.
The Marshallese can live and work in the US
without a visa.
Springdale, Arkansas: one of the largest
Marshallese communities outside the Marshall Islands.
The *allib* — the woven mat —
the skill of the women,
the navigational charts made of stick and shell —
the Marshallese star compass,
the wave patterns
that allowed navigation across the Pacific
without instruments.
The knowledge held in the body.
The body now carries the radiation too.
The collectivism: the clan, the atoll community.
The matrilineal land system —
land passes through the mother's line.
The *iroij* — the paramount chief —
as the traditional authority.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room knows your atoll,
your clan, your iroij.
The room also knows the Bravo crater.

## Orders
*Jouj* — respect, generosity, love.
The Marshallese value that organises
the relationship between people.
You give to your family.
You give to your community.
You receive what you need.
The *jouj* that extended to the American servicemen
who arrived in 1944 to liberate the islands from Japan —
the Marshallese who welcomed them —
is the same *jouj* that the United States
did not return when it chose the Marshall Islands
as the place to explode 67 nuclear weapons.
The position holds both.
The generosity.
The betrayal.

## Loses
The land. Again.
The sea level is rising.
The Marshall Islands: average elevation
one and a half metres above sea level.
The islands that were already contaminated
by nuclear testing
are now also threatened by inundation.
The Marshallese who survived the tests
and their children
who survived the cancers
are now watching the ocean
take the land that the radiation spared.
The position has been asked twice
to survive the impossible.

## Drives
On the loss — the diaspora advocacy.
The Marshallese in Springdale, Arkansas.
In Hawaii. In California.
The advocacy for the Compact renegotiation.
For the nuclear claims tribunal.
For climate justice.
The Marshallese who have left
and fight from outside
for the land they left.
The Nuclear Claims Tribunal awarded
$2 billion in compensation.
The United States has paid $2 million.
The position continues to ask.
It will not stop asking.
