## Shown
- The D-U-D urban area.
  Delap-Uliga-Djarrit.
  The three connected islets.
  The narrow road.
  The ocean on both sides.
  The overcrowding: half the population
  on these strips of land.
  The same geography as Tarawa —
  the Pacific micro-capital condition.
- The Alele Museum.
  The stick charts.
  The traditional Marshallese navigation instruments —
  grids of sticks and shells
  that encode wave and current patterns.
  The instrument for reading the ocean
  without seeing land.
  The navigational knowledge
  that brought the ancestors here
  and kept them here for 3,000 years.
  In the museum now.
  The ocean outside: the same ocean.
- The US Embassy.
  The Compact administration offices.
  The American military presence
  at Kwajalein — not Majuro, but the same islands.
  The F-35 testing range.
  The infrastructure of the relationship
  that has defined Marshallese foreign policy
  since 1946.
  The relationship is not equal.
  The infrastructure is present.
- The church.
  The United Church of Christ —
  the denomination the American missionaries brought.
  The Marshallese who converted
  and maintained the Christianity
  through the testing years.
  The prayers for the nuclear dead
  said in the same church
  as the prayers for the sea level dead.
  Both congregations: the same people.
