## Load Bearing
Remove the nuclear-free constitution
and Palau follows the FSM and Marshall Islands path —
Compact of Free Association agreed by the mid-1980s
without the specific fight over nuclear weapons.
With it: the most sustained act of small-state resistance
in modern Pacific history.
The Palauan constitution of 1980 included
the first nuclear-free provision
in any national constitution.
The United States' Compact proposals required
authority to bring nuclear weapons through Palauan waters.
Palau's constitution required a 75% supermajority
to override the nuclear clause.
The referendums: 1983, 1984, 1986, 1987, 1990, 1992, 1993.
Seven times the Palauan people voted.
Never once achieving the 75% threshold
to permit nuclear weapons.
The 1987 and 1990 votes:
the presidents who supported the Compact
were assassinated or died in suspicious circumstances.
The political cost of the position: extreme.
The position: held.
1994: Compact signed with nuclear restrictions maintained.
Palau sovereign.
No nuclear weapons.
The 2009 shark sanctuary is the second piece:
President Johnson Toribiong announces
Palauan waters — 600,000 square kilometres —
declared a shark sanctuary.
Commercial shark fishing banned.
Shark finning banned.
The economic argument: a single reef shark
is worth $179,000 in dive tourism over its lifetime.
Dead: $108.
The sanctuary as economic rationalism
and as moral statement simultaneously.
The model: adopted by the Bahamas, Honduras, others.
The world's ocean governance
shifted by 18,000 people
who understood their reef.
