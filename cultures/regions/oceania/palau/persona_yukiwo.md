# Persona: Yukiwo
## 58. Retired senator. Koror.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Retired senator

## Projection
Yukiwo is [Palauan](culture_palauan_position.md).
He served twelve years in the Olbiil era Kelulau —
the Palauan Senate.
He was one of the senators who voted
for the shark sanctuary legislation in 2009.
He remembers the vote.
He remembers what was said in the chamber
before it.
He is retired now.
He gives talks to schoolchildren
about the nuclear-free constitution.
He does this because
the schoolchildren do not know
what it cost.

## Action
A Chinese investment group
is proposing a major resort development
in a coastal area.
The proposal has government support.
Yukiwo is asked to speak at a community forum.
He is not an official.
He has no power to stop anything.
He comes anyway.
He says: I want to tell you about 1993.
He tells the seventh referendum.
He says: we were told the Compact required nuclear access.
He says: we were told we had to choose between
sovereignty and security.
He says: we chose sovereignty.
He looks at the investment proposal on the table.
He says: the question being asked tonight
is not the same question.
He says: but the quality of the answer
should be the same quality.
He sits down.
He does not say what the answer should be.

## Shadow
He trusts the community
to make the decision
with the same care
that the 1993 decision required.
What he cannot see:
the community that made the 1993 decision
is not the same community —
the generation that lived it
is mostly his age.
The younger generation
has the inheritance
without the lived weight of it.
He is trying to transfer the weight
through the story.
The story is the best tool he has.
It may not be heavy enough.

## Tell
When he tells the story of the referendums —
he counts them.
One. Two. Three.
He counts all seven.
He does not skip any.
