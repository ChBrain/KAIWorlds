# Position: Vanuatuan
## The country that was governed by two colonial powers simultaneously — and emerged with more languages than any comparably-sized nation on earth.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Condominium and Kastom](culture_vanuatuan_piece_condominium.md).
The New Hebrides Condominium: 1906–1980.
France and Britain governing the same islands simultaneously.
Two administrations. Two court systems.
Two currencies. Two police forces.
The ni-Vanuatu people navigating both —
and both navigating the third system,
which was neither French nor British:
*kastom*.
The traditional governance.
The chiefs. The ceremonies. The knowledge systems.
The *nimangki* — the graded society
of northern Vanuatu —
where status is earned through ceremonies,
through the sacrifice of pigs,
through the accumulation of knowledge
that cannot be bought,
only performed.
138 languages in a country of 320,000 people.
The highest language density per capita on earth.
Bislama — Vanuatu's creole —
is the language the languages talk to each other through.
The kastom and the Christianity:
Vanuatu is 95% Christian.
The missionaries arrived before the colonisers
and stayed longer.
The Christianity and the kastom
operate in the same person —
the chief who is also a deacon,
the ceremony that includes the prayer,
the pigs sacrificed before the church service.
The collectivism: the village.
The island. The language group.
The obligation to the chief.
The state is what you use
when the village cannot solve it.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room knows which island,
which chief, which kastom area.

## Orders
Maintain kastom.
Not as cultural heritage performance —
as the living governance
that the condominium never replaced
and independence did not replace
because nothing more functional was available.
The kastom court resolves land disputes.
The chief mediates family conflict.
The ceremony marks the significant events.
The government in Port Vila
handles the passport and the international agreements.
The kastom handles the rest.

## Loses
The land.
Land in Vanuatu is constitutionally
owned by indigenous ni-Vanuatu only.
No freehold sale to foreigners.
The developers lease.
The leases run 75 years.
The kastom landowners sign leases
they do not always fully understand —
negotiated in a language
that is not their first, second, or third —
and the resort is built
on the land that the chief's grandfather
held under kastom tenure
for the whole of living memory.
The lease revenue is real.
The replacement of kastom tenure
with lease tenure is also real.
The position carries both.

## Drives
On the loss —
the resilience.
Vanuatu sits in the Pacific Ring of Fire.
Cyclones. Earthquakes. Volcanoes.
Ambae evacuated twice in three years
due to volcanic activity.
The kastom knowledge of which hills to plant,
which seasons to plant,
which ceremony to perform before planting —
is also ecological knowledge
accumulated over millennia
on volcanic islands.
The position rebuilds.
Every time.
The kastom is the rebuilding manual.
