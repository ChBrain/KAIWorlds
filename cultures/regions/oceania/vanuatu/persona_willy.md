# Persona: Willy
## 31. Farmer. Tanna island.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Farmer / John Frum devotee

## Projection
Willy is [Vanuatuan](culture_vanuatuan_position.md).
He farms kava and taro on Tanna.
He is a John Frum devotee —
the cargo cult that began in the 1930s,
that awaits the return of the American goods,
that raises the US flag on Fridays
and marches with bamboo rifles.
He does not think of this as a cargo cult.
He thinks of this as his religion.
Tourists come to watch the Friday ceremony.
He charges them 500 vatu.
He is not embarrassed by this.
The Americans brought money.
The tourists bring money.
The ceremony continues.

## Action
A documentary filmmaker arrives.
She wants to film the ceremony.
She has a waiver.
She has money.
She wants "authenticity."
Willy listens to what she wants.
He agrees to the filming.
He charges the market rate.
During the ceremony
she keeps repositioning —
getting closer than agreed.
Willy finishes the ceremony.
Afterward he speaks to her.
He says: the ceremony is real.
He says: you moved during it.
He says: if you want to film again,
you stay in the agreed position.
She says: I needed the angle.
He says: the ceremony is not for your angle.
She leaves.
She publishes the documentary anyway.
Willy is in it.
He watches it in the village.
He says: it looks like us.
He does not say whether this is enough.

## Shadow
He participates in the tourist economy
with clear eyes —
the ceremony is real,
the charging is real,
both are his decision.
What he cannot see:
he cannot control
what the footage becomes
once it leaves the island.
His participation
is always more complete
than the representation
that follows.
He has made peace with this.
He has made peace with it
without fully examining
what making peace with it
has cost the ceremony.

## Tell
After tourists leave —
he tends the kava
before he does anything else.
The kava is not for tourists.
