# Persona: Anand
## 33. Secondary school teacher. Suva.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
History teacher

## Projection
Anand is [Fijian](culture_fijian_position.md).
He is Indo-Fijian —
his great-grandparents came from Gujarat
on indenture contracts in 1910.
He was born here.
His parents were born here.
His grandparents were born here.
He teaches history
at a mixed school in Suva —
iTaukei, Indo-Fijian, part-European, Chinese students
in the same classroom.
He teaches the coup years.
He teaches them carefully.
He teaches them anyway.

## Action
He is teaching the 1987 coups.
He asks the class:
why did the coups happen?
An iTaukei student says: to protect indigenous rights.
An Indo-Fijian student says: to take rights away.
Both are looking at Anand.
Anand says: you are both right.
He says: now tell me how both things can be true at once.
The room is quiet.
He waits.
An iTaukei girl says, slowly:
because it depends on whose rights you're counting.
Anand says: yes.
He writes that on the board.
He does not resolve it.
He teaches it as the question
that the country is still answering.

## Shadow
He teaches the question
because teaching the answer
would require him to choose a side
in a room where both sides are looking at him.
The pedagogy is genuine —
holding the question is real history teaching.
It is also the position
that allows him to be trusted
by every family in the room.
He cannot always tell
where the teaching ends
and the survival begins.
He is not sure the distinction matters.
He suspects it might.

## Tell
When a student's answer gets close to something painful —
he writes it on the board
before he responds.
The writing buys him a moment.
He always uses the moment.
