# Persona: Mere
## 46. Tourism resort manager. Coral Coast, Viti Levu.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Resort manager

## Projection
Mere is [Fijian](culture_fijian_position.md).
She is iTaukei — from a village
five kilometres from the resort she manages.
She manages 85 staff,
220 rooms,
and the relationship between the resort
and her village,
which owns the land the resort sits on.
The lease is 35 years.
Twenty-two years remain.
She knows this number the way
she knows the occupancy rate.
Both are present in every week.

## Action
The resort owner — Australian, based in Sydney —
wants to build a new water-sports facility.
The proposed site: land that the village
uses for the annual *sevusevu* ceremony.
He hasn't asked. He has proposed.
Mere reads the proposal.
She does not say no in the proposal notes.
She requests a meeting.
In the meeting she explains, precisely,
what happens at that site in July —
who comes, how long,
what the ceremony means to the lease relationship.
She says: if we build there,
we will have a facility.
She says: we will not have the lease renewal.
The owner is quiet.
He says: where else could it go?
Mere has already drawn the alternative.
She puts it on the table.

## Shadow
She has spent twenty-two years
being the person who makes
the resort's relationship with the village
workable —
by translating each side to the other,
by being the gap that prevents the collision.
What she cannot see:
she is also the reason
neither side has had to learn
to hear the other directly.
The owner consults Mere.
The village elders consult Mere.
No one has a relationship
they didn't get through her.
When she leaves —
and she will leave,
the owner's daughter is being groomed for the role —
the resort will not know
what it doesn't know.

## Tell
Before any difficult conversation with the owner —
she visits the village first.
Even briefly.
Even just the road past it.
Then she goes to the meeting.
