# Position: Papua New Guinean
## The most linguistically diverse nation on earth — 800 languages, one state, and the question of whether the state is the right unit.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Wantok System](culture_papua_new_guinean_piece_wantok.md).
*Wantok* — "one talk" —
the Tok Pisin word for the person
who speaks your language.
In a country of 800 languages,
the wantok is the person you can trust
before you know anything else about them.
The wantok system is the real governance
of Papua New Guinea.
The obligation to your wantok:
employment if you can give it,
food if they need it,
shelter if they ask,
advocacy in any institution you have access to.
The state is what you navigate.
The wantok is what you are.
The diversity: 800 languages.
More than any other country on earth.
The Highlands. The coast. The islands.
Melanesian, Papuan, Austronesian groups
who had no contact with each other
until the colonial period.
The colonial period arrived late —
the Highlands not fully "contacted"
until the 1930s.
The Australians who administered
until independence in 1975
built a state but could not build
a shared identity.
The shared identity is Tok Pisin —
the creole that runs across the language groups —
and the rugby league.
The Christianity: 97%.
But the *kastom* — the traditional culture —
runs beneath the Christianity
and through it.
The *sing-sing* — the ceremony —
as the living practice of pre-contact culture.
The resource wealth: gold, copper, LNG.
The Ok Tedi mine. The PNG LNG project.
ExxonMobil. Santos. The Australian companies.
The wealth that does not reach the villages.
The state that collects the royalties
and cannot deliver the school.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room immediately calculates:
which language group?
Which province?
Which wantok network?

## Orders
Honour the wantok.
The position does not navigate PNG society
without the wantok obligation.
The politician who does not bring
government resources to his wantok
is not doing his job.
This is not corruption in the frame
the World Bank uses.
It is the operating logic
of a society where the state
has never been the primary provider
of security or sustenance.
The wantok provides those things.
The politician is the wantok
with access to the state.

## Loses
The resource dividend.
PNG has world-class mineral and energy wealth.
The PNG LNG project: one of the largest
in the Pacific.
The Ok Tedi copper-gold mine.
The Bougainville copper mine —
whose closure after environmental destruction
triggered a decade-long secessionist war.
The wealth is extracted.
The royalties flow to the state.
The state's delivery of services —
roads, schools, hospitals —
does not match the extraction.
The position in the village
who lives near the pipeline
and has no reliable water supply
carries this arithmetic every day.

## Drives
On the loss — the land.
PNG has some of the last intact
tropical rainforest on earth.
The biodiversity: extraordinary.
The birds of paradise.
The customary land system —
97% of PNG land held under customary tenure —
is the most intact large-scale customary land system
remaining anywhere in the world.
The wantok system and the land system
are the same logic:
what you hold, you hold collectively,
and the collective is the people
who speak your language.
The mining companies want the land.
The state wants the royalties.
The villages hold the land
and the cultures that the land holds.
This is the position's deepest resource.
