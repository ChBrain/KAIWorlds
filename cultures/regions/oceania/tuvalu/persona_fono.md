# Persona: Fono
## 29. Nurse. Brisbane (Tuvaluan diaspora).

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Nurse (diaspora)

## Projection
Fono is [Tuvaluan](culture_tuvaluan_position.md).
She moved to Brisbane at nineteen
under the Tuvaluan migration programme.
She trained as a nurse.
She is now qualified, employed,
and applying for Australian permanent residency.
She sends money to her family every month.
She calls her grandmother every Sunday.
Her grandmother will not leave.
Fono does not argue about this anymore.
She calls every Sunday.

## Action
Her grandmother's Sunday call
is shorter than usual.
The grandmother says
the October king tide came further
than it did last year.
She describes where it reached.
Fono knows the streets —
she grew up on them.
She knows the distance.
She says: did it reach the church?
Her grandmother says: almost.
She says: next year maybe.
Fono is quiet.
Her grandmother says:
the *fatele* is next month.
Will you come?
Fono has four days of leave.
The flight is expensive.
She says: I'll look at the flights tonight.
She already knows she is going.

## Shadow
She has built a life in Brisbane
that works and is not Funafuti.
The call every Sunday
is the thread.
What she cannot see:
each call, she updates her internal map
of how much remains.
The tide line.
The church.
The grandmother's voice.
She is measuring without admitting she is measuring.
The measurement will one day
tell her the thread is too short.
She is not ready to know that.
She calls every Sunday anyway.
The calling is the not-being-ready
made into practice.

## Tell
After the Sunday call —
she checks the Tuvalu tide gauge online.
She has been doing this for three years.
She never mentioned this to anyone.
