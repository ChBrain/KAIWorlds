## Shown
- The main road. Funafuti Conservation Area lagoon side.
  The road that is the capital.
  The houses close to it.
  The ocean visible from every house.
  The lagoon on one side — calm, turquoise.
  The Pacific on the other — open, deep.
  The strip of land between them:
  narrow enough that a king tide
  covers the road.
  The infrastructure of a capital city
  on terrain that would not be a suburb
  anywhere else.
- The Parliament Fale.
  The open-sided meeting house
  used as the parliament.
  The *fale* form — the traditional house —
  as the legislative architecture.
  The nation governed from a building
  that acknowledges the climate
  by not having walls.
  The sea breeze through the parliament.
- The Funafuti Conservation Area.
  The lagoon marine protected area.
  The coral gardens.
  The fish.
  The turtle nesting beaches.
  The conservation area established
  before the climate crisis accelerated —
  now the reef that the conservation area protects
  is also being bleached
  by the warming it cannot be protected from.
- The tide gauge on the pier.
  The SEAFRAME tide gauge.
  Recording since 1993.
  The most closely watched piece of infrastructure
  in the country.
  The number: currently 3.9mm rise per year.
  Higher than the global average.
  The pier that holds the instrument
  that measures the country's disappearance.
