# Position: Micronesian
## The Federated States that chose America — and have been navigating what that choice costs ever since.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Compact of Free Association](culture_micronesian_piece_compact.md).
Four states. Four cultures.
Chuuk, Pohnpei, Yap, Kosrae.
Each with its own language,
its own customs,
its own relationship to the federation
that the Compact with the United States made possible.
The Compact of Free Association: 1986.
The FSM is self-governing.
The United States provides financial assistance
and handles defence.
The FSM citizens can live and work in the US
without a visa.
The US military has exclusive access.
The Chuukese in Hawaii, in Guam, in the mainland US —
the diaspora enabled by the Compact,
driven by the economy.
The traditional governance:
Yap's caste system — one of the most hierarchical
in the Pacific.
The *biit* — the traditional exchange networks.
The stone money of Yap: the *rai* —
large stone discs, some three metres across,
quarried in Palau, carried by canoe to Yap.
The largest are too heavy to move.
They sit where they were placed.
Their ownership transfers.
Their location does not.
The value is the knowledge of ownership,
not the possession of the object.
The collectivism: the clan, the matrilineal family,
the island community.
The hierarchy: high in Yap,
more egalitarian in Chuuk and Kosrae.
The Christianity: 97%.
The church as the second pillar
alongside the traditional governance.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room knows which state,
which island, which clan.
The room knows the Compact.

## Orders
Navigate the Compact.
The position does not exist outside
the relationship with the United States
that the Compact defines.
The funding, the migration option,
the military alignment —
all structured by the Compact.
The position's foreign policy is the Compact.
The position's domestic policy is:
what do we build with what the Compact provides,
before it expires again?

## Loses
The young.
The Compact migration option:
the FSM citizens who leave for Guam, Hawaii, Oregon.
Who send remittances.
Who do not return.
The health system staffed by the Compact's provisions —
partially.
The education system that prepares students
for Compact-enabled migration
rather than for staying.
The position that exports its people
because the domestic economy cannot absorb them
and receives money back
but not the people.

## Drives
On the loss — the ocean.
The FSM's EEZ: one of the largest in the Pacific.
The tuna fisheries.
The fishing licence revenue.
The position that holds three million square kilometres of ocean
with 115,000 people.
The ocean is the scale.
The people are the custodians.
The custodians are moving to Honolulu.
The position works on what to do
with the ocean when they get back.
