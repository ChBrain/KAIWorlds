# Place: Palikir
## Location: Federated States of Micronesia

## Owner

## Shown
- The FSM Capitol Complex.
  The national government buildings.
  The four state flags alongside the FSM flag.
  The deliberate architecture of federation:
  four very different cultures
  under one constitutional structure.
  The building that makes the claim
  that they are one country.
  The claim is real. The differences are also real.
- The Nan Madol ruins. Pohnpei.
  The ancient city built on artificial islands
  in the lagoon.
  90 artificial islets.
  Basalt log construction.
  The administrative and ceremonial centre
  of the Saudeleur dynasty,
  circa 1200–1628 CE.
  UNESCO World Heritage since 2016.
  Built by a people who moved stone
  across water
  without metal tools.
  The same engineering knowledge
  that made the Yapese *rai* possible.
  The Pacific capacity for monumental work
  in the service of social organisation.
- The Chuuk Lagoon.
  The WWII Japanese fleet — sunk 1944.
  60 ships, 250 aircraft on the ocean floor.
  The world's most famous wreck diving.
  The Japanese bones in the ships.
  The Chuukese fishing above them.
  The history that the ocean holds
  and the dive operators monetise.
- The Yap stone money site.
  The *rai* beside the path.
  Some as tall as a person.
  The village knows who owns each stone.
  The ownership has not changed
  in living memory.
  The stone has not moved
  in living memory.
  Both facts are the same fact.
