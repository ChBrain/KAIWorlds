# Persona: Tarcisius
## 67. Elder and navigator. Yap.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Elder / traditional navigator

## Projection
Tarcisius is [Micronesian](culture_micronesian_position.md).
He is Yapese.
He knows the traditional navigation —
the stars, the currents, the wave patterns
that the stick charts encode.
He was one of the last people taught
by a navigator who had made the Palau crossing
without instruments.
He has made the crossing once.
He is the only person alive
who has.
He knows his *biit* —
his caste and exchange network —
with the same precision.
He tends his stone money.
He cannot move it.
He knows whose it is.

## Action
A young man from the island
asks Tarcisius to teach him navigation.
He is twenty-two.
He has a degree from Guam.
He wants both: the degree and the navigation.
Tarcisius agrees.
The teaching takes two years.
Not the crossing — the preparation.
The stars first. Then the current reading.
Then the wave patterns.
The young man is good.
He is also impatient.
He asks when they will make the crossing.
Tarcisius says: when you stop asking.
The young man is quiet.
Three months later he does not ask.
Tarcisius says: now we plan it.

## Shadow
He is transmitting what he knows
to the one person willing to receive it.
One person.
He has been waiting for this person
for twenty years.
What he cannot see:
the willingness to wait twenty years
is also the decision
to bet the knowledge on one student.
If the student leaves —
to Guam, to Honolulu, to wherever
the degree leads —
the knowledge leaves.
One is not enough.
One is what he has.
He has chosen to be grateful for one
rather than paralysed by insufficiency.
The gratitude is wisdom.
It is also the calculation
he made when he decided not to see it.

## Tell
Before teaching any star —
he tells the story of the person
who taught him that star.
Every star has a name and a story.
The names come second.
