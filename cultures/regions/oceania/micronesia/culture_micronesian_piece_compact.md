# Piece: The Compact of Free Association
## The deal that gave FSM sovereignty and tied it to the United States — and the Yapese stone money that predates the dollar.

## Owner
- **Place:** [Palikir](culture_micronesian_place_palikir.md)

## Place
Palikir. Pohnpei island.
The government complex on the hillside.
The FSM national government.
The flags of the four states.
The ocean visible through the breadfruit trees.
The capital that was purpose-built
for an independent nation
that had never had a capital before.

## Load Bearing
Remove the Compact
and the FSM is a Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands
under US administration
with no path to sovereignty
other than full independence (Palau's eventual path)
or absorption into the United States.
With it: the specific FSM position —
sovereign state, US-aligned,
dependent on US funding,
permeable to US migration.
The Compact has been renewed twice (1986, 2003).
The third Compact was renegotiated 2023.
Each renewal: negotiation over the terms.
Each renewal: the FSM with less leverage than it appears.
The US military exclusivity clause
is the US's leverage.
The migration option
is the FSM's concession that has become dependency.
The Yapese stone money is the counter-piece:
the *rai* — stone discs up to 3 metres across,
quarried in Palau by Yapese navigators,
floated on rafts across 450 kilometres of open ocean.
The largest are immovable.
Their value is social: the community knows who owns what.
The ownership record is oral, collective, maintained.
This is among the earliest examples of fiat currency:
value established by collective agreement,
not by the physical possession of the object.
The FSM's relationship to the US dollar
is, from one angle, the modern version of the *rai*:
value established by the Compact agreement,
flowing between the islands and Washington,
the ledger maintained in the treasury.
The stone money has lasted 2,000 years.
The Compact has lasted 40.

## Apparent
Circa 2000 BCE: Micronesian settlers in the Caroline Islands.
1525: Portuguese contact.
1899: Germany purchases Carolines from Spain.
1914: Japan occupies German Micronesia.
1920: League of Nations mandate to Japan.
1944–45: US forces take island by island.
Enormous casualties — Chuuk Lagoon, Peleliu.
1947: UN Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands.
US as administering power.
Four administrative districts become FSM plus Palau plus Northern Marianas.
1979: FSM constitution. Self-governing.
1986: Compact of Free Association. Full sovereignty.
2003: Compact II. Revised funding, extended terms.
2023: Compact III negotiations. New 20-year framework.
The US military exclusivity: maintained.
The migration option: maintained.
The funding: restructured with more oversight.
The FSM: still sovereign.
Still navigating.
