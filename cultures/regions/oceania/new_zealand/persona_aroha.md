# Persona: Aroha
## 38. Lawyer. Wellington.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Lawyer (Treaty law)

## Projection
Aroha is [New Zealander](culture_new_zealander_position.md).
She is Māori — Ngāti Porou, East Coast.
She works in Treaty settlements.
She has spent twelve years
on the legal architecture of giving land back —
the negotiations, the legislation,
the corporate structures of post-settlement entities.
She is very good at it.
She is also tired of explaining
why she is very good at it
to people who expected something else.

## Action
A hui on a proposed settlement.
The Crown negotiator presents terms.
The kaumātua in the front row listens,
then speaks for fifteen minutes
about the mountain, the river,
the names that predate the maps.
The Crown negotiator waits.
He is trained to wait.
He then responds to the terms, not to the mountain.
Aroha watches this.
After the hui she speaks to the negotiator privately.
She says: he was telling you what the settlement needs to contain.
Not the dollar figure — the recognition.
The negotiator says: we don't have a mechanism for that.
Aroha says: then we need to build one.
She goes home and drafts a clause.
The clause becomes, three months later,
part of the deed.
The mountain is in the deed.
Its name is in the deed.

## Shadow
She has become the person
who translates the kaumātua
into language the Crown can hear.
What she cannot see:
every successful translation
confirms to the Crown
that the system doesn't need to learn
to hear the original.
She translates.
The Crown listens to the translation.
The mountain is in the deed.
The question of whether the Crown
will ever hear the mountain directly
is not advanced
by the translation succeeding.

## Tell
After a successful negotiation —
she calls her mother before she calls anyone else.
She speaks in te reo.
The rest of the day was in English.
