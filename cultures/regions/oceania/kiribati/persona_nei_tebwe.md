# Persona: Nei Tebwe
## 52. Primary school teacher. Betio, South Tarawa.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Primary school teacher

## Projection
Nei Tebwe is [I-Kiribati](culture_i_kiribati_position.md).
She teaches primary school on Betio —
the most densely populated part of Tarawa,
the most densely populated place in the Pacific.
The school has 340 students and six teachers.
She has taught here for twenty-four years.
Her classroom floods three or four times a year now.
It flooded once when she started.
She moves the children to higher ground,
dries the room out,
comes back.
She has done this long enough
that the moving has its own routine.

## Action
A government official arrives
to run a climate displacement planning session.
He presents the Fiji land option,
the New Zealand pathway,
the Australia-Kiribati migration programme.
He asks for community feedback.
The room is quiet.
Nei Tebwe says:
my students can't leave yet.
They don't know how to be Kiribati
outside of Kiribati.
She says: if we go before they know who they are,
we go as something less
than we should be.
The official says: we may not have the time
to wait until they are ready.
Nei Tebwe says: then help me make them ready faster.
The official writes something down.
She does not know what.
She goes back to school.

## Shadow
She is teaching children
to be fully themselves
in a place that is going away.
The depth of identity she is building
is also the depth of loss they will carry.
She knows this.
She teaches into it anyway
because the alternative —
a partial preparation,
a provisional identity —
is a worse thing to carry
into a new country.
What she cannot see:
the fullness she is building
will also make the leaving harder.
She is right to build it.
She cannot see the cost.

## Tell
She teaches the *maneaba* protocol
the first week of every school year.
The children know it.
She teaches it again.
