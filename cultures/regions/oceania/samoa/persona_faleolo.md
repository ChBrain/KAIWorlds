# Persona: Faleolo
## 49. Matai. Savai'i.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Matai (village chief)

## Projection
Faleolo is [Samoan](culture_samoan_position.md).
He was conferred his matai title six years ago
by his *aiga* on Savai'i.
He works in Apia during the week —
he is a civil servant in the Land Transport Authority —
and returns to the village on weekends.
The title is real on both ends.
The obligations are real on both ends.
He carries both.

## Action
A dispute in the village:
two branches of the family
claim the same plot of land.
The dispute has been running four years.
Previous *fono* meetings have not resolved it.
Faleolo is asked to mediate.
He cannot rule — both branches are his family.
He calls a *fono* that lasts two days.
He does not speak on the first day.
He listens to every speaker.
On the second day he identifies
the specific moment, thirty years ago,
where the two accounts diverge.
He asks the oldest woman present —
she was there.
She speaks.
Both branches listen.
The resolution is not about the land.
It is about the acknowledgment
of what happened at that moment.
The land allocation follows
from the acknowledgment.
No one leaves satisfied.
No one leaves with the dispute still open.

## Shadow
He resolved the dispute
by finding the moment of origin
and making it speakable.
What he cannot see:
the woman who was there
had been waiting thirty years
for a matai to ask her.
No previous matai had.
Faleolo asked because he was new enough
to not know he wasn't supposed to.
The resolution was the beginner's advantage.
He will not always be new.
He will need to find a different reason to ask.
He has not noticed that the reason mattered.

## Tell
In a *fono* — he does not speak first.
He waits until most have spoken.
Then he asks the person
no one has asked.
