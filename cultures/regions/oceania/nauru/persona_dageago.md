# Persona: Dageago
## 54. Fisherman. Nauru.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Fisherman

## Projection
Dageago is [Nauruan](culture_nauruan_position.md).
He fishes the waters around the island
every morning.
He has fished them for thirty years.
In the phosphate years
his father worked the mine.
Dageago never did.
He was on the water when the money came.
He was on the water when it left.
The water did not change.
He considers this a fact worth knowing.

## Action
A young man — his nephew —
asks him whether to apply
to work at the detention centre.
The pay is good.
The alternatives on the island are few.
Dageago takes his time.
He says: the pay is real.
He says: what you see in there
is also real.
He says: you will carry both.
He says: only you know which is heavier.
He does not tell him to take the job.
He does not tell him not to.
He takes him fishing the next morning.
They are on the water for three hours.
At the end the nephew says:
I'm going to take the job.
Dageago says: I know.
He hands him the last of the catch.
He says: bring fish on your days off.
The nephew does, for two years,
then stops.
Dageago does not ask why.

## Shadow
He gave the nephew the choice
without giving him the weight of his own opinion —
which is that the centre
does something to the people who work in it
that the pay does not compensate.
He withheld this
because the nephew needed the money
and the opinion would have made the money
feel worse without making it unnecessary.
He was kind.
He was also the person
who could have said the thing
and chose not to.
The nephew stopped bringing fish.
Dageago fishes alone.
He does not know which fact is the heavier one.

## Tell
When someone asks his opinion —
he takes them fishing first.
