## Shown
- The topside.
  The phosphate plateau in the island's interior.
  The limestone pinnacles — *pinnacles* —
  where the phosphate was extracted.
  Each pillar: what was left behind
  when the matrix around it was removed.
  The moonscape.
  The visual proof of what was taken.
  Not metaphorical.
  Literally: what the island looked like before,
  and what it looks like now.
- The Regional Processing Centre.
  The Australian-funded detention facility.
  On the edge of the island.
  The fences.
  The contractors in their uniforms.
  The asylum seekers who have been here
  for years in some cases.
  The facility that is Nauru's largest employer.
  The economy that replaced the phosphate economy.
  Both economies: extractive.
  Both with Nauru as the site.
- The Buada Lagoon.
  The internal lagoon in the island's centre.
  The freshwater.
  The pandanus palms.
  The canoes.
  The part of the island
  that the phosphate mining could not reach.
  The ecological core.
  The Nauruans who fish it.
  The birds that nest around it.
  The freshwater that supplements the desalination.
- The Civic Centre.
  The parliament building.
  The government offices.
  The institution of a sovereign state
  on 21 square kilometres.
  The independence that the phosphate made possible
  and that continues when the phosphate cannot.
