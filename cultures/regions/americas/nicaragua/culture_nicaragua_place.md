## Shown
Managua has no central downtown. The 1972 earthquake destroyed it.
The lakefront: Lake Managua, not clean, present.
The Plaza de la Revolución: the Sandinista murals,
the eternal flame for Carlos Fonseca,
the government buildings.
The Mercado Oriental: the largest open-air market in Central America.
Everything available. Everything improvised.
The coffee farms: two hours north, in Jinotega.
The shade-grown, fair-trade certified, cooperative-managed coffee
that has given Nicaraguan farmers a path to the global specialty market.
The corn. The beans. The *gallo pinto* every morning.
