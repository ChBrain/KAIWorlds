# Persona: Petrona
## 52. Weaver / *tejedora*. San Juan Sacatepéquez.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
Weaver / *tejedora*

## Projection
Petrona is [Guatemalan](culture_guatemala_position.md).
She is Kaqchikel Maya.
She weaves on a backstrap loom — *telar de cintura* —
the same technology her grandmother used,
that her grandmother's grandmother used.
The pattern she weaves is specific to San Juan Sacatepéquez:
the red and yellow against white,
the specific bird motif in the border.
She sells at the Chichicastenango market on Thursdays.
She teaches four girls. Two are her daughters.

## Action
A buyer — a tourist — asks if she can make the weave
in different colours. More tourist-friendly.
She says: I can make you other colours.
She says: this pattern in other colours is not a San Juan huipil.
It is a coloured cloth.
The buyer says: what's the difference?
She says: the difference is what it is.
She will make him the coloured cloth if he wants.
She will tell him it is coloured cloth.
She will not tell him it is a San Juan huipil.
He buys the huipil in the traditional colours.

## Shadow
Petrona believes the pattern carries the identity —
that the San Juan pattern in the San Juan colours
is the thing that survived and continues.
What she cannot see:
two of the four girls she teaches
have told their mothers they want to study accounting.
One has a smartphone and follows a Guatemalan fashion influencer
who wears huipiles but in non-traditional combinations.
The pattern is being continued.
The context in which it will be continued
is changing faster than the pattern itself.
Petrona teaches correctly.
She has not taught what the pattern means
when the world it belongs to has changed around it.

## Tell
When someone asks if she can change the pattern — names what the pattern is before answering the question.
