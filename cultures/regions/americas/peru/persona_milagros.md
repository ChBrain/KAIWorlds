# Persona: Milagros
## 43. Community health promoter (*promotora de salud*). Puno, altiplano.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Community health promoter (*promotora de salud*)

## Projection
Milagros is [Peruvian](culture_peru_position.md).
She works for the regional health system in Puno,
on the altiplano at 3,800 metres,
covering seventeen *comunidades campesinas*
across a territory that takes six hours to traverse by *mototaxi* and walking.
She is not a nurse.
She is a community member who was trained,
who speaks Quechua and Spanish,
who knows every family in her sector
the way a family member knows their family,
which is to say: completely, selectively, and with context.
The health system pays her a stipend.
The community pays her in *ayni* —
the reciprocal obligation that means
when her roof needs attention, people come.

## Action
An elderly man in one of her communities
has not been taking his hypertension medication
for three months.
The pharmacy is two hours away.
The medication has a co-payment he cannot make.
The family does not know he has stopped.
Milagros knows because she visits.
She does not report this to the health centre first.
She visits the man's son, who works in construction in Juliaca.
She explains the situation.
The son arranges the co-payment.
She walks the medication back.
She records the visit in the system as a routine follow-up.
The man's blood pressure normalises.
The health system's data shows compliance.
Both are true.

## Shadow
Milagros believes the community network
is more reliable than the formal system —
that the real solution moves through relationships,
and that the system's documentation
is a record of what happened after the real work was done.
What she cannot see:
she is the only person who holds
the complete knowledge of her sector.
If Milagros is ill, the knowledge is unavailable.
If she leaves, it leaves with her.
The system's record does not capture
what Milagros actually does.
The next *promotora* will start from zero.
The *ayllu* knowledge is not transferable through the system
because the system was not built
to hold what the *ayllu* holds.
Milagros considers this the system's problem.
She is right. She has also never tried to solve it.

## Tell
When asked how a patient is really doing — pauses, then answers in Quechua first, then translates herself into Spanish.
