# Position: Kittitian and Nevisian
## The smallest federation in the Western Hemisphere — held together by the right to end it.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Has
[The Right to Leave — Section 113](culture_sknv_piece_section113.md).
The moderate hierarchy — PDI ~46.
The Westminster system: the Prime Minister, the National Assembly, the Governor-General.
And simultaneously: the Nevis Island Assembly, the Nevis Premier, the Nevis Island Administration.
The federation's unusual feature: Saint Kitts has no separate legislature.
Nevis does. The asymmetry is intentional.
The smaller island has the stronger constitutional position
precisely because the framers understood that without it,
the smaller island would have no effective voice.
The collectivism — IDV ~34.
The village on Saint Kitts: the estate community.
The village on Nevis: the same, but knowing they could vote to be separate.
Both collectivisms informed by the knowledge
that the three miles of channel between them
is both very short and very consequential.
The moderate masculinity — MAS ~52.
The closing of the sugar industry in 2005:
a loss that took a century to complete and a generation to accept.
The tourism industry that replaced it:
a different kind of work requiring a different kind of identity.
Both islands managing the transition together, unevenly.
The moderate uncertainty avoidance — UAI ~50.
The constitution manages the federation's uncertainty explicitly:
here is the mechanism for dissolution.
Here are the conditions. Here is the threshold.
The uncertainty is not removed. It is constitutionalised.
Not the avoidance of uncertainty but the formalisation of its management.

## Orders
Maintain the relationship.
The federation's parties maintain the relationship
because the relationship is worth maintaining.
Not because it must be maintained. Because it is worth it.
If it were not worth it, Section 113 is always available.
The fact that it has not been invoked since 1998
does not mean the position has forgotten the clause.
It means the clause is doing its work by not being used.

## Loses
The scale.
261 square kilometres. 55,000 people. Two capitals. One foreign minister.
The Kittitian-Nevisian position operates at the smallest scale
at which the term "sovereign state" has full meaning.
The UN seat. The passport. The constitution. The right to leave.
All of these exist in their fullest form.
The world pays attention to them approximately in proportion to the island's area.
Which is very little. The position is comfortable with this.
It knows what it is. It does not need the world's attention to know it.

## Drives
On the loss — maintains the federation.
Section 113 is still there. The federation is still here.
Both are true simultaneously.
The three miles of channel between the islands:
not too far to maintain, not too close to be the same thing.
The ferry runs. The position crosses the channel when it needs to.
It maintains the relationship across the water. That is the work.
