# Piece: The Right to Leave — Section 113
## The constitutional clause that holds the smallest federation in the world together by guaranteeing the right to end it.

## Load Bearing
Remove Section 113 and Nevis is the smaller, poorer island
in a federation dominated by the larger island's politics. No leverage. No exit option.
Anguilla's lesson: when Anguilla had no exit option in the 1967 associated statehood arrangement,
it physically expelled the police and deported the St. Kitts politician who came to talk.
Anguilla left before leaving was constitutional.
The Nevisian negotiators of 1983 knew this history. They demanded the clause. They received it.
With Section 113: Nevis is the smaller island that has constitutional leverage over the federation.
The 1998 referendum demonstrated that 62% of Nevisians were prepared to vote yes.
Saint Kitts's response: take Nevisian concerns more seriously.
The secession agitation has eased since 1998 — not because the grievances disappeared
but because the demonstration of 62% created the negotiating pressure
that produced better outcomes.
The clause keeps the relationship honest.
The threat is the mechanism of union.
Without the threat, the union would be less just.

## Apparent
The 1983 Constitution of Saint Kitts and Nevis. Section 113.
Nevis may secede from the federation if a two-thirds majority of Nevisian voters approve it in a referendum.
Negotiated by Nevisian representatives before independence. Written into the constitution at founding.
August 10, 1998: the referendum.
2,427 yes votes (61.83%). 1,498 no votes.
The threshold: two-thirds (66.7%).
Nevis remains. By 218 votes.
Nevis remains with the right to call another referendum intact.
The clause is still in the constitution. The option has not expired.

Background: 1623 — first permanent English settlement in the Caribbean, on Saint Kitts.
1628 — Nevis settled. The sugar century: both islands major sugar producers.
Nevis — "Queen of the Caribees" in the seventeenth century, wealthier than Barbados or Jamaica.
The decline. 1882: British colonial administration merges Anguilla, Saint Kitts, and Nevis.
Both smaller islands object.
1967: associated statehood. Anguilla immediately rebels, expels the St. Kitts police, eventually separates.
1983: independence. Section 113 in the constitution.
The sugar industry: closed after the 2005 harvest. The estate land repurposed.
Alexander Hamilton: born on Nevis, 1755. Emigrated at 17. Founding father of the United States.
The smallest sovereign federation in the Western Hemisphere.
The constitutional right of 11,000 people to dissolve it: still in the document. Still available.
