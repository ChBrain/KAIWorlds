## Orders
Include the twelve cases in the *minga*, not just the four.
Inés tracks the four wins.
The order demands: build the twelve into the strategy.
The cases that lost know something the four do not.
They know the edges of what Article 71 can currently do.
A *minga* that includes only the successful cases
is planning for a narrower territory than the one that exists.
Damián documents the treatment. Not the meta-record.
The *minga* around the object's preservation
must include the preservation of the knowledge about how to preserve it.
The constitution was written in a *minga*.
The jurisprudence is being written in one.
The order demands the twelve sit at the table as clearly as the four.
The position does not want to count the twelve.
It is asked to count them anyway.

## Loses
The capacity to enforce what it writes.
Twenty constitutions is not incoherence.
It is a society that keeps arriving at the same conviction —
that the group includes the river —
and keeps finding that the economic infrastructure
does not fully support that conviction.
The Yasuní Initiative was the clearest statement:
we will not extract this.
Will you pay us not to?
The world said no.
Ecuador drilled.
The conviction is unchanged.
The capacity to act on it unilaterally
requires resources Ecuador does not fully have.
The 2023 referendum is the loss reclaimed partially:
the people voted Article 71 into enforcement.
The position is not defeated.
It is working on the gap between what it knows is right
and what it can sustain.

## Drives
On the loss — calls another *minga*.
Wider this time.
The 2023 referendum was a *minga* at national scale.
The position believes the group, convened fully, produces the correct decision.
It has 180 years of evidence that convening is difficult.
It keeps convening.
