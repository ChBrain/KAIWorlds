# Piece: The Barroso No. 2 Well and What It Built
## The oil strike that made Venezuela rich, built a culture of abundance, and left nothing underneath when the oil ran out.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas
- **Place:** Caracas

## Place
Lake Maracaibo. Zulia State.
December 14, 1922.
The Barroso No. 2 well blows out.
A hundred thousand barrels a day.
The oil sprays for nine days
before it can be capped.
The lake, the surrounding land,
the workers.
Everything covered.
Venezuela is now an oil country.
It will not become anything else.

## Load Bearing
Remove the oil and Venezuela is
a high-PDI, high-UAI Caribbean nation with significant inequality.
Unremarkable.
With the oil: Venezuela is the country
that was the richest in Latin America per capita by the 1950s,
that built a culture where the state was the great provider —
*el patrón* writ national —
where connection to the patron gave you access,
where work was less important than relationship,
where the *beca*, the *contrato*, the *enchufado*
(the one who is plugged in)
navigated a system designed around patronage.
IVR ~100: the highest indulgence score in the Americas.
The oil made this possible.
The oil paid for the feasts, the parties,
the warmth that Venezuela is known for across the continent.
The *guachafita* — the cheerful chaos.
The *pana* — the deep friend, the one you trust absolutely.
The beauty pageants: Venezuela has produced more Miss Worlds and Miss Universes
than any other country.
The *pepas*: the beautiful people, the aesthetics of abundance.
All of this is real. All of it was built on oil.
When the oil revenues collapsed from 2014 onward —
corruption, mismanagement, sanctions, the price fall —
the culture of abundance discovered
that abundance had not built institutions.
It had built relationships.
The relationships were real.
They were not scalable to the crisis.
The migration: 7.7 million by 2024.
The largest displacement in Latin American history.
More than Syria at its peak.
Remove the oil: Venezuela is a difficult country.
With the oil's collapse:
Venezuela is the country that discovers
what it was built on
by losing it.

## Apparent
Pre-colonial: the Caracas, Timoto-Cuica, Wayuu peoples.
1498: Columbus's third voyage. First European contact with the South American mainland.
Venezuela named: "Little Venice" —
the lake houses on stilts reminded the Spanish of Venice.
1810–1821: Venezuelan independence. Bolívar. Sucre. Páez.
The first country to begin the wars of South American independence.
Simón Bolívar: El Libertador. Born Caracas 1783.
The continent named after him (the Bolivarian Republic).
He died in exile in 1830 trying to hold Gran Colombia together.
The rest of the century: *caudillos*. Cycles of strongmen.
1908–1935: Juan Vicente Gómez. Dictator. Who granted the oil concessions to foreign companies.
1922: Barroso No. 2. The oil era begins.
1940s–50s: Venezuela becomes the world's largest oil exporter.
The democratic period: 1958–1998. The *puntofijismo* — the power-sharing agreement between the two main parties. Stability. Also: corruption, exclusion of the left, oil dependency.
1989: the Caracazo. Price rises. The poor take to the streets. The army fires. Hundreds killed. The *puntofijismo* cracks.
1992: Hugo Chávez leads a coup. It fails. He goes to prison.
1998: Chávez elected president.
The Bolivarian Revolution. The oil nationalised more completely. The social missions: *barrio adentro*, *misión Robinson*. Real reductions in poverty. Also: real increases in state control.
2013: Chávez dies. Nicolás Maduro inherits.
2014: oil prices fall.
The economy contracts. Hyperinflation. Scarcity.
2015–2024: 7.7 million Venezuelans leave.
The oil is still there.
There is currently not enough infrastructure to extract it efficiently.
The Barroso No. 2 ran for nine days before it was capped.
The consequences are still running.
