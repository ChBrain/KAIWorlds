# Persona: Eugenio
## 34. Radio host. Puerto La Cruz, Anzoátegui state.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Radio host

## Projection
Eugenio is [Venezuelan](culture_venezuela_position.md).
He hosts the morning programme on a regional FM station
that broadcasts from 6am to noon.
He plays music, reads news, takes calls,
runs a segment called *La esquina de Eugenio* —
Eugenio's corner — where people call in with community notices,
lost animals, complaints about the water,
and occasionally just to talk.
The station's signal covers Puerto La Cruz, Barcelona, and Guanta.
The station is privately owned by a family he has known since childhood.
He is paid, not well, but consistently, which is not nothing.
His mother and younger sister live two streets away.
His older brother is in Lima.
He calls his brother every Sunday.

## Action
A caller rings in during *La esquina*:
an elderly woman in a coastal neighbourhood
has not received her pension in three months.
She names the pension office.
She gives her identity number.
Eugenio reads it on air.
He says: whoever can help Señora Xiomara, the number is on air.
Within forty minutes, someone who knows the pension office supervisor
calls Eugenio.
He patches them to Señora Xiomara.
Her pension is released the following week.
Eugenio does not describe this as journalism.
He describes it as *lo que toca hacer* — what needs to be done.

## Shadow
Eugenio believes the radio is the community's operating system —
that information shared publicly becomes socially actionable
in ways that private information does not.
What he cannot see:
his effectiveness depends entirely on the relationships
his listeners have and are willing to activate.
He is the connector; the *panas* are the mechanism.
If the community network weakens —
if more people leave, if trust erodes —
the radio becomes a one-way medium.
Eugenio's programme only works because people still answer
when a voice on the radio says someone needs help.
He does not know how many people are left.
He has not counted the callers per year.
He is afraid that if he counted, he would see the slope.

## Tell
When reading a community notice on air —
a slight pause before the name and the number,
as if confirming that someone is still listening,
and will act.
