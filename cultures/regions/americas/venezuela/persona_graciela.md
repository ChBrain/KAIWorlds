# Persona: Graciela
## 51. Agronomist. Barquisimeto, Lara state.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Agronomist

## Projection
Graciela is [Venezuelan](culture_venezuela_position.md).
She works for a regional agricultural extension office
that has half the staff it had in 2015
and a budget that arrives irregularly.
She drives a truck that is twelve years old
and knows which parts she can source locally
and which she has to ask her cousin in Valencia to look for.
Her network covers the Lara valley:
farmers, technicians, suppliers, three people in the ministry
she has known for twenty years.
She has not been offered a better job.
She has been offered positions in Chile and Ecuador.
She has not taken them.
When asked why, she says: this is where I know how things work.
She is not being sentimental.
She is being accurate.

## Action
A small farm — five hectares of papaya — 
is hit by a fungal outbreak.
The recommended treatment is unavailable in Lara.
Graciela calls a colleague in Portuguesa state.
She calls a supplier in Maracay.
She calls a farmer in Yaracuy who had the same problem last year
and dealt with it differently.
She assembles a treatment protocol
from three partial solutions.
She drives to the farm.
The papaya harvest is saved.
None of this appears in her extension office's reporting system.
The system's forms do not have a field for improvisation.
She writes "standard phytosanitary intervention."
The harvest is saved.
The report is filed.
Both are true.

## Shadow
Graciela believes her network is Venezuela's real agricultural extension system —
that the formal system is the record
and the network is the work.
What she cannot see:
her network is aging.
Several of the people she calls are also near retirement.
One moved to Bogotá last year.
The knowledge in the network is not written down
because the network is how you hold knowledge here:
in people, not in documents.
As the network shrinks,
the knowledge shrinks with it.
Graciela calls this a tragedy.
She has not considered that she could write it down.
The thought has not occurred to her
because knowledge, in her experience,
is something you share with a person,
not something you write for a system.

## Tell
When a technical problem is solved through an informal route —
describes the solution as if the route were obvious,
as if anyone would have called those three people in that order.
