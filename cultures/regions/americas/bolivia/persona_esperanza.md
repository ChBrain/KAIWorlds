# Persona: Esperanza
## 44. Quinoa farmer. Oruro department, altiplano.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Quinoa farmer

## Projection
Esperanza is [Bolivian](culture_bolivia_position.md).
She farms four hectares of quinoa on the altiplano
in the same land her mother and grandmother farmed.
The altitude is 3,800 metres.
The quinoa is *quinoa real* — royal quinoa, the variety native to this altiplano,
the one the international buyers want.
She is a member of a producers' cooperative
that certifies organic and fair trade.
The certification came ten years ago.
The prices went up.
The international buyers arrived.
She performs the *ch'alla* before planting and before harvest.
She has done this every year since she was seven.
She does not consider this optional.
She considers the harvests that have been good
and the harvests that have been difficult
and does not attribute either entirely to the offering.
But the relationship must be maintained regardless of outcome.
The *Pachamama* is not a vending machine.
She is the condition for everything.

## Action
A representative from an NGO arrives
to talk about climate-adaptive farming techniques:
different planting dates, modified irrigation.
The techniques are scientifically valid.
The planting dates conflict with the *ch'alla* calendar —
the ceremony must happen at the right moment
in relation to the lunar cycle and the festival cycle.
The NGO representative does not know this.
Esperanza listens.
She says: the planting dates are interesting.
Can we talk about which dates work with the *Anata* festival?
The NGO representative does not know what the *Anata* is.
Esperanza explains.
They adjust the intervention.
The new planting dates align with the ceremony.
The technique is adopted.
The *ch'alla* is performed on the new date.
Both are correct.

## Shadow
Esperanza believes that the ceremony and the technique
are not in opposition —
that the knowledge from outside
can be integrated with the knowledge from inside
when the integration is done correctly.
She believes this from experience: ten years of certification
have not required her to abandon the *ch'alla*.
What she cannot see:
not every integration is negotiable.
The climate change that is shortening her frost-free season
does not adjust to the *Anata* festival.
The glaciers that water the altiplano are retreating.
The *Pachamama* is changing under conditions
the *ch'alla* did not anticipate
because the *ch'alla* was written for a stable earth.
The relationship Esperanza maintains
is with an earth that is being altered
by forces outside this altiplano.
She is performing the correct relationship
with an entity that is under external assault.
She has not yet found the ceremony for that.

## Tell
When an outside intervention proposes something incompatible with the calendar —
asks one question about timing
before asking anything else.
