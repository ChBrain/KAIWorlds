# Position: Antiguan and Barbudan
## The nation that has two answers to the same question about land — and is currently deciding which one wins.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Has
[Barbuda Land Is Not for Sale](culture_antigua_barbuda_piece_land.md).
The moderate hierarchy — PDI ~47.
Antigua: the tourist economy hierarchy — the hotel owner, the manager, the worker.
Barbuda: the Council, the community, the individual Barbudan with equal land rights by law.
Both in the same nation. Both present simultaneously.
The collectivism — IDV ~33.
Antigua's collectivism: the neighbourhood, the church, the cricket ground.
Barbuda's collectivism: the communal land system.
The latter is the most formal expression of IDV in any country in the Americas —
a law that says: you cannot sell the island to yourself or to anyone else.
The island belongs to everyone who is from here.
The moderate masculinity — MAS ~55.
Antigua's sailing regatta: achievement, competition, excellence.
Barbuda's position: we survive. We hold the land. We rebuild.
The moderate uncertainty avoidance — UAI ~50.
Antigua: manages tourism uncertainty with infrastructure and experience.
Barbuda: manages development pressure with the Barbuda Council and the courts.
Both are managing uncertainty. With different tools. In the same nation.

## Orders
Hold the land.
The Barbudan position — within the combined national position —
orders itself to maintain the communal system.
Not as nostalgia. As the material basis of Barbudan life.
The land is the certainty.
When the certainty is threatened, everything built on it becomes uncertain.
*Barbuda land is not for sale.*
This is not a slogan. It is a description of the legal and social structure
that makes Barbudan life possible.

## Loses
The numbers.
1,800 Barbudans. One parliamentary seat out of nineteen.
A government headquartered in St. John's, forty kilometres away.
The courts have gone both ways. The developers are there.
The frigatebirds are still nesting in the Codrington Lagoon. For now.
The position fights with what it has.
It has the word of Clifton Walbrook, whose house was destroyed:
*Barbuda land is not for sale.*

## Drives
On the loss — returns to the island.
After Irma, after the evacuation: the Barbudans came back.
Not all of them. But enough.
The houses that are rebuilt are solid.
The frigatebirds returned to nest in the lagoon.
The position returns, like the birds. It builds what it can build.
It fights what it can fight. It remains on the island.
