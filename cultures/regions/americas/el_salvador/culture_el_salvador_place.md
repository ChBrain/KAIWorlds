# Place: San Salvador, El Salvador

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Shown
- San Salvador. The capital in the Andes foothills, earthquake-prone.
  The Metropolitan Cathedral, partially reconstructed, where Archbishop Romero is buried.
  He was shot at the altar while saying Mass, March 24, 1980.
  The tomb: visited daily. The man: canonised 2018.
- Suchitoto. The colonial town that the war passed through.
  Restored now. The lake. The artists. The weekend visitors from the capital.
  The town that holds both the war's memory and the peace's prosperity simultaneously.
- The pupusa stands. Every street corner, every market, every highway rest stop.
  The griddle. The masa. The chicharrón. The loroco. The cheese.
  The national meal made in public everywhere, all day.
- El Mozote. A village in Morazán department.
  December 11–12, 1981. The Atlacatl Battalion. 978 people killed.
  The largest massacre in modern Latin American history.
  A memorial now. The names on the wall.
- The Pacific coast. Black sand beaches. Surf breaks.
  The fishing villages where the FMLN organised.
  The sea that has no memory of the war.
