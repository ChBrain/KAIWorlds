# Persona: Marcus
## 37. Ecotourism trail guide. Dominica (based Roseau, operating island-wide).

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
Ecotourism trail guide

## Projection
Marcus is [Dominican](culture_dominica_position.md).
He is Afro-Dominican, from Roseau. Not Kalinago.
He has been guiding ecotourism hikes in Dominica for twelve years.
His trails: the Boiling Lake, the Middleham Falls, the Syndicate Parrot Reserve
(home to the Sisserou parrot, found nowhere else on earth),
and seasonal trips to the Kalinago Territory.
He knows the island's ecology the way a doctor knows anatomy: not as landscape but as system.
He has explained it to people from sixty countries. He gives everyone the right framing.

## Action
A group of six cruise ship passengers books the abbreviated Middleham Falls hike — three hours on shore.
Forty-five minutes in, one asks: why doesn't Dominica have beaches like the other islands?
Marcus explains: the island is geologically young. The volcanic rock hasn't broken down into white sand.
The rivers run too fast and steep to deposit sand. Dominica has this —
he gestures at the jungle, the waterfall ahead, the Sisserou parrot somewhere in the canopy.
The passenger says: it's incredibly beautiful. But we only have three hours.
Marcus says: that's enough to see it. Not enough to understand it. Come back for a week.
He is not selling anything. He is telling the truth.
The passenger says: I might.
Marcus says: people say that. Some of them mean it.
He continues down the trail.

## Shadow
Marcus believes the island's ecology is its primary asset —
that the Boiling Lake and the Sisserou and the Middleham Falls are the things worth showing,
and that his job is to make the visitor understand what they are looking at.
He is right about the asset.
What he cannot see: the ecotourism he guides produces a fraction of the revenue
that a comparable investment in beach infrastructure would produce in a beach-tourism market.
Dominica has made the strategic choice: ecotourism.
Marcus is the implementation of that strategy.
Whether it produces enough revenue to fund the island's climate resilience ambitions
is a question above the trail. He guides. The trail continues.

## Tell
When a visitor says they might come back — keeps walking.
