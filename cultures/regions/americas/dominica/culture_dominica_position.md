# Position: Dominican (Nature Isle)
## The island that resisted colonisation longest — and the community within it that has resisted longest of all.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Has
[The Last Kalinago Territory](culture_dominica_piece_kalinago.md).
The moderate hierarchy — PDI ~44.
Dominica's political history: the left-labour tradition.
The Kalinago Territory has its own governance structure
running parallel to the national government.
The moderate collectivism — IDV ~32.
The Nature Isle ethos: the island for everyone who comes to experience it.
The Kalinago community: a tighter collectivism inside the national one.
The basket cooperative. The communal land tenure.
The moderate femininity — MAS ~45.
The emphasis on nature, sustainability, resilience.
Dominica's declared ambition: to become the world's first climate-resilient nation.
The Kalinago Territory's declared ambition: to remain.
Both are forms of the same orientation.
The moderate uncertainty avoidance — UAI ~55.
The volcanic island manages with a certain equanimity.
The hurricanes: they come.
The Territory was hit by Maria. No deaths.
The community walked door to door the next morning and counted who was still there.

## Orders
Stay legible as Kalinago.
The position does not permit quiet assimilation into the general Dominican culture
in exchange for the easier life assimilation offers.
The basket must be woven in public, not just in the home.
The Barana Auté must be built and kept open.
The Chief must be elected and known.
The identity must be visible, which is more expensive than being invisible.
The position is asking the community to carry the cost of visibility
because invisible survival is survival of a different kind —
the kind that ends when the last person who remembers stops remembering.
Stay legible. So the next generation knows what to pass on.

## Loses
The language.
Kalinago is considered extinct.
The specific way of naming the world that died in the mouths of the last speakers
before anyone wrote it down carefully enough.
The basket is the language in a different medium.
The canoe is the language in a different medium.
The larouman reed carries what the words cannot be recovered to say.
The practice is the language's living form. The position maintains the practice.

## Drives
On the loss — weaves.
The larouman basket. Every basket is a word. Every weaver is a speaker.
The language is gone. The weaving is not gone.
*Fwen nou touléjou.* We are always here. (In Dominican Creole, not Kalinago —
because the Kalinago is gone, and what remains is the Dominican Creole
in which the Kalinago now speak.)
They are here. They are saying so in the language they have.
