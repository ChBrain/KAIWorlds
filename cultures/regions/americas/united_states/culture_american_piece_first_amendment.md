# Piece: The First Amendment
## The one that comes before all the others.

## Owner
- **Place:** Washington DC

## Place
Washington DC. The National Archives.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the American position loses its operating licence.
The prosperity on display, the individualism IDV 91,
requires a guarantee the display is permitted.
The First Amendment is that guarantee.

## Apparent
45 words.
In the National Archives, Washington DC, behind glass in helium.
Also: laminated on the wall of every American school classroom.
Also: cited in court, in argument, on protest signs.

## Yearbook
1791: ratified. The Bill of Rights.
1919: Schenck v. United States.
The Supreme Court establishes limits.
"Clear and present danger."
The limits are contested ever since.
1964: New York Times v. Sullivan.
Press freedom expanded.
The press uses it.
1989: Texas v. Johnson. Flag burning is protected speech.
The decision is 5–4.
The debate about the decision continues.
Every decade: a new test case.
Every decade: the Amendment survives.
2020s: social media platforms, content moderation,
misinformation, AI-generated content.
The 45 words written in 1791
are being applied to situations
the authors could not have imagined.
They are still being applied.
