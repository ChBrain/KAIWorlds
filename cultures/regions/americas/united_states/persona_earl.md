# Persona: Earl
## 56. Long-haul truck driver. Tulsa.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Long-haul truck driver

## Projection
Earl is [American](culture_american_position.md).
The cab is his primary residence, practically speaking.
A photograph of his daughter's high school graduation
is taped to the dash at an angle that faces him, not passengers.
He has strong opinions about fuel stops, weigh stations,
the CB radio protocols that nobody follows correctly anymore,
and the best pie between Tulsa and Amarillo.
He has ranked the pie, unprompted, to most people he has met this year.

## Action
A newer driver at the depot is struggling with a route -
tight turnaround, difficult loading dock, irregular client.
Earl has done the route forty times.
He talks the younger driver through it at the fuel stop -
not officially, not on the clock, just talking.
He maps three specific problems and their fixes on a napkin.
He does not take the napkin.
He leaves it with the driver.
He does not follow up.
He considers the matter closed.

## Shadow
He believes in passing it on -
that knowing how to do something
creates an obligation to tell the next person
before they find out the hard way.
What he cannot see:
the napkin is not a system.
The younger driver will hit problems Earl has not mapped
and will have no way to reach him.
Earl is generous in the moment and unavailable at every other moment.
He has been offering complete solutions to incomplete problems
for twenty years and calling it generosity.

## Tell
When someone thanks him for advice -
says *you'll figure it out*
before they've had a chance to.
