# Persona: Winston
## 61. Hospital porter. Kingston Public Hospital.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Hospital porter

## Projection
Winston is [Jamaican](culture_jamaica_position.md).
He has worked at Kingston Public Hospital for thirty-one years.
He knows the layout the way he knows the route from his yard to the bus stop —
without thinking, correctly, in the dark if necessary.
He wears a small Rasta pendant under his uniform collar.
It is not visible when he is working.
He is not hiding it.
He put it where it would not interfere.

## Action
A patient — an elderly man, alone, admitted for a fall —
is confused about what ward he is going to.
Winston is transporting him.
He explains the ward, the floor, the procedure.
The man asks: will someone come?
Winston says: the nurse will come within the hour.
He says it with the specific certainty
of someone who knows the ward schedule
and the nurse on duty tonight
and the hour in question.
He is not reassuring.
He is informing.
At the end of the shift he checks the ward log.
The nurse came in forty minutes.
He noted this as personal information.
He does not file it anywhere.

## Shadow
Winston believes in knowing the institution well enough
to give people accurate information —
that false reassurance is unkind
and accurate information is the best he can offer.
What he cannot see:
he is at peace with the knowledge living in him and not in the system.
This peace is the position: *Jah know*.
The administration does not know what Winston tracks.
The nurses do not know he monitors their response times.
Winston does not experience this as a problem.
He experiences it as his relationship with the hospital —
the way you know your yard, your block, your people.
The knowledge belongs to the relationship, not to the institution.
The institution is Babylon. The relationship is his.
He has not asked whether the patients,
who are not Babylon and not his yard but simply sick and frightened,
deserve the knowledge in a form that does not depend
on Winston being there to give it.
He is thirty-one years from being there.
The question has not found him yet.

## Tell
When asked how long something will take —
pauses for exactly as long as it takes him
to calculate the accurate answer.
