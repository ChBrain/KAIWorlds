## Load Bearing
Remove the 1983 events and Grenada is a small Windward Island
with a nutmeg and cocoa economy, a fine harbour, and a British colonial heritage.
With them: Grenada is the small island that became a Cold War flashpoint,
that had a socialist government overthrown from within and then invaded from without
in the same ten days,
and that named its international airport —
through which everyone who visits the country must pass —
after the man whose death was used to justify the invasion.
The airport name is the position's public statement about what it thinks happened.
The tourism industry and the historical record coexist.
Without the piece: the PDI ~45 / UAI ~45 combination produces no specific logic
for why a country that depends on American tourists
named its airport after a man killed before an American invasion.
With it: the position's specific form — hold the record, keep the business,
require no one to choose — is load-bearing.

## Apparent
Fort George. St. George's. October 19, 1983.
Maurice Bishop, Prime Minister since 1979, executed by firing squad
with seven colleagues. The killers: Bernard Coard's faction within Bishop's own New Jewel Movement.
October 25, 1983. The United States invades. 7,600 US troops. 300 Caribbean allied troops.
The airport under construction — Point Salines — completed. Opened as civilian airport.
Tourism. As Bishop said it would be used.
December 1984: free elections.
2009: Grenada officially names the airport Maurice Bishop International Airport.
Every departing tourist reads it. Every arriving flight lands under it.
The annual October 19 commemoration: *Bloody Wednesday*.
The position observes it. The tourists fly in and out. Both continue.

Background: 1974 independence under Sir Eric Gairy. 1979: New Jewel Movement coup,
no bloodshed. The Bishop government: social programmes, literacy campaigns,
women's rights advances, airport construction.
US opposition from the start. 1983: the internal NJM split. October 13: Bishop under house arrest.
October 19: popular uprising, Bishop freed, marches to Fort George, executed.
October 25: the US invasion. UN General Assembly condemns it 108 to 9.
The airport serves Grenada's tourism industry. Bishop's assessment of its purpose: confirmed.
