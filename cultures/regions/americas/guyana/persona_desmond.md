# Persona: Desmond
## 61. cassava bread maker. Lethem, Region 9.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
cassava bread maker

## Projection
Desmond is [Guyanese](culture_guyana_position.md).
He makes cassava bread in Lethem, a border town in Region 9 — Upper Takutu-Upper Essequibo —
on the border with Brazil.
Lethem is Amerindian country: the Macushi and Wapishiana peoples.
Desmond is Macushi.
He bakes cassava bread using the flat circular bread — *cassava bread* — 
traditional to the Guyanese interior.
He supplies local households, the market, and regional travellers.
He also supplies a small export operation to Boa Vista, across the border.
His bread is different from supermarket cassava products.
He uses bitter cassava, squeezed in a *matapi* — the traditional press —
to remove the prussic acid.
The process takes two days. He does it correctly.
He has been doing it correctly since he was twelve.

## Action
A government official arrives in Lethem.
He is conducting a land title survey — part of the oil revenue redistribution programme.
He needs community land data for the formalities.
He asks Desmond: do you own this property?
Desmond says: the community owns this land.
The official says: but where is the title?
Desmond says: we do not have a title. We have been here.
The official writes something. He says the community should apply for formal title.
He gives a form. He explains the process.
Desmond takes the form.
He will bring it to the village council.
The council has discussed land title before.
The discussion has not produced a title.
The official drives away.
Desmond watches the road until the vehicle is gone before returning to the bread.

## Shadow
Desmond believes the cassava bread is the same bread his grandmother made — that the process (the grating, the pressing, the cooking on the *boudou*) produces the same food across generations. What he cannot see: the cassava variety his grandmother used is less commonly planted now. The hybrid varieties produce more, are more disease-resistant, and taste different. He plants the old variety on his plot. He does not know how many other people still do.

## Tell
When an official leaves after a meeting — watches the road until the vehicle is gone before returning to the bread.
