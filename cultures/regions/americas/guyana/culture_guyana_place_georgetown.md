# Place: Georgetown, Guyana

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Shown
- Georgetown. The city of canals and wooden buildings.
  The Dutch colonial grid: streets raised above sea level on a drainage system
  built with enslaved labour in the eighteenth century.
  The sea wall holding the Atlantic back. The city is below sea level.
  This is the permanent condition.
- St. George's Cathedral. Wooden Anglican cathedral. One of the tallest wooden buildings
  in the world. Built 1892. Survived everything: floods, fires, political upheaval.
- Stabroek Market. The cast-iron clock tower over the market hall.
  The vendors: Indo-Guyanese, Afro-Guyanese, Amerindian.
  The national plurality visible in one market at 7am.
- The Interior. Kaieteur Falls — one of the most powerful waterfalls in the world.
  Accessible only by small plane or multi-day trek.
  The Rupununi savannah. The Iwokrama rainforest.
  The 80% of Guyana that is not Georgetown, where most of Guyana is.
- The oil rigs offshore. Not visible from Georgetown but their revenue is visible:
  in the construction sites, the new hotels, the price of land.

## Withheld
The ethnic arithmetic that runs every election.
Guyana has two major ethnic communities: Indo-Guyanese (~40%) and Afro-Guyanese (~30%).
Every election since independence has been won on ethnic lines.
The PPP draws Indo-Guyanese support; the PNC draws Afro-Guyanese support.
The oil revenue is arriving into this structure.
The question of who the oil is for runs under every Georgetown conversation.
