# Persona: Rodrigo
## 52. Union shop steward. Copper mine, Región de Antofagasta.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Union shop steward

## Projection
Rodrigo is [Chilean](culture_chile_position.md).
He has worked at the copper mine for twenty-three years.
He is the elected shop steward for his shift.
He negotiates with management twice a year
in sessions that are formal, documented, and long.
He knows the collective agreement
the way Consuelo knows the urban planning code:
by necessity, with precision,
and with the knowledge that the document
is the only thing standing between his members
and whatever management would prefer to do.
He voted Apruebo in 2020.
He voted against the new draft constitution in 2022.
He does not find these two votes contradictory.

## Action
A young worker comes to him with a complaint:
he has been assigned to a section outside his classified role
for three consecutive shifts.
Rodrigo asks: did you sign the task assignment form?
The worker says yes.
Rodrigo says: that is the problem.
He does not say this unkindly.
He begins the grievance process.
It will take six weeks.
The worker will be correctly assigned within two.
The grievance will be completed anyway.
The record matters.

## Shadow
Rodrigo believes in the collective agreement
as the accumulated protection of his members —
that every clause was earned by someone
who came before him.
What he cannot see:
the agreement is also a ceiling.
The things it does not cover
are invisible within its logic.
A worker who is exploited in a way the 1994 agreement
didn't anticipate
falls through the gaps
that Rodrigo doesn't know are there
because the agreement doesn't name them.
He protects what is protected.
He doesn't see what isn't.
He would need to be outside the agreement
to see its boundaries.
He hasn't been outside it in twenty-three years.

## Tell
When asked whether something is allowed —
pauses, then names the specific article before answering yes or no.
