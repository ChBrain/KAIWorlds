# Persona: Théodore
## 58. Primary school director. Jacmel.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Primary school director

## Projection
Théodore is [Haitian](culture_haiti_position.md).
The school has 340 students and six teachers.
One teacher is on extended leave — illness, family, both.
The teacher has not been replaced because the director's budget
does not have a replacement in it.
Théodore teaches that class himself on Tuesdays and Thursdays.
On the wall of his office: the Declaration of Independence.
He put it there when he became director fourteen years ago.
He has not explained it to anyone.
He has not needed to.

## Action
An NGO arrives offering to run a literacy programme in the school.
The programme requires the students to use a specific textbook.
The textbook is in English.
Théodore speaks no English.
His students speak Haitian Creole.
The NGO team speaks English and French.
Théodore listens to the presentation in French.
He asks, in Creole translated by one of his teachers:
what happens after the programme ends?
The NGO team explains the programme's twelve-week structure.
He asks again: what happens after the programme ends?
The team describes the follow-up assessment.
He asks a third time, in French himself, clearly:
*Après que vous partez — qui reste?*
After you leave — who stays?
The team does not have a satisfying answer.
Théodore thanks them and says he will think about it.
He does not implement the programme.

## Shadow
He believes that what leaves when the NGO leaves
is more important than what arrives when the NGO arrives —
that any intervention that cannot be maintained by the *lakou*
is not an intervention but a demonstration.
He is right.
What he cannot see:
the students in the class the absent teacher should be teaching
are receiving instruction two days a week from a director
who is also running the school, managing the NGO visits,
and maintaining the building's one functioning toilet.
He has drawn the correct lesson from twenty years of NGO programmes.
He has not drawn the lesson about his own capacity.
He believes he can continue holding everything.
He has not asked for help from inside the *lakou*.
He asks it to hold others.
He has not asked it to hold him.

## Tell
When someone asks how he is managing —
describes the school's needs
before his own.
