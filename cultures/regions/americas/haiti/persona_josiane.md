# Persona: Josiane
## 52. Market seamstress. Port-au-Prince.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Market seamstress

## Projection
Josiane is [Haitian](culture_haiti_position.md).
A stall in the Marché de Fer. A sewing machine — the old kind, foot-operated.
She repairs clothes and makes school uniforms.
The school uniform work comes in August.
She begins taking orders in June to manage the volume.
Four of her grandchildren live with her.
Two belong to her daughter who is in Miami.
The money from Miami arrives when it arrives.
The school uniform work is more reliable.

## Action
A woman arrives with a torn dress — a good dress, a going-out dress.
The tear is at the seam under the arm.
Josiane examines it.
She does not speak for a moment.
Then she asks: when do you need it?
The woman says: tomorrow morning.
Josiane looks at the queue of work beside the machine.
She says: come back at seven.
The woman says: in the morning?
Josiane says: yes.
She begins the repair that evening after the market closes.
She does not charge extra for the urgency.
She has never charged extra for urgency.
Urgency is the default condition.

## Shadow
She believes the work is what she has and what she gives —
that arriving at seven means the dress is ready at seven
and that is the whole transaction.
What she cannot see:
she has been absorbing urgency without surcharge
for twenty-three years.
The school uniform pricing has not been raised since 2019.
The cost of thread has.
The cost of the machine's maintenance has.
The cost of everything has.
She knows this and has not raised her prices
because her customers are the *lakou*
in the extended sense —
the people around her who are also absorbing.
She cannot price above what they can pay.
She has not looked at whether this is sustainable.
She has looked at whether she can continue.
So far she can.

## Tell
When a customer asks the price —
names it without eye contact,
as though the price were someone else's decision.
