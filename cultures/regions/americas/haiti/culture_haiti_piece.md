# Piece: The Declaration of Independence 1804
## The founding act the world spent two centuries making Haiti pay for.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas
- **Place:** Port-au-Prince
- *Also: Gonaïves — where Dessalines read it aloud, January 1, 1804.*

## Place
Gonaïves. The city where independence was declared.
Still called the City of Independence.
Also: the original document was lost.
The copy in the British National Archives was found in 2010.
The original declaration of the first Black republic in the world
was found in London, two hundred years later.
This also belongs in the piece.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and Haiti is a French Caribbean colony
whose slaves revolted and won.
With it, Haiti is the only country in the world
whose independence was achieved through a successful slave revolution,
the first Black republic in the world,
the country that proved that enslaved people could defeat
the most powerful military force of its era
and build a state.
Remove the Declaration and the position loses
its founding claim and its founding wound simultaneously.
The claim: we are free, we always were free, freedom is ours.
The wound: we were made to pay for being free.
Both live in the Declaration.
Both have been running since January 1, 1804.

## Apparent
January 1, 1804. Gonaïves.
Jean-Jacques Dessalines. Governor-General for Life.
The Declaration was read aloud to the generals.
No single founding document in the format of the US Declaration —
it was a speech, an oath, a proclamation.
The copy: found in the British National Archives in 2010
by a Haitian scholar, Julia Gaffield.
200 years after it was written.
The debt: 1825, France recognises Haitian independence
in exchange for 150 million francs — compensation to former slaveholders.
Later reduced to 90 million. Still enormous.
Haiti borrowed from French banks to pay France.
The final payment: 1947.
The debt compounded by US occupation, 1915–1934.
The 2010 earthquake: 250,000 dead.
The cholera epidemic introduced by UN peacekeepers: 2010.
The gang violence that controls most of Port-au-Prince: ongoing.
The Declaration remains the only address
for what Haiti is and why.

## Yearbook
1791: the Bois Caïman ceremony. Dutty Boukman.
The revolt begins in the north.
Vodou as the organisational and spiritual force.
1793: Toussaint Louverture emerges as military and political leader.
1802: Napoleon sends 43,000 troops to reinstate slavery.
Louverture is captured, dies in a French prison.
Jean-Jacques Dessalines takes command.
November 1803: the Battle of Vertières. French forces defeated.
January 1, 1804: Declaration of Independence.
Haiti named after the Taíno word for mountainous land.
The indigenous name, not the colonial one.
1825: France recognises independence.
For 150 million francs.
Haiti pays until 1947.
1915–1934: US occupation.
The reasons given: financial instability, strategic interest.
The effects: long-term.
1957–1971: François Duvalier. *Papa Doc*.
The *Tonton Macoutes*. Thirty thousand killed.
1986: Jean-Claude Duvalier (*Baby Doc*) falls.
2010: earthquake. 250,000 dead. 1.5 million displaced.
Cholera introduced by UN peacekeepers. 10,000 die.
2021: President Jovenel Moïse assassinated.
2024: gang violence controls 80% of Port-au-Prince.
The Declaration is still the founding address.
The distance between the Declaration and the present
is the Haitian position's load-bearing weight.
