# Position: Trinidadian
## The society that made something out of plurality by deciding to enjoy the present.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Has
[1834 and 1845: The Two Arrivals](culture_trinidad_tobago_piece.md).
The moderate hierarchy — PDI 47.
The boss is acknowledged but not untouchable.
The Trinidadian who disagrees with the boss
will find a way to say so
that maintains the relationship.
The disagreement will be in the subtext.
The subtext will be understood.
The deep collectivism — IDV 16.
The crew. The lime group. The family, extended far beyond nuclear.
The *pardner* — the rotating savings pool,
where a group commits to regular contributions
and each member takes the pot in turn.
Trust as the operating currency.
The moderate masculinity — MAS 58.
Achievement matters: the Trinidadian who has done well
wants it known.
But so does the *lime* — the sociability, the ease, the being together.
Both are real.
The Trinidadian who drove to the lime in a new car
and told no one about it
would be considered strange.
The Trinidadian who talked only about the car
and not about the company would also be considered strange.
The moderate uncertainty avoidance — UAI 55.
Between the two Caribbean extremes:
not the Jamaica UAI 13 (everything *irie*),
not the rules-and-anxiety of high UAI Latin America.
The Trinidadian *lime*: relaxed about time, precise about certain things.
The doubles vendor who runs out at 10am sharp
and won't make more.
The precision is in the product, not the schedule.
The extreme short-term normative orientation — LTO 13.
The present. Now. This evening. This Carnival.
The planning horizon in T&T is, culturally, the next major event.
The next event after Carnival is Divali.
After Divali: Parang. After Parang: Carnival again.
The cultural calendar is the planning document.
The IVR 80: the indulgence.
The food. The music. The fête. The lime.
To be Trinidadian is to understand that enjoyment
is not postponable.
The position holds the plurality underneath all of this:
the Afro- and Indo-Trinidadian communities
who built the same country on different terms
and found, in the lime and the fête and the Savannah,
that the present is the only time
when they are simply all Trinidadian.

## Orders
Show up at the lime that is uncomfortable.
The lime where both communities are present.
The *pardner* hand that crosses the ethnic arithmetic.
The lime is easy when it is the crew you already know.
The position demands the lime that requires effort:
the Panorama rehearsal where the arrangement includes players
outside your immediate community.
The guidance session where the student's path runs
through a network that is not your own.
The *pardner* hand with the colleague you do not know as well.
The lime is not only celebration.
It is the mechanism by which the arithmetic is held.
The arithmetic requires the mechanism.
The mechanism requires showing up.
Every time, not only when it is convenient.
## Loses
The structural resolution of what it built.
The lime works in the present.
The ethnic arithmetic works over time.
The position has never found a way
to make the lime's plural warmth
durable beyond the lime.
Carnival is six weeks of preparation and two days of experience.
The experience is real.
The political arithmetic resumes on Wednesday.
The position knows this.
It goes to Carnival anyway.
The present is worth having
even when what follows the present
is the same old problem.

## Drives
On the loss — another lime.
Not in denial:
the position knows the ethnic arithmetic has not been resolved
in 180 years.
It also knows that the lime has kept the country together
when the arithmetic should have broken it.
The position trusts the lime
more than it trusts any other institution.
It has historical evidence for this.
It will not stop liming.
