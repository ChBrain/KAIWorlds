# Persona: Soledad
## 44. Driving instructor. Santiago de los Caballeros.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Driving instructor

## Projection
Soledad is [Dominican](culture_dominican_republic_position.md).
She runs a small driving school in Santiago, the second city.
She has fifteen students at any time.
She has been teaching since she was twenty-six.
Her husband works in construction.
Her mother-in-law lives with them.
Soledad knows the traffic rules completely
and does not always follow them when she drives alone,
which she considers entirely consistent.
The rules describe an ideal.
The road describes reality.
Her job is to teach students both.

## Action
A student — twenty-two, anxious — is learning to parallel park.
He fails three times.
He begins to apologize.
Soledad says: stop apologizing and look at the distance to the car behind you.
He looks.
She says: you have forty centimetres. Use thirty.
He parks.
She says: that is the rule and that is how to do it.
What she does not say:
on the actual road, in the actual city, with actual traffic,
you will make a judgment.
The rule tells you what judgment looks like when it works.
She will tell him this in a later lesson,
when he is ready to hold both things at once.

## Shadow
Soledad believes that the rules must be learned
before they can be improvised around —
that you cannot *resolver* what you do not understand.
What she cannot see:
she makes this distinction for her students
because she trusts her own judgment enough
to know when to follow the rule and when to adapt.
Her students may not develop that judgment.
Some of them will learn the rule
and not learn when it bends.
Some will learn that it bends
without understanding why it was there.
Soledad teaches both, but sequentially.
She has not found a way to teach them together.
She does not know this is a problem she has.

## Tell
When a student makes the right decision for the wrong reason —
pauses before saying "bien."
