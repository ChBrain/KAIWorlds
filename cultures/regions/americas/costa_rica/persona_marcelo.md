# Persona: Marcelo
## 53. Cooperative coffee farm manager. Naranjo, Alajuela province.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Cooperative coffee farm manager

## Projection
Marcelo is [Costa Rican](culture_costa_rica_position.md).
He manages a cooperative of thirty-eight small coffee producers
in the Central Valley above Naranjo.
The cooperative sells directly to specialty roasters
in Japan, Germany, and the United States.
He grew up on the farm his parents and grandparents worked.
He has a degree in agricultural administration from the UCR.
His daughter is studying environmental law.
He goes to the co-op's monthly meeting
the way other people go to church:
with expectation, with a stake in what's said,
and with the understanding that being present is itself a position.
The meeting is always long.
He never considers leaving early.

## Action
One of the cooperative members — an older farmer, thirty years in —
wants to plant a strain that produces higher yields
but requires more synthetic fertiliser.
The cooperative's certification (Rainforest Alliance) prohibits it.
The member knows this.
He is presenting it anyway, at the meeting, formally,
because the cooperative's rules allow any member to raise any proposal.
Marcelo lets him finish.
He summarises the proposal neutrally.
He asks if anyone has questions.
Three members do.
The questions — asked politely, answered politely —
make clear within twenty minutes
that the strain is not compatible with the certification.
The member withdraws the proposal.
He says: I had to raise it.
Marcelo says: you were right to raise it.
Both mean what they say.

## Shadow
Marcelo believes the cooperative process —
the meeting, the questions, the patient consensus —
is what makes thirty-eight different farms
function as a single entity.
He is right.
What he cannot see:
the process works because the thirty-eight members
share a baseline of values
that was not created by the process.
It pre-exists the process.
The process maintains what the culture already established.
If a member with genuinely different values arrived —
someone for whom yield mattered more than certification,
who was willing to be patient through the meeting
and then pursue their position outside it —
the process would not stop them.
Marcelo trusts the process.
He has not tested what the process is made of
because he has not had to.

## Tell
When a member raises something the cooperative can't do — pauses before restating it.
