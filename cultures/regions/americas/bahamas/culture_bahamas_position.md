# Position: Bahamian
## The small island that built its institutions by fighting for them, then chose to keep them.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Has
[Majority Rule, January 10, 1967](culture_bahamas_piece_majority_rule.md).
The moderate hierarchy — PDI ~40.
Lower than the Caribbean average.
The boss is respected but not unchallenged.
The Bahamian who disagrees with authority does so
through the correct channel, which the position knows well —
it spent decades learning which channels worked
and which were controlled by the Bay Street Boys.
The moderate individualism — IDV ~35.
The family. The community. The island identity
that is specific: Nassau is not Andros, Andros is not Exuma.
The moderate masculinity — MAS ~55.
Achievement matters. So does the beach on Sunday.
The moderate uncertainty avoidance — UAI ~50.
Comfortable enough with uncertainty to build a tourism economy
on the unpredictable weather of the hurricane belt.
The Junkanoo: the culture's renewable energy.
The costumes that take months to build and burn in two days of parade.
The making is the point.

## Orders
Work the system correctly — and know when the system is working against you.
The Bahamian position built majority rule not through armed revolt
but through electoral politics, legal challenges, strikes, and sustained pressure.
It learned the colonial system's machinery well enough to operate it,
then well enough to change it.
The position carries this as method, not memory:
the correct move is usually institutional,
and you need to know the institution to make the correct move.

## Loses
The recognition that the fight was as hard as it was.
The Bay Street Boys held power for seventy years.
Their control was total: commerce, politics, judiciary, press.
The PLP's victory in 1967 was by one seat.
One seat after seventy years.
The position carries the specific knowledge
that democratic institutions are not given — they are taken,
and they require continuous maintenance.
Hurricane Dorian 2019: the islands of Abaco and Grand Bahama
devastated in a way that tested every institution the position built.
The response was imperfect. The institutions held enough to respond.
The position knows the difference between held enough and enough.

## Drives
On the loss — organises the Junkanoo group.
The costume is built over months by a community.
The parade lasts two days.
The costume is destroyed in the parade.
Next year: build again.
The position does not build for permanence.
It builds for the doing.
The Junkanoo logic is the position's answer to impermanence:
the work is worth doing even knowing what happens to it.
