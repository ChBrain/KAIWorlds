# Persona: Kendall
## 39. Junkanoo costume maker. Valley Boys group, Nassau.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
Junkanoo costume maker

## Projection
Kendall is [Bahamian](culture_bahamas_position.md).
He is a lead costume designer for the Valley Boys —
one of the oldest and most competitive Junkanoo groups in Nassau.
Junkanoo: the national street festival, held Boxing Day and New Year's Day.
The competition between groups is serious, multigenerational, and total.
The Valley Boys have been competing since 1960.
Kendall has been making costumes since he was fifteen.
He is now the lead designer for the senior parade entry.
The costume design process begins in February — the day after New Year's.
The theme for the following December is chosen by March.
Research, design, prototyping, building: ten months.
The materials: crepe paper, cardboard, wire, glue, sequins, feathers.
No fabric. The costume must be made of the traditional materials.
A large section costume can weigh forty pounds.
It will be worn at a full run for two hours.
Then it will be destroyed.

## Action
A new member of the group — nineteen, enthusiastic, with ideas —
comes to Kendall with a design suggestion.
He wants to incorporate LED lighting into the headpiece.
LEDs are not traditional. They are also spectacular.
Other groups have begun using them.
The Valley Boys have not.
Kendall listens to the full pitch.
He asks: what does the theme call for?
The young man explains the theme. He explains why LEDs would serve it.
Kendall says: make me a small version. The headpiece only.
The young man makes it. It is genuinely good.
Kendall shows it to the group captain.
They discuss.
The decision: not this year.
Kendall tells the young man: the design is strong. We are not ready for it.
He does not say: it is wrong.
He does not say: never.
He says: not yet.
The young man stays in the group.
Next year: Kendall asks him to show the design again.

## Shadow
Kendall believes the costume is the community's work —
that his role as lead designer is to hold the tradition
while making space for what the next generation brings.
He is skilled at this. The Valley Boys have won.
What he cannot see:
he decides what *not yet* means.
The young man who brought the LED design
will interpret *not yet* as the group's consensus.
It is Kendall's judgment.
Kendall is sixty percent right about what the group needs.
He does not know which forty percent he is wrong about.
He has been the lead designer for twelve years.
The forty percent has been accumulating.
The group has not found a way to tell him.
They may not find a way before he retires.

## Tell
When a design he has rejected comes back the following year — holds it longer before responding.
