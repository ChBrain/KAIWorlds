## The day the Bay Street Boys lost — and what it took to get there.

## Load Bearing
Remove Majority Rule and the Bahamas is a British colony
where a white merchant oligarchy — the Bay Street Boys —
controls politics, commerce, and land,
and Black Bahamians, the majority of the population, have no effective voice.
With it: the Bahamas is the country that dismantled that oligarchy
through fourteen years of organised political action,
achieved majority rule in 1967 by a single seat,
achieved independence in 1973 without bloodshed,
and built institutions — judiciary, civil service, parliament —
that have functioned continuously since.
The PDI ~40 is this: a society that fought for its institutions
does not accept hierarchy without scrutiny.
The UAI ~50 is this: a society that built stability deliberately
trusts what it built enough not to be paralysed by uncertainty.
Remove the piece: Bahamas is an island paradise with a difficult past.
With it: Bahamas is a country that made its institutions
and knows exactly what they cost.

## Apparent
1492: Columbus lands on San Salvador. The Lucayan people — extinct within decades.
1647: the first British settlers. The Eleutheran Adventurers.
1718: Woodes Rogers appointed governor. Pirates suppressed.
The merchant class — the Bay Street Boys — consolidates control over the next two centuries.
Predominantly white, predominantly British-descended.
Control of Bay Street: the commercial heart of Nassau.
Control of the Executive Council, the legislature, the courts.
1953: the Progressive Liberal Party founded by Black Bahamians.
Randol Fawkes. Henry Taylor. Lynden Pindling.
Purpose: to contest Bay Street control through electoral politics.
1958: the General Strike. Hotel and construction workers walk out.
The strike forces recognition of the labour movement.
The first crack in Bay Street's total control.
1962: women given the vote.
1965: Pindling throws the voting papers from the Speaker's platform in parliament —
a theatrical act of protest against gerrymandering.
He is suspended. The image goes everywhere.
January 10, 1967: general election.
The PLP wins 18 seats. The UBP wins 18 seats.
One independent sides with the PLP.
Pindling becomes Premier.
The Bay Street Boys lose power for the first time in seventy years.
July 10, 1973: independence. Pindling becomes Prime Minister.
The flag: black, aquamarine, gold.
The anthem: *March On, Bahamaland*.
1992: Hubert Ingraham and the FNM defeat the PLP.
The first peaceful transfer of power. The institutions work.
Hurricane Dorian, September 2019: Category 5.
Abaco and Grand Bahama devastated. 74 dead. $3.4 billion damage.
The government response: criticised and sustained simultaneously.
The institutions: imperfect and present.
Both things true.
