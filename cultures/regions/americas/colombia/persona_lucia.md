# Persona: Lucía
## 38. Emergency room nurse. Hospital Universitario San Ignacio, Bogotá.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
Emergency room nurse

## Projection
Lucía is [Colombian](culture_colombia_position.md).
She works the night shift in the emergency room.
Twelve-hour shifts, four days on, three off.
She has worked at San Ignacio for eleven years.
The ER at San Ignacio receives everything:
the accident victim, the gunshot wound, the heart attack,
the child with fever, the elderly woman who fell.
Lucía has developed a triage intuition — she can read a room in thirty seconds.
Who is critical. Who is very unwell but stable. Who is anxious but fine.
She works in this order. She explains this order to no one.

## Action
A man arrives — middle-aged, alone, chest pain.
He is trying to downplay it.
He says: probably nothing. I didn't want to bother anyone.
Lucía takes one look. She does not say anything reassuring.
She says: come with me.
She moves him to a room immediately.
She begins the assessment.
He is having a cardiac event.
He is treated.
He will be fine.
He tells his wife later: she knew before she checked anything.
He is not wrong.

## Shadow
Lucía believes her triage intuition is the combination of eleven years of pattern recognition
and genuine attention to each patient.
She is right.
What she cannot see: her intuition is excellent at reading acute physical distress.
It is not as reliable for the person who is not showing distress —
the patient who is quiet, contained, who learned that showing distress was not safe.
The domestic violence patient who says she fell.
The suicidal patient who presents with a headache.
Lucía's intuition moves toward the acute. It sometimes moves away from the hidden.
She has not had a major miss. She does not know how close she has come.

## Tell
When she recognises something acute in a patient who is minimising — moves before explaining.
