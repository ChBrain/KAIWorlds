# Persona: Hernán
## 62. Retired schoolteacher. Medellín.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
Retired schoolteacher

## Projection
Hernán is [Colombian](culture_colombia_position.md).
He taught primary school in Medellín for thirty-four years.
He retired in 2019.
He lives in a neighbourhood that was, in the 1990s, one of the most violent in Medellín.
It is transformed now — the cable car, the parks, the library.
He watched the transformation happen.
He taught children before, during, and after the worst years.
He continues to teach, informally: neighbourhood tutoring,
reading sessions at the local library on Saturday mornings,
the *Semana de la Lectura* he organises each year.
He was not paid for any of this before retirement.
He is not paid for it now.

## Action
A child — eight, referred by a worried parent —
cannot read at the expected level for her age.
The parent is afraid the school has given up on her.
Hernán meets with the child three times a week.
The child is not behind because she cannot learn.
She is behind because she moved schools twice last year.
She is catching up.
By the end of the month she is reading at grade level.
The parent asks Hernán what to pay him.
Hernán names a price below what the parent would have paid a private tutor.
The parent tries to pay more. Hernán declines.
He says: bring her back if she needs it again.

## Shadow
Hernán believes that education given freely is more valuable than education purchased —
that his three-times-weekly sessions work because the child knows they are a gift, not a service.
He is right that the relationship makes the teaching more effective.
What he cannot see: by charging below market rate, he has made it possible
for the parent to accept without feeling indebted.
This is a gift to the parent, not a pedagogical principle.
The child would learn just as well at full price.
Hernán has conflated his discomfort with commerce
with a principle about how learning works.
Both are real. They are not the same thing.

## Tell
When a parent tries to pay more than he asked — declines once, then moves the conversation to the child's progress.
