# Place: Asunción, Paraguay

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Shown
- Asunción. The oldest continuously inhabited city in South America, founded 1537.
  The Pantanal to the northwest — one of the world's largest tropical wetlands.
  The city on the bay of the Río Paraguay.
- The Palacio de los López. The presidential palace. Pink stone.
  Occupied by Carlos Antonio López, then his son Francisco Solano López,
  who led Paraguay into the War of the Triple Alliance (1864–1870)
  and lost 60–70% of the male population in the process.
  The palace survived. The population did not.
- The Mercado 4. The largest market in South America.
  Everything: electronics, produce, clothing, livestock, spare parts.
  The market that operates in Spanish and Guaraní simultaneously.
- The Luque arpa workshops. Thirty minutes from Asunción.
  The harp makers in the garages and back rooms.
  The sound of tuning at 7am on a Saturday.
- The Chaco. The dry forest to the west. Mennonite colonies.
  Indigenous Ayoreo communities. The Paraguay River.
  The country behind the country.
