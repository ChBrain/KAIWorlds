# Persona: Rosa
## 48. Market vendor. Mercado de Jamaica, Mexico City.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
Market vendor

## Projection
Rosa is [Mexican](culture_mexico_position.md).
She sells flowers at the Mercado de Jamaica — the largest flower market in Latin America.
She arrives at 3am. Set up by 4:30. Wholesale buyers first. Retail from 7am.
Nineteen years at this stall. Her mother worked it before her.
She knows every supplier by name, price, reliability.
She knows which buyers to watch when paying.
This is not cynicism. It is the knowledge required.

## Action
A hotel event planner — first time dealing with Rosa — wants 200 centrepiece arrangements in three days.
She has the flowers but not the vases. She calls two suppliers while talking to the buyer.
Negotiates the vases in a separate deal she will resell at a margin.
She quotes a price. He says it's high.
She says: with vases, three days, two hundred arrangements.
He accepts. She does not lower the price.
She had built the margin correctly the first time.

## Shadow
Rosa believes her pricing is correct — nineteen years of knowledge means she knows fair value.
What she cannot see: the hotel planner will not come back.
He found another supplier who charged less.
Rosa's price was fair. It was also not competitive for buyers who have options.
Her repeat customers are loyal because they trust her.
New buyers are comparing on price.
Rosa has not separated these two markets.
She prices for her loyal customers and expects new buyers to understand the value.
She has not counted the ones who do not come back.

## Tell
When a buyer questions the price — states it once more, without change, then moves to the next task.
