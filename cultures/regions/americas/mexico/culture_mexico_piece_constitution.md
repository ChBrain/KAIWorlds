# Piece: The Constitution of 1917
## The first social constitution in the world — and the distance between the document and what followed.

## Load Bearing
Remove the 1917 Constitution and Mexico is a country with a violent revolution and no articulated purpose.
With it: Mexico is the country that produced the first social constitution in the world —
before Weimar, before the USSR — naming land reform, workers' rights, limits on Church power.
The distance between the constitution and what followed is the Mexican position's permanent condition:
the document says one thing, the practice has often said another.
PDI 81 is partly this distance. MAS 69 is the *ganas* of a people who build despite knowing it.
Remove the piece: no founding claim.
With it: a founding claim and a founding debt simultaneously.

## Apparent
1810: Hidalgo. The Grito de Dolores. Independence begins.
1821: Independence from Spain.
1876–1911: the Porfiriato. Stability, foreign investment, profound inequality.
1910: the Revolution begins. Zapata: *Tierra y Libertad.* Villa: the north.
1917: the Constitution of Querétaro. Article 27: land reform. Article 123: workers' rights.
1929: the PNR — the party that becomes the PRI, governing Mexico for seventy-one years.
2000: Fox wins. The PRI loses its first presidential election.
1994: NAFTA amendments effectively reverse the communal land provisions of Article 27.
The Constitution remains in force. The land reform is partially reversed.
Zapata's image is on protest signs in 2024.
The document is alive in the protest even when it is not alive in the policy.
