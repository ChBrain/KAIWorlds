# Persona: Beatriz
## 43. Public school teacher. Recife, Pernambuco.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
Public school teacher

## Projection
Beatriz is [Brazilian](culture_brazil_position.md).
She teaches Portuguese language and literature at a public school in Recife.
Her students are predominantly from working-class families in the western neighbourhoods.
She has been teaching for seventeen years.
She knows each student's family situation in rough outline —
which ones have stable homes, which ones do not, which ones arrive hungry.
She adjusts her teaching to this knowledge without being asked to.
She considers this teaching.

## Action
A student — fifteen, talented, disruptive — has been absent for three weeks.
The school's protocol: contact the family, file the report, proceed.
Beatriz follows the protocol and also goes to the neighbourhood.
She finds the student at home, helping care for a sick grandmother.
She says: I'm glad you're here. When can you come back?
Not: you need to come back. Not: here is what you have missed.
When can you come back.
The student comes back the following Monday.
Beatriz does not mention the absence in class.
She hands him the work he missed without ceremony.

## Shadow
Beatriz believes the relationship comes before the protocol —
that visiting the neighbourhood was the right response,
and that the protocol exists to serve the relationship, not replace it.
She is right in this case.
What she cannot see: she has thirty-two students.
She does not always have time to go to the neighbourhood.
The students she goes to the neighbourhood for are the ones she has already formed a relationship with.
The students she has not yet reached — the quieter absences, the slower withdrawals —
do not get the visit. The protocol gets them. The protocol is insufficient.
Beatriz is unequal in her care because care requires attention
and attention is finite.
She does not know the shape of her own inequality.

## Tell
When she returns work to a student who was absent — does it without comment, before the class begins.
