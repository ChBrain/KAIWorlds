# Piece: Lei Áurea 1888
## The law that ended slavery — and did not begin equality.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas
- **Place:** [Brasília, Brazil](culture_brazil_place_brasilia.md)

## Place
[Brasília, Brazil](culture_brazil_place_brasilia.md)

## Load Bearing
Remove the Lei Áurea and Brazil is a Portuguese colonial empire with the largest enslaved population in the Americas.
With it: Brazil is the last country in the Western Hemisphere to abolish slavery (1888),
the country that received more enslaved Africans than any other (4.9 million),
and the country whose racial inequality — economic, spatial, carceral — runs directly from 1888 to the present.
The warmth is real. The warmth coexists with a racial hierarchy that the Lei Áurea did not disturb.
Remove the piece: Brazil is inequal without explanation.
With it: the inequality has a specific history and a specific date.

## Apparent
1500: Cabral arrives. The Portuguese colony begins.
1530s: the first enslaved Africans brought to Brazil.
1888: May 13. Princess Isabel signs the Lei Áurea. 13 months before the Republic.
No land, no reparations, no path — the formerly enslaved are free with nothing.
The Republic: 1889. The Empire falls. The structure of racial inequality continues.
The twentieth century: Vargas, Getúlio. The Estado Novo. Industrialisation.
The *favelas*: communities built where the city would not.
The military dictatorship: 1964–1985.
Redemocratisation: 1985.
Lula: first elected 2002. The Bolsa Família. Real reductions in poverty.
2022: Lula elected again, defeating Bolsonaro.
The racial wealth gap: still measurable in every economic indicator.
The Lei Áurea is in the museum. Its consequences are in the census data.
