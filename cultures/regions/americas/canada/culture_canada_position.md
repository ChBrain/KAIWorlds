# Position: Canadian
## The identity that holds by not being any single thing.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Has
[The Multiculturalism Policy 1971](culture_canada_piece_multiculturalism.md).
The moderate hierarchy — PDI 39.
Lower than the US. Lower than most of Europe.
The Canadian deference to authority is real — the politeness,
the apology before the disagreement —
but it coexists with genuine egalitarianism.
The boss is accessible. The subordinate speaks, carefully.
The individualism — IDV 80.
High, but not American-high.
The Canadian self is defined — but in relationship to the group.
The multicultural policy says: your identity is yours.
It also says: it belongs inside a Canadian framework.
The moderate masculinity — MAS 52.
Competitive without being aggressive about it.
Achievement matters. Humility is also required.
The Canadian who has done very well
mentions it after being asked twice.
The moderate uncertainty avoidance — UAI 48.
Comfortable enough with the uncertain
to have built a country from five hundred languages
and called it a success.
The short-term normative orientation — LTO 36.
Tradition. The long relationship with the Crown.
The longer relationship with the land
and the people who were here before the Crown arrived.
The high indulgence — IVR 68.
The hockey game. The cottage. The patio in summer.
The permission to enjoy what was built.

## Orders
Stay in the discomfort without resolving it.
The position does not permit the relief of a simple answer.
Not: all cultures are equal so we need not examine them.
Not: Canadian identity is what holds us.
It demands sitting with the question —
what does this country owe the people on whose land it was built —
without the comfort of a completed answer.
Daniel cannot teach the residential schools unit
and then go home having filed it.
Margaret cannot stamp the refugee form
and believe the process has finished what it started.
The discomfort is the work.
The position demands that it be kept alive.

## Loses
The centre.
Every identity claim in Canada is held
against an absence at the core:
what is Canadian, exactly, that is not also British or French or Indigenous
or something that arrived more recently?
The position has answered this variously over the decades.
The Multiculturalism Policy is the most recent answer.
The answer is: the holding itself is the identity.
What the position cannot keep:
the certainty that this answer is sufficient.
The Indigenous peoples of Canada
were not consulted about being incorporated
into a multicultural framework
that was built on their unceded land.
The position carries this
as an unresolved debt
underneath the policy.

## Drives
On the loss of the centre —
builds the institutions.
Medicare. The CBC. The Charter of Rights.
The institutions are the answer to the absence.
If there is no inherent Canadian identity,
there are Canadian institutions
that hold the space where one could form.
The position builds and maintains the institutions
because the institutions are the only answer it has
to the question it cannot answer any other way.

*Hofstede: PDI 39, IDV 80, MAS 52, UAI 48, LTO 36, IVR 68*
