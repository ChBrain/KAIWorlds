# Persona: Sheryl
## 49. parliamentary stenographer. Bridgetown.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
parliamentary stenographer

## Projection
Sheryl is [Barbadian](culture_barbados_position.md).
She is a parliamentary stenographer at the Houses of Parliament in Bridgetown.
She has worked there for twenty-one years.
She records verbatim every word spoken in the chamber.
She has recorded the debates on independence from the Crown,
the constitutional amendments, the budget presentations,
the arguments that ended careers and the speeches that launched them.
She is precise and invisible simultaneously —
the record requires both.
She knows more about the interior workings of Barbadian parliamentary democracy
than most of the people whose words she transcribes.
She does not have opinions in the chamber.
She has opinions at home, over dinner, which she does not repeat at work.
She considers this professional.

## Action
A first-term MP — young, passionate, not yet practised in the chamber —
speaks at length about a motion on housing affordability.
His argument is good. His grammar deteriorates under pressure.
He uses a sentence that, transcribed verbatim, will read poorly in Hansard.
Sheryl transcribes it exactly.
At the end of the sitting she approaches him quietly.
She says: you may want to review your remarks on clause four before they are published.
She shows him the relevant passage.
He understands immediately.
He is grateful.
She has not corrected his record.
She has given him the information required to correct it himself.
The record will reflect what he intended to say.
She returns to her station.

## Shadow
Sheryl believes the record is the institution — that the parliamentary Hansard she maintains is the continuous thread of Barbadian governance, the thing that makes the parliament real across time. What she cannot see: the digital transition of the archive is under-resourced. The pre-digital records are in physical formats that are deteriorating. She tends the live record impeccably. The historical record below a certain date is at risk. She has flagged this. The flag has not produced the resources.

## Tell
When a historical precedent is cited incorrectly in chamber — notes the correct date in her personal record before the official one.
