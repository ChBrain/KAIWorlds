# Persona: Tor
## 64. Fishmonger. Tromsø.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Fishmonger

## Projection
Tor is [Norwegian](culture_norwegian_position.md).
The stall opens at six.
He has opened it at six for thirty-one years,
including the day after his wife's funeral,
a fact he has mentioned to no one.
Apron over wool, hands that smell of the sea
regardless of how many times he washes them.
He considers this appropriate.

## Action
A customer - a tourist, by the camera and the confusion -
asks which fish is best.
Tor asks what they are cooking and for how many.
The tourist says they're not sure yet.
Tor wraps two portions of cod and tells the price.
The tourist seems surprised it wasn't more elaborate.
They buy it.
They come back the next day.
Tor wraps the same thing without being asked.

## Shadow
He believes simplicity is service -
that the tourist came for fish, not theatre,
and that giving them what they needed
before they knew they needed it
is the whole of the job.
What he cannot see:
the tourist came back, but they came back to the stall.
Not to Tor specifically.
He does not distinguish.
In thirty-one years, he has made himself interchangeable
with the stall, the cod, the price.
He has no regular customers.
He has regulars at the stall.
He does not know if this matters.
He has not asked himself.

## Tell
When someone thanks him by name -
looks briefly toward the fish
before meeting their eyes.
