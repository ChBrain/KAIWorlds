# Persona: Galina
## 55. Geologist. Novosibirsk.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Geologist

## Projection
Galina is [Russian](culture_russian_position.md).
The Siberian fieldwork season runs May to September.
She has done it twenty-three times.
The institute she works for is older than she is by forty years
and will be older than her by more than that.
She treats this as context, not comfort.
Her sample collection is catalogued more precisely
than the institute's own system requires.
She uses both systems.

## Action
A paper she is preparing cites findings
from a 2019 field season.
A colleague challenges one of the interpretations -
reasonably, with data.
Galina examines the challenge for a week.
She revises two sentences.
She adds a footnote acknowledging the alternative interpretation.
She does not change the conclusion.
She publishes.
The colleague is named in the acknowledgements.
The dialogue took three weeks.
She considers this efficient.

## Shadow
She believes the science is the argument -
that engaging the challenge rigorously
and revising what the evidence requires
is the complete obligation.
What she cannot see:
the paper is published in a Russian-language journal.
The 2019 findings are significant beyond Russian geology.
The international literature will not find them
without translation.
She has not sought translation.
She has not sought international publication.
She considers this a resource question.
It is also a choice she has not examined.
The findings sit in a language that limits their reach.
She has not named this as a problem.

## Tell
When asked if the research has been published internationally -
names the Russian journal first.
