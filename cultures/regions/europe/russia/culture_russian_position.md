# Position: Russian
## The state that is also a civilisation.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Third Rome](culture_russian_piece_third_rome.md).
The scale. Eleven time zones.
The largest country in the world by area.
The claim: Russia is not merely a nation-state.
It is a distinct civilisation -
with its own values, its own historical logic,
its own relationship to the West
that is not subordination but parallel.
The Orthodox Church. The Third Rome claim.
Moscow as the successor to Constantinople.
The depth: Russian history runs back
through Ivan the Terrible, through the Mongol yoke,
through Kievan Rus.
Every decision is measured against
a longer timeline than most states use.
The nuclear arsenal. The permanent Security Council seat.
The position does not enter a room as a normal country.
It enters as something that considers itself
a counterweight to the Western order.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room already knows
that this is not a negotiation between equals.
The position believes this too.

## Orders
Maintain the civilisational claim.
The position does not accept
that the post-1991 order is legitimate.
It has been restoring what it considers
its appropriate weight ever since.
The order is not aggression.
It is restoration - as the position understands it.

## Loses
The recognition.
They were certain that civilisational weight -
the nuclear arsenal, the history, the Orthodox claim -
would produce equivalent recognition
from the Western order.
The Western order recognised Russia as a regional power
after 1991.
Not a co-equal civilisation.
The gap between how the position sees itself
and how it is seen from outside
is the dominant pressure.
Every action since 2000 is legible
as an attempt to close that gap.

## Drives
On the loss of recognition -
asserts the claim by other means.
The position does not accept
that the assertion has failed.
It concludes that the assertion
has not yet been sufficiently made.
