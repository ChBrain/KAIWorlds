# Piece: The Revolution of 1989
## The moment Romania paid the highest price in Eastern Europe.

## Owner
- **Place:** Bucharest

## Place
Bucharest. Revolution Square.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Romanian position loses its specific weight.
The 1989 revolution was the moment the private account was presented -
the evidence of what fifty years of internal accounting was for.

## Apparent
The balcony of the Central Committee building, Bucharest.
December 21, 1989. Ceaușescu speaking. The crowd booing.
The television broadcast cutting out. Then resuming.
December 25: the execution broadcast on television.

## Yearbook
1965–1989: Nicolae Ceaușescu in power.
The Securitate - secret police - 40,000 officers,
500,000 informants in a country of 22 million.
One informant per 44 people.
The private accounting was a survival skill.
December 16, 1989: protests begin in Timișoara.
December 21: Bucharest. The booing begins.
December 22: Ceaușescu flees.
December 25: executed.
The fastest revolution in Eastern Europe.
Also the most violent - over 1,000 dead.
The transition that followed: slow, contested, incomplete.
The private accounting of what was lost
under communism is still open.
It was never formally closed.
