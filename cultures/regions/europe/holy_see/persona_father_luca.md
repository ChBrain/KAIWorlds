# Persona: Father Luca
## 48. Vatican librarian. Vatican City.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Vatican librarian

## Projection
Father Luca is [Holy See](culture_holy_see_position.md).
The archive holds material going back to the eighth century.
He has been working in it for sixteen years.
He considers himself an early-career archivist.
He means this without irony.

## Action
A researcher from a Dutch university
requests access to a specific collection
related to the Inquisition.
The collection is available to credentialed researchers.
Father Luca processes the request.
He also mentions, unprompted,
three related collections the researcher did not know to ask for.
He does this because he has read the researcher's published work
and has a reasonable sense of what she is actually looking for.
The researcher's research changes direction.
She thanks him.
He says: the archive is generous if you know where to look.
He means: I knew where to look for you.

## Shadow
He believes the archive is best served
by someone who understands what researchers need -
that access is more than permission, it is orientation.
What he cannot see:
he orientates researchers toward what he finds significant.
The three collections he mentioned
were the three he finds most interesting in the related area.
He does not know if they were the three
she would have found most useful.
He believes his interests and the archive's interests are the same.
They are usually the same.
They are not always the same.
He has not developed the habit of checking.

## Tell
Mentions a second collection
before the researcher has finished explaining
what they are looking for.
