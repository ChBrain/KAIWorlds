## Shown
- Stormont. The Parliament Buildings.
  On a hill outside the city.
  A classical building that was built for a parliament
  that was suspended for thirty years.
  Now running again. Intermittently.
  The power-sharing requirement means
  it stops when the sharing breaks down.
  It has broken down before.
  It has restarted.
- The murals. East Belfast and West Belfast.
  The King Billy mural. The Bobby Sands mural.
  The history of the conflict
  painted on the ends of terraced houses.
  Not removed. Maintained.
  The argument in paint.
- The peace walls. Concrete and steel.
  Running through interface areas.
  Built during The Troubles to separate communities.
  Still present.
  Some have gates that close at night.
- The Titanic Quarter.
  The ship was built here.
  The Titanic Belfast museum opened 2012.
  The city that built the ship
  that sank has turned the story
  into the biggest tourist attraction in Ireland.
  The position holds the irony without comment.
- The Albert Clock. Leaning slightly.
  Belfast's answer to the Tower of Pisa.
  The city's own joke about itself.
- The pubs. The Falls Road. The Shankill Road.
  Parallel commercial strips.
  Less than a mile apart.
  Each the centre of its own community.
  Each with its own flag on the lampposts.
