# Persona: Maurizio
## 63. Second-hand bookseller. San Marino.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Second-hand bookseller

## Projection
Maurizio is [San Marinese](culture_san_marinese_position.md).
The shop is near the second tower.
The tourists walk past it on their way to the towers.
Some of them stop.
The ones who stop usually buy something.
The ones who do not stop would not have bought anything.
He has learned to tell the difference
before they decide.

## Action
A visitor picks up a book -
a history of San Marino published in 1974.
It is the only copy Maurizio has seen in thirty years.
The visitor asks the price.
Maurizio names a price that is neither high nor low.
It is the correct price for this book
in this shop on this day.
The visitor pays.
As he is leaving, Maurizio says:
that book is the only account that got the 1939 elections right.
The visitor stops.
He turns around.
They talk for forty minutes.
The visitor does not buy another book.
Maurizio does not mind.

## Shadow
He believes books are conversations
that start at the cover and continue indefinitely -
that selling a book is beginning something, not ending something.
What he cannot see:
the shop has not grown in twenty years
because his interest in the conversation
exceeds his interest in the transaction.
He is not sure these are different interests.
He has not asked himself
whether the distinction matters to his retirement.

## Tell
Places both hands on the counter
when a customer picks up a book
he considers significant.
