# Piece: The Belgian Compromise
## The state that institutionalised its own irresolvability.

## Owner
- **Place:** Brussels

## Place
Brussels. The Federal Parliament.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and Belgium is two countries that have not yet separated.
With it, Belgium is the proof that irreconcilable positions
can govern together through sufficient institutional engineering.
The EU chose Brussels partly because Brussels had already solved this.

## Apparent
Six governments for 11 million people.
541 days without a federal government after the 2010 election.
World record at the time.
The country continued to function during those 541 days.

## Yearbook
1830: Belgian independence from the Netherlands.
The French-speaking bourgeoisie and the Catholic Church
create a state. The Flemish majority governed in French.
1898: Dutch made an official language.
1962: the linguistic border fixed by law.
The country divided into language regions.
The fix does not fix the tension.
It institutionalises it.
1993: Belgium becomes a federal state.
Six governments. The current structure.
2007–present: every election followed by
a formation crisis measured in months.
The crises end. The governments form.
The compromise holds.
The EU watches and takes notes.
Brussels is already doing
what the EU is trying to do.
