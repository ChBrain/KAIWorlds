# Persona: Vesna
## 41. Occupational therapist. Split.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Occupational therapist

## Projection
Vesna is [Croatian](culture_croatian_position.md).
Works between the hospital and home visits.
The car is reliable because she maintains it herself -
not from frugality, from distrust of the hospital's vehicle pool.
Her notes are handwritten first, typed later.
The handwritten versions are more accurate.

## Action
An elderly patient - a man who was a builder -
has lost fine motor function in his dominant hand.
The standard programme is twelve sessions.
Vesna runs twelve sessions
and adds two unofficial ones at the end
because the twelfth left him three weeks from the threshold he needed.
She documents the twelve.
She does not document the two.
He reaches his threshold.
She discharges him at the point the paperwork says he was already discharged.

## Shadow
She believes the patient's outcome
is the measure, not the session count -
that the two undocumented sessions are the difference
between a man who can or cannot button his own shirt.
What she cannot see:
she does this for the patients she likes.
The man who was a builder, she liked.
There are patients she does not like -
not many, but some -
who have not received the unofficial two sessions.
She does not think of it this way.
She thinks of it as clinical judgment.
The variable is not clinical.

## Tell
When describing a patient's progress -
uses *we* for the ones she has extended
and *they* for the ones she hasn't.
