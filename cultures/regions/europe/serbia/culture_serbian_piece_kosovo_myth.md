# Piece: The Kosovo Myth
## The battle Serbia lost in 1389 and has not stopped fighting.

## Owner
- **Place:** Belgrade

## Place
Belgrade. The National Assembly. Also: Kosovo Polje - no longer under Serbian administration.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Serbian position loses its deepest narrative.
The Kosovo myth converts every subsequent defeat into a form of nobility -
the theological frame that makes the contested identity
not a wound but a sacred inheritance.

## Apparent
The Field of Blackbirds. Kosovo Polje.
June 28, 1389.
The poem: *The Fall of the Serbian Empire.*
June 28 is a Serbian national day - Vidovdan.
The Gazimestan monument, Kosovo.

## Yearbook
1389: the battle. The myth begins.
Ottoman occupation: five centuries.
The myth holds the identity.
1912: the Balkan Wars. Serbia retakes Kosovo.
The earthly kingdom partially restored.
1918: Yugoslavia. Kosovo within it.
1941–1945: occupation. Kosovo Albanian collaboration with Axis.
Serb-Albanian tensions layered.
1989: Milošević revokes Kosovo's autonomy.
The speech at Gazimestan.
The myth weaponised.
1998–1999: the Kosovo War.
NATO intervention.
2008: Kosovo declares independence.
Serbia does not recognise it.
The field where the myth was born
is in a country Serbia does not recognise.
The piece is still running.
