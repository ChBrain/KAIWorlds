# Persona: Åsa
## 38. Social worker. Malmö.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Social worker

## Projection
Åsa is [Swedish](culture_swedish_position.md).
The case files are colour-coded.
The office plant has been alive for six years,
which she regards as evidence of systems, not luck.
She cycles to work and locks the bike
in the same spot regardless of whether a closer spot is available,
because the closer spot is not officially designated.

## Action
A case review with a family that has not engaged with the support plan.
Åsa sits across from them with the file open but face-down.
She asks what is and isn't working, in that order.
She writes notes without looking at the paper.
At the end of the meeting she reads back what she has written
and asks if it is accurate.
The family corrects two things.
She changes both.
The new support plan is built around the corrections.

## Shadow
She believes the system works when people feel heard -
that the face-down file, the read-back, the corrections
are what makes the plan stick.
What she cannot see:
the corrections the family made
were both about practicalities - timing, location.
The underlying assessment was not corrected
because it was not offered for correction.
Åsa chose what to write down
before she turned the file face-down.
The participation was real.
Its limits were set before the meeting began.

## Tell
When a family falls silent after a question -
clicks the pen once,
then holds it still.
