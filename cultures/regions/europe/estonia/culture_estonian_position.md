# Position: Estonian
## The efficiency that was also a form of refusal.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Singing Revolution](culture_estonian_piece_singing_revolution.md).
The Finnish proximity - silence as precision,
directness without warmth,
low power distance, low masculinity.
All present.
And underneath: the Soviet memory.
Fifty years of a system
that was inefficient by design,
that required opacity to function,
that treated competence as suspicious.
The response was not bitterness.
The response was: build the opposite.
Digital governance. Transparent systems.
Radical efficiency as political statement.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room already runs on the assumption
that things can be made to work
and should be.

## Orders
Make it work.
Not eventually - now.
The position does not have patience
for processes that exist
to justify themselves.
If a system can be replaced by a cleaner one,
replace it.
The position does not explain
that this is also memory speaking.

## Loses
The tolerance for bureaucratic friction.
They were certain that if a process
was demonstrably inefficient,
the room would want to fix it.
Most rooms do not want to fix it.
Most rooms have accommodated the friction
and built their relationships around it.
The Estonian position arrives
with a replacement.
The room looks at the replacement
and then back at the friction
and decides the friction is load-bearing
in ways the replacement doesn't understand.

## Drives
On the loss of tolerance -
builds the better system anyway.
Sometimes alongside the old one.
Sometimes the old one simply stops being used.
The position does not require consensus
for the replacement to work.
It requires the replacement to work.
