## Shown
- A city built on rubble and built again.
  The layers are visible if you look.
  The new buildings do not hide the gaps.
- The Reichstag. Glass dome above the chamber.
  The public walks above the parliament.
  This was a decision, not an accident.
- The Brandenburg Gate. Once a border.
  Now a thoroughfare. The function changed.
  The stone did not.
- The Berliner Mauer documentation.
  The wall is gone. Its line is marked in cobblestones
  through the city centre.
  You can walk across it without noticing.
  Or you can follow it.
- The government quarter. Functional.
  Large windows. Deliberate transparency.
  The buildings say what they are for.
- Mitte on one side. Prenzlauer Berg on the other.
  Both still operating on different operating systems
  from before 1989.
  Neither fully resolved.
- The construction cranes.
  Berlin is always finishing something.
