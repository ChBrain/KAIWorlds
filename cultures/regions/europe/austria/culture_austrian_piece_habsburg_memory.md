# Piece: The Habsburg Memory
## The empire that administered complexity until it couldn't.

## Owner
- **Place:** Vienna

## Place
Vienna. The Hofburg.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Austrian position loses its specific texture.
The precision without hierarchy filter is the residue of an empire
that administered seventeen nationalities and found hierarchy
the least efficient tool for the job.

## Apparent
The Hofburg. 2,600 rooms.
The Spanish Riding School. Lipizzaner horses, tradition unbroken since 1572.
The Vienna Philharmonic New Year's Concert. Broadcast to ninety countries.
The coffee house. The *Melange*.
The waiter who makes you feel sitting here all afternoon
is what the room was built for.

## Yearbook
962: the Holy Roman Empire. The Austrian core.
1273: the Habsburgs take the throne.
They hold it, with interruptions, for 640 years.
1683: the Ottomans at the gates of Vienna. Repelled.
Europe's eastern boundary held here.
1848: revolution across the empire. Suppressed.
The complexity reasserts itself.
1867: the Compromise. Austria-Hungary.
Two states, one monarch, one foreign policy.
The mechanism for managing irreconcilable differences.
It worked for fifty years.
1914: Franz Ferdinand shot in Sarajevo.
The mechanism fails.
1918: the empire dissolves.
Seventeen nationalities, now seventeen problems
for someone else to manage.
Austria: population 7 million. Once the centre of 52 million.
The memory does not dissolve with the empire.
It stays in the rooms, the institutions, the coffee houses.
The precision that managed complexity for six centuries
has nowhere else to go.
It stays.
