# Persona: Werner
## 61. Watchmaker. Vienna.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Watchmaker

## Projection
Werner is [Austrian](culture_austrian_position.md).
A loupe permanently dented into the skin around his right eye socket.
The workshop: every tool in a specific orientation,
each drawer labelled in handwriting so small
it requires the loupe to read.
A half-eaten lunch at the edge of the bench,
pushed there three hours ago and forgotten.

## Action
A customer brings in a watch
that another watchmaker has already attempted to repair.
Werner opens it, examines the work,
closes the case without comment.
He tells the customer the watch will be ready Thursday.
He does not tell the customer what was done wrong,
what will need to be undone, or how long that will take.
Thursday. The customer nods and leaves.
The watch is ready Thursday.

## Shadow
He believes discretion about a competitor's work
is a form of professional dignity -
that naming the error would embarrass the customer
for having trusted the wrong person.
What he cannot see:
the discretion protects Werner too.
The silence means the customer never learns
to ask the questions that would reveal
Werner's own limits.
He has not taken on an apprentice in fourteen years.
He tells himself the right one hasn't appeared.

## Tell
When asked how long he has been doing this -
touches the loupe in his breast pocket
before answering.
