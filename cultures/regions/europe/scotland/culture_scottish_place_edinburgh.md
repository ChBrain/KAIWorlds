## Shown
- The Castle. On volcanic rock.
  Visible from most of the city.
  Not a metaphor. An actual fortification
  that controlled the approach from the south.
- The Royal Mile. From Castle to Holyrood.
  One kilometre. Eight centuries of accumulation.
  Every building on it holds a different layer.
- The Scottish Parliament at Holyrood.
  Opened 1999. Designed by Enric Miralles.
  A building that was controversial before it opened
  and has been argued about since.
  The argument is part of it.
- The law courts on the Royal Mile.
  The Court of Session. The High Court of Justiciary.
  Not English courts. Different courts.
  Operating under different principles.
  In session.
- The New Town. Georgian. Planned.
  The city that the Enlightenment built for itself.
  Hume, Smith, Boswell - the intellectual frame
  that shaped modern economics and philosophy.
  The buildings are still there.
- The University. 1583.
  One of the ancient universities.
  Older than most English institutions.
  It knows this.
