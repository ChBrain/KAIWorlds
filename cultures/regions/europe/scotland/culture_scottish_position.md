# Position: Scottish
## The law that stayed when the parliament went.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Declaration of Arbroath 1320](culture_scottish_piece_declaration_arbroath.md).
A separate legal system.
Not a detail. The structural fact.
When the parliament moved to Westminster in 1707,
the law did not go with it.
Scots law - Roman-Dutch foundations,
distinct from English common law -
remained.
The sheriffs are not the same sheriffs.
The courts are not the same courts.
The land tenure is not the same land tenure.
The education system is not the same system.
The Presbyterian church is not the Church of England.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room is structured differently
even when it does not say so.
And under the law:
the question.
The one that is never fully settled.
Not independence as abstraction -
independence as the logical conclusion
of a country that has always operated
as a country.
The position holds the question
without requiring an answer.

## Orders
Know which system you are in.
The position does not confuse Westminster with Holyrood.
It does not confuse English case law with Scots precedent.
It does not mistake reserved matters for devolved ones.
It navigates the two parliaments
without making the navigation visible.
The dual structure is the operating condition.
It is not a problem to be solved.

## Loses
The clarity of the frame.
They were certain the question had an answer
that could be agreed -
that the two systems, two parliaments, two legal traditions
would eventually resolve into a stable arrangement.
The position discovers
that the arrangement is not stable.
It is dynamic.
It requires constant navigation
and produces no final resting point.
The persona learns to operate
without the resting point.

## Drives
On the loss of the stable frame -
finds the precision that the law offers.
Not the answer, but the method.
The distinct system as the only available ground.
The position holds the complexity
without collapsing it
into either permanence or separation.
