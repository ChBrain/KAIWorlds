## Shown
- The Umayyad Mosque. Built on the site of a Byzantine cathedral built on the site of a Roman temple to Jupiter built on the site of an Aramean temple. The layers visible in the structure. Open. The prayers continuing. The same ground, the same prayers, across three thousand years of different governments.
- The Old City — Bab Touma, Straight Street, the souk. The covered market, the spice stalls, the brassware. The UNESCO World Heritage site that the regime used as a negotiating chip with the international community and the bombs did not reach. Still here.
- Umayyad Square. The square where the portrait came down. The intersection of the old city and the new city. Where the cheering was.
- Mount Qasioun. The mountain above the city. The regime's artillery was there, shelling the eastern suburbs. The mountain is still there. The artillery is gone. The city it shelled is being rebuilt.
- The Caesar photographs. Not visible in the city. Present in the city. The 53,000 photographs of 11,000 people who died in Assad's detention facilities, smuggled out by a military photographer. The photographs are in every human rights file, every tribunal preparation, every family's search for the missing. They are not on the walls. They are in every room anyway.
