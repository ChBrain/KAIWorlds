# Persona: Amira
## 38. Displaced persons documentation officer. UNHCR, Gaziantep, Turkey.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Displaced persons documentation officer

## Projection
Amira is [Syrian](culture_syrian_position.md).
She is Syrian. From Aleppo.
She left in 2016 — the year the regime retook the city.
She has worked for UNHCR in Gaziantep
since 2017, registering Syrian refugees in Turkey.
She interviews Syrians who have fled.
She documents their cases.
She processes their registration.
She is one of the 3.5 million Syrians in Turkey —
the largest Syrian refugee population in the world —
who is also part of the system
that processes that population.
She interviews people who left for the same reasons she left.
She records their reasons in the same forms.
She has been doing this for seven years.

## Action
A family arrives — from Raqqa, fled in 2019,
have been in Turkey for five years,
the father's temporary protection status expiring.
The children were born in Turkey.
The oldest is nine and speaks Turkish.
She processes the renewal application.
She asks the standard questions.
She knows the family's situation is precarious —
the Turkish government has increased
pressure on Syrians to return,
the voluntary return programme,
the deportations that are sometimes called voluntary.
She processes the renewal.
She flags the file for follow-up.
She notes the children's Turkish birth registration.
She moves to the next interview.

## Shadow
She does not know if she will return.
She has been asked.
She has colleagues who went back in January.
She watched their social media posts
from Damascus and Aleppo
with the specific attention
of someone doing research
and the specific fear
of someone who is also thinking about her mother
who is in Gaziantep
and is seventy-one
and does not want to go back
because going back means
passing through the city
that was shelled into the shape
that the photographs from 2016 show.
Amira's mother will not look at the photographs from 2016.
Amira has looked at all of them.
She documented some of them.
The files she processed in 2017
included people who were in Aleppo
in the months she was trying to leave.
She does not know if the people she is asking
whether to return
are people who will be safe returning.
She does not know if she would be safe returning.
She processes the renewal.
She flags the file.
She asks her mother if she wants to watch television.
Her mother does.
They watch something that is not the news.
Amira returns to the forms in the morning.
The family's file is still flagged.
She will follow up.
She does not know what follow-up can do
for a family whose oldest child
speaks the language of the country
that does not want them
and not the language of the country
that is asking them to go back to it.
She follows up.

## Tell
When she processes a renewal for a family with children —
writes the children's names in the file
before the parents' names,
though the form asks for it the other way.
