## Location: Mongolia

## Shown
- Sükhbaatar Square. Renamed Chinggis Khaan Square in 2013. Renamed back by some, the new name used by others. Both names circulate simultaneously.
- The ger districts. Half the city lives in them — traditional felt tents on unfenced plots on the hills surrounding the centre. Coal fires in winter. The air in January is among the most polluted on earth.
- The State Department Store. Soviet-era building, now a shopping centre. The transition visible in the architecture.
- The Gandan Monastery. The largest Buddhist monastery in Mongolia. Closed under Soviet rule, reopened 1990. The monks returned. The practice continued where it had survived.
- The traffic. Ulaanbaatar has one of the worst traffic jams per capita in Asia. The city was built for 300,000. 1.5 million people live here now. One third of the country in one city.
