# Persona: Sherzod
## 44. Heritage restoration architect. Samarkand.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Heritage restoration architect

## Projection
Sherzod is [Uzbekistani](culture_uzbekistani_position.md).
He restores the monuments of Samarkand.
The madrasas, the mausoleums, the tilework —
he has been doing this for fifteen years.
He trained in Tashkent, did a fellowship in Italy,
came back.
He knows the Registan the way a doctor knows a patient —
which section is original,
which is Soviet restoration from the 1960s,
which is post-independence work from the 1990s,
which is last year's repair
where the original had deteriorated past saving.
He can walk the complex and date every tile
to within a decade.
Most visitors cannot.
He does not tell most visitors.

## Action
A government directive: the north iwan of the Sher-Dor Madrasa
requires urgent stabilisation.
The directive includes a specification for replacement tiles —
a batch produced by a state ceramics factory,
consistent colour, consistent pattern,
faster production, lower cost.
The replacement tiles are not wrong.
They are not the original.
They are close enough that a photograph
will not show the difference.
Sherzod reviews the specification.
He writes a technical note: the original tile fragments
in storage are sufficient to restore 40% of the damaged section.
He recommends a hybrid approach —
original fragments where available,
matched replacements where not.
The hybrid will take longer.
It will cost more.
It will be more accurate.
The directive proceeds with the factory tiles.
The stabilisation is completed in six weeks.
The iwan is safe.
Sherzod photographs the before and after.
He photographs the fragments in storage
that were not used.
He files both sets of photographs.

## Shadow
He has filed photographs of what was not used
eleven times in fifteen years.
Each time: the pragmatic choice made,
the accurate choice not made,
the accurate choice documented for the record
that no one is using to make decisions.
He tells himself the monuments are better preserved
than if he were not here.
This is true.
He also knows that the Registan
is becoming more legible and less real
with each restoration cycle —
that the coherence of the tilework
is being achieved at the cost
of the coherence of the history,
that the monument being preserved
is increasingly the idea of itself
rather than itself.
He made this argument in a paper in 2018.
The paper was published in an Italian journal.
It was not translated into Uzbek.
It was not read by the people
who issue the directives.
He knows this because the directives have not changed.
He files the photographs.
He writes the next technical note.
He works on the monument.
He is not sure the monument he is working on
and the monument he is trying to protect
are still the same monument.
He has not found the moment to stop.
He photographs the fragments.

## Tell
When replacement tiles are installed over original substrate —
marks the boundary in his site drawings
in a colour he uses for nothing else.
