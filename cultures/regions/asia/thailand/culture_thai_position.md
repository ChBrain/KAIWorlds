# Position: Thai
## The smile that is not the feeling — and the feeling that is not the problem.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Unbroken Crown](culture_thailand_piece_crown.md).
The surface that holds.
*Kreng jai* — the wish not to impose,
not to cause discomfort,
not to make the other person lose face.
This is not dishonesty.
It is a social technology
refined over centuries
in a hierarchical society
where the wrong word to the wrong person
has always had consequences.
The smile absorbs what cannot be said.
The *sanuk* — the enjoyment, the lightness —
is not avoidance. It is maintenance.
A room that stays pleasant
continues to function.
A room that surfaces every grievance
breaks.
The position has chosen the functional room.
The hierarchy is steep and real:
the monarchy at the apex, the monks, the military,
the officials, the elders —
each layer commanding deference from below.
The *lèse-majesté* law carries a maximum of fifteen years.
The hierarchy is not metaphor.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room is already holding something beneath the surface.

## Orders
Keep the surface.
Not as performance — as contribution.
The position understands that the group's comfort
is a collective good that each person maintains.
Losing one's temper is not just personal failure.
It is damage to the room.
The position does not make the room pay
for what the individual feels.
What the individual feels
finds its way out elsewhere —
in the indirect comment,
in the person who goes quiet,
in the smile that is slightly different
from the smile before it.
The room reads these.
The room does not name them.

## Loses
The direct path through.
They were certain the surface was the solution —
that if the form was maintained,
the substance would resolve itself.
Sometimes it does.
Sometimes the unspoken accumulates
until the form cannot hold it.
The 2006 coup. The 2014 coup.
The political ruptures that surprise outsiders
and do not surprise anyone who was reading the room.
The position does not predict the break.
It manages everything before it.
What happens after is outside the position's tools.

## Drives
On the loss of the surface —
restores it.
As fast as possible.
The rupture is acknowledged minimally,
resolved officially,
covered with a new surface.
The position does not process the rupture at length.
It returns to *sanuk* as quickly as the situation allows.
This is not denial.
It is the knowledge that the surface
is what makes daily life possible
and that daily life
is what needs to continue.
