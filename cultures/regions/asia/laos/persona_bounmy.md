# Persona: Bounmy
## 46. UXO clearance team leader. Xieng Khouang province.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
UXO clearance team leader

## Projection
Bounmy is [Lao](culture_lao_position.md).
He grew up in Xieng Khouang — the Plain of Jars,
the most bombed province in the most bombed country.
His father lost two fingers to a bomblet in 1987.
Bounmy was nine.
He has been clearing unexploded ordnance
for sixteen years.
He leads a team of eleven.
They clear approximately 1.2 hectares per month.
He knows the cluster bomblet variants —
BLU-26, BLU-36, BLU-63 —
by sight and by the specific care each requires.
He is methodical and he is still alive
and in this work those two things are related.

## Action
A village outside Phonsavan needs a new rice field.
The land is on a hillside that has not been assessed.
The village chief asks Bounmy's team to clear it.
The assessment takes three days.
The team finds 23 items — bomblets, a larger device, fragments.
All cleared. Eleven days total.
The village plants the field.
Bounmy completes the clearance certificate.
He marks the area on the district map as cleared.
He moves to the next task on the list.
The list has 340 tasks on it.
His team processes approximately 18 per year.

## Shadow
He has done the arithmetic.
340 tasks. 18 per year. His team.
There are other teams.
He has done the broader arithmetic too —
the national clearance programme, all teams combined,
the estimated remaining contamination,
the rate.
It comes out to between 80 and 100 years
at current funding and staffing.
He will be dead before the province is clear.
His children will be middle-aged.
His grandchildren will inherit a province
that is still not fully safe to farm.
He knows this.
He clears 1.2 hectares per month.
The *bor pen nyang* is available to him as a frame —
you do what you can do, you do not destroy yourself
over what you cannot.
He uses it.
He also does not fully believe it.
He believes the rice field matters.
He also believes the rice field is not enough
and that someone — the Americans, the international community,
the government, someone —
should be moving faster than they are
and he does not say this loudly
because saying it loudly is not how things work here
and saying it loudly would not make the funding come faster
and the work would still need doing
and he is the one who does it.
He marks the next task on the list.
He does not look at how many are below it.
He learned, in year three, not to look at the full list.
He looks only at the next task.
This is how he continues.
He is not sure it is how he should continue.
He continues anyway.

## Tell
When a field is certified clear —
stands at the edge of it for a moment
before signing the certificate.
