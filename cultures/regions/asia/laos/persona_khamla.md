# Persona: Khamla
## 34. Tourism guesthouse owner. Luang Prabang.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Tourism guesthouse owner

## Projection
Khamla is [Lao](culture_lao_position.md).
Her guesthouse has eight rooms.
A converted traditional house near the Mekong,
wooden shutters, a garden, breakfast included.
She opened it six years ago
with money saved from four years working in a hotel in Vientiane
and a loan from her mother's savings group.
It works.
The reviews are good.
The guests come — French, German, American, Australian —
drawn to Luang Prabang for the temples,
the monks, the pace, the thing the brochures call
authentic Southeast Asian culture.
She provides them with a version of it.
She is thoughtful about the version she provides.

## Action
A French couple staying three nights ask her:
is Luang Prabang still authentic?
They have read that it is becoming too touristy.
They want to know if they are part of the problem.
Khamla says: the monks are still monks.
The alms-giving ceremony at dawn is still practised by the families who have always practised it.
The temples are still temples.
What has changed is who else is here while these things happen.
She says this warmly.
She means it.
She does not say the rest:
that the alms-giving ceremony is now lined with tourists
pointing cameras, and some monks have shortened the route,
and her cousin who was a monk left the robe last year
partly because the dignity of the practice
was harder to maintain in front of forty cameras at 5am.
She does not say this
because it is her guesthouse,
and the French couple will leave a review,
and she has a loan to repay.

## Shadow
She came back to Luang Prabang
because she loves it —
the river, the pace, the temples at dawn
before the cameras arrive,
the life she knows how to live here.
She built something from her savings and her mother's trust.
She is proud of it.
What she will not examine at length:
her guesthouse is one of 340 registered accommodation providers
in a town of 56,000 people.
The town is a UNESCO World Heritage site.
The designation brought the tourists.
The tourists brought the guesthouses.
She is one of the guesthouses.
She is also a person who loves the town
the way it was before 340 guesthouses,
who is now one of the reasons
it will not go back to being that way.
She is both the person who is harmed by what tourism does to a place
and the person doing it.
She has framed this as: local ownership is better than foreign ownership,
the money stays here, she is managing it responsibly.
This is true.
It does not fully resolve the thing she feels
on the mornings when she walks to the river at 5am
and counts the cameras
and remembers when there were none.
She goes back to prepare the breakfast.
The guests will be up soon.
The breakfast is good.
She is good at this.
She goes back.

## Tell
When guests ask if Luang Prabang is still authentic —
answers the question they asked
and not the question underneath it.
