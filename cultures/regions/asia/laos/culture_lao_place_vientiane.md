## Shown
- Pha That Luang. The golden stupa. The national symbol of Laos. Built in the sixteenth century on the site of a Khmer temple. Destroyed by the Siamese in 1828. Reconstructed by the French in 1930. The reconstruction based on incomplete drawings. The stupa that exists is an approximation of the stupa that was. Both are called Pha That Luang.
- Patuxai — the Victory Gate. Modelled on the Arc de Triomphe. Built in the 1960s with American concrete intended for an airport runway. The Americans called it the vertical runway. The Lao called it Patuxai. It is a monument now. The concrete was used.
- The Mekong riverfront. The promenade. At dusk: families, food vendors, the river wide and slow and brown. Thailand is on the other side. You can see the Thai bank clearly. Many Lao have relatives there.
- Morning Market — Talat Sao. Electronics, fabric, silver, food. Open at dawn, winding down by noon. The pace of the city is in the market's hours.
- The COPE Visitor Centre. The prosthetics. The photographs. The UXO clearance maps. Open to visitors. Admission free. The centre makes the invisible visible: the war that ended fifty years ago and did not end.
