# Piece: The Gas and the Cult
## The fourth largest natural gas reserves in the world — and the system they built around them.

## Owner
- **Place:** Ashgabat

## Place
Ashgabat. The white marble city.
Everything white. The marble came from Turkey.
The buildings: ministries, hotels, monuments —
all white, all monumental, all new.
The Guinness records: most white marble buildings in a city,
most fountains, largest indoor Ferris wheel.
The city as argument: we have gas money
and we are spending it on this.
The Darvaza Gas Crater — the Door to Hell —
300 kilometres north.
A natural gas field that collapsed in 1971.
The Soviets set it on fire to prevent methane release.
It has been burning since.
The fire in the desert.
The gas that will not stop.
Both are Turkmenistan.

## Load Bearing
Remove the gas and Turkmenistan is a desert country
of 6 million people with no particular strategic significance.
With it: Turkmenistan is the country
that has the fourth largest natural gas reserves in the world,
that sells almost all of it to China through a single pipeline,
that has used the revenue to build
the world's most elaborate personality cult infrastructure
in a city of white marble
while its population — outside the capital —
lives in conditions
that the gas revenue has not reached.
The gas is the system's foundation.
Without the gas, the white marble city cannot be maintained.
Without the gas, the population cannot be kept compliant
through subsidised fuel, water, and electricity.
Without the gas, the isolation is untenable.
The gas subsidises the enclosure.
Remove the piece: a repressive Central Asian state.
With it: a repressive Central Asian state
that can afford to be more repressive than most
because the gas revenue removes
the economic pressure that topples other systems.
The Darvaza crater has been burning for fifty years.
The system has been burning for thirty.
Both are sustained by the same gas.

## Apparent
1881: Russian Empire absorbs Turkmenistan.
1924: the Turkmen Soviet Socialist Republic.
1991: independence. Saparmurat Niyazov becomes president.
1993: Niyazov takes the title Turkmenbashi — Leader of all Turkmen.
The *Ruhnama* — Book of the Soul — written by Niyazov.
Required reading for all exams, all schools, all mosques.
The golden rotating statue in Ashgabat — always facing the sun.
The months renamed: January becomes Turkmenbashi.
April becomes the name of his mother.
Opera banned. Ballet banned. Circuses banned.
Gold teeth banned — Niyazov recommended chewing bones instead.
Doctors required to renounce the Hippocratic Oath and swear to Niyazov instead.
The Darvaza crater: burning.
2006: Niyazov dies of heart failure.
Gurbanguly Berdymukhamedov takes power.
The golden rotating statue removed. The months renamed back.
The *Ruhnama* reduced from two to one required subject.
The cult of Berdymukhamedov begins.
2022: Berdymukhamedov's son Serdar becomes president.
Berdymukhamedov remains as head of the upper house.
The dynasty is explicit.
The gas flows to China.
The marble is white.
The crater burns.
