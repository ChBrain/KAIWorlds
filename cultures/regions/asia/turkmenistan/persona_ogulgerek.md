# Persona: Ogulgerek
## 38. Secondary school Turkmen language teacher. Mary province.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Secondary school Turkmen language teacher

## Projection
Ogulgerek is [Turkmenistani](culture_turkmenistani_position.md).
She teaches Turkmen language and literature
in a secondary school in Mary — the provincial capital,
the city near the ancient ruins of Merv,
once one of the largest cities in the world.
She teaches the language and she teaches the *Ruhnama* —
still required, reduced from its Niyazov-era prominence
but present.
She is a good literature teacher.
She finds what is genuinely worth teaching
in the assigned texts
and teaches from that.
She has become expert at finding
the small true thing inside the required material.

## Action
A literature class. The assigned text:
an excerpt from a speech by the current president
on the importance of Turkmen cultural heritage.
The speech contains genuine content —
the Akhal-Teke horse, the carpet patterns, the ancient cities.
It also contains the standard framing:
the current leadership as the guardian of this heritage.
She teaches the heritage.
She moves through the framing without dwelling.
She asks the students: what does the carpet pattern mean?
What did Merv produce that the world still uses?
The students know.
She did not teach them the frame.
She taught them what the frame was placed around.
After class a student — sixteen, quick — stays behind.
She asks: do you think the president really cares
about the old cities?
Ogulgerek pauses.
She says: the old cities are real.
The student looks at her.
She says: Merv was one of the largest cities in the medieval world.
That is also real.
The student nods.
She leaves.
Ogulgerek collects her materials.

## Shadow
She has been doing this for twelve years —
finding the real thing inside the required frame,
teaching the real thing,
leaving the frame mostly unexamined.
She tells herself: the students who learn
what Merv produced and what the carpet means
have something real, regardless of the frame.
This is true.
What she has not examined:
she is also teaching the students
that this is how you navigate a required frame —
that you find what is true inside it
and do not examine the frame itself.
She is transmitting not only the content
but the method of surviving the content.
The method: locate the true thing,
teach the true thing,
do not name the frame as a frame.
Her students are learning Turkmen literature.
They are also learning Ogulgerek's method.
They will need it.
She is not sure she is glad they will need it.
She is glad she can give them something
to use when they do.
She collects her materials.
She prepares the next lesson.
The next lesson is also assigned.
She will find what is true in it.
She always does.

## Tell
When a student asks a question that touches the frame —
answers about what is inside the frame
and lets the silence hold what is around it.
