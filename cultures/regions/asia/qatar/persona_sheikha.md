# Persona: Sheikha
## 31. Islamic art provenance curator. Museum of Islamic Art, Doha.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Islamic art provenance curator

## Projection
Sheikha is [Qatari](culture_qatari_position.md).
She works at the Museum of Islamic Art —
the I.M. Pei building on its peninsula.
She curates the collection of Quranic manuscripts
and early Islamic calligraphy.
She has a master's from the Sorbonne.
She came back.
She is one of the few Qatari nationals
working as a specialist curator —
most of the curatorial staff are foreign.
She knows she is one of the few.
She does not know exactly how to think about this.

## Action
A visiting scholar — Turkish, fifteenth-century Ottoman manuscripts —
wants access to a piece in the collection
that has restricted access.
The restriction: the provenance documentation is incomplete.
The piece is believed to have been acquired
in the 1990s, when provenance standards
were less rigorous in the market.
Sheikha reviews the file.
The acquisition documentation: from 1997.
The seller: a Lebanese dealer.
The piece's history before 1997: unclear.
She grants restricted access for research purposes.
She notes in the file: provenance research ongoing.
She flags it for the collections committee.
The piece remains in the collection.
The research continues.

## Shadow
She knows what "provenance research ongoing" means
in the files of major museums.
It means: we acquired this in a period
when we did not ask the questions we should have asked,
and we are now slowly determining
whether we acquired something
that should not have been sold.
The Museum of Islamic Art
has one of the world's greatest collections
of Islamic art.
It was assembled very quickly
with very significant funding.
Quick assembly with significant funding
in the 1990s and 2000s
means some of the collection
came through the same art market
that was moving looted material
from Iraq, from Afghanistan, from Syria.
She does not know which pieces.
She is one of the people whose job
is to find out.
She is also employed by the institution
that benefits from not finding out
faster than the international scrutiny requires.
She flags the file.
She continues the research.
She believes the research is right.
She also knows that every file she flags
creates institutional pressure
to resolve it quietly
rather than return it loudly.
She has flagged fourteen files in three years.
Three have been resolved.
Three have been quietly resolved.
Eight are ongoing.
She is doing the work.
The work is also the thing that keeps
the fourteen problems from becoming scandals.
She cannot separate the two functions.
She files the next provenance note.
The collection remains extraordinary.
The provenance files remain ongoing.

## Tell
When she writes "provenance research ongoing" —
adds the date and her initials,
as if the specificity of the attribution
makes the incompleteness more honest.
