## Shown
- The Museum of Islamic Art. On its peninsula. I.M. Pei. The geometric facade. The collection: 1,400 years of Islamic art from three continents. The best-funded Islamic art museum in the world. The statement: we are the custodians of this civilisation's visual heritage.
- The Souq Waqif. The restored traditional market. The falconers, the spice merchants, the restaurants. Built in traditional Qatari style — actually reconstructed in the 2000s to look traditional. The authenticity is staged and also genuine: the falcon market is real.
- West Bay. The glass towers. The QatarEnergy headquarters. The skyline that did not exist in 1990. Built in thirty years. The argument from glass and steel.
- Lusail. The new city north of Doha. The stadium where the World Cup final was played. The city built around the stadium. The urban planning project that moved faster than any city has moved.
- Education City. The campuses of eight international universities. American and European institutions operating in Qatar. The Qatar Foundation's flagship. 45,000 students. The knowledge economy argument made in stone and wire.
