## Shown
- Ramallah. The de facto capital.
  The Mukata — the Palestinian Authority compound.
  Yasser Arafat's mausoleum within it.
  The Palestinian government
  that governs parts of the West Bank
  under Israeli military administration
  with negotiated civil authority.
  The city that has a stock exchange,
  a craft beer scene, a film festival,
  and Israeli checkpoints
  at the roads that leave it.
  The city that is building something
  inside the conditions that constrain everything.
  Both true.
- The Old City. Jerusalem.
  The Palestinian quarters — Muslim, Christian.
  The Damascus Gate.
  The markets.
  The families who have been here
  for more generations than the names they know.
  The city that is claimed by two nations
  and administered by one
  and whose east is occupied territory
  under international law
  and whose undivided sovereignty
  is claimed by the Israeli government.
  The three Abrahamic faiths
  in the same square kilometre.
  The prayer that sounds different
  from different minarets, towers, walls.
  The city that is always the city
  at the centre of the argument.
- The camps. Balata. Dheisheh. Shatila.
  Established 1948.
  Still operating.
  Now cities, in density and infrastructure —
  running water, electricity, clinics, schools.
  Still called camps
  because to call them cities
  would be to say the return is not coming.
  The camp is the political statement
  held in the building code.
  The families who have never known
  any other home
  but call it camp
  because their grandparents called it camp
  and the calling is the claim.
- Gaza.
  Before October 2023:
  the strip of land forty kilometres long,
  ten kilometres wide,
  2.3 million people,
  under blockade since 2007.
  The fishermen who could not fish
  beyond six nautical miles.
  The import restrictions on cement, on medicine.
  The electricity that ran four hours a day.
  After October 2023:
  the place where 40,000 people died
  and most of the buildings were destroyed
  and the world watched
  and disagreed about what to call it
  and the dying continued.
  The room shows what it must show:
  that Gaza was a place where people lived,
  and is a place where people are still living,
  and is also a place where the scale of destruction
  has no precedent in this century.

## Withheld
- Nothing.
  The Palestinian position does not have
  the luxury of the withheld.
  The suffering is public.
  The grief is public.
  The evidence is public.
  The argument about the evidence
  is also public.
  What the room withholds
  is not information.
  It is the interior:
  the exhaustion of explaining
  the same thing for seventy-seven years
  to a world that keeps requiring the explanation
  and keeps deferring the response.
  The interior is not for the room.
  The interior is what *sumud* protects.
