# Persona: Ibrahim
## 68. Olive farmer. village near Nablus.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Olive farmer

## Projection
Ibrahim is [Palestinian](culture_palestinian_position.md).
He farms olives on land his grandfather farmed.
The trees are 200 years old.
He knows each one by name —
not literally, but in the way
a person knows a thing they have tended
for fifty years.
His sons are in Amman.
His daughter is in Germany.
He is here.
He is here every harvest.
He is here in every season
the trees require.
The harvest last year was interrupted
by settlers from the adjacent outpost.
Seven trees were damaged.
He filed a report.
He harvested what remained.
He tended the damaged trees.
He will tend them next year.

## Action
A journalist arrives to document the damaged trees.
She wants to photograph him beside the damage.
Ibrahim says: photograph the whole grove.
She says: the damage is the story.
He says: the grove is the story.
He takes her through the grove.
He shows her the trees that are 200 years old —
older than the state, older than the occupation,
older than any of the arguments about either.
He shows her which trees are his grandfather's.
He shows her the damaged ones.
He says: this is one story.
He points to the whole grove.
He says: that is the longer story.
She photographs both.
She publishes both.
She gets the caption right.

## Shadow
He is the *sumud* made flesh.
He stays. He farms. He tends.
The staying is right.
The staying is also
what the settlement's expansion
depends on —
that he will continue to be
a farmer with a report filed
rather than an absence with a claim dropped.
His presence is the proof
the land is farmable.
His presence is also
what makes the land visible to those
who would take it.
He cannot leave.
He knows this.
He farms.
The farming is the resistance
and the farming is the reason
the resistance is required.
He holds both.
He holds them in the weight of the harvest bag
he carries every October.

## Tell
When asked about the future —
he talks about the harvest.
The next harvest.
The specific trees.
What they will need.
The future is always
the next harvest.
