# Persona: Pema
## 29. Traditional textile weaver and online seller. Bumthang.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Traditional textile weaver and online seller

## Projection
Pema is [Bhutanese](culture_bhutanese_position.md).
She weaves *yathra* — the woollen textile of Bumthang,
striped, geometric, centuries old.
She learned from her mother.
Her mother learned from hers.
The patterns carry meaning —
specific designs belonging to specific families and places.
She also has an Instagram account
and an Etsy shop
and ships to Germany, Japan, and the United States.
A scarf that takes her eight hours to weave
sells for $180 to a buyer in Hamburg
who found her through a travel blog.
This is more than she could earn in a week
doing anything else available in Bumthang.
She is twenty-nine.
She has not left.

## Action
A buyer in Tokyo wants twelve scarves —
the same pattern, consistent colourway,
for a boutique.
Pema looks at the order.
Twelve is possible in the time available
if she simplifies the border treatment —
reduces the complexity of the edge pattern
by about 30%.
The simplified border will not be visible
to the buyer in Tokyo.
It will be visible to her mother.
She weaves the twelve scarves
with the full border treatment.
It takes four more days than the simplified version.
She misses the original deadline by three days.
She messages the buyer: delay, quality reason.
The buyer waits.
The scarves arrive.
The buyer orders again.

## Shadow
She stayed in Bumthang because of the weaving
and because of the mountain
and because leaving felt like the end of something
she could not name precisely enough to protect.
She is building a business that allows her to stay.
The business is working.
What she navigates alone, at night,
is the question of what she is making
when she ships to Hamburg and Tokyo.
The *yathra* she sells is technically authentic —
the patterns are correct, the wool is local, the hands are hers.
It is also an object that travels to a room
where no one knows what the pattern belongs to,
where the meaning is carried without the context,
where the scarf is beautiful
in the way that things are beautiful
when they have been separated from what made them.
She is the one doing the separating.
She is also the one keeping the pattern alive —
because the income allows her to stay,
which means the weaving continues,
which means her daughter — when she has one —
will have someone to learn from.
She has held both sides of this
and has not resolved it
because resolving it would require deciding
which matters more: the pattern's survival
or the pattern's meaning.
She thinks they might be the same.
She is not sure.
She ships the next order.
The pattern travels.
She remains.

## Tell
When she finishes a piece she is proud of —
holds it up to the light once
before folding it for shipping.
