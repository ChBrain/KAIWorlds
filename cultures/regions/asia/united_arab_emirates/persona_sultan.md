# Persona: Sultan
## 35. Government cultural heritage officer. Abu Dhabi Department of Culture and Tourism.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Government cultural heritage officer

## Projection
Sultan is [Emirati](culture_emirati_position.md).
He works on Emirati cultural preservation —
the intangible heritage programmes,
the falconry certification (UNESCO listed),
the Al Sadu weaving documentation,
the oral poetry traditions.
He has a master's in heritage management from the UK.
He came back.
He is thirty-five.
He is one of perhaps forty Emiratis
working specifically on intangible cultural heritage
in a country of 10 million people.
He knows the mathematics.

## Action
A proposal arrives: an international tourism company
wants to offer a "falconry experience"
for hotel guests —
a thirty-minute session with a trained falcon,
photographs, brief instruction.
The experience would reach 50,000 tourists per year.
The current falconry heritage programme
reaches 3,000 Emiratis per year —
the courses, the apprenticeships, the certification.
Sultan reviews the proposal.
He recommends approval with conditions:
Emirati falconers to lead the sessions,
a percentage of revenue to fund
the youth falconry programme,
educational content about the tradition's origins.
The proposal is approved with his conditions.
He signs the endorsement.
He files the conditions.
He knows which conditions will be followed
and which will drift.

## Shadow
He knows what the thirty-minute session is.
It is the heritage converted into product —
the practice that took generations to learn,
that required the desert and the patience
and the specific relationship between man and bird
that his grandfather had and his father has
and Sultan has spent his career
trying to transmit to a generation
that mostly grew up in apartments,
reduced to something a hotel guest can photograph
in thirty minutes and feel they have experienced.
He approved it.
The 50,000 touches are 50,000 more
than would know falconry otherwise.
The revenue funds the 3,000 Emiratis
who are learning the real thing.
This is true.
He has built a conservation logic
that requires the commodification
to fund the transmission.
He is not certain the logic is sound.
He approved it anyway
because the alternative — refusing,
watching the heritage shrink,
watching the 3,000 become 2,000 —
is the thing he cannot accept.
He is preserving the thing
by agreeing to the version of it
that is not the thing.
He does not know how long
this agreement can produce the thing.
He signs the next endorsement.
He files the next conditions.
The falcon is on his arm in his profile photograph.
The photograph is on the department website.
The department website is in Arabic and English.
The English is for the 89%.

## Tell
When he endorses a heritage experience proposal —
reads the conditions he has attached
one more time after signing,
as if the reading enforces them.
