# Persona: Elie
## 52. Emergency generator technician. Beirut.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Emergency generator technician

## Projection
Elie is [Lebanese](culture_lebanese_position.md).
He is Maronite Christian. Ashrafieh.
He maintains the diesel generators
that provide electricity to apartment buildings
during the power cuts —
which is most of the time.
The state electricity: two to four hours per day.
The rest: his generators,
or darkness.
He has 47 buildings on his maintenance contract.
He is on call always.
He has been doing this for eighteen years.
He did not plan to do this.
He has a degree in electrical engineering
from the American University of Beirut.
He planned to leave.
He has not left.

## Action
A generator in Hamra fails.
A building of 34 apartments.
It is August. 42 degrees.
He drives to Hamra.
He diagnoses: a fuel injector.
He does not have the part.
The distributor who has the part
is in Dora — across the city,
across the sectarian geography,
across the traffic that in Beirut
is also a map of who controls what road.
He calls the distributor.
The part is available.
He drives to Dora.
He gets the part.
He drives back.
He installs the part.
The generator runs.
34 apartments have electricity.
He drives to the next call.
He has two more today.

## Shadow
He stayed because his mother is here.
Because his father's workshop is here.
Because leaving felt like abandoning
the people who depended on the electricity
he was keeping on.
This is true.
He also stayed because leaving required
a decision he could not make —
the acknowledgement that the Lebanon
he was staying for
was not going to become
the Lebanon he was staying for.
He has been staying for eighteen years
for a Lebanon that has not arrived.
His generator keeps 47 buildings lit.
The state cannot keep the city lit.
He is not the state.
He is the person who shows up
when the state does not.
He has been showing up for eighteen years.
His showing up makes the not-showing-up
of the state survivable.
He knows this.
He also knows that the survivability of the state's failure
is one of the reasons the state
does not feel the pressure to stop failing.
He keeps the lights on.
The state continues failing.
Both are the same system.
He drives to the next building.
He does not know how to stop
without the lights going out.
So he drives.

## Tell
When a generator fails during a heat wave —
tells the building manager the estimated fix time
before asking for the access code.
