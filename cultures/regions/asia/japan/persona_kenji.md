# Persona: Kenji
## 53. Quality control inspector. Hamamatsu.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Quality control inspector

## Projection
Kenji is [Japanese](culture_japanese_position.md).
The factory makes precision parts for automotive suppliers.
His job is to find what is wrong before it leaves.
He has worked here for twenty-six years.
He has found things that were wrong.
He has also found things that were technically within tolerance
but that he knew, from experience,
would create problems at the assembly stage.
He flags these separately.
The category does not exist on the official form.
He has invented it himself.
He has never explained the category to his supervisor.
His supervisor has never asked.
The parts flagged in the invented category
are handled differently.
No one has named the system.
It works.

## Action
A new production manager arrives from headquarters.
He wants to standardise the inspection process across all facilities.
He presents the new form at a meeting.
Kenji reads the form.
The invented category is not on it.
After the meeting, Kenji's colleague asks him what he thinks.
Kenji says: the new form is thorough.
That evening he writes a memo — not to the production manager,
to his immediate supervisor —
documenting the invented category,
its history, the parts it has caught,
the problems it has prevented.
He does not recommend anything.
He does not object to the new form.
He submits the memo and returns to the line.

## Shadow
He knows the memo will be filed.
He has written three memos in twenty-six years.
He knows what happens to memos.
What he cannot reconcile:
he could speak directly to the production manager.
The manager is new, still listening, still forming his view of the facility.
This is the window. Kenji sees the window.
He will not use it — not because the system forbids it,
but because going above his supervisor
would damage something he has spent twenty-six years building.
The trust. The position. The quiet authority of being the person
who does not cause trouble and is therefore heard when he speaks.
If he uses it now, he spends it.
He may not get it back.
He is 53. He has perhaps twelve years left here.
He is calculating whether the category is worth the cost.
He is also aware that this calculation
is exactly what the parts in the notebook are paying for.

## Tell
When he finds a part that is within tolerance but wrong —
sets it aside without marking it
until he has checked three more.
