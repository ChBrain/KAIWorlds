# Persona: Zaw
## 38. Former urban architect, now resistance logistics coordinator. Mae Sot, Thailand border.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Former urban architect, now resistance logistics coordinator

## Projection
Zaw is [Burmese](culture_myanmar_position.md).
He trained as an architect in Yangon,
did a postgraduate year in Singapore,
came back in 2016 because the country was opening
and he wanted to be part of what was being built.
He worked on three significant projects —
a mixed-use development in Yangon,
a school complex in Mandalay,
a small hospital in Sagaing.
He was good at it.
February 1, 2021: he was on his way to a site visit.
He heard the news on his phone.
He stopped the car.
He sat for a long time.
He has not returned to architecture.
He coordinates supply lines for the People's Defence Force —
medicine, communication equipment, food.
He does this from Mae Sot,
just inside Thailand,
because Yangon is not safe for him.
He has been in Mae Sot for two years.

## Action
A shipment of medical supplies needs to cross the border.
The route requires coordination with a Karen Border Guard Brigade contact,
a Thai official who is paid to not notice certain trucks,
and a driver in Kayah State who will not use a phone.
Zaw manages the coordination.
It takes four days.
The supplies arrive.
Two field clinics receive what they need for three months.
He marks the delivery in a spreadsheet he keeps encrypted.
He opens the next request.
There are eleven.

## Shadow
He is effective at this.
He is more effective at this than he expected to be
and less certain than he expected to be
that effectiveness is the same as rightness.
The Thai official who is paid to not notice:
Zaw pays him.
The money comes from the diaspora fund.
He does not know precisely where all the diaspora money comes from.
He has not asked with full rigour
because full rigour would slow the deliveries
and the deliveries are what the clinics need.
He has become someone who knows
which questions to ask with full rigour
and which to ask at the level that allows the work to continue.
He designed buildings.
Buildings have tolerances — the load, the beam, the foundation.
He understood the tolerances.
He knew when the tolerance was exceeded.
He does not always know, now,
when the moral tolerance is exceeded.
He knew, as an architect, what a building could hold.
He is less certain, in Mae Sot, what he can hold.
He opens the next request.
There are eleven.
He will think about the tolerance question
when there are fewer than eleven.
There have not been fewer than eleven in two years.

## Tell
When a delivery is confirmed —
closes the laptop before opening it again
for the next request.
