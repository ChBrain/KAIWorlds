# Piece: The 2021 Coup
## February 1, 2021. The morning the military took back what it had never fully surrendered.

## Owner
- **Place:** Naypyidaw / Yangon

## Place
Naypyidaw. The capital built by the junta in the jungle,
unveiled in 2006.
Eight-lane highways with almost no traffic.
A parliament building surrounded by empty boulevards.
The Tatmadaw built a capital for a country it controlled
and located it where surveillance was total
and escape was difficult.
Yangon — the old capital, the real city —
is where the resistance gathered.

## Load Bearing
Remove the 2021 coup and Myanmar is a fragile democracy
navigating a difficult transition
with an unresolved ethnic conflict and an army
that retained significant constitutional power.
Difficult but open.
With it: Myanmar is a country that discovered
the transition was not a transition —
that the 2008 constitution, written by the military,
had preserved the military's position
regardless of who won the election,
and that the election result was revoked
when the military decided to revoke it.
The piece is load-bearing not because coups are unusual in Myanmar —
they are not — but because this coup came after hope.
The generation that voted in 2015 and 2020
had grown up believing the opening was real.
The 2021 coup is not the same as the 1962 coup or the 1988 coup.
It is the coup that happened to people
who thought they had moved past coups.
That is the wound the piece carries.
Remove it: a military government among others.
With it: the specific grief of a generation
that was shown something real
and had it taken back.

## Apparent
1948: independence from Britain.
1962: General Ne Win's coup. The Burmese Way to Socialism.
The country closed. The economy destroyed over thirty years.
1988: mass pro-democracy protests. The 8888 Uprising.
The military massacres the protesters. Thousands dead.
SLORC — the State Law and Order Restoration Council — takes power.
Aung San Suu Kyi placed under house arrest.
1990: elections held. The NLD wins 80% of seats.
The military refuses to recognise the result.
2007: the Saffron Revolution. Monks lead protests.
The military suppresses it.
2010: elections. The NLD boycotts.
2011: quasi-civilian government. Thein Sein.
The opening begins.
Aung San Suu Kyi released.
2015: the NLD wins a landslide.
2020: the NLD wins again. An even larger landslide.
February 1, 2021, 3am: the military arrests Aung San Suu Kyi
and the elected government.
The coup announced at dawn.
The civil disobedience movement begins within days.
Medical workers, teachers, railway workers, civil servants — they stop.
The Tatmadaw responds with live ammunition.
By 2024: over 50,000 civilians killed or displaced.
The resistance controls significant territory.
The junta controls the cities — partially, at cost.
The country is at war with itself.
The war has no announced end.
