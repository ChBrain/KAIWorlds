# Piece: The Pearl Roundabout and What Followed
## February–March 2011. The protests that filled the roundabout — and the crackdown that cleared it.

## Owner
- **Place:** Manama

## Place
Manama. The Pearl Roundabout — Lulu Roundabout.
Named for the pearl sculpture at its centre —
six arcing supports holding a giant pearl above the traffic.
In February 2011, protesters filled it.
Tens of thousands. Shia and Sunni together.
On March 16, 2011, Saudi and UAE forces crossed the causeway.
The Bahraini security forces cleared the roundabout.
The pearl monument was demolished.
The roundabout was renamed.
The sculpture: gone.
The roundabout: still a traffic circle.
The memory: still present.

## Load Bearing
Remove the 2011 uprising and Bahrain is a small Gulf monarchy
managing a sectarian division
with oil revenues declining
and a US naval base providing security.
With it: Bahrain is the Gulf state
where the Arab Spring produced
the most sustained challenge to a ruling dynasty —
where the Shia majority,
joined by Sunni liberals and opposition figures,
occupied the central roundabout
for a month
and demanded constitutional reform,
an elected government, a new constitution —
and where the crackdown came
with Saudi troops across the causeway
and the emergency law
and the trials of doctors who treated protesters
and the dismissal of thousands of workers
who participated in the strikes
and the demolition of the pearl monument
so that the image would not persist.
Remove the piece: a managed political tension.
With it: a managed political tension
that became a test case for how Gulf monarchies
respond to the Arab Spring
and established the template:
call it an Iranian-backed sectarian plot,
bring in the Peninsula Shield Force,
suppress with enough force to hold,
release some prisoners under international pressure,
begin a national dialogue that produces
a modified status quo,
continue developing the economy,
never hold the free elections that were demanded.
The template held.
It was used.
The pearl is gone.
The tension it stood above: still present.

## Apparent
1783: the Al Khalifa family takes Bahrain from the Persians.
1820: British protection.
1932: oil discovered. First in the Gulf.
1971: independence.
1994–1999: the first Bahraini intifada. Shia uprising. Suppressed.
2001: the National Action Charter. Referendum. King Hamad promises reform.
2002: the constitution: the promised elected legislature balanced by an appointed upper house.
The opposition: the elected chamber is effectively powerless.
February 14, 2011: the Day of Rage begins.
The Pearl Roundabout filled.
February 17: security forces clear the camp. Deaths.
February 18: protesters return in larger numbers.
February 19: the army withdraws. The roundabout: reoccupied.
March 14–15: Saudi and UAE forces enter Bahrain.
March 16: the crackdown. The state of emergency.
The roundabout cleared. The monument demolished.
The trials: 47 medical personnel tried for treating protesters.
Convictions: later overturned under international pressure.
Thousands dismissed from jobs. Hundreds imprisoned.
2011–2024: the political situation: frozen at the post-crackdown settlement.
Opposition leaders: imprisoned, exiled, stripped of citizenship.
The economic diversification: ongoing.
The Formula 1 Grand Prix: ongoing.
The Fifth Fleet: ongoing.
The political prisoners: ongoing.
