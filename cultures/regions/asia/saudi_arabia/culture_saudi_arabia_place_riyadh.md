## Shown
- The Masmak Fortress. Mud brick. The spear in the door. The beginning of the kingdom, preserved in the centre of a city that has been rebuilt around it. The heritage site surrounded by the glass towers.
- King Fahd Road. The spine of modern Riyadh. Eight lanes. The towers on both sides. The Kingdom Tower at the northern end. The Faisaliah Tower. The skyline that did not exist forty years ago.
- Diriyah. The original home of the Al Saud family. Being rebuilt as a heritage and tourism destination — mud-brick palaces, the site of the 1744 pact, restaurants, a hotel. The founding moment of the kingdom being converted into an experience economy. The conversion is deliberate and is happening fast.
- The Boulevard Riyadh City. The new entertainment district. Restaurants, cinemas, live music venues, mixed-gender spaces. The surface that is changing. Open on a Friday evening: couples, families, young men and women in the same space, something that was not possible here five years ago.
- The mosques. Everywhere. The call to prayer five times. The city that organises itself around the prayer and is simultaneously building entertainment venues that operate between the prayers. Both are the city. Neither is cancelling the other yet.
