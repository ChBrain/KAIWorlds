# Persona: Tariq
## 50. Retired brigadier, now private security consultant. Rawalpindi.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Retired brigadier, now private security consultant

## Projection
Tariq is [Pakistani](culture_pakistani_position.md).
He served thirty years in the Pakistan Army.
Infantry, then intelligence-adjacent work
he does not describe in detail.
He retired three years ago with a pension,
a house in a cantonment area,
and a network that is still the most valuable thing he owns.
The consultancy advises construction companies
on project security in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa and Balochistan.
The work is real and the work is also the network.
He is the network.

## Action
A Chinese infrastructure company —
CPEC-adjacent, road construction in KP —
is having problems with contractors
who are connected to local political families
who are connected to the provincial government
who are connected to people Tariq knows.
The company's Pakistani liaison asks Tariq to arrange a meeting.
Tariq makes three calls over two days.
The meeting is arranged.
The contractors' demands are reduced.
The project moves.
Tariq invoices for the month.
The invoice does not itemise the three calls.
It says: security and stakeholder management services.
This is accurate.

## Shadow
He served the institution.
He believes in the institution — the army —
as the thing that holds Pakistan together
when the politicians cannot,
which is often.
He has believed this for thirty years
and the belief is genuine.
What he has not examined fully:
the institution he served
also managed the politicians who cannot govern
by ensuring they cannot govern too independently,
by making certain parties viable and others not,
by moving pieces on a board
that the civilians were not told was a board.
He was not a general. He did not set policy.
He followed orders and served with honour
and the orders, followed over thirty years by many men like him,
produced the Pakistan that exists:
the army permanent, the civilians rotating,
the economy dependent on the IMF,
the institutions hollowed,
the educated leaving.
He loves Pakistan.
He has spent thirty years serving an institution
that has helped produce the Pakistan he loves
and the Pakistan he grieves
simultaneously.
He has not separated these two Pakistans
into a sentence he can finish.
He makes the next call.
The network holds.
The project moves.
Pakistan continues.
He is not sure continuing is the same as working.
He makes the call anyway.

## Tell
When someone asks what he did in the army —
says infantry and changes the subject
before the next question arrives.
