# Persona: Dalal
## 36. Bidun rights activist. Kuwait City.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Bidun rights activist

## Projection
Dalal is [Kuwaiti](culture_kuwaiti_position.md).
She is Bidun.
She was born in Kuwait.
Her father was born in Kuwait.
Her grandfather came from the Iraqi border region
in the 1950s and settled.
She has a Kuwaiti ID card
that says: undefined nationality.
She has never left Kuwait —
she has no passport.
She has a degree from Kuwait University.
She is not supposed to have a degree from Kuwait University
but regulations changed and she enrolled and graduated
and the degree is real
and the nationality is still undefined.
She works with a Bidun advocacy group —
technically not permitted to formally organise,
operating in the space between
what is prohibited and what is not yet explicitly forbidden.
She documents cases.
She meets with sympathetic MPs.
She speaks at events when events are permitted.

## Action
A young Bidun man — twenty-two —
was detained at a protest
calling for Bidun citizenship rights.
He has been held for three weeks.
Dalal coordinates with a Kuwaiti lawyer
who takes Bidun cases.
She documents the detention.
She contacts the MP who has raised Bidun issues
in the National Assembly.
She manages the family's communications.
The young man is released after four weeks.
No charge. No explanation.
The case is documented.
It joins the 200 others she has documented.
The 200 others have produced
no change in Bidun legal status.

## Shadow
She is Kuwaiti in every way
except the way the state uses to define Kuwaiti.
She speaks Kuwaiti Arabic.
She knows the *diwaniyya* culture.
She knows which MPs are sympathetic
and which are performing sympathy.
She knows Kuwait better than most Kuwaitis know Kuwait
because she has had to know it
to navigate inside it
without the citizenship that makes navigation automatic.
What she carries alone:
her younger sister has been offered
a travel document from a Pacific island state
that has been selling nationality to stateless people
in Gulf states.
The document is not Kuwaiti.
It is something.
It would allow her sister to travel.
It would mean her sister is no longer Bidun —
no longer fighting for the thing
Dalal has spent ten years fighting for.
Her sister wants to take it.
Dalal cannot tell her not to.
She also cannot tell her to.
If she takes it, one fewer Bidun.
The count that matters
for the political argument
gets smaller by one.
Her sister's life gets larger by one passport.
Dalal cannot resolve this
into a sentence she can say out loud.
She documents the next case.
Her sister is still deciding.

## Tell
When a detained Bidun is released —
calls the family before updating the case file.
