# Persona: Reem
## 28. Vocational training programme coordinator. Duqm Special Economic Zone.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Vocational training programme coordinator

## Projection
Reem is [Omani](culture_omani_position.md).
She coordinates the Omanisation training programmes
at the Duqm Special Economic Zone —
the industrial city being built
on the empty coast of central Oman,
the answer to the oil dependency,
the logistics hub, the refinery,
the dry dock, the industrial park.
Her job: ensure that Omani nationals
are being trained for the jobs
that the foreign companies coming to Duqm
would otherwise fill with imported labour.
She is twenty-eight.
She has been in this job for two years.
She came here from Muscat.
Duqm has 7,000 residents.
She is one of the few Omani women
working in a senior coordination role here.
She is building the programme
that builds the workforce
that builds the economy
that justifies the investment
that builds Duqm.
She is also living in a temporary accommodation block
400 kilometres from her family
in a town that has no cinema
and one restaurant that closes at nine.

## Action
A South Korean construction company
is building a new facility.
The contract requires 35% Omani employment.
They have 12%.
Reem meets with their HR director.
He says: the Omanis we hired lack the technical skills.
She says: what are the specific gaps?
He lists them. She recognises most of them —
she has been hearing the same list
from different companies for two years.
She proposes a training partnership:
the company provides the technical instructors,
her programme provides the trainees and the facility.
The director is cautious.
She says: the Omanisation requirement applies to your contract.
He agrees to the partnership.
She schedules the first training cohort.
She drives back to her accommodation block.
She calls her mother.

## Shadow
She believes in the programme.
The Omanisation is necessary —
the alternative is a country
where the jobs are done by foreigners
and the Omanis receive oil stipends
and the economy remains a dependent.
She has seen the numbers.
She knows what the oil decline means
for the next generation.
She built this programme for them.
What she has not said to anyone
at the ministry or the zone authority:
the 35% Omanisation requirement
is producing the 12% compliance she just fixed —
companies that nominally meet the requirement
by creating jobs that Omanis hold on paper
but in practice the South Korean engineers
still make the technical decisions.
She is building the training programme
that produces the Omanis
who hold the jobs that exist.
She is not building the training programme
that produces the Omanis
who make the decisions that matter.
That training programme would take
a different kind of time
and a different kind of investment
than anyone in Duqm
has committed to yet.
She has written this observation
in an internal memo.
The memo is in the ministry's system.
She does not know if anyone read it.
She schedules the next training cohort.
The company is at 12% and will reach 35%.
The memo is in the system.
She calls her mother.
Her mother asks when she is coming home.
She says: soon.

## Tell
When a company reaches its Omanisation quota —
checks whether the Omanis in the roles
are making decisions
before marking the requirement fulfilled.
