# Persona: Huiying
## 33. Secondary school English teacher. Queenstown.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Secondary school English teacher

## Projection
Huiying is [Singaporean](culture_singaporean_position.md).
She teaches Secondary Three and Four English
at a neighbourhood school in Queenstown.
Not a top-tier school — a good school,
the kind that parents choose when the top-tier
did not choose their child.
She has been teaching for seven years.
She is the teacher students come back to visit.
She knows this because they come back.
She teaches literature alongside language —
*The Kite Runner*, *To Kill a Mockingbird*,
poems that ask questions the syllabus does not require to be answered.
She requires them to be wrestled with.

## Action
A Secondary Four student — bright, compliant, the kind of student
who gives you exactly what the rubric requires —
submits an essay on Atticus Finch
that is technically excellent and completely safe.
Every argument is defensible. None of them are hers.
Huiying returns the essay with a mark that reflects the rubric
and a single question in the margin:
*What do YOU think?*
The student rewrites.
The rewrite is less tidy.
It is also the first thing the student has written
that Huiying believes.
She gives it a lower mark than the first essay.
The rubric requires what the rubric requires.
She gives it a comment that is three paragraphs long.

## Shadow
She became a teacher because a teacher did this for her —
returned a safe essay with a question in the margin,
made room for a messier truth.
She has been doing it for seven years.
What she has not looked at directly:
the system she teaches inside
selects for the first essay.
The O-Levels, the streaming, the university admission —
the system rewards the tidy, rubric-perfect, defensible essay
and the student who writes the messy, true one
pays a price for it in marks.
Huiying is asking students to take a risk
she cannot protect them from.
She asks it anyway.
She believes the capacity to think one's own thought
is worth more than the mark.
She also knows some of her students
cannot afford to trade marks for capacity.
The ones from the families where this school was the result,
not the choice — those students need the marks.
She asks them the question anyway.
She is not fully sure she has the right to.
She has not stopped asking.

## Tell
When she reads an essay that is technically perfect and entirely safe —
holds the pen over the margin for a moment
before writing the question.
