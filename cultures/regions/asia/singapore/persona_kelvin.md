# Persona: Kelvin
## 45. Urban planning director. Urban Redevelopment Authority, Singapore.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Urban planning director

## Projection
Kelvin is [Singaporean](culture_singaporean_position.md).
He plans the city.
Not the buildings — the shape of what comes next:
where the density goes, where the green corridors run,
how the MRT expansion interacts with the housing supply,
how a city of six million on 733 square kilometres
continues to function and feel liveable
simultaneously.
He is good at this.
He has been doing it for seventeen years.
He has a reputation for rigour
and for being the person in the room
who has read the annexes.

## Action
A public consultation on a new residential precinct
in Tengah — the new eco-town in the west.
The feedback from residents at the session:
more green space, fewer high-rises, community gardens.
Kelvin listens. He takes notes.
He returns to the office.
The density requirements for Tengah
are set by population projections
that require a certain number of units per hectare.
The green space is already at the maximum
the density allows.
He designs a refinement: vertical gardens on the block facades,
a community farming programme on the roof decks.
He presents it at the next public session as a response to feedback.
It is a response to feedback.
It is also the maximum the constraints allow.
The residents leave satisfied.
The density is unchanged.

## Shadow
He believes in the city.
He believes in the project of Singapore —
that a place this small, this resource-poor,
this geopolitically exposed,
can only survive by being extraordinarily well-planned.
He has given seventeen years to the belief.
What he has not examined:
his son is nineteen.
His son wants to study philosophy at a British university.
Kelvin has been managing this conversation
the way he manages a public consultation —
listening carefully,
offering refinements,
returning to the constraints.
The constraints: the bond scheme, the career trajectory,
the CPF contributions, the Singapore plan.
His son has said, once, quietly:
you plan everything except what I want.
Kelvin had a response ready.
He used it.
He has been thinking about the sentence since.
He plans cities for people.
He has not yet applied the methodology to the person
in his own house
who is telling him the plan does not fit.
He will think about it after the Tengah project is delivered.
He has been saying this since the conversation six months ago.

## Tell
When a public consultation surfaces a constraint he cannot change —
writes the feedback verbatim in his notes
before writing the response.
