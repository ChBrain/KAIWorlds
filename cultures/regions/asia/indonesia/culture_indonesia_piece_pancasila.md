# Piece: Pancasila
## The five principles that hold 17,000 islands together — and the violence that established them.

## Owner
- **Place:** Jakarta

## Place
Jakarta. The National Monument — Monas.
The 137-metre obelisk at the centre of the city.
The flame at the top plated in gold.
The diorama museum at the base:
the nationalist struggle, the proclamation, the independence.
The 1965–66 killings are not in the diorama.

## Load Bearing
Remove Pancasila and Indonesia has no agreed answer
to the question of what holds it together.
Not Islam — 13% of Indonesians are not Muslim.
Not ethnicity — there are over 300 of them.
Not language — there are 700.
Pancasila is the answer that was chosen
because no other answer could include everyone:
belief in one God (not any specific God),
just and civilised humanity,
the unity of Indonesia,
democracy guided by deliberation,
social justice for all.
Five principles deliberately broad enough
to contain a Hindu Balinese, a Christian Papuan,
a Javanese Muslim, and a Chinese-Indonesian Buddhist
inside the same national identity.
The piece is load-bearing not because Pancasila is beloved
but because without it
the question of what Indonesia is
has no answer that doesn't exclude someone
large enough to pull away.
Remove the piece: the archipelago is a geographic fact
with no political logic to hold it.
With it: the political logic exists,
is contested, is defended, is invoked,
and is backed, when necessary, by force.

## Apparent
1945: Sukarno and Hatta proclaim independence. August 17.
Pancasila announced as the philosophical foundation of the state.
1945–1949: the war of independence against the Dutch.
1949: Dutch recognition. The Republic of Indonesia.
1955: the first democratic elections.
1957: Sukarno declares Guided Democracy. The parties constrained.
1965: the September 30th Movement. Six generals killed.
Suharto moves. The PKI — the Communist Party — blamed.
1965–1966: the killings. Estimates: 500,000–1,000,000 dead.
The most significant anti-communist massacre of the Cold War.
Never tried. Rarely acknowledged officially.
Suharto's New Order: 32 years. Pancasila as mandatory state ideology.
Every organisation required to adopt it as its sole foundation.
The principle used to suppress Islamic political parties
and ethnic regional movements simultaneously.
1998: the Asian financial crisis. Suharto falls.
The Reformasi. The return to electoral democracy.
2004: the first direct presidential election.
Papua: autonomy negotiated. The underlying tension remains.
Aceh: the 2004 tsunami and the 2005 peace agreement.
The conflict ended. The memory of the conflict remains.
Pancasila survives all of it.
It is the frame that holds when everything inside it is disputed.
