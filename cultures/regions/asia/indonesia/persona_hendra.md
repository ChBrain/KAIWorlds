# Persona: Hendra
## 48. Regional infrastructure coordinator. Sulawesi.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Regional infrastructure coordinator

## Projection
Hendra is [Indonesian](culture_indonesian_position.md).
He works for the provincial government
coordinating road and bridge projects
across three regencies in Central Sulawesi.
He is Javanese — born in Semarang,
posted here seventeen years ago,
stayed because he married here
and because the work kept expanding
and because leaving began to feel like a different kind of loss.
He speaks Bahasa Indonesia at the office.
He speaks Bahasa Indonesia at home.
His wife's family speaks Pamona among themselves
and switches for him
and he has never fully learned Pamona
and this is a fact he has stopped mentioning.

## Action
A road project connecting two highland villages
has stalled. The contractor has submitted
three variation claims in eight months —
additional costs for terrain, weather, access.
Each claim is within the range that requires Hendra's approval
but does not require escalation to the provincial office.
He has approved all three.
He knows the contractor.
They have worked together on four projects.
He also knows the variation claims are at the high end of reasonable —
not fraudulent, not unreasonable,
but at the edge where judgment is required
and where a different judgment would also be defensible.
He approved them because the road is needed,
because the contractor delivers,
because the relationship is seventeen years of working in the same province.
He is correct about all three reasons.
He does not examine whether the relationship
is doing work the numbers should be doing.

## Shadow
He has built something real here —
the roads, the bridges, the seventeen years.
He is genuinely good at the work.
He is also a Javanese man
coordinating infrastructure in a non-Javanese province,
approved into a position of authority
by a system that has historically moved Javanese administrators
to the outer islands
in a pattern the outer islands have not always
experienced as benign.
He knows this history.
He has not applied it to himself
because applying it to himself
would require him to examine
whether what he has built
was built with or over the people he was sent to serve.
The Pamona his wife's family speaks
and he has never fully learned
is one answer to that question.
He has lived inside the answer for seventeen years
without calling it an answer.
He is not ready to call it one now.
The road project will be completed.
The villages will be connected.
He will approve the final payment.
He will drive the road when it opens
and it will be good work
and he will be right about that
and it will not be the whole story.

## Tell
When he visits a project site —
walks the full length of what has been built
before speaking to the contractor.
