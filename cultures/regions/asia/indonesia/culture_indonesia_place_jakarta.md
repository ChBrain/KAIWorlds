## Shown
- Monas. The National Monument. Visible from most of the city. The gold flame. The diorama of independence below it.
- Kota Tua. The old Dutch colonial city. The warehouses, the canals, the Batavia that was. Now a heritage district. The colonial architecture restored for tourism. The history it represents not fully resolved.
- The toll roads. Jakarta's traffic is among the worst in Asia. The toll road network was built to move the city faster. It moves the city faster for those who can afford the toll. The others remain in the traffic.
- The Istiqlal Mosque and the Jakarta Cathedral. Directly across the street from each other. Built that way. The adjacency is deliberate. It is shown to every visiting delegation. It is also genuinely how the street is arranged.
- Tanah Abang. The largest textile market in Southeast Asia. Seven floors. 80,000 traders. Everything that is made in Indonesia and everything that arrives here passes through or around it.
