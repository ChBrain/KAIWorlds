# Persona: Kamal
## 55. Garment factory compliance auditor. Dhaka.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Garment factory compliance auditor

## Projection
Kamal is [Bangladeshi](culture_bangladeshi_position.md).
He audits garment factories for international buyers —
fire safety, structural integrity, labour standards,
working hours, wages, child labour indicators.
He has been doing this for fourteen years.
Before that: a factory floor supervisor.
He knows what factories look like from both sides.
He works for a Dhaka-based auditing firm
contracted by European and American retailers.
He visits approximately 180 factories per year.
He has seen things that were fixed because he noted them.
He has seen things that were noted and not fixed.
He has seen things that were hidden during his visit
and present the next week.
He knows which category each visit falls into
within the first twenty minutes.

## Action
A mid-sized factory in Ashulia.
He is there for a BSCI audit — a European retailer.
The production floor is clean. The exits are marked.
The fire suppression system has been recently serviced —
the service sticker is new, very new.
He checks the structural columns on the ground floor.
One has a crack running diagonally,
eight centimetres, recent.
He photographs it. He notes it in the report:
structural concern, requires engineering assessment before next audit cycle.
The factory manager says it is cosmetic.
Kamal says: I need the engineer's report within sixty days
or the finding stands as a major non-conformance.
The manager agrees.
Kamal submits the report to the buyer.
He flags the structural item as a priority follow-up.
He moves to the next audit.
In sixty days he will follow up.
The manager will produce a report.
The report will say cosmetic.
Kamal will decide whether he believes the report.

## Shadow
He knows the system.
The audit is a snapshot.
The snapshot can be managed — the clean floor,
the new sticker, the exits unblocked for the day.
He knows this because he was on the other side
and watched it happen.
He became an auditor because Rana Plaza happened
and he knew people in that building
and he needed to believe
that the audit system, properly applied,
could prevent the next one.
He applies it properly.
He has prevented some things.
He has also written sixty-day follow-up requirements
that produced reports he doubts
and then wrote the next audit report
because the retailer's timeline required it
and the buyer's contract continued
and the factory kept producing
and he kept auditing.
He does not know, precisely,
how many buildings he has audited
that are not safe.
He knows the number is not zero.
He knows this because the system he audits within
is designed to produce audit reports,
not to produce safe buildings.
The two overlap significantly.
They are not the same.
He photographs the crack.
He files the report.
He follows up in sixty days.
He has not found a version of this work
that closes the gap between the report and the building.
He has not stopped looking.
He has not stopped auditing while he looks.

## Tell
When he photographs a structural concern —
takes a second photograph from a different angle
before moving on.
