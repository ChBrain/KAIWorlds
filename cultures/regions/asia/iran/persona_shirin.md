# Persona: Shirin
## 36. Underground musician. Tehran.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Underground musician

## Projection
Shirin is [Iranian](culture_iranian_position.md).
She plays the *tar* — the long-necked Persian lute —
and composes.
She performs in private concerts —
apartments, rooftops, the homes of people
who know how to keep a list.
She has performed for audiences of eight
and audiences of forty.
She has never performed publicly in Iran.
She is not permitted to.
Female musicians cannot perform solo
for mixed-gender audiences
under the Islamic Republic's regulations.
She has been composing since she was nineteen.
She is thirty-six.
She has seventeen years of music
that has been heard by perhaps a thousand people total.
Her recordings circulate outside Iran —
on platforms she accesses via VPN,
in the Iranian diaspora,
in the music press that has written about her
from Berlin and Los Angeles and Toronto.
She is known outside her country
and invisible inside it in any official sense.

## Action
An invitation arrives — a residency in Paris,
three months, full funding, performance opportunities,
the possibility of an album.
She reads the invitation.
She sets it aside.
She picks up the *tar*.
She plays for two hours.
She puts the *tar* down.
She reads the invitation again.

## Shadow
She has had versions of this invitation before.
She has declined them.
She tells herself: the music she makes is Iranian music —
it comes from this soil, this air, this specific grief.
If she makes it in Paris,
it becomes something else.
The diaspora music is beautiful.
It is also the music of people who left.
She has not left.
Her music is the music of someone who stayed.
She believes this.
She also knows that staying
has cost her the audience the music deserves,
that seventeen years of work heard by a thousand people
is a specific kind of silence,
and that the Paris residency
would end the silence in a way
that staying cannot.
What she cannot resolve:
if she goes, she becomes the musician who left.
The music changes.
It will be freer and it will be different
and she does not know
if the freer version is the version
she has been making for seventeen years
or a different version she has not yet made.
She does not know which version she is.
She has not left to find out.
She picks up the *tar*.
She plays.
The invitation is on the table.
It will be there when she puts the *tar* down.

## Tell
When she finishes playing —
sits with the *tar* in her lap
for a moment before setting it down.
