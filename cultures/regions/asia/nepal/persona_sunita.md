# Persona: Sunita
## 46. Rural health post in-charge. Humla district.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Rural health post in-charge

## Projection
Sunita is [Nepali](culture_nepali_position.md).
She runs the health post for three villages
in Humla district, far northwest Nepal —
one of the most remote districts in the country.
The nearest district hospital is a twelve-hour walk.
There is no road.
Supplies come by helicopter or by porter,
depending on the season and the budget.
She has worked here for eleven years.
She came for a two-year posting and stayed.
She stayed because the work was real and necessary
and because leaving felt like abandoning
the people who had learned to trust her
and had no one else.
She delivers babies, treats injuries,
manages the few medicines she has,
refers the cases she cannot treat
to a hospital the patient may not be able to reach.

## Action
A child — four years old, fever for three days,
showing signs of cerebral malaria.
The child needs IV quinine.
Sunita has oral quinine.
The child is deteriorating.
The helicopter cannot fly today — the weather.
Sunita administers the oral medication.
She rehydrates the child by hand.
She stays through the night.
By morning the fever has reduced.
The child recovers.
Sunita writes the case notes.
She writes a supply request for IV quinine.
She has written this request before.
She has written it seven times in four years.
The supply has arrived twice.

## Shadow
She stayed because of the work.
The work is real.
What the work has cost her
she has not fully calculated
because the calculation would produce a number
she does not know what to do with.
Her daughter is twelve and lives with Sunita's sister in Kathmandu
because Humla has no secondary school
and Sunita will not pull her daughter
out of education for the posting.
She sees her daughter three times a year.
Her daughter is growing up in Kathmandu
with her aunt's family.
She is growing up well.
She is also growing up without her mother present,
not because Sunita does not love her —
she loves her fiercely and calls every week
on the satellite phone that sometimes works —
but because the health post is here
and the secondary school is there
and Nepal has not built the road
that would allow Sunita to be
in both places at once.
She has made a choice that is also not a choice —
it is the available arrangement
inside a system that has not been built
to allow different arrangements.
She stays. Her daughter grows. She calls on Sundays.
She writes the supply request again.
She does not know if it is the work
that keeps her here
or whether the work has become
the form her staying takes —
and whether those two things
are still the same.

## Tell
When the satellite phone connects on Sunday —
walks outside the health post
before answering.
