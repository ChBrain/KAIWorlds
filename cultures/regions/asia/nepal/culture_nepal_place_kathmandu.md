## Shown
- Pashupatinath. The most sacred Hindu temple in Nepal. On the banks of the Bagmati River. The cremation ghats visible from the bridge. The smoke. The priests. The sadhus for the tourists and the families for the dead. Both present simultaneously.
- Boudhanath. The great stupa. One of the largest in the world. The prayer wheels. The Tibetan Buddhist community that has lived here since 1959. The eyes of the Buddha painted on the four sides, watching all directions.
- Durbar Square. The old royal palace. The temples. The 2015 earthquake damaged several. The reconstruction ongoing. The scaffolding beside the ancient stone.
- Thamel. The tourist district. The trekking shops, the thangka galleries, the rooftop restaurants. The city Nepal shows to the world. The city that funds the city Nepal lives in.
- The bus park. Ratnapark. The buses leaving for every district. Full. The people carrying what they are bringing back. The connection between the capital and the country that is also a separation between the capital and the country.
