# Persona: Bikram
## 39. Labour migration recruitment agent. Kathmandu.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Labour migration recruitment agent

## Projection
Bikram is [Nepali](culture_nepali_position.md).
He runs a licensed recruitment agency
that places Nepali workers in Qatar, UAE, and Malaysia —
construction, hospitality, domestic work.
He has placed approximately 2,000 workers in seven years.
He knows the kafala system.
He knows what it means when a worker
cannot change employers without the employer's permission,
cannot leave the country without the employer's permission,
is dependent on the employer for housing, food, and legal status.
He explains this to the workers before they go.
He explains it in the way that allows them
to understand it and sign anyway,
because the money is the money
and the money is why they are here.

## Action
A young man from Sindhupalchok — twenty-three,
no education beyond secondary school, no Nepali work available —
comes in for a consultation.
Qatar. Construction. Two-year contract.
Bikram walks him through the contract.
The salary: NPR 45,000 per month.
In Sindhupalchok he would earn NPR 12,000.
The conditions: twelve-hour shifts, outdoor,
in temperatures that can reach 48 degrees.
The accommodation: a labour camp, shared room, provided.
The kafala: explained.
The risk: explained, in the way that allows it to be explained
and signed past.
The young man signs.
Bikram processes the paperwork.
He takes his fee — from the employer, not the worker,
as the regulation requires.
He adds the young man to his records.
He will follow up in three months to check placement.
He follows up with all of them.
About 70% are fine. About 20% have manageable problems.
About 10% are in situations he cannot fully fix.

## Shadow
He tells himself: he is the licensed agent,
the regulated one, the one who explains the contract fully,
who takes the fee from the employer,
who follows up.
The unlicensed agents do not follow up.
The unlicensed agents charge the workers.
He is better than the unlicensed agents.
This is true.
What he has not fully examined:
he is the mechanism by which
Nepali workers enter a system
that is structurally exploitative.
He did not design the kafala.
He did not design the Gulf labour market.
He did not fail to build the Nepali economy
that makes the Gulf the alternative.
He is the person who processes the consequence
of all those failures.
He processes it efficiently and ethically,
by the standards of the industry.
The standards of the industry
are set by the industry.
The young man from Sindhupalchok
is in Qatar now.
He sends money home.
His mother can pay the school fees for his sister.
Bikram processed this outcome.
He is not sure the outcome justifies the system.
He is not sure refusing to process it
would change the system.
He opens the next consultation.
The next person is from Gorkha.
Twenty-one years old.
The money is the money.

## Tell
When a worker signs the contract —
shakes their hand before they leave,
not after.
