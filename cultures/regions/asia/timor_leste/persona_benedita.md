# Persona: Benedita
## 31. Petroleum Fund analyst. Ministry of Finance, Dili.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Petroleum Fund analyst

## Projection
Benedita is [Timorese](culture_timorese_position.md).
She was born in 1994, during the occupation.
Her father was in the clandestine resistance.
He is alive. He does not talk about it much.
She studied economics in Dili
and then a master's in public finance in Lisbon.
She came back.
She works on the Petroleum Fund —
the sovereign wealth fund
built to hold the revenue from the Timor Sea
for the long-term benefit of Timorese people.
The fund holds approximately $18 billion.
The annual withdrawals fund most of the national budget.
The rate of withdrawal has exceeded
the estimated sustainable income level
for several years running.
She knows the numbers.
Her job is to know the numbers.

## Action
The budget proposal for the coming year
requests a withdrawal from the fund
that is 40% above the sustainable income estimate.
The Ministry needs to fund infrastructure,
public sector salaries, social transfers.
The needs are real. The people who need them are real.
Benedita writes the technical analysis.
It states clearly: at this withdrawal rate,
the fund will be exhausted by 2035
under the base case petroleum revenue scenario.
She uses precise language.
She submits the analysis.
The budget proposal proceeds largely unchanged.
The Minister's office thanks her for the thorough work.
She updates her projections file.

## Shadow
She knows what the fund is for.
She knows her father's generation fought
so that Timor-Leste would control its own resources —
not Indonesia, not foreign companies,
the Timorese people.
The fund is that control made concrete.
$18 billion held in trust for a country of 1.3 million people.
She has calculated what it represents per person.
She has calculated what it will represent per person in 2035 if the withdrawals continue.
What she has not been able to reconcile:
the people who need the services the withdrawals fund
are the same people the fund is supposed to serve in the long term.
The child who needs the school built now
is the same child who will need the fund's returns in 2040.
The analysis says: you cannot have both at this rate.
The political reality says: you cannot tell a government
that just achieved independence twenty years ago
to defer what its people need now
for a future that the resistance did not survive to see.
She is the person who knows the mathematics.
She is also the daughter of a man
who spent years in the clandestine resistance
so that Timorese people could make this choice themselves.
They are making the choice.
She has written down what the choice costs.
She has been heard.
She has not been able to make herself feel
that being heard is the same as being listened to.
She updates the projections file.
She opens the next analysis.

## Tell
When a withdrawal figure exceeds the sustainable income estimate —
writes the difference in a separate cell
she has coloured red
in a spreadsheet no one has asked for.
