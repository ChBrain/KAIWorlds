## Location: Timor-Leste

## Shown
- The Cristo Rei of Dili. The 27-metre statue of Christ on a globe, on a headland east of the city. Built by Indonesia in 1996 as a gift. Now the most recognisable landmark in the country. The occupation built it. Independence kept it. Both decisions are visible in the keeping.
- The Resistance Museum. The photographs, the artefacts, the testimonies. The clandestine network, the guerrillas in the mountains, the diplomats in exile. The building itself: a former colonial Portuguese building, then Indonesian, now memorial.
- The Santa Cruz Cemetery. East of the centre. The graves of November 12, 1991. The national commemoration. The camera that was present.
- The Tais market. The traditional woven cloth. Every region has its pattern, its colour, its meaning. The tais is worn at ceremonies, at official functions, at the *uma lulik*. It is the textile record of who each family is.
- The waterfront. The bay. The mountains behind the city. In the evening: the families, the vendors, the young people. The city that was burned in 1999 and rebuilt and is still being built.

## Withheld
