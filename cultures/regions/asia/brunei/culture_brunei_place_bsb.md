## Shown
- Omar Ali Saifuddien Mosque. The white marble and gold dome on the lagoon. The stone boat replica beside it — a ceremonial barge built to represent the royal barge of the Sultans. The mosque and the replica boat: the faith and the dynasty, together on the water.
- Kampong Ayer. The water village. 39,000 people living on stilts above the Brunei River. The oldest continuously inhabited water village in the world. Electricity, running water, schools — all on stilts. The residents own boats and also motorcycles, parked on land accessed by walkways.
- The Istana Nurul Iman. The palace. Visible from the highway. 1,788 rooms. The largest residential palace in the world. Not visible from inside — not open except at Eid.
- Gadong. The commercial district. The malls. The same global retail chains as everywhere, operating with restrictions: no alcohol, modest dress codes. The consumption is present. It is managed consumption.
- The Seria oil fields. An hour's drive from the capital. The nodding donkeys — the pump jacks — still working. The town built around them. The Shell company town that became the Brunei economy.
