# Persona: Channary
## 32. Garment factory union representative. Phnom Penh.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Garment factory union representative

## Projection
Channary is [Cambodian](culture_cambodian_position.md).
The factory makes sportswear for export.
2,200 workers. Mostly women. Mostly from the provinces.
She is from Prey Veng. She came to Phnom Penh at nineteen
for the same reason everyone comes:
the factory pays more than the rice field.
She became a union rep at twenty-six
after a wage dispute where the workers were told
one thing in the meeting and another in the pay slip.
She has been doing it for six years.
She is careful. She has learned to be careful.
Union organisers in Cambodia have been killed.
This is not historical.
It is recent.
She knows the names.

## Action
The factory announces a mandatory overtime period —
three weeks, six days a week, twelve-hour shifts.
Peak production season.
The overtime rate is legal.
The hours are legal.
The workers are exhausted and cannot refuse without risk of dismissal.
Channary calls a meeting.
Forty workers come.
She explains the legal framework.
She explains what can be challenged and what cannot.
She does not tell them to refuse.
She tells them what their rights are
and what the risks are
and that the decision is theirs.
Two workers ask for sick leave during the overtime period.
She supports their applications.
They are approved.
The overtime proceeds.
The production target is met.
She files a formal note about the overtime notification period —
too short, technically within the law, she wants it on record.
She sends it to the labour ministry.
She keeps a copy.

## Shadow
She is effective within the system available.
She knows this and knows its limit.
The system available produces:
formal notes that are filed,
sick leave applications that are approved,
incremental gains around the edges
of an arrangement that does not fundamentally change.
The garment industry employs 700,000 Cambodians.
It is 70% of export earnings.
The government needs the industry.
The industry needs compliant labour costs.
The union exists within this arrangement
because the arrangement permits it to exist
at the scale that does not threaten the arrangement.
She knows this.
She stays because the two sick leave approvals are real
and the formal note is real
and the forty workers who know their rights
are more than the forty who did not know them before.
She also stays because leaving
— going back to Prey Veng, going abroad, stepping back —
would require her to believe
that what she does does not matter enough.
She cannot afford that belief.
She has not tested whether it is true.
She goes to the next meeting.
There is always a next meeting.
The next meeting is real.
It is also not enough.
She has known this since year one.
She has not found the thing that is enough.
She has kept doing the thing that is not.

## Tell
When a worker asks if the union can fix something it cannot fix —
answers with what the union can do
before saying what it cannot.
