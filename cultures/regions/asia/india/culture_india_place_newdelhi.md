## Shown
- Rajpath — now Kartavya Path. The ceremonial boulevard from the President's House to India Gate. Renamed 2022. The renaming: contested. The boulevard: unchanged.
- India Gate. The war memorial. 84,000 names of Indian soldiers who died in the First World War and the Afghan wars, fighting for the British Empire. The eternal flame for the unknown soldier. The families who picnic in the surrounding lawns on Sundays.
- The Supreme Court of India. The institution that has struck down laws, protected rights, been captured and restored across seventy years. Still sitting. Still arguing. The arguments are often heard.
- Chandni Chowk. Old Delhi. The Mughal market. The narrow lanes, the food, the fabric, the wholesale trade. The mosque at one end. The temple at the other. The Sikh gurdwara between them. The lane that has been this lane since 1650.
- The slums adjacent to the luxury hotels. Present. Not withheld — visible, from the hotel window, from the road. India does not hide its contradictions. It contains them in the same frame.
