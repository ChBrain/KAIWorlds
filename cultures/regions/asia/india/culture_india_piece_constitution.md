# Piece: The Constitution of India
## January 26, 1950. The most ambitious democratic experiment in history.

## Owner
- **Place:** New Delhi

## Place
New Delhi. Parliament House.
The original circular building, 1927.
The new Parliament building, 2023 —
opened by the Prime Minister without the President.
The constitutional question of that ceremony
was discussed loudly.
The building stands. The discussion continues.
Both are India.

## Load Bearing
Remove the Constitution and India has no agreed framework
for managing its own plurality.
The question of what holds 1.4 billion people —
speaking hundreds of languages,
practising every major religion,
organised by a caste system the constitution abolished
and daily life perpetuates —
in one democratic state
has no answer except the constitution.
Not the military. Not ethnicity. Not religion.
The constitution: universal suffrage, fundamental rights,
directive principles, independent judiciary,
federalism that gives states significant autonomy.
B.R. Ambedkar, its principal architect,
was a Dalit — from the untouchable caste —
who used the constitution to dismantle
the legal basis of the system that had oppressed him.
He knew the document was the argument.
He knew it was not the outcome.
He said, at the adoption ceremony,
that the constitution would not survive
if the social and economic democracy
did not accompany the political democracy.
He was prescient.
The political democracy has survived.
The social democracy is contested.
The economic democracy is contested.
The argument is ongoing.
Remove the piece: the plurality becomes ungovernable.
With it: the plurality is argued over, litigated,
sometimes violated, sometimes defended —
inside a framework that was built
to contain exactly this.
The framework is strained.
It has been strained before.
It is still the framework.

## Apparent
1947: independence and partition. August 15.
The partition: 14 million displaced, 1–2 million killed.
The largest mass migration in human history.
Nehru: a tryst with destiny.
Jinnah: Pakistan.
Gandhi: assassinated six months later, January 1948.
The constituent assembly: 299 members, 2 years, 11 sessions.
B.R. Ambedkar chairs the drafting committee.
January 26, 1950: the constitution comes into force. Republic Day.
The first general election: 1952. Universal suffrage for 176 million voters.
The world doubted it could work.
It worked.
The Emergency: 1975–1977. Indira Gandhi suspends the constitution.
The press censored. Opponents jailed.
1977: the election. The Emergency government loses.
The constitution restored. By election.
The demolition of the Babri Masjid: December 6, 1992.
The Gujarat riots: 2002. Over 1,000 dead.
The citizenship amendment debates.
The farmers' protests.
The press freedom index: declining.
The judiciary: under pressure.
The constitution: still in force.
Ambedkar's warning: still pending.
