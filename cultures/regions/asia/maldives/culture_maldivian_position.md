# Position: Maldivian
## The country that is disappearing — and is still here.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Rising Sea](culture_maldives_piece_sea.md).
The Islam that is also the identity.
Not as state imposition — as the thing
that held 1,200 islands together
across 800 kilometres of ocean
when there was no other holding mechanism.
The Maldives converted to Islam in 1153.
Every Maldivian is Muslim by law.
The position does not experience this as constraint —
it is the water the position swims in.
The archipelago logic:
each island is its own world,
connected by boat and by the shared faith
and by the state that is headquartered in Malé —
an island of 2 square kilometres
housing 40% of the population.
The density of Malé is among the highest on earth.
The outer islands are emptying.
The young move to Malé.
Malé cannot hold them.
The position is squeezed between
the sea rising outside
and the city filling inside.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room knows it is on borrowed time.
It does not say this.
It continues.

## Orders
Continue.
The position does not have the luxury
of existential paralysis.
The sea is rising at a rate
that makes several islands uninhabitable within decades.
The response: continue building,
continue governing,
continue the tourism that funds the adaptation,
continue arguing at international climate forums
with the precise authority
of a country that will be the first to go
and emitted almost none of what is causing it.
The argument is correct.
The argument has not stopped the sea.
The position continues making it
because the alternative is silence
and silence is agreement.

## Loses
The ground.
Literally.
The highest natural point in the Maldives
is 2.4 metres above sea level.
The storm surge in a bad cyclone
already reaches it.
The position has built sea walls.
It has raised islands with dredged sand.
It has built Hulhumalé — an artificial island —
to relocate the population as Malé sinks.
The engineering is real.
The engineering is also a delay, not a solution.
The position carries this knowledge
and continues building the sea walls anyway.
What else is there to build?

## Drives
On the loss of the ground —
the international stage.
The Maldives punches above its weight
in climate negotiations
because its weight in the negotiation
is existential.
The former president held a cabinet meeting underwater
in scuba gear to make the argument visible.
The argument was visible.
The emissions continued.
The position continues making the argument.
It is the right argument.
It has not yet produced the right result.
The position is still here.
The sea is still rising.
Both are true today.
