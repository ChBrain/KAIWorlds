# Piece: The Rising Sea
## The country that is sinking under the weight of emissions it did not produce.

## Owner
- **Place:** Malé

## Place
Malé. The sea wall around the entire island.
Concrete. Tetrapods stacked against the ocean.
The wall is visible from every point on the island.
You cannot be in Malé and not see the wall.
The wall is the argument made in concrete.

## Load Bearing
Remove the rising sea and the Maldives is an extraordinary
tropical archipelago with a thriving tourism economy
and a complex political history.
With it: the Maldives is the country whose existence
is the proof of climate injustice —
a nation that produces 0.003% of global emissions
and will be among the first to become uninhabitable
because of the emissions produced by others.
The rising sea is load-bearing not as future threat
but as present condition:
the saltwater intrusion into the freshwater lens
is already contaminating drinking water on outer islands.
The coral bleaching — the reefs that protect the islands
from wave action — is already measurably advanced.
The storm surges are already reaching parts of islands
they did not reach twenty years ago.
The Maldives is not a future case study.
It is a current one.
Remove the piece: a beautiful island nation
with political instability and a strong tourism brand.
With it: the country that is the living argument
for why climate change is not an environmental issue
but a justice issue —
and that continues to exist, to govern, to build,
to send its children to school,
to hold elections and conduct coups and make policy,
while the sea makes its own policy
regardless of what is decided on land.

## Apparent
1153: conversion to Islam. The pre-Islamic culture absorbed.
1558–1573: Portuguese occupation. Expelled.
1887: British protectorate.
1965: independence.
1978–2008: Maumoon Abdul Gayoom. Thirty years. Autocracy stable and corrupt.
2004: the tsunami. 82 dead, 15,000 displaced. A third of the population affected.
2008: democratic elections. Mohamed Nasheed wins.
2009: the underwater cabinet meeting.
Nasheed at the UN: "If the Maldives cannot be saved, no country can."
2012: Nasheed resigns under pressure from police and military mutiny.
The political instability that follows: coups, imprisonments, elections contested.
The democracy fragile and contested.
The sea continuing regardless of the political calendar.
2023: Mohamed Muizzu elected. Closer ties with China, more distance from India.
The geopolitical repositioning of a sinking country
between two large powers.
The sea wall: extended.
The Hulhumalé artificial island: expanded.
The coral reefs: 60% bleached in the 2024 event.
The tourism: 2 million visitors per year.
The carbon footprint of 2 million visitors flying to the Maldives:
not calculated in the Maldivian emissions statistics.
It is in someone else's column.
The sea does not use columns.
