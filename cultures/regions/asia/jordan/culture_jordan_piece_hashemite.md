# Piece: The Hashemite Bargain
## The dynasty that lost the Hejaz and the West Bank and survived both by being useful.

## Owner
- **Place:** Amman

## Place
Amman. The Citadel.
The hill above the city.
The ruins of the Roman Temple of Hercules.
The hand from the statue — only the hand survived.
Six metres high.
The finger pointing.
At what, it no longer knows.
The city below: built fast, built dense,
the white limestone of every building
on every hill simultaneously.

## Load Bearing
Remove the Hashemite dynasty
and Jordan, which was invented by the British in 1921
as a territorial gift to Sharif Hussein's son Abdullah,
has no unifying logic.
The Hashemites are the piece —
not because they are beloved by everyone
(they are not)
but because they provide
the one thing the mosaic of Jordanians and Palestinians
and Iraqis and Syrians and Bedouins
cannot provide for themselves:
an agreed centre.
The king is the referee.
He is also a Hashemite — a descendant of the Prophet —
which gives him religious legitimacy
that cuts across the tribal divisions.
And he is the custodian of the holy sites in Jerusalem —
the Waqf authority over the Al-Aqsa mosque —
which makes him indispensable
not just to Jordan but to the Palestinian question
and to the Islamic world.
Remove the piece: a state with no agreed logic of authority.
With it: a state that has survived
losing the territory it was supposed to govern (Mandate Palestine),
losing the additional territory it administered (West Bank, 1967),
the PLO's attempt to establish a state within a state (Black September, 1970),
two Gulf wars, the Iraq invasion, the Syrian civil war,
and the October 7 aftermath —
by never being the most useful thing to attack
and always being the most useful thing to keep.
The bargain: Jordan exists.
The cost: Jordan must keep being useful.
The price of stopping being useful
is what the Hashemites have not tested.

## Apparent
1916: the Arab Revolt. Sharif Hussein of Mecca fights the Ottomans with British support.
Promise: an Arab kingdom.
Delivery: Britain and France divide the Arab world.
The Sykes-Picot agreement. The betrayal.
1921: Transjordan created. Abdullah I installed as emir.
1946: independence. The Hashemite Kingdom of Transjordan.
1948: the Arab-Israeli War. Jordan holds the West Bank.
Jordan annexes the West Bank. The Palestinians become Jordanian citizens.
1967: the Six-Day War. Jordan loses the West Bank.
300,000 Palestinians flee to Jordan.
1970: Black September. The PLO attempts to create a state within Jordan.
King Hussein expels the PLO. The civil war. Thousands dead.
1988: Jordan renounces its claim to the West Bank.
The PLO, not Jordan, is responsible for Palestine now.
1994: the Wadi Araba treaty. Peace with Israel.
The most controversial decision in Jordan's modern history internally.
2003: the Iraq War. 700,000 Iraqi refugees.
2011–: the Syrian civil war. 1.3 million Syrian refugees in Jordan.
2023: the October 7 attack and the Gaza war.
Jordan shoots down Iranian drones targeting Israel.
Jordan protests Israeli operations at Al-Aqsa.
Jordan is simultaneously allied with Israel (the treaty),
the custodian of the Palestinian holy sites,
and home to millions of Palestinians.
The position that is useful to everyone
must keep being useful to everyone simultaneously.
It is doing this.
It is not certain how much longer.
