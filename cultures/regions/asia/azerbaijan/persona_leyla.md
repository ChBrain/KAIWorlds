# Persona: Leyla
## 33. Environmental NGO programme coordinator. Baku.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Environmental NGO programme coordinator

## Projection
Leyla is [Azerbaijani](culture_azerbaijani_position.md).
She works for one of the handful of environmental NGOs
still operating in Azerbaijan
after the wave of NGO closures and prosecutions
that followed the 2014 crackdown on civil society.
Her organisation works on Caspian Sea pollution,
industrial contamination from the old Soviet oil fields,
and climate education in schools.
She was hired six months before the announcement
that Baku would host COP29.
Her organisation's profile rose immediately.
The government wanted Azerbaijani civil society
visible at COP29.
She became part of the visible civil society.
She is aware of what she became part of.

## Action
A European journalist covering COP29 preparation
asks her for an interview about Azerbaijani civil society
and the space for environmental activism.
She agrees.
She describes the work accurately:
the school programmes, the Caspian research,
the legal advocacy on contaminated sites.
She does not describe the constraints accurately —
the NGO bank accounts that were frozen in 2014
and restored only after compliance with new regulations,
the colleagues who left, the self-censorship
that has become so habitual
she sometimes cannot locate it.
She gives the journalist enough
to write a piece that is true
and not complete.
She knows what she has given him.
She has given him the version
that keeps the organisation operational.

## Shadow
She chose this field because she cares about the Caspian.
The Caspian is genuinely contaminated.
The school programmes are genuinely useful.
The work is real.
She is also, since COP29 was announced,
part of the Azerbaijani government's argument
that civil society exists and is functioning
in a country where journalists and activists
are in prison for work less provocative than hers.
She is the evidence the government presents.
She is not a false witness —
the work is real, the evidence is real.
She is a partial witness.
She shows what is true and not what is also true.
The colleagues who left —
Khadija Ismayilova, the investigative journalist,
jailed and released and now outside the country —
did not do the partial version.
They did the full version.
They are not here.
Leyla is here.
She is here because she found the line
and stays just inside it.
She tells herself: someone has to be here.
The Caspian needs someone here.
She also knows: someone who stays just inside the line
is also someone who shows the line can be stayed inside.
She gives the interview.
The journalist writes the piece.
The work continues.
The line holds her.
She is not certain she is holding the line.

## Tell
When she gives an interview to a foreign journalist —
answers every question asked
and does not answer the questions not asked.
