# Piece: Karabakh and the Oil
## The frozen conflict that wasn't frozen — and the petro-state that built the army to unfreeze it.

## Owner
- **Place:** Baku

## Place
Baku. The Flame Towers.
Three towers shaped like flames
on the hill above the old city.
They are clad in LED screens
that display the Azerbaijani flag at night.
The flame is the symbol of Azerbaijan —
the Land of Fire, the eternal flames
from the natural gas seeps
that Zoroastrians worshipped here for centuries.
The towers are the oil money made architecture.
They are the argument from the hill.

## Load Bearing
Remove Karabakh and Azerbaijan is an oil state
in the South Caucasus
with a managed democracy and a Caspian coastline.
With it: Azerbaijan is the country
defined by the loss and recovery of territory —
the 1994 ceasefire that froze the conflict
with 20% of Azerbaijani territory
under Armenian control and 800,000 Azerbaijanis displaced,
and the 44-day war of 2020
that recovered most of it,
and the September 2023 operation
that recovered the rest
as 100,000 Armenians of Karabakh fled.
The Karabakh conflict is load-bearing
because it explains the oil:
the oil revenue was used to build the army
that won the war.
The Baku-Tbilisi-Ceyhan pipeline
was built to give Azerbaijan
an export route that bypassed both Russia and Iran —
to ensure that the oil revenue
would be controlled by Baku,
not by transit states with their own interests.
The independence, the oil, the pipeline, the army, the war —
these are a single logic.
Remove any element: the sequence changes.
With all of them: a small country in a difficult neighbourhood
that played its hand with precision
over thirty years
and emerged with everything it started with
plus the confidence of having done it.
The confidence is the piece's secondary product.
It will outlast the oil.

## Apparent
1918: the Azerbaijan Democratic Republic.
The first democratic republic in the Muslim world.
Lasted 23 months before Soviet takeover.
1920–1991: the Azerbaijan Soviet Socialist Republic.
The oil: Baku was the world's largest oil producer in 1900.
Soviet period: oil production industrialised, revenues sent to Moscow.
1988: the Karabakh conflict begins. Armenian demands for unification with Armenia.
1991: independence. The conflict intensifies.
1992–1994: the First Karabakh War. Azerbaijan loses.
800,000 Azerbaijanis displaced. 20% of territory under Armenian control.
1994: the ceasefire. The conflict frozen.
1994: the Contract of the Century — the Baku oil deal.
BP and a consortium of Western companies.
The pipeline revenue begins.
2003: Heydar Aliyev dies. His son Ilham takes power.
The oil revenue funds military modernisation.
Turkish drones. Israeli technology. Israeli-Azerbaijani defence cooperation.
September–November 2020: the 44-day war. Azerbaijan wins.
Seven districts recovered. Shusha — the cultural capital — recovered.
November 10, 2020: the ceasefire agreement.
Russian peacekeepers deployed to Karabakh.
September 19, 2023: Azerbaijan launches a one-day operation.
The Armenian forces in Karabakh surrender.
100,000 Armenians of Karabakh leave within weeks.
The conflict is resolved.
The displacement is complete — in the other direction.
November 2024: COP29 in Baku.
The oil state hosting the climate summit.
The position at the table.
