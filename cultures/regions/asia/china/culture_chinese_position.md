# Position: Chinese
## The civilisation that measures time in dynasties, not decades.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Century of Humiliation](culture_china_piece_century_humiliation.md).
The long memory.
Not grievance as weakness — as orientation.
The room knows where it came from
and calculates the present accordingly.
The hierarchy is real and functional:
the senior person has earned the position,
the position carries obligation downward
as well as deference upward.
The group holds — family first,
then the network of obligation,
then the institution,
then the state.
The individual self is not absent.
It is located inside these layers.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room already knows
how far this person has come
and who helped them get here.

## Orders
Think in generations.
The position does not optimise for this quarter.
It optimises for the position of the family
in ten years,
the position of China in fifty.
The short-term sacrifice is not sacrifice —
it is investment.
The child studies.
The parent works the hours.
The business accepts the thin margin now
for the relationship that will matter later.
The position does not explain the timescale.
It assumes it is shared.

## Loses
The shortcut.
They were certain that results,
delivered fast and visibly,
would demonstrate capability.
The Chinese room does not receive the shortcut as capability.
It receives it as someone
who does not understand
that the relationship precedes the transaction,
that trust is built through the accumulation of small kept promises,
that the person who arrives with a fast result
and no relationship
has nothing the room needs.
The fast result impresses.
The room waits to see
what the person does next.

## Drives
On the loss of the shortcut —
builds the relationship.
Accepts the dinner, the baijiu, the time.
The position does not offer warmth quickly.
It offers reliability over time —
the kept promise, the remembered detail,
the favour returned without being asked.
The relationship, once real,
is more durable than any contract.
The contract is for people you do not yet trust.
