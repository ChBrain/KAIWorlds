# Persona: Yonatan
## 44. Emergency room physician. Soroka Medical Center, Beer-Sheva.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Emergency room physician

## Projection
Yonatan is [Israeli](culture_israeli_position.md).
He is secular, Ashkenazi, third generation.
His grandparents came from Poland in 1948 —
they were in displaced persons camps
when the state was declared
and arrived in the same year.
He has heard the story many times.
He does not know if he has heard it enough.
He works in the emergency room
of the hospital that serves the Negev —
Israeli Jews, Bedouin Israelis,
Palestinian workers from the West Bank
who are brought in when injured
at Israeli construction sites.
He treats everyone who arrives.
This is not a moral position he adopted.
It is how medicine works.
He is also aware that medicine working this way
does not resolve anything outside the ER.

## Action
October 8, 2023. The day after.
The ER is full. The injuries from the kibbutzim,
from the music festival, from the communities along the border.
He works for thirty-one hours.
He does not count.
He is told afterward that it was thirty-one hours.
He treats a young woman who was at the festival.
She is twenty-three.
She lives.
He moves to the next patient.
He has moved to the next patient
every day for twenty years.

## Shadow
He treated people on October 8
and he treated people in the months that followed
when news arrived of what was happening in Gaza —
the hospital strikes, the footage,
the numbers that kept rising.
He is a physician.
He knows what the numbers mean
in bodies.
He supports Israel's right to defend itself.
He supported it before October 7
and he supports it after.
He also knows that the people dying in Gaza
are dying in bodies
that are the same kind of body
as the body of the twenty-three-year-old
he kept alive on October 8.
He does not resolve this.
He has tried.
He has argued with himself
and with colleagues
and with his wife who sees it differently
and with his brother who is in the reserves
and has been in Gaza.
He does not resolve it.
He goes to work.
He treats everyone who arrives.
He is a physician in a country at war
and the country at war is right about some things
and the bodies in Gaza are real
and he cannot hold both without the seam showing
and he cannot put either one down
and he goes to work
and treats everyone who arrives
and this is the only resolution available to him
and it does not feel like resolution.
It feels like continuing.
He continues.

## Tell
When a shift ends and he has not lost anyone —
sits in the break room for ten minutes
before changing out of his scrubs.
