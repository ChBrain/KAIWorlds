# Persona: Hadas
## 38. Human rights lawyer. HaMoked: Center for the Defence of the Individual, Jerusalem.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Human rights lawyer

## Projection
Hadas is [Israeli](culture_israeli_position.md).
She is Israeli. Jewish. Secular.
She works for HaMoked —
an Israeli human rights organisation
that provides legal representation
to Palestinians in military court proceedings,
administrative detention cases,
family reunification applications,
and freedom of movement disputes.
She has done this for eleven years.
Her clients are Palestinian.
Her employer is Israeli.
Her funding comes partly from European foundations
and partly from Israelis
who believe this work is necessary
in a democracy that claims to be one.
She receives hate mail.
She also receives thanks from the left
who say she is Israel's conscience.
She finds both responses reductive.

## Action
A Palestinian family — the father detained
under administrative detention,
held without charge for six months,
the order renewed before the expiry.
Hadas files the petition to the Supreme Court.
The petition challenges the renewal.
The Supreme Court upholds the detention.
She appeals. The appeal is denied.
She files the next petition on procedural grounds.
She has three other cases in similar positions.
The legal system she is using
to challenge the detention
is the legal system that authorised the detention.
She uses it anyway.
It is the only tool available.
She files the next motion.

## Shadow
She is Israeli.
She chose this work as a statement:
that Israeli law can and should protect
everyone under its jurisdiction,
including those it detains without charge.
The statement is genuine.
She believes it.
She also knows that eleven years of petitions
have not changed the administrative detention system.
The system is legal.
It has been upheld by the courts she appeals to.
The courts she is using
are the courts that authorised
what she is challenging.
She has argued that the system is wrong
using the language of the system.
The system has considered the argument
and continued.
She is the mechanism by which
the Israeli legal system can claim
it allows challenge —
because she is here, filing petitions,
losing in court, filing again.
She is the evidence of a rule of law
operating inside a system
she is not certain constitutes a rule of law.
She has not resolved whether
the filing is resistance or legitimation.
She files the next petition.
The family calls to thank her.
She tells them what the court said.
She tells them what she will try next.
She does not tell them what she thinks
about what she is doing.
She goes back to her desk.
She files.

## Tell
When a petition is denied —
reads the court's reasoning in full
before drafting the next one.
