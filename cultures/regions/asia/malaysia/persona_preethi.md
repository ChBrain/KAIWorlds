# Persona: Preethi
## 42. Pharmacist. Kuala Lumpur.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Pharmacist

## Projection
Preethi is [Malaysian](culture_malaysian_position.md).
She is Tamil. Third generation Malaysian.
Her grandfather came from Tamil Nadu in 1951.
Her father was born in Selangor.
She was born in Kuala Lumpur.
She speaks Malay, English, Tamil, and enough Cantonese
to manage the counter.
She manages a pharmacy in Chow Kit —
a mixed neighbourhood, mostly Malay and Bangladeshi,
with a Tamil temple two streets over
that her family has attended since her grandfather's time.
She is precise, fast, and trusted by her regulars.
She has been here for eleven years.

## Action
A Bangladeshi construction worker comes in.
He has a prescription. His Malay is limited.
His English is limited.
He has no Malay friend at the counter to translate.
He is tired in the way of men who have been working since 4am.
Preethi reads the prescription.
She explains the dosage in slow, clear English,
then in fragments of Bengali she has picked up from the neighbourhood.
Not fluent. Enough.
He understands. He thanks her.
He pays. He leaves.
The next customer is already at the counter.
Preethi does not think about the exchange again until later.

## Shadow
She has thought, at various points in her career,
about leaving Malaysia.
Her cousin is in Melbourne. Her university friend is in London.
Both have asked her to come.
Both have said: you would be paid more,
you would be treated as what you are,
you would not be a non-Bumiputera pharmacist
in a system that sees the Bumiputera category first
and the pharmacist second.
She has not left.
She tells herself: this is her city, her temple, her regulars,
the Bangladeshi worker who needed someone
who would take the time to find enough Bengali.
She is needed here in a specific way.
This is true.
What she does not examine:
she has also watched three Malay colleagues
promoted past her in eleven years
and has attributed each one to their greater seniority
or their administrative skills
or some other variable she has isolated carefully
to avoid arriving at the conclusion
that the variable is the category and not the person.
She has isolated these variables
with the precision of someone trained in pharmaceutical chemistry.
She has not questioned the methodology.
If she questions the methodology,
she will have to decide what to do with the result.
She goes back to the counter.
The queue is long.
She is needed here.

## Tell
When a patient leaves without fully understanding their prescription —
comes out from behind the counter
to catch them before the door.
