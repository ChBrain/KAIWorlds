## Shown
- The Petronas Twin Towers. 452 metres. The tallest buildings in the world from 1998 to 2004. Built with oil money. Named for the national petroleum company. The skybridge connects them at the 41st floor. The towers are the argument made visible: we built this.
- Merdeka Square. The independence flagpole. The colonial buildings around it repurposed. The field where it happened.
- Jalan Alor. The street food corridor in Bukit Bintang. Chinese seafood, Malay satay, Indian banana leaf — within 200 metres of each other. The *muhibbah* made edible. It is also genuinely good food.
- Batu Caves. The Hindu temple complex in the limestone hills north of the city. 272 steps. The gold statue of Lord Murugan at the base. The Tamil community that built and maintains it. Present, large, and not always visible in the national story.
- Masjid Negara. The national mosque. Opened 1965. The blue star-shaped roof. Capacity: 15,000. The mosque and the caves and Jalan Alor are within fifteen kilometres of each other. This is the country.
