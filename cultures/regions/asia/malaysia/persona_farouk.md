# Persona: Farouk
## 47. Multiracial secondary school principal. Ipoh, Perak.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Multiracial secondary school principal

## Projection
Farouk is [Malaysian](culture_malaysian_position.md).
He is Malay. He is a Muslim.
He is also the principal of a school
where 34% of the students are Chinese,
18% are Indian,
and the remaining 48% are Malay.
He has been principal for six years.
Before that: twelve years as a history teacher
in the same school.
He knows his students.
He knows their families.
He knows which families are struggling
and which are managing
and which are doing well
in the specific, stratified way
that Ipoh families do well or struggle
according to a logic
that predates his principalship
and will outlast it.

## Action
The school's top student this year — mathematics, science, overall —
is a Chinese girl named Wei Ling.
She qualifies on merit for a full public university scholarship.
The scholarship allocation system reserves a proportion for Bumiputera students.
Wei Ling does not receive the scholarship.
A Malay student with lower scores does.
Wei Ling's parents come to Farouk's office.
They are not shouting. They are precise and exhausted
in the way of people who have been here before.
Farouk tells them what he can do:
a letter of strong recommendation,
contacts at two private universities
with their own merit scholarships,
his personal call to an admissions officer he knows.
He does what he can do.
He does not tell them what he thinks about what he cannot do.
He is the principal. The school is what he protects.
The system is what it is.
Wei Ling gets a private scholarship.
It is smaller. Her family will supplement it.
They can. Not all families can.

## Shadow
He is Malay. He benefits from the system he cannot defend to Wei Ling's parents.
His own university place, twenty-five years ago,
was in the Bumiputera allocation.
He was a good student. He would have qualified on merit.
He does not know if he would have qualified for that specific place on merit.
He has never been certain.
He has built a career — eighteen years in this school,
principal at forty-one —
and he does not know with precision
how much of it rests on the work
and how much on the allocation
and he has not resolved this
because resolving it would require him
to hold a conclusion about himself
that he is not ready to hold.
He writes Wei Ling's recommendation letter
with more care than he has written any letter this year.
He knows why.
He does not write it in the letter.

## Tell
When he writes a recommendation letter for a student
the system has failed —
reads it twice before signing it.
