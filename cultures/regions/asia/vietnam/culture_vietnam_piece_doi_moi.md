# Piece: Đổi Mới
## The renovation that saved the country by admitting the country had been wrong.

## Owner
- **Place:** Hanoi

## Place
Hanoi. The Old Quarter.
36 streets, each named for what was sold there.
Silk Street. Paper Street. Tin Street.
The market logic that predates the revolution
and survived it.

## Load Bearing
Remove Đổi Mới and Vietnam in the 1980s
continues toward famine and institutional collapse.
The command economy after reunification
had produced hyperinflation, food shortages,
and a country that had won the war
and could not feed itself.
The 1986 decision to move toward a market economy —
within a one-party state,
without naming what was being abandoned —
is the hinge on which modern Vietnam turns.
Remove the piece: Vietnam is a unified socialist state
that proved socialism could win a war
and demonstrated, immediately after, that it could not run a country.
With it: Vietnam is the country that found a third path —
not capitalism, not the Soviet model,
something pragmatic and unnamed
that produced one of the fastest economic developments
in the twentieth century's final decades.
The position's flexibility — its willingness to adapt
without calling the adaptation a defeat —
is the Đổi Mới logic applied to everything.

## Apparent
1975: reunification. Saigon renamed Ho Chi Minh City.
The South absorbed into the socialist framework.
The re-education camps. The boat people — 800,000 to 1 million flee.
The collectivisation of agriculture. Output falls.
1978: Vietnam invades Cambodia, removes the Khmer Rouge.
China invades Vietnam in response. Vietnam repels China.
The 1980s: hyperinflation. Food shortages. International isolation.
1986: the Sixth National Congress of the Communist Party.
Đổi Mới announced. Renovation.
The word chosen carefully: not reform, not abandonment — renovation.
The house is the same. The interior is being changed.
Agricultural land returned to household farming.
Private enterprise permitted.
Foreign investment welcomed.
1995: normalisation of relations with the United States.
Vietnam joins ASEAN.
2007: Vietnam joins the WTO.
2024: GDP per capita approximately $4,300.
In 1986 it was under $100.
The party remains. The market operates within it.
The contradiction is managed by not naming it.
The management is the achievement.
