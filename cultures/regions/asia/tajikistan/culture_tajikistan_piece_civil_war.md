# Piece: The Civil War
## 1992–1997. The war that defined who governs Tajikistan — and who does not.

## Owner
- **Place:** Dushanbe

## Place
Dushanbe. Rudaki Avenue.
Named for the tenth-century Persian poet.
The statue of Ismoil Somoni — the Samanid ruler —
in the central square.
The national identity built on medieval Persian glory
in a city that was a small village
until the Soviets made it a capital in 1929.

## Load Bearing
Remove the civil war and Tajikistan is the poorest
Soviet successor state, navigating independence
with a weak economy and strong regional divisions.
With it: Tajikistan is the country
where the regional and clan tensions
suppressed by Soviet authority
exploded immediately upon independence —
and where the resolution of those tensions
produced the current system:
Emomali Rahmon, from Kulyab,
in power since 1994,
the longest-serving non-royal ruler in the former Soviet space.
The civil war is load-bearing
because it determined the settlement —
who got power, who was excluded,
which regions are represented in government
and which are watched.
The Islamist opposition that fought in the war
was incorporated, then suppressed, then outlawed.
The regional opposition from Gharm and Badakhshan
was incorporated, then suppressed, then periodically arrested.
The settlement ended the war.
It did not end the conditions that produced it.
Remove the piece: a poor mountainous country
with a strong president.
With it: a country whose strong president
is the product of a specific bargain
made in the blood of the 1990s,
a bargain that the position lives inside
without always naming what it is.

## Apparent
1991: independence. September 9.
The Soviet structures collapse.
The regional and clan networks that had been
subordinated to Soviet authority
reassert themselves.
November 1992: the civil war begins in earnest.
The Popular Front — Kulyabi and Hissar forces —
versus the opposition coalition
of Islamists, democrats, and Gharmi regionalists.
1993–1994: the Popular Front wins the military phase.
Rahmon comes to power.
Hundreds of thousands displaced to Afghanistan.
The war continues as insurgency until 1997.
1997: the General Agreement on Peace.
The United Tajik Opposition integrated into the government.
United Tajik Opposition participation: peaked and then eliminated.
2015: the Islamic Renaissance Party — the only legal Islamist party
in Central Asia — banned as a terrorist organisation.
Its leaders jailed.
2022: protests in Badakhshan — the Pamiri region.
Security forces deployed. The protests suppressed.
The Pamiris — ethnically and linguistically distinct —
arrested in numbers.
Rahmon: still president.
His son Rustam Emomali: head of the upper house of parliament.
The succession is visible.
The civil war's settlement is becoming a dynasty.
The mountains are the same mountains.
