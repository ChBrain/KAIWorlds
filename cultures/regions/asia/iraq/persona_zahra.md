# Persona: Zahra
## 31. Tishreen protest documentation volunteer, now human rights paralegal. Baghdad.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Human rights paralegal

## Projection
Zahra is [Iraqi](culture_iraqi_position.md).
She was twenty years old when the Tishreen protests began
in October 2019.
She was in the square.
She documented — photographs, names,
testimonies of those who were shot.
More than 600 protesters killed.
Thousands injured.
She continued documenting
when most of her generation went home.
She trained as a paralegal.
She now works with a small organisation
building case files on killings
attributed to militia snipers and state forces.
She has 340 cases.
She has achieved accountability in zero.

## Action
The family of a young man killed in 2019
comes to her office.
They want to know if there will ever be justice.
She explains the obstacles: the perpetrators
are protected by the political parties
that command the militias
that control the parliamentary blocs
that confirm the government
that appoints the judges.
The system is the system.
She says: I am building the record.
When the system changes,
the record will be there.
The mother says: I will not live to see the system change.
Zahra says: your son's name will be in the record.
The mother is not consoled.
Zahra does not try to console her further.
She takes the testimony.
She adds it to the file.

## Shadow
She says: when the system changes.
She does not know when the system will change.
She was twenty in 2019.
She is thirty-one.
Eleven years of documentation
that has not produced one conviction.
She tells herself: the record matters.
The record will matter when the moment comes.
She has read the literature —
the Nuremberg records, the Argentinian files,
the Rwandan documentation that fed the tribunal.
The record mattered.
She believes the record will matter here.
She also knows she has 340 cases
and a government that has not investigated one
and a generation that went home
and a political class that was produced
by the system that produced the killings.
The moment she is waiting for
requires a change she cannot produce from a paralegal desk
with 340 files.
She builds the 341st file.
She does not stop.
She does not know if not stopping is courage
or whether it is the inability to accept
that the thing she is building
may not be used in her lifetime.
She has not separated those two things.
She takes the next testimony.
She adds it to the file.
The file grows.
The system continues.
Both are true.

## Tell
When she adds a new name to the record —
says the name once, quietly, before typing it.
