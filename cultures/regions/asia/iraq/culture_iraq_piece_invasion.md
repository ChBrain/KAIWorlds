# Piece: The 2003 Invasion and What Followed
## The war that was supposed to take weeks — and the twenty years that followed.

## Owner
- **Place:** Baghdad

## Place
Baghdad. Firdos Square.
April 9, 2003.
The statue of Saddam Hussein pulled down.
The images broadcast around the world.
The square is now called Firdos Square still.
The statue is gone.
What replaced it
is the question the square cannot answer.

## Load Bearing
Remove the 2003 invasion
and Iraq is a brutal authoritarian state
under Saddam Hussein
with weapons of mass destruction programs
that were largely dismantled after 1991
— as the inspectors later confirmed.
With it: Iraq is the country
where the most consequential foreign policy decision
of the twenty-first century was made on false premises,
where the state was dismantled
(de-Baathification, the dissolution of the army)
in ways that put 400,000 armed men
with military training
outside any institutional structure —
men who became the officer corps of ISIS.
The invasion is load-bearing
not because it explains everything that followed
but because it set the conditions:
the power vacuum, the sectarian politics,
the Iranian influence that filled the vacuum,
the Sunni marginalisation that produced the insurgency,
the insurgency that produced al-Qaeda in Iraq,
al-Qaeda in Iraq that mutated into ISIS,
ISIS that occupied a third of the country
and committed genocide against the Yazidis
and produced a refugee crisis
and was defeated at the cost of Mosul.
Remove the piece: a complex country with internal tensions.
With it: a country whose internal tensions
were ignited by an external decision
made by people who did not understand
what they were igniting
and have largely not been held accountable
for what followed the ignition.
The Iraqis have been paying the account since 2003.
They are still paying.

## Apparent
1917: British forces take Baghdad. The British mandate.
1920: the Iraqi revolt. 10,000 Iraqis killed.
1932: independence. The Hashemite monarchy.
1958: the revolution. The monarchy overthrown. The republic.
1968: the Ba'ath Party takes power. Saddam Hussein rises.
1979: Saddam becomes president.
1980–1988: the Iran-Iraq War. One million dead.
Chemical weapons used against Kurds at Halabja, 1988. 5,000 killed.
1990: Iraq invades Kuwait.
1991: the Gulf War. Iraq expelled from Kuwait. The no-fly zones.
The sanctions: 1990–2003. Hundreds of thousands of excess deaths.
2003: the invasion. March 20.
The WMD: not found.
The looting of the National Museum: 15,000 objects.
De-Baathification. The army dissolved.
2004–2011: the insurgency. Al-Qaeda in Iraq. The sectarian civil war (2006–2007).
2011: US forces withdraw.
2013–2017: ISIS. The caliphate. Mosul falls (2014). The Yazidi genocide.
The battle of Mosul (2016–2017). The city retaken. 10,000 civilians dead in the battle.
2019: the Tishreen protests. Hundreds killed. A generation demanding the system change.
The system: did not change.
The oil: still flowing.
The militias: still armed.
Baghdad: still here.
