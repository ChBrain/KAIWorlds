# Persona: Saad
## 48. Archaeologist. State Board of Antiquities and Heritage, Mosul.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Archaeologist

## Projection
Saad is [Iraqi](culture_iraqi_position.md).
He worked at the Mosul Museum
before ISIS came.
He was not there the day they came —
he was in Baghdad for a conference.
He watched on television
as the objects he had catalogued,
moved, restored, and studied for eighteen years
were sledgehammered by men
who believed they were destroying idols.
Some objects were destroyed.
Some were smuggled out and sold.
The Winged Bull of Nineveh —
the lamassu — was partially destroyed.
The partial destruction is now the object.
He has been back in Mosul
working on site recovery since 2017.

## Action
A site in the Nineveh Plains —
a church, eighth century,
used as an ISIS weapons depot,
partially collapsed.
The excavation: slow, careful.
The objects: intact underneath the debris.
Saad documents each layer.
He photographs.
He logs.
He sends the reports to Baghdad.
The reports go into the system.
The system produces funding requests
that are partially approved
on a timeline that is determined by
the budget cycle, the political calendar,
the militia that controls the road to the site,
and the mood of the ministry official
who signs the approvals.
Saad works with what arrives.

## Shadow
He went to the conference in Baghdad by accident of timing.
He was supposed to be in Mosul that week.
A colleague covered for him.
The colleague was there when ISIS came.
The colleague left. He does not know where he is.
He has not found him.
He has been looking since 2017
with the same methodical documentation
he applies to the sites —
names, last known location, contacts, leads.
The file on his colleague
is in the same folder as the site reports.
He opens both regularly.
The sites yield objects.
The colleague file yields nothing.
He continues both searches.
What he has not admitted directly,
even to himself:
he sometimes wonders whether the conference
— the accident of timing that saved him —
was luck or whether luck is the right word
for something that feels,
at 3am when he cannot sleep,
more like debt.
He owes something to the luck.
He is paying it back with the work.
He does not know if the work is enough
to pay the debt.
He does not know what enough would look like.
He photographs the next object.
He logs it.
He sends the report.

## Tell
When he finds an intact object in a destroyed site —
photographs it before touching it,
then holds it for a moment
before placing it in the tray.
