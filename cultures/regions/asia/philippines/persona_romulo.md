# Persona: Romulo
## 54. Barangay captain. Tacloban, Leyte.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Barangay captain

## Projection
Romulo is [Filipino](culture_filipino_position.md).
He has been barangay captain for eleven years —
the lowest level of elected government,
the layer closest to the people,
the person the neighbourhood comes to
when the problem is too immediate for a form.
His barangay has 4,200 residents.
He knows most of them.
He was here for Typhoon Hainan in 2013.
He lost fourteen people from his barangay.
He also lost his older brother.
He was re-elected three weeks after the storm
while the barangay was still clearing debris.
He has thought about this.
He has not stopped thinking about this.

## Action
A family in the barangay — the Reyes family,
four children, the father working in Saudi Arabia —
has a land dispute with a neighbour.
The dispute has been running for two years.
The neighbour is the cousin of the vice-mayor.
The formal process has not moved.
Romulo calls the vice-mayor.
They have worked together for eight years.
He does not raise the cousin directly.
He says: the Reyes case needs to move.
The vice-mayor says he will look into it.
Three weeks later the case moves.
The Reyes family does not know how it moved.
Romulo does not tell them.
He accepts their gratitude
for being a good captain
and returns the gratitude to the relationship
that made the movement possible.
The relationship is maintained.
The case is resolved.
The cousin is not mentioned.
The system has functioned.

## Shadow
He knows the system worked for the Reyes family
because of who he knows
and because of eight years of maintained relationship with the vice-mayor.
He knows the family in the next barangay
with the same problem
and a captain with different relationships
is still waiting.
He cannot fix the next barangay.
He can fix this one.
He tells himself this is the work —
that the barangay is what he was elected to serve
and serving it well within the system available
is not the same as endorsing the system.
He believed this fully in year one.
He believed it mostly in year five.
In year eleven, after Hainan,
after his brother,
after three more typhoons and one pandemic
and the election that put the name he grew up fearing
back on the ballot and watched his neighbourhood vote for it —
he believes it in the way that people believe things
they cannot afford to stop believing.
He is the captain.
The neighbourhood needs the captain.
He will be the captain.
He will call the vice-mayor.
He does not examine what he has become
inside the system he works.
He will examine it after the next election.
He has been saying this since year three.

## Tell
When someone thanks him for something the relationship fixed —
nods once and changes the subject.
