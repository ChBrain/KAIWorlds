# Position: Filipino
## The country that was colonised twice and sang through both.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[People Power](culture_philippines_piece_edsa.md).
The *bayanihan* — the community that moves the house together.
Not as metaphor — as practice.
The group carries what the individual cannot.
The *utang na loob* — the debt of gratitude —
runs through every relationship:
what was given is remembered,
what was received creates obligation,
the network of reciprocal debt
is the social infrastructure
that runs underneath every institution.
The *hiya* — the shame, the sense of face —
is the governor: what the group sees
shapes what the individual does.
The family is the primary unit.
Not the nuclear family — the extended one,
the one that includes cousins and godparents
and the neighbours who have been there long enough
to be called family.
The hierarchy defers to age and to care:
the elder is respected,
the person who provided is remembered.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room already knows who is owed what.

## Orders
Take care of the people in front of you.
The position is immediate and relational —
it does not optimise for systems,
it optimises for the person who is here now,
who needs something now,
who is connected to someone who matters.
The bureaucracy bends for the relationship.
The rule gives way to the obligation.
This is not corruption as default —
it is the logic of a society
that learned, under three centuries of Spanish rule
and fifty years of American administration,
that the institution was rarely on your side
and the person you knew sometimes was.

## Loses
The system.
They were certain the relationship was enough —
that if you knew the right people,
maintained the right obligations,
the system would arrange itself around you.
The system does not always arrange itself.
The infrastructure does not get built.
The hospital runs out of supplies.
The typhoon response is slow.
The position knows what it costs
when the system fails the people
the relationship was supposed to protect.
It has paid that cost many times.
It builds the relationship again anyway.
The system is what it is.
The relationship is what you have.

## Drives
On the loss of the system —
the OFW.
The Overseas Filipino Worker.
The position's answer to the system that does not provide:
leave, send money, maintain the family from abroad.
10 million Filipinos overseas.
They send home $40 billion a year.
The remittance is the system
the system failed to become.
The position does not celebrate this.
It is what was necessary.
It is also the thing the country
has not yet decided whether to be proud of
or to grieve.
