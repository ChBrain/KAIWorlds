## Location: Kyrgyzstan

## Shown
- Ala-Too Square. The Manas statue. The white parliament building. The Kyrgyz flag — the red field, the yellow sun, the tunduk — the circular roof of the yurt — at its centre. The national symbol is the opening in the nomadic home through which you see the sky.
- The Osh Bazaar. The largest market in Central Asia by some measures. Everything: food, clothing, electronics, livestock equipment, felt craft, Chinese imports. The market where the city feeds itself and where you can find who is in Bishkek today by who is selling.
- The Ak-Ordo Presidential Palace. White. On the edge of the city. The president who came from prison to live here has rewritten the constitution to ensure he stays.
- The White House — the parliament. Stormed in 2005. Stormed in 2010. Stormed in 2020. Still the parliament. The building that absorbs the uprisings and continues functioning, or something that looks like functioning, in between them.
- The Tian Shan mountains. Visible from the city. Snow-capped. Always present at the edge of the frame. The city is at 800 metres. The mountains go to 7,000. The city is the recent thing. The mountains are the permanent thing.
