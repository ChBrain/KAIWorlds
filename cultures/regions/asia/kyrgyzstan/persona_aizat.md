# Persona: Aizat
## 31. Felt craft cooperative manager. Kochkor village, Naryn province.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Felt craft cooperative manager

## Projection
Aizat is [Kyrgyzstani](culture_kyrgyzstani_position.md).
She manages a cooperative of fourteen women
in Kochkor — a village on the road to Song-Köl lake,
one of the stops on the tourist route
through the Tian Shan.
The cooperative makes felt — *shyrdak*, *ala-kiyiz* —
the traditional Kyrgyz craft
of cut and stitched felt in geometric patterns.
The patterns carry meaning:
the ram's horns, the owl's feathers,
the running dog — each design from the steppe,
from the herder's world,
from the repertoire her grandmother knew
and her grandmother's grandmother knew.
She has a degree in economics from Bishkek.
She came back to Kochkor three years ago.

## Action
A German tour group visits the cooperative.
They watch the women work.
They ask questions through a guide.
They buy pieces — a wall hanging, a cushion cover, a small rug.
The average sale: 3,500 som.
The cooperative earns more in this hour
than in a week of non-tourist production.
Aizat manages the visit with the fluency of someone
who has done it many times —
the welcome, the demonstration, the story of the patterns,
the pricing that reflects quality without alarming the budget tourist.
After the group leaves she counts the receipts.
She distributes the earnings.
She records everything in the cooperative's accounts.
She calls the next group's tour operator to confirm the date.

## Shadow
She knows what the cooperative is —
it is a craft production unit
that has been partially restructured
around the tourist visit.
The pieces made for tourists
are mostly the pieces tourists expect:
the bold colours, the large patterns,
the designs that photograph well.
The more intricate, more traditional work —
the smaller patterns, the regional variations,
the pieces her grandmother's generation made —
takes longer and sells for less
because tourists cannot see the difference.
She is making the craft economically viable
by making it legible to outsiders.
She is also, in making it legible,
narrowing it.
The fourteen women in the cooperative
are making more money than before.
They are also making fewer of the pieces
that only people who know felt craft
can fully appreciate.
Aizat knows both things.
She came back from Bishkek
because she believed the craft was worth preserving.
She is preserving it
in the form that can be sold.
She is not certain those are the same thing.
She has not found a version of the cooperative
that preserves it in both forms simultaneously
and keeps the fourteen women employed.
She records the receipts.
She calls the next group.
The pattern continues.
She is not sure which pattern she means.

## Tell
When she shows a tourist a piece —
describes the pattern's name
before describing its price.
