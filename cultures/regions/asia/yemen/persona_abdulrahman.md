# Persona: Abdulrahman
## 43. Humanitarian logistics coordinator. WFP, Hodeidah.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Humanitarian logistics coordinator

## Projection
Abdulrahman is [Yemeni](culture_yemeni_position.md).
He coordinates the movement of food aid
through Hodeidah port — the port
through which 70% of Yemen's food imports pass,
which was blockaded, partially lifted,
contested, damaged, partially repaired,
and is currently functioning
at reduced but operational capacity.
He works for the World Food Programme.
He has a degree in civil engineering
from Sanaa University, class of 2005.
He was supposed to build bridges.
He has spent twelve years
moving food through a port
that is also a military objective.

## Action
A shipment of wheat — 40,000 metric tons,
enough to feed 2 million people for a month —
is delayed at the port.
The delay: a documentation dispute
between the Houthi port authority
and the WFP's procurement office
over the inspection certification.
Abdulrahman knows the certification is valid.
He also knows that resolving the dispute
requires calling a Houthi official
who controls the port
and who will ask for something
in exchange for expediting the process.
He makes the call.
He does not record what was asked.
He does not record what was offered.
The shipment moves within 48 hours.
2 million people will eat this month
who were uncertain before the call.
He files the logistics report.
The delay is noted as: documentation resolved.

## Shadow
He has been making these calls for eight years.
He knows what the calls cost —
not in money, in what the calls are.
The Houthi official controls the port
because the Houthis control the city
because the war produced the Houthis
as the power and the WFP
must work through the power
or the food does not move.
He is feeding people
by working with the people
who are part of the reason
the people need to be fed.
This is the logic of humanitarian operations
in conflict zones.
He knows the logic.
He uses the logic.
He has never found a way
to use the logic
that does not also sustain
some part of what the logic requires him to sustain.
The 40,000 tons move.
2 million people eat.
He files the report.
He makes the next call.
He has not stopped making the calls.
He has also not stopped knowing
what the calls are.
Both continue.

## Tell
When a shipment clears after a difficult negotiation —
checks the delivery confirmation
before updating the logistics report,
in the order that puts the food first.
