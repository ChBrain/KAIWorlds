# Position: Yemeni
## The oldest civilisation on the Arabian Peninsula — and the country the world decided it could ignore.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The War That Didn't End](culture_yemen_piece_war.md).
The *qat* afternoon.
Every day, after noon,
the men gather — in homes, in shops, in the shade —
and chew *qat*, the mild stimulant leaf,
and talk.
The *qat* session is not leisure.
It is the parliament that functions
when the parliament does not —
the space where decisions are made,
disputes are mediated,
alliances are formed.
The *qat* is also 40% of the water usage
in a country that is running out of water.
Both are true of the same leaf.
The tribal structure — the *qabila* —
is the primary loyalty.
The north and the south
are different countries in memory if not in law —
the Yemen Arab Republic and the People's Democratic Republic of Yemen
were unified in 1990 and have been fracturing since.
The Zaydi Shia of the north.
The Sunni majority of the south.
The Houthi movement that emerged
from the Zaydi revival
and became a military force
and controls Sanaa since 2014.
The internationally recognised government
in Aden, backed by the Saudi coalition.
The Southern Transitional Council
that wants independence.
And the ordinary Yemeni
who is hungry
and wants to know when this ends.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room knows which Yemen
and which faction
before it knows anything else.

## Orders
Endure.
The position has endured
the Romans, the Ethiopians, the Ottomans,
the British in Aden,
the Cold War proxy conflict,
the unification that was more a takeover,
and the current war
that has been running since 2015.
The endurance is not passive.
The *qabila* holds when the state does not.
The extended family feeds the family
the state cannot feed.
The remittances from the diaspora —
from Saudi Arabia, from the US,
from wherever the Yemeni went
when the war made staying impossible —
arrive and are distributed
through networks the war has not disrupted
because the networks are older than the war.

## Loses
The children.
The war has produced
the worst humanitarian crisis in the world
by the UN's own measure —
21 million people needing humanitarian assistance,
half of them children,
malnourishment in numbers
that the position cannot absorb
as statistics
because the statistics are children
in villages the position
has always fed its children in.
The position has been losing children
since 2015.
It has not stopped counting.

## Drives
On the loss of the children —
continues.
The position does not have a better option.
The ceasefire that holds in some places.
The humanitarian corridors that function
some of the time.
The *qat* session in the afternoon
where the men argue about what to do next.
The position continues
because stopping is not available
as a choice.
The ancient terraced farms of the highlands —
built over two thousand years,
irrigated by the monsoon —
are still being farmed
in the breaks between the fighting.
The terraces were here before the war.
The position intends them to be here after.
It does not know when after is.
It farms the terraces.
It waits.
