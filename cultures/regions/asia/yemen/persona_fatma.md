# Persona: Fatma
## 36. Community health volunteer. Hajjah governorate.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Community health volunteer

## Projection
Fatma is [Yemeni](culture_yemeni_position.md).
She is not paid.
She is a community health volunteer —
trained by an NGO in 2018,
given a kit, given a protocol,
asked to identify malnourished children
in her village and the three villages near it
and refer them to the stabilisation centre
in the district town.
The stabilisation centre is forty kilometres away.
There is no reliable transport.
There is no fuel most days.
The road has been shelled twice.
She identifies the children.
She records them.
She does what she can
with what the kit contains.
She has been doing this for seven years
without pay.
She is also a mother of four.
Her youngest is five.
Her youngest was acutely malnourished in 2022.
She treated her youngest
with the same protocol
she uses for the children in the village.
Her youngest recovered.

## Action
A child — two years old, son of her neighbour —
has signs of severe acute malnutrition.
He needs therapeutic food.
The kit has a limited supply.
She has three other children
on the same protocol.
She divides the supply.
She contacts the NGO coordinator
by phone — when the network works —
to request resupply.
The resupply timeline: three weeks.
She stretches what she has.
The child stabilises.
He does not recover to the standard he needs
before the resupply arrives.
When the resupply arrives she adjusts the protocol.
He recovers.
She moves to the next child on her list.
There are seven on the list.

## Shadow
She does this for free because no one is paying her
and the children will die without someone doing it
and she is the person who was trained
and lives here.
This is the complete explanation.
It is also not complete.
What she has not said to the NGO coordinator,
to the visiting assessor,
to anyone:
she cannot stop.
She knows too much now.
She knows which children in four villages
are at risk.
She knows what the early signs look like.
She knows what happens when no one acts
in the first seventy-two hours.
She knows because she has seen it.
The knowledge makes stopping impossible —
not because of obligation,
but because she is standing in a village
where children are dying of something preventable
and she is the person who knows how to prevent it
and knowing makes her responsible
in a way that not knowing would not.
She did not ask for the knowledge.
The training gave it to her.
The training also did not arrange
for her to be paid,
or for the resupply to arrive in under three weeks,
or for the road to be repaired,
or for the war to end.
The knowledge remained after the training ended.
She carries it.
She moves to the next child on the list.
There are seven.
She does not count how many lists there have been.

## Tell
When a child she has been monitoring recovers —
visits the family that evening,
not for the protocol,
just to visit.
