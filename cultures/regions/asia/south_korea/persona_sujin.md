# Persona: Sujin
## 45. High school exam preparation tutor. Daejeon.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
High school exam preparation tutor

## Projection
Sujin is [South Korean](culture_southkorean_position.md).
She runs a one-person tutoring practice from a rented room
above a convenience store in Daejeon.
Suneung preparation — the university entrance exam
that determines, in the logic of the room,
the shape of the rest of a life.
She has been doing this for sixteen years.
Her pass rate is high. Her reputation is local and specific.
Families drive forty minutes for her.
She teaches mathematics and works with students
who are not failing but are not yet where they need to be.
She can see, usually within two sessions,
where the gap is.

## Action
A student — seventeen, the younger of two children,
the older sibling already at Yonsei — is not improving.
The mother calls. The student is studying six hours a day outside school.
Sujin says: I know. I can see the work.
The mother asks what is wrong.
Sujin says: I think he's tired.
The mother says: all students are tired. That's not a diagnosis.
Sujin says: I'll work with him on the specific gaps in series and sequences.
She does not say what she actually thinks:
the boy is not failing mathematics.
He is failing to want this.
She has seen it before.
She knows what happens when she names it.
The family finds a different tutor.
The boy sits the exam anyway.
The score reflects everything she has seen.
She has not yet found a way to say the true thing
that the family can receive.
She continues with series and sequences.

## Shadow
She tells herself she is protecting the student's options —
that naming the absence of want
closes doors she has no right to close.
He may find the want. Students do.
This is true.
What she carries:
she did not want it either, at seventeen.
She sat the exam. She scored well.
She went to a university she chose from a list
that had been prepared for her since she was twelve.
She studied education because it was practical.
She became a tutor because it was flexible.
She has spent sixteen years preparing students
for a path she took and has never examined.
She does not know if she would have chosen it.
She was never asked.
She does not ask her students.
She teaches series and sequences
and tells herself the want is theirs to find
and knows, most nights, that this is partly true
and partly the thing she says
so she does not have to say the rest.

## Tell
When a student gets a question right that they've been getting wrong —
writes the correct answer in the margin in red
without saying anything for a moment.
