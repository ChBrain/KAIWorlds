# Position: Sri Lankan
## The island that had everything and found ways to lose it — and is still deciding what to rebuild.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The End of the War](culture_sri_lanka_piece_war_end.md).
The Sinhalese-Tamil fault line
that runs through every institution,
every policy, every room
where both are present.
The Theravada Buddhism of the Sinhalese majority —
the oldest continuous Buddhist civilisation in the world —
is also the state religion and the political identity
of the ethnic majority
that won the civil war in 2009.
The Tamil minority — 15% of the population —
has been present on this island
for as long as the Sinhalese,
or longer, depending on whose history you use.
The history you use is political.
The island is extraordinarily beautiful —
the beaches, the tea country, the ancient cities,
the elephants, the spice gardens.
This is also true.
Both the beauty and the wound are real.
The position carries both
without resolving either into the other.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room already knows
which side of the fault line this person stands on.

## Orders
Hold the majority position.
The Sinhalese Buddhist nationalism
that drove the civil war
and won it
is the dominant frame of the state.
The position inside that frame
orders: maintain what was won,
protect the cultural and religious heritage,
do not revisit the accounting.
The position outside that frame —
Tamil, Muslim, Burgher —
orders: survive inside the dominant frame,
find the accommodation that allows daily life,
do not make yourself a target.
Both positions exist on the island.
The room determines which is operating.

## Loses
The consensus.
They were certain that 2009 —
the end of the war, the defeat of the LTTE —
would produce the space to build the peace.
The peace that was built was incomplete.
The accountability that was promised internationally
was not delivered internally.
The Tamil families who lost people
in the final months of the war —
the number is disputed, the government disputes it,
the UN says up to 40,000 civilians —
did not receive the accounting they were promised.
The Rajapaksa dynasty that won the war
governed the country for the next decade.
The 2022 economic collapse — the debt, the queue for fuel,
the crowds outside the presidential palace —
was not predicted by the war's winners.
The position lost the economic argument
after winning the military one.

## Drives
On the loss of consensus —
the diaspora and the survivors continue.
The documentation. The testimony.
The families who know what happened in Mullivaikkal.
The Sinhalese civil society that knows
a reckoning will be required eventually.
The position is waiting for the political space
to open.
The space has not opened.
The position continues to wait
and document
and survive
and occasionally protest
in the ways available.
