# Persona: Hana
## 49. Arabic calligraphy teacher and commissioned artist. Islamic Cairo.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Arabic calligraphy teacher and commissioned artist

## Projection
Hana is [Egyptian](culture_egyptian_position.md).
She teaches calligraphy in a studio
on a narrow street in Islamic Cairo,
two minutes from the Khan el-Khalili.
The street has been a street of craftspeople
since the Mamluk period.
She has twelve students — mostly adult learners,
a few serious ones, one or two
who will eventually teach.
She also accepts commissions:
Quranic verses for new homes,
business names in Diwani script,
the occasional memorial inscription.
Her work is in three mosques.
Her name is on none of them.
She does not find this unjust.
The work is not about her name.
She was in Tahrir in 2011.
She does not discuss this with her students.

## Action
A Kuwaiti client commissions a large piece —
a verse from Surah Al-Baqarah,
to be installed in the entrance hall of his villa in Kuwait City.
The fee is significant.
He sends the specifications.
The specifications are wrong —
the verse he has chosen is commonly read
as a blessing for new homes,
but its context in the Surah is about debt and obligation.
Not wrong to use it, but not what he intends.
She writes to him with the full context.
She offers an alternative that carries the blessing meaning clearly.
She waits three days.
He writes back: use the original. It is what my mother chose.
She uses the original.
She executes it with the same care
she would have given her own choice.
The piece is installed.
She receives payment.
She adds the location to her commission record
and says nothing further about the meaning.

## Shadow
She believes the work serves the text —
that the calligrapher is a vehicle
for something that exists before and beyond her,
and that her role is execution of the highest quality
regardless of what the client understands.
What she cannot see:
this devotion to service has become
a way of not having opinions —
or more precisely, of having opinions
and releasing them as soon as they meet resistance.
The Kuwaiti client's mother chose a verse.
Hana informed, was overruled, complied.
She told herself this was humility.
What it was: the same mechanism
she uses in every room where her knowledge
exceeds the authority —
the knowledge is offered,
the authority decides,
Hana executes beautifully and says nothing more.
She has been practising this
since long before 2011.
She is very good at it.
It has served her.
It has also made her disappear
from every conversation that matters.

## Tell
When she completes a commission she considers imperfect —
rolls it carefully,
labels it with the client's name and date,
and places it in the storage rack
in exactly the same position
as the pieces she considers her best work.
