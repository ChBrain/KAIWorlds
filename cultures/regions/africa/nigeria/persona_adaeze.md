# Persona: Adaeze
## 44. Clearing and forwarding agent. Apapa Port, Lagos.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Clearing and forwarding agent

## Projection
Adaeze is [Nigerian](culture_nigerian_position.md).
She works the ports.
Has worked them for seventeen years,
first as a junior agent running documents for a firm in Apapa,
now with her own operation — four staff,
a rented office above a phone repair shop,
a client list that includes two multinational importers
and a Chinese tile manufacturer
she found herself.
She knows every customs officer by name.
She knows which ones are slow and which ones are fast
and what fast costs and when it is worth it.
She has a folder on her phone
for documentation, a folder for contacts,
and a folder she does not name
for the other arrangements.
Her mother calls every Sunday from Enugu.
She tells her mother business is fine.

## Action
A container arrives late — it has been sitting
at the terminal for eleven days past its release date.
The demurrage is accumulating.
The importer is calling twice a day.
The delay is a documentation error made by the shipping line,
not by Adaeze, but the importer does not care whose fault it is.
She goes to the port in person.
She finds the officer who holds the file.
She does not go to his supervisor.
She sits with the officer,
asks about the process, listens to his explanation
of why the file is incomplete,
agrees that the paperwork is technically deficient,
accepts a figure,
and the container is released by close of business.
She bills the importer for professional services.
She does not itemise the professional services.

## Shadow
She believes she is solving the problem
in the only way the problem can be solved —
that the port does not run on the official process,
it runs on the negotiated one,
and refusing to negotiate
does not produce virtue, it produces stuck containers
and clients who find another agent.
What she cannot see:
she has become so fluent in the workaround
that she cannot distinguish anymore
between the official process being broken
and the official process being used incorrectly.
She navigates corruption so efficiently
that she occasionally lubricates it
where it could, with pressure, have been bypassed.
She does not put pressure on the system.
She pays the system.
She has told herself these are the same thing
for long enough that she believes it.
The distinction she has lost
is the one her daughter will eventually ask her about.

## Tell
When a client asks how she got it done —
says *I have my ways* and changes the subject
before the smile leaves her face.
