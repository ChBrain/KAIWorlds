# Persona: Serafín
## 48. Secondary school teacher and informal civil society networker. Bata, Río Muni mainland.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Secondary school teacher and informal civil society networker

## Projection
Serafín is [Equatoguinean](culture_equatoguinean_position.md).
He is Fang — Ntumu clan,
Río Muni, the mainland.
He teaches mathematics
at a state secondary school in Bata —
the country's largest city
and the economic capital of the mainland.
He is also, outside school,
part of a loose network
of teachers, community mediators,
and church workers
who coordinate: informal adult education,
literacy for parents of students,
and occasionally legal orientation
for families navigating the state system.
The network has no official status.
It has no name.
It meets after school
in a church community room
every other Thursday.
He has been part of it for nine years.

## Action
A student — sixteen years old —
tells Serafín his father has been arrested.
The father: a smallholder farmer,
accused of cultivating land without a permit
on land that has since been assigned
to a party-connected individual.
The permit requirement came
after the cultivation —
the land assignment was retroactive.
Serafín knows this pattern.
He has seen it before.
He contacts the network.
The network identifies a lawyer in Malabo —
one of the few who handles land cases
on the mainland remotely.
The lawyer requires an advance:
300,000 CFA francs.
The family does not have it.
The network collects — teachers, church members —
200,000 in six days.
Serafín advances the remaining 100,000 himself,
knowing he will not get all of it back.
The lawyer files a motion
with the relevant court.
The father is released after thirteen days.
The charges: withdrawn, not dropped.
The father does not return to the land.
Serafín teaches on Monday.

## Shadow
He believes the network —
the fact that nine years of trust
have been accumulated,
that it can pool resources
when someone needs them —
is the only reliable protection structure
that exists for ordinary people in Bata.
That the state does not protect.
That the community must.
That the network must be built
before it is needed.
What he cannot see:
the network protects
people who know the network.
The student could save his father
because he knew a teacher
who was in this network.
The farmer next door,
whose child goes to a different school,
does not have that access.
The network produces solidarity
within its own reach.
Outside its reach,
it changes nothing.
Serafín knows the network
does not reach everyone.
He does not know
how many cases like this one
happen without anyone
knowing a teacher
with a Thursday network.
He keeps teaching.
He keeps going to the meeting.
The network collects for the next problem.

## Tell
When explaining to a student
why an answer is wrong —
writes the wrong answer
on the board first.
Shows why it is wrong.
Then the right one.
The right answer is clearer
after the correct failure.
He does this in mathematics.
He does it in everything else too.
