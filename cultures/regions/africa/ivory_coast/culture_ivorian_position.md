# Position: Ivorian
## The miracle and its price — the country built on openness that invented a word to close it.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[Ivoirité and the Two Civil Wars](culture_ivorian_piece_ivoirite.md).
The *miracle ivoirien* —
the 1960s and 1970s economic boom
built on cocoa and coffee,
on Houphouët-Boigny's openness to immigrants,
on the French technicians who stayed after independence,
on the pragmatism of a president
who chose prosperity over ideology
and made it work for twenty years.
Then: the crash of the 1980s.
Then: Houphouët-Boigny dead in 1993.
Then: *ivoirité* — the word invented to ask
who was a *real* Ivorian.
The word that became a civil war.
Two civil wars.
The collectivism: moderate by West African standards —
the miracle required immigrants,
required a broader community,
required the northern Muslim
to build the same country as the southern Christian.
The hierarchy is real — PDI high.
The chief, the elder, the boss.
But the *will'a* — the chieftaincy — is less dominant
than in neighbouring countries.
The urban bourgeoisie of Abidjan
is the most French-inflected
in Francophone Africa.
The business culture is sophisticated.
The country has the largest economy in West Africa
by some measures.
It also had a decade of civil war.
Both are the country.

## Orders
*L'Ivoirien se débrouille* — the Ivorian makes do.
The specific Abidjan pragmatism:
Houphouët-Boigny said the land belongs to those who work it.
This was a policy.
It became a culture.
The migrant who cleared the forest and planted cocoa
was as Ivorian as the man whose grandfather did the same.
For thirty years this worked.
The crisis revealed that it had not been resolved,
only managed.
The position navigates the question
of who belongs
with a care that reflects
how destructive the question became.

## Loses
The openness.
The country that was defined by inclusion —
60 ethnic groups, significant immigrant populations,
the most diverse workforce in West Africa —
invented a concept of exclusion
that sent the country to war.
*Ivoirité* cost 3,000 lives in the first civil war.
The second civil war — 2010–11 — cost 3,000 more.
The word was retired.
The president who invented it died in exile.
But the question the word answered —
*who is Ivorian?* — is still present.
The North-South divide, the Muslim-Christian geography,
the immigrant question.
The position carries
the specific cost of a word
that told people their neighbours
might not be Ivorian enough.

## Drives
On the loss —
*on avance* — we move forward.
The economy rebuilt.
Abidjan grew after the civil wars.
The commercial capital of Francophone Africa.
The Ivorian cocoa — 40% of the world's supply —
still feeding chocolate factories worldwide.
The tech sector: Côte d'Ivoire as a fintech hub.
The music: Coupé-Décalé,
the distinctly Ivorian dance music
that came out of the civil war era —
excess as survival, excess as joy.
The position does not stop.
It cannot afford to stop.
The country has too much to rebuild
and too much to lose.
