# Position: Angolan
## The liberation movement that became the state — fifty years of MPLA, from Marxism to oil kleptocracy to cautious reform.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The 27-Year Civil War and the MPLA's Half-Century](culture_angolan_piece_civil_war.md).
Luanda. The *musseques* —
the shanty towns on the city's periphery
that house most of its six million people.
The oil platforms offshore.
The Gulf of Guinea.
The Ambundu in the northwest — Luanda's ethnic base,
the MPLA's original constituency.
The Ovimbundu in the central plateau —
UNITA's base, the largest ethnic group, 37%.
The Bakongo in the north — 13%.
The Lunda-Chokwe in the east.
Portuguese as the official language —
more universally spoken in Angola
than in almost any other African country.
Portuguese colonialism was 500 years.
The language stuck.
The Creole population of Luanda,
the *mestiços* — the mixed-race urban class —
who were central to MPLA's leadership
and whose complex relationship
with Angolan identity
runs through every account of the liberation.
The collectivism: high — the family,
the *kandengues* (neighbourhood networks).
The hierarchy: high and resented —
the MPLA's vertical authority
is real and experienced as real.

## Orders
*Desenrascanço* — the art of improvised problem-solving.
Borrowed from Portuguese.
Fully Angolan in its practice.
The country that rebuilt after 27 years of war —
the landmines still being cleared,
the road network still incomplete —
learned to make do.
The informal economy of Luanda
is one of the largest in sub-Saharan Africa.
The formal economy is oil.
The informal economy is everything else.
The *desenrascanço* runs the everything else.

## Loses
The civil war.
500,000 dead.
4 million displaced.
The land mines in the central highlands
that are still being found.
The UNITA fighters absorbed into ZANU-PF's model —
UNITA became a political party in 2002,
winning enough seats in 2017 and 2022
to be a credible opposition for the first time.
Savimbi's body was finally given a proper burial in 2019 —
17 years after his death —
a government concession
to the Ovimbundu memory
it had spent 27 years suppressing.
The position carries the war
the way a country carries a war
it was born into:
present in the landscape,
in the missing men of the central highlands,
in the legs that aren't there.

## Drives
On the loss —
Angolan music. *Kuduro*. *Semba*. *Kizomba*.
Music that came from Luanda's musseques
and went to the world.
The oil revenue — declining now,
peaked in 2012,
still the economy's backbone —
that rebuilt Luanda
into one of Africa's most expensive cities.
The Lourenço reform moment —
since 2017, a cautious anti-corruption push,
Dos Santos's family prosecuted,
a slightly more competitive political space.
Whether it holds: contested.
The position builds.
It has always built on damaged ground.
It knows how.
