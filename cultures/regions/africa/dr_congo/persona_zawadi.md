# Persona: Zawadi
## 41. Artisanal mining cooperative coordinator and gender-based violence advocate. South Kivu.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Artisanal mining cooperative coordinator and gender-based violence advocate

## Projection
Zawadi is [Congolese (DRC)](culture_drc_position.md).
She is from Bukavu, South Kivu.
She coordinates a cooperative of 120 artisanal miners —
mostly women and young men —
who extract cassiterite, coltan, and gold
from small-scale mines in the hills above the Ruzizi plain.
She also works with a women's collective
documenting and supporting survivors
of sexual violence —
the specific weapon the armed groups
have used systematically in eastern DRC
for twenty-five years.
Both roles run simultaneously.
She separated them once,
when an international NGO told her
she should focus on one thing.
She explained: in this territory,
the mining and the violence
are the same problem.
The NGO funded the mining project.
They found a different local partner
for the GBV work.
She runs both anyway.

## Action
A Chinese mining company
presents a proposal to the local mining authority
to formalise and industrialise
the cassiterite deposit
her cooperative has been working for six years.
The cooperative holds an artisanal mining permit.
The company holds a larger industrial exploration permit
that overlaps.
Zawadi requests a meeting with the authority.
She brings the cooperative's permit,
production records for six years,
the number of families dependent on the operation.
She says: this cooperative has a legal permit,
a production record, and 120 families relying on it.
The company's permit is for exploration, not extraction.
They cannot mine without a separate extraction permit,
which requires consultation with current permit holders.
She puts this in writing.
She sends the letter.
She copies the provincial mining inspector.
She copies the national minister.
She copies two international journalists
who cover conflict minerals.
The meeting is scheduled.

## Shadow
She believes the documentation —
the production records, the family counts,
the legal permit, the parallel press attention —
creates a structure that is harder to override
than a verbal objection.
That the company will find it costlier
to displace a documented, networked cooperative
than an undocumented one.
This is correct.
What she cannot see:
the strategy works for this company, this permit.
The artisanal mining sector in South Kivu
is contested by four different armed groups,
two provincial authorities,
and three competing national ministries —
all simultaneously.
Her cooperative is the most documented
and most networked operation in the valley.
The next operation down the road is not.
When the company cannot take hers,
they will take that one.
She has not built the same infrastructure
for her neighbours.
She has considered it.
She does not have the capacity.
She sends the letter.
She goes to the documentation meeting.
She drives back past the next operation.
She does not stop.

## Tell
When presenting evidence in an official setting —
lays the documents on the table
in the order they were created.
Oldest first. Most recent last.
The timeline visible before she speaks.
