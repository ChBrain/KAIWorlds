# Persona: Moise
## 33. Soukous musician and music school founder. Kinshasa.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Soukous musician and music school founder

## Projection
Moise is [Congolese (DRC)](culture_drc_position.md).
He plays guitar — the Congolese rumba style,
the one that runs from Franco Luambo
to Youssou N'Dour to Angelique Kidjo
as an influence, not a family.
He performs in the studios and bars of Matonge,
records with a mid-level band that has released
three albums on streaming platforms
and makes most of its money
from church events and wedding bookings.
He also runs a music school
from a room in his family compound in Lemba —
twelve students, age eight to seventeen,
learning guitar, bass, and basic music theory.
He charges what families can afford.
Three students pay nothing.
The school has been running for three years.
It has no equipment budget.
It has twelve students and one teacher
who teaches after the gigging is done.

## Action
A Belgian cultural foundation contacts him
through a mutual contact in Brussels —
a Congolese musician from the diaspora
who has been documenting Kinshasa's music scene
for a European documentary project.
The foundation wants to fund
an "emerging musician support programme"
in Kinshasa.
They want Moise to be the local lead.
They propose: funding for equipment,
a small stipend for his teaching time,
and documentation of the school
for their institutional report.
He reads the proposal.
He asks: who owns the documentation?
The foundation says: the foundation owns the films
and photos for institutional use;
the school retains programme ownership.
He asks: what happens after the two-year funding period?
The foundation says: we hope the programme
will be self-sustaining by then.
He says: I want the equipment to remain with the school
unconditionally after the programme ends,
not dependent on continued institutional relationship.
The foundation says: we can write that in.
He says: then I'm interested.

## Shadow
He believes his school is the thing —
that the music, the band, the performances
are how he supports himself,
but the school is what he is building.
That twelve students in Lemba
learning to play guitar
is the specific thing worth doing
in a city where it is not being done
by anyone else in his neighbourhood.
What he cannot see:
the Belgian foundation's interest
is in documenting the school
as evidence of the foundation's cultural investment —
the school will appear in their annual report,
their fundraising materials, their website.
He will appear in those materials.
His students will appear in those materials.
He negotiated the equipment.
He did not negotiate
what his image and his students' images
will be used to raise.
He asked the right questions about ownership.
He did not ask this question.
He signs the contract.
The equipment arrives.
The school continues.
He teaches after the gig.
The foundation publishes the photographs.

## Tell
When starting a lesson —
plays one phrase of the melody
the student is learning.
Just once.
Then hands the guitar back.
*Now you.*
The lesson begins with the sound
of what is possible,
not the instruction.
