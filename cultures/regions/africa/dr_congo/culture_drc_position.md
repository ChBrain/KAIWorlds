# Position: Congolese (DRC)
## The richest poor country — and the seventy-one days that determined the next sixty years.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Assassination of Lumumba](culture_drc_piece_lumumba.md).
The river.
The Congo River — the second largest in the world by discharge,
the deepest river on Earth.
It runs through the centre of the country
the way the country's contradictions run through itself:
enormous, generative, difficult.
The DRC is the size of Western Europe.
It has 250 ethnic groups, four national languages,
the world's second largest rainforest,
the greatest mineral wealth per square kilometre
of any country on Earth.
Cobalt. Coltan. Gold. Diamonds. Copper.
The minerals in every smartphone,
every electric vehicle battery,
every semiconductor — they begin here.
The collectivism: high — the *butu*, the village, the clan.
The hierarchy: high — the chief, the elder, the *nganga*.
Lingala runs through Kinshasa like blood.
The music — rumba, soukous, ndombolo —
travels from here to Africa and back
with more authority than any Congolese government.
The music is the country's most reliable institution.

## Orders
*Débrouillardise* — the art of making do.
The Congolese who cannot change the system
develops the intelligence to survive within it.
The informal economy is larger than the formal economy.
The *article 15* — the popular joke:
when the government cannot pay or provide,
it tells people to *se débrouiller* —
figure it out yourselves.
Article 15 of the constitution, in popular culture,
is the article that doesn't exist:
*Fend for yourself.*
The position has elevated survival
from a necessity to a cultural art.

## Loses
The possibility Lumumba represented.
The country was seventy-one days old
when its first Prime Minister was killed.
Seventy-one days.
What followed: Mobutu, thirty-two years.
The *Zaïrianisation* that destroyed the economy.
The *authenticité* that renamed the country
and the people and the cities
while stealing everything.
The first Congo War. The second Congo War.
The eastern DRC crisis that has lasted thirty years.
Five million dead since 1998.
The position carries the specific weight
of a country that has everything
and receives nothing from it —
whose resources are the reason
it has never been allowed to be stable.

## Drives
On the loss —
the music plays.
Franco Luambo, Tabu Ley, Papa Wemba.
The soukous that became the sound of Africa.
The kinship networks — the *mutuelle*, the *likelemba* —
that move money and labour
through channels the state cannot track.
The Congolese church — Catholic, Kimbanguist, evangelical —
providing the social services the state withdraws from.
The diaspora in Brussels, Paris, Montreal, Cape Town,
sending money, sending ideas, sometimes returning.
The position does not wait for the state.
The state has not been reliable enough to wait for.
