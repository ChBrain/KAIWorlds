# Piece: The Bongo Dynasty and the Coup That Kept It
## Fifty-six years of father-and-son rule — ended by the president's cousin, who was also a Bongo.

## Owner
- **Place:** [Libreville](culture_gabonese_place_libreville.md)

## Place
Libreville. August 30, 2023.
The election results announced at midnight:
Ali Bongo re-elected with 64%.
The internet cut. The curfew imposed.
Hours later: military officers on state television.
The results annulled.
The borders closed.
Ali Bongo under house arrest.
His son Noureddin — Eton-educated —
arrested for corruption.
The coup leader: Brice Oligui Nguema.
The Republican Guard commander.
Ali Bongo's cousin.
The dynasty ended from inside the family.

## Load Bearing
Remove the Bongo dynasty
and Gabon has the post-independence trajectory
of a small oil-rich Central African state —
French backing, single-party system,
slow democratisation under external pressure,
uncertain reform.
With it: Gabon has the clearest case study
in Francophone Africa
of the *Françafrique* system —
the French political-business-intelligence network
that maintained client regimes
in exchange for resource access and strategic loyalty.
Omar Bongo was installed by de Gaulle in 1967.
He was thirty-one years old.
He converted to Islam to please Qaddafi,
for aid.
He maintained Elf's concessions
to please Paris,
for military guarantees.
His daughter married Omar Bongo of Congo-Brazzaville.
His family owned 39 properties in France,
70 bank accounts.
Ali inherited the system.
He hired his son as chief adviser.
He manipulated the electoral code
months before the 2023 election —
changing from two-round to single-round,
abolishing term limits,
requiring voters to support presidential
and parliamentary candidates from the same party —
making it structurally impossible
for the opposition to win.
Then announced the results at midnight
with the internet cut.
The military's calculation: this was not an election,
it was an embarrassment,
and the embarrassment was costing them
their own standing internationally.
The coup was a professional decision
by people who had benefited from the dynasty
and decided it had overplayed its hand.
Oligui Nguema is a Bongo cousin.
The coup was the dynasty pruning itself
to survive without the name.
This is the piece's most precise dimension:
the 56 years did not end.
The family connection ended.
The system that created those 56 years —
the French relationship, the oil revenue structure,
the elite network — remains.
The elections expected in 2025–2026
have Oligui Nguema as the expected winner.
He ran the Republican Guard.
He knows where everything is.
The dynasty of persons ended.
The dynasty of arrangements continues.

## Apparent
1960: independence. Léon M'ba first president.
1967: M'ba dying. Omar Bongo appointed vice president.
M'ba dies November 28, 1967.
Bongo becomes president aged 32.
1968: single-party state decreed.
Elf Aquitaine's concessions secured.
French military guarantee maintained.
1990: under Western pressure, multiparty politics introduced.
1993: first competitive election. Bongo wins.
Contested. He stays.
1998, 2005: re-elected.
2007: French police investigation.
39 properties in France. 70 bank accounts.
Case proceeds slowly for fifteen years.
2009: Omar Bongo dies. Spain.
Ali Bongo wins the 2009 election.
Post-election violence: worst in decades.
2016: Ali Bongo re-elected by 5,594 votes.
International observers: serious irregularities.
Parliament set on fire by protesters.
2018: Ali Bongo suffers a stroke in Saudi Arabia.
Not seen publicly for nearly a year.
2019: failed coup attempt while he is rehabilitating.
2021: Ali Bongo's son Noureddin consolidated as power broker.
April 2023: electoral code changed.
Single-round ballot. Term limits abolished.
August 26, 2023: elections.
Internet blocked election day.
August 30, 2023: results announced.
Oligui Nguema launches coup within hours.
Ali Bongo arrested.
Noureddin arrested.
Oligui inaugurated as transitional president.
September 2023: Raymond Ndong Sima — former PM — appointed transitional PM.
November 2024: new constitution approved by referendum. 92%.
April 2025: elections announced. Oligui expected to run.
April 2026: elections ongoing or recently concluded.
The system continues.
Different person at the apex.
