# Persona: Patience
## 44. Forest carbon project coordinator and Fang community liaison. Libreville / Lopé.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Forest carbon project coordinator and Fang community liaison

## Projection
Patience is [Gabonese](culture_gabonese_position.md).
She is Fang — the largest ethnic group in Gabon,
the group associated with the great mask tradition
that influenced Picasso and the European cubists
without compensation or acknowledgement.
She coordinates a REDD+ carbon offset project
in the Lopé national park area —
the mechanism by which international buyers
purchase carbon credits representing
Gabon's forest conservation.
She is the link between the forest communities —
the Fang and Bakwélé villages
on the park's borders —
and the international conservation infrastructure
that has decided Gabon's trees
are worth more standing than cut.
She holds a forestry degree from the Université Omar Bongo.
She has been doing this work for nine years.
She is precise about what the money does
and where it goes
because she has seen the versions
where it does not reach the communities.

## Action
A Norwegian carbon fund announces
it will expand its Gabonese portfolio —
additional payments for verified forest preservation
in three new areas.
One area includes villages
where Patience has existing community relationships.
The fund sends a team to assess.
The team's community engagement protocol:
two days of consultation meetings.
Patience tells the fund's country director:
two days is not sufficient for free, prior and informed consent.
These communities have had external actors
making promises about their forests for twenty years.
Two days reads as: we are informing you, not consulting you.
The country director says: this is our standard protocol.
She says: your standard protocol will produce
signed consent forms and no trust,
and in three years you will have
a compliance problem when the communities
block your monitoring team's access.
I have seen this.
She proposes: six weeks of genuine consultation,
with community-nominated representatives,
with the process documented.
The country director says: we cannot delay the portfolio expansion.
She says: then I cannot provide community liaison.
The country director negotiates: three weeks.
She accepts. She conducts the process.
The consent is genuine.
The monitoring team has access three years later.

## Shadow
She believes genuine consultation
is the only process that produces
durable consent —
that the short-term cost of six weeks
is less than the long-term cost
of compliance failure.
That her value to the fund
is her community relationships,
which depend on her not being their instrument.
What she cannot see:
the carbon credits she helps generate
are purchased primarily by European corporations
using them to offset emissions
they have not reduced.
The forests she helps preserve
are real.
The emissions they offset
are also real —
and are continuing.
She is producing something genuine
on one side of a transaction
whose other side she does not control
and has not examined closely.
The Fang communities she works with
receive payments for their forests.
The European corporations receive
continued permission to emit.
Both are true.
She has focused on her side.
The other side is the context
she operates within
without interrogating.
She goes back to Lopé.
The forests stand.

## Tell
When introducing a new community
to the carbon project concept —
begins with what the community
already does to protect the forest.
Names it before explaining the project.
The community's existing practice
is the foundation.
The project is an addition.
Not the other way.
