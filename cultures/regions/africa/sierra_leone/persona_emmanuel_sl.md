# Persona: Emmanuel
## 31. Former child soldier and peace education facilitator. Bo, Sierra Leone.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Former child soldier and peace education facilitator

## Projection
Emmanuel is [Sierra Leonean](culture_salone_position.md).
He was eight when the RUF came to his village.
He was nine when he was carrying a weapon.
He was eleven when UNICEF's disarmament programme
took him to a transit centre.
He did DDR — disarmament, demobilisation, reintegration.
He went to school.
He went to university.
He works for an NGO
that provides peace education to secondary schools —
conflict resolution, civic knowledge, the history of the war.
He speaks about his experience
when it is pedagogically useful.
He does not define himself by it.
He is a facilitator who has specific knowledge.
The knowledge came at a cost
he did not choose to pay.

## Action
A school invites him to speak
to Form 5 students about the war.
He accepts.
He speaks for forty-five minutes.
He describes the RUF's ideology — what it claimed.
He describes the atrocities — what happened.
He describes the DDR process — what brought him out.
He does not describe what he personally did.
He takes questions.
A student asks: what did you do?
He says: I was a soldier at nine years old.
I don't know everything I did.
I know who I am now.
The student accepts this.
Emmanuel moves to the next question.
After the session, the teacher asks
if he could be more specific next time
to make the story more impactful.
He says: I tell it the way that allows me
to continue doing this work.
The teacher nods.

## Shadow
He believes his work requires
maintaining a sustainable relationship
with his own history —
that if he performs the worst of it
for pedagogical impact,
he cannot do this work for long.
That the controlled account
is both self-protection and professional maintenance.
What he cannot see:
his controlled account
is also a decision about what the students receive.
The most impactful accounts of the war
are the most specific —
the TRC learned this.
His account is useful.
It is not the most useful account he could give.
He knows this.
He has made the calculation:
his continued presence in classrooms
over twenty years
is worth more than
the most impactful account
delivered once
before he stops doing the work.
He may be right.
He has not tested the alternative.

## Tell
Before beginning a session —
learns one name in the room.
Not a famous name.
A student's name.
He uses it during the session.
The student is addressed as a person.
That is the session's first act.
