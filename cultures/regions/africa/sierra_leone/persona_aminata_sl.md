# Persona: Aminata SL
## 44. Krio heritage preservationist and secondary school teacher. Freetown.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Krio heritage preservationist and secondary school teacher

## Projection
Aminata (SL) is [Sierra Leonean](culture_salone_position.md).
She teaches history at a secondary school in Freetown.
She is Krio — descended from the freed slaves
who returned to Sierra Leone in the 18th and 19th centuries.
She also runs a voluntary project
documenting the built heritage of the East End —
the Krio neighbourhood
of wooden Victorian houses,
verandas, gingerbread woodwork,
that constitutes Freetown's most distinctive architecture.
Many of the houses were damaged in the war.
Many have been demolished for concrete replacement.
She photographs, measures, archives.
She teaches her students about the houses.
She is fighting a losing battle
and knows it.

## Action
A developer proposes to demolish three adjacent Krio houses
on Pademba Road —
the most intact section of heritage architecture remaining.
The planning authority approves it.
Aminata submits a heritage objection.
It is processed.
It does not stop the demolition.
She photographs every room before the wrecking crew arrives.
She teaches a class about the houses.
She brings her students to see the demolition.
She says: this is what it looks like
when a community does not protect
what it has.
She does not say: and this is how to stop it.
She has not found a way to stop it.
She is teaching loss as pedagogy.
She has not decided whether this is enough.

## Shadow
She believes documentation is the form of preservation
when physical preservation fails —
that the photographs, the measurements,
the stories she teaches
keep the knowledge alive
even when the wood is gone.
What she cannot see:
she is teaching loss without a theory of prevention.
Her students learn what was there.
They do not learn how to make
the next generation of planners
value what is still there.
The documentation archive is growing.
The houses are shrinking.
She has not joined these two facts
into a strategy.
The photographs are beautiful.
The houses are coming down.

## Tell
When teaching about something
that no longer exists —
shows the photograph first.
Always the image before the words.
