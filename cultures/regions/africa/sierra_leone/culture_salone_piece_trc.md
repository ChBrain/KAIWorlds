# Piece: The War and the TRC
## The country that examined itself honestly — and found corruption at the root.

## Owner
- **Place:** [Freetown](culture_salone_place_freetown.md)

## Place
Freetown. The peninsula.
The mountains behind the city —
green, steep, forested.
The most beautiful capital in West Africa
by some accounts.
The city that was built by freed people
and then had to survive
what was done to it.

## Load Bearing
Remove the 1991–2002 civil war
and Sierra Leone is a small West African state
with diamonds, iron ore, good harbours,
a literate population,
and a fragile democracy.
With it: Sierra Leone is the country
where the amputations — the signature atrocity
of the RUF — became the image
the world associated with African civil war
for a generation.
The TRC that followed
was the most comprehensive self-examination
any post-conflict African society has produced.
The finding: not diamonds. Corruption.
The rot that denied young people dignity.
The rot that Charles Taylor exploited
to fund the RUF through Liberia.
The rot that the TRC named
and the government partially acknowledged
and partially addressed.
The load-bearing element is the honest accounting:
the TRC did not let any side off.
The RUF, the SLA, the CDF —
all found responsible.
The root cause — governmental corruption and exclusion —
named without euphemism.
Remove the TRC: a war is over, the causes are unaddressed.
With it: a society that knows exactly why the war happened
and has the documentary evidence
to prevent itself from forgetting.
Whether it acts on the knowledge
is the ongoing question.

## Apparent
1961: independence.
1967–1968: Siaka Stevens.
Rigged elections. Single-party state.
Alluvial diamond mining controlled by the APC party.
Revenue to patrons, not public services.
1985: Joseph Momoh. Austerity. IMF.
Rice subsidies cut. Student protests.
1990: multi-party constitution promised.
Some exiles in Benghazi with Libyan training.
1991: March 23. RUF invades from Liberia.
Charles Taylor supporting Sankoh.
Foday Sankoh's first attacks.
1991–93: Phase 1. Conventional warfare.
1993–97: guerrilla warfare. Atrocities begin.
Amputations as terror tactic.
Abduction of child soldiers.
1996: Ahmad Tejan Kabbah elected.
1997: AFRC coup. SLA joins RUF.
Junta in Freetown.
1998: ECOMOG restores Kabbah.
January 6, 1999: Operation No Living Thing.
RUF enters Freetown. 6,000 killed.
The worst single atrocity of the war.
July 1999: Lomé Peace Agreement.
Blanket amnesty.
TRC promised.
2000: Sankoh arrested.
British forces intervene.
2002: war officially ends.
2002: TRC begins.
2004: TRC final report. 5,000 pages.
Finding: corruption was the root cause.
The diamonds funded but did not cause the war.
The recommendations: 33 categories.
Reparations: partially implemented.
Accountability: selective.
The Special Court convicts Taylor in 2012.
The TRC report: exists.
Its recommendations: not fully implemented.
Its knowledge: present.
The country: still building.
