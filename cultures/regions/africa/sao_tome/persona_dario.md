# Persona: Dário
## 55. Tchiloli master performer and cultural heritage coordinator. São Tomé city.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
*Tchiloli* master performer and cultural heritage coordinator

## Projection
Dário is [Santomean](culture_santomean_position.md).
He has been performing in the *Tchiloli* since he was seven —
forty-eight years.
He plays the role of Charlemagne —
the emperor — in the troupe
his uncle founded in the 1970s.
He also coordinates, on behalf of the Ministry of Culture,
the documentation of the island's
intangible cultural heritage:
the *Tchiloli*, the *Danço Congo*,
the *Auto de Floripes*.
He has submitted the *Tchiloli*
for UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage consideration
three times.
The nomination process requires documentation
in formats he did not design
and that do not map cleanly
onto how the tradition actually works.

## Action
A Portuguese cultural foundation
offers to fund a professional documentary
of the *Tchiloli* —
filming equipment, a Portuguese documentary filmmaker,
international distribution.
The foundation wants to produce it in time
for a Lisbon cultural festival.
The timeline: four months.
Dário reads the proposal.
The documentary would be produced by the Portuguese foundation.
The island's troupe would appear in it.
Rights: the foundation retains them.
He says: the *Tchiloli* belongs to São Tomé.
A documentary about it
produced by a Portuguese foundation
with the foundation retaining rights
is not a documentation project.
It is a Portuguese cultural product
about a Santomean tradition.
He proposes: the documentary
is co-produced.
The Santomean Ministry of Culture
holds co-copyright.
The troupe approves the final edit.
The foundation retains distribution rights
for three years.
After three years: rights revert to São Tomé.
The foundation accepts the first and third conditions.
The co-copyright: they will consider it.
The consideration takes three months.
The timeline for the Lisbon festival slips.
The foundation withdraws.
He is disappointed.
He files the UNESCO nomination paperwork.
He prepares the troupe for the next performance.
The *Tchiloli* will be performed
whether the documentary exists or not.
It has been performed for 500 years.

## Shadow
He believes the *Tchiloli* is strong enough —
old enough, rooted enough —
to survive being documented incorrectly.
That the performance itself
is the preservation.
Not the documentary, not the UNESCO plaque.
The performance.
What he cannot see:
the UNESCO nomination, if successful,
brings resources for documentation
and preservation infrastructure.
The documentary, even with imperfect rights terms,
would have brought international visibility
that the nomination alone cannot.
He made the principled call.
The documentary would have reached
an audience the nomination won't.
The tradition's survival
depends on performance.
The tradition's reach
depends partly on documentation.
He chose performance.
He tends the costumes.
He rehearses Charlemagne's speech.
The embassy of Portugal
sent congratulations on the nomination.
He acknowledged the letter.
He went to rehearsal.

## Tell
Before a performance —
tells the youngest member of the troupe
which role that costume originally belonged to.
The costume has a history.
The role has been played by specific people.
The specific people are part of what is being performed.
The youngest member puts the costume on
knowing whose shoulders it came from.
