# Position: Santomean
## The island that was the world's largest cocoa producer — built on slavery, destroyed by independence, now reclaiming itself bean by bean.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Roças, the Forros, and the Batepa Massacre](culture_santomean_piece_batepa.md).
São Tomé city. The Gulf of Guinea.
The equator runs just south of the island —
the *Ilhéu das Rolas* sits directly on it.
The island that was uninhabited
until the Portuguese arrived in 1470
and populated it deliberately,
with enslaved Africans and exiled Jewish children.
The *forros* — the Creole descendants
of freed slaves and early settlers,
who formed an indigenous Santomean identity —
the oldest community on the island.
The *serviçais* — the contract workers
brought from Angola, Mozambique, Cape Verde
to work the *roças* after formal slavery ended.
The *angolares* — descendants of escaped slaves
who live in the south of the island,
speaking their own Bantu language.
The *roças* themselves —
the plantation estates
that covered 90% of the cultivated land at independence.
The collectivism: high — the extended family,
the neighbourhood, the *bairro*.
The hierarchy: present but informal —
the *forros* claiming the oldest
Santomean identity;
the post-independence state
having distributed the land
in ways that reshaped those distinctions.

## Orders
The position navigates through *leve-leve* —
the Santomean attitude:
slowly, gently, without urgency.
Not passivity.
The management of scarcity
through calibrated expectation.
The island is small.
The resources are modest.
The *leve-leve* is the cultural technology
for living within those limits
without breaking.

## Loses
The cocoa economy the island built
and then mismanaged into near-collapse.
35,000 tons in 1908 — world's largest producer.
Post-independence nationalisation: 5,000 tons by the 1980s.
The plantation managers left.
The production collapsed.
The price collapsed simultaneously.
The position carries the specific grief
of a country that had mastery —
the world's finest cocoa,
grown here, by people whose ancestors
were brought here to grow it —
and watched the mastery
scatter with the Portuguese managers
whose departure was necessary
but whose absence
was not prepared for.

## Drives
On the loss —
the ethical cocoa.
The island is rebuilding
from a different foundation:
small farms, organic cultivation,
single-origin chocolate
for high-end European markets.
The *forros* and their descendants
controlling their own agricultural destiny
for the first time.
The *Tchiloli* — the theatrical performance tradition
that stages the medieval Portuguese play
*A Tragédia do Marquês de Mântua*
with Santomean actors, costumes, music —
a piece of European theatre
that the island transformed
into something entirely its own.
The position rebuilds.
The cocoa grows.
The *Tchiloli* performs.
