# Persona: Haingo
## 41. Vanilla cooperative manager and fair trade certification coordinator. Sava region, northeast Madagascar.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Vanilla cooperative manager and fair trade certification coordinator

## Projection
Haingo is [Malagasy](culture_malagasy_position.md).
She manages a cooperative of 340 small vanilla farmers
in the Sava region —
the northeastern strip
where 80% of the world's natural vanilla grows.
She coordinates the cooperative's compliance
with two fair trade certification bodies,
manages the relationship with a European buyer,
and maintains the quality standards
that keep the premium price.
She also, one weekend per year,
travels to her home village
for the *famadihana* of her maternal grandmother,
who died in 1997.
These two things —
the vanilla certification spreadsheets
and the ritual rewrapping of her grandmother's remains —
are both part of the same week.
She does not experience a tension between them.

## Action
The European buyer contacts her
with a revised contract proposal.
Price: held. Volume: reduced by 25%.
Reason given: market conditions,
higher competition from synthetic vanilla.
She calculates: a 25% volume reduction
at the current price
removes the income of approximately 85 families
from the cooperative's guaranteed purchasing.
She requests a call.
On the call, she asks:
what is the timeline?
Is the reduction permanent
or a single-season adjustment?
The buyer says: we cannot guarantee beyond this season.
She says: understood.
She presents three alternatives.
First: maintain volume, accept a 5% price reduction.
The cooperative earns the same revenue.
The buyer saves on unit price.
Second: reduce volume as proposed,
but the cooperative sells the removed 25%
on the spot market — she will need
to break the exclusivity clause.
Third: the buyer connects her
with two other European buyers
who might absorb the removed volume
as a secondary supply relationship.
The buyer chooses the third option.
She spends three months
building the secondary buyer relationship.
The cooperative's volume is maintained.
The price is unchanged.
The 85 families are still in the purchase plan.

## Shadow
She believes that the cooperative's position —
premium fair trade vanilla, certified,
traceable to individual farms —
is the correct long-term strategy
for Sava region farmers
against the synthetic vanilla market.
That the premium relationship with European buyers
protects farmers better than the spot market.
That her job is to make that premium
relationship durable.
What she cannot see:
the vanilla market's volatility
is structural.
Vanilla prices swung from $20/kg in 2012
to $600/kg in 2018
back toward $100 by 2022.
No certification or relationship
fully insulates farmers from that swing.
The cooperative provides stability
relative to the spot market —
but stability relative to chaos
is not security.
The 85 families who remained in the purchase plan
this season
are one more market shift
from the same risk.
She maintains the relationship.
She maintains the spreadsheets.
She goes to the *famadihana*.
She comes back.
The vanilla grows.

## Tell
When visiting a farmer's plot —
asks to see where the vanilla flowers
were hand-pollinated this season.
Not the beans. The flowers.
The moment before the product.
The part tourists never see.
The farmer always knows
exactly which vines they pollinated.
The telling is in knowing that.
