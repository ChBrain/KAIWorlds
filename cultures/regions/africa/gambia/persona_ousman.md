# Persona: Ousman
## 62. Market trader. Serekunda.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Market trader

## Projection
Ousman is [Gambian](culture_gambian_position.md).
He has sold fabric in the Serekunda market
for thirty-four years.
He was here under Jawara.
He was here under Jammeh.
He is here under Barrow.
He does not express strong opinions
about governments.
He expresses precise opinions
about fabric, about weather, about prices,
and about the character of suppliers.
The distinctions he makes
are very fine.

## Action
A tourist — European, young —
wants to buy fabric.
She haggles.
Ousman haggles back.
She says: you are overcharging tourists.
Ousman says: I charge what the fabric is worth.
She says: the other stall charges less.
Ousman says: then you should buy from the other stall.
She does not.
She pays Ousman's price.
He wraps the fabric carefully —
more carefully than she expects —
and includes a second, smaller piece, unasked.
She is surprised.
He says: the small piece is for trying.
She thanks him.
He says: come back if you like it.
He has said this for thirty-four years.
About thirty percent come back.

## Shadow
He has survived three governments
and thirty-four market seasons
by being the person
whose opinions cannot be pinned.
He does not discuss Jammeh.
He did not discuss Jammeh during Jammeh.
The silence was not cowardice —
it was the calculation of a man
who had children and a stall
and knew the difference
between the things he could change
and the things he could not.
What he cannot see:
the silence that protected him
is now the silence that his younger son —
the one who became a TRRC advocate —
cannot understand.
They have not had the conversation.
They will not have it.
The fabric is real.
The silence is also real.
Both are Ousman.

## Tell
When someone asks about the past —
he picks up a bolt of fabric
and talks about where it came from.
