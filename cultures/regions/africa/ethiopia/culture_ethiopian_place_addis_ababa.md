## Shown
- Meskel Square. The largest public square in Africa.
  Where the OAU was founded in 1963.
  Where the crowds come for Meskel — the Finding of the True Cross.
  The bonfire lit at the centre.
  The whole city present.
  The same square where the Derg held military parades
  and where Ethiopians now walk their children on Sunday mornings.
  The square holds all of it.
- The African Union headquarters.
  Built here because this was the uncolonised country.
  The glass and steel complex China built and donated in 2012.
  The building that now houses the continent's
  most important diplomatic body
  was built by a foreign power and given as a gift.
  The Ethiopians accepted.
  The irony is noted quietly.
- Merkato. The largest open-air market in Africa.
  Everything. Absolutely everything.
  The market that runs on its own logic,
  its own pricing, its own channels.
  The formal economy and the informal economy
  indistinguishable from each other at street level.
- The Holy Trinity Cathedral.
  Where Haile Selassie is buried.
  Ethiopians still visit.
  Some to mourn. Some to understand.
  Some because he is still the last emperor
  and the position has not decided
  what to do with that.
- The National Museum of Ethiopia.
  Lucy — Dinknesh in Amharic, meaning *you are wonderful* —
  the 3.2 million year old hominid skeleton.
  Found in the Afar region.
  The oldest confirmed human ancestor on display anywhere.
  The room where humanity began
  is also the room that has just emerged
  from a civil war.
  Both facts are in the same building.
