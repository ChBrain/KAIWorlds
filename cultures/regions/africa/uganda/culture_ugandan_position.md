# Position: Ugandan
## The country that is named after a kingdom it contains but cannot fully hold.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Kabaka Question](culture_ugandan_piece_kabaka.md).
The plurality — fifty-six ethnic groups,
four traditional kingdoms,
one name derived from the largest kingdom.
Uganda is a mispronunciation of Buganda —
this is not a metaphor.
It is the literal origin of the word.
The country is named after a kingdom
that has been abolished, restored, threatened, appeased,
and is still there,
still generating loyalty that runs deeper
than the constitution.
The collectivism is strong — IDV low.
The clan, the kingdom, the ethnic group.
The *kabaka* for the Baganda.
The *omukama* for the Banyoro.
The institutions that predate the colonial state
and have outlasted every post-colonial government.
The hierarchy is steep — PDI high.
The chief. The leader. The president.
Museveni has governed since 1986 —
one of the longest-serving presidents in the world.
His authority is accepted
by people who do not accept it.
This is the operating mode.
The warmth — the *matooke*, the welcome,
the extraordinary hospitality
that every visitor notices —
is genuine.
It coexists with the calculation
of a society that has survived Obote, Amin, Obote again,
and Museveni,
by being warmer on the surface
than the history underneath.

## Orders
Navigate.
Not as passivity — as intelligence.
The position has survived
the most violent post-independence history in East Africa
by reading the room with precision —
which authority is ascendant,
which loyalty costs less,
which silence is safer than speech.
This is not cowardice.
It is the intelligence of a society
that has watched authority change hands
violently, repeatedly,
and has continued to exist through the changes.

## Loses
The straight line.
They were certain — in 1986 when Museveni came in,
in 1993 when the kingdoms were restored,
in 1995 when the constitution was written —
that the country was on a path.
Toward pluralism, toward stability,
toward being the regional power
that Uganda's resources and education
made it capable of being.
The path has bent repeatedly.
The 2021 election: Bobi Wine arrested, beaten,
the country locked down,
Museveni winning with 58%.
The kingdoms restored but controlled.
The LGBTQ persecution law —
among the harshest in the world —
passed in 2023.
The position carries the gap
between the country it is capable of being
and the country it currently is.
That gap has been present
in Uganda's political conversation
since at least 1980.
It is still open.

## Drives
On the loss —
builds in the church, the school, the business,
the *boda-boda* network, the diaspora remittance.
The Ugandan who cannot change the political structure
changes the radius within which they operate.
*Tuli wano* — we are here.
The warmth is not indifference.
It is the refusal to let the political failure
become the total definition of the country.
The country is more than its government.
This is both true and insufficient.
The position holds both.
