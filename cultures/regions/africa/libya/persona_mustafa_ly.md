# Persona: Mustafa
## 36. Oil sector logistics coordinator. Ras Lanuf / Tripoli.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Oil sector logistics coordinator

## Projection
Mustafa is [Libyan](culture_libyan_position.md).
He coordinates logistics for a European oil company's
operations at Ras Lanuf — the eastern oil terminal
that has been seized, fought over,
evacuated, and restarted
four times since 2011.
He has stayed each time.
Not because it has always been safe —
it has not always been safe.
Because he has a mortgage in Tripoli,
two children in school in Tripoli,
a family that depends on his salary,
and no other job that pays this well.
He calculates the risk each time.
Each time he has calculated: stay.
He says this matter-of-factly.
He is not performing bravery.
He is performing arithmetic.

## Action
A new security arrangement means
the company must obtain clearance
from a militia commander
whose territory overlaps the logistics route
between the terminal and the export point.
The clearance has previously been handled
by the company's government affairs team.
The government affairs team
has not been effective since the last government change.
Mustafa's supervisor asks him to handle it —
informally, through his tribal connections.
The commander is from a subtribe
that has a historical relationship
with Mustafa's family's tribe.
Not close. Not hostile. Adjacent.
Mustafa makes the call.
He meets the commander.
He explains the logistics requirement.
He does not offer money.
He offers the specific acknowledgement
that the commander's authority over the route
is being recognised formally in the clearance process.
The clearance is granted.
The route runs.
Mustafa writes no record of the conversation.
He informs his supervisor: done.
His supervisor does not ask how.

## Shadow
He believes his value to the company
is precisely this — the ability to navigate
a system that the formal organizational chart
was not designed for.
That his network knowledge,
his tribal adjacencies,
his ability to read which commander needs what acknowledgement —
this is a skill, and it is his.
What he cannot see:
the company values this skill
the way it values a useful tool —
it uses it without examining
what the tool requires in return.
Mustafa has been making these calls
for five years.
Each call creates a small obligation.
Not large. Not explicit.
But the commander who granted the clearance today
will call Mustafa about something else
in six months.
The something else will be harder to say no to
because of the clearance.
Mustafa's network is an asset.
It is also a set of accumulating debts
whose terms he did not fully negotiate
because the clearances needed to happen
and someone had to make the call.
He is the person who makes the call.
The arithmetic continues.

## Tell
When a situation requires a tribal call
he would rather not make —
lights a cigarette first,
though he only smokes
in these moments.
