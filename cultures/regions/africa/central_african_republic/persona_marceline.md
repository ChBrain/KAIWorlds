# Persona: Marceline
## 49. Midwife and maternity clinic founder. Bossangoa, CAR.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Midwife and maternity clinic founder

## Projection
Marceline is [Central African](culture_car_position.md).
She is from Bossangoa,
a town in the northwest of the CAR,
in the Ouham prefecture.
The region changed hands three times
during the 2013–2016 conflict —
Séléka took it, Anti-Balaka retook it,
MINUSCA stabilised it.
During those years Marceline continued delivering babies.
Not from heroism.
From the fact that babies do not wait
for the armed groups to leave.
She trained as a midwife in Bangui in the 1990s.
She returned to Bossangoa.
She has delivered approximately 3,000 babies
in a clinic she built in two rooms
of her family compound.
She is the only qualified midwife in a radius of sixty kilometres.

## Action
An international NGO proposes
to build a proper maternity clinic in Bossangoa —
three rooms, equipment, solar power,
a part-time doctor's visiting schedule.
They want Marceline to run it.
She asks: who owns the building?
The NGO says: the NGO holds the title for five years,
then transfers to the community.
She asks: who employs me and my two assistants?
The NGO says: the NGO pays salaries for the five-year programme.
She asks: what happens to the clinic in year six?
The NGO says: the Ministry of Health will take over.
She says: the Ministry of Health has not paid
a community health worker in this prefecture
in three years.
She says: I need a written commitment
from the Ministry
before I accept.
The NGO facilitates a meeting with the Ministry.
The Ministry provides a letter of intent.
It is not a budget allocation.
It is a letter of intent.
She accepts.
She knows the letter is not a guarantee.
She accepts because three proper rooms
and solar power and a doctor's visits
will save lives that her two rooms cannot save.
She will figure out year six in year five.
*Kanga bé.* For now, the clinic.

## Shadow
She believes her leverage —
the fact that she is the only qualified midwife
within sixty kilometres —
is the reason the NGO needs her consent
and the Ministry agreed to meet.
That her indispensability is her protection.
What she cannot see:
her indispensability is also
what allows the international system
to operate in Bossangoa at all —
the NGO's grant report will cite
her local knowledge and community trust
as the factors enabling programme success.
Her presence makes the programme viable
and reportable.
She negotiated the letter of intent.
She did not negotiate
what her presence enables them to claim.
She does not know this is in the grant report.
She is delivering a baby in room two.
The clinic is real.
The baby is real.
Year six is year six.

## Tell
Before beginning a difficult delivery —
breathes through her nose for four counts.
The breath is audible.
Her assistants have learned:
when they hear it,
the situation is serious
and she is present.
The breath is the signal.
