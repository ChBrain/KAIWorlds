# Place: Conakry
## Location: Guinea

## Owner
- *

## Shown
- The Place des Martyrs.
  Formerly Place du 8 Novembre.
  Renamed after the 2009 stadium massacre.
  The square where Touré spoke in 1958.
  The square where the rally was held in 2009
  when security forces killed 157 people.
  Same square. Two events. Same demand.
  The square holds both
  because there is only one square.
  The city is narrow — built on a peninsula.
  There is not room to avoid your history.
- The Conakry Grand Mosque.
  Built with Saudi money.
  One of the largest in West Africa.
  The call to prayer audible from most of the peninsula.
  Guinea is 85% Muslim.
  Touré suppressed religion —
  he called himself the supreme guide.
  The religion survived.
  The mosque grew.
  This is the Guinean relationship
  to the state: the state claims authority,
  the community maintains its own.
- The port.
  Bauxite.
  The ships loading.
  Guinea has more aluminium ore
  than any other country on earth.
  The ships that carry it
  pass through channels that bring
  the wealth of the ground
  to buyers in China and Europe and America.
  The revenue: significant.
  The roads in Conakry: broken.
  The water: intermittent.
  The hospital equipment: thirty years old.
  The gap between the wealth underground
  and the conditions above it
  is visible from the port.
- Kaloum. The administrative and commercial centre.
  The buildings from the French era,
  some maintained, some decaying.
  The ministries.
  The banks.
  The generator sound that fills every office
  because the national grid
  is not reliable for twelve consecutive hours.
  Conakry generates more power than it receives.
  The rest of the generation goes elsewhere.
  The city that sits on the power station
  buys flashlights.
- Dixinn. The residential neighbourhood.
  Where the professionals live.
  The doctors, the lawyers, the NGO workers.
  The diaspora members who returned.
  The houses with generators.
  The houses with filtered water.
  The specific Guinean middle class
  that formed in exile
  and came back after 1984
  and is trying to build
  what Touré did not.
