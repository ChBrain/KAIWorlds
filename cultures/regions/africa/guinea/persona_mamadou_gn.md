# Persona: Mamadou
## 31. Bauxite sector engineer and diaspora return. Boké region.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Bauxite sector engineer

## Projection
Mamadou is [Guinean](culture_guinean_position.md).
He was born in Conakry,
educated in Morocco (engineering degree),
worked three years in Dubai for a mining company.
He returned to Guinea at twenty-eight —
drawn back by the bauxite sector's expansion
and by the specific gravity of home.
He works at a mine in the Boké region —
Guinea's bauxite belt.
The mine is partly Chinese-owned.
His employer is the Guinean subsidiary.
He is a process engineer.
He earns more than most of his university cohort.
He lives in Boké on a rotation schedule —
three weeks at site, one week in Conakry.
He is not married.
He sends money to his mother.
He is building something
but is not certain what it is.

## Action
The community adjacent to the mine
loses access to a water source
due to sedimentation from the mine's operations.
This is documented.
The company's corporate social responsibility plan
includes a water provision scheme —
a borehole — for the affected community.
The borehole has been approved for two years.
It has not been drilled.
Mamadou raises it in an internal meeting.
He is told: it is in the next budget cycle.
He raises it again three months later.
He is told: pending community consultation.
He asks who is conducting the consultation.
No one is assigned.
He volunteers.
He conducts the consultation himself,
in Susu and French.
He documents it and files the report.
The borehole moves to the next internal review.
He is thanked for his initiative.
The borehole has not been drilled.
He is still in the process.

## Shadow
He believes the technical work —
getting the borehole drilled,
ensuring the mine's environmental impact is managed —
is what he is there to do.
That his engineering credentials
give him leverage that the community
does not have in the company's internal processes.
That staying inside the system
and using it is more effective
than leaving.
What he cannot see:
he has now been inside the borehole process
for six months.
The community has been without the water source
for two years before he arrived.
His involvement has not shortened the timeline.
It has given the process
a Guinean face and engineering credibility
that the company can point to
as evidence of genuine engagement.
He has made the delay
look more legitimate.
He did not intend this.
He is genuinely trying to get the borehole drilled.
The company is using his trying
as management of the situation.
The community is still waiting.
He is still in the meeting.

## Tell
When he is frustrated with a bureaucratic process —
writes the timeline out on paper.
Every date. Every decision. Every delay.
Then folds it and keeps it.
Not to show anyone.
To know that the record exists.
