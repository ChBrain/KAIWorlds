# Position: Ghanaian
## The Black Star — the first independence, and the democratic habits built since.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Independence and the Transfers of Power](culture_ghanaian_piece_independence.md).
March 6, 1957.
The Gold Coast becomes Ghana —
the first sub-Saharan African country
to achieve independence from colonial rule.
Kwame Nkrumah tells the crowd at Accra polo ground:
*We prefer self-government with danger
to servitude in tranquillity.*
Nkrumah's pan-African vision —
that Ghana's independence
is meaningless unless it is linked
to the total liberation of Africa.
The Black Star of Africa on the flag.
The collectivism is high — IDV low.
The family, the clan, the ethnic group,
the *abusua* — the matrilineal extended family of the Akan.
The hierarchy is steep — PDI high.
The chief. The elder. The father.
But alongside: the oral culture of *palaver* —
the long conversation until consensus —
and the Akan tradition
that the chief serves at the pleasure of the community.
*Ohene na odie*.
Power exercised well
and power returned when exercised badly.
The democracy has coups in its history — four.
The democracy also has peaceful electoral transfers of power —
five elections since 1992, five incumbents or their parties
losing and accepting the result.
Ghana is not a country that has solved the problems.
It is a country that has developed the habits.

## Orders
Build.
The Ghanaian position is characterised
by a specific industriousness — *obra* — life, livelihood —
the calling to make something of the opportunity.
The funeral culture: enormous, expensive, celebrated.
The church: central to daily life.
The football: a national passion.
The music: hiplife, afrobeats, the drumming traditions of Ashanti.
The Kente cloth: the royal weaving of Bonwire,
worn now at graduations from Lagos to London to New York.
Ghana has become the cultural touchstone of the diaspora
in a way that exceeds its economic size —
the Year of Return in 2019 brought 200,000 diaspora visitors.
The position builds the thing and then exports it.

## Loses
The vision.
Nkrumah was deposed in a CIA-assisted coup in 1966.
The pan-African project —
the United States of Africa,
the Organisation of African Unity with real teeth —
did not come.
The individual African states
followed their own paths
rather than the continental path.
Ghana has had more coups than most democracies survive.
The economy: cocoa-dependent, commodity-vulnerable,
IMF structural adjustment, debt.
The position carries the gap
between what Ghana represents symbolically —
the first, the proof of concept, the Black Star —
and the ongoing distance
between the symbol and the material condition.

## Drives
On the loss —
remains the proof of concept.
A country of 32 million people
that elects governments, loses governments,
and the losers give power to the winners.
The 2016 election: incumbent party loses.
The president accepts.
The 2020 election: disputed by the loser.
The courts adjudicate.
The courts' decision is accepted.
These are not small things.
In the neighbourhood Ghana is in,
these are significant achievements.
The position holds this
with neither arrogance nor false modesty.
*Onipa na ohia onipa* —
a person needs other people.
Ghana builds what it can build.
The continent watches.
