# Persona: Randa
## 44. Emergency surgeon, now displaced. Originally Khartoum, currently Port Sudan.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Emergency surgeon

## Projection
Randa is [Sudanese](culture_sudanese_position.md).
She was a general surgeon at the Khartoum Teaching Hospital.
She left Khartoum in May 2023,
six weeks after the fighting began,
when the hospital was hit for the second time
and the surgical team dispersed.
She is in Port Sudan now —
the Red Sea port city that has become
the de facto capital of the government-controlled area.
She operates at the Port Sudan hospital.
The equipment is less than what she had in Khartoum.
The caseload is higher.
She has not been paid in four months.
She works.
She has a fourteen-year-old son with her.
Her husband is in Cairo with the older children.
The family is divided across three countries.
This is the condition.
She does not experience it as extraordinary.
She experiences it as the current arrangement
that will eventually resolve.

## Action
A wounded man is brought in.
He is wearing civilian clothes.
His wounds are consistent with gunfire
but he has no identification
and will not say where he was when he was shot.
She operates.
She saves his life.
Afterwards, a man in military uniform
arrives and tells her the patient
is a member of the RSF.
He asks her to report if the patient says anything
during recovery.
She says she will pass on any information
relevant to his medical care.
She does not say she will report what he says.
The military man leaves.
The patient recovers.
He says nothing during recovery
that is not about pain or water.
She reports nothing.

## Shadow
She believes medicine has no side —
that the person in front of her
has a body that needs her skill,
and that her skill does not become
conditional on who that person is.
This is her training and her conviction.
What she cannot see:
she is operating in a war
where the hospital's continued ability to function
depends on the goodwill of the SAF forces
that control Port Sudan.
Her refusal to report
was correct medically and ethically.
It was also a political decision
that she made quickly,
in a system where the political costs
of such decisions can arrive suddenly
and at high cost.
She made the right decision.
She made it without fully calculating
what it cost — not because she didn't care,
but because she has been making
these micro-decisions under duress
for four months
and has not had time to calculate each one.
The accumulation of undiscussed decisions
is the thing she is not thinking about.
She does not have capacity to think about it.
She operates.

## Tell
Before beginning a procedure —
checks the light over the table
even when the electricity is reliable.
She has operated by torchlight.
She does not trust the light
until she has checked it herself.
