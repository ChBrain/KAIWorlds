## Shown
- The confluence.
  The Blue Nile and the White Nile.
  The point where they meet is visible —
  the two colours running parallel
  for several kilometres before mixing.
  The city built at this meeting point.
  The name: *al-Khurṭūm* —
  the elephant's trunk,
  for the shape of the land
  at the river's junction.
  The geography is the oldest thing here.
  Everything else has been built and rebuilt.
- The National Museum of Sudan.
  The Nubian antiquities.
  The rescued temples from the Nile Valley
  moved before the Aswan Dam flooded the sites.
  The artefacts of twenty civilisations
  in a building that was damaged in the 2023 war.
  The items that were looted: disputed.
  What was saved before the fighting: incomplete.
  The museum holds Sudan's pre-Arab history —
  the history the Khartoum elite marginalised.
  The history is still there.
  The building: partially functioning.
- Omdurman. Across the White Nile.
  The *souk* — the largest traditional market in Africa.
  The Khalifa's house, now a museum.
  The Mahdist state's capital.
  The whirling dervishes every Friday
  at the Hamed el-Nil mosque —
  the Sufi ceremony running
  through every government the country has had.
  The dervishes were there before al-Bashir.
  They were there during al-Bashir.
  They are there during the current war
  in the weeks when the fighting allows.
- The Republican Palace. Khartoum.
  The presidential palace on the Blue Nile.
  Occupied by different governments
  approximately every seven years on average
  since 1956.
  Currently contested between the SAF and RSF.
  The palace that represents the state
  is, as of 2023–2025,
  itself the site of the war over what the state is.
  The symbol and the question
  are in the same building.
- The tent cities outside Khartoum.
  The displaced persons camps
  that exist in every war.
  This war has produced the largest displacement
  in Sudan's history — 12 million people.
  The camps are the current condition.
  They are also the continuity —
  people who have been displaced before,
  who know how to build a camp,
  who will rebuild when they return.
  They will return.
  They always have.
