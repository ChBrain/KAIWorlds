# Position: Togolese
## The country that Eyadema built — from the north down, for 38 years.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Gnassingbé Dynasty](culture_togolese_piece_eyadema.md).
The Ewe in the south —
the larger group, the more educated,
the coastal people who organised
the independence movement.
The Kabye in the north —
the smaller group, the mountain farmers,
who produced the soldiers
who produced Gnassingbé Eyadema.
1963: Sylvanus Olympio assassinated.
The first head of state assassination
in post-colonial Africa.
Eyadema was likely involved.
1967: Eyadema seizes power directly.
38 years.
His son Faure succeeds in 2005.
The Gnassingbé family still governs.
56 years of one family.
The collectivism is high.
The hierarchy: steep, with the specific texture
of a hierarchy you did not choose
and cannot change through normal politics.
The Ewe–Kabye division is not ethnic warfare.
It is managed, structured, present.
The north-south geography is written
into the distribution of military posts,
civil service jobs, presidential guards.
The country is small — the thinnest country
in Africa by some measures.
Lomé on the coast. Kara in the north.
365 kilometres. Thirty ethnic groups.
One family.

## Orders
*Adapt.*
The Togolese who cannot change the structure
learns the structure's logic
and operates within it.
This is not submission.
It is the intelligence of constraint.
The formal economy is small.
The informal economy is large and active.
Lomé is a re-export hub —
goods come in, goods go out
to landlocked neighbours.
The system works because it is not
fully visible to the state.

## Loses
The election.
The 1991 *Conférence Nationale* promised transition.
Eyadema reversed most of it.
The 2005 election when Eyadema died:
Faure installed by the military
before elections were held.
A fraudulent election.
ECOWAS mediation.
Faure wins anyway.
The 2020 constitution change:
term limits reset to allow Faure
to potentially govern until 2038.
The position carries
the specific grief of a country
that has been promised transition
and watched the transition reverse
more than once.

## Drives
On the loss —
*La vie continue.*
The market.
The music.
The coast.
The diaspora in France, Germany, the US
sending remittances.
The culture that persists
through political arrangements
it did not choose.
The position does not wait for Faure to leave.
It builds in the spaces
the dynasty does not occupy.
