# Piece: Moshoeshoe's Diplomacy and the Mountain Kingdom
## The Sotho chief who asked Britain to protect him from the Boers — and created the only country entirely surrounded by another.

## Owner
- **Place:** [Maseru / Thaba Bosiu](culture_lesotho_place_maseru.md)

## Place
Thaba Bosiu. The Mountain of the Night.
The mesa where Moshoeshoe I
established his capital in 1824.
The mountain fortress that repelled
multiple Boer and British attacks.
From here, Moshoeshoe sent his famous letter
to the British High Commissioner in 1868:
*My country is covered with blood,
my nation is being eaten up.
If I am to save what is left,
I must go under the protection
of a Great Nation.*
Britain declared Basutoland a protectorate.
The Boers were stopped.
The mountain fortress is still there.
The nation is still there.
The decision is still the load-bearing event.

## Load Bearing
Remove Moshoeshoe's 1868 request
and Basutoland is absorbed
into what becomes South Africa.
The Sotho people become a minority
within Boer-controlled territory —
the same legal structure as the Zulu and Xhosa,
who were colonised and not protected.
With it: Lesotho exists.
The only country in the world
entirely enclosed by another.
Moshoeshoe's calculation:
he would rather be a protectorate
of a power that was less immediately predatory
than be absorbed by the Boers.
Britain's calculation: easier to protect
than to incorporate.
The mountain fortress made
the cost of conquest higher than
the cost of the protectorate arrangement.
Geography and diplomacy together
created a country.
After independence in 1966,
Lesotho's situation is structurally unchanged:
surrounded by South Africa,
dependent on South Africa for trade,
for employment, for its water project payments.
The independence is real.
The dependency is real.
Moshoeshoe's calculation — preserve sovereignty
within a constraining relationship —
is still the governing logic.
It has worked for 158 years.

## Apparent
1822: Moshoeshoe I begins uniting
Sotho clans displaced by the *Difaqane* wars.
The *Difaqane* — the forced migrations
caused by Zulu expansion
under Shaka — displaced hundreds of thousands.
Moshoeshoe took in refugees.
He built alliances instead of enemies.
1824: Moshoeshoe establishes Thaba Bosiu.
1836–1840: Boer settlers arrive in the Great Trek.
Conflict over land begins.
1858, 1865: Boer-Basotho wars.
Moshoeshoe outmanoeuvred militarily but not politically.
1868: Moshoeshoe's letter. British protectorate declared.
1869: Treaty of Aliwal North.
Portions of Lesotho's lowlands ceded to the Orange Free State.
The western lowlands are lost.
The mountains are retained.
1966: independence. King Moshoeshoe II.
Prime Minister Leabua Jonathan.
1986, 1991, 1994, 1998, 2014, 2022:
coups or coup attempts.
The formal politics in perpetual instability.
The monarchy: continuous.
The mountain: unchanged.
The water project: negotiated with South Africa in 1986.
Water flows. Royalties paid.
Lesotho survives.
As Moshoeshoe calculated it would.
