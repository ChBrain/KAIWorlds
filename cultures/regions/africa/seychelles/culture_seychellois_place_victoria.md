## Shown
- The State House.
  The colonial-era building on the hill above Victoria.
  Used by French administrators, British governors,
  Mancham for one year,
  René for 27,
  Michel, Faure, Ramkalawan.
  The building that holds
  the entire post-colonial history of Seychelles
  in one structure.
  The 2019–2020 Truth and Reconciliation Commission
  heard testimony about events
  that occurred under people
  who worked in this building.
  The building continues.
  The Commission's recommendations
  are being processed.
- The Clock Tower.
  Victoria's central landmark —
  a replica of a London clock tower,
  erected in 1903 when Seychelles became
  a separate Crown Colony.
  The smallest capital city in Africa
  has a clock tower from a London blueprint.
  The colonial inheritance
  is miniature and exact.
  Everyone in Victoria
  navigates by it.
- The Sir Selwyn Selwyn-Clarke Market.
  The central market.
  Fish — the red snapper, the job fish,
  the parrotfish from the reef.
  Cinnamon, vanilla, nutmeg.
  The spice economy that the French
  established and that persists
  alongside the tourism economy.
  The Kreol language running
  through every transaction.
  The market where the
  *noubann* of the island meets itself
  every morning.
- Beau Vallon beach.
  The main beach on Mahé.
  Where tourists and Seychellois
  use the same water.
  The coral bleaching visible
  offshore — the warming Indian Ocean
  damaging the reef
  that is both ecosystem and economy.
  The tourism depends on the reef.
  The reef is under pressure from
  the tourism's carbon footprint.
  The position navigates this
  without a solution.
- The Lenstiti Kreol —
  the Kreole Institute.
  The institution that formalized
  *Seselwa* as a written language,
  built the curriculum,
  produced the literature.
  René's one-party state
  did this genuinely well:
  the Creole identity that colonialism
  had dismissed as mere patois
  was given institutional form,
  a grammar, a canon.
  The *Moutya* UNESCO inscription in 2021
  is partly the result of documentation
  the Lenstiti built over decades.
  The slave-era resistance dance
  is now on the heritage list.
  The institute that documented it
  was built by the coup government.
  The complexity is present.
  The institute runs.
