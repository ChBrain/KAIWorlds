# Position: Seychellois
## The Creole nation that René built from a coup — the world's only socialist tax haven, and Africa's highest HDI.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[René's Coup and the Creole Nation](culture_seychellois_piece_rene.md).
Victoria. Mahé. The granite peaks.
The 115 islands.
97,000 people —
Africa's smallest country by population.
The *Seselwa* — Seychellois Kreol:
the French-based Creole language
that everyone speaks.
French, English, Kreol: three official languages.
Kreol is the first.
The Afro-Creole majority:
descendants of enslaved Africans,
Malagasy workers, Indian labourers,
French settlers, Chinese traders —
all mixed, over two centuries,
into the identity that was initially denied,
then reclaimed, then constitutionally centred.
The Franco-Seychellois — the *gran'bla* —
who owned the land under colonialism
and still hold much of the economic base.
The tourism: the main industry.
The tuna fisheries.
The offshore financial sector —
the tax haven René built
while declaring himself a socialist.
Both things are the country.
The collectivism: moderate —
the small island social density
where everyone knows everyone
across all hierarchies simultaneously.
The hierarchy: softened since multiparty democracy,
but the *gran'bla* economic position
and the Creole political majority
are both real.

## Orders
*Finis Coronat Opus* — the end crowns the work.
The national motto.
The position navigates the achievement
of a country that built
the highest human development index in Africa
from a population of 97,000,
from an uninhabited island chain,
through a coup, a one-party state,
a tax haven,
and a peaceful democratic transition.
The end, so far, crowns the work.
The work is not done.

## Loses
The political plurality
that the one-party state suppressed
from 1977 to 1991.
The opposition figures who disappeared.
The senior official killed in René's own office.
The 2019–2020 Truth, Reconciliation
and National Unity Commission hearings:
participants acknowledged involvement in the 1977 coup.
René had denied it until his death in 2019.
The commission heard testimony
about people who died under the state
and whose families received no accounting.
The position carries the cost
of the development that was built
on foundations that included
that suppression.
The development is real.
The suppression was real.
The commission tried to hold both.

## Drives
On the loss —
the *Moutya*.
The slave-era percussion tradition
that UNESCO inscribed in 2021
as a symbol of resistance
against hardship, servitude, social injustice.
The dance that was banned under colonialism.
The dance that survived.
The dance that is now performed
at festivals and on beaches
and in school programmes.
The 2020 election:
Wavel Ramkalawan, first opposition president.
First peaceful opposition victory
since independence.
The country that René's coup built
peacefully transferred power
away from René's party.
That is the thing the position
is most proud of.
The *Moutya* still plays.
The new president governs.
The coral reefs are bleaching.
The position continues.
