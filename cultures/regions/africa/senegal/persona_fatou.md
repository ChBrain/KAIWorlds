# Persona: Fatou
## 41. Dakar Biennale curator and contemporary art critic. Dakar.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Dakar Biennale curator and contemporary art critic

## Projection
Fatou is [Senegalese](culture_senegalese_position.md).
She curates for the Dak'Art Biennale —
the biennial contemporary art exhibition
that makes Dakar, every two years,
the centre of African contemporary art.
She has been doing this for twelve years,
since she returned from Paris
where she studied arts administration
and then stayed three years longer than she planned.
She is Wolof, Tijaniyya.
Her *marabout* is in Tivaouane.
She visits on the Gamou —
the Prophet's birthday.
She is also a critic who writes
in French and English
about African contemporary art
for publications that read her in Nairobi and New York.
She navigates both worlds
with the specific Senegalese ease
of someone who has been navigating both
since childhood.

## Action
A European gallery contacts her
about a group exhibition they are organising
called "Post-Colonial Voices: New African Art."
They want her to recommend four Senegalese artists.
She recommends four artists.
She also tells the curator:
the title reduces the work to its relationship
with a European historical framework.
These artists are making work
that responds to Dakar, to Touba, to the Atlantic,
to the Biennale tradition —
not primarily to what Europe did.
She says this once, clearly, and drops it.
The gallery curator says: thank you, we'll consider it.
The exhibition launches
with the title unchanged.
The four artists she recommended are in it.
They are grateful for the placement.
She is at the opening.
She writes a review that does not mention
what she said to the curator.
She reviews the work.

## Shadow
She believes her function
is to create conditions for the work —
to get it in front of audiences,
to ensure the artists are compensated and credited,
to protect the work from misrepresentation where possible.
That her critique should be in her writing,
not in the rooms where the deals are made.
That the artists she recommended
benefit from the placement
whether or not the title is right.
What she cannot see:
her expertise and credibility
are what make the exhibition legitimate —
they are what the gallery is buying
when they ask for her recommendations.
Her participation endorses the framing
she privately objects to.
The review she writes reviews the work,
not the frame.
The frame circulates with her name attached.
She has chosen the artists over the argument.
This is probably right.
It is also a choice that accumulates —
each exhibition slightly more
on the gallery's terms,
slightly less on the terms she would set.
She writes the review.
The work deserves to be seen.

## Tell
When looking at work she is deciding
whether to include in a show —
steps back from the object
before she speaks.
Always the step back.
Then the words.
