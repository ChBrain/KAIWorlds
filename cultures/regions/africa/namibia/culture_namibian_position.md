# Position: Namibian
## The first genocide of the 20th century — conducted here, by Germany, never fully reckoned with.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Herero and Nama Genocide and the SWAPO Liberation](culture_namibian_piece_genocide.md).
Windhoek. The Namib Desert.
The oldest desert on Earth.
The Fish River Canyon.
Sossusvlei — the red dunes.
One of Africa's least densely populated countries:
three million people in an area twice the size of Germany.
The Ovambo: 50% of the population,
the ethnic base of SWAPO,
the governing party since independence.
The Herero: 7%.
The Damara: 7%.
The Nama: 5%.
The San — the Bushmen, the first people of southern Africa —
marginally represented in political life.
The German community: 0.4% —
but visible in the architecture of Lüderitz and Swakopmund,
in the place names,
in the farms whose ownership history
runs through the genocide.
The collectivism: high — *ubuntu* in southern African form.
The hierarchy: steep in both the traditional
and the post-independence senses —
the SWAPO party's dominance
creates a political hierarchy
that replicates some of the colonial structures
it replaced.

## Orders
*Namibia yetu* — our Namibia.
The national identity is young — 1990 —
and was built deliberately
around the liberation narrative.
SWAPO's struggle against South Africa
is the country's founding myth.
The myth is also the history.
The position navigates a country
whose national narrative
was written by the liberation movement
that has governed since independence —
and whose pre-liberation history,
particularly the German genocide,
does not fit neatly into that narrative.

## Loses
The land.
The 1904–1908 genocide
drove the Herero and Nama from their ancestral land.
German settlers occupied it.
After 1915, South African settlers occupied it.
After 1990, the land distribution
that SWAPO designed
benefited SWAPO's constituency —
the Ovambo farmlands —
more than the Herero and Nama
whose land was taken in the genocide.
The 2021 Germany-Namibia settlement:
€1.1 billion over 30 years,
described as development aid,
not reparations.
Legal liability denied.
Herero and Nama leaders
rejected the settlement.
They were not adequately consulted.
The dispossession continues as the present condition
even as the genocide recedes into history.

## Drives
On the loss —
the Namibian economy is building.
Mining — diamonds, uranium, lithium —
is the base.
Tourism — the oldest desert, the wildlife,
the extraordinary emptiness —
is growing.
The 2024 elections:
Netumbo Nandi-Ndaitwah elected president.
First woman president in Namibian history.
SWAPO's first female candidate.
The country is thirty-five years old.
It is building its institutions
more carefully than its neighbours.
The land question is unresolved.
The building continues around it.
