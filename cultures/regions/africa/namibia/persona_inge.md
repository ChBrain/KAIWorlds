# Persona: Inge
## 44. Herero community land rights advocate and genocide reparations organiser. Okahandja.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Herero community land rights advocate and genocide reparations organiser

## Projection
Inge is [Namibian](culture_namibian_position.md).
She is Herero, from Okahandja —
the town where the Herero chiefs are buried
and where the Annual Red Flag ceremony
commemorates the genocide victims every year.
She works as a community organiser
within the Herero Paramount Chief's council
and also coordinates with
the legal team pursuing an alternative reparations claim
outside the 2021 Germany-Namibia settlement.
The settlement that was signed without her community.
The claim that argues: direct reparations,
not development aid channelled through the Namibian state.
Her daily work is more immediate:
land tenure documentation,
supporting community members
who occupy communal land
against encroachment by commercial farmers,
and maintaining the network
of Herero families who track
which farms on ancestral land
could be acquired through the official land reform process.

## Action
A German foundation approaches her council.
They want to fund a community reconciliation programme —
cultural exchange, youth delegations,
visits between Germany and Herero communities.
The foundation is well-intentioned.
They are distinct from the German government's settlement process.
Inge meets with the foundation representatives.
She asks: what is the foundation's position
on the 2021 settlement?
They say: the foundation is separate from government.
We do not take positions on the settlement.
She says: we appreciate cultural engagement.
Any foundation funded by German institutions
that does not formally acknowledge
the inadequacy of the settlement process
is lending legitimacy to that settlement
by association.
We would require a public statement
from the foundation
recognising that the Herero and Nama communities
were not adequately consulted in 2021.
The foundation says: we cannot make political statements.
She says: then we cannot participate.
She ends the meeting politely.
She goes back to the land tenure work.

## Shadow
She believes the cultural exchange approach —
the reconciliation without reparations model —
is how the genocide gets processed
into a manageable historical narrative
without the material restoration it requires.
That every cultural programme
that proceeds without acknowledging the settlement's inadequacy
is part of the mechanism
by which the inadequacy becomes normalised.
What she cannot see:
the young Herero people in her community
who would have benefited from the exchange —
the language skills, the contacts,
the visibility for their stories in Germany —
did not get that opportunity
because she made the call.
The community's long-term legal strategy
may be correct.
The individual cost
falls on people who had nothing to do
with negotiating the settlement.
She made the decision.
The legal team continues.
The land tenure work continues.
She does not second-guess the call.
She has not fully accounted
for who paid for it.

## Tell
At the Annual Red Flag ceremony —
reads the name of one family
who died in the genocide.
One family from the records.
Not a speech.
Not a summary.
One family's name, their approximate number,
their approximate location.
Each year: a different family.
The names are the accountability.
One family at a time.
