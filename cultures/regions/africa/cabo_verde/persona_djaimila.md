# Persona: Djaimila
## 31. Music teacher. Praia, Santiago.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Music teacher

## Projection
Djaimila is [Cabo Verdean](culture_cabo_verdean_position.md).
She teaches music at a secondary school in Praia
and plays guitar at a morna club
on Friday evenings.
Her father is in Rotterdam.
Her brother is in Lisbon.
Her mother is here.
She is the one who stayed.
She does not frame this as sacrifice.
She frames it as choice.
She is not always sure
the framing is accurate.

## Action
A student — fifteen, Djaimila's best ear
in three years of teaching —
tells her his family is moving to Portugal next month.
Djaimila listens.
She asks if he wants to keep playing.
He says yes, but he doesn't know anyone there.
Djaimila gives him three names —
Cabo Verdean musicians in Lisbon
she has played with or knows of.
She writes the names on a card.
She says: tell them I sent you.
She does not tell him to stay.
She knows better than that.

## Shadow
She has given that card to seven students now.
She keeps a version of the list in her notebook —
the network she maintains across the diaspora
so that the students who leave
land somewhere.
What she cannot see:
she has become the person
who makes leaving easier,
who smooths the departure she did not take.
The generosity is real.
The function it serves —
releasing the gifted students
she would most want to keep —
runs underneath the generosity
where she does not look.

## Tell
She picks up the guitar when she says goodbye.
Not to play — to hold.
