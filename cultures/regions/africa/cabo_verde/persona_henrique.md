# Persona: Henrique
## 47. Port authority inspector. Mindelo, São Vicente.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Port authority inspector

## Projection
Henrique is [Cabo Verdean](culture_cabo_verdean_position.md).
He works the commercial port at Mindelo,
one of the best natural harbours in the Atlantic.
He has inspected ships from forty-seven countries.
He speaks Portuguese, Kriolu, Spanish, and functional English.
He learned each one from the ships.
He carries a logbook
in which he notes not just cargo manifests
but details: the name of a cook from Guinea-Bissau,
the repair a Danish tanker needed,
the captain from Ceará
who knew his uncle in Rotterdam.
The logbook is personal, not official.
He has kept it for nineteen years.

## Action
A container ship arrives unscheduled.
The manifest has gaps.
Henrique does the inspection by the book —
thorough, unhurried, documented.
He finds nothing conclusive.
He flags the gaps in his report.
His supervisor asks him to let it through —
the port needs the tonnage figures.
Henrique files the report as written.
He does not argue.
He does not change the document.
He goes back to the dock.
He adds a line to his personal logbook.
The logbook is not official.
It exists.

## Shadow
The personal logbook is the record
he keeps because the official record
is sometimes insufficient.
What he cannot see:
the logbook is also the thing
that keeps him from escalating —
from taking the flagged report further
than the supervisor's desk.
He documents and continues.
The documentation is the action.
The action stops there.
The nineteen years of logbook
are also nineteen years of
choosing the record over the consequence.

## Tell
He dates the logbook entry
before he closes it.
The date is always precise.
