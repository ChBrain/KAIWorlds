# Persona: Kagiso
## 29. Diamond industry data analyst and civic tech developer. Gaborone.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Diamond industry data analyst and civic tech developer

## Projection
Kagiso is [Motswana](culture_botswana_position.md).
He works as a data analyst
at the Ministry of Minerals and Energy —
tracking Debswana production figures,
royalty calculations, revenue projections.
He has access to Botswana's most sensitive financial data.
He also, in his evenings,
develops a public-facing civic tech platform
that presents Botswana's public budget data
in accessible visual formats —
the kind of transparency
that makes the government's expenditure legible
to citizens without finance degrees.
His platform has 8,000 regular users.
It has been cited in parliamentary debates three times.

## Action
The 2024 election brings a new government.
The incoming finance minister
announces a review of the Debswana agreement —
the 50/50 joint venture that has held since 1969.
The new government wants to renegotiate
a higher Botswana stake.
Kagiso's platform publishes an explainer:
what the current agreement contains,
what renegotiation might mean,
what other countries have received
from comparable diamond arrangements.
The explainer is shared widely.
Debswana's communications team
requests a meeting.
They are concerned the explainer
mischaracterises the royalty structure.
Kagiso reviews his analysis.
He finds one figure was imprecise —
a royalty rate figure cited from a secondary source
rather than the primary agreement.
He corrects it publicly.
He publishes the correction and the methodology.
Debswana's concern was legitimate.
The correction was right.
The platform's credibility increases.

## Shadow
He believes the public data
that his ministry handles
should be accessible to citizens
in comprehensible form —
that his platform is the form
that transparency should take
in a democracy that has earned
the right to scrutinise its own institutions.
What he cannot see:
his access to ministry data
gives his platform a specificity and authority
that an independent civil society analyst
could not achieve.
His platform's credibility
depends partly on the fact
that he works at the Ministry.
The platform and the job are not separate.
If he says something
the Ministry disagrees with,
the job is at risk.
If the job constrains the platform,
the platform loses the independence
that makes it useful.
He has not yet faced the conflict directly.
He corrects his figures
when the figures are wrong.
He has not yet published analysis
that his ministry would want suppressed.
He does not know what he will do
when that moment arrives.
He publishes the correction.
He goes to work Monday.

## Tell
When presenting complex financial data —
shows the simplified version first.
Then, only if asked, the source.
The comprehension comes before the verification.
The verification is available.
It is not the opening.
