## Shown
- The Parliament building.
  Where the BDP governed for 58 years.
  Where Duma Boko's UDC took its seats in October 2024.
  The first transfer to opposition.
  The building exists for this.
  The building was used for this.
  The institutions held.
- The Three Dikgosi Monument.
  The three paramount chiefs — Sebele, Bathoen, Khama III —
  who travelled to London in 1895
  to petition the British government
  against incorporation into Cecil Rhodes's British South Africa Company.
  They succeeded.
  Bechuanaland remained a protectorate,
  not a company territory.
  The monument to the chiefs who said no to Rhodes.
  This is the origin story Botswana tells itself:
  we negotiated from strength.
  We have been doing it ever since.
- The Kgotla grounds, Gaborone East.
  The traditional assembly space.
  Still used.
  For community consultations,
  for local disputes,
  for the President who comes to explain policy
  rather than simply announce it.
  The *kgotla* is not tourism.
  It is governance infrastructure.
- The Debswana Headquarters.
  The joint venture that manages the diamonds.
  The building that represents the choice
  Seretse Khama made in 1969 —
  50/50, not extraction.
  The revenue that funded
  the schools and hospitals
  that made Botswana's development real.
  The building is modest for what it represents.
  Botswana's modesty is part of the piece.
- The Central Kalahari Game Reserve entrance.
  560 kilometres north.
  Not technically in Gaborone
  but present in Gaborone's conscience.
  Where the Basarwa/San communities
  were removed and have been fighting
  to return since the 1990s.
  The 2006 court ruling in their favour.
  The government's compliance: incomplete.
  The model that the monument celebrates
  failed the people the monument could not see.
  Both things are true.
  Gaborone holds both.
