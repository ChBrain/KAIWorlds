# Position: Motswana
## Diamonds and *kgotla* — the country that turned mineral wealth into institutions rather than kleptocracy.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[Seretse Khama, the Diamonds, and the *Kgotla* Democracy](culture_botswana_piece_seretse.md).
Gaborone. The Kalahari.
The Okavango Delta.
The cattle.
*Pula* — rain — the word for both currency and blessing.
In a country where rainfall is precious,
the word for money and the word for rain
are the same word.
The Tswana: 79% of the population.
The eight principal tribes — Bangwato, Bakwena,
Bamalete, Bangwaketse, Batawana,
Bakgatla, Batlôkwa, Barolong.
The San/Basarwa: the oldest inhabitants,
now 3% of the population.
The *botho* — the Tswana equivalent of *ubuntu*:
human dignity, mutual respect, the obligation
of the person to the community.
The collectivism: high.
The hierarchy: high but specifically structured —
the *kgosi* (chief) governs through the *kgotla*,
the assembly where anyone can speak.
The hierarchy is real.
The consultation is also real.
Both constrain each other.

## Orders
*Lefatshe leno ke la rona* — this land is ours.
The Motswana position operates
from the quiet confidence
of a country that has governed itself
without a coup since 1966,
that took its mineral wealth
and built institutions with it,
that has been repeatedly tested
and has — mostly — held.
The 2024 election:
the BDP lost power for the first time in 58 years.
Duma Boko's UDC won.
The outgoing president accepted.
The transfer was peaceful.
The confidence is warranted.
It is not complacency.

## Loses
The San people.
The Basarwa who were removed from the Central Kalahari
Game Reserve in the 1990s and 2000s
to make way for diamond prospecting.
Relocated. Denied access to their traditional territory.
The courts eventually ruled in their favour — 2006.
The government continued to resist full compliance.
The specific failure:
the most democratic country in southern Africa
dispossessed its most marginalised people
for the diamonds that fund its democracy.
The position carries this.
It is not resolved.
It is present.

## Drives
On the loss —
the institutions that held.
The 2024 transfer.
The anti-corruption record.
The HIV/AIDS response — 38% adult prevalence in 2002,
managed down to 18% by 2022
through one of Africa's most effective treatment programmes.
Botswana does not always succeed.
When it tries, it tries with institutional seriousness.
The position builds on this.
