# Position: Somali
## The nation that survived without a state — because the clan was always the country.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Collapse of 1991](culture_somali_piece_1991.md).
The clan. *Reer* — household, lineage, people.
Not as a political arrangement — as a cosmology.
The Somali child learns their genealogy
before they learn to read.
Twenty, thirty generations back.
The patrilineal chain is the identity.
You know who you are
because you know where you come from
and the chain goes back far enough
to place you precisely
in relation to every other Somali you will ever meet.
The four major clan-families: Darod, Hawiye, Dir, Rahanweyn.
Below them: sub-clans, sub-sub-clans,
each with its own geography, its own alliances,
its own debts.
The language — Somali — is one of the most uniform
in the continent. One people, one language, one Islam.
This is rare in Africa.
It did not produce a stable state.
It produced the most ethnically homogeneous country
in sub-Saharan Africa
and the longest-running state collapse.
Both are the same country.
The Islam is Sunni, moderate historically —
the Sufi traditions deep,
the extremist intrusion recent and contested.
The poetry: Somalis are a nation of poets.
The oral tradition carries law, history, love, insult.
The most devastating weapon in precolonial Somalia
was the satirical poem.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room already knows your clan.
The room has already calculated the alignment.
The room is also hospitable.
Both things are operating simultaneously.

## Orders
Hold the clan.
Not as tribalism — as the only insurance
that has never failed.
The state failed in 1991.
International organisations failed.
The NGOs failed and left.
The clan did not fail.
The clan fed its members.
The clan mediated disputes.
The clan sent remittances from Mogadishu to Minneapolis
and from London to Baidoa.
When everything else collapsed,
the clan held.
The position does not order loyalty to the state.
It does not trust the state.
The position orders loyalty to what has proven durable.

## Loses
The country.
They were certain in 1960 — independence, union of British and Italian Somaliland,
the flag with the five-pointed star for the five Somali territories —
that the nation would become the state.
That the remarkable homogeneity —
one people, one language, one religion —
would translate into political cohesion.
It did not.
The political elite used the clan
the way the colonial powers had taught them:
as a unit of patronage and exclusion.
The state became a vehicle for clan accumulation.
When the vehicle crashed in 1991,
the clan was all that was left.
The position carries the specific grief
of a people who were the most obvious candidates
for a successful African nation-state —
linguistically unified, religiously unified,
culturally coherent —
and who watched the project destroyed
from the inside.

## Drives
On the loss —
builds through the clan
and sometimes despite the clan.
The Somali diaspora:
the remittance economy that flows
from Minneapolis, London, Toronto, Oslo
back to Mogadishu, Hargeisa, Baidoa —
estimated at over $2 billion per year.
Somaliland — the northwestern breakaway region —
functioning as a de facto state
with elections, courts, peace,
since 1991.
Not recognised. Functioning.
The position does not wait for international recognition
to build what needs to be built.
*Isku kalsoon* — self-reliance.
The word Nyerere used.
The Somali version is older
and more tested.
