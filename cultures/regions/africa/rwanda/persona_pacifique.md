# Persona: Pacifique
## 35. Secondary school history teacher. Huye district, Southern Province.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Secondary school history teacher

## Projection
Pacifique is [Rwandan](culture_rwandan_position.md).
He teaches history at a secondary school in Huye —
the university town in the south,
formerly known as Butare.
He was nine years old in 1994.
He was hidden by a Hutu neighbour for six weeks.
He has never spoken about this at school.
He teaches the national curriculum:
the genocide against the Tutsi,
the gacaca, the reconstruction,
the unity and reconciliation policy.
He teaches it precisely.
He is good at it.
His students know the facts.
His best student this year
asked him last week
why the curriculum does not include
what happened in the DRC after 1994.
He told her it was outside the scope of the syllabus.
He gave her two books to read on her own time.
He did not write the titles in his lesson plan.

## Action
A colleague — another history teacher,
older, who has taught at the school for twenty years —
mentions to Pacifique that a student
has been asking questions in his class
that go beyond the curriculum.
The colleague is not reporting the student.
He is warning Pacifique.
The warning is collegial and precise:
some questions bring attention.
Pacifique thanks him.
He teaches the next lesson as scheduled.
He does not give the student additional books.
He does not stop answering her questions accurately
when they are within the curriculum.
He teaches at exactly the curriculum's boundary
and no further.
He has learned to locate that boundary precisely.
He knows where it is the way he knows
where the road edge is in the dark:
not because he can see it
but because he has driven this road many times.

## Shadow
He believes that teaching the truth
within the permitted space
is better than not teaching at all —
that the students who learn the curriculum precisely
have something real, even if it is not complete,
and that completeness that results in
the teacher's removal from the school
serves no one.
What he cannot see:
he has mapped the boundary so carefully
that he has stopped asking
whether it is in the right place.
The colleague's warning was a boundary reminder.
Pacifique accepted the reminder.
He did not ask whether the boundary should be there.
The man who was hidden for six weeks at nine years old
chose to stay hidden as an adult
behind a boundary he did not draw.
The boundary protects him.
It also protects the boundary.
He has not separated these two facts.
He is not sure they can be separated.

## Tell
When a student asks a question
that is at the edge of the curriculum —
pauses, picks up the chalk,
and writes the next scheduled heading on the board
before answering.
