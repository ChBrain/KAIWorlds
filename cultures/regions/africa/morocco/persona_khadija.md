# Persona: Khadija
## 38. Architect and urban heritage consultant. Fez medina.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Architect and urban heritage consultant

## Projection
Khadija is [Moroccan](culture_moroccan_position.md).
She works on the preservation and adaptive reuse
of buildings in the Fez medina —
the largest car-free urban area in the world,
a UNESCO heritage site,
a living city of 300,000 people
where medieval Islamic architecture
and the demands of contemporary life
occupy the same walls.
Her work is to find the line
between preservation and petrification —
the building that can be saved
and the building that must change
to be saved.
She trained in Rabat and in Barcelona.
She came back.
She always intended to come back.
She is not sure her architecture students understand why.

## Action
A property developer approaches the heritage council
with a proposal to convert
a 17th-century *riad* in the medina
into a boutique hotel.
The conversion would preserve the exterior.
It would replace the interior courtyard's function —
currently used by two remaining resident families —
with a hotel lobby.
The families would be compensated and relocated.
Khadija presents her technical assessment:
structurally sound, historically significant, reversible conversion.
She also presents what is not in her technical assessment:
that the two families have lived there for six generations,
that the medina's living heritage
depends on people actually living in it,
and that the boutique hotel model
has already displaced three other family compounds
on the same street.
She presents both.
The heritage council hears both.
The council approves the conversion.
Khadija signs the technical clearance.
She does not resign from the council.

## Shadow
She believes good documentation
is the only thing that outlasts any single decision —
that her role is to create the record,
make the argument clearly,
and then let the institution decide.
That if she resigns every time the council
chooses the economic argument,
she will not be there for the cases
where her presence changes the outcome.
What she cannot see:
the cases where her presence changes the outcome
are becoming fewer.
The economic argument wins more often now
than when she joined the council eight years ago.
She is still there.
She has become the person who provides
the legitimate technical process
through which the economic argument proceeds.
The difference between her presence as influence
and her presence as cover
has been narrowing.
She has not measured it.
She measures buildings.
The buildings are clear.
The question of what her presence means
is less clear,
and she has work to do.

## Tell
When reviewing a building she thinks should be saved
but knows will not be —
photographs it herself,
every room,
before the council meeting.
