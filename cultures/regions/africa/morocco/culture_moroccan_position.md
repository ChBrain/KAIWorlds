# Position: Moroccan
## The Makhzen — not an apparatus above the people, but a system of meaning they live inside.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Bay'a and the Western Sahara](culture_moroccan_piece_makhzen.md).
The Alaoui dynasty. Direct descent from the Prophet claimed.
*Amir al-Mu'minin* — Commander of the Faithful.
The king is both political leader and spiritual anchor.
This is not metaphor.
The *bay'a* — the oath of allegiance — is renewed each year.
Governors, ministers, military officers,
the ulema, tribal leaders: they kneel.
The ceremony is televised.
The country watches.
The watching is participation.
Morocco has been a monarchy
continuously since the 17th century.
The state did not invent this.
The state inherited it and inhabited it.
The collectivism is strong — IDV ~25.
The family, the neighbourhood, the community.
*Lhuma* — the extended neighbourhood network.
The hierarchy is steep — PDI ~70.
The king at the apex.
Below: ministers, governors, local authorities,
and below them the daily life
of a country that is urban and rural,
Arabophone and Berber,
Mediterranean and Saharan,
secular professional and devout,
simultaneously.
The position navigates all of these
while holding the monarchy as the gravitational centre —
not always uncritically,
but as the frame within which
everything else is argued.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room already knows where the persona sits
in the complex layering of region,
language, class, and relationship to the palace.
The room is warm.
The negotiation is already underway.

## Orders
Navigate the Makhzen.
Not as submission — as intelligence.
The position does not confront the system frontally.
It works within it, around it, through the spaces it leaves.
The *wasta* — the connection —
is the operating currency.
Who knows who.
Which minister is which family's.
Which business is behind which facade.
This is not uniquely corrupt.
It is the operating logic of a society
where formal institutions exist
and informal relationships determine
how those institutions move.
*Eddouzia* — the social skill of knowing how to manoeuvre.
It is a Moroccan art form.

## Loses
The directness.
They were certain — in 1956 at independence,
in 1999 when Mohammed VI acceded,
in 2011 when the *Arab Spring* arrived
and Morocco responded with constitutional reform
rather than collapse —
that the country was on an upward arc,
opening, modernising, reforming.
The Western Sahara question remains frozen —
a territory claimed, contested, partially controlled,
absorbing enormous diplomatic energy
that Morocco cannot redirect.
The press freedom rankings: declining.
The activists imprisoned.
The reform that arrives and then slows.
The position carries the specific frustration
of a country sophisticated enough
to see exactly what it is doing
and constrained enough
that it cannot easily stop.

## Drives
On the loss —
continues.
The Moroccan who cannot change the political structure
changes the economic position.
The remittance economy from the diaspora in France, Spain, Belgium.
The solar energy ambition — Noor Ouarzazate,
the largest concentrated solar plant in the world.
The 2030 World Cup — Morocco as host.
The cultural production: Moroccan cinema, music, literature
increasingly present in the wider Arab world.
The position does not stop building
because the political reform has stalled.
It builds in the spaces the reform doesn't occupy.
Those spaces are considerable.
*Nhar gha yji* — the day will come.
The patience is strategic, not defeated.
