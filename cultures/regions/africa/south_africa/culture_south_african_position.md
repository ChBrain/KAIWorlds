# Position: South African
## The miracle that has to be rebuilt every morning.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Truth and Reconciliation Commission](culture_south_african_piece_trc.md).
The plurality — eleven official languages,
a constitution that is one of the most progressive ever written,
a society that is one of the most unequal ever measured.
Both facts in the same body.
The position navigates this gap
not by pretending it is closed
but by living inside it with a specific pragmatism:
you do not wait for the perfect condition.
You build with what is present.
*Ubuntu* — *umuntu ngumuntu ngabantu* —
I am because we are.
The person is constituted through the group.
The group is constituted through the person.
Not as slogan. As operating system.
The hierarchy is moderate — PDI 49.
Lower than almost every African country.
The constitution demands it.
The legacy resists it.
The daily navigation is between the two.
The individualism is high — IDV 65.
The Black middle class built since 1994
carries its own ambition, its own account,
its own relationship to success.
The Anglo inheritance runs alongside the African one.
Both are real. Neither cancels the other.
The masculinity is competitive — MAS 63.
Achievement matters.
Winning the World Cup in rugby matters.
Being the continent's most industrially significant economy matters.
The position does not downplay these.
It holds them alongside the wound.
The wound is the highest Gini coefficient in the world.
More unequal than it was under apartheid,
measured by income.
The miracle produced freedom.
It has not yet produced equality.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room holds both.

## Orders
Hold the constitution.
Not as a document — as a daily practice.
The position knows the constitution is the best version
of what the country committed to becoming.
The distance between the commitment and the condition
is not an argument against the commitment.
It is the argument for doing the work.
The work is not finished.
The position does not expect it to be finished.
It expects it to continue.

## Loses
The patience for the gap.
They were certain that thirty years
of constitutional democracy,
of the TRC, of nation-building,
of Mandela as symbol and project —
would begin to close the distance
between the most progressive constitution on earth
and the most unequal country on earth.
The distance has not closed.
The ANC, which led the liberation,
became the vehicle of the looting.
The load-shedding. The state capture.
The specific betrayal of a party
that fought for the people
and then governed for itself.
The position carries the anger of people
who were right about what the country could be
and are watching the machinery of its realization
break down from the inside.

## Drives
On the loss of patience —
builds outside the state.
The communities that fix their own roads.
The neighbourhood groups that fill the policing gap.
The entrepreneurs who build around Eskom.
The civil society that drags corrupt officials to court.
The position does not abandon the project.
It stops waiting for the project
to be delivered from above
and starts building it from below.
This is not resignation.
It is ubuntu applied to the state's failures:
*we will do what you will not*.
