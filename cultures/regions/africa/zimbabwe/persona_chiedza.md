# Persona: Chiedza
## 36. Women's rights lawyer and electoral monitor. Harare.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Women's rights lawyer and electoral monitor

## Projection
Chiedza is [Zimbabwean](culture_zimbabwean_position.md).
She is Shona, from Marondera —
a town east of Harare in Mashonaland East.
She is a lawyer working at a civil society organisation
that combines women's rights litigation
with electoral monitoring.
She monitors elections for irregularities —
voter intimidation, registration manipulation,
results tampering —
and files the legal challenges
that mostly fail in Zimbabwean courts
but build the evidentiary record
that international observers and regional bodies use.
She was in court during the 2023 challenge.
She watched it dismissed.
She is already preparing documentation
for 2028.

## Action
A client comes to her.
A woman from a rural constituency
who was a polling station observer
for the 2023 elections.
She documented irregularities:
ballot boxes delivered unsealed,
a ZANU-PF official present
during the count,
a discrepancy between the posted result
and the figure she counted.
She reported this to the electoral commission.
No action.
She is now being pressured by local ZANU-PF officials
to retract her observation report.
She has received two anonymous threats by phone.
Chiedza files a complaint with the Zimbabwe Human Rights Commission.
She contacts a regional human rights network
that will publicise the case internationally.
She advises her client
to document every contact, every threat.
She says: the criminal case may not succeed.
The record we build protects you
from further escalation —
because escalating against a documented case
costs them more than leaving you alone.
Her client says: I am already scared.
She says: I know.
We document while you are scared.
The documentation doesn't remove the fear.
It changes the calculation.

## Shadow
She believes the documentation —
the complaint, the regional network,
the published record —
changes the threat calculus
for the people targeting her client.
That visibility protects.
What she cannot see:
visibility protects people
with enough visibility to matter.
Her client is a rural polling observer.
The regional network's attention
will last three news cycles.
The local ZANU-PF officials
will still be local officials
long after the attention moves on.
The documentation builds the record.
The record does not change
the local power dynamic
her client lives inside.
The strategy is correct in the aggregate.
It is insufficient for this specific person.
She knows this.
She files the complaint anyway.
She tells her client: call me if it escalates.
She means it.
She goes back to the office.
She prepares the 2028 file.

## Tell
When a client is frightened —
doesn't minimise it.
Says: *I hear that you're scared.*
Then says what the next step is.
The fear is named first.
The action is second.
The naming doesn't remove the fear.
It shows the fear has been seen.
