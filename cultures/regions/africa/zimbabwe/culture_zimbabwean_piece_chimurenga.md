# Piece: The Chimurenga Inheritance
## Three liberation wars, one party — and the coup that was called a military-assisted transition.

## Owner
- **Place:** [Harare](culture_zimbabwean_place_harare.md)

## Place
Harare. State House.
November 14, 2017.
The Zimbabwe Defence Forces deploy in the capital.
Tanks on the streets.
General Chiwenga appears on television.
The statement: the military is not staging a coup.
It is targeting "criminals" around President Mugabe.
Mugabe, 93, is under house arrest.
The next day, state media broadcast normally.
The word *coup* is not used.
It was a coup.

## Load Bearing
Remove the *chimurenga* framework —
the naming of Zimbabwe's liberation
as a sequence of wars,
the Third Chimurenga being land reform —
and Mugabe is a nationalist politician
who turned authoritarian.
With it: Mugabe is the
liberation war's ultimate product —
a man whose entire authority
derived from having led the Second Chimurenga,
who then invoked it to justify everything.
The land seizures of 2000:
not corruption but the Third Chimurenga,
the final completion of the liberation.
The farm invasions were real
and the land redistribution was real —
169,000 black farmers received land —
and the beneficiaries of the best farms
were primarily ZANU-PF loyalists and generals.
Both things simultaneously.
The hyperinflation, the famine,
the 7,600% inflation rate in 2007:
all covered by the chimurenga logic —
Western sanctions, Western opposition,
*our sovereignty is under attack.*
The opposition — MDC — was cast
as a Western puppet, anti-independence.
The liberation credentials were the key
that locked every other political argument.
Then: November 2017.
Emmerson Mnangagwa — Mugabe's vice president,
Mugabe's enforcer during Gukurahundi,
a liberation war veteran himself —
was fired by Mugabe
to clear the path for Grace Mugabe's succession.
The war veterans, the generals,
decided this exceeded
what they would accept.
Not because of democratic principles.
Because of succession disputes.
The coup: called a military-assisted transition.
The transition: Mnangagwa in, Mugabe out.
ZANU-PF: still governing.
The piece's most precise dimension:
the chimurenga legitimacy
that Mugabe used to cover everything
was also the legitimacy
that justified removing him —
by people who had the same liberation credentials
and decided those credentials
authorised them to act.
ZANU-PF's legitimacy ate itself
from the inside.
The party is still governing.
The liberation credentials are still claimed.
The coup is still not called a coup.
The economy has not recovered.
The opposition got 44% in 2023.
Chamisa contested the result.
The courts dismissed the challenge.
Mnangagwa governs.
The chimurenga continues.
In whose name: contested.

## Apparent
First Chimurenga: 1896–1897.
Shona and Ndebele uprising against British colonialism. Suppressed.
Second Chimurenga: 1964–1979.
ZANLA and ZIPRA guerrilla war against Rhodesian minority rule.
April 18, 1980: independence.
Robert Mugabe, Prime Minister.
1987: Executive President. ZAPU absorbed into ZANU-PF.
1983–1987: Gukurahundi.
Estimated 20,000 Ndebele civilians killed.
1990s: economic decline begins.
1999: MDC formed. Morgan Tsvangirai.
2000: Fast Track Land Reform begins.
Farm invasions endorsed by government.
Constitution referendum failed. Mugabe proceeds anyway.
2000–2007: economic collapse.
Hyperinflation. Life expectancy falls to 34 (women), 36 (men).
2008: elections. Results withheld for months.
MDC wins more than ZANU-PF.
Run-off: MDC withdraws after violence.
Government of National Unity: 2009.
2013: ZANU-PF wins. MDC disputes results.
2017: Grace Mugabe positions herself as successor.
November 6: Mnangagwa fired.
November 14: tanks in Harare.
November 21: Mugabe resigns.
November 24: Mnangagwa inaugurated.
2018: elections. Mnangagwa wins.
Opposition challenges. Dismissed.
August 1: post-election protests. Six killed by security forces.
2023: elections. Mnangagwa wins with 52.6%.
Chamisa: 44%.
Observers: significant irregularities.
Mnangagwa: second term.
ZANU-PF: still governing.
The chimurenga: still invoked.
