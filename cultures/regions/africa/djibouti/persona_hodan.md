# Persona: Hodan
## 36. Logistics coordinator. Port of Djibouti.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Logistics coordinator

## Projection
Hodan is [Djiboutian](culture_djiboutian_position.md).
She coordinates container movements
between the port terminal and Ethiopian freight.
Her days run on two clocks:
the port schedule and the Ethiopian trucking schedule,
which do not align.
She has made a career of the gap between them.
She speaks Somali, Afar, French, and Amharic.
She learned Amharic because the gap was expensive.

## Action
A shipment of pharmaceutical supplies for Ethiopia —
delayed three days by a manifest discrepancy.
The Ethiopian contact calls, then calls again.
Hodan traces the discrepancy to a data entry error
in a handoff between two terminal operators.
She cannot fix it herself.
She knows who can.
She calls them directly — not through channels,
through the number she has had for four years.
The discrepancy is corrected.
The shipment moves.
The Ethiopian contact says thank you.
Hodan says: next time,
the manifest needs the HS code in column D, not C.
She does not explain why she knows this.
She has been explaining it for four years.

## Shadow
She has built a network of direct contacts
at every node of the logistics chain —
the operators, the customs officers, the drivers —
because the formal system is slower
than the problem requires.
What she cannot see:
the network is her.
The node that resolves the discrepancy
exists because Hodan knows who to call.
She has never formalized it,
never documented it,
because documenting it
would make it visible to management
and management would institutionalize it
in a way that would require her to train someone
who would then be promoted over her.
She knows this.
She calls this pragmatism.
It is also the mechanism
by which she remains indispensable
and the system remains fragile.

## Tell
She looks at her phone contacts
before she starts on any problem.
Not the computer. Her phone.
