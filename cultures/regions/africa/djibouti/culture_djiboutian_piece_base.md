# Piece: The Base and the Strait
## The port at the mouth of the Red Sea that turned geography into foreign policy.

## Owner
- **Place:** [Djibouti City](culture_djiboutian_place_djibouti_city.md)

## Place
Djibouti City. The port.
The cranes.
The container stacks.
The French Foreign Legion base and the American base
visible from the same road
in a country smaller than Wales.

## Load Bearing
Remove the Bab-el-Mandeb —
the strait between Djibouti and Yemen,
27 kilometres wide,
through which 6.2 million barrels of oil pass daily —
and Djibouti is a hot, arid microstate
on the Horn of Africa
with no resources and difficult neighbours.
With it: Djibouti is one of the most strategically
valuable pieces of real estate on earth.
The base economy is load-bearing:
France pays rent. America pays rent. China pays rent.
Japan pays rent. Italy. Germany.
The rental income and port revenues
are the state budget.
The Ethiopia relationship is the second piece:
Ethiopia is landlocked since Eritrean independence in 1993.
100 million people.
Djibouti is their port.
The Addis Ababa–Djibouti Railway,
reopened 2018 — Chinese-built, Chinese-financed —
carries Ethiopian imports and exports
through Djiboutian infrastructure.
Ethiopia is Djibouti's largest trading partner.
Ethiopia is also Djibouti's main political leverage:
a dispute with Ethiopia is a dispute
with Djibouti's largest customer.
The geometry keeps both in line.
Remove the strait and the Ethiopia relationship:
a country surviving on aid.
With them: a country that has turned
the accident of its position
into a coherent foreign policy
of strategic indispensability.

## Apparent
1862: France signs treaty with Afar sultan.
1888: Djibouti City founded as French Somaliland.
1977: independence. Hassan Gouled Aptidon, first president.
The Issa-Afar balance institutionalised.
1991–1994: civil war. Afar insurgency.
Peace accord 2001.
1999: Ismail Omar Guelleh succeeds his uncle.
2002: Camp Lemonnier leased to US post-9/11.
Rent: $38 million per year, later renegotiated upward.
2008: China opens its first overseas military base.
Camp Lemonnier expanded. French base modernised.
Japanese base opened — Japan's first overseas military presence since WWII.
Italian, German, Spanish facilities follow.
Djibouti Port expansion — Chinese-financed.
Doraleh Container Terminal controversy:
DP World contract cancelled, terminal nationalised 2018.
Chinese-connected company awarded new management.
The US and France raise concerns.
Guelleh re-elected 2021. Fifth term.
Constitutional term limits amended to permit it.
The base rents paid on schedule.
The strait open.
The foreign policy continues.
