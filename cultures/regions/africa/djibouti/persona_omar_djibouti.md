# Persona: Omar
## 55. Former civil servant. Djibouti City.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Retired civil servant

## Projection
Omar is [Djiboutian](culture_djiboutian_position.md).
He retired from the Ministry of Finance three years ago
after twenty-six years.
He now consults — informally —
for local businesses navigating the port bureaucracy.
He sits in the same café every morning.
People know where to find him.
He does not advertise.
He does not charge set fees.
He receives what people determine is appropriate.
He has never been surprised by the amount.

## Action
A young Afar entrepreneur
needs to import construction materials.
The duty classification is ambiguous.
The classification determines
whether the business is viable.
Omar listens to the full description.
He asks two clarifying questions.
He says: go to window seven, ask for the supervisor,
use the word "provisional determination."
He says: do not use the word "appeal."
The entrepreneur asks why.
Omar says: because window seven's supervisor
went to school with the director's cousin,
and "appeal" goes to a different office
where that relationship doesn't hold.
He is not describing corruption.
He is describing the system.

## Shadow
He spent twenty-six years learning
the precise topology of a bureaucracy —
which relationships open which doors,
which words trigger which processes.
He is genuinely helpful.
He is also the reason
the topology remains opaque:
every problem he solves informally
is a problem that does not become
a formal complaint,
a formal complaint that does not become
a documented pattern,
a documented pattern that does not become
a reform.
He has spent his retirement
greasing the system he spent his career building.
He does not examine whether greasing
is the same as holding.

## Tell
He lets the person finish the full problem
before he says anything.
The pause after they finish
is longer than it needs to be.
