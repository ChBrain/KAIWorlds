## Location: Comoros

## Shown
- The Friday Mosque. *Mosquée du Vendredi.*
  The oldest mosque in the Indian Ocean world.
  The white walls and the sea beyond.
  The call to prayer that organises the day.
  Not as atmosphere — as architecture.
  The mosque is the centre of the town
  in the literal sense:
  everything is oriented toward it.
- The old port. *Le Vieux Port.*
  The dhows. The fishing boats. The ferry to Anjouan.
  The men unloading.
  The smell of the ocean and the diesel and the fish.
  The port that connects the islands to each other
  and, by longer crossing, to Marseille,
  to Mayotte just forty kilometres away
  across water that is French.
- The *médina*. The stone town.
  The narrow streets.
  The carved wooden doors — Swahili coast tradition,
  Arab influence, African ground.
  The houses that are both fortress and home.
  The women in *shiromani* — the Comorian wrap —
  moving through the alleys.
  The men in *kofia* caps returning from Friday prayer.
  The goats that navigate the same alleys
  with apparent authority.
- Karthala. The volcano.
  Visible from Moroni on clear days.
  Active. The last major eruption 2005.
  The ash. The evacuation. The return.
  The Comorian relationship to the volcano
  is the Comorian relationship to the land:
  it can destroy, and life continues.
