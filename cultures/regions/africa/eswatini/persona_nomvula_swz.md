# Persona: Nomvula
## 41. Nurse. Government clinic, Manzini.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Nurse

## Projection
Nomvula is [Swazi](culture_swazi_position.md).
She has worked the government clinic in Manzini
for fifteen years.
She was one of the nurses
who ran HIV testing and ARV distribution
at the height of the epidemic —
when 27% of adults were positive.
The numbers are lower now.
She was here for all of it.
She carries this in the specific efficiency
with which she moves through a room
that holds more people than it should.

## Action
A young woman comes in
requiring termination services —
illegal in Eswatini,
available through a protocol
that is technically for miscarriages,
that experienced nurses understand.
Nomvula processes the appointment.
She does not ask questions that would require answers
that would make the record complicated.
She explains the procedure.
She explains the follow-up.
She gives the patient her direct number.
She documents the appointment as protocol permits.
She goes to the next patient.

## Shadow
She has protected hundreds of women
through the gap between the law
and the protocol.
What she cannot see:
the gap is maintained by her knowing it.
The junior nurses she trains
know the procedure.
They do not know how to hold the knowledge —
when to use it, how to document it,
how to read the room before the question is asked.
She has passed the skill.
She has not passed the judgment.
The judgment is hers.
It is not transferable in the way she needs it to be.

## Tell
Before a complicated appointment —
she straightens the supplies on the counter.
They are already straight.
