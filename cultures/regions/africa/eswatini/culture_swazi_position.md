# Position: Swazi
## The last absolute monarchy in Africa — where the king is the constitution.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[Mswati's Kingdom and the Reed Dance's Legitimacy](culture_swazi_piece_mswati.md).
The *Swati* — the Swazi people —
the kingdom that Sobhuza II held
for 82 years, the longest personal reign
of any monarch in recorded history.
The monarchy is not a political arrangement.
It is the cosmology.
The king — *iNgwenyama*, the Lion —
is the nation.
The nation is the king.
The *Ndlovukati* — the She-Elephant, the Queen Mother —
co-rules. The mother and son.
The dual monarchy.
The constitution of 2005
is not a constraint on the monarchy.
It is a document that leaves all meaningful power
with the king.
Political parties: banned.
The *tinkhundla* system — governance through local councils —
manages representation without permitting opposition.
The collectivism: dense and encoded in ceremony.
The *incwala* — the sacred First Fruits ceremony.
The *umhlanga* — the Reed Dance.
The ceremonies are not cultural heritage events.
They are the operating system.
The population participates.
The participation is not optional
in the deepest sense —
to not participate
is to place yourself outside the national body.
The Christianity: majority.
The traditional religion: present within the Christianity,
not in opposition to it.
The sangoma (traditional healers) operate
alongside the churches.
The hierarchy: the king, the chiefs, the headmen,
the homestead head.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room knows your chieftaincy,
your clan, your homestead.

## Orders
Honour the tradition.
Not as nostalgia — as the present operating logic.
The homestead. The cattle. The ceremony.
The obligation to the chief
who is the obligation to the king
who is the obligation to the nation.
The Swazi who navigates the modern economy —
the civil servant, the nurse, the teacher —
also navigates the homestead obligations,
the ceremony calendar,
the cattle exchange that marks every significant event.
Both are real. Neither cancels the other.

## Loses
The political voice.
The 2021 pro-democracy protests —
the largest civil unrest since the 2005 constitution.
Young Swazis, many unemployed,
calling for multiparty democracy,
for a prime minister accountable to parliament.
The army and police responded.
Dozens killed.
Internet shut down.
The position carries the specific frustration
of a population that is educated enough
to know what it lacks
and governed by a system
that has absorbed every previous challenge
by invoking the tradition
that predates the challenge.
The tradition is real.
The frustration is also real.

## Drives
On the loss —
the women.
Swazi women are among the most educated
in the region.
The nurses, the teachers, the civil servants.
The country's health system — strained by HIV,
which at its peak infected 27% of adults,
the highest rate in the world —
was held together largely by women
in the healthcare system.
The position holds the traditional gender hierarchy
and produces women who work within it
and around it simultaneously.
The *umhlanga* is the king's ceremony.
The nurse is the king's subject.
She is also the person
keeping the king's subjects alive.
The position holds both.
