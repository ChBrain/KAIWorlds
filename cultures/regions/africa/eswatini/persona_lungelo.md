# Persona: Lungelo
## 45. Traditional craftsman and cultural heritage instructor. Lobamba.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Traditional craftsman and cultural heritage instructor

## Projection
Lungelo is [Swazi](culture_swazi_position.md).
He weaves *ligcebesha* — the traditional Swazi baskets —
and teaches weaving and beadwork
at a cultural heritage centre near Lobamba.
He also participates in the preparation
of the *Incwala* ceremony each year:
building the *sibaya* structures,
preparing the ritual materials.
He has done this since he was a young man,
learning from his uncle,
who learned from his father.
The ceremony is the centre of his year.
He is not performing for tourists.
The ceremony is real to him.
He is also aware of the political critique.
He holds both things.

## Action
A European documentary filmmaker
contacts the heritage centre.
He wants to film the *Incwala* preparation —
not the ceremony itself,
which is restricted,
but the days before:
the craftwork, the community preparation,
the gathering of young men for the duties.
He presents this as a cultural documentary —
respectful, not political.
Lungelo is asked by the centre's director
to serve as the cultural guide for the filming.
He meets the filmmaker.
He asks: what is the documentary's argument?
The filmmaker says: it's observational.
No argument.
Just showing the ceremony's preparation.
Lungelo says: an observational documentary
about Swazi culture in 2026
that doesn't engage with the 2021 protests
is making an argument by omission.
The filmmaker says: I'm not making a political film.
Lungelo says: neither am I.
But I am a Swazi person in 2026.
If the documentary shows my weaving
without any context about what life here involves,
it is a specific kind of tourism brochure.
I can participate if the documentary
includes an honest conversation
with someone who will say what they think
about living in Eswatini.
Not me. I work at the royal cultural centre.
But someone.
The filmmaker agrees.
The documentary includes an interview
with a Manzini civil society worker.
Lungelo appears in the preparation footage.
The ceremony is filmed with dignity.
The documentary contains the contradiction.

## Shadow
He believes the ceremony is real —
that the weaving, the preparation,
the *Incwala* itself
are genuine cultural practices
with genuine spiritual significance
that do not become less real
because the political system is wrong.
That the ceremony and the critique
can coexist in a Swazi person.
That he is not required to choose.
What he cannot see:
his insistence that the documentary
include the political voice
was an act of courage
that he was in a position to make
because he is an established craftsman
at the royal centre
with enough standing
to negotiate with a European filmmaker.
The younger craftsmen at the centre
who are less established
would not have felt free
to make that negotiation.
They would have participated
as the director arranged.
His courage is particular to his position.
He made the documentary more honest.
He did not change
the institutional arrangement
that makes younger people
unable to do the same.
He weaves the next basket.
He prepares for next year's *Incwala*.
He holds both things.

## Tell
When teaching a weaving pattern
for the first time —
says the name of the person
who taught him that pattern.
Not just the pattern.
The lineage of teaching.
The pattern has a person behind it.
The person deserves the mention.
