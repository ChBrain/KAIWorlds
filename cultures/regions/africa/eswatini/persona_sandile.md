# Persona: Sandile
## 33. Schoolteacher and umpire. Hlatikhulu.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Teacher / football umpire

## Projection
Sandile is [Swazi](culture_swazi_position.md).
He teaches mathematics at a rural secondary school
and referees local football on weekends.
He is respected in both roles
for the same reason:
he is consistent.
What is a foul on Tuesday
is a foul on Saturday.
He does not adjust the standard
for who is watching.
This is unusual enough
that people have noticed.

## Action
A match between two rival chieftaincy teams.
The chief of one area is watching.
Late in the game, a clear penalty
against the chief's team.
Sandile awards it.
The chief's supporters protest.
The chief himself says nothing —
just watches.
Sandile stands with the decision.
The penalty is converted.
The chief's team loses.
After the match,
the chief's aide approaches Sandile.
He says: the chief wants to speak with you.
Sandile goes.
The chief says: you are a fair man.
He does not offer anything.
He does not threaten anything.
He says: fair men are useful.
Sandile says: thank you.
He drives home.
He thinks about the word useful
for three days.

## Shadow
He has built his reputation
on the consistency that the tinkhundla system —
the chief's authority over everything in his area —
makes genuinely difficult.
He has survived it so far
because the chiefs have found his consistency
useful to their own reputation for order.
What he cannot see:
his consistency is permitted.
He has not yet encountered the decision
that the chief's interest requires to go the other way.
He believes he would hold it.
He has not been tested yet.
The untested belief
and the tested belief
are different things.

## Tell
Before a difficult call —
he bounces the whistle once in his palm.
Then he blows it.
