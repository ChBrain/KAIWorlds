# Piece: FRELIMO, the Forgotten North, and the Cabo Delgado Insurgency
## The province that gave the most to liberation — and received the least from independence.

## Owner
- **Place:** [Maputo / Pemba](culture_mozambican_place_maputo.md)

## Place
Two places, again.
Maputo: 2,700 kilometres south of Cabo Delgado.
The capital. The seat of FRELIMO.
Pemba: the capital of Cabo Delgado province.
The closest city to where the gas is
and where the insurgency runs.
The distance between them
is the piece.

## Load Bearing
Remove FRELIMO's post-independence governance
and Mozambique has an imperfect transition
from a liberation front to a contested state —
with a civil war anyway (RENAMO was externally financed),
with a north-south development gap anyway.
With it: FRELIMO turns the liberation front
into an uninterruptible governing party —
Mozambique has had FRELIMO governments
since 1975.
The party equates itself with the state:
*FRELIMO is Mozambique.*
The north is central to this mythology —
Cabo Delgado province is where
the liberation war began in 1964.
The Makonde people of the Mueda plateau
were FRELIMO's original foot soldiers.
Their sacrifice built the party's legitimacy.
What Cabo Delgado received in return:
the highest chronic malnutrition rates in Mozambique.
The lowest levels of private investment.
One of the few functioning economic activities:
ruby mining.
The rubies leave.
The poverty stays.
Then the gas was found.
2010: massive offshore natural gas deposits discovered
in the Rovuma Basin off Cabo Delgado.
The scale: among the largest in the world.
The promise: transformative wealth.
The deals: signed primarily with TotalEnergies and Eni.
The local communities: not consulted adequately.
The land: taken for the LNG facility.
The fishermen: displaced.
2015: the Mozambican government signs
a secret $2 billion loan
— the *dívida oculta* —
using state enterprises as collateral,
without parliamentary approval.
The money: to fund a maritime security company
and fishing enterprises.
Much of it: unaccounted for.
2017: the insurgency begins in Cabo Delgado.
Young men attack a police station in Mocímboa da Praia.
The insurgency calls itself *Al-Shabaab* locally
(no formal link to the Somali group).
Formally: Ansar al-Sunna.
Later acquiring links to ISIS.
The grievances it exploits:
poverty, exclusion, the gas wealth arriving
without benefit to locals,
FRELIMO's secular nationalism
seen as hostile to Islamic identity.
2021: insurgents take Palma —
the town nearest TotalEnergies' $20 billion LNG facility.
TotalEnergies suspends operations.
Rwandan troops deployed.
SADC mission deployed.
The insurgency pushed back from the coast.
The LNG project: partially resumed.
The insurgency: not resolved.
Remove the piece: a development challenge.
With it: the proof that a liberation movement
can liberate a country from one oppressor
and become a different kind of oppressor —
not with the same violence,
but with the same indifference
to the people who can't leave
and can't change what is done to them.
The Makonde who fought for FRELIMO in 1964
have grandchildren who are internally displaced
by FRELIMO's failure to govern what they fought for.

## Apparent
1962: FRELIMO founded in Dar es Salaam.
September 25, 1964: first attacks in Cabo Delgado.
1975: independence. Samora Machel first president.
1977: civil war begins. RENAMO formed with Rhodesian and South African backing.
1986: Machel dies in plane crash. Chissano succeeds.
1992: Rome Peace Accords. Civil war ends.
1994: first multiparty elections.
2004: Guebuza elected.
2014: Nyusi elected.
2014–2016: *dívida oculta* contracted. Parliament unaware.
2016: *dívida oculta* revealed by investigative journalists.
IMF suspends budget support.
Mozambique enters debt crisis.
2017: insurgency begins in Cabo Delgado.
2021: insurgents take Palma. TotalEnergies suspends LNG.
Rwandan troops intervene.
2021: SADC mission deployed.
2023: insurgents pushed back from coast.
LNG project partially resuming.
2024: Mozambican elections.
Frelimo's Mondlane claims victory.
Disputed. Protests. Venancio Mondlane — opposition —
claims fraud. Large demonstrations.
Post-election violence.
Nyusi's term: still contesting legitimacy.
2025–2026: situation still evolving.
The gas is still there.
The insurgency has not ended.
The FRELIMO state is still governing.
The arithmetic of 1964 — who sacrificed, who benefited —
is still unresolved.
