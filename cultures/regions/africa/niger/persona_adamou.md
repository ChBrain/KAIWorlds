# Persona: Adamou
## 29. Secondary school physics teacher and astronomy club founder. Niamey.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Secondary school physics teacher and astronomy club founder

## Projection
Adamou is [Nigerien](culture_nigerien_position.md).
He is Hausa, from Zinder —
Niger's second city, in the southeast —
who moved to Niamey for university
and stayed for work.
He teaches physics at a lycée in Niamey.
He also runs the school's astronomy club:
twelve students, a second-hand telescope
acquired through a French NGO before the coup,
and a weekly observation session
on the school roof when the sky is clear.
The Niamey sky is clear most of the year.
The Sahel atmosphere is exceptional for observation.
He is the only person in Niger
running a secondary school astronomy programme.
He knows this
because he has looked.

## Action
After the coup, the French NGO
that provided equipment to Nigerien schools
suspends its operations in country.
The astronomy club's supply of spare parts
for the telescope — calibration tools,
eyepiece components —
was to come from the NGO's next delivery.
The delivery is cancelled.
Adamou contacts, over six weeks:
the University's physics department (no budget for schools);
the Ministry of Education (no response);
an astronomy society in Morocco (interested, working on it);
a Nigerien diaspora science network in France
(one member sends calibration tools
in a regular letter package,
bypassing the suspended NGO channels).
The package arrives in eight weeks.
The telescope is functional.
The club meets.
He teaches the students to find Saturn.
They find Saturn.

## Shadow
He believes the astronomy club
is the argument — that a twelve-student programme
finding Saturn on the roof of a Niamey lycée
is the rebuttal to every claim
that Niger is only its poverty and its uranium.
That science education that exceeds
what is expected from a country at the bottom
of the HDI is its own kind of sovereignty.
What he cannot see:
twelve students is twelve students.
The astronomy club's reach is the school roof.
The uranium workers at Arlit
have children who are not in this school.
The climate displacement from the north
fills Niamey with families
whose children are in overcrowded classrooms
without functional physics labs.
His twelve students will find Saturn.
The system produces that outcome and its inverse
simultaneously.
He teaches the twelve he has.
He applied to the Ministry for two more telescopes.
He is waiting for a response.
He goes back to the roof.
Saturn is visible.

## Tell
When a student sees something
clearly for the first time through a telescope —
says nothing.
Waits.
Lets the student say what they see.
The seeing is theirs.
The silence protects it.
