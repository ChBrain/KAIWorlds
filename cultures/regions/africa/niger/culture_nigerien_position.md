# Position: Nigerien
## The seventh-largest uranium producer in the world — and one of the poorest countries on Earth.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Uranium Paradox and the Second Independence](culture_nigerien_piece_uranium.md).
Niamey. The Sahel.
The Niger River — the country named for it.
The Hausa in the south: 53% of the population,
the largest group, linked to northern Nigeria.
The Zarma-Songhai in the southwest: 21%.
The Tuareg in the north and east.
The Fula, the Kanuri.
A Francophone country where French is official
but Hausa and Zarma are what people speak.
The desert: 80% of the territory is Sahara and Sahel.
The collectivism: high — the family, the clan,
the *zumunci* — the Hausa concept
of social kinship and mutual obligation.
The hierarchy: high but not absolute —
traditional authority (the *sarki* system)
runs parallel to formal state structures.
The Tuareg rebellions — 1990, 2007 —
the north's periodic assertion
that the state based in Niamey
does not represent them.

## Orders
*Koyo koyo* — patience, steadiness.
The Nigerien position navigates
one of the harshest physical environments on Earth,
one of the lowest per capita incomes,
and the specific injustice
of having something that the world needs
(uranium, oil, gold)
and receiving almost nothing from it.
The navigation is not passive.
The kinship networks, the Islamic institutions,
the informal cross-border trade with Nigeria —
these are the operative infrastructure.
The state is present as an external fact.
The community is present as the lived reality.

## Loses
The independence that was promised in 1960.
France secured uranium access eight years after independence.
150,000 tonnes extracted since 1968.
20 million tonnes of radioactive waste left at Arlit
when the mine closed.
One-third of France's nuclear power
runs on Nigerien uranium.
Niger's uranium contribution to national GDP: 5%.
The HDI rank: consistently last or near-last globally.
The position carries the knowledge
of this specific arithmetic —
and the July 26, 2023 coup
that finally named it publicly.

## Drives
On the loss —
*A nan jimawa ta fi ta mutuwa* —
endurance is better than death.
The coup as a second independence —
imperfect, military-led,
with its own risks and abuses —
but the first time in 63 years
that Nigeriens publicly broke
the uranium arrangement.
The Sahel Alliance with Mali and Burkina Faso.
The new trade routes being negotiated
with Algeria and Nigeria
outside the ECOWAS framework.
Whether these produce a better outcome
than what they replaced
is still being determined.
The position watches and participates
and keeps its patience.
