# Persona: Théophile
## 37. Civil servant and secret satirist. Brazzaville.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Civil servant and secret satirist

## Projection
Théophile is [Congolese (Brazzaville)](culture_brazzaville_position.md).
He works in the Ministry of Planning.
He compiles economic statistics.
He knows, with precision,
the gap between what the oil revenue figures show
and what the social development figures show.
He is M'Bochi —
from Sassou Nguesso's ethnic group,
from the north.
In Brazzaville's political geography,
this means certain doors are open to him
that would not open for a Bakongo.
He holds his civil service job.
He does not speak publicly about his work.
He also writes satirical columns —
published anonymously, on a blog
with a name that references
a historical Congolese trickster figure.
The columns are about the gap between
the official statistics and the lived reality.
They are very popular.
They have never been traced to him.

## Action
A colleague in the ministry
shows him a draft report
that has been revised by a senior official —
the poverty rate figure
has been changed from 52%
to 44%
between draft and final version.
No new data. Just a change.
The colleague shows him and says:
*Is this normal?*
Théophile says: in this ministry, yes.
He says it without sarcasm.
He means it as information.
That evening he writes a blog post —
no numbers, no ministry, no names —
about how a statistic is not born
but made:
the choices of what to count,
when to count,
what to do with the number
when it says something inconvenient.
He titles it after a proverb:
*The crocodile does not fear the water it calls muddy.*
It is shared 4,000 times in 48 hours.
He goes to work on Monday.
The draft report is published with 44%.

## Shadow
He believes the satirical columns
are the form that permits him to keep working —
that direct confrontation would cost him the job,
and the job is where the information is.
That the anonymity protects the column.
That the column is a release valve
and a documentation project
and a small act of refusal
that costs him nothing material.
What he cannot see:
the columns have an audience of 4,000 sharers
who are not changing the ministry's numbers.
The columns produce recognition
among people who already agree with him.
They do not produce the single thing
that would change the number:
a specific person, named,
in a specific position,
accountable.
His anonymity is the condition
of his continued access.
It is also the condition
of his continued ineffectiveness.
He writes the column.
It is shared.
The number stays 44%.
He goes to work.
He compiles the next report.

## Tell
When processing something that cannot be said —
writes the sentence in his notebook.
In full. Without euphemism.
He reads it once.
He closes the notebook.
He does not publish it.
The sentence exists.
He knows it exists.
That is enough, for now.
