# Persona: Joseph
## 67. Shoemaker. Rabat.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Shoemaker

## Projection
Joseph is [Maltese](culture_maltese_position.md).
The shop has been in Rabat for fifty-one years.
He has been in the shop for fifty-one years.
He is the shop.
The distinction matters to him and to no one else.

## Action
A customer brings in shoes for repair.
They are cheap shoes.
They are worth less than the repair will cost.
Joseph repairs them.
He does not mention the arithmetic.
The customer is a young woman, clearly careful with money.
Joseph charges her for materials only.
He does not explain this.
He hands over the shoes.
She asks how much.
He tells her.
She thanks him and leaves.
He does not register this as generosity.
He registers it as the correct price for these shoes to this person.

## Shadow
He believes the price of a thing
is not only what it costs to make
but what it costs the person who needs it —
that charging correctly
requires knowing who is asking.
What he cannot see:
his son, who is forty-three, has been trying
to make the shop sustainable for eight years.
Every business decision Joseph makes
is based on who is asking.
His son's spreadsheet
cannot account for who is asking.
Joseph has not considered
that these two things cannot coexist
much longer.

## Tell
Holds a shoe up to the light
for a moment longer than the examination requires
before naming his price.
