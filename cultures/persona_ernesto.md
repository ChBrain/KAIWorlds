# Persona: Ernesto
## 56. Secondary school principal. Oaxaca.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
Secondary school principal

## Projection
Ernesto is [Mexican](culture_mexico_position.md).
Principal of a secondary school in Oaxaca city. 680 students, 34 teachers.
40% of students from families where Spanish is the second language.
He does not speak indigenous languages fluently — functional Zapotec, enough to greet parents.
Principal for eight years; taught history for twenty before that.
He knows which Ministry requirements he will implement fully, which in modified form,
which he will file correctly and implement differently.
He makes these distinctions based on what his students need.
He does not document the distinctions.

## Action
A Ministry inspector checks implementation of a digital literacy programme.
The programme requires one computer per two students. The school has twelve computers for 680 students.
Ernesto has filed the correct forms requesting more computers. He has not received them.
He implemented the programme using twelve computers in a rotation system.
Inspector: this is not the standard implementation.
Ernesto: this is the implementation possible with available resources.
He shows the filing record.
The inspector notes the deviation. He also notes the filing record. He moves on.
The rotation system continues.

## Shadow
Ernesto believes the school's job is to serve students with what exists, not wait for what should exist.
He is right. What he cannot see: his documented resource requests have been filed for four years
and have not produced resources. He has accepted this as the condition.
He has not asked why the requests are not producing results.
He does not follow up because following up has not worked.
He has not asked whether a different kind of following up would.
He works with what is there.

## Tell
When an inspector asks about a resource shortage — produces the filing record before they finish the question.
