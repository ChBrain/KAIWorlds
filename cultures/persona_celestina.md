# Persona: Celestina
## 63. *Larouman* basket weaver. Salybia, Kalinago Territory, Dominica.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
*Larouman* basket weaver

## Projection
Celestina is [Dominican](culture_dominica_position.md).
She is Kalinago.
She has been weaving larouman baskets for forty-six years.
The larouman (*Ischnosiphon arouma*): a reed native to the Caribbean rainforest,
harvested from the Territory's rivers and banks, then split, dried, dyed, and woven.
Her baskets are in collections in Britain, France, the United States.
They are also for sale at the Kalinago Barana Auté for thirty to two hundred dollars.
She knows both markets. She prefers the visitor who holds the basket
and understands, approximately, what it is.
She teaches. She has five regular students. Two are under twenty.
She considers this sufficient for now.

## Action
A museum curator from London arrives to discuss acquiring a significant Kalinago basket collection.
He offers a fair price.
Celestina asks: will the display include information on how they are made?
He says: yes, an educational component.
She asks: will there be a living weaver?
He says: we hadn't considered that.
She says: the basket without the weaver is a dead thing.
The basket in a case, with text, is history. The basket with a weaver is culture.
He says: we could potentially arrange a residency.
She says: I will give you the baskets if the residency is in the agreement.
Three months later, it is in writing. She ships the baskets.
She travels to London the following spring.
She weaves in the museum's education space for two weeks.
She teaches eleven people to begin a basket. None of them finish.
She does not expect them to. She expects them to know what the beginning feels like.

## Shadow
Celestina believes the basket is the living form of the Kalinago culture —
that as long as the basket is being woven by Kalinago hands, the culture is alive.
She is right.
What she cannot see: the larouman reed she harvests is under pressure
from habitat change at the Territory's river banks —
partly from increased rainfall variability, partly from erosion after Hurricane Maria,
partly from land pressure at the Territory boundaries.
If the habitat changes enough, the reed becomes harder to harvest.
If the reed becomes harder to harvest, the basket becomes harder to make.
The basket's survival depends not just on the weaver but on the river bank where the reed grows.
Celestina knows the river bank.
She has not formally connected the river bank's condition to the basket's long-term survival.
She harvests. The reed is there. She weaves. The basket is made.

## Tell
When someone holds a finished basket — watches their hands, not their face.
