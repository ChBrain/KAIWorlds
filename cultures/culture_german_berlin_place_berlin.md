## Location: Berlin, Germany

## Shown
- The Reichstag. The parliament.
  The glass dome: the public walks above the politicians.
  Designed by Norman Foster.
  Opened 1999 when the government arrived.
  The decision to put the dome there
  was a decision about transparency.
  It was a decision, not an accident.
- The Brandenburg Gate. Once a border.
  For 28 years: in the death strip.
  Nobody passed through.
  Now: a thoroughfare.
  Tourists photograph it every hour.
  The function changed.
  The stone did not.
- The cobblestone line. Through the city.
  Two rows of brass cobblestones.
  The wall's path.
  You can cross it without noticing.
  Or you can follow it.
- Mitte. Prenzlauer Berg. Kreuzberg. Neukölln.
  Each district: a different operating system.
  Mitte: government, tourism, money.
  Prenzlauer Berg: the Swabians, the children, the coffee.
  Kreuzberg: Turkish-German, alternative, gentrifying.
  Neukölln: the next Kreuzberg.
  The districts are the city.
  The city is the districts.
  No single Berlin exists.
- The construction cranes.
  Always present.
  Berlin is always unfinished.
  The city that was bombed to rubble,
  divided by a wall,
  reunified,
  and declared the capital
  has been building since 1990
  without stopping.
  The cranes are the Tell.
