# Piece: The Sassou Nguesso Dynasty
## The man who has governed for 47 years — Marxist, oil baron, civil war winner, dynasty builder.

## Owner
- **Place:** [Brazzaville](culture_brazzaville_place_brazzaville.md)

## Place
Brazzaville. The Palais du Peuple.
Where Sassou Nguesso has governed
since 1979 —
with one interruption, 1992 to 1997,
which he ended with a war.

## Load Bearing
Remove Sassou Nguesso
and the Republic of Congo has the transition it briefly had:
Lissouba's democracy, imperfect,
contested, fragile —
but an elected government.
With it: a small Central African petro-state
in which the same man
has governed for 47 years,
shifting ideology as needed —
Marxist-Leninist until 1990,
when the Soviet Union was collapsing,
then pluralist, then back to authoritarian,
whatever was required to hold power.
The oil: discovered offshore in the 1970s.
French company Elf — later Total — extracts it.
The revenue goes to the state.
The state is Sassou Nguesso.
His nickname: *Otchouembe* — the palm nut,
the invincible wrestler.
80% of exports are oil.
50% of the population lives below the poverty line.
The resource curse runs precisely
through the man who manages the resource.
His daughter Edith Lucie married Omar Bongo of Gabon.
His family has interests in property,
construction, media.
The French investigated the dynasty's assets.
The 2010 court case that forced French prosecutors
to investigate African heads of state
covered Sassou Nguesso along with Bongo and Obiang.
Forty bank accounts. Luxury properties. Paris apartments.
Remove the piece: a small Central African country
with its north-south tensions and its oil.
With it: the proof that the Cold War's logic —
a reliable client governs, regardless of
the cost to the population —
outlasts the Cold War itself.
Sassou Nguesso was the Soviet Union's man in 1979.
He is France's man now.
The man is the same.
The sponsor changed.
The oil was always the point.

## Apparent
1960: independence.
Fulbert Youlou: first president. Catholic priest. Anti-communist.
1963: *Les Trois Glorieuses* — popular uprising.
Youlou falls.
1968: Marien Ngouabi takes power.
Marxist-Leninist single-party state.
1969: Congolese Labour Party (PCT) founded.
Sassou Nguesso joins the leadership.
March 18, 1977: Ngouabi assassinated.
Sassou Nguesso serves as interim president briefly.
Declines to take permanent power.
1979: Sassou Nguesso takes power through PCT congress.
Becomes president.
Signs twenty-year friendship pact with Soviet Union.
Simultaneously maintains close relationship with France.
Maintains Elf's oil concessions.
1990: Berlin Wall falls.
Sassou Nguesso introduces multiparty politics.
1991: National Conference strips his executive powers.
He remains as ceremonial president.
1992: free elections. Pascal Lissouba wins.
Sassou Nguesso places third.
1993–1994: first civil war. Militia fighting.
1997: Second civil war.
Sassou Nguesso's Cobra militia fights Lissouba's Zulus and Ninja militias.
Angola sends troops in support of Sassou Nguesso.
October 1997: Sassou Nguesso retakes power.
8,000–10,000 dead. 800,000 displaced.
December 1999: peace agreement.
2002: elections. Sassou Nguesso wins.
2009: re-elected.
2015: referendum changes constitution. Third term permitted.
2016: re-elected.
2021: re-elected.
March 2026: re-elected.
82 years old.
Still governing.
The palm nut.
Invincible.
