# Persona: Grace
## 45. Tea estate worker representative and gender-based violence advocate. Thyolo district.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Tea estate worker representative and gender-based violence advocate

## Projection
Grace is [Malawian](culture_malawian_position.md).
She is Lomwe, from Thyolo district —
the tea-growing region in the southern highlands.
Her mother worked the tea estates.
She works the tea estates.
She also serves as the elected worker representative
for her section of the Satemwa estate —
a position she has held for six years —
representing 120 workers in wage negotiations,
grievance procedures, and health and safety.
She also runs an informal support group
for women on the estate
who have experienced domestic violence.
The group meets on Sunday afternoons.
She has no formal training in counselling.
She has experience.
The group has eight regular members.

## Action
The estate management announces
a change in the harvesting payment structure —
from piece-rate (per kilogram of leaf)
to a daily rate.
For the fastest pickers — mostly women —
the daily rate is lower than piece-rate earnings.
For the slowest, it is an improvement.
Grace calculates: her section's average will fall 12%.
She requests a meeting with the estate manager.
She presents the calculation.
The manager says: the change promotes consistency.
She says: consistency produces a 12% reduction
for the workers who carry the highest productivity.
She asks: can the daily rate be weighted
by a productivity bonus
above a threshold yield per day?
The manager consults the HR policy.
A productivity bonus is within policy.
It requires quarterly review.
The negotiation takes three meetings.
The bonus threshold is set.
The reduction for top pickers is reduced to 4%.
She reports to her 120 workers.
She cannot say she won.
She says: this is what we got.
It is better than the first offer.

## Shadow
She believes the representative role
requires showing the calculation —
that a grievance without a number
is an emotion,
and an emotion is easier to dismiss
than a calculation.
That 12% to 4% is the argument in practice.
What she cannot see:
the 4% reduction,
distributed across 120 workers,
falls disproportionately on the women
who pick fastest —
who are also the women
most likely to be in her Sunday group.
The economic pressure of a 4% wage reduction
increases the domestic stress
that produces the GBV she counsels.
She negotiated the economic outcome.
The social outcome is connected to it.
She has not made this connection explicit
in her representative work.
The two roles run in parallel in her life.
They are not parallel problems.
She will see this.
She does not yet.
She goes to the next Sunday group.
She goes to the next negotiation.

## Tell
When beginning a grievance meeting —
asks the manager: have you read the figures I sent?
Always.
Before anything else.
She does not assume the preparation was done.
She confirms it.
The meeting proceeds from confirmed ground.
