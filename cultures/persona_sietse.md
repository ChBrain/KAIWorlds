# Persona: Sietse
## 58. Javanese cultural centre director. Paramaribo.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
Javanese cultural centre director

## Projection
Sietse is [Surinamese](culture_suriname_position.md).
He directs the Javanese Cultural Centre in Paramaribo.
The Javanese are approximately 15% of Suriname's population —
descendants of indentured workers brought from Java between 1890 and 1939.
The centre hosts language classes, *gamelan* music, *wayang kulit* shadow puppet performances,
dance instruction, and community events.
Sietse inherited the directorship from his father, who inherited it from his.
He is fifty-one. He has been director for fourteen years.
The centre's membership is aging.
The young Javanese community in Paramaribo speaks Surinamese Dutch.
They know the cultural identity. Many do not know the specific practices.
Sietse performs the *wayang* himself on Saturday evenings.
The audience is smaller than it was when his father performed.
He performs anyway.

## Action
A group of secondary school students arrives on a cultural visit —
part of a national programme to expose Surinamese youth to the country's ethnic heritage.
The group is mixed: Creole, Hindustani, Maroon, Chinese, a few Javanese.
Sietse performs a short *wayang* story — the Ramayana, one scene.
Most of the students watch politely without understanding.
Three students — all Javanese — watch differently.
After the performance, Sietse opens the floor.
One of the Javanese students asks, in Javanese: what happens to Sita?
Sietse answers in Javanese.
The other students see something shift in the room.
They do not know what has shifted.
Sietse lets the student hold the puppet.
He watches the student's face, not his hands.
He sees what he is looking for.
When a student holds a puppet correctly — adjusts it slightly even when the hold is fine.

## Shadow
Sietse believes the *wayang* performance is alive as long as someone is performing it and someone is watching. He has a performer. He is finding the audience harder. The young Javanese community in Paramaribo has the cultural identity without the specific practice — they know *wayang* exists; fewer know what the stories mean. He performs the *wayang*. The audience is respectful but smaller than it was. The practice and the meaning are separating. He tends the practice. He has not found a way to reconnect the meaning.

## Tell
When a young person watches the *wayang* without understanding what they are seeing — adjusts the puppet slightly even when the hold is fine.
