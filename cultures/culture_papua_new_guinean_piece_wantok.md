# Piece: The Wantok System
## The obligation network that holds 800 languages together — and the resource wealth that keeps not reaching it.

## Owner
- **Place:** [Port Moresby](culture_papua_new_guinean_place_port_moresby.md)

## Place
Port Moresby. The capital.
The city that consistently ranks
among the least liveable in the world.
The settlements on the hills.
The razor wire on the suburban houses.
The LNG tankers in the harbour.
The gap between the tankers and the settlements
is the PNG economy made visible.

## Load Bearing
Remove the wantok system
and PNG is a linguistically fragmented post-colonial state
with no coherent social infrastructure
and extractive resource industries.
With it: the most comprehensive informal welfare system
in the Pacific —
the obligation network that means
the person who comes to Port Moresby
from the Highlands
arrives with a web of obligation already in place,
a series of doors that will open
because the person behind them speaks
a related language or shares a clan connection.
The wantok system is load-bearing for the society.
It is also load-bearing for the dysfunction:
the public servant who hires his wantok
ahead of the more qualified candidate,
the minister who directs infrastructure funding
to his home province,
the police officer who does not investigate
an assault by his wantok —
these are not failures of individual morality.
They are the operation of an obligation system
applied to a state infrastructure
built on different premises.
The Bougainville conflict is the counter-piece:
1988–1998, the Bougainville Revolutionary Army
against the PNG government and Rio Tinto's
Panguna copper mine.
The mine had taken 40% of PNG's export earnings.
The environmental destruction was total.
The landowners received minimal compensation.
The war killed 15,000–20,000 people.
The mine closed.
2019: the Bougainville independence referendum.
97.7% voted for independence.
The most decisive referendum result
in the modern Pacific.
Bougainville and PNG negotiating
the transition.
Independence not yet granted.
The result stands.
The negotiation continues.

## Apparent
40,000 BCE: first human habitation.
Among the earliest human settlements outside Africa.
1526: Portuguese contact.
1884: Germany claims northeastern Papua.
Britain claims southeastern Papua.
1906: Britain transfers Papua to Australia.
1919: Australia receives German New Guinea as League of Nations mandate.
1942–1945: Japanese occupation. Kokoda Track campaign.
1945: Australian administration resumes.
1975: independence. Michael Somare, first PM.
The *Melanesian Way* as founding philosophy.
1988: Bougainville Revolutionary Army formed.
Panguna mine closure.
1998: Bougainville Peace Agreement.
2000s: the Ok Tedi mine environmental settlement.
PNG LNG project: 2014 production begins.
ExxonMobil, Santos.
Revenue to state: significant.
Service delivery: inadequate.
2019: Bougainville referendum. 97.7% for independence.
2022: James Marape re-elected PM.
Slogan: "Take Back PNG."
The taking back: ongoing.
