# Piece: The Crown of Saint Stephen
## The crown that makes the king, not the king that wears the crown.

## Owner
- **Place:** Budapest

## Place
Budapest. The Parliament Building. Rotunda. Guard of honour, 24 hours.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and Hungary loses its continuity claim.
The Crown is a thousand years of Hungarian statehood in one object —
the frame against which post-1989 institutional disappointment
is experienced as disappointment rather than simply normal.

## Apparent
A crown. Byzantine-influenced.
The cross on top is bent — has been for centuries.
The bent cross reproduced exactly on the Hungarian coat of arms.
On every official document.
The parliament rotunda. Armed guard, 24 hours.

## Yearbook
1000 AD: Pope Sylvester II sends a crown to Stephen I.
The first King of Hungary.
The crown legitimises the kingdom.
Every Hungarian king until 1916 is crowned with it
or a crown claimed to be it.
1944: the Crown taken by Hungarian authorities
to prevent Soviet capture.
1945: handed to American forces.
Fort Knox, Kentucky. For 33 years.
1978: Jimmy Carter returns the Crown to Hungary.
Over domestic protest in the US.
The decision was diplomatic.
The Hungarians received it as sacred.
2000: moved to the Parliament.
Placed under permanent armed guard.
2011: the new Hungarian constitution
references the Crown of Saint Stephen
as the source of constitutional continuity.
A mediaeval crown made load-bearing
in a 21st century legal document.
