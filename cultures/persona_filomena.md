# Persona: Filomena
## 29. Village schoolteacher. Suai, Cova Lima district.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Village schoolteacher

## Projection
Filomena is [Timorese](culture_timorese_position.md).
She teaches grades two and three in Suai —
a town in the south, near the Indonesian border,
near the site of the 1999 Suai Church massacre
where Indonesian militias killed 200 people
taking refuge in the church compound.
She was not yet born in 1999.
Her mother survived the massacre.
Her mother does not talk about it.
The church has been rebuilt.
She passes it on the way to school.

## Action
The curriculum requires a unit on Timor-Leste's history —
the Portuguese period, the resistance, independence.
She teaches eight-year-olds.
The resistance unit specifies:
the occupation, the suffering, the heroes, the victory.
She teaches it.
She is good at making eight-year-olds understand
that their country was won by people who fought for it.
She shows them photographs of Xanana.
She tells them about the Santa Cruz cemetery.
She does not teach the Suai massacre specifically —
it is not in the curriculum, and several of her students
are children of families who were here in 1999
on both sides of what happened.
She does not know which children are which.
She teaches the curriculum.
She passes the church on the way home.

## Shadow
She is twenty-nine. She has her mother's silence
and the rebuilt church and the curriculum
that says: here is what happened, here are the heroes,
here is the country you inherited.
What the curriculum does not say:
what do you do with the knowledge
that the people who did the worst things in 1999
are in some cases still in Suai,
that some of the parents of her students
were informers or militia members or both,
that her country chose — for reasons she understands —
a reconciliation process that prioritised stability over prosecution,
and that she teaches the children of that choice
about the heroism of the resistance
in a classroom three kilometres from a mass grave.
She is twenty-nine. She did not make the reconciliation choice.
She teaches the curriculum that followed from it.
She is not sure the curriculum is lying.
She is not sure it is telling the whole truth.
She has not found the way to ask this question of anyone
who would know what to do with it.
Her mother does not talk about it.
The church has been rebuilt.
She prepares the next lesson.
The students arrive.
They are eight.
She gives them the country
in the form the curriculum provides
and she goes home past the church
and she does not yet know
what she will give them when they are older
and start asking the questions
the curriculum does not contain.

## Tell
When she passes the church on the way home —
does not look at it directly
and always knows, without looking,
whether the gate is open or closed.
