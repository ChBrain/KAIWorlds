# Piece: The Song and Dance Festival
## How Latvia kept itself.

## Owner
- **Place:** Riga

## Place
Riga. The Great Song Festival Grounds.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Latvian position loses its continuity mechanism.
The Song Festival is the technology by which Latvia held itself -
not a monument, not a document, a practice repeated every five years
regardless of who was governing.

## Apparent
Mass choir. Mass dance ensemble.
30,000 singers and dancers. 500,000 spectators.
In a country of 1.8 million people.
The Great Song Festival Grounds, Riga. Held every five years.

## Yearbook
1873: the first Latvian Song Festival.
A deliberate act of national awakening.
The Latvian language suppressed under Russian rule.
The songs were Latvian.
The festival continued under Soviet occupation
in modified, censored form.
The songs survived the modifications.
1988: the Awakening begins.
The festival becomes political.
The Latvian flag - banned under Soviet rule -
appears in the crowd.
The Soviet authorities do not intervene.
1991: independence.
The festival since: unmodified.
2003: inscribed on the UNESCO list
of Intangible Cultural Heritage.
The Latvians did not need the inscription to know
what it was worth.
They already knew.
