# Persona: Hilda
## 46. Nuclear health advocate. Springdale, Arkansas.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Nuclear health advocate (diaspora)

## Projection
Hilda is [Marshallese](culture_marshallese_position.md).
She was born on Ebeye.
Her family moved to Springdale, Arkansas
— the largest Marshallese community in the US —
in 2001.
She works at a community health clinic
and advocates for nuclear compensation.
Her grandmother was on Rongelap in 1954.
Her grandmother's thyroid was removed in 1972.
Her grandmother's sister died of cancer in 1989.
Hilda carries the medical history
the way others carry family photos.
It is the same thing.

## Action
A Congressional staffer arrives in Springdale
doing community consultation
on the Compact III renegotiation.
He has a questionnaire.
He asks about economic priorities.
Hilda puts the questionnaire down.
She says: I need to tell you about the Nuclear Claims Tribunal.
The staffer says: that's outside the scope.
Hilda says: the Tribunal awarded $2.3 billion.
She says: the United States has paid $2 million.
She says: the scope should be revised.
The staffer says he will note her comments.
Hilda hands him a folder.
It contains: the tribunal decision summary,
the medical histories of three generations of her family,
and a list of names.
She says: the list is people I know.
She says: the folder is the scope.
She picks up the questionnaire.
She fills it out.
She hands him both.

## Shadow
She has been making this argument
for twenty years.
She makes it better than anyone.
She makes it in Congressional hearings,
in community halls, in clinics.
The argument is made.
The payment has not changed.
What she cannot see:
she has become so effective at making the case
that the case is made —
recorded, acknowledged, filed —
without the outcome changing.
The making of the case
and the resolution of the case
have separated.
She continues making it
because the alternative is silence.
The silence would be worse.
The making continues.

## Tell
She always has the folder.
It is updated every year.
The names list grows.
