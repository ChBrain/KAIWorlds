# Piece: The 27-Year Civil War and the MPLA's Half-Century
## Cuba vs. South Africa in Angola's bloodstream — and the liberation movement that became the only government.

## Owner
- **Place:** [Luanda](culture_angolan_place_luanda.md)

## Place
Luanda. November 11, 1975.
Independence. The three movements
could not agree on a government.
Portugal left without handing power to anyone.
The MPLA held Luanda.
They declared the People's Republic of Angola.
UNITA and FNLA declared a rival state in Huambo.
The war had already started.
It would run for 27 years.

## Load Bearing
Remove the Cold War's intervention in Angola
and the country's three liberation movements
have a negotiated post-colonial transition —
imperfect, contested, but within the range
of what other post-colonial African states managed.
With it: Angola becomes the site
of the Cold War's most extensive African proxy war.
Cuba deploys 40,000–50,000 troops
to support the MPLA against South African invasion.
South Africa deploys in support of UNITA.
The United States funds UNITA through the CIA.
The Soviet Union arms both Cuba and the MPLA.
The war kills 500,000 people.
It destroys the infrastructure of a country
that could have been one of Africa's wealthiest —
oil, diamonds, fertile land, navigable rivers.
Then: Savimbi is killed in February 2002.
The MPLA and UNITA sign a ceasefire in April.
The war ends.
The MPLA has been governing for 27 years.
It continues governing.
Dos Santos governs for 38 years.
The oil funds the reconstruction
and the reconstruction funds the MPLA's legitimacy
and the MPLA's legitimacy funds the oil concessions
to the same operators who funded the war.
Remove the piece: a post-colonial state
with an imperfect liberation movement in power.
With it: the Cold War's most comprehensive
African destruction —
and the specific form of post-war governance
that follows when the party that won the war
has governed uninterrupted for 50 years
and the oil has made it
simultaneously wealthy and unreformable.
The UNITA burial in 2019 —
Savimbi's body, finally given back —
was the MPLA acknowledging
that the narrative it had maintained
for 17 years was costing more
than the concession.
The costs are still being calculated.
The oil is declining.
The MPLA is still governing.
The calculation continues.

## Apparent
December 1956: MPLA founded in Luanda.
February 1961: MPLA attack on Luanda prison. War of independence begins.
1966: UNITA founded by Jonas Savimbi.
April 25, 1974: Carnation Revolution in Portugal. Colonial war ends.
January 1975: Alvor Agreement. All three movements sign.
May 1975: agreement collapses. Fighting begins.
November 11, 1975: independence. MPLA in Luanda.
November–December 1975: Cuban troops arrive. South African forces advance.
South Africans pushed back. MPLA consolidates.
1977: Fractionism — MPLA internal purge.
Thousands killed.
1979: Neto dies. Dos Santos president.
1991: Bicesse Accords. Ceasefire.
1992: elections. MPLA wins. UNITA disputes results.
October 1992: Halloween Massacre — MPLA and crowds
kill thousands of UNITA members and civilians in Luanda.
War resumes. More brutal than before.
2002: Savimbi killed. War ends. Ceasefire.
2002–2017: Dos Santos governs.
Oil boom: 2002–2012.
Luanda briefly becomes Africa's most expensive city.
2017: Dos Santos retires. Lourenço elected.
2019: Savimbi burial. Partial reconciliation.
2022: elections. MPLA wins with 51%.
Lowest result ever.
UNITA: 44%. Strongest opposition showing.
Lourenço's anti-corruption process continues selectively.
Isabel dos Santos — Dos Santos's daughter —
prosecuted for corruption.
MPLA remains in power.
50 years.
