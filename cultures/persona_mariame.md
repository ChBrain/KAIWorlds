# Persona: Mariame
## 52. Former political prisoner's daughter and human rights documentation coordinator. Conakry.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Human rights documentation coordinator

## Projection
Mariame is [Guinean](culture_guinean_position.md).
Her father was detained at Camp Boiro in 1971
during Touré's purge of suspected plotters.
He was there for three years.
He came out. Many did not.
He never spoke about what happened.
He died in 2003.
Mariame learned what Camp Boiro was
from a French researcher in 2008
who found her father's name in an archive.
She has spent since 2009 —
since the stadium massacre —
building a documentation project:
gathering testimonies from Camp Boiro survivors
and from September 28, 2009 survivors and witnesses.
She works for an independent organisation.
She has received death threats twice.
She has not stopped.

## Action
A survivor she has been working with for two years —
a woman who witnessed the 2009 massacre
and survived it —
tells her she wants to withdraw her testimony.
She is afraid.
Her son has been told
by men she cannot identify
that his mother should be quiet.
Mariame meets with the woman.
She explains the withdrawal process:
the testimony can be removed from the public archive.
The documentary record of what happened
does not disappear — it is held in encrypted form
separately from the attributed testimonies.
She does not pressure the woman.
She explains exactly what withdrawal means
and what it does not mean.
The woman withdraws her attributed testimony.
She asks Mariame: will it still count?
Mariame says: yes.
It counts in ways that don't require your name.
The woman nods.
She leaves.
Mariame updates the archive.
She adds a note to the secure file:
the testimony was withdrawn under pressure.
The note is dated.
The documentation continues.

## Shadow
She believes testimony matters
even when it cannot be attributed —
that the accumulation of documented accounts
creates a record that eventually
exceeds the government's capacity to deny.
That each testimony added,
even anonymised,
moves the weight of evidence.
What she cannot see:
she is building toward a reckoning
she may not live to see.
The documentation project
is accelerating the timeline
at which the government
will need to respond.
The government knows this.
The death threats are the government's response.
She has accepted this as the price.
She has not, with full attention,
considered what the threats mean
for the people who work with her.
The woman who withdrew her testimony
is safer than she would have been
without Mariame's project.
She is also in danger she would not have been in
without Mariame's project.
Mariame knows this.
She continues.
She has decided
the documentation matters more
than the safety cost.
She has not checked this decision
with the people paying the safety cost.

## Tell
At the end of every testimony session —
offers tea and asks about the witness's family
before discussing the documentation.
The family question first.
Always.
