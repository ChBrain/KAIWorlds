## Load Bearing
Remove Guaraní and Paraguay is a landlocked South American country with a Spanish colonial heritage and one of the most extreme inequality profiles in the Americas. With it: Paraguay is the only country in the Americas where an indigenous language is co-official and genuinely spoken by the majority of the population — not as a museum language but as the daily language of intimacy, family, and the interior life.

## Apparent
1537: Asunción founded. The Guaraní and Spanish intermarry from the beginning. 1811: independence. The Guaraní-speaking majority remains. The Triple Alliance War (1864–1870): Paraguay loses 60–90% of its male population. The country rebuilt by the surviving women, in Guaraní. 1992: Guaraní becomes co-official with Spanish. Schools required to teach in both. The result: most Paraguayans are genuinely bilingual. The switch: when a Paraguayan switches from Spanish to Guaraní mid-conversation, they are often switching from the formal register to the real one.
