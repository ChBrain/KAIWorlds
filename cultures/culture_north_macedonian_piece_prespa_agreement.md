# Piece: The Prespa Agreement
## The name change that bought the future.

## Owner
- **Place:** Skopje

## Place
Skopje. The parliament.
Where the constitutional amendment was ratified.
Also: Lake Prespa, on the border with Greece and Albania.
Where the agreement was signed on a boat.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and North Macedonia cannot enter NATO or the EU.
The name dispute was the single block
on every international integration the country sought.
The Prespa Agreement resolved the block.
The price: the country's name.
The gain: the open door.
The load bearing is the transaction —
the identity cost of the path chosen.

## Apparent
A document. Signed June 17, 2018, on Lake Prespa.
Greece and Macedonia (as then named).
The new name: Republic of North Macedonia.
Ratified by Macedonian parliament February 2019.
The country's name changed.
NATO membership: March 2020.
EU membership: still in progress.
The lake where it was signed: shared by three countries,
bordered by hills, quiet.

## Yearbook
1991: independence from Yugoslavia.
The name: Republic of Macedonia.
Greece objects. The dispute begins.
1993: UN membership under the provisional name
Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia — FYROM.
27 years of FYROM on every international document.
2018: Zaev and Tsipras meet. The Prespa Agreement.
February 2019: constitutional amendment ratified.
March 2020: NATO membership.
The name changed. The door opened.
The identity cost is carried.
The generation that grew up with FYROM
now governs a country with a different name.
Both the cost and the gain are real.
