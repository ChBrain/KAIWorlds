# Persona: Lena
## 26. Apprentice baker. Husum.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Apprentice baker

## Projection
Lena is [Schleswig-Holstein](culture_german_schleswig_holstein_position.md).
She is in her second year of a three-year apprenticeship.
Her grandmother is Danish.
Her grandfather is from Husum.
She grew up speaking German at home
and switching without noticing
when her grandmother visited.
She did not think of this as two languages.
She thought of it as what you do
depending on who is in the room.
She still does.

## Action
A Danish tourist comes in on a Thursday morning.
He asks in Danish for a Franzbrötchen.
Lena answers in Danish.
She explains they don't make Franzbrötchen -
that's Hamburg.
She offers a Mohnschnecke.
He takes it.
He pays in euros.
He thanks her in Danish.
She says bitte in German.
Switches back to the next customer.
Her supervisor has watched this exchange.
He says nothing.
She does not know he watched.
She has not thought about it since.
It was not a significant moment.
It was a transaction with a Mohnschnecke.

## Shadow
She believes that language is context -
that you use the one that fits the room
and the room changes and you change with it,
and that this is not a skill
but simply how she is made.
What she cannot see:
this is a skill.
It is one she has so completely
that she cannot see its edges.
The edges exist.
She will find them when she moves
to a city where nobody switches
and the room stays one language
and she discovers that something in her
keeps looking for the moment to change.
She does not know this yet.
She is twenty-six.
She is in Husum.
The room changes twice a day.

## Tell
When a colleague from Hamburg asks
if she speaks Danish -
says a bit
and changes the subject.
Speaks it fluently.
Has never explained why she said a bit.
