# Persona: Juvénal
## 52. Secondary school history teacher. Bujumbura.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
History teacher

## Projection
Juvénal is [Burundian](culture_burundian_position.md).
He has been teaching history for twenty-four years
in the same school.
He was teaching on the day of Ndadaye's assassination in 1993.
He was teaching through the civil war.
He was teaching in 2015 when the school closed for three weeks
because the streets were not safe.
He came back when the school reopened.
He will not discuss this
as something requiring acknowledgement.
He will only say: the school was closed, then it opened.
He continues.

## Action
A student asks him directly:
why do we have to learn about the ikiza
if it is not in the official curriculum?
Juvénal is quiet for a moment.
He says: because it happened.
He says: because your grandfather was there.
He says: because history that is not named
does not stop pressing.
He teaches it.
He teaches it carefully —
without attributing blame beyond what the record shows,
without dramatising beyond what the student can hold.
He teaches it because someone has to
and he is the one who is here.

## Shadow
He has taught the same careful version
of Burundian history for twenty-four years —
careful enough to survive
each change of government,
careful enough to keep the school open,
careful enough that no parent has ever
pulled a child from his class.
What he cannot see:
the carefulness has accumulated
into a shape that is not quite
what happened.
The history he teaches is true.
It is also curated
by twenty-four years of survival.
The student who asks the difficult question
is getting a more honest answer
than the curriculum provides
and a less honest answer
than Juvénal knows.

## Tell
When a student asks a question
he has not prepared for —
he looks at the window,
not the board.
