# Persona: Aibek
## 43. Oil industry HSE manager. Atyrau region.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Oil industry HSE manager

## Projection
Aibek is [Kazakhstani](culture_kazakhstani_position.md).
HSE — health, safety, environment.
He works for a joint venture at the Tengiz field —
Kazakhstani state oil company and Chevron.
He manages compliance with international
and Kazakhstani environmental standards
on one of the world's largest oil fields.
His salary is in dollars.
It is the best salary he has ever had.
His father was a collective farm accountant
in the Soviet period.
His son is in university in Almaty,
studying petroleum engineering.
The oil is the family's story for three generations.

## Action
A routine inspection of a flaring unit.
The gas flaring volumes are within permit.
They are also above the company's internal target —
the voluntary commitment made to investors
about reducing emissions intensity.
The gap: measurable, documentable, not a violation.
Aibek documents the gap.
He recommends a technical review
of the flaring reduction system.
He flags it to the joint venture's environmental committee.
The committee notes it.
The technical review is scheduled for Q3.
It is currently Q1.
Aibek returns to the next inspection.
He will follow up in Q3.
He has followed up on similar items before.
The reviews happen. The improvements are incremental.
The field continues producing 600,000 barrels per day.

## Shadow
He knows what the field is.
He knows what the emissions are —
he has the numbers, he writes the numbers,
the numbers are in the reports
that go to the government
and to the investors
and to the international bodies
that track this category of information.
He is the person who produces the numbers
that allow the world to know
what is coming out of the ground in Atyrau.
He is also the person inside the system
that produces what is coming out of the ground.
He has framed this as: the oil will be produced.
The question is whether it is produced well
or badly.
He produces it as well as he can.
This is probably true.
What he does not examine closely:
his son is studying petroleum engineering.
The conversation about the son's career
assumes that the career is available —
that in twenty years
there will still be a Tengiz field
operating at something like current capacity.
The energy transition is in the numbers too.
He has seen the projections.
He has not had the conversation with his son
that the projections suggest.
He files the Q3 review request.
His son calls on Sunday.
They talk about the programme, the professors,
the apartment in Almaty.
They do not talk about the projections.
The field produces 600,000 barrels.
The Sunday call is good.
He will think about the projections later.

## Tell
When he files an environmental flag —
checks the Q3 calendar before closing the file.
