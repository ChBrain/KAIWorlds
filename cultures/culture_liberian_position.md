# Position: Liberian
## The land of the free — founded on the freedom of some and the colonisation of others.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Americo-Liberian Contradiction and the Civil Wars](culture_liberian_piece_americo.md).
1847: the Declaration of Independence.
A country founded by formerly enslaved African Americans
who escaped one system of oppression
and built another.
The motto: *The love of liberty brought us here.*
The indigenous people: 95% of the population,
excluded from citizenship until 1904,
governed under a caste system
until 1980.
The collectivism: strong within each group.
The Americo-Liberian *Congo* community
with its Masonic lodges and its clapboard houses.
The 16 indigenous communities —
Kpelle, Bassa, Gio, Kru, Grebo, Loma,
Mano, Krahn, Gola, Gbandi, Mende,
Mandingo, Kissi, Vai, Belle, Dei —
with their own languages, authority structures,
land rights, social organisation.
The hierarchy: steep within each group.
The chief, the elder, the *zoe* — the sacred society.
The Poro and Sande societies:
the male and female initiation societies
that govern social transitions,
resolve disputes, hold knowledge.
They were here before the Americo-Liberians came.
They are here now.
The two worlds coexisted in separate registers
for 133 years
until the 1980 coup
brought them into the same room violently.

## Orders
*Survive.*
Not as resignation — as the specific intelligence
of a country that has been in crisis
for most of its modern history.
The 1980 coup. The civil wars: 1989–97, 1999–2003.
250,000 dead. 90% economic contraction.
A country that was destroyed
and rebuilt
and is building again.
The position navigates the rebuilt country
with the knowledge of what the destruction cost
and the vigilance of someone
who knows it can happen again.

## Loses
The promise.
The Americo-Liberians promised a republic.
They built a caste system.
The indigenous majority overthrew it in 1980 —
Samuel Doe's coup —
and the new order was worse.
Then the civil wars.
Then Ellen Johnson Sirleaf — Nobel Peace Prize, 2011.
Then the Ebola crisis, 2014–16: 4,800 dead.
Then George Weah — football legend, elected president 2018.
Then Joseph Boakai — elected 2024.
The position carries the accumulation of
promised fresh starts
and the knowledge that the foundation
was never fully repaired.

## Drives
On the loss —
*Go on.*
Liberia rebuilds.
The rubber industry runs.
The iron ore runs.
Monrovia grows.
The diaspora sends money.
The country that was 90% economically contracted
in 2003
has rebuilt its institutions slowly.
Not enough. Not fast enough.
But: present.
The Poro and Sande societies persist.
The indigenous communities maintain their authority structures.
The country exists.
The building continues.
