# Piece: The Empty Villages
## The houses are still standing. The people who built them left.

## Owner
- **Place:** Rostock

## Place
Mecklenburg-Vorpommern. The inland.
Not the Baltic coast - the coast has tourists.
The inland: Brandenburg Strasse in a village
whose population has halved since 1990.
The houses: still standing.
The school: closed 2003.
The post office: closed 2008.
The last shop: closed 2015.
The church: open Sundays, when the travelling pastor comes.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the MV position loses its defining pressure.
The emptying is not a background condition.
It is the foreground.
Everything else - the Baltic coast, the Hanseatic towns,
the Brick Gothic, the lakes -
is experienced against the knowledge
that the people who could have stayed
mostly didn't.
200,000 left between 1990 and 2000.
The median age exceeds 51.
The position carries the weight of the people
who are not there.

## Apparent
The Leerstand - the empty standing.
Vacant houses in villages.
Some decaying. Some purchased by outsiders
for renovation.
The renovation sometimes brings new people.
Sometimes it brings weekend houses.
The village is still empty Monday through Friday.
The Wendehals - the turning neck -
the person who turned with the political wind in 1990
and is now neither trusted by the old system
nor at home in the new one.
The AfD result in MV: among the highest in Germany.
Not because MV is uniquely far-right.
Because the people who remained
include a high proportion
who felt the promise of 1990 arrive only partially.
The Baltic coast: white sand, chalk cliffs on Rügen,
resort towns from the 19th century
that still look like the 19th century.
In July: full.
In November: empty.
The emptying seasonal. The inland emptying permanent.

## Yearbook
1990: reunification. The shipyards cannot compete.
The collective farms are dissolved.
Employment falls 70% in some sectors.
1991: unemployment above 20% in some districts.
The exodus begins.
1990–2000: net migration loss of 90,000.
By 2000: total population decline over 200,000.
2007: Angela Merkel hosts the G8 at Heiligendamm.
On the Baltic coast. The rest of the state watches.
2011: population 1.6 million.
It was 1.95 million in 1990.
The projection: 1.4 million by 2040.
The Baltic coast: growing.
The inland: continuing to empty.
The houses: still standing.
