# Piece: The Separation
## August 9, 1965. The day Singapore was expelled from Malaysia and had to become a country.

## Owner
- **Place:** Singapore

## Place
Singapore. The Padang.
The field in front of the colonial buildings
where Lee Kuan Yew announced independence
on a television broadcast
that the cameras recorded
and the nation watched.
He was crying.
This is not forgotten.

## Load Bearing
Remove the Separation and Singapore is a trading port
with a disputed ethnic composition
and a plausible case for being part of Malaysia.
With it: Singapore is the country that was expelled —
not liberated, expelled —
and turned the expulsion into the founding logic
of everything it subsequently built.
The anxiety is the engine.
The position that has no right to exist
works harder than the position that does.
Lee Kuan Yew cried on television
because he understood what had happened:
a city of three million people,
no resources, surrounded by larger and sometimes hostile neighbours,
had just been required to survive on its own.
The survival project became the state.
The state became the model.
Remove the piece: Singapore is a well-run city.
With it: Singapore is a well-run city
that knows, at a foundational level,
that the alternative to being well-run is extinction —
and that this knowledge, transmitted across generations,
produces a specific kind of person
who performs not from confidence
but from the memory of a morning in 1965
when there was no margin.

## Apparent
1819: Stamford Raffles establishes a British trading post.
The strategic location: the Malacca Strait, the trade routes.
1942: the Japanese invasion. The fall of Singapore.
Churchill calls it the worst disaster in British military history.
The colonial myth of invincibility: gone.
1945: liberation. The British return.
The question: return to what?
1959: self-government. Lee Kuan Yew and the PAP win the election.
1963: merger with Malaysia. The Malaysian Malaysia debate.
Lee Kuan Yew argues for equal citizenship regardless of ethnicity.
The Malay political establishment does not accept this.
1964: racial riots in Singapore.
1965: the separation. July 9, the agreement. August 9, the announcement.
Lee Kuan Yew at the microphone, composing himself.
The founding of the Republic of Singapore.
1965 GDP per capita: $516.
2024 GDP per capita: $88,000.
One of the highest in the world.
The Changi Airport, consistently ranked first globally.
The port: the second busiest in the world.
The water: imported from Malaysia, recycled as NEWater, desalinated.
The survival problem: solved by engineering.
The anxiety that drove the engineering: not solved.
It is the position.
It runs continuously.
