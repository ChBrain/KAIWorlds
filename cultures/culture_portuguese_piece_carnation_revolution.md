# Piece: The Carnation Revolution
## The revolution where soldiers put flowers in their rifles.

## Owner
- **Place:** Lisbon

## Place
Lisbon. The Carmo Square.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and Portuguese saudade has no counterpoint.
The Carnation Revolution was the release of weight
carried for 48 years of dictatorship —
the lightness that makes the grief saudade rather than simply sadness.

## Apparent
April 25, 1974. A Thursday.
The song on the radio at 12:20 AM: *Grândola, Vila Morena.*
Banned under the dictatorship.
Carnations from the Ribeira market in the rifle barrels.
The soldiers did not remove them.

## Yearbook
1926: military coup. The republic ends.
1933: Salazar. The Estado Novo.
48 years of authoritarian rule.
Colonial wars in Africa: Angola, Mozambique, Guinea-Bissau.
Draining. Contested.
The junior officers fight the colonial wars.
They come home with questions.
April 25, 1974: the revolution.
The colonial wars end immediately.
1975: independence for the African colonies.
Portugal smaller but lighter.
1986: EU membership.
The weight carried for 48 years deposited somewhere.
The country moves.
*Saudade* for what was lost.
The Carnation Revolution for what was found.
Both operating simultaneously.
This is the Portuguese position.
