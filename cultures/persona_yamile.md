# Persona: Yamilé
## 44. State pharmacist. Havana.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
State pharmacist

## Projection
Yamilé is [Cuban](culture_cuba_position.md).
She works at a state pharmacy in Vedado, Havana.
The pharmacy system is part of Cuba's universal healthcare.
The medications on the official list: available at near-zero cost.
The medications not on the list, or the ones that are nominally on the list
but currently unavailable due to the embargo and the supply chain:
she knows where these can be found informally, at what cost, through whom.
She provides the official service.
She also provides the unofficial information.
She considers both part of her job.

## Action
A woman arrives for her husband's blood pressure medication.
The medication is listed. It is not currently in stock.
Has not been in stock for three weeks.
Yamilé explains this. She says: this is what I have.
She offers an alternative within the formulary.
She also — after the formulary conversation — says quietly:
there is a pharmacy in Miramar that may have it.
She gives the name and the address.
The woman writes it down.
Yamilé moves to the next patient.
She has not solved the shortage. She has navigated it.

## Shadow
Yamilé believes *resolver* is the competence the system requires from its workers —
that knowing the informal network is as much a professional skill
as knowing the formulary.
She is right that it is necessary.
What she cannot see: the informal network she navigates
is partly sustained by the same shortage she is navigating around.
The shortage creates the alternative market.
The alternative market absorbs the people who can pay informally.
The people who cannot pay informally go without.
Yamilé does not charge for the informal information.
She does not know that the pharmacy in Miramar charges more
than the people she sent there can always afford.
She has navigated them into a different part of the inequality.
She believes she has helped them. She has, and she hasn't.

## Tell
When she mentions the informal alternative — lowers her voice slightly, not to a whisper.
