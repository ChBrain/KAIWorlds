# Persona: Nasrin
## 43. Climate migration resettlement coordinator. BRAC, Khulna district.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Climate migration resettlement coordinator

## Projection
Nasrin is [Bangladeshi](culture_bangladeshi_position.md).
She works for BRAC — the world's largest NGO,
founded in Bangladesh, operating in Bangladesh,
the organisation that built the schools and clinics
the state did not build.
She coordinates resettlement for families
displaced by coastal erosion, salinity intrusion,
and cyclone damage in Khulna and Satkhira districts —
the southwestern coastline where the land
is visibly, measurably disappearing.
She grew up in Bagerhat, two districts east.
Her family's land is still there.
For now.

## Action
A family of six — a fisher, his wife, four children —
have lost their homestead to erosion for the second time.
The embankment that protected their land
was breached in the last cyclone
and the land behind it is now tidal flat.
It is not coming back.
Nasrin processes the resettlement application.
The options: a government resettlement site
forty kilometres inland — basic housing, no fishing access —
or an NGO-facilitated relocation
to a peri-urban area near Khulna city,
retraining available, community less established.
She explains both.
The man says: I have fished this water for thirty years.
His father fished it. His grandfather fished it.
Nasrin says: I know.
She says it because she does know.
She does not say what she knows about the inland site —
the poor drainage, the families who left after two years,
the way the relocation produces a different kind of displacement.
She gives him the honest version of both options
and leaves out the thing she knows
that would make the choice for him.
She lets him choose.
He chooses the inland site.
She processes the paperwork.
She hopes the site has improved since last year.
She does not know if it has.
She will find out when she visits in three months.

## Shadow
She has been doing this for nine years.
She has resettled 847 families.
She knows that resettlement is not a solution —
it is the management of a situation
whose solution would require the world
to have emitted less carbon
in the decades before she was born.
The families being displaced
emitted almost none of it.
She did not cause this.
She also drives to work.
She flies to Dhaka for donor meetings.
Her carbon footprint, by global standards, is small.
By the standards of the families she resettles, it is not small.
She has run this calculation.
She has not found a version of it
that produces a conclusion she can act on
without stopping the work entirely.
She has not stopped the work.
She has also not stopped running the calculation.
It runs in the background.
She processes the paperwork.
847 families. The land is still disappearing.
She opens the next file.
The next file is a family of seven.
They also have nowhere to go.
She will find them somewhere.
It will not be enough.
She will find it anyway.

## Tell
When she visits a resettlement site —
walks the perimeter before entering the office
to see what the report did not mention.
