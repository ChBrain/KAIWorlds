# Piece: The Winter War
## The 105 days that told Finland who it was.

## Owner
- **Place:** Helsinki

## Place
Finland. The cemeteries.
25,000 Finnish dead, buried in Finnish ground.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and *sisu* is a word without evidence.
The Winter War field-tested the Finnish position
against a force twelve times its size
and found it structurally real.

## Apparent
The Mannerheim Line — improvised defensive fortifications.
Finnish ski troops in white camouflage.
The temperature: minus 40 Celsius on some days.
Field Marshal Mannerheim.
His portrait in every Finnish school.

## Yearbook
November 30, 1939: the Soviet Union invades Finland.
The League of Nations expels the USSR.
The world watches. Nobody intervenes.
Finland fights.
The Mannerheim Line holds thirty Soviet divisions for three months.
February 1940: the Soviets break through.
March 12, 1940: the Moscow Peace Treaty.
Finland cedes 11% of its territory.
Finland keeps its sovereignty.
The distinction between ceding territory and ceasing to exist
is what *sisu* produced.
1941–1944: the Continuation War. Finland fights again.
Sovereignty retained again.
1995: Finland joins the EU.
2023: Finland joins NATO.
The silence about what it cost to get here
is still in the room.
