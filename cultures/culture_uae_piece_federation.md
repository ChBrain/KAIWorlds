# Piece: The Federation and the Pearl
## 1971. Seven sheikhdoms become one country. And the pearl diving economy that preceded everything.

## Owner
- **Place:** Abu Dhabi

## Place
Abu Dhabi. The Corniche.
The palm trees. The Gulf.
Across the water: nothing visible.
The oil platforms are below the horizon.
The pearl beds are deeper still.
The Heritage Village: reconstructed
pearl diving boats, reconstructed
Bedouin tents.
The thing that was, shown to the thing that came after.

## Load Bearing
Remove the 1971 federation
and the UAE is seven separate sheikhdoms —
Abu Dhabi with the oil,
Dubai with the trade ambition,
the others smaller still —
each navigating the British withdrawal
and the Saudi pressure and the Iranian threat alone.
With it: a country that pooled
its sovereignty into a federal structure
while maintaining the emirate-level autonomy
that allows Abu Dhabi and Dubai
to run essentially different economic models
under the same flag.
The pearl diving is also load-bearing —
not as economy (it ended when Japanese cultured pearls arrived in the 1930s)
but as the founding story:
the Emirati position before the oil
was a position built on the most dangerous
and most communal labour —
the diver who holds his breath
for ninety seconds at the bottom
to bring up what may or may not be there,
the community of the dhow that cannot function
without every person pulling.
The pearling economy produced
the cultural values that the oil economy
then had to preserve against the logic of wealth:
the *majlis*, the communal obligation,
the hospitality that does not require
the guest to have anything to offer.
The oil replaced the pearl.
The values the pearl produced
became heritage the moment the pearl was gone.
Remove the piece: an oil federation with trade ambitions.
With it: a federation that knows what it was
before the oil — knows it precisely,
in the family stories and the maritime museum —
and is using that knowledge
to hold an identity together
in a city where 89% of the residents
have no connection to what it was.

## Apparent
Pre-oil: the Trucial States. British protection treaties.
The pearl economy. Seasonal diving. The debt system that trapped divers.
The traders: Indian, Persian, Baluchi — already cosmopolitan before the oil.
1930s: the Japanese cultured pearl arrives. The pearling economy collapses.
The Trucial States in poverty.
1958: oil discovered in Abu Dhabi.
1966: Sheikh Zayed becomes ruler of Abu Dhabi.
1971: the British withdraw from the Gulf. December 2.
Six sheikhdoms federate. Ras Al Khaimah joins in 1972.
The United Arab Emirates.
Sheikh Zayed: the founding father. His image everywhere.
Dubai: Sheikh Rashid's vision. The port. The free zone. No oil, so trade.
1985: Emirates airline founded.
1999: the Burj Al Arab opens. The sail-shaped hotel. The announcement.
2010: the Burj Khalifa. 828 metres. The tallest building in the world.
Named for the president of the UAE after Abu Dhabi provided the financing
when Dubai's debt crisis hit.
The name change: politics made architecture.
2020: the Abraham Accords. UAE normalises relations with Israel.
The position plays the long game.
The pearls are in the museum.
The towers are visible from everywhere.
Both are the same place.
