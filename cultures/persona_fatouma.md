# Persona: Fatouma
## 29. Midwife. Mutsamudu, Anjouan.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Midwife

## Projection
Fatouma is [Comorian](culture_comorian_position.md).
She trained in Réunion on a scholarship,
returned to Anjouan because her mother asked her to.
She delivers 200 babies a year
in a facility that should handle 80.
She is the only qualified midwife in her district.
She knows this.
She does not say it.
The saying would require someone to respond.
There is no one to respond.

## Action
A woman arrives in labour, third child,
previous complications not recorded anywhere
because the records are in a system
that was replaced and the transfer was incomplete.
Fatouma asks the woman directly.
The woman does not know the clinical terms.
Fatouma asks differently —
what happened last time, in her own words.
The woman describes it.
Fatouma hears what she needs to hear.
She adjusts her approach before the delivery.
The delivery is difficult.
It is managed.
The child is born.
Fatouma writes the woman's history
in her own notebook,
in Shikomori,
in terms the woman used.
It will not transfer to the system.
It will be available next time
if it is Fatouma who attends.

## Shadow
She has built a parallel record system —
her own notebooks —
because the official system
cannot be trusted to hold
what the official system should hold.
She trains the junior nurses
from her notebooks.
What she cannot see:
the parallel system is brilliant
and is entirely inside her.
When she leaves — to Mayotte,
to France, to wherever the salary
eventually makes the leaving rational —
the system leaves with her.
She has made herself the institution.
She has not built the institution.

## Tell
She checks her notebook
before any complicated delivery.
Not the patient's file.
Her notebook.
