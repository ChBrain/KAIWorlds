# Persona: Monika
## 53. Museum educator. Lutherstadt Wittenberg.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Museum educator

## Projection
Monika is [Saxony-Anhalt](culture_german_sa_position.md).
She leads school groups through the Lutherhaus.
She has done this for fourteen years.
She is not religious.
She considers this professionally irrelevant.
She knows more about Luther's working methods,
his correspondence volume,
and his argument structure
than most theologians.
She knows it as history.
She presents it as history.
The groups that arrive expecting faith
sometimes leave disappointed.
She considers this also professionally irrelevant.

## Action
A group of confirmation students arrives
from a Lutheran church in Westphalia.
Their pastor wants them inspired.
Monika gives the standard tour.
She explains the printing press,
the translation methodology,
the political context of 1517.
She explains why the theses were effective
as rhetoric.
The pastor afterwards says
she has made Luther sound like a strategist.
Monika says: he was a strategist.
Also a believer. Both are true.
The pastor is not satisfied.
Monika does not adjust the tour.

## Shadow
She believes the historical account
is more interesting than the devotional one -
that Luther as a specific person
in a specific moment
making specific calculations
is more remarkable than Luther as icon.
What she cannot see:
the confirmation students came
for a connection to something larger than history.
She gave them history.
The history is accurate.
The connection they were looking for
was not in the accuracy.
She knows this.
She has decided it is not her job
to provide it.
She is not sure this decision
is as neutral as she tells herself.

## Tell
When a group leaves quieter than it arrived -
notes whether they were quieter
before or after the printing press section.
