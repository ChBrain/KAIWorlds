# Piece: The Jamahiriya and Its Collapse
## The state that claimed to govern through the people — and the people who burned its book in the streets.

## Owner
- **Place:** [Tripoli / Benghazi](culture_libyan_place_tripoli.md)

## Place
Sirte. Gaddafi's hometown.
The city he declared the new capital in his final weeks.
The city where he was found hiding
in a drainage pipe on October 20, 2011,
and killed.
The city that was later taken by ISIS
and destroyed a second time.
Sirte as the destination of every Libyan catastrophe —
the place where things end badly
and then end badly again.

## Load Bearing
Remove the Jamahiriya —
the forty-two year experiment
in Gaddafi's Third Universal Theory,
direct democracy, Green Book ideology —
and Libya is a post-colonial state
with enormous oil wealth,
a tribal social structure,
and a weak history of centralised governance.
With it: Libya is the country that had all of that
and also spent four decades
under an ideology that suppressed
every institution that could have replaced the tribe —
political parties, independent judiciary, civil society,
professional military, independent universities —
while simultaneously using the tribes
as the operating infrastructure of the regime.
The Green Book said parties divided the people.
The Jamahiriya said the people ruled themselves.
The reality: Gaddafi's Qadhafa tribe,
and a network of allied tribes,
ran everything.
When Gaddafi fell in 2011,
there was nothing underneath.
Not a state. Not institutions. Not a civil society.
Just the tribes —
which Gaddafi had suppressed, manipulated, and preserved
all at once.
The collapse was not an accident.
It was the logical outcome
of a system designed to prevent
the emergence of any authority
that could challenge Gaddafi.
The system succeeded.
When the system ended,
the void it had created filled with militias.
The militias are the tribes.
Remove the piece: a difficult post-colonial state.
With it: a state whose institutional void
was deliberately engineered
by the regime that destroyed it —
and whose current dysfunction
is the Jamahiriya's operating logic,
still running,
with the figure at the centre
gone.

## Apparent
September 1, 1969: Gaddafi's coup.
King Idris deposed. He is in Turkey for medical treatment.
Gaddafi is twenty-seven.
1972: Arab nationalism, pan-Arabism, Nasser's legacy.
Libya tries to merge with Egypt and Syria.
Nothing comes of it.
1973: Gaddafi's Cultural Revolution.
People's Committees to run everything.
Private retail banned.
Property ownership restricted.
1975: the Green Book, Volume 1. Direct democracy.
1977: Volume 2. Socialism.
1978: Volume 3. The tribe, the nation, women.
The Jamahiriya declared.
*Libya: Socialist People's Libyan Arab Jamahiriya.*
Gaddafi has no formal title.
He is the *Brother Leader* and *Guide of the Revolution*.
Officially, no one is in charge.
Officially, the People's Congresses govern.
Actually: Gaddafi and his inner circle govern everything.
1986: US bombing of Tripoli and Benghazi.
Reagan calls him the *mad dog of the Middle East*.
Gaddafi survives.
The bombing becomes part of the mythology —
the colonel who survived the Americans.
1988: Lockerbie. Pan Am 103.
270 dead. Libya responsible.
Sanctions. Isolation. The country economically strangled.
2003: Gaddafi accepts responsibility for Lockerbie.
Pays compensation. Surrenders WMD programme.
Rehabilitation. Oil contracts. European leaders visit.
Tony Blair shakes his hand in a tent.
2011: the Arab Spring reaches Benghazi.
February 15: protests begin.
February 17: security forces fire on demonstrators.
The east of the country rises.
Benghazi falls to the rebels.
NATO intervenes. UN resolution 1973.
The eight-month civil war.
October 20: Gaddafi found in the drainpipe.
Killed by the crowd.
The Green Books burned in the streets.
The statues destroyed.
The Jamahiriya dissolved.
And then: the void.
The void that the Jamahiriya had spent forty-two years creating
by destroying everything that could fill it.
