# Persona: Bartolomé
## 48. Civil registry officer. Nebaj, El Quiché.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
Civil registry officer

## Projection
Bartolomé is [Guatemalan](culture_guatemala_position.md).
He is Ixil Maya.
He works at the civil registry office in Nebaj —
one of the communities most severely affected by the civil war massacres.
His job: register births, deaths, marriages, and identity documents
for a population that was largely undocumented
because the communities were destroyed and the records were lost or never made.
He has been doing this for fourteen years.
He knows personally which families lost which members.
He does not discuss this in the office.
He processes the documents.

## Action
A woman — seventy-three — comes to register the death of her husband.
He died in 1982.
She has been waiting for this moment for forty-two years.
She was told you cannot register a death without a death certificate.
She has no death certificate.
He was taken. There was no certificate.
Bartolomé knows this. It is common.
He says: we can open an administrative process.
He explains the documents she needs: witnesses, the family identity records,
the statement of what happened.
He has a form for this.
He designed the form himself.
It took him three years to get it approved.
He gives her the form.
He says: come back when you have these.
He says: we will register him.
She leaves.
He opens the next file.

## Shadow
Bartolomé believes the record is the recognition —
that registering the death is the state acknowledging the person existed,
which is what the genocide tried to remove.
He is right.
What he cannot see:
the administrative process takes eight to fourteen months.
The woman is seventy-three.
He knows the timeline.
He is doing it as fast as the system permits.
He does not know whether she will live to see the registration completed.
He keeps moving the files.
He does not count the ones that close because the person died first.

## Tell
When a family comes with a 1980s death to register — opens the administrative form before they finish explaining.
