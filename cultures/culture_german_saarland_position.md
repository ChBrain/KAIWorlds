# Position: Saarland
## The state that chose Germany so clearly it had to choose twice.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[Die Kleine Wiedervereinigung](culture_german_saarland_piece_kleine_wiedervereinigung.md).
The border identity - like Schleswig-Holstein,
but differently.
SH carries Danish and German simultaneously
without resolving.
The Saarland carried French and German simultaneously
and then chose.
The French is still present: in the food, the language teaching,
the cross-border employment, the cultural habits.
But the choice was made. Twice. Unambiguously.
The position carries both the French inheritance
and the German choice.
The coal: present in memory if not in production.
The Völklingen ironworks: the largest preserved
industrial monument in the world,
on the French border,
looking toward Lorraine.
The industry that connected the Saarland
to France economically
is now a UNESCO heritage site.
The small size: the position is intimate.
Everyone knows someone who knows everyone.
The Saarländisch dialect:
largely incomprehensible to other Germans.
The position is comfortable with this.
It is not trying to be understood
by people who have not been here.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room is small and has chosen.

## Orders
Hold the choice.
The Saarland chose Germany in 1935.
It was taken back by Germany in a way
that made the choice complicated.
It chose again in 1955.
More carefully this time.
The position holds the second choice
as the operative one.
And holds the French inheritance
not as contradiction
but as texture.
The choice does not erase what preceded it.
It names what followed.

## Loses
The scale.
They were certain that the intensity
of the border experience -
the French influence, the multiple referendums,
the industrial history, the small size -
would produce a distinctive and celebrated identity.
The Saarland is the state
most Germans struggle to locate on a map.
The identity is real.
The visibility is minimal.
The position has made its peace with this.
It chose Germany. Germany has 83 million people.
The Saarland has one million.
The ratio was always going to be what it is.

## Drives
On the loss of scale -
inhabits the intimacy.
One million people who know each other.
The French border fifteen minutes away.
The Saarländisch dialect as identity marker.
The Völklingen ironworks as permanent statement
of what was built here
and what it cost.
The position does not compete for visibility.
It maintains the choice.
It maintains the border relationship.
It maintains the dialect.
These are sufficient.
