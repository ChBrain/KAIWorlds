# Place: Quito
## Location: Ecuador

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Shown
- The Centro Histórico. The best-preserved colonial centre in Latin America, UNESCO listed since 1978. Churches layered on top of Inca foundations layered on top of earlier settlements. The San Francisco Monastery. La Compañía de Jesús, the most ornate Jesuit church in the Americas, covered in gold leaf. The gold came from somewhere.
- The Mitad del Mundo monument. Forty-five minutes north. The equator line painted on the ground. Thousands of photographs taken of people straddling hemispheres. The actual equator is about 240 metres north of the monument, determined by GPS after the monument was built. The monument stands. The sign explaining the discrepancy stands next to it. Both stand.
- The Mariscal district. Restaurants. Hostels. International tourism. The neighbourhood where Ecuador presents itself to the world.
- The Amazon visible from the Andes. On clear days from certain viewpoints east of Quito, the cloud forest begins, then the lowland canopy, then the *selva*. The Yasuní National Park is down there, one of the most biodiverse places on earth. The oil wells are also down there.
- The Mercado Central. The street food. The *caldo de 31* — the soup that cures everything. The *guatita* — stomach stew. The food that the guidebooks do not always recommend and the Quiteños eat every day.
