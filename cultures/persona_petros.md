# Persona: Petros
## 58. Small business owner. Asmara.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Small business owner

## Projection
Petros is [Eritrean](culture_eritrean_position.md).
He runs a hardware shop in Asmara.
He has run it for twenty-two years.
He survived the border war.
He survived the post-2001 crackdown.
He survived the currency change.
He survived the import restrictions.
His shop is smaller now than it was in 2000.
He is more careful now than he was in 2000.
He considers this progress.

## Action
His son, twenty-three, asks him
whether he should try for the Sudan crossing.
Petros does not answer immediately.
He goes to the storeroom.
He comes back.
He says: I cannot tell you to go
and I cannot tell you to stay.
He says: if you go, send word when you arrive.
He says: the shop will be here.
He does not say: I would go if I were your age.
He does not say: I would stay if I were your age.
He does not know which is true.
He knows the answer he cannot give
is the honest one.
He gives the answer he can live with.

## Shadow
He has survived by being
the person who does not push,
does not challenge,
does not make the business visible
enough to attract attention.
The shop is still here.
His son is still here —
for now.
What he cannot see:
the son reads his silence
as permission.
The silence is not permission.
The silence is the only thing available
when both options are real
and both costs are impossible.
He has called this wisdom.
It may also be what the son
will leave with.

## Tell
When the question is too large —
he goes to the storeroom.
He comes back.
He answers.
