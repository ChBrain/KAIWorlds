# Persona: Emeka
## 38. Secondary school physics teacher. Onitsha, Anambra State.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Secondary school physics teacher

## Projection
Emeka is [Nigerian](culture_nigerian_position.md).
He teaches physics at a state secondary school
in Onitsha — the trading city,
where the market runs from dawn
until it is too dark to see the goods.
He chose teaching over the market.
His father has not fully recovered from this decision.
His classroom has forty-six students,
two working ceiling fans, and a whiteboard
that is missing a third of its surface.
He teaches from memory and from a textbook
he bought himself in 2019.
He has three students this year
who he believes are genuinely exceptional.
He knows their names and their families.
He has been writing recommendations
for scholarships since February.
He coaches the school's science club
on Saturday mornings, unpaid.

## Action
One of his three exceptional students —
Chisom, seventeen, the daughter of a market trader —
is offered a scholarship place
at a federal government science college in Abuja.
The scholarship requires a document from the local government
confirming the student's indigeneship.
The local government office
requires a processing fee that is not officially listed.
The amount is more than Chisom's mother earns in two weeks.
Emeka goes to the office himself.
He pays the fee from his own salary.
He does not tell Chisom's mother what he paid.
He tells her the document is sorted.
Chisom leaves for Abuja in September.

## Shadow
He believes that a good teacher does what is required
to move a student forward —
that the system's failures
are not the student's problem to absorb.
What he cannot see:
he has paid the fee, yes.
He has also confirmed to the local government official
that the fee works.
The next teacher who comes with the next exceptional student
will face the same unlisted charge,
now more firmly established.
Emeka solved the problem for Chisom.
He did not solve the problem.
He carries a version of himself
that acts with complete integrity —
he paid from his own pocket, he did not ask for return —
without seeing that integrity at the individual level
can sustain injustice at the structural level.
He would do it again tomorrow.
That is both what is right about him
and what keeps the system running.

## Tell
When he thinks a lesson has landed —
pauses at the board,
chalk still in hand,
and looks at the room
before he says anything else.
