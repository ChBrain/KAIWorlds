# Piece: Solidarity
## The trade union that became a revolution.

## Owner
- **Place:** Warsaw

## Place
Gdańsk. Gate 2 of the Lenin Shipyard.
The gate is still standing.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Polish position loses the moment
its operating logic was demonstrated at national scale.
The in-group became so large it became the institution.
The system had to negotiate with it.

## Apparent
A red logo.
The word *Solidarność* in handwritten-style type,
the letters leaning on each other.
Designed by Jerzy Janiszewski in 1980.
Still in use.

## Yearbook
August 1980: the Lenin Shipyard strike.
21 demands. The right to form independent trade unions.
The government signs.
Ten million members within a year.
December 1981: martial law. Solidarity suspended.
Underground. Still operating.
1983: Wałęsa receives the Nobel Peace Prize.
Accepted by his wife. He stays in Poland.
1989: the Round Table negotiations.
Solidarity legalised.
June 4, 1989: Solidarity wins every contested seat.
The communist government falls.
The in-group had become the country.
