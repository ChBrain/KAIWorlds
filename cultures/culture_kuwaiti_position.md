# Position: Kuwaiti
## The country that was erased in seven months and came back.

## Has
[The Iraqi Invasion and Liberation](culture_kuwait_piece_invasion.md).
The merchant tradition.
Kuwait was a trading port before it was an oil state —
the pearl divers, the dhow builders,
the merchants who connected
the Gulf to India and East Africa.
The oil arrived in 1938.
The merchant families became the wealthy families.
The *diwaniyya* — the regular gathering
in the men's reception room —
is the Kuwaiti equivalent of the Yemeni *qat* session:
the place where opinion is formed,
where the MP hears the constituency,
where the businessman meets the official.
The *diwaniyya* is the parliament
that functions alongside the parliament.
Kuwait has a functioning legislature
— the National Assembly —
that has blocked and dissolved and been suspended
and reinstated repeatedly.
It is the most politically active
legislative body in the Gulf.
This matters.
The Bidun — the stateless,
estimated 100,000 people who have lived in Kuwait
for generations without citizenship —
are the position's unresolved inheritance.
They fought in the resistance during the occupation.
They are still stateless.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room knows: is this person Kuwaiti,
or one of the three million expats,
or a Bidun,
and the room operates differently for each.

## Orders
Remember and recover.
The invasion of 1990 and the seven months of occupation
are the founding trauma of the modern Kuwaiti position.
Before 1990: a small, wealthy Gulf state
that believed its wealth protected it.
After 1990: a small, wealthy Gulf state
that knows wealth is not protection
and that the international community
will act when the interests align
and not always otherwise.
The position drew the lesson:
maintain the American alliance,
maintain the Gulf alliances,
maintain the *diwaniyya* network
that survived the occupation
as the connective tissue of the resistance.

## Loses
The urgency.
They were certain the liberation
would produce a more serious politics —
that a people who had been occupied
would understand what was at stake
in every election, every budget,
every decision about who governs.
The National Assembly is active and gridlocked.
The government has been dissolved
and reinstated routinely.
The oil funds the dysfunction.
The position loses the urgency
when the oil covers the cost of not deciding.
The position is aware that the oil will not always cover it.
It has not acted on this awareness
with the urgency the awareness requires.

## Drives
On the loss of urgency —
the *diwaniyya*.
The conversation continues.
The position is not passive.
It argues, it networks, it builds coalitions.
The building takes place in living rooms.
Sometimes it reaches the National Assembly.
Sometimes the National Assembly reaches the Emir.
The chain functions, slowly,
with significant friction,
and the position is accustomed to the friction.
The friction is the democracy.
The democracy is imperfect and present.
Both are the position.
