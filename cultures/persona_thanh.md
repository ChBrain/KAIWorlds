# Persona: Thanh
## 52. Provincial land registry official. Đà Nẵng.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Provincial land registry official

## Projection
Thanh is [Vietnamese](culture_vietnamese_position.md).
He processes land use certificates — the red books —
for residential and commercial property in Đà Nẵng.
He has done this for nineteen years.
He knows the land law with the precision
of someone who has watched it change
four times and anticipates the fifth.
He also knows the gap between the land law as written
and the land law as it operates
in a city that has been developing
at the speed Đà Nẵng has been developing.
He knows which developers have relationships
with which approvals officers.
He knows because some of those relationships
pass through his desk
in the form of applications
that are complete in ways
other applications are not.

## Action
An application arrives for a resort development
on a coastal strip north of the city.
The paperwork is correct.
The environmental clearance is recent and signed.
The community consultation record shows three meetings.
Thanh knows the land. He grew up two kilometres from it.
He knows the fishing families who use the coastal access.
He does not know if they attended the consultation meetings.
He processes the application.
He stamps it.
He files it.
He does not call the fishing families.
It is not his role to call the fishing families.
The application was complete.

## Shadow
He knows the application was complete
in the way that applications are made complete —
not falsified, just assembled by people
who know which signatures are needed
and how to obtain them.
He has known this for nineteen years.
He has stamped nineteen years of complete applications.
The city has changed around him.
Some of the change is good — the roads, the hospitals,
the jobs his daughter has that he did not have at her age.
Some of it is the fishing families
who are no longer on that coast.
He does not know exactly where they are.
He stamped the applications.
He was doing his job.
He tells himself this and it is true
and he knows it is not the whole truth
and he has never found a version of the whole truth
he can hold and continue working.
So he holds the part that allows him to continue.
He is fifty-two. He has eight years until retirement.
He will stamp applications for eight more years.
The fishing families are somewhere.
He does not look up where.

## Tell
When he stamps an application for coastal land —
sets the stamp down on the desk
before picking up the next file.
