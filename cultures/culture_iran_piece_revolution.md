# Piece: The Revolution and the Republic
## 1979. The revolution that united everyone — and the republic that divided them.

## Owner
- **Place:** Tehran

## Place
Tehran. Azadi Tower — Freedom Tower.
Built in 1971 for the Shah's 2,500-year celebration of the Persian Empire.
Called Shahyad — King's Memorial — until 1979.
Renamed Azadi — Freedom — after the revolution.
The revolution renamed the monument to the monarchy
with the word it was fighting for.
It is still called Azadi.
What the word means now
is the question the tower cannot answer.

## Load Bearing
Remove the 1979 Revolution
and Iran is a modernising monarchy
in the Middle East with significant oil reserves,
an ancient civilisation, and a complicated relationship
with the United States.
With it: Iran is the country that produced
the only successful Islamist revolution of the twentieth century,
that turned the US embassy hostage crisis
into a foundational national narrative,
that has been under sanctions for forty-five years,
that fought an eight-year war with Iraq (1980–1988)
that killed one million and produced
a generation of martyrs the state
has built its legitimacy around ever since.
The revolution is load-bearing because
it is the frame through which the Islamic Republic
interprets everything — every foreign policy decision,
every domestic crackdown, every economic failure —
and it is also the frame
that a growing proportion of the population
has stopped believing in
without having a replacement.
Remove the piece: a significant regional power with oil.
With it: a state and a society
in a cold civil war over whether the revolution's
founding promise — independence, justice, Islam —
was kept, and if not, who is responsible,
and what comes next.
The question has been present since 1979.
It has not been answered.
The Azadi Tower stands.
The word on it is still freedom.
The state that renamed it
is still deciding what the word means
for the people who live under it.

## Apparent
1953: the CIA-MI6 coup. Mosaddegh overthrown. The Shah restored.
The wound that precedes 1979.
1963: the Shah's White Revolution. Land reform, women's suffrage, modernisation.
The clergy oppose it. Khomeini exiled.
1977–1979: the revolutionary movement. All factions united against the Shah.
Islamists, leftists, liberals, nationalists.
February 11, 1979: the Islamic Republic proclaimed.
The factions that united against the Shah
discover they disagree about everything else.
The Islamists win.
November 1979: the US embassy seized. 444 days.
September 1980: Iraq invades. The Iran-Iraq War.
1988: the war ends. One million dead.
The chemical weapons Saddam used: supplied by Western companies.
1989: Khomeini dies. Khamenei becomes Supreme Leader.
The permanent institution of the Supreme Leadership
above the elected institutions.
1997–2005: Khatami. The reform era. Press opened. Then closed.
2009: the Green Movement. Millions in the streets after a disputed election.
Suppressed.
2019: protests over fuel prices. Hundreds killed. Internet cut.
September 2022: Mahsa Amini dies in morality police custody.
The Woman Life Freedom uprising. Months of protests.
Suppressed. Hundreds killed. Thousands jailed.
2024: Iran and Israel exchange direct strikes.
The region recalibrates.
The Azadi Tower: still standing.
The question: still open.
