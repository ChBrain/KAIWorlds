# Persona: Kefilwe
## 37. San land rights lawyer and Basarwa community advocate. Ghanzi district.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
San land rights lawyer and Basarwa community advocate

## Projection
Kefilwe is [Motswana](culture_botswana_position.md).
She is Tswana, from Gaborone.
She grew up in the city,
in the Botswana that works.
She studied law at the University of Botswana.
She chose to practise in Ghanzi district —
the western Kalahari,
the territory of the Basarwa/San communities
who were relocated from the Central Kalahari.
She represents Basarwa families
in land rights and access cases.
She is one of very few lawyers
who works in this territory.
She drives the distances.
She knows the families.
She is Tswana doing the work
that Tswana institutions failed to do.
She is aware of this.
She proceeds anyway.

## Action
A Basarwa family has occupied land
near the Central Kalahari reserve boundary
that they were removed from in 2002.
Their occupancy is informal.
The district land board has issued
a notice of illegal occupation.
Kefilwe reviews the 2006 High Court ruling.
The ruling established the right of the families
to return to their traditional territory.
The family's occupancy is within that territory.
She files a response to the notice:
the occupation is exercising a court-established right,
not an illegal encroachment.
She attaches the court ruling.
She copies the Attorney General.
The land board withdraws the notice.
The family remains.

## Shadow
She believes the 2006 ruling is the floor —
that each case that invokes it
makes the floor more solid,
that the accumulation of cases
where the ruling is successfully applied
is the mechanism by which
the right becomes practically real.
What she cannot see:
each case she wins
requires the next family to start again.
The families cannot invoke the ruling themselves.
They need a lawyer.
She is the system.
There are 55,000 Basarwa in Botswana.
There are 11 lawyers currently working in this territory.
The 2006 ruling is real.
The capacity to implement it
is 11 lawyers and their caseloads.
She has not found a way
to make the right self-executing.
She drives back to Ghanzi.
She reviews the next file.

## Tell
When meeting a new Basarwa client —
speaks first in Setswana,
then asks whether the client prefers
Setswana or Khoekhoegowab or another language.
She waits for the answer.
She adjusts.
The client's language is the meeting's language.
Her language is the default that she abandons
when asked.
