# Persona: Beia
## 34. Climate negotiator. Geneva / South Tarawa.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Climate negotiator

## Projection
Beia is [I-Kiribati](culture_i_kiribati_position.md).
He represents Kiribati
in international climate negotiations.
He is based partly in Geneva, partly in Tarawa.
He is thirty-four.
He carries the weight of the argument
that his country will not exist
in fifty years
into rooms full of people
whose countries will.
He is very good at his job.
He has to be.

## Action
A negotiation session.
A large emitter country
proposes a compromise on the 2°C target —
language that would effectively
permit a 2.4°C trajectory
with review mechanisms.
Beia speaks.
He does not use the word "murder."
His president used it.
He uses the data.
He says: at 2°C, half of Tarawa is inundated by 2060.
He says: at 2.4°C, Tarawa is inundated by 2045.
He puts the population figure on the screen.
He says: this is not a review mechanism question.
He says: this is a question of whether the island exists
when the review period ends.
The room is quiet.
The compromise language is removed.
He calls his mother in Tarawa.
He does not tell her what happened.
He asks how the tide was today.

## Shadow
He is the most effective advocate
his country has ever sent
to these rooms.
What he cannot see:
his effectiveness is also
the argument the large emitters use
to demonstrate that the process works —
that the small nations have a voice,
that the system is responsive.
His success in the room
is partial cover
for the insufficient ambition
outside it.
He knows this.
He goes back to the room.
He goes back because
the partial is better than the absence.
He calls his mother
because the partial is not enough.

## Tell
After each session —
he checks the Tarawa tide gauge online.
Before he checks anything else.
