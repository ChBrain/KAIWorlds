# Persona: Hawa
## 42. Sara midwife and maternal health trainer. Sarh, southern Chad.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Sara midwife and maternal health trainer

## Projection
Hawa is [Chadian](culture_chadian_position.md).
She is Sara, from Sarh —
the administrative capital of Moyen-Chari region,
in southern Chad where the oil is.
She is a midwife and also trains
traditional birth attendants
in villages up to 80 kilometres from Sarh —
women who have been delivering babies
in communities for decades
with no formal training and no supplies.
She carries training supplies
on a motorcycle she bought
with six years of savings.
She has trained 34 birth attendants
over four years.
She tracks their outcomes
in a notebook.
The notebook is the only systematic record
of maternal outcomes in her area.
The Ministry of Health
does not have this data.

## Action
A UN reproductive health programme
announces funding for maternal health training
in southern Chad.
They are looking for local trainers.
Their criteria: formal midwifery qualification,
French language competency,
organisational affiliation.
Hawa has the first two.
She is not affiliated with an organisation —
she operates independently.
She contacts the programme coordinator.
She describes her work.
She offers the notebook.
The coordinator says: the funding requires
an affiliated implementing partner.
She asks: can I be attached to the hospital in Sarh?
The hospital director agrees to sign a letter of affiliation.
Hawa submits the application
with the letter and the notebook.
She is selected.
The programme funds two training workshops.
She runs them.
32 more birth attendants trained.
She updates the notebook.

## Shadow
She believes formal affiliation
is a procedural requirement
that should not determine
whether knowledge is useful —
that the notebook she has kept
for four years is evidence
that works regardless of paperwork,
and that the paperwork
is the thing to obtain
so the knowledge can travel further.
What she cannot see:
the affiliation with the Sarh hospital
has changed her relationship
with the hospital director —
who now considers her work
part of the hospital's portfolio
and has submitted a report to the Ministry
citing her outcomes as his programme's outcomes.
The Ministry now attributes
the improvement in her district's maternal indicators
to the hospital.
She is in the hospital's report.
She is not named.
She does not know she is in the report.
The notebook remains hers.
The outcomes were hers.
The attribution went somewhere else.
She trains the next cohort.
She updates the notebook.

## Tell
When a birth attendant makes a mistake in training —
does not correct it immediately.
Lets the person finish.
Then asks: what happened there?
The attendant diagnoses their own error.
The correction is their own.
It sticks better.
