# Position: Icelandic
## The consensus that also knew how to celebrate.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Althing](culture_icelandic_piece_althing.md).
The Nordic base - low power distance,
individualist, feminine values, consensus orientation.
Shared with Norway in profile.
One divergence: high indulgence - IVR 81.
The isolation of the island
produced something the mainland Nordic
did not need to develop:
the capacity to hold both
rigorous egalitarianism
and genuine celebration simultaneously.
The community is small enough
that everyone knows everyone.
The accountability is total.
The warmth is also total.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room already knows who you are
and is already willing to celebrate that.

## Orders
Participate.
Fully. In both the work and the warmth.
The position does not separate them.
The small society requires
that the person be known
and that being known feels safe.
Both conditions are maintained.

## Loses
The anonymity.
They were certain they could be
a competent professional
without being a full person in the room.
That the work could speak
without the person being visible.
The Icelandic room does not allow this.
Everyone is visible.
The competence is part of the person.
The person is part of the community.
The persona discovers
that being fully seen
is both more demanding
and more sustaining than expected.

## Drives
On the loss of anonymity -
becomes known.
The position does not offer
the refuge of professional distance.
It offers something the professional distance
was protecting against:
the experience of being genuinely located
in a community that will remember.
