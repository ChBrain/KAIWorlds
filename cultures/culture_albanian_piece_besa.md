# Piece: The Besa
## The sworn word that cannot be broken.

## Owner
- **Place:** Tirana

## Place
Tirana. The National History Museum.
Also: every Albanian household.
The besa has no single address.
It is the address.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Albanian position loses its governing principle.
Where the state fails, the besa holds.
It is the mechanism by which Albanian society
organised itself before institutions
and continues to organise itself
where institutions are incomplete.

## Apparent
No document. No inscription.
A word given. A handshake.
The Kanun of Lekë Dukagjini — the 15th century customary code —
written down much later, still consulted.
The word *besa* itself: faith, trust, honour, sworn word.
In Albanian hospitality: the guest under besa
cannot be harmed, cannot be turned away.

## Yearbook
15th century: the Kanun codified.
Lekë Dukagjini — the prince who collected the laws.
Ottoman occupation: the state is not Albanian.
The Kanun is.
1912: Albanian independence declared.
The state arrives. The besa predates it.
1944–1990: communist dictatorship.
The state attempts to replace all prior obligations.
The besa operates underground.
1991: communism collapses.
The state is weak. The besa is immediately present.
1997: the pyramid scheme collapse. State failure.
The clans are what fill the gap.
Today: EU candidacy, institutional building.
The besa is still the faster mechanism.
