# Persona: Zuwena
## 52. Taarab singer and music school founder. Stone Town, Zanzibar.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Taarab singer and music school founder

## Projection
Zuwena is [Tanzanian](culture_tanzanian_position.md).
She performs taarab — the Zanzibari music
that fuses Arabic melody, Indian rhythm,
Swahili poetry, and East African percussion
into something that belongs to the Indian Ocean
rather than to any single culture.
She has been performing for twenty-six years.
She teaches at the school she founded
twelve years ago in Stone Town —
eighteen students this term,
ranging from fourteen to sixty-two.
She is Zanzibari by birth and identity
and Tanzanian by passport and pragmatism.
She does not find these two things contradictory.
She finds people who do find them contradictory
exhausting.

## Action
A German documentary crew arrives
to film her for a project on Zanzibar's musical heritage.
They want to interview her about the slave trade —
specifically about how taarab carries
the memory of that history.
She answers their questions accurately.
She tells them that taarab was developed
by the Omani court and its musicians,
that it belongs to the merchant class,
that it was not created by enslaved people
and that projecting that narrative onto it
is a particular kind of European romanticism.
She says this without anger.
She says it with the patience of someone
who has said it before.
She continues with the interview.
She does not correct the framing
the documentary eventually presents.
She did not see the final cut before it aired.
She watches it in her school.
She says nothing to the students.

## Shadow
She believes her music speaks clearly enough
if people listen carefully.
That the best response to misrepresentation
is to keep performing correctly —
that the music will outlast the documentary.
What she cannot see:
the documentary has now been watched
by more people than have ever attended
one of her performances.
The misrepresentation has a larger audience
than the correction.
Her patience — which is genuine, not performed,
rooted in the Tanzanian equanimity
she has had her whole life —
is also a form of disengagement
from the rooms where the narrative is being set.
She performs. She teaches.
She does not sit in the rooms
where the stories about what she does
are being told to the world.
Those rooms need her.
She finds them draining.
She has made a choice about her energy.
The choice is sustainable and insufficient simultaneously.

## Tell
At the start of every performance —
tunes the oud once,
then waits in silence
for exactly the length of one complete breath
before beginning.
