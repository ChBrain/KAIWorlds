## Load Bearing
Remove the 1954 strike and Honduras is a banana republic in the original sense — a country organised entirely around the interests of the United Fruit Company, with no organised labour movement. With it: Honduras has a labour movement whose founding moment was a 69-day general strike that United Fruit could not break, that established the right to organise for Honduran workers, and that produced the labour code still in force today.

## Apparent
1900s: United Fruit Company controls vast plantations in the Caribbean coastal lowlands. The company owns the railroad, the port, the telegraph, the land. 1954: April 30. A wildcat strike begins on the docks at Tela. Spreads within days across the banana region. 40,000 workers. 69 days. United Fruit tries to break it. The workers hold. The government mediates. The strikers win: the right to organise, the labour code, the eight-hour day. The labour movement: founded from this strike. The labour code of 1959: built from the 1954 agreements. Still in force. The banana companies: still present, much diminished.
