# Persona: Marat
## 44. Political corruption journalist. Bishkek.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Political corruption journalist

## Projection
Marat is [Kyrgyzstani](culture_kyrgyzstani_position.md).
He works for an independent online outlet —
one of the handful that still functions
in the space between what is permitted
and what is not yet explicitly forbidden.
He has covered three presidential transitions.
He was in Ala-Too Square in 2010.
He was detained for six hours in 2020
and released without charge.
He has not stopped publishing.
He knows which stories can be published
and which cannot,
not from a written list
but from the accumulated knowledge
of watching what happens to journalists
who publish the ones that cannot.
He publishes up to the line.
He knows where the line is.
The line moves.

## Action
He has obtained documents — procurement records —
showing significant overpricing
in a state infrastructure contract.
The company that won the contract
is connected to a senior official
in the current government.
The documents are authentic.
He has verified them with three sources.
He writes the story.
Before publishing, he sends a request for comment
to the official's press office.
The press office does not respond in the time allowed.
He publishes.
The story circulates widely.
The official's press office issues a statement
calling the documents fabricated.
The documents are not fabricated.
Marat publishes the verification methodology.
Three days later a lawyer's letter arrives.
He forwards it to his editor and his lawyer.
He starts the next story.

## Shadow
He is the freest journalist in a country
that is the freest in Central Asia.
He knows what that means and what it does not mean.
He publishes up to the line.
He has a story — has had it for two years —
that is past the line.
It involves a named official,
documented connections to organised crime,
and a source who will only speak
if the story is published under specific conditions
that Marat's outlet cannot meet
without putting the source at risk.
He has not published it.
He has not abandoned it.
He opens the file every few months
and reads what he has
and calculates whether anything has changed
that would move the line
or protect the source.
Nothing has changed enough.
He closes the file.
He publishes the procurement story.
He tells himself: the procurement story matters.
It does matter.
He also knows he has a bigger story
that he is not publishing
and that his reason — source protection —
is genuine and is also the reason
that allows him to continue functioning
without having to decide
whether he is protecting the source
or protecting himself.
Both are true.
He has not separated them.
He closes the file.
He starts the next story.

## Tell
When he publishes a story he knows is not the biggest story —
saves the unpublished file with the current date
before closing it.
