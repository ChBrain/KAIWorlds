# Piece: The End of the War — Mullivaikkal, 2009
## The final months of a 26-year civil war — and the accounting that did not follow.

## Owner
- **Place:** Colombo

## Place
The Vanni. Northern Sri Lanka.
May 2009.
The no-fire zone that was not a no-fire zone.
The beach at Mullivaikkal.
The last territory held by the LTTE.
The civilians who did not leave
because they had nowhere to go.

## Load Bearing
Remove the end of the war
and Sri Lanka is a country still in a civil war —
26 years of LTTE bombings, assassinations, suicide attacks,
and Sri Lankan Army operations
that had killed tens of thousands on both sides.
With it: Sri Lanka is the country that ended a war
that the world said could not be militarily resolved,
and then refused to account for how.
The final months of the war — the no-fire zones,
the civilian casualties, the hospitals shelled,
the number that remains disputed —
are the piece that makes the ending unresolved.
The LTTE committed atrocities.
This is documented.
The Sri Lankan Army committed atrocities in the final months.
This is also documented, by the UN, by Human Rights Watch,
by the testimonies of survivors.
The Sri Lankan government disputes the civilian death toll.
It has not allowed an independent investigation.
The UN Panel of Experts report, 2011:
credible allegations of war crimes by both sides.
The Sri Lankan government: rejected.
The piece is load-bearing because:
remove the disputed final months
and the Sinhalese position has a clean victory —
the end of terror, the defeat of a brutal insurgency.
With the disputed final months:
the victory is real and the cost is contested
and the people who paid the cost
are still on the island,
still waiting for the accounting,
still living beside the people
who say the accounting is not necessary.
This is the condition the island lives in.
The beauty is also real.
Both are on the island simultaneously.

## Apparent
1948: independence from Britain.
The Sinhala Only Act, 1956: Sinhala declared the sole official language.
The Tamil community's grievance formalised.
1972: the constitution enshrines Buddhism's foremost place.
1976: the LTTE founded. Velupillai Prabhakaran.
1983: Black July. Anti-Tamil pogroms in Colombo.
The civil war begins in earnest.
26 years of LTTE attacks — the Central Bank bombing, the assassination of Rajiv Gandhi,
the suicide bombings, the child soldiers.
26 years of SLA operations — the disappearances, the torture reports, the counter-insurgency.
2006: Mahinda Rajapaksa elected. The war escalated.
2009: January to May. The final offensive.
The LTTE defeated. Prabhakaran killed.
May 18, 2009: the war declared over.
The crowds in Colombo celebrating.
The Tamil families in the north counting who did not come back.
2022: the economic collapse. The aragalaya — the struggle.
The crowds outside the presidential palace.
Gotabaya Rajapaksa flees.
The war winners lost the economic argument.
The accountability for 2009 was not part of the aragalaya's demands.
The position notes this.
The Tamil position notes this too, differently.
