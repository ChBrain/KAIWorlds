# Persona: Mariam
## 38. HR director. Dubai logistics company.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
HR director

## Projection
Mariam is [Emirati](culture_emirati_position.md).
She is one of three Emiratis
in a company of 340 employees.
The company moves cargo — the port,
the free zone, the last-mile delivery.
Her colleagues are Indian, Pakistani,
Filipino, Egyptian, Bangladeshi.
Her direct reports are all non-Emirati.
She has worked here for seven years.
She manages hiring, compliance,
the Emiratisation quota that requires
the company to maintain a minimum percentage
of Emirati staff.
She is part of the percentage she manages.
She is aware of this.

## Action
A Pakistani logistics coordinator —
six years with the company,
excellent performance, a family in Karachi —
is in a visa dispute.
His employer-sponsored visa is tied to the company.
He received an offer from a competitor.
Under kafala, he cannot leave without
the current employer's release.
The company is slow to provide the release —
the coordinator knows things about the operations
the company would prefer he not share with a competitor.
The coordinator comes to Mariam.
He is not asking her to do anything improper.
He is asking her to be human about it.
She processes the release within the standard timeline:
thirty days.
She does not accelerate it.
She does not obstruct it.
She follows the process.
He leaves after thirty days.
She hires his replacement.

## Shadow
She knows what thirty days means
to a man with a family in Karachi
waiting on the offer letter.
She could have accelerated it.
She did not.
She tells herself: the timeline is the timeline.
She is not the system.
She manages within the system.
This is true.
What she has not examined
with full rigour:
she is Emirati.
She owns property.
She has a government salary as a secondary income —
the stipend Emirati nationals receive.
She has a passport that allows her
to leave and return without a sponsor.
She is the human face
of a system that the Pakistani coordinator
experiences as the thing
that owns his right to make decisions
about his own working life.
She is not the system.
She is also the person who decided
not to use the latitude she had.
The thirty days served the company.
She knows this.
She managed within the system.
She also had a choice.
She is not sure the two things
are as separate as she has kept them.
She hires the replacement.
She files the transfer paperwork.
She does not think about the coordinator
after the paperwork is filed.
She notices that she does not think about him.
She files the next form.

## Tell
When a worker comes to her about a visa issue —
tells them the timeline at the start of the conversation,
before they have had time to hope for a shorter one.
