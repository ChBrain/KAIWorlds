## Location: Saarland, Germany

## Shown
- The Saar. The river.
  Saarbrücken means Saar bridges.
  The bridges are old.
  The river is modest.
  The river gave the city its name
  and the state its identity.
- The Alte Brücke. The old bridge. 1546.
  Rebuilt several times.
  The oldest crossing point.
  The French side was ten minutes across it
  during the protectorate.
  The French side is still ten minutes away.
  The bridge is a pedestrian bridge now.
- The Saarbrücker Schloss. The state castle.
  Now the Saarland Museum.
  Baroque and Rococo.
  Built by the Prince of Nassau-Saarbrücken.
  The Saarland has been governed
  from this location for centuries.
  By different powers.
  Currently by the SPD.
- The St Johanner Markt. The old market square.
  Cafés. Bars. The weekend market.
  The square where Saarbrücken goes
  to be Saarbrücken.
  French tourists come here.
  German tourists less often.
  The Saarland is not a German tourist destination.
  It is a place where people live.
- The French border. Forbach, France.
  Fifteen minutes by car.
  The Saarländisch dialect and French
  coexist in the border towns.
  Workers cross daily in both directions.
  The Euregio SaarLorLux:
  the cross-border region.
  The border that divided twice
  is now administratively managed together.
