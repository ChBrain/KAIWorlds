## Location: Equatorial Guinea

## Shown
- Black Beach Prison.
  On the outskirts of Malabo.
  Still operational.
  Both regimes' enemies have passed through it.
  The prison is the institutional continuity
  between Macías and Obiang.
  International human rights organisations
  document conditions.
  The documentation exists.
  The conditions continue.
- The new government district.
  Built with oil revenue.
  Conference centres. Wide roads.
  The infrastructure of international legitimacy.
  Malabo hosts the African Union summit.
  The conference centre is modern.
  20 kilometres away,
  access to clean water is not reliable.
  Both things are in the same country.
  The government built one.
- The colonial Spanish quarter.
  Santa Isabel — the former colonial name.
  The cathedral. The market.
  The wooden buildings
  where pidgin English runs
  as the Bioko informal lingua franca
  alongside Bubi and Fang.
  The colonial architecture
  inhabited by the post-colonial population
  the same way everywhere:
  pragmatically,
  without celebration or mourning,
  because it is the available structure.
- Bioko's forests.
  The island has some of the highest
  primate diversity in Africa.
  The drill monkey.
  The Bioko Allen's bushbaby.
  The conservation work
  running alongside the oil infrastructure.
  Both on the same island.
  The conservation scientists
  and the oil workers
  use the same roads.
- The Bubi villages.
  In the mountains of Bioko.
  The original inhabitants.
  The community that predates
  the state that marginalises them.
  The *Abira* ceremony —
  the Bubi ancestor ritual —
  performed in the villages.
  Not visible from the government district.
  The government does not celebrate it.
  The community performs it anyway.
  The performance is the continuity.
