# Piece: The Baltic Chain
## Two million people holding hands across three countries.

## Owner
- **Place:** Vilnius

## Place
Vilnius. The southern end of the chain.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Lithuanian position loses its fullest expression.
The Baltic Chain was three countries with three different histories
making one gesture simultaneously — the crossroads capacity
demonstrated at two-million-person scale.

## Apparent
Two million people holding hands.
A line 675 kilometres long.
August 23, 1989. Fifteen minutes.
From Tallinn through Riga to Vilnius.

## Yearbook
August 23, 1939: the Molotov-Ribbentrop Pact.
The secret protocol. The Baltic states assigned to the USSR.
1940: occupation. All three countries.
August 23, 1989: exactly fifty years later.
Two million people. Three countries.
One chain, 675 kilometres.
Organised in weeks.
No violence.
The Soviet Union condemned it as "nationalist hysteria."
The chain held.
1990: Lithuania declares independence.
March 11. First of the three.
Soviet economic blockade. Lithuania holds.
1991: independence recognised.
The three countries that held the chain
are now NATO members and EU members.
The crossroads produced allies
who knew, from the chain,
that they could hold together.
