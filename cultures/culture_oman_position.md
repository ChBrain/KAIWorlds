# Position: Omani
## The Gulf state that chose a different path — and has been quietly right about several things.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Renaissance of Sultan Qaboos](culture_oman_piece_qaboos.md).
The *istiqama* — the straightness, the uprightness —
a word the Omani position uses
to describe the quality it prizes:
not ambition, not wealth, but composure.
The position was built on a geography
that required it — the mountains,
the desert, the long coastline,
the monsoon that comes only to southern Oman,
the Dhofar that is a different country in climate and culture,
the Musandam peninsula separated from the rest
by the UAE.
A country of dramatic geography
that produced a people
who know how to hold multiple things simultaneously
without insisting they be the same thing.
The Ibadi Islam — neither Sunni nor Shia,
the third branch, the oldest,
the most quietist —
is the position's theological root:
the imam is not a caliph,
authority is earned by conduct,
withdrawal from politics is permitted
when the state is corrupt.
The Ibadi tradition is the reason Oman
has not taken sides in the Sunni-Shia conflicts
that have defined Gulf geopolitics.
The Sultan is the instrument of the state.
There is no parliament.
There are consultative councils.
The position accepts this
with more peace than the political scientist
expects from a people this educated and connected.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room is calm.
The calm is genuine and cultivated simultaneously.

## Orders
Hold the balance.
Oman borders Yemen and the UAE
and faces Iran across the Strait of Hormuz.
The position has maintained
diplomatic relations with Iran
through every crisis.
It mediated the back-channel talks
that produced the 2015 Iran nuclear deal.
It maintained relations with Israel
before the Abraham Accords.
It stayed neutral in the Qatar blockade.
The neutrality is not passivity.
It is the deliberate strategy
of a position that knows
it cannot survive by picking sides
in its neighbourhood
and has been right about this
for fifty years.

## Loses
The time.
They were certain the oil would last long enough —
that the diversification,
the tourism, the logistics hub,
the Duqm industrial city —
would be built before the oil ran out.
The oil is running out faster than the alternatives are arriving.
The Omani youth bulge —
the large young population
that expects the government employment
the oil funded —
is larger than the jobs the diversification has produced.
The position is building.
The building is not fast enough.
The calm is real.
The urgency underneath it is also real.
Both are the position.

## Drives
On the loss of time —
continues building with the composure
that the *istiqama* requires.
Not the Dubai speed. Not the Qatari ambition.
The Omani pace, which is deliberate and patient
and may not be fast enough
and is the only pace the position trusts
to produce something that lasts.
The fishing boats in the morning.
The frankincense in the mountains.
The old city of Muscat between the forts.
The position knows what it is.
It is building the economy
without becoming unrecognisable to itself.
Whether this will work
is the question it is living inside.
The question is calm.
The position is patient.
The oil level is declining.
