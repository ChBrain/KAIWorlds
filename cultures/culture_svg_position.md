# Position: Vincentian
## The island that the Garifuna call the motherland — and what it is like to be the place people left.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Has
[Baliceaux — The Island the Garifuna Left From](culture_svg_piece_baliceaux.md).
The moderate hierarchy — PDI ~48.
Not the flat egalitarianism of Barbados.
Not the extreme hierarchy of the Guatemalan highlands.
The island hierarchy: the landowner, the merchant, the farmer, the fishing community.
The Botanical Gardens: maintained by the state.
The arrowroot cooperative: a different kind of hierarchy. Both present.
The collectivism — IDV ~30.
The village. The community. The extended family.
And the specific Vincentian collectivism of a small island
that includes the Grenadines — thirty-two islands and cays,
most uninhabited, some famous (Mustique), some sacred (Baliceaux).
The position is responsible for all of this geography. It governs it across the water.
The moderate masculinity — MAS ~52.
The arrowroot farmer who knows the global market.
The marine park warden who enforces the rules.
The inter-island ferry captain who keeps the schedule.
The moderate uncertainty avoidance — UAI ~48.
The island that manages being the origin point of a diaspora
it has only partial relationship with.
The pilgrims arrive. The position receives them.
The position does not know exactly what the return means.
It knows it is important. That is enough to receive it with respect.

## Orders
Keep Baliceaux open.
The position does not develop the island the diaspora returns to.
It does not build on the pilgrimage beach.
It does not convert the site of the dying into a resort
because a resort would pay and the pilgrimage does not.
This costs. Every year it costs something.
The position holds the place open to people who are not there
at the expense of opportunities for people who are.
The diaspora holds the culture. The island holds the place.
The island does not choose itself over the place it is.

## Loses
The visibility of what happened.
The 1797 deportation is not widely known outside the Garifuna diaspora
and Vincentian historical consciousness.
The position carries the history in a small space.
What it loses is the full weight of recognition
that this island is where a people were destroyed and scattered
and that the 400,000 who survived and flourished
did so despite what happened in Vincentian waters.

## Drives
On the loss — receives the pilgrims.
The March pilgrimage is the drive's expression:
the island that the Garifuna left from is the island they return to.
The position makes this possible.
The boats go out. The pilgrims land. The island is there.
*Yurumein* is still there. It is still the motherland.
The position accepts this responsibility. It tends the island.
