## Shown
- The Flame Towers. On the hill. Visible from the old city, from the bay, from the airport. The LED flags at night. The argument the city makes about itself, every night, automatically.
- The Old City — İçəri Şəhər. The walled medieval city. UNESCO World Heritage. The Maiden Tower, the Palace of the Shirvanshahs. The carpet shops and the tea houses. The ancient city inside the oil city.
- The Boulevard. The Caspian promenade. The new development along the waterfront — the Heydar Aliyev Centre (Zaha Hadid), the Carpet Museum shaped like a rolled carpet, the Crystal Hall. The money made architecture along the water's edge.
- Martyrs' Lane — Şəhidlər Xiyabanı. The hillside cemetery above the old city. The graves of those killed by Soviet troops in January 1990 — Black January. The graves of those killed in the Karabakh wars. The flame burning continuously. The view of the city from the graves.
- The White City. The new residential development on the former oil-contaminated land east of the centre. Clean, expensive, designed. The oil industry's environmental debt being converted into real estate.
