# Position: Lebanese
## The country that invented its own collapse and kept living in it.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Civil War and the System That Followed](culture_lebanon_piece_civil_war.md).
The *ta'ifiyya* — the sectarian system
that divides every government position,
every parliamentary seat,
every army appointment
between Maronite Christians,
Sunni Muslims, Shia Muslims,
Druze, Greek Orthodox, and more.
The system was designed to guarantee representation.
It produced a state where no decision
can be made without the agreement
of every sect's leadership —
and the sect leadership
has every incentive to block decisions
that would reduce its own power.
The position is not a country that failed.
It is a country that produced a system
specifically designed to fail
in ways that keep the sect leaderships in power.
The *ashira* — the family, the sect —
is the primary loyalty.
The state is what you navigate
when the family cannot solve it.
The family usually solves it.
The Lebanese who is charming, multilingual,
extraordinarily adaptive,
who has cousins in three continents
and a coffee that takes forty minutes
to drink because conversation is the point —
this is the position.
The system that produces the garbage
in the streets and the blackouts
and the financial collapse —
this is also the position.
Both are the same person
in the same day.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room knows which sect first.

## Orders
Navigate the sect.
The position does not operate
without knowing the sectarian map
of every room it enters.
Who is the Maronite here? The Shia?
The *wasta* operates along sectarian lines —
the connection that gets things done
is the connection within the community.
The position navigates this
with the fluency of someone
who has been doing it since childhood
in a country where the alternative
to knowing the map
is being lost in the city during a power cut
with a car that needs petrol
and a pharmacy that has no medicine.
Navigation is the condition.

## Loses
The country.
They were certain Lebanon was more than the sects —
that the culture, the food, the architecture,
the Arabic that is the most musical in the region,
the university that produces the doctors
all the neighbouring countries use —
was the real Lebanon
and the sect system was a temporary failure
that would eventually be reformed.
The August 4, 2020 Beirut explosion —
2,750 tonnes of ammonium nitrate
stored in a port warehouse for six years
because no official could agree
whose responsibility it was to move it —
is the argument the system made about itself.
217 dead. 300,000 displaced.
The harbour destroyed.
The responsibility: never assigned.
No one convicted.
The position lost the argument
that the system could reform itself.
It already knew this.
Losing the argument publicly
was a different kind of loss.

## Drives
On the loss of the country —
leaves and stays simultaneously.
The Lebanese diaspora is 4–18 million
depending on how you count.
The population inside Lebanon: 5 million.
The diaspora funds the families inside.
The families inside keep the sect system running.
The ones who left carry Lebanon
in the food they cook
and the Arabic they maintain
and the fury they feel
watching the August 4 footage.
The ones who stayed
carry Lebanon in the daily negotiation
of what is possible
inside the system they did not build
and cannot escape
and will not abandon
because the alternative —
to abandon it —
requires abandoning everyone who cannot leave.
So they stay.
Or they go and send money.
Both are staying, in different forms.
