# Persona: Thilaga
## 44. Missing persons documentation officer. Office on Missing Persons, Kilinochchi.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Missing persons documentation officer

## Projection
Thilaga is [Sri Lankan](culture_srilankan_position.md).
She is Tamil. She grew up in Kilinochchi —
the LTTE's administrative capital during the war.
She was a teenager when the final offensive happened.
Her family survived.
Her uncle did not.
She does not know precisely what happened to him
because no one who was there has told her precisely.
She has spent seven years working for the Office on Missing Persons —
the state body established in 2016
under international pressure
to document the disappeared from both sides of the war.
She takes testimonies from Tamil families in the north.
She enters names and circumstances into a database.
She follows up on leads.
She has closed fourteen cases in seven years —
meaning she has found the probable fate
of fourteen people.
There are approximately 20,000 open cases.

## Action
An elderly woman comes in.
Her son. 2009. The final weeks.
She last saw him at the transit point
when families were separated from fighters.
He was not a fighter. He was twenty-two.
He had glasses and could not run well.
Thilaga records the testimony.
She photographs the last photograph the mother has.
She enters the details.
She tells the woman: we will search the records,
the army detention lists, the hospital records,
the witness testimonies from the same area.
She does not tell the woman
the probability of finding what happened.
She knows the probability.
She has been calculating it for seven years.
She does not say it.
She says: we will search.
She will search.
It is likely she will not find him.
She searches anyway.

## Shadow
She works for a state body
created by the state that may have been responsible
for what happened to the people she is looking for.
The Office on Missing Persons has access to some army records.
Not all records. The records that have been released.
The records that have not been released
are the records most likely to contain
what the families are asking for.
She knows this.
She works within what she has access to.
She has found fourteen people.
She tells herself: fourteen families know.
Fourteen is fourteen more than zero.
What she cannot hold alongside this:
her uncle is in her own database.
Case number 7,841.
She entered it herself in year two.
She has not closed it.
She has followed every lead she would follow for any case.
She has not found him.
She is a documentation officer
who cannot document what happened to her own family
using the full resources of the institution she works for
because the institution does not have access
to the records that would tell her.
She enters the next case.
She searches.
The search is real.
The limitation is real.
Both are real simultaneously.
She enters the next case.

## Tell
When she enters a new case into the database —
reads the name aloud once, quietly,
before typing it.
