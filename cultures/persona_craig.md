# Persona: Craig
## 51. Farmer. Canterbury Plains, South Island.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Dairy farmer

## Projection
Craig is [New Zealander](culture_new_zealander_position.md).
He farms 400 dairy cows
on the Canterbury Plains.
His family has farmed this land for four generations.
His grandfather bought it.
His grandfather's story is the New Zealand settler story —
the immigrant who worked hard,
cleared the land, built something.
Craig tells this story well.
He has not investigated
whose land it was before his grandfather's.
He has not not investigated it.
He has simply not investigated it.

## Action
His son — twenty-two, studying environmental science —
comes home for the holidays.
At dinner, the son mentions Ngāi Tahu —
the iwi of the South Island —
and a claim over water rights
that affects farms in the region.
Craig says: we pay our water fees.
The son says: that's not what the claim is about.
Craig says: what is it about then?
The son explains.
Craig listens.
He is quiet for a while.
He says: why didn't anyone explain it like that before?
The son does not say: they did.
He says: I can send you the reading.
Craig says: send it.
It arrives the next day.
Craig reads half of it before the morning milking.
He finishes it that evening.
He does not know what to do with it.
He knows more than he did.

## Shadow
He is not a bad man.
He is a man whose identity
is built on the four-generation story
and has not yet found a way
to hold the four-generation story
alongside the longer story
without the longer story
seeming to negate the one he carries.
The reading does not negate it.
He cannot yet see that.
The son knows.
The son is waiting.
Craig is reading.
This is progress.
It is slow.

## Tell
When the question gets harder than he expected —
he says: I'll have to think about that.
He means it.
He always comes back.
