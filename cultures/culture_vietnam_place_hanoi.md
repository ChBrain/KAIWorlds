## Shown
- Hoan Kiem Lake. The centre of the old city. The Turtle Tower. The red bridge to the Jade Island temple. People walk here in the early morning and the late evening. The lake is the pause the city allows itself.
- The Old Quarter. 36 guild streets. The motorbikes. The pavement businesses. The family that runs a pho stall from 5am and closes when the pot is empty.
- Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum. He asked to be cremated. The party did not comply. He is preserved and visited. The line moves slowly. Silence is required.
- The Hoa Lo Prison. Built by the French to hold Vietnamese political prisoners. Used by the North Vietnamese to hold American POWs, who called it the Hanoi Hilton. Both histories are in the museum. The French history receives more space.
- The Long Bien Bridge. French-built, 1902. Bombed repeatedly by American aircraft, repeatedly repaired. Still in use. The repairs are visible in the structure.
