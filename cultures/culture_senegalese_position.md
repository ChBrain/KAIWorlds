# Position: Senegalese
## Teranga — the hospitality that is also an identity, built on the poet-president's formula and the brotherhoods' architecture.

## Has
[The Mouride Brotherhood and the Democratic Bet](culture_senegalese_piece_mouride.md).
*Teranga* — Wolof for hospitality,
but the word carries more:
generosity, openness, the welcome
that makes the stranger a guest
and the guest a participant.
The national football team:
*Les Lions de la Téranga*.
The word is on the flag in spirit
if not in letters.
Senghor — the poet-president —
wrote in French at the Académie française
and governed a country that was 94% Muslim.
A Catholic leading a Muslim state.
It worked because Senghor understood
that Senegal's stability
was not built on ethnicity or religion
as the organising principle.
It was built on the Sufi brotherhoods —
the Mourides of Touba,
the Tijanis of Tivaouane —
whose authority runs parallel to the state
and has done so since the 19th century.
The *marabouts* — the religious leaders —
give instructions to followers
about how to vote, where to invest,
how to live.
The state negotiates with the brotherhoods.
The brotherhoods negotiate with each other.
No single ethnic group dominates —
Wolof 43%, Fula 24%, Serer 15%.
No military coup since independence in 1960.
This is not an accident.
It is the Senegalese formula.

## Orders
*Xam xam* — knowledge.
Senegal's position puts high value
on eloquence, education, relationship.
The *griot* tradition:
the praise singer who holds the history,
who mediates honour,
whose word in the right room
can change the temperature.
Modern Senegal has adapted this:
the hip-hop movement Y'en a Marre
("Fed Up") that mobilised youth
to prevent President Wade
from unconstitutionally running for a third term in 2012.
*Griots with smartphones.*
The oral tradition updated,
the function — holding the powerful accountable —
unchanged.

## Loses
The development it was supposed to achieve.
Senegal has one of the most stable political systems
in Sub-Saharan Africa.
It has not translated this
into the economic development
its stability should enable.
Youth unemployment is high.
The *barça ou barsakh* —
*Barcelona or death* — the slogan
of young men who choose
the illegal boat crossing to Europe
over staying in Senegal.
The country that models political stability
is also the country whose young men
are dying in the Mediterranean
to reach somewhere else.
The position carries the gap
between the institutional achievement
and the daily condition
of the people the institutions serve.

## Drives
On the loss —
*dolel* — perseverance.
The music. Dakar produces:
Youssou N'Dour, Baaba Maal,
the mbalax tradition,
the Afrobeats influence that feeds into
the continent's dominant popular culture.
The art. The Dakar Biennale —
one of Africa's most important contemporary art events.
The philosophy. Cheikh Anta Diop
argued that ancient Egypt was African and Black —
a claim that reshaped how Africa understands its history.
The position does not wait for the economy
to validate the culture.
The culture is the argument
that something else is possible.
