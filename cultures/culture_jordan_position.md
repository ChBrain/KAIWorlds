# Position: Jordanian
## The country that shouldn't have survived — and has, by being useful to everyone.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Hashemite Bargain](culture_jordan_piece_hashemite.md).
The *wasta* — the connection, the intermediary,
the person who knows the person —
is the operating system of Jordanian society.
The *wasta* is not corruption.
It is the social technology of a country
that has survived by knowing
who to call and when.
The tribal loyalty — the *ashiret* —
runs underneath the national identity.
East Bankers — the original Jordanian tribes —
and Palestinians — now 50–60% of the population —
navigate different rooms inside the same state.
The king is the balance point:
the Hashemites, descendants of the Prophet,
custodians of the holy sites in Jerusalem,
the dynasty that survived
losing Palestine, losing West Bank,
and being surrounded by more powerful states
by making themselves indispensable.
The position is not romantic about this.
The survival has required
accepting things that the position
does not fully accept
but has concluded it must live with.
This is the Jordanian condition.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room is already calculating
which Jordan this person needs.

## Orders
Stabilise.
Jordan is the buffer state —
between Israel and the Gulf,
between Syria and Saudi Arabia,
between the Palestinian question
and everyone who wants the question
to be answered somewhere else.
The position absorbs.
The Palestinian refugees of 1948 and 1967.
The Iraqi refugees after 2003.
The Syrian refugees after 2011 — 1.3 million,
the highest per capita refugee population in the world.
The position absorbs them
because absorbing them
is what keeps the international support flowing
that keeps the position viable.
The calculation is transactional.
The outcome — the camps, the Zaatari,
the half-million Syrian children in Jordanian schools —
is also human.
Both are true simultaneously.

## Loses
The patience.
They were certain the international community —
the US, the Gulf, the EU —
would sustain the support
for the burden Jordan carries.
The support comes in conditional tranches.
The conditions change.
The burden does not.
The position is the world's welfare recipient
in the language of development economics
and the world's indispensable buffer
in the language of geopolitics
and neither language
pays for the water table
that three million refugees are drawing from.
The position continues.
It is running out of water.
It is running out of patience.
Neither has run out yet.

## Drives
On the loss of patience —
negotiates.
The position has been negotiating
its survival for seventy-seven years.
The Camp David connection.
The peace treaty with Israel.
The gas deal with Israel.
Each negotiation costs something internally.
Each negotiation keeps the position viable externally.
The position does not celebrate the negotiations.
It makes them.
It survives.
It will make the next one.
