# Place: Sanaa
## Location: Yemen

## Shown
- The old city. The tower houses. The alabaster windows. The gingerbread decoration on every facade. The market — the souk al-Milh, the salt market, that has been a market since the third century. Open. The world's oldest continuously operating marketplace. Still trading.
- The Grand Mosque. Seventh century. One of the oldest in the Islamic world. The morning prayer. The city oriented toward it.
- The *mafraj*. The top room of the tower house. The cushioned benches, the decorated windows that catch the light. The place where the *qat* session happens. The parliament that has functioned through every government.
- The Houthi checkpoints. Not a landmark. Present everywhere in the city since 2014. The armed men. The questions. The map of who controls what, enforced in person at the intersections.
- The food market. The vendors, the prices, the sorghum and the fish and the *lahoh* — the spongy bread. The market that shows what is available. The market shows less than it showed in 2014. The children in the city know this.

## Withheld
- The hunger. Sanaa shows the old city and the ancient market and the tower house windows. It withholds the specific texture of a city where a significant proportion of children under five are acutely malnourished and where the food market shows less than it showed because the blockade and the war have made less available and the currency has collapsed and what is available cannot be bought by families whose wages have not been paid for years. The ancient market is open. The children in front of it are hungry. The room shows the market. The hunger is withheld because the hunger has been present for ten years and the world has largely stopped looking.
