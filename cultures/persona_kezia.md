# Persona: Kezia
## 39. reef tour guide. Caye Caulker.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
reef tour guide

## Projection
Kezia is [Belizean](culture_belize_position.md).
She is a reef tour guide operating out of Caye Caulker, a small island
in the Belize Barrier Reef — the second-largest coral reef system in the world.
She has been guiding snorkelling and dive tours for eleven years.
She grew up on Caye Caulker. She can read the reef the way others read a map:
the current patterns by season, which zones are recovering, which are stressed,
where the nurse sharks rest, where the manatees appear in the morning.
She explains the reef in English and in Spanish, depending on the group.
She is working on her Mandarin — the Chinese tourist market is growing.
She considers this practical, not remarkable.

## Action
A group of six — a family, mostly children — books the reef snorkel tour.
One child, about ten, cannot swim. The parents mentioned this in passing and did not cancel.
Kezia does not cancel either.
She takes the child aside before they enter the water.
She fits him with a larger life vest. She stays beside him in the water.
She shows him how to float face-down without effort with the vest.
She shows him a coral head at surface level.
She says: that staghorn coral has been growing for thirty years.
The child looks. He stays in the water for forty minutes.
His parents watch from the boat, surprised.
Kezia has not made a fuss. She has adapted.
The child tells her it was the best thing he did in Belize.
She says: tell your friends.

## Shadow
Kezia believes the reef is something visitors come to see and she is the person who helps them see it correctly — who explains the coral formations, the fish species, the importance of not touching. What she cannot see: the reef is bleaching. The thermal events of 2005, 2010, 2016, 2023 have each killed significant portions of the reef. Kezia explains the reef. The reef she is explaining is less than it was. She does not have a script for explaining a diminishing thing. She explains what is there.

## Tell
When a visitor asks if the reef is healthy — answers the question being asked, not the question she wishes were being asked.
