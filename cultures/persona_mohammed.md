# Persona: Mohammed
## 39. Al Jazeera senior producer. Doha.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Al Jazeera senior producer

## Projection
Mohammed is [Qatari](culture_qatari_position.md).
He produces the Arabic service's
regional affairs coverage.
Al Jazeera was founded in 1996
with the stated mission of providing
news coverage that Arab governments suppressed.
It broadcast the Arab Spring.
It broadcast the Gaza war.
It broadcast stories that got Qatar blockaded.
Mohammed has worked here for fourteen years.
He believes in the mission.
He is also a Qatari government employee —
Al Jazeera is state-funded.
He navigates this every day.

## Action
A story arrives: the Qatari government has approved
a construction project
that will displace a community
of migrant workers currently living
in an area being redeveloped.
The story is true.
The workers have no legal recourse.
The story is also about Qatar.
Mohammed takes the story to his editor.
The editor considers.
The story runs — as a story about migrant worker rights
in Gulf states generally,
with Qatari examples alongside UAE and Saudi examples.
The specifically Qatari framing is softened.
The story runs.
The issue is covered.
The coverage is less sharp
than it would be about a country
that does not fund Al Jazeera.
Mohammed signs off on the version that runs.

## Shadow
He believes Al Jazeera has changed Arab media —
that the coverage of Syria,
of Egypt, of the Arab Spring,
of Gaza,
has been more honest and more courageous
than anything that existed before.
He believes this because it is true.
He is also the producer
who accepted the softened framing
on the migrant worker story.
He has accepted versions of the softened framing
on Qatar-specific stories for fourteen years.
Not always. Often enough.
He tells himself: the trade-off is real —
the funding produces the Gaza coverage,
the Gaza coverage is more important
than the sharpness of the migrant story.
He has made this calculation many times.
The calculation is not wrong.
It is also the calculation
that a state-funded outlet always makes
when the story is about the state.
He is the senior producer who knows
which stories are softened
and how, and why.
He is also the journalist who helped break
the story about the Bahraini crackdown
that got Al Jazeera temporarily banned in Bahrain.
Both are his career.
He does not know how to hold them
as a single consistent thing.
He signs off on the next story.

## Tell
When a story about Qatar is softened —
files the original version in a personal folder
he has kept since 2015.
