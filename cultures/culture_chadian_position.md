# Position: Chadian
## The desert kingdom — where the president dies on the frontline the day after his re-election, and his son inherits the state.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Déby Dynasty](culture_chadian_piece_deby.md).
N'Djamena. The Chari River.
Lake Chad — the lake that has shrunk
by 90% since 1963
due to climate change, irrigation, population growth.
Once the size of the Caspian Sea.
Now smaller than Corsica.
The lake that feeds the Basin —
Nigeria, Niger, Cameroon, Chad —
and whose shrinking
is one of the world's most concrete
demonstrations of climate disruption.
The Zaghawa in the north and east —
Déby's group, the military backbone.
The Sara in the south — Christian,
agricultural, historically excluded from power.
The Arab communities in the east.
The Toubou in the north.
Over 200 distinct groups.
A country held together by force
and mediated by patronage.
The collectivism: high.
The hierarchy: exceptionally steep —
military, ethnic, and regional.
N'Djamena is the north's city.
The south's exclusion is structural.

## Orders
*Sabr* — patience, endurance, the Arabic-influenced concept
that runs through the predominantly Muslim north.
The Chadian position navigates
a country where the state
has always been a military operation
managed by a patron
whose primary obligation is to his clan
and only secondarily to the country.
Navigation means being useful to the patron
or invisible to the patron.
Both are survival strategies.
Both are deployed.

## Loses
The democratic possibility.
Chad is surrounded by countries
with military juntas —
Mali, Niger, Burkina Faso, Sudan —
and is itself governed by a president
who inherited the position
from his father's corpse.
The constitutional succession was clear:
the National Assembly speaker should take over,
elections within 90 days.
France called the military council
a necessary response to exceptional circumstances
and pledged support.
The position carries the knowledge
that the democratic rule
which was written into the constitution
was overridden by the same people
who wrote it —
and the world's most powerful
democracy-promoting power
applauded.

## Drives
On the loss —
the trade routes.
N'Djamena is the crossroads of Central Africa —
linking the Sahel to the forest zone,
the oil fields of the south
to the trading networks of the north.
The city's commerce does not stop
because the president changes.
The Chadian music —
*bikutsi* rhythms absorbed from the south,
the griot traditions of the north.
The oil that has funded
a construction boom in N'Djamena
even as the south remains underserved.
The position builds.
It has always built
around whatever the state does.
The state has never been reliable enough
to build toward.
