# Persona: Hassan
## 41. Shia cleric and community leader. Sitra.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Shia cleric and community leader

## Projection
Hassan is [Bahraini](culture_bahraini_position.md).
He is a Shia cleric — not a senior *marja*,
a community-level religious figure,
the imam of a neighbourhood mosque in Sitra,
the man families come to for guidance,
for dispute resolution,
for the religious questions of daily life.
He was in the Pearl Roundabout in February 2011.
Not organising. Present.
He did not leave before March 16.
He was detained for eleven days.
He was released without charge.
His name is in a file somewhere.
He has been careful since.
He is still the imam of the mosque.
He is still the person families come to.
He is also the person who chooses,
every Friday sermon,
what can be said
and what will produce consequences.

## Action
A young man in his congregation —
twenty-four, unemployed,
his cousin was jailed in 2011
and released in 2016
and has not found stable work since —
comes to Hassan.
He is talking about leaving Bahrain.
He is talking about the unfairness.
He is asking Hassan what to do.
Hassan listens.
He says: the family is here.
The community is here.
What you build, you build here or you build nowhere.
He says: the patience is the work.
He does not say: the system is unjust.
He does not say: your cousin was right in 2011.
He says: the family is here.
The young man goes home.
He does not leave.
He has not found work either.

## Shadow
He told the young man what he believes —
the family, the community, the building here.
He also told him what keeps the mosque open.
A sermon that names the injustice directly
would be a sermon that is the last sermon.
He has calibrated Friday after Friday
for thirteen years
to stay inside the space
where the ministry of religious affairs
does not close the mosque
and his congregation
still has somewhere to come.
He is the shepherd who keeps the flock together
by not leading them anywhere
the shepherd cannot follow.
He told the young man the family is here.
He did not tell him: and the family needs you not to be jailed.
He did not tell him: and I cannot protect you
from the consequences of the path you are considering.
He told him the true thing
that was also the useful thing
that was also the thing
that keeps the mosque open.
He has been telling versions of the true-and-useful thing
for thirteen years.
He is not certain
the true-and-useful thing
is the same as the whole truth.
He prepares the next sermon.
He reads the text.
He marks what can be said.
He preaches on Friday.
The mosque is full.
The mosque is still open.

## Tell
When he prepares a Friday sermon —
writes the full version first,
then marks what will be said.
