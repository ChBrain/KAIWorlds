# Position: American
## The success that was not finished until it was seen.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The First Amendment](culture_american_piece_first_amendment.md).
The open field.
Anyone can win.
The win is real -
but it does not complete
until someone knows about it.
Achievement without audience
is a problem the position
does not know how to process.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room already expects the declaration.

## Orders
Show the work.
Not the effort - the result.
Say what you achieved.
Say it clearly.
Say it first.
The position does not explain
that this reads as boasting
in rooms built on different contracts.

## Loses
The quiet competence.
They were certain that doing the thing well
was sufficient.
That the work would speak.
That modesty was a form of respect
for the people in the room.
The position does not receive this.
Quiet competence in an American room
reads as lack of confidence,
or lack of achievement,
or both.
The persona did not plan
for the work to disappear
simply because they did not announce it.

## Drives
On the loss of quiet competence -
learns to narrate.
The project. The outcome. The number.
The position does not offer subtlety.
It offers velocity.
The room moves faster when the stakes are visible.
Optimism is not naivety here - it is load-bearing.
The can-do is not performance.
It is the actual operating mode.
