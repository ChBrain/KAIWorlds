# Piece: The Aapravasi Ghat and the Great Experiment
## The first site of Indian indentured labour — where the British proved 'free' workers could replace slaves, and built a nation in the process.

## Owner
- **Place:** [Port Louis](culture_mauritian_place_portlouis.md)

## Place
The Aapravasi Ghat.
Port Louis harbour.
The 14 stone steps
where every indentured labourer
set foot in Mauritius
after crossing the *kala pani* —
the black water,
the ocean crossing
that was itself a taboo.
High-caste Hindus were not supposed
to cross the ocean.
They crossed anyway.
They could not go back
once they had.
The steps are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
The steps are partial.
The rest of the depot:
demolished by 20th century development.
The 14 steps and a few walls remain.
The foundation of a nation
in a partial ruin.

## Load Bearing
Remove the Great Experiment
and Mauritius is a small island
that moved from French to British control
in 1814 with an existing Afro-Creole population
and Franco-Mauritian planters.
A sugar island. Maybe tourism by now.
With it: the specific outcome
of the British decision in 1834
to make Mauritius the first site
of the replacement of slave labour
with indentured labour.
The experiment was this:
could 'free' workers — workers with contracts,
not chains — produce as much sugar
as enslaved workers?
The answer the British needed: yes.
The answer they got: yes, more.
Because indentured workers
who completed their contracts
and chose to stay
invested in the island.
They bought small parcels of land.
They established temples and schools.
They sent children to those schools.
They became the peasant farming class
that the plantation economy
had never had.
452,602 workers arrived between 1834 and 1920.
Approximately two-thirds stayed.
By 1871, 68% of the population was Indian.
By independence in 1968,
the demographic majority was Indo-Mauritian.
The electoral system
weighted toward Indo-Mauritians.
The first prime minister: Seewoosagur Ramgoolam.
Indo-Mauritian.
The dynasty: his son Navin Ramgoolam
became prime minister three times.
The Great Experiment produced
not a labour outcome
but a demographic outcome:
a nation assembled from elsewhere,
where the people who arrived latest
in the worst conditions
became the majority
and eventually the governing class.
Remove the piece:
a sugar island.
With it: the most ethnically complex,
economically successful,
genuinely multiracial democracy
in Africa —
built on a system
designed to exploit,
that produced something else.
The exploitation is not erased.
The something else is also real.

## Apparent
1638: Dutch establish settlements.
Abandon by 1710.
1715: French settlement. Isle de France.
Sugar plantations begin.
Enslaved Africans brought.
Primarily from Madagascar and Mozambique.
1810: British capture during Napoleonic Wars.
1814: Treaty of Paris. British colony.
French community, language, culture: remain.
1835: Abolition of slavery.
August 1, 1835: the day.
Former slaves: technically free.
Required to work for former owners
as 'apprentices' for four years.
1835: first ship of Indian indentured labourers.
1834–1920: 452,602 labourers arrive.
Majority from Bihar, UP, Tamil Nadu.
Also Andhra Pradesh, Maharashtra.
Small numbers from China, Mozambique, Madagascar.
The Aapravasi Ghat depot: built 1849.
Medical inspection. Registration.
Distribution to sugar estates.
Contract: 5 years. Then: renew or return.
Most renewed. Or stayed without renewing.
1968: independence. Seewoosagur Ramgoolam, PM.
1970: sugar boom.
1971: Export Processing Zone established.
Textile industry.
The diversification from sugar begins.
1982: Anerood Jugnauth becomes PM.
Economic reform. Finance sector.
Mauritius becomes a regional financial hub.
2006: Aapravasi Ghat — UNESCO World Heritage Site.
Current: highest HDI in Africa.
GDP per capita well above African average.
The communal system: intact.
The hierarchy within it: intact.
The 14 steps: still there.
Still visited.
