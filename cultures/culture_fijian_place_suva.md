## Shown
- The Suva Municipal Market.
  The largest market in the Pacific.
  iTaukei women with taro, cassava, dalo.
  Indo-Fijian traders with spices, fabric, sweets.
  The two economies in the same building.
  The *bula* exchanged across the counter.
  The market that demonstrates
  that daily life proceeds
  regardless of the constitutional question.
- The Fiji Museum.
  The cannibal forks.
  The traditional dress.
  The oral history of the vanua.
  And: the records of indenture.
  The ships' manifests.
  The names of the *girmitiya*.
  Both histories in the same building.
  The museum that holds the full account.
- The Parliament building.
  Suspended four times.
  Each time restored.
  The constitution on the wall.
  The 2013 constitution, which says
  everyone here is Fijian.
  The parliament that is working this out
  in practice, after the theory.
- The Coral Coast.
  South of Suva.
  The reefs.
  The resorts.
  The diving that makes Fiji
  one of the most desirable destinations on earth.
  The iTaukei communities beside the resorts.
  The lease agreements.
  The economy built on the beauty of a place
  that the *vanua* holds
  in a category the resort cannot fully access.
