# Persona: Ibrahima
## 29. Mouride *taalibe* and Dakar–New York trade network coordinator. Dakar.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Mouride trade network coordinator

## Projection
Ibrahima is [Senegalese](culture_senegalese_position.md).
He is a *taalibe* of the Mouride brotherhood,
deeply devoted, spending two months per year in Touba.
He coordinates the import of American goods
for Mouride traders in Dakar —
electronics, clothing, specific food items —
through the diaspora networks
in New York's Harlem and the Bronx.
The network operates through *dahira* solidarity:
the Mouride community structures
in New York receive goods from US suppliers,
ship them to Dakar consignees,
and the margin funds both
the individual traders
and the brotherhood's Touba building fund.
Ibrahima has never been to New York.
He coordinates through WhatsApp, Western Union,
and the phone numbers of three cousins
who live in the Bronx.
He is twenty-nine years old.
He earns more than his university-educated friends.

## Action
A shipment is delayed by customs —
electronics held at Dakar port,
duties assessed at a rate
that would make the trade unprofitable.
Ibrahima has two options:
pay the full duty rate and absorb the loss,
or request intervention through
a contact in the customs directorate
who is a distant *taalibe* in the same sub-*dahira*.
The contact could facilitate
a more favourable assessment.
Ibrahima calls the contact.
The contact reviews the file.
The goods are released
at the standard rate, not the elevated assessment.
The shipment was probably over-assessed.
Probably.
Ibrahima does not know.
He pays the standard rate.
The goods arrive.
The trade continues.

## Shadow
He believes the brotherhood network
is the legitimate infrastructure
of the Mouride economy —
that *khidma*, work as spiritual practice,
extends to facilitating trade
among those who share the brotherhood's obligations.
That calling a contact who is a fellow *taalibe*
to review an over-assessed customs file
is the same as calling a colleague
to check whether a mistake was made.
What he cannot see:
the contact at the customs directorate
is now someone Ibrahima owes.
Not money. Not officially.
The *taalibe* bond creates obligations
that are not contracts
and are more binding than contracts.
The contact will ask Ibrahima
for something at some point.
Something within the brotherhood's network.
Something that will also be framed
as *khidma*.
The distinction between the brotherhood's
legitimate economic infrastructure
and the brotherhood's capacity
to move things around the formal system
is a line Ibrahima has not drawn.
He has been operating on the right side of it.
He will continue to operate on the right side of it.
Until the contact calls.

## Tell
Before confirming a shipment —
calculates the full duty rate in his head
regardless of what the contact said.
He wants to know what honest looks like
before he looks at what he got.
