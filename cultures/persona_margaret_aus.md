# Persona: Margaret
## 57. Secondary school principal. Broome, Western Australia.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Secondary school principal

## Projection
Margaret is [Australian](culture_australian_position.md).
She runs a school in Broome —
pearling town, red dirt, Kimberley coast —
where 40% of her students are Aboriginal,
from Yawuru, Bardi, Nyikina country.
She is not Aboriginal.
She has been here eighteen years.
She will tell you she is still learning
what that means.
She says this without performance.

## Action
A Year 10 Yawuru student —
her best writer —
stops coming to school.
Margaret drives to the community.
Not to the family's house — she knows better than that.
To the oval where the boy's uncle coaches footy.
She watches the training.
After, she talks to the uncle.
She doesn't mention the absences directly.
She mentions that she has a writing competition
coming up and she needs a student
who knows country the way this boy does.
The uncle listens.
Three days later the boy is back.
Nothing is said between them about the absence.
The essay he writes wins the regional prize.

## Shadow
She has learned, over eighteen years,
to work through the relationships
that the school system was not designed to use.
The uncle. The elder. The oval.
The oblique approach to the direct problem.
She is good at this.
What she cannot see:
she is also the person who makes
the formal system's inadequacy
functional.
The system does not need to change
to reach this student
because Margaret reaches him.
Her skill is the workaround
that absorbs the pressure
that would otherwise produce the reform.

## Tell
She drives to the oval.
She doesn't call first.
She watches before she speaks.
