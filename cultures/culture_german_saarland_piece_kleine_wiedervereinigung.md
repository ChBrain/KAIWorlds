# Piece: Die Kleine Wiedervereinigung
## The little reunification. The state that voted to be German, twice.

## Owner
- **Place:** Saarbrücken

## Place
Saarbrücken. The Saar river.
The French border: fifteen minutes west.
The Lorraine steelworks visible
from the high points of the city
on a clear day.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Saarland position loses its defining act.
The Kleine Wiedervereinigung - the little reunification -
is what the Saarland calls its return to Germany in 1957.
It voted in 1955: 67.7% against remaining a French protectorate.
The vote was interpreted as a vote for Germany.
It was.
But the piece is load-bearing not because of the vote
but because of what the vote revealed:
a population that had lived under French economic and political control
since 1919, with a brief return to Germany 1935–1945,
and that voted emphatically
to be German.
Not European. Not independent.
German.
The Saarland has voted to return to Germany
more times than any other territory in German history.
1935: 90.7% for reunion with Germany.
1955: 67.7% against remaining outside Germany.
Both votes: unambiguous.
The position carries the weight of having chosen.

## Apparent
The border: a ten-minute drive.
Until Schengen: a real border with customs.
Now: open.
The French language: taught as first foreign language
in Saarland schools since 1957.
The agreement with France
required this as a condition of return.
The language is still taught.
The agreement is no longer binding.
The teaching continues.
The French influence: in the food,
in the occasional loan word,
in the architectural style of some districts.
Present. Not dominant.
The coal: mostly gone.
The last Saarland mine closed
before the last Ruhr mine.
The Saarland had less coal
and it ran out sooner.
The Völklingen ironworks: UNESCO World Heritage.
A blast furnace complex, 1873–1986.
The largest preserved industrial monument in the world.
The French border: visible from the car park.

## Yearbook
1919: the Saar separated from Germany.
League of Nations mandate. French coal administration.
1935: referendum. 90.7% for return to Germany.
The Nazis receive the Saar back.
1945: French troops occupy the Saar.
Again.
1947: the Saar Protectorate established.
French protectorate. Economic union with France.
Own currency: the Saar franc.
Own postage stamps.
Own Olympic team: 1952 Helsinki.
Own football team: 1954 World Cup qualifying.
1955: the referendum.
67.7% against European status under French influence.
Interpreted as: we want to be German.
1957: the Kleine Wiedervereinigung.
1 January. The Saar becomes the tenth West German state.
The Saar franc: replaced by Deutsche Mark in 1959.
The French language teaching: agreed.
The Völklingen ironworks: operating until 1986.
UNESCO: 1994.
Today: the smallest Bundesland by area
that is not a city-state.
