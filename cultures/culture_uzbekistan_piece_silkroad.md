# Piece: The Silk Road Cities
## Samarkand, Bukhara, Khiva — what they were, what they became, and what they are being made into.

## Owner
- **Place:** Tashkent

## Place
Samarkand. The Registan.
Three madrasas around a central square.
Tilework in cobalt and turquoise.
Tamerlane's empire administered from here.
The tourists come to see it.
The UNESCO listing protects it.
The restoration has replaced some original tiles
with tiles that are more uniform
and less old
and more photographable.
Both the original and the replacement are there.
Most visitors cannot tell the difference.
Some can.

## Load Bearing
Remove the Silk Road cities
and Uzbekistan is a double-landlocked Central Asian state
with a cotton monoculture legacy
and an authoritarian political history.
With it: Uzbekistan is the heir
to one of the great civilisational crossroads —
the place where the trade routes met,
where Islamic scholarship flourished,
where Tamerlane built an empire
and filled Samarkand with the artists and scholars
he brought back from every conquest.
Al-Biruni. Ibn Sina — Avicenna.
Ulugh Beg's observatory.
The mathematics and astronomy and medicine
that preserved Greek learning
and transmitted it to Europe.
This inheritance is real.
It is also the inheritance
that the Uzbekistani state is currently converting
into tourism revenue and national prestige
in ways that are not always
what the inheritance itself would recommend.
The restoration of Samarkand:
the tiles replaced, the streets cleared,
the vendors relocated,
the living city beside the monument
made cleaner and more photogenic
and less itself.
Remove the piece: a post-Soviet state.
With it: a post-Soviet state
that is the custodian of something the world
does not fully value
until it is too late —
and that is in the process of deciding
how to value it itself,
with the tools of a state
that learned heritage management from the Soviets
and is learning tourism marketing from the internet
and has not yet decided
what Samarkand is for.

## Apparent
329 BCE: Alexander passes through Marakanda — Samarkand.
Says it is more beautiful than he imagined.
The Islamic conquest: 7th century.
The Abbasid period: the great scholarship.
1220: Genghis Khan destroys Samarkand.
1370: Tamerlane — Timur — makes Samarkand his capital.
The Timurid renaissance. Ulugh Beg's observatory, 1420.
1500: the Shaybanid Uzbeks. The Timurid dynasty ends.
1868: Russian conquest. Samarkand becomes part of Russian Turkestan.
1924: the Uzbek Soviet Socialist Republic.
The cotton monoculture begins.
The Aral Sea irrigation begins.
1991: independence. Islam Karimov.
Karimov governs until his death in 2016.
The country closed, the opposition suppressed,
the Andijan massacre 2005: government forces fire on protesters.
Estimates: 200–1,500 dead.
2016: Shavkat Mirziyoyev.
The cautious opening.
Samarkand: UNESCO World Heritage Site.
International tourist arrivals tripling.
The restoration continues.
The tiles continue being replaced.
The Registan is more visited than at any point
since the Silk Road was the Silk Road.
Whether it is more itself is the question
the restoration has not answered.
