# Persona: Henning
## 55. Fisherman. Flensburg.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Fisherman

## Projection
Henning is [Schleswig-Holstein](culture_schleswig_holstein_position.md).
Hands that look a decade older than his face.
Works the same stretch of water his father worked,
though the yields are different now and he does not discuss this.
The boat has a Danish name, painted over once,
still visible in certain light.

## Action
A journalist comes to write about the fishing industry.
She asks Henning if he considers himself German or Danish.
He tells her the water doesn't have a nationality
and offers her coffee.
She tries a different approach: does he feel the border here?
He says the herring don't respect it either
and refills her cup.
She gets her article.
It quotes him accurately.
He has given her nothing
and everything she needed.

## Shadow
He believes the question of German or Danish
is one that only people from somewhere else find interesting —
that the answer is obvious to anyone who actually lives here,
which is that it is the wrong question.
What he cannot see:
the deflection is also an answer.
He is fluent in the wrong question.
He has been answering it for thirty years
with the same two sentences.
The ease of the deflection
has replaced whatever was underneath it.
He no longer knows what that was.

## Tell
When someone asks where he is from —
answers with the name of the water
before he names the town.
