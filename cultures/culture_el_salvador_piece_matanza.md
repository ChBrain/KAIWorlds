## Load Bearing
Remove La Matanza and El Salvador is a small Central American country with a coffee oligarchy and indigenous population. With it: El Salvador is the country that killed 30,000 people in three weeks in 1932 — primarily indigenous — and produced a society in which the indigenous identity went underground for sixty years. The IDV 19 and UAI 94 together tell this story: the collectivism survived but it went interior; the uncertainty avoidance is calibrated to an event that proved the worst is not hypothetical.

## Apparent
1932: January. An indigenous and campesino uprising in the western highlands, partly organised by the Communist Party. The government response: General Maximiliano Hernández Martínez orders the massacre. 30,000 killed in three weeks. The survivors: destroy their traditional dress. Stop speaking Náhuatl and Pipil. The census categories for indigenous populations: effectively disappear for decades. The civil war (1979–1992): 75,000 dead. Archbishop Oscar Romero assassinated at the altar, 1980. The peace accords: 1992. The FMLN becomes a political party. 2009: Mauricio Funes, FMLN, elected president. The Bukele era (2019–present): Bitcoin as legal tender, mass incarceration of gang members, constitutional questions. The *pupusa*: still made every morning.
