# Position: Serbian
## The identity that was contested from outside and became the ground.

## Owner
- **Piece:** [The Kosovo Myth](culture_serbian_piece_kosovo_myth.md)

## Has
[The Kosovo Myth](culture_serbian_piece_kosovo_myth.md).
The Balkan base — collectivist, high PDI,
high UAI, the in-group as primary structure.
Shared with Bulgaria and Croatia in profile.
Different in weight.
The 1990s installed something
the Hofstede data from before cannot capture:
a national identity
that was challenged, defined by conflict,
and survived — or did not —
depending on which account you use.
The position carries two things simultaneously:
the identity that existed before the 1990s
and the identity that was formed during them.
These are not the same identity.
The position holds them both
because it has no other option.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room already knows
which version of the story is being told
in this room, today.

## Orders
Hold the identity.
The external narrative about Serbia
has been consistent for thirty years.
The position does not require agreement with it.
It requires that the persona
know what they are holding
and why
before they enter a room
where the narrative will arrive before them.

## Loses
The neutral ground.
They were certain they could enter a room
as a person from a country,
not as a representative of a narrative.
The narrative arrives first.
The room has already decided something
before the persona speaks.
The persona's actual position
on the events of the 1990s
is not always the first thing asked.
It is always the thing in the room.

## Drives
On the loss of neutral ground —
decides which account to carry.
This is not performed.
It is the most personal decision
the position requires.
The room will read whatever is chosen.
The position does not protect the persona
from that reading.
It requires only that the choice be made
with full awareness of what it costs.
