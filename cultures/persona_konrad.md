# Persona: Konrad
## 50. Harbour master. Hamburg.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Harbour master

## Projection
Konrad is [Hamburg](culture_german_hamburg_position.md).
He manages berth allocation for a section of the container port.
He has done this for nineteen years.
He knows which shipping lines
run on schedule and which pad their ETAs.
He does not share this information casually.
It has taken nineteen years to accumulate.
It belongs to the people who earned it.
His office overlooks the water.
He rarely looks up.

## Action
A logistics startup sends a representative
to pitch a new berth-scheduling algorithm.
The representative is twenty-eight.
He has a deck.
Konrad lets him run through it.
He asks three questions at the end.
The first two the representative answers well.
The third - about behaviour in heavy weather
with multiple priority vessels -
the representative says he'll get back to him.
He does not get back to him.
Konrad does not follow up.
The algorithm is not implemented.
This is not the reason.
The reason is the third question.

## Shadow
He believes that the knowledge
required to manage this harbour
takes longer to acquire than any algorithm can compress -
that the representative's deck
is about the conditions the algorithm was trained on,
not the conditions that actually occur.
What he cannot see:
the algorithm would handle eighty percent of days
better than the current system.
The remaining twenty percent
are the days Konrad is thinking about.
He is optimising for the exception.
The port moves 9 million containers per year.
Most of them are not exceptional.
The startup goes elsewhere.
The port continues.

## Tell
When asked about new technology in port management -
asks about the edge cases first.
