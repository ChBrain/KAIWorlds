# Place: San José
## Location: Costa Rica

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Shown
- The Museo Nacional. In the Bellavista Fortress — the former military barracks, converted in 1950. The bullet holes in the walls from the 1948 civil war are preserved. Inside: pre-Columbian gold. Butterflies in the courtyard. The building that was a military institution and is now culture and natural history. The transformation is the exhibit.
- The Mercado Central. The oldest market in the city, 1880. Covered. Narrow aisles. *Casados* at the lunch counters. The smell of *chorreado* coffee — drip coffee through a cloth sock. Everyone sitting together at long counters.
- Parque La Sabana. The former international airport, converted into a park. Football fields, cycling paths, the national stadium. Infrastructure that was one thing and became another.
- The Jade Museum. The largest collection of pre-Columbian jade in the world. The jade was currency, art, power, identity. It is now in a temperature-controlled building on Avenida Central. The past held carefully.
- The roads. San José's traffic is biblical and unremarked upon. The *Ticos* discuss it at length and accept it completely. *Pura vida* from inside a two-hour gridlock.
