# Piece: The Treaty of London 1867
## The document that made Luxembourg permanently neutral and smaller.

## Owner
- **Place:** Luxembourg City

## Place
Luxembourg City. The Bock promontory. The casemates beneath it.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and Luxembourg has no neutral ground.
The 1867 treaty made Luxembourg safe for contracts
by removing it from the military equation —
the condition that allowed it to become the EU's administrative home.

## Apparent
The Bock promontory, Luxembourg City.
The fortress: demolished 1867 under the treaty.
The casemates: 23 kilometres of tunnels carved into the rock.
Open to tourists. The fortress above them: gone.

## Yearbook
1815: the Congress of Vienna.
Luxembourg becomes a Grand Duchy,
personal union with the Netherlands,
Prussian garrison in the fortress.
A buffer state by committee.
1830: Belgian revolution. Luxembourg split.
The western part becomes Belgian.
The eastern part remains Luxembourg.
1867: the treaty. Fortress demolished.
Neutrality guaranteed.
The garrison leaves.
Luxembourg keeps the tunnels.
1914: Germany invades despite the neutrality.
1940: Germany invades again.
The neutrality does not hold against force.
It holds against everything else.
1944: liberation.
1948: Luxembourg joins NATO. Neutrality formally ended.
1952: founding member of the European Coal and Steel Community.
The first step toward the EU.
The neutral ground that couldn't stay neutral militarily
became the neutral ground for European integration.
The EU Court of Justice: Luxembourg.
The EU Commission: Brussels, but Luxembourg is where it processes.
The 1867 treaty is still the operating condition.
