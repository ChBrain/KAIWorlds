# Piece: The Althing
## The parliament that has been meeting since 930 AD.

## Owner
- **Place:** Reykjavík

## Place
Reykjavík. The Althing building. Also: Þingvellir National Park.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and Icelandic consensus loses its 1,100-year operating record.
The Althing is not a monument — it is a practice.
The small society has been managing its own visibility
through this mechanism since 930 AD.

## Apparent
Þingvellir: a rift valley where two tectonic plates meet.
Natural amphitheatre. Sound carries.
The Althing building, Reykjavík. 63 members.
The oldest continuous parliament in the world.

## Yearbook
930: the Althing established.
The Norse settlers needed a mechanism
for resolving disputes without a king.
They built one.
1262: Iceland submits to Norwegian rule.
The Althing continues as a court.
1662: absolute monarchy imposed.
The Althing suspended as a legislature.
Continues as a court.
1843: re-established as a consultative body.
1874: Iceland gets its own constitution.
The Althing restored as legislature.
1944: independence from Denmark.
The Althing has been here longer than Denmark has governed Iceland.
2008: the financial crisis.
Iceland's banks collapse. Largest per-capita banking failure in history.
The Althing governs the response.
The Icesave dispute. The referendums.
The recovery.
The small society visible to itself
decided, collectively, how to survive.
1,100 years of practice helped.
