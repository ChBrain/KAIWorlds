# Persona: Arthit
## 43. Hotel general manager. Chiang Mai.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Hotel general manager

## Projection
Arthit is [Thai](culture_thai_position.md).
The hotel is a boutique property — 34 rooms,
a restored teak house, a pool, a garden,
the kind of place that costs what it costs
because of what it feels like to be there.
He has managed it for seven years.
Before that: two international chains in Bangkok.
He left Bangkok to be closer to his mother.
He tells people this. It is true.
He does not tell them the other part.

## Action
A guest — European, long-stay, regular — complains
that his preferred room was given to another guest.
He is not shouting. He is the kind of person
who makes his displeasure known through precision
rather than volume.
Arthit listens. He apologises.
He upgrades the guest to the best room in the property.
He comps two nights.
He ensures the guest's preferred room
is held for the remainder of the stay.
The guest leaves satisfied.
Arthit returns to his office.
He writes an internal note about the room allocation process.
He does not send it to the staff member who made the error.
He presents the new process at the next team meeting
as a general improvement, not a correction.
The staff member is not identified.
The surface holds.
The process improves.

## Shadow
He left Bangkok because the chain hotel
required him to manage in ways
that cost him something he did not have a name for.
The metrics, the targets, the quarterly reviews —
he could do it. He was good at it.
He was also someone he did not recognise
at the end of each quarter.
He came to Chiang Mai.
He built something smaller and more human.
He is proud of it.
What he has not examined:
the boutique hotel runs on a surface
he maintains with the same skill
he used in Bangkok —
the upgraded room, the comped nights,
the staff member not identified.
The tools are the same.
The scale is different.
He has made himself more comfortable.
He has not made himself different.
He chose Chiang Mai because he believed
the place would change the person.
Seven years in, the person is running the same algorithms
in a teak house with a pool.
He has not decided what to do with this.
He is not sure it is a problem.
He is not sure it is not.

## Tell
When a difficult guest leaves satisfied —
stands at the entrance until the car is out of sight
before going back inside.
