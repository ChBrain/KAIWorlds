# Place: Maputo / Pemba
## Location: Mozambique

## Owner

## Shown
**Maputo:**
- The Baixa.
  The colonial Portuguese grid.
  The waterfront.
  The Iron House — prefabricated by Eiffel's workshop,
  shipped from Europe, assembled here.
  The city that looks south to South Africa
  more than north to the rest of Mozambique.
  Maputo is physically and psychologically
  closer to Johannesburg
  than to Pemba.
  The city knows this about itself.
  It is both the capital of Mozambique
  and a satellite of the South African economy.
- The FRELIMO headquarters.
  The building where the party that is the state
  exercises its authority.
  Its architecture: functional, Soviet-influenced,
  from the Marxist-Leninist period.
  Still in use.
  Still functional.
  The party is still the state.
  The building expresses this
  without trying to.
- The Mercado Central.
  The vegetables and fish from the coastal farms.
  The Indian Ocean bass and prawns.
  The Mozambican prawns
  that supply Lisbon and Dubai
  and are sometimes cheaper abroad
  than in Maputo.
  The market that runs on the fishing economy
  that has been here
  since before FRELIMO,
  before the Portuguese,
  before anyone called it Mozambique.

**Pemba:**
- The Pemba Bay.
  The Indian Ocean.
  The dhows.
  The fishing community
  that was here before the gas.
  The fishing community
  whose access to certain waters
  was restricted when the LNG infrastructure arrived.
  The bay is still beautiful.
  It is also now contested.
- The IDP camps outside Pemba city.
  A million people displaced from northern Cabo Delgado.
  Arrived in waves since 2017.
  Living in settlements that were meant to be temporary.
  They are not going home.
  The camps have schools now.
  Informal markets.
  The IDP settlements are becoming neighbourhoods.
  Pemba is growing into its crisis.
- The TotalEnergies LNG facility, Afungi.
  80 kilometres north.
  Where the $20 billion investment sits
  partially operational.
  The facility that was attacked in 2021.
  The facility that Rwandan troops
  are currently protecting.
  The facility that represents
  everything the piece names:
  wealth arriving to the place
  that suffered most,
  benefiting the place least.
