# Persona: Bernardo
## 27. *Kuduro* musician and music producer. Luanda musseques.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
*Kuduro* musician and music producer

## Projection
Bernardo is [Angolan](culture_angolan_position.md).
He was born in the musseques of Luanda —
Cazenga, the neighbourhood
where *kuduro* developed in the 1980s
from the MPLA-era music scene
and the economic chaos
of the war's last decade.
His uncle was a *kuduro* DJ
in the 1990s.
He produces music in a home studio
built from secondhand equipment
and remixed software.
He has released four mixtapes.
He has 80,000 followers on social media.
He earns from bookings —
clubs, weddings, private events —
and from the small percentages
that come through a Lisbon-based distributor
who handles African music
on streaming platforms.
The percentages are small.
The bookings are inconsistent.
He supplements with sound engineering
for other producers in his neighbourhood.
He is building something.
He knows the building will take time.

## Action
A European fashion brand
contacts him through his distributor.
They want to license one of his tracks
for an advertising campaign.
The offer: a flat fee, one-time use,
no royalties.
The amount: enough for two months' expenses.
He asks: what exposure?
The distributor says: a campaign across
France, Germany, Italy.
Hundreds of millions of impressions.
He asks: can we structure it as a license
with a performance royalty
rather than a flat fee?
If the campaign runs longer or expands,
I would receive something additional.
The distributor consults the brand.
The brand says: our standard is flat fee.
He says: I understand that.
I am asking for an exception
because the impressions you're projecting
suggest significant value for the campaign.
The brand declines the exception.
He asks: can the royalty window be 12 months
rather than unlimited?
After 12 months, if the campaign continues,
we renegotiate.
The brand accepts a 12-month window.
He accepts the deal.

## Shadow
He believes his music's global reach —
the fashion brand found him,
which means *kuduro* from Cazenga
has arrived somewhere —
is the evidence that he has leverage
he is only beginning to use correctly.
That the 12-month window is better
than no window.
That the distributor relationship
is both his access and his constraint —
because the distributor's standard terms
are designed for the scale of producers
larger than him.
What he cannot see:
the 12-month renegotiation window
he negotiated will expire
and the brand will have moved on.
His leverage was in the moment of licensing —
when they needed the track.
At month 12, they will not need it.
The window that felt like progress
is a window that closes
at the moment he can't reopen it.
He could have asked for more —
an upfront advance against royalties,
a co-promotion clause —
at the moment of the first negotiation.
He didn't know to ask then.
He knows now.
He licenses the next track differently.

## Tell
When listening to music he might sample —
doesn't look at the screen.
Eyes closed.
Listening only.
The look comes after.
The sound has to decide first.
