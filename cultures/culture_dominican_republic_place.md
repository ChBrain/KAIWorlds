# Place: Santo Domingo
## Location: Dominican Republic

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Shown
- The Zona Colonial. The oldest European city in the Americas, continuously inhabited. Cobblestone streets, sixteenth-century cathedral, the first university in the Western Hemisphere. The weight of first contact here is architectural — you can walk it.
- The Malecón. The coastal avenue running along the Caribbean. Where people gather in the evenings. Where merengue plays from car windows. Where the city breathes.
- The Fortaleza Ozama. The oldest European military fortress in the Americas. It has been used as a prison, a garrison, a monument. It has been all three at once.
- El Faro a Colón — the Columbus Lighthouse. Built 1992, five hundred years after the landing. Controversial at construction: 180 families displaced, frequent power cuts meant the rotating beam regularly went dark while the surrounding barrios had no electricity. The lighthouse was lit; the neighbourhoods were not.
- The Jardín Botánico. Where families go on Sundays. The largest botanical garden in the Caribbean. Quiet, enormous, the city outside audible but distant.
