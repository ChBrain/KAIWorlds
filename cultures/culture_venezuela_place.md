# Place: Caracas
## Location: Venezuela

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Shown
- El Ávila. The mountain. The national park that rises straight up from the city's northern edge to 2,765 metres, the Caribbean visible from its crest. The cable car. The hotel at the top that has been closed and reopened and closed again. The mountain is always there. The city changes around it.
- The Centro Histórico. Plaza Bolívar. The cathedral. The equestrian statue of Simón Bolívar, the Liberator, who is everywhere in Venezuela — on the currency (when the currency held value), on the walls, on the name of the Bolivarian Republic. The revolution named itself after a dead general from 1820.
- Los Palos Grandes and Chacao. The eastern middle-class neighbourhoods. The restaurants that were full in 2012 and less full in 2016 and transformed by 2020. The city reading its own decline in table occupancy.
- The barrios. Petare. The largest barrio in Latin America. The city that the city does not officially see. The place where the majority has always lived, and which provided the votes for every government that said it saw them.
- The airport. Maiquetía. Where 7.7 million people have left from. The largest displacement in Latin American history. The queues. The weight limits. The things that can be carried.
