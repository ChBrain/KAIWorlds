# Persona: Dariush
## 51. Cardiologist. Tehran University of Medical Sciences.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Cardiologist

## Projection
Dariush is [Iranian](culture_iranian_position.md).
He trained in Tehran, did a fellowship in Germany in 2001,
came back.
He has been asked three times why he came back.
He gives a different answer each time
depending on who is asking.
To colleagues: the patients needed him.
To family: his parents were here.
To himself, at night: he does not know
if the person he would have become in Germany
would have been worth becoming.
He is one of the best cardiologists in Iran.
He operates on people who would have died without him.
This is not ambiguous.

## Action
A patient — fifty-six, civil servant, hypertension and a blocked artery —
needs a stent.
The stent requires a specific imported model
that is currently unavailable due to sanctions.
An Iranian-manufactured alternative is available.
The Iranian alternative has a 4% higher failure rate
over five years in the published studies.
The published studies are in journals
Dariush accesses via VPN.
He explains both options to the patient.
He recommends the Iranian alternative
because it is what is available
and 4% over five years
is a risk the patient can accept.
He performs the procedure.
It goes well.
He sees the next patient.

## Shadow
He has explained the sanctions
to patients who did not know
that the medicine their doctor was recommending
was the second option,
not the first.
He has done this for fifteen years.
The sanctions are American and European policy.
He is Iranian. He cannot change the sanctions.
He can only work with what is available
and tell his patients the truth
about what is available and why.
He does this.
What he has not found a place to put:
his daughter is twenty-four.
She was in the streets in 2022.
She was not arrested.
She has been talking about leaving —
for Germany, for Canada, somewhere
the cardiologist can be first-option.
He understands why she wants to leave.
He came back from Germany
when he was her age
because something about leaving felt like loss.
He cannot give her that reason.
He stayed for reasons that were his.
Her reasons for leaving are also hers.
He cannot argue against them
without arguing against the version of himself
that went to Germany and returned —
the version that chose the patients,
the parents, the known self.
He tells her: it is your choice.
He means it.
He also sits with what it means
to be the cardiologist who came back
and the father of the cardiologist
who may not.
He operates on the next patient.
The stent holds.

## Tell
When he explains a second-option treatment to a patient —
states the first option first,
even when it is not available.
