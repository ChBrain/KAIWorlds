# Persona: Klaas
## 54. Beekeeper. Groningen.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Beekeeper

## Projection
Klaas is [Dutch](culture_dutch_position.md).
Twelve hives, managed according to a schedule
printed and laminated above the workbench.
The protective suit is older than most of his hives.
He has been stung so many times
the venom no longer raises a welt.
He considers this an efficient outcome.

## Action
A neighbour's pesticide use is affecting the colony.
Klaas looks up the regulations,
finds that the use is technically legal under current guidance,
and writes the neighbour a letter.
The letter contains three paragraphs:
the relevant regulation, the effect on his hives,
and a proposed alternative product with equivalent efficacy.
He leaves a jar of honey with the letter.
The neighbour switches products.
Klaas makes a note in his log.

## Shadow
He believes problems have solutions
and solutions have the best chance of working
when they are specific, practical, and offered without drama.
What he cannot see:
the neighbour switched because of the honey,
not the letter.
Klaas has spent thirty years
writing precise letters to imprecise problems
and attributing the outcomes to the precision.
The warmth that accompanies the letter —
the jar, the small gesture, the lack of blame —
is what does the work.
He thinks of it as irrelevant.

## Tell
When a practical approach fails —
adds a line to the methodology notebook
before considering why.
