# Position: Bangladeshi
## The country that was born in blood, survived flood, and kept moving.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Liberation War](culture_bangladesh_piece_liberation.md).
The *mati* — the soil, the delta,
the land that is also the problem.
Bangladesh is the Ganges-Brahmaputra-Meghna delta —
the largest delta in the world.
The land is extraordinarily fertile.
It is also underwater several times per decade.
The position was formed by this:
the knowledge that what you build
may be taken by the river
and that you build again anyway.
The *adda* — the conversation, the gathering,
the hours of talk without fixed purpose —
is the social infrastructure of a society
that thinks together before it acts.
The hierarchy is present: age, class, the patron,
the political party that is also the network —
the Awami League and the BNP
have divided the political space since independence
like two families who own the same house
and cannot agree on the rooms.
The garment industry employs four million people,
mostly women.
The country that was called a basket case in 1971
is now a lower-middle-income economy
growing at 6% per year.
The position knows what it has built.
It also knows how much of it
is held together by the women
on the factory floor.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room is already moving.

## Orders
Keep moving.
The position does not permit stillness —
the river does not permit it,
the density does not permit it,
the 170 million people on 147,000 square kilometres
do not permit it.
The *jugaad* — the improvised solution —
is here too, in the Bangladeshi form:
the NGO sector that built
the health and education infrastructure
the state could not,
the microfinance model that Grameen Bank
gave to the world,
the innovation born from the constraint
of having no margin.

## Loses
The recognition.
They were certain the achievement would be seen —
that a country that survived partition, liberation war,
famine, cyclone, flood, and military coups
and still grew its economy and reduced its poverty
faster than most of its neighbours
would be recognised for what it had done.
The room outside often does not see it.
Bangladesh is the country of floods and garment factory fires
and political dysfunction.
The position is tired of the summary.
It has more than the summary.
It does not always find a room
that has time for more than the summary.

## Drives
On the loss of recognition —
builds the next thing.
The climate adaptation infrastructure.
The floating schools.
The cyclone shelters that saved hundreds of thousands
in storms that killed tens of thousands in 1970.
The position has learned that recognition
follows the build, not the other way.
It builds.
It waits for the room to catch up.
The room is catching up, slowly.
The position is already on the next thing.
