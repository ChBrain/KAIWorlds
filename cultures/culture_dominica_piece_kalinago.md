## Load Bearing
Remove the Kalinago Territory and Dominica is the most forested, most volcanic island
in the Eastern Caribbean — the "Nature Isle" — with a French Creole cultural layer,
a strong labour history, and exceptional ecotourism.
With it: Dominica is the island where the last living indigenous Caribbean community
has maintained continuous presence since before the Europeans arrived —
the only place in the Caribbean where the word "indigenous" is not historical but present tense.
The "last" is not passive. The "last" has a Chief.
The "last" weaves baskets. The "last" grows cassava. The "last" is deciding what comes next.
Without the piece: no specific load on the Dominican position's instruction to "remain and pass it on."
With it: the remaining and passing on is the work of a community
that has been remaining for 500 years of attempted erasure.

## Apparent
Salybia. The administrative centre of the Kalinago Territory.
The Atlantic coast, northeast Dominica. Eight hamlets.
Approximately 3,000 people. 3,700 acres established by British colonial authorities 1903.
The Kalinago Barana Auté: the heritage village by the sea, built in traditional construction styles.
The larouman baskets at the craft shop. The canoe on the beach.
The houses: some traditional, most concrete. The gardens: cassava, dasheen, yam.
The larouman reed growing in the river banks.
The Kalinago language: considered extinct by linguists.
The Kalinago cultural practices: actively maintained.
Hurricane Maria (September 18, 2017): the most powerful storm ever to hit Dominica.
The Territory directly in the path. Nearly every home damaged. No deaths.
Chief Lorenzo Sanford — 26 years old, youngest Chief in Kalinago history —
walks door to door the morning after. He runs for Chief. He wins.
The Territory: still there. The "last" is still here.
