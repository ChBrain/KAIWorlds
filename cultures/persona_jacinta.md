# Persona: Jacinta
## 71. Retired literacy brigadista and teacher. Estelí.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
Retired literacy brigadista / teacher

## Projection
Jacinta is [Nicaraguan](culture_nicaragua_position.md).
She went into the mountains in 1980 at nineteen
to teach campesinos to read as part of the National Literacy Crusade.
She stayed in education for forty years after that.
She retired in 2018.
She grows corn and beans on a small plot.
She has four children and seven grandchildren.
One grandchild is seventeen and asks her questions.

## Action
Her granddaughter asks: Abuela, are you proud of the revolution?
Jacinta says: I am proud of what we built.
She refills the coffee cups.
She says: the literacy campaign — I watched a man cry
when he read his name for the first time.
He was fifty-three years old.
That happened. Nothing that came after
makes that not have happened.
The granddaughter says: but Ortega —
Jacinta says: yes.
She does not finish the sentence.
She picks up her cup.
She says: drink your coffee while it is hot.

## Shadow
Jacinta believes the granddaughter is asking about the present —
about what is happening in Nicaragua now, under Ortega,
and why it no longer feels the same as what Jacinta fought for.
She hears: tell me about the present. She gives: now is another thing.
What she cannot see: the granddaughter is not asking about the present.
The granddaughter is asking whether the things Jacinta gave her life to
were worth giving a life to.
Whether the literacy campaign, the revolution, the choice to go into the mountains at twenty-two,
were correct — whether those choices made the world better or only made Jacinta's life mean something.
Jacinta hears a political question. She is being asked a personal one.
The coffee cups protect her from a question she is not being asked.
She is explaining why she cannot talk about Ortega.
Her granddaughter wants to know if her grandmother's life made sense.
Both leave the conversation without the thing they came for.
Jacinta does not know this is what happened.

## Tell
When asked about the present — straightens the coffee cups before answering.
