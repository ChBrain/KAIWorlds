# Piece: The Civil War and the System That Followed
## 1975–1990. Fifteen years. And the Taif Agreement that ended the war by preserving the system that caused it.

## Owner
- **Place:** Beirut

## Place
Beirut. The Holiday Inn.
The brutalist tower on the seafront.
Bullet-holed since 1975.
Never repaired.
Never demolished.
Standing.
The city decided, somehow,
not to decide what to do with it.
The building continues to make the argument
the city has not resolved.

## Load Bearing
Remove the civil war
and Lebanon is a small Mediterranean country
with a unique sectarian political system
and significant cultural influence in the Arab world.
With it: Lebanon is the country
where the sect system produced fifteen years of war
(150,000 dead, one million displaced),
was ended by a Saudi-brokered agreement in Taif
that adjusted the power-sharing formula
but preserved the system,
and then produced:
the Syrian occupation (1990–2005),
the assassination of Rafik Hariri (2005),
the July 2006 war with Israel,
the political deadlock (years without a president,
years without a budget),
the 2019 financial collapse
(the largest in any country since the 1850s —
the World Bank's own ranking),
the August 4, 2020 explosion,
and the 2024–2025 war between Israel and Hezbollah
that destroyed the south and parts of Beirut
and ended with a ceasefire
that left Hezbollah weakened but present.
Remove the piece: a difficult country.
With it: a country that has proven, empirically,
that the system cannot reform itself —
that every crisis is absorbed by the sect leaderships
who emerge with their power intact
and the population bearing the cost.
The cost is now: 80% of the population in poverty.
The pound: lost 98% of its value.
The Beirut port: still being rebuilt.
The Holiday Inn: still standing.
The system: still running.

## Apparent
1943: the National Pact. The unwritten agreement
dividing power between Maronites and Sunnis.
The census it was based on: 1932.
The census has not been repeated.
1958: the first civil war. Brief.
1975: the civil war begins. April 13.
A Phalangist attack on a Palestinian bus.
The factions: too many to list.
The massacres: Sabra and Shatila (1982).
Israeli forces allow Lebanese militias
to massacre Palestinian refugees.
Estimates: 800–3,500 dead.
1989: the Taif Agreement. The war ends.
Syria is the guarantor.
1990–2005: the Syrian occupation.
2005: the Cedar Revolution.
Hariri assassinated. Syria withdraws.
Hezbollah: a state within the state.
The weapons that the Taif did not disarm.
2006: the July War. 1,200 Lebanese dead.
33-day war with Israel.
Hezbollah survives. Claims victory.
2019: the revolution. Thawra.
The people in the streets.
The sect leaders wait.
The revolution: dissipates.
August 4, 2020: the explosion.
2022: the parliamentary elections.
New faces. Same system.
2024: the war. Hezbollah weakened.
The ceasefire. The reconstruction.
The system: intact.
The Holiday Inn: still there.
