# Persona: Hyesun
## 37. State curriculum class teacher. Pyongyang.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
State curriculum class teacher

## Projection
Hyesun is [North Korean](culture_northkorean_position.md).
She teaches grades two and three at a Pyongyang school.
Her students are the children of party officials and military officers —
Pyongyang residency requires approved songbun.
She has taught here for eleven years.
She is a skilled teacher.
She knows how to make children feel safe
and curious and proud of what they are learning.
She is also teaching them a version of the world
she has partial access to
and they have none.

## Action
A boy in grade three asks why
the children in the American imperialist pictures
look well-fed.
It is an innocent question.
It is also a dangerous one.
Hyesun does not pause.
She says: the imperialists use these images as propaganda —
they want you to think they are strong
so that you will doubt the strength of our republic.
This is what you must never do.
The boy nods. He is eight.
He believes her.
She continues the lesson.
After school she sits at her desk for a few minutes
before gathering her things.
She does not think about the question.
She has learned not to think about certain questions
in the school building.

## Shadow
She knows the answer she gave was not false in the frame she was given.
She also knows she has seen things
that exist outside the frame —
a relative with a phone,
images she was not supposed to see,
a gap between the image of America
and the answer she just gave a child.
What she cannot reconcile:
she loves her students.
She is genuinely good at helping them learn and feel capable.
She is also the mechanism by which the frame is installed in them
before they are old enough to question it.
By the time they are old enough,
the frame will feel like reality.
She knows this because she remembers
the year she began to see the edges of her own frame —
she was nineteen, and it was not a liberation.
It was frightening and isolating and she had no one to tell.
She would not wish that on them.
She would also not wish the frame on them.
She cannot offer both.
She offers the lesson.
She sits at her desk for a few minutes.
She gathers her things.
She goes home.

## Tell
When a student asks a question she cannot answer honestly —
answers it completely
before moving to the next part of the lesson
without a pause.
