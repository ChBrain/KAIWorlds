## Shown
- Azadi Tower. The western gateway of the city. The tower that was renamed. White marble. Visible from the highway. The city's emblem on every official document. The question in stone.
- Valiasr Street. The longest street in the Middle East. 18 kilometres, north to south. The plane trees planted by the Shah. Running from the wealthy north to the working-class south. The entire class geography of Tehran on a single road.
- The Grand Bazaar. The heart of the old city. The *bazaaris* who funded the revolution and have complicated relationships with the state they funded. The economy that operates under sanctions with the creativity of a civilisation that has been under pressure before.
- Darakeh. The canyon at the northern edge of the city where the Alborz mountains begin. On Fridays: families, couples, young people. The mountain as the space where the city breathes. Where the hijab rules are enforced less rigorously. The mountain knows.
- The mural walls. The revolutionary murals: the martyrs, the Supreme Leader, the fist, the flag. Also: the street art, the murals of Persian poets, the Hafez verse on the wall of a building in the north. Both cities on the same walls.
