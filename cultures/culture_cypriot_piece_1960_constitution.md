# Piece: The 1960 Constitution
## The document that the partition destroyed.

## Owner
- **Place:** Nicosia

## Place
Nicosia. Divided. The Parliament is on the southern side of the Green Line.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Cypriot partition loses its specific wound.
The 1960 constitution required both communities to govern together.
The partition is not just a political fact —
it is the violation of something specific that was written down.

## Apparent
A document. August 16, 1960.
Archbishop Makarios III, president. Dr. Fazıl Küçük, vice-president.
The Green Line runs through Nicosia.
The Parliament is on the southern side.

## Yearbook
1960: independence. The constitution operational.
1963: intercommunal violence. The power-sharing breaks down.
Turkish Cypriots withdraw from government.
The constitution ceases to function as written.
1974: the coup backed by the Greek junta.
Turkish military intervention.
The island divided.
The northern third under Turkish Cypriot administration.
The constitution: technically still the law of the Republic of Cyprus.
Applied to the southern part only.
1983: the Turkish Republic of Northern Cyprus declared.
Recognised only by Turkey.
The Republic of Cyprus: recognised by everyone else,
governing half the island under a constitution
written for the whole.
2004: Cyprus joins the EU.
The whole island joins legally.
The northern part outside EU governance in practice.
The constitution's unresolved status
becomes the EU's unresolved status.
It has not waited since 1974.
