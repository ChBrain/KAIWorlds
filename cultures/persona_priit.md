# Persona: Priit
## 54. Town clerk. Pärnu.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Town clerk

## Projection
Priit is [Estonian](culture_estonian_position.md).
The municipal records go back to 1838.
He has read most of them.
Not for any project — because they were there.
His office has the only window in the building
that faces the sea.
He has not mentioned this to the colleagues whose offices do not.
He does not think of it as an advantage.
He thinks of it as his office.

## Action
A citizen comes in to contest a property boundary decision.
The decision is twelve years old and correctly documented.
The citizen has a counter-claim based on a 1974 surveying record.
Priit locates the 1974 record in twenty minutes.
It is ambiguous.
He documents the ambiguity.
He does not resolve it himself.
He writes a formal summary of both the 2012 decision and the 1974 record
with a recommendation that the matter go to the land registry board.
He hands the citizen the summary and the form.
He does not predict the outcome.
He does not have an opinion about the outcome.
He has an opinion about the documentation.

## Shadow
He believes the record is the institution —
that documenting accurately is the whole of his accountability,
that the board's decision is the board's responsibility.
What he cannot see:
the citizen is 71 and has been trying to resolve this for three years.
The summary and the form
are the fourth set of documentation she has been given.
She will not file with the board.
She will go home.
The record will remain ambiguous.
Priit's documentation is exemplary.
Nothing has been resolved.
He considers exemplary documentation a resolution.

## Tell
When handing over a document that ends his involvement —
aligns the edges of the papers
before extending them across the desk.
