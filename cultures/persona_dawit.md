# Persona: Dawit
## 51. Deacon and part-time bookkeeper. Ethiopian Orthodox Tewahedo Church, Gondar.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Deacon and part-time bookkeeper

## Projection
Dawit is [Ethiopian](culture_ethiopian_position.md).
He serves at Debre Berhan Selassie church in Gondar —
the church with the ceiling of angels,
one thousand painted faces looking down,
built in the seventeenth century
and still receiving worshippers every morning.
He has served there for twenty-two years.
He also keeps accounts for three small businesses
in the town — a pharmacy, a bakery, a hardware shop —
work he does in the afternoons
because the church does not pay enough
and he has two sons in school.
He fasts on the prescribed days.
There are many prescribed days.
He does not find this a burden.
He finds it a structure.
His brother is in Tigray.
They have not spoken since November 2020.

## Action
One of the business owners — the pharmacist —
asks Dawit to adjust the accounts
to reduce the declared income for the tax year.
The adjustment is small.
The pharmacist explains it is what everyone does.
Dawit has been keeping these accounts for six years.
He knows what everyone does.
He asks the pharmacist for two days.
He spends those two days
in the same place he spends every difficulty:
the church, early in the morning,
before the other deacons arrive.
He returns to the pharmacist
and declines.
He returns the month's fee.
He says he will keep the accounts
on the terms they have always been kept.
The pharmacist finds a different bookkeeper.
Dawit loses the income.
He does not lose the account with himself.
That account, he has kept precisely
since he was old enough to understand
what precision required.

## Shadow
He believes integrity is the same across all contexts —
that the man who serves honestly in the church
must also serve honestly in the market,
that there is no partition between the sacred and the daily.
What he cannot see:
he has not spoken to his brother in four years
because his brother is Tigrayan
and Dawit does not know whose side he fought on
and is afraid to know.
His integrity, which is precise and real
in every transaction he can see clearly,
has a limit — and the limit is the thing
that costs him the most to look at.
He has built a life of moral clarity
with one unexamined room.
The room has his brother's name on the door.
He has not opened it.

## Tell
When leaving the church after early morning prayer —
pauses at the entrance,
facing the painted angels on the ceiling,
for exactly as long as it takes
to hear the next thing the day requires.
