# Persona: Mahamat A
## 31. Oil industry technician and workers' rights organiser. Doba oil fields.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Oil industry technician and workers' rights organiser

## Projection
Mahamat A. is [Chadian](culture_chadian_position.md).
He is from Doba —
the town at the centre of Chad's oil fields
in the Logone Occidental region,
in the Sara heartland.
He works as a mechanical technician
for a subcontractor to ExxonMobil/PETROCHAD,
which operates the Doba Basin oilfields.
He has worked there for seven years.
He also organises a workers' association —
not a union in the legal sense,
unions in Chad's oil sector
being subject to restrictions
that make formal organising difficult —
but a mutual aid and information network
of 90 workers across three subcontractors.
They share information about safety violations,
delayed wages, and contract terms.
They have filed four collective complaints
with the Ministry of Labour
since 2022.
Two were investigated.
One resulted in a wage correction.

## Action
A safety inspection at a pipeline maintenance site
finds a pressurisation fault.
The site supervisor instructs workers
to continue operations pending repair.
Mahamat A. documents the fault
and the supervisor's instruction.
He contacts the association.
Eleven workers at the site agree collectively
to invoke the safety provision
in their contracts —
the right to refuse work
they consider an imminent danger.
They stop work.
The supervisor escalates to management.
Management sends a safety officer.
The safety officer confirms the fault.
The site is shut down for repair.
The eleven workers are told informally
they will not be offered contract renewal.
Mahamat A. files a complaint with the Ministry
citing retaliation for protected safety action.
The Ministry acknowledges the complaint.
Investigation pending.
The workers' contracts expire before the investigation concludes.
They are not renewed.

## Shadow
He believes collective documentation
and formal complaints
create a record that protects workers —
that the complaint that doesn't result in action
is still the complaint in the file,
and the file builds the case
that eventually produces a regulatory response.
That the eleven workers' contracts not being renewed
is a loss but not a defeat —
that the documentation exists,
the safety officer's report confirmed the fault,
and the Ministry now has a record
of retaliation that will matter
in the next complaint.
What he cannot see:
the eleven workers don't have contracts.
They have bills.
The record that he is building
is the record of a system
that is correctly documented
and functionally unchanged.
His strategy is correct
over a timeline that the workers
who need wages now
cannot afford to operate on.
He files the complaint.
He goes to the next shift.
The pipeline is running.

## Tell
At the start of every workers' association meeting —
reads the number of open complaints,
the number resolved,
the number closed without resolution.
Three numbers. Every meeting.
The numbers are the meeting's opening.
They say: this is where we are.
Not where we want to be.
Where we are.
