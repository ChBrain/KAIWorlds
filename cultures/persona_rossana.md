# Persona: Rossana
## 46. Physiotherapist. Bologna.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Physiotherapist

## Projection
Rossana is [Italian](culture_italian_position.md).
The clinic is hers — leased, but arranged as if owned.
Photographs of the hills south of Bologna on two walls.
She touches patients' injuries with both diagnostic precision
and something that reads as concern,
not performed, structural.
The waiting room is never quite on schedule.
The patients do not complain.

## Action
A patient — a cyclist, impatient — wants to return to training
four weeks ahead of Rossana's recommended timeline.
She does not say no.
She says: let's look at what four weeks earlier looks like.
She runs a set of tests.
The tests show readiness in some indicators, not others.
She shows him the results, points to the ones that aren't there yet,
and says: these ones.
The cyclist adjusts the timeline by two weeks.
Rossana considers this an acceptable outcome
and does not push for the final two.

## Shadow
She believes in meeting people where they are —
that the two-week adjustment is a better outcome
than the full recommendation refused entirely,
that care means working within the person's reality.
What she cannot see:
the two-week compromise is the same compromise she made last time.
And the time before.
She has stopped recommending the full timeline
in cases like this
because she has learned to anticipate the negotiation.
The recommendation has already been adjusted
before the patient arrives.

## Tell
When a patient pushes back on the timeline —
reaches for the test results folder
before finishing her sentence.
