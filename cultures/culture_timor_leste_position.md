# Position: Timorese
## The country that waited 24 years to exist — and is still learning what existing means.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Resistance and Independence](culture_timor_leste_piece_resistance.md).
The *lisan* — the customary law, the ancestral obligation.
The *uma lulik* — the sacred house, the ancestral origin point
that every Timorese family traces back to.
The identity is not the state.
The identity is older than the state by centuries.
The Portuguese came. The identity remained.
The Indonesians came. The identity remained.
The UN came. The identity remained.
The state arrived in 2002 and the identity
looked at it with the patience
of something that has outlasted everything before.
The Catholic faith runs alongside the *lisan* —
not in opposition, in coexistence.
The same person attends mass
and maintains the sacred house
and does not experience contradiction.
The hierarchy is present: the elders, the *liurai* — the traditional rulers —
the church, the state.
The resistance generation holds authority
that the constitution does not fully account for.
Xanana Gusmão does not need a title to be the most powerful person in the room.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room is already deciding
which authority it is in.

## Orders
Endure and remember.
The position was formed by a resistance
that lasted twenty-four years —
1975 to 1999 —
and lost a third of the population.
The memory is not abstract.
It is in the generation that survived
and is now the generation in power
and is transmitting to a generation
that was born after independence
and knows the resistance as history
rather than as the air they breathed.
The position is watching the transmission carefully.
The resistance generation does not fully trust
that what it paid for will be maintained
by people who did not pay for it.

## Loses
The coherence.
They were certain that independence — achieved, finally —
would produce the country they had been fighting for.
The country that arrived is complicated:
two official languages (Tetum and Portuguese)
that most Timorese speak to varying degrees,
plus Indonesian (widely spoken, not official),
plus local languages (fifteen or more).
The state institutions: built largely from scratch,
with UN help, by people who had been in the jungle or in exile.
The oil revenue from the Timor Sea:
significant, finite, not yet transformed into
the development the resistance imagined.
The position carries the gap between what was won
and what was built with the winning.

## Drives
On the loss of coherence —
returns to the *uma lulik*.
When the state fails to cohere,
the ancestral house holds.
The community holds.
The obligation to the dead holds.
The position does not abandon the state.
It holds the state and the sacred house simultaneously
and navigates between them
as it has always navigated between
the systems that came from outside
and the system that was always there.
