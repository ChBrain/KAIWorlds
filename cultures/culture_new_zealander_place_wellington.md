## Shown
- Te Papa Tongarewa. Wellington.
  The national museum.
  The Treaty of Waitangi on display —
  the original document.
  The Māori collection.
  The natural history.
  The museum that tries to hold
  both the *pounamu* (greenstone)
  and the colonial history
  in the same building
  and largely succeeds.
  The building that sits on the waterfront
  between the parliament and the harbour —
  between the state and the sea.
- The Beehive. Wellington.
  The executive wing of the New Zealand parliament.
  The circular building.
  The government that changes
  its view on the Treaty
  with each election.
  The permanence of the building
  against the impermanence of the position.
- Waitangi Treaty Grounds. Bay of Islands.
  The lawn where the Treaty was signed.
  The *wharenui* (meeting house).
  The flagpoles: the New Zealand flag,
  the Tino Rangatiratanga flag —
  the Māori sovereignty flag.
  Both flying on Waitangi Day.
  The argument embodied in the flagpoles.
  The ground where the argument has its annual performance.
  The protesters at the water's edge.
  The PM at the microphone.
  The haka that begins the ceremony.
  The haka that ends it.
- The Southern Alps. The South Island.
  The mountains that run the length of the island.
  The fiords. The glaciers.
  The landscape that is genuinely extraordinary
  and that the Māori named in full —
  every peak, every river, every valley
  with a name that carries a story.
  The names being restored to the maps
  as the settlements proceed.
  The land being given back its own language.

## Withheld
- The gap.
  Māori incarceration rates: over 50% of the prison population,
  16% of the general population.
  Māori children in state care: over-represented.
  The health gap. The education gap.
  The settlements that have returned land
  and have not yet closed the gap.
  The room shows Te Papa and the mountains.
  The gap is not hidden — it is measured and published —
  but the room does not lead with it.
  The room leads with the Treaty and the landscape.
  The gap is the distance between those two things.
