# Piece: The Kabaka Question
## The kingdom within the country — and the question every Ugandan government has tried and failed to answer.

## Owner
- **Place:** [Kampala](culture_ugandan_place_kampala.md)

## Place
Mengo. The hill in Kampala
where the Lubiri — the Kabaka's palace — stands.
The palace that Idi Amin used as a torture chamber.
The palace restored to the kingdom in 1993.
The palace that is now also a museum
where the torture chambers are visible.
The same building. Different functions.
Still the Kabaka's palace.
Still generating loyalty.

## Load Bearing
Remove the Kabaka question
and Uganda is a multi-ethnic East African state
negotiating the relationship between central government
and regional/ethnic loyalties.
With it: Uganda is a country whose central political problem —
how to accommodate the most powerful pre-colonial kingdom
within a modern centralised state —
has never been resolved,
and whose periodic crises can almost all be traced
to the point where the Buganda question
was either mishandled or ignored.
The name Uganda is Buganda — this is the starting point.
The British built the protectorate around Buganda.
They used Baganda as colonial administrators
across the wider territory.
This created resentment that Independence inherited.
Obote abolished the kingdoms in 1967
to break Buganda's power.
Amin abolished everything.
Museveni restored the kingdoms in 1993
to secure Buganda's political support.
The restoration was tactical.
The kingdom's loyalty has remained conditional.
Remove the piece: a country managing diversity.
With it: a country that cannot be governed
without addressing Buganda —
and in which every government
that has tried to govern without addressing Buganda
has eventually discovered this
at significant cost.

## Apparent
Pre-colonial Buganda: the largest, most sophisticated
kingdom in the Great Lakes region.
The Kabaka governed through a system of chiefs.
The Lukiiko — parliament — functioned.
The Ganda people were literate in Arabic before the British.
1894: the Uganda Protectorate established.
Buganda signs the 1900 Agreement — extraordinary autonomy,
unique in British colonial Africa.
The Baganda are used as colonial administrators.
This creates a Buganda-centric political order
that the rest of the country resents.
1962: independence. A compromise:
Uganda as a constitutional monarchy,
Kabaka Mutesa II as ceremonial president.
1966: Obote dissolves the constitution.
Sends army to storm the Lubiri.
The Kabaka escapes. Dies in exile in London, 1969.
The kingdoms abolished.
1971: Amin overthrows Obote.
1979: Amin overthrown by Tanzanian forces.
The Kagera war.
1980s: civil war. Museveni's NRA fighting from Buganda.
Baganda fighters central to the NRM victory.
1986: Museveni takes Kampala.
1993: kingdoms restored.
Ronald Mutebi crowned Kabaka.
The Lubiri returned to the kingdom.
2009: riots in Kampala over Museveni's government
blocking the Kabaka from visiting the Kayunga district.
Three days of violence. At least three dead.
The question is still not resolved.
It has not been resolved for sixty years.
It will not be resolved by the next government either.
It will have to be lived with.
It is being lived with.
