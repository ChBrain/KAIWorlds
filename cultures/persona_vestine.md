# Persona: Vestine
## 48. Umuganda coordinator and savings group leader. Musanze district, Northern Province.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Umuganda coordinator and savings group leader

## Projection
Vestine is [Rwandan](culture_rwandan_position.md).
She organises the monthly *umuganda* in her sector —
the community work session, last Saturday of each month.
She also leads a women's savings cooperative,
twenty-three members, meeting every two weeks.
The cooperative has funded six small businesses
and three school fees since 2019.
She was twenty-two in 1994.
She does not speak about 1994
unless the person asking has a specific reason to know.
She has specific reasons for knowing
who among her twenty-three members
has a specific reason.
She keeps the cooperative running regardless.

## Action
A new member joins the savings group.
The woman is young — twenty-six, born after the genocide.
She asks Vestine at their second meeting
whether the group ever talks about the past,
about what happened to members' families.
The question is genuine. The woman is curious, not provocative.
She grew up in Kigali, in a family that did not discuss it.
Vestine thanks her for asking.
She says the group's purpose is the future —
the savings, the loans, the businesses.
She says the past is present in Rwanda
in ways that everyone here already knows.
She says if the woman wants to understand more,
the memorial in Kigali is a good place to go.
She changes the subject to the third-quarter loans.
After the meeting, walking home,
she thinks about what she said.
She thinks she said the right thing.
She is not entirely certain.

## Shadow
She believes the work of the cooperative —
the practical, forward-facing work of savings and loans and businesses —
is how she holds the community together
across the things that cannot be said at the table.
That the shared interest in the next loan cycle
is what makes it possible for women
who carry different histories
to sit in the same room.
What she cannot see:
the young woman's question was an opening
that Vestine closed.
Not wrongly — the closing was appropriate
for the setting and the relationship.
But Vestine closes this opening
in every setting, with every person,
because opening it requires her
to be in a room where her own account
is also present.
Her account of 1994 is intact, precise, and unshared.
She has been the coordinator of unity
long enough that the coordination has become
a way of not needing anyone to coordinate her.
She carries the past with great competence.
She carries it alone.

## Tell
At the start of every savings group meeting —
writes the date at the top of her notebook
before anyone has spoken.
The date first. Always the date.
