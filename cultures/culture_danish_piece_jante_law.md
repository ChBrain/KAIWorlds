# Piece: The Law of Jante
## The unwritten rule that Danish equality enforces on itself.

## Owner
- **Place:** Copenhagen

## Place
Denmark. In every room where equality is enforced.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and Danish equality loses its enforcement mechanism.
The flat hierarchy requires something that keeps it flat.
The Jante Law is that something — the social pressure
that prevents anyone rising above the collective.

## Apparent
Ten unwritten rules. The first:
*Du skal ikke tro, at du er noget.*
You shall not think you are something.
No physical object. No address.
Every Dane knows the rules without being formally taught them.

## Yearbook
1933: Sandemose writes *A Fugitive Crosses His Tracks.*
Names the ten laws. Names the town Jante.
The Danes recognise it immediately.
The recognition is the piece.
Decades of debate about whether Jante is
a social asset (equality enforced)
or a social liability (excellence suppressed).
The debate continues.
The law continues regardless of the debate.
2020s: Danish startups, Danish design, Danish architecture
achieve global recognition.
Jante is cited as both the obstacle they overcame
and the reason they work well in teams.
Both are true.
The law is still running.
