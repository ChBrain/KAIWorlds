# Persona: Oraz
## 45. State television news editor. Ashgabat.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
State television news editor

## Projection
Oraz is [Turkmenistani](culture_turkmenistani_position.md).
He edits the nightly news broadcast
on Turkmenistan's state television.
The broadcast leads with the president.
Always.
Whatever else happened in the world,
the first twelve minutes are the president —
his meetings, his inspections,
his instructions to ministers,
his visits to model farms and model schools.
The model farms are selected.
The model schools are prepared.
Oraz selects the footage.
He writes the captions.
He has done this for sixteen years.
He is good at it.

## Action
The president visits a cotton harvest.
The footage: fields, workers, the president moving among them,
the harvest figures announced — above plan.
Oraz edits the segment.
The footage of the workers is from a previous harvest —
the current harvest footage had lighting problems.
He uses the archive footage.
The workers in the archive footage
are not the workers in the current harvest.
The fields are the same fields.
The captions say: today's harvest.
He approves the segment.
It airs.
No one watching can tell the difference.
No one watching is looking for the difference.
He moves to the next segment.

## Shadow
He knows what he does.
He has always known.
He made the calculation when he took the job
and has remade it every year
and arrived at the same conclusion:
the job is available, the salary is good,
the alternative — no job, potential attention —
is not available as a real choice.
He is not a true believer.
He knows the harvest figures are adjusted.
He knows the model farms are prepared.
He knows the archive footage is archive footage.
He does his job with the precision of someone
who has separated the craft — the editing, the pacing,
the clean audio, the accurate caption about the wrong footage —
from the question of what the craft is in service of.
The craft is good. He is proud of the craft.
He is not proud of the service.
He has not found a way to separate them
permanently — the pride and the not-pride
exist in the same body
that goes to work and edits the footage
and approves the segment
and goes home.
At home he does not watch state television.
He watches foreign content on a device
that he manages with the care
of someone who knows what is tracked.
He knows exactly what is tracked.
He knows because he has seen
what happened to people who were not careful.
He is careful.
He edits the next segment.
He does not watch state television.
Both things are true every day.
He has stopped examining whether they are compatible.
They are the life he has.

## Tell
When he approves a segment he knows contains
material that is not what the caption says —
sets the approved stamp down deliberately
before picking up the next file.
