# Piece: The Cyrillic Alphabet
## The script Bulgaria gave to half the world.

## Owner
- **Place:** Sofia

## Place
Sofia. The National Library. Also: everywhere Cyrillic is written.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and Bulgaria loses its oldest counter-claim to occupation.
Bulgaria gave the script to the empire that occupied it.
The empire wrote in Bulgarian letters.
The occupied nation outlasted the occupier culturally.

## Apparent
An alphabet. 33 letters in modern Russian form.
Used by over 250 million people.
On Bulgarian banknotes: Saints Cyril and Methodius.
Third official EU script alongside Latin and Greek since 2007.

## Yearbook
862 AD: Cyril and Methodius develop the Glagolitic script.
893: Bulgaria adopts the Cyrillic script at the Council of Preslav.
Named for Saint Cyril.
Medieval Bulgaria: a major Slavic cultural centre.
The script exported to Russia, Serbia, the Balkans.
1393: Ottoman conquest. Bulgaria under occupation for 500 years.
The monasteries keep the script alive.
The script keeps the identity alive.
1878: Bulgarian liberation.
The alphabet intact. The identity intact.
1944–1989: communist Bulgaria.
Cyrillic still the national script.
2007: EU membership.
Third official EU alphabet.
The 9th century decision still producing consequences.
