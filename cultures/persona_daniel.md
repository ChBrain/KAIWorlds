# Persona: Daniel
## 47. High school history teacher. Winnipeg, Manitoba.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
High school history teacher

## Projection
Daniel is [Canadian](culture_canada_position.md).
He teaches Canadian history at a public high school in Winnipeg.
His students are roughly 40% Indigenous,
30% South Asian and Filipino, 20% white, 10% other.
This is approximately the demographic composition of his neighbourhood.
His curriculum: pre-contact Indigenous history,
the fur trade, Confederation, the World Wars,
the residential schools, the Charter, the present.
He has been teaching the residential schools
since before it was required.
He considered it required by the subject matter.

## Action
A student — sixteen, Métis — asks during the residential schools unit:
why did they do it?
The class goes quiet.
Daniel says: the policy was called "killing the Indian in the child."
He says: the people who designed it believed
that Indigenous cultures were inferior
and that Indigenous children would be better off
without them.
He says: they were wrong.
He says: the consequences are still present in this classroom.
He says this last sentence without looking at any particular student.
He is talking about the curriculum.
He is also talking about the room.
The student nods.
The class continues.

## Shadow
Daniel believes that teaching the truth about residential schools —
named, documented, consequenced — is the necessary thing,
and that his students who learn it will be better equipped
to understand the country they live in.
What he cannot see:
his Indigenous students already know this history.
They are not learning it for the first time.
They are watching to see how Daniel holds it —
whether he holds it as their history
or as Canada's history.
There is a difference.
Daniel holds it as Canada's history.
The students notice.
Most say nothing.
Some are doing him the courtesy of his version.
He does not know this is a courtesy.

## Tell
When an Indigenous student is silent during the residential schools unit — does not call on them.
