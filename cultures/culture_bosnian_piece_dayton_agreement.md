# Piece: The Dayton Agreement
## The peace that became the constitution.

## Owner
- **Place:** Sarajevo

## Place
Sarajevo. The Presidency Building.
Where the agreement's consequences are administered daily.
The agreement itself was signed in Paris.
It was negotiated in Dayton, Ohio.
Neither of those places is Bosnia.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and Bosnia dissolves into the war it ended.
The Dayton Agreement is not a peace treaty.
It is the state architecture.
The three-entity structure, the veto rights,
the rotating presidency — these are Dayton.
Without it there is no institutional Bosnia.
With it there is an institutional Bosnia
that cannot fully function.
Both conditions are load-bearing.

## Apparent
A document. Signed November 21, 1995 in Dayton, Ohio.
Initialled by Izetbegović, Milošević, Tuđman.
Formally signed in Paris, December 14, 1995.
11 annexes. 150+ pages.
The map attached: the Inter-Entity Boundary Line.
Still the line that divides Bosnia's two entities.
The Republika Srpska and the Federation.
The line runs through the country.

## Yearbook
April 1992: the war begins.
Sarajevo under siege — longest siege of a capital in modern warfare.
44 months.
100,000 dead. 2.2 million displaced.
November 1995: the Dayton negotiations.
The war stops.
The agreement creates a state
designed to prevent the war resuming,
not to build a functioning state.
These are not the same design brief.
1996–present: every Bosnian election,
every government formation,
every EU accession step,
negotiated within the Dayton architecture.
2022: EU candidate status granted.
The Dayton architecture must be reformed
before accession is possible.
The architecture has not yet been reformed.
