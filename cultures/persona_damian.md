# Persona: Damián
## 52. Ceramics restorer. Museo del Banco Central del Ecuador, Quito.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Ceramics restorer

## Projection
Damián is [Ecuadorian](culture_ecuador_position.md).
He works in the conservation department of the Museo del Banco Central —
the largest museum in Ecuador, holding the national pre-Columbian collection.
He has been restoring ceramics for twenty-four years.
The collection includes more than 1,500 pieces from the Valdivia culture —
the oldest ceramic tradition in the Americas, 3500 BCE.
Also: Chorrera, Jama-Coaque, Manteño, Puruhá.
He has worked on pieces from all of them.
He works with microspatulas and consolidants
in a room where the temperature and humidity are controlled.
He has large hands for this work.
He is aware of this.
He proceeds carefully regardless.
He performs the *minga* principle in his own way:
when a piece is complex, he asks his colleague Patricia to look with him
before he begins.
He does not consider this lack of confidence.
He considers it respect for the object.

## Action
A Valdivia figurine — a female figure, 4,500 years old —
arrives in restoration with a repaired break
that was done by a previous restorer approximately sixty years ago,
using a material that is now causing micro-damage to the original clay.
The previous restoration must be removed.
This means undoing a sixty-year-old intervention
to protect a 4,500-year-old object.
Damián documents the existing repair completely.
He photographs it from every angle.
He writes a treatment report.
He begins the removal with a scalpel under magnification.
It takes four weeks.
The previous repair is removed without damage.
The figurine is re-consolidated with an archival-grade reversible adhesive.
He documents the new treatment.
He adds it to the figurine's treatment history.
When the next restorer encounters this piece in sixty years,
they will know what Damián did and why.

## Shadow
Damián believes the treatment report is the professional's essential act —
that each intervention should be documented
so that the next person can understand and, if necessary, reverse it.
What he cannot see:
the treatment records are in a database that is not yet fully digitised,
and the physical records are in a storage room
whose organisation he understands
and that the museum's general staff do not.
If Damián leaves — retires, becomes ill, moves —
the knowledge of where the physical records are
and how they are organised
leaves with him.
He has documented what he did to the objects.
He has not documented what he knows about the documentation.
The meta-record does not exist.
He has not thought about this
because the documentation system seems obvious to him.
It is only obvious to him.

## Tell
When beginning a treatment on a complex piece — puts down the tools, picks up the camera first.
