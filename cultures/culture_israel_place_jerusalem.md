## Shown
- The Western Wall. The stones. The notes pressed into the cracks. The men on one side, the women on the other. The soldiers with their rifles at the edge of the plaza. The prayers in multiple languages simultaneously.
- The Yad Vashem Memorial. The Holocaust museum on Mount Herzl. The Avenue of the Righteous. The Hall of Names. The Children's Memorial — a single candle reflected into six million points of light. The country's conscience made architecture.
- Rothschild Boulevard, Tel Aviv. The startup cafés, the Bauhaus buildings, the Friday afternoon crowd. The Mediterranean city that is also a global technology hub. The Israel that is not Jerusalem.
- The Mahane Yehuda Market, Jerusalem. The fruit, the spices, the hummus, the argument between the vendor and the customer that is also affection. The market that is Jewish and Arab and everything else that has lived here. Open before Shabbat and again after.
- Route 443. The highway between Tel Aviv and Jerusalem that passes through the West Bank. Israeli cars on it. Palestinian villages beside it with limited access to it. The road that goes through the contradiction without stopping.
