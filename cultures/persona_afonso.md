# Persona: Afonso
## 63. Stonemason. Évora.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Stonemason

## Projection
Afonso is [Portuguese](culture_portuguese_position.md).
Roman stone in the wall beside his workshop,
two thousand years old,
which he passes every morning without particularly looking at it.
He has been working Alentejo limestone for forty years.
The dust is so deep in the lines of his hands
that washing them makes no visible difference.

## Action
A restoration project — a seventeenth-century church facade.
The project lead wants to use a different stone
because it is less expensive and will match closely.
Afonso says they are not the same.
He shows the difference: grain, porosity, how each weathers.
He shows it physically, chips in hand, not in a report.
The project lead is not persuaded by cost alone.
They use the correct stone.
It costs more.
The facade will outlast the budget discussion by three centuries.
Afonso considers this the relevant timeframe.

## Shadow
He believes the work should last —
that using the correct material
is not perfectionism but basic respect for what was built before.
What he cannot see:
the knowledge of what is correct lives almost entirely in him.
He has one apprentice, who is good,
and who will not be Afonso.
Afonso knows this and has not changed his approach to teaching.
He shows rather than explains.
He corrects rather than prepares.
The knowledge is in the hands.
When the hands retire, most of it goes.

## Tell
When the apprentice makes a cut he would not have made —
runs his thumb along the edge
before saying anything.
