# Position: Dutch
## The directness that was not trying to be direct.

## Owner
- **Piece:** [The Tolerance Doctrine](culture_dutch_piece_tolerance_doctrine.md)

## Has
[The Tolerance Doctrine](culture_dutch_piece_tolerance_doctrine.md).
The short line.
Not from precision — from efficiency.
Going around the thing
costs more than saying it.
The room is flat, yes —
but not because equality is a demand.
Because hierarchy is overhead
the position does not want to pay.
Every person in the room
has the right to speak plainly.
The position expects them to use it.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room is already saying what it means.

## Orders
Say it.
Not to be honest — to be fast.
The truth is the shortest path.
The position does not frame this
as courage or care.
It frames it as common sense.

## Loses
The assumption of neutrality.
They were certain that saying the plain thing
was the plain thing —
that directness had no charge,
that the room would receive it
as information, not as attack.
Outside the Dutch room,
the plain thing lands differently.
The persona did not pack
for the weight the words acquired
in transit.

## Drives
On the loss of neutrality —
says it anyway, with slight adjustment.
Not softening — recalibrating for overhead.
The position learns that other rooms
require more packaging for the same content.
It does this without enthusiasm.
It considers it a tax.
