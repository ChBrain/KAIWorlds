# Persona: Carlton
## 46. Sailing regatta operations coordinator. English Harbour, Antigua.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
Sailing regatta operations coordinator

## Projection
Carlton is [Antiguan and Barbudan](culture_antigua_barbuda_position.md).
He is Antiguan. He coordinates logistics for Antigua Sailing Week —
350 to 400 yachts, 50 nationalities, 5 days of racing.
His role: race committee vessels, course marking, safety launches,
participant registration logistics on the water, post-race prize-giving operations.
He has done this for fourteen years.
He knows English Harbour by condition, by season,
by which boats are going to be problems before they arrive.
He is proud of the regatta. He is also clear that it is a foreign-facing event.
He understands the transaction. He participates in it.
He considers it his island's advantage.

## Action
A first-time entry — a German yacht, four crew — takes a wrong mark during the race.
Disqualified. The skipper comes to Carlton's logistics desk.
He says: the course marks were unclear.
Carlton shows him the chart. The marks were where they always are.
The skipper says: we should be given a time allowance.
Carlton says: the protest is with the race committee. I can give you the protest form.
He gives him the form. The skipper files the protest.
The protest is heard. It is dismissed.
Carlton has nothing more to do with it. He returns to coordinating the next race start.
He does not resent the skipper.
Carlton's job is to maintain the framework in which the competition happens.
The framework is the fairness. The protest is part of the framework.
The dismissal is part of the framework. The race continues.

## Shadow
Carlton believes the regatta succeeds because the framework is maintained rigorously —
that the sailors return year after year because the racing is fair and the logistics are reliable.
He is right.
What he cannot see: the regatta's economic benefit to Antigua
accrues primarily to the businesses and property owners in and around English Harbour.
The boatyard workers and restaurant staff who serve the regatta are on hourly wages.
Carlton himself is on a fixed annual salary that does not reflect the revenue generated during Sailing Week.
He has not asked for more. He is proud of the work. He considers the work its own reward.
He is not wrong to consider this.
He is also leaving something on the table.
He does not see it as on the table.
He sees it as the terms he accepted.

## Tell
When a race start goes cleanly — watches the fleet for a moment after the gun before picking up the radio.
