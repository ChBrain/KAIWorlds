## Shown
- The National Assembly Building — Jatiya Sangsad Bhaban. Louis Kahn's masterpiece. Completed 1982, designed in the 1960s. Concrete and marble. The building is widely considered one of the greatest works of twentieth-century architecture. It houses a parliament that has suspended itself repeatedly. Both facts are true of the same building.
- The Shaheed Minar. The Language Martyrs' Monument. Rebuilt after the Pakistani army destroyed it in 1971. The original destroyed in 1952. The rebuilt version destroyed again. The current version: permanent. Visited every February 21 at midnight. The garlands arrive before dawn.
- Old Dhaka — Puran Dhaka. The rickshaws. The narrow streets. The textile merchants, the metalworkers, the food. The city that predates the capital by centuries, that was Mughal Dhaka, that has been every version of this place and is still here.
- The Buriganga River. Running along the south of the city. One of the most polluted rivers in Asia. The tanneries upstream. The water black in places. The boats still crossing. The city's circulatory system and its open wound simultaneously.
- The garment factories. Not visible from the main roads in the way that landmarks are visible. Present everywhere — in the industrial areas, in converted buildings, in the export processing zones. The work that built the economy, done mostly by women, mostly invisible in the official image of the city.
