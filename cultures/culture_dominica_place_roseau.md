# Place: Roseau / Salybia, Dominica
## The capital and the Territory on the Atlantic coast.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Shown
Roseau: the capital. The Old Market Square — where the slave market was. Now a craft market.
The Cathedral of Our Lady of Fair Haven: built on the ruins of a French Catholic church.
The Dominican dialect: English with French Creole structure, French place names, French loan words.
The Boiling Lake: the second-largest flooded fumarole in the world, in the interior.
The hike: three to four hours through the Valley of Desolation — a volcanic landscape
of boiling streams and sulphurous vents. The lake itself: grey, steaming, violently boiling at the edges.
Salybia: the administrative centre of the Kalinago Territory.
On the Atlantic coast, northeast Dominica. 45 minutes from Roseau — switchbacks, ravines,
the Atlantic visible from the ridges.
The Kalinago Barana Auté: the heritage village by the sea.
The Territory: eight hamlets along the Atlantic coast. The larouman reed in the river banks.
The sea facing the trade winds. The roughest coast on the island.
