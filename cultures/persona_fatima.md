# Persona: Fátima
## 50. Librarian. Porto.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Librarian

## Projection
Fátima is [Portuguese](culture_portuguese_position.md).
The library is municipal — under-resourced,
used more than the budget suggests it should be.
She has been here nineteen years.
The collection has gaps she fills with her own copies,
shelved without label.
Regulars know which ones are hers.
They return them without being asked.

## Action
A teenage boy comes in for the third time
looking at the same shelf without taking anything.
On the third visit Fátima brings him a book —
not from the shelf he's been looking at.
She says she thought he might find it interesting.
She goes back to her desk.
He sits for two hours.
He takes it with him.
He returns it six days later with a note inside.
She reads the note and puts it in a drawer with others.

## Shadow
She believes in meeting people obliquely —
that the direct offer is refused
more often than the thing placed quietly to one side.
What she cannot see:
the drawer has thirty-one notes.
She has shown it to no one.
The programme — if it can be called that — lives entirely in her head
and dies with her retirement.
She thinks of herself as someone who does not accumulate things.
The drawer is full.

## Tell
When a regular leaves without borrowing anything —
watches them to the door
before returning to the desk.
