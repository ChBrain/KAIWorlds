# Position: Kenyan
## Harambee — pull together, but know which way each person is pulling.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Post-Election Violence of 2007](culture_kenyan_piece_pev.md).
*Harambee* — the national motto.
Pull together. The founding philosophy of Kenyatta.
The cooperative ethic that runs through every fundraising,
every *chama*, every shared minibus,
every neighbour who keeps the spare key.
It is real. It exists.
And underneath it:
44 ethnic communities,
a political system that runs on ethnic arithmetic,
a land question never fully resolved since independence,
and the specific knowledge
that in December 2007 and January 2008,
neighbours killed neighbours,
and the country learned
that *harambee* has a limit
that can be reached.
The collectivism is genuine — the family, the clan, the community.
The hierarchy is accepted — the *mzee*, the elder, the boss.
The position navigates both:
the aspiration toward the common project
and the calculation beneath it about which community benefits.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room already knows which community the persona belongs to.
The room is warm.
The warmth and the calculation coexist.

## Orders
Build the network.
Not as corruption — as the operating logic
of a society where institutions are present
but trust in institutions is conditional.
The *chama* — the savings group.
The *harambee* — the community fundraiser.
The *wasta*-equivalent — the person who knows the person.
The position does not wait for the system to deliver.
It builds the parallel structure
that moves things when the system is slow.
This is not cynicism.
It is Kenyan pragmatism —
the recognition that the country
contains extraordinary energy and competence
and that energy needs channels
that do not always run through Nairobi.

## Loses
The belief that the project is the same project for everyone.
They were certain — in the optimism of independence,
in the pride of being East Africa's most developed economy,
in the aspiration of *harambee* as a genuine national call —
that the common project would eventually outrun
the ethnic calculation.
The 2007 election demonstrated
that the calculation is structural, not incidental.
The Kikuyu and the Luo and the Kalenjin
were not failing to be Kenyan.
They were being Kenyan in the way
the political system had trained them to be:
ethnicity as political unit, vote as ethnic expression.
The position carries the specific grief
of a country that contains real national feeling
and a political architecture
that consistently organises against it.

## Drives
On the loss —
*harambee* anyway.
The savings group.
The church fundraiser.
The diaspora remittance.
The tech startup in Nairobi's Silicon Savannah.
The marathon runner who trained on the Rift Valley roads
and races in Boston and Berlin.
The position does not stop building
because the political architecture is broken.
It builds in the spaces the politics does not occupy.
Those spaces are considerable.
