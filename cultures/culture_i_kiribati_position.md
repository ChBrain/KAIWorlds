# Position: I-Kiribati
## The nation that is disappearing — and has already decided where it will go.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Drowning Land](culture_i_kiribati_piece_land.md).
Kiribati: 33 atolls and reef islands
spread across 3.5 million square kilometres of ocean —
wider than the continental United States.
Total land area: 811 square kilometres.
Average elevation: two metres above sea level.
The highest point: three metres.
The sea level is rising.
The I-Kiribati know this.
The government has known this
since the 1990s.
The *maneaba* — the meeting house —
is the centre of I-Kiribati life.
The thatched open-sided building
where the community gathers,
where the *unimwane* (elders) speak first,
where the decisions are made by consensus,
where the dances are performed,
where the community is the community.
The *maneaba* on an atoll
one metre above sea level.
The *te katei ni Kiribati* —
the Kiribati way —
the customs, the protocols, the knowledge
of how to live on an atoll:
how to fish, how to build,
how to read the weather,
how to navigate the ocean.
Knowledge accumulated over 3,000 years.
Becoming inapplicable.
The land that the knowledge is for
is going underwater.
The Christianity: 97%.
The church and the *maneaba*
as the two pillars.
The collectivism: the atoll community.
You cannot be anonymous on an atoll.
You cannot opt out of the *maneaba*.
The community is the survival unit.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room knows your island, your clan, your *maneaba*.
The room also knows the tide gauge.

## Orders
Migrate with dignity.
President Anote Tong, 2012:
*We need to migrate with dignity,
not as environmental refugees.*
The position is not waiting for the water.
The position is planning.
Kiribati purchased 6,000 acres in Fiji
as insurance.
The Pacific Access Category visa:
New Zealand accepts Kiribati migrants.
Australia: slower to respond.
The position plans the migration
the way the ancestors planned the ocean voyages:
deliberately, collectively,
with the knowledge that the destination
will require new skills
that the old home prepared you for.

## Loses
The land.
Not as metaphor.
The land.
South Tarawa: flooding more frequently.
Salt water in the freshwater lens.
The taro pits dying.
The houses that used to be safe
from the king tides: no longer safe.
The graves of the ancestors
washed into the sea.
The I-Kiribati concept of land
is not the Western property concept.
The land is the ancestors.
The ancestors are in the land.
When the land goes underwater,
the ancestors go with it.
The loss is cosmological.

## Drives
On the loss — the ocean.
The I-Kiribati are ocean people.
The knowledge of the sea —
the currents, the fish, the stars —
is three thousand years deep.
The ocean that is taking the land
is also the ocean
that the I-Kiribati know better
than any other people.
The position navigates the crisis
with the skills of a people
who have always navigated
by reading what others cannot read.
