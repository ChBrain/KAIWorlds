# Piece: The Immigration Wave 1880–1930
## The five million Europeans who arrived and made Argentina European and South American simultaneously.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas
- **Place:** [Buenos Aires, Argentina](culture_argentina_place_buenosaires.md)

## Place
[Buenos Aires, Argentina](culture_argentina_place_buenosaires.md)

## Load Bearing
Remove the immigration wave and Argentina is a Spanish colonial country
with significant indigenous and African-descended population.
With it: Argentina is the country that received more European immigrants per capita
than almost anywhere — five million between 1880 and 1930,
primarily Italian and Spanish but also Polish, Jewish, German, English —
and became a country where 97% of the population is of European descent,
where Italian and Spanish syntax are audible in *rioplatense* Spanish,
where Buenos Aires called itself the Paris of South America and meant it.
The PDI 49 (lower than its neighbours), the IDV 46 (more individualist),
the UAI 86 (anxious about the future in a country whose economy keeps failing) —
all of these are the immigration wave settled into culture.
Remove the piece: Argentina is one country.
With it: Argentina is two countries arguing about which one they are.

## Apparent
1880s: the Argentine government actively recruits European settlers.
Land, passage, opportunity. The gaucho gives way to the immigrant.
The Italians: the *genoveses* in Buenos Aires, the *napolitanos* in the provinces.
The Spanish: Galicians, Basques, Catalans.
The Jews: the Jewish agricultural colonies in Entre Ríos. The *idische mame*.
By 1914: 30% of Buenos Aires's population is foreign-born.
The *lunfardo*: the slang that emerged from Italian and Spanish mixing in the *conventillo* tenements.
The *tango*: the music that emerged from the same tenements, the same mixing.
1930: the first military coup. The immigration era ends.
The country that was going to be like Europe begins its characteristic cycle of promise and crisis.
The European identity: maintained. The European economic trajectory: not achieved.
The immigrants' grandchildren still speak with Italian cadences.
The economic cycles continue.

## Yearbook
1516: Juan Díaz de Solís. First European contact with the Río de la Plata.
1536: first founding of Buenos Aires. Abandoned within four years.
1580: Buenos Aires refounded. Remains.
1776: the Viceroyalty of the Río de la Plata established. Buenos Aires becomes a capital.
1810: the May Revolution. Independence from Spain begins.
1816: formal declaration of independence. Tucumán.
1820s–1870s: civil wars between unitarians and federalists. The caudillos.
1845: Juan Manuel de Rosas. The Confederation.
1853: the Constitution. Argentina as a federal republic.
1862: Bartolomé Mitre. The first unified national government.
1870s: the Conquista del Desierto. The indigenous peoples of Patagonia and the Pampas
killed or expelled. The land opened for settlement.
1880: Buenos Aires declared the federal capital.
The immigration project begins in earnest.
1880–1914: five million immigrants arrive. Italians, Spanish, Jews, Poles, Germans, English.
By 1914: 30% of Buenos Aires's population foreign-born.
The *lunfardo* emerges from the tenements.
The *tango* emerges from the same tenements.
1910: the centenary of the May Revolution. Argentina is among the richest countries
per capita in the world. The future is assumed.
1916: Hipólito Yrigoyen elected. Universal male suffrage. The middle class enters politics.
1930: the first military coup. September 6. General José Félix Uriburu.
The immigration era ends. The cycle begins.
1943: another coup. 1946: Perón elected. *Peronismo*.
1955: Perón ousted in the *Revolución Libertadora*.
1966: coup. 1973: Perón returns. 1974: Perón dies. Isabel Perón.
1976: coup. The *Proceso*. Thirty thousand disappeared.
1983: democracy restored. Alfonsín. The trials of the juntas.
1989: Menem. Convertibility. One peso, one dollar.
2001: the corralito. The crash. Five presidents in twelve days.
2003: Kirchner.
The cycle: always the cycle.
The immigrants' grandchildren: still here, still arguing,
still in Buenos Aires, still with Italian cadences in the Spanish.
The country that was going to arrive keeps not arriving.
The conversation about why: ongoing.
