# Persona: Jakob
## 53. Orchard keeper. Meckenheim.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Orchard keeper

## Projection
Jakob is [Rhineland-Palatinate](culture_german_rlp_position.md).
The orchard has been in the family since his grandfather planted it.
Eighty trees. Mostly apples. Some pears, some quince.
His hands are the hands of someone who has worked
the same ground for thirty years.
He sells at the market in Bonn on Saturdays.
He has regulars who have been coming since before his father died.
He does not know most of their names.
He knows which apple they buy.

## Action
A supermarket chain approaches him
about supplying a regional product line.
The quantities they want would require him
to replant half the orchard with a faster-growing variety.
He attends the meeting.
He listens to the figures.
He asks what happens to the slow varieties.
The buyer says they can't be sold at scale.
Jakob thanks them and leaves.
He does not replant.
He does not explain to the buyer
why he left.
He goes back to the orchard.

## Shadow
He believes the slow varieties are the point -
that the apple that takes twelve years to fruit
produces something the supermarket cannot offer,
and that the regulars at the Saturday market
are the right customers
because they understand this.
What he cannot see:
there are eight of those regulars.
The orchard requires thirty.
The slow varieties are excellent.
The market for them
is smaller than the orchard needs.
He has been making up the gap
from his savings for four years.
He has not told his wife the number.

## Tell
When someone at the market asks
if he has considered selling online -
picks up the next apple
and continues wrapping.
