# Position: Swazi (Eswatini)
## Africa's last absolute monarchy — the kingdom that never promised democracy and therefore never betrayed the promise.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[Mswati's Kingdom and the Reed Dance's Legitimacy](culture_swazi_piece_mswati.md).
Mbabane — the administrative capital.
Lobamba — the royal and legislative capital.
The Ezulwini Valley — the Valley of Heaven.
The Lubombo Mountains on the east.
The Highveld, the Middleveld, the Lowveld.
A tiny country — smaller than Wales,
smaller than Sardinia —
between South Africa and Mozambique.
1.1 million people.
The Swazi: 90% of the population.
The siSwati language.
The *Dlamini* royal lineage — unbroken
as the governing structure
since the 18th century.
The collectivism: very high —
the *imphakatsini* (inner royal council),
the *libandla* (national assembly),
the village community.
The hierarchy: absolute in formal terms.
The king makes all decisions.
No other formulation is permitted.
Political parties banned since 1973.

## Orders
*Sive Siyinye* — we are one.
The Swazi national motto.
The position navigates a country
where the monarchy is not the government —
it is the country.
The government derives from the monarchy.
The country derives from the monarchy.
The *sibaya* (cattle kraal) at Lobamba
is where Swazi sovereignty physically lives.
The ceremonies — the *Umhlanga*, the *Incwala* —
are not decorative.
They are the governing mechanism
made visible.

## Loses
The political plurality
that the 2021 protests named.
60% of the population below the poverty line.
Mswati III's lifestyle:
multiple palaces, 14 wives,
a private jet,
an estimated personal wealth
while the country runs one of southern Africa's
highest HIV rates
and one of its lowest life expectancies.
The protests of June 2021:
young people asked for democracy.
The security forces killed at least nineteen.
The petition boxes were burned before submission.
The position carries the knowledge
of that specific event —
when the silence of fifty years
was tested and the response
was bullets.

## Drives
On the loss —
the ceremonies persist.
The *Umhlanga* Reed Dance:
young women bring reeds to the queen mother.
A living national assembly.
Fifty thousand participants.
The spectacle of the kingdom
at its most visible.
The *Incwala* First Fruits ceremony:
the king ritually leads
the nation into the new season.
The performance of sovereignty
that requires participation
and generates, in the participation,
a real social bond.
The tourist economy — small.
The manufacturing sector — textiles.
South Africa's economic gravity.
The position endures
as the Swazi always have:
by being specific,
by being themselves,
by performing sovereignty
as identity.
