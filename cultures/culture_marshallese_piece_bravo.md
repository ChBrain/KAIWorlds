# Piece: Bravo and the Compact
## The largest US nuclear test in history, the people it poisoned, and the treaty that settled it for a fraction of the damage.

## Owner
- **Place:** [Majuro](culture_marshallese_place_majuro.md)

## Place
Majuro. The capital atoll.
The long road on the reef strip.
The lagoon on one side.
The ocean on the other.
The US Embassy.
The Nuclear Claims Tribunal building —
now functionally inactive
because the United States has not paid the award.

## Load Bearing
Remove Castle Bravo
and the Marshall Islands is a Micronesian atoll nation
navigating post-colonial development
under the Compact of Free Association.
With it: the country that bears the most concentrated
nuclear damage of any civilian population on earth.
Bikini Atoll: still uninhabitable.
The Bikini islanders: still displaced,
60 years later.
Enewetak: partially cleaned up
using a concrete dome —
the Runit Dome —
that is now cracking
and threatening to release
its nuclear waste into the Pacific.
The Rongelap community:
evacuated in 1985 by Greenpeace's Rainbow Warrior
(the same vessel bombed by France
two months later in New Zealand)
after the US government failed to act
on continued radiation levels.
The Compact of Free Association (1986) is the second piece:
$150 million in full and final settlement.
The Nuclear Claims Tribunal subsequently assessed:
$2.3 billion in damages.
The United States has paid $2 million
of the tribunal's award.
The Compact gives the US
exclusive military access —
the Ronald Reagan Ballistic Missile Defense Test Site
at Kwajalein Atoll.
The US pays rent.
The Marshallese can live in the US without visa.
The asymmetry: the largest nuclear power
tests its weapons on the smallest Pacific nation.
The nuclear power is still there.
The missiles are still there.
The people are still sick.
The award is still unpaid.

## Apparent
1944: US forces take the Marshall Islands from Japan.
1945: Bikini selected as nuclear test site.
1946: Operation Crossroads. First tests.
Bikini Islanders told they are helping all mankind.
They agree.
They are moved to Rongerik — insufficient food, no adequate water.
1954: Castle Bravo. March 1.
15 megatons. 1,000 times Hiroshima.
Predicted yield: 4–6 megatons.
Fallout: 7,000 square miles.
Rongelap (110 miles away): heavy fallout.
Not evacuated for 52 hours.
Japanese fishing vessel Lucky Dragon No. 5: crew irradiated.
International outcry.
US nuclear testing programme continues until 1958.
Total: 67 tests at Bikini and Enewetak.
1968: US declares Bikini safe. Returns.
1978: Bikini declared unsafe again.
Evacuated again.
Still uninhabitable.
1979: Marshall Islands self-governing.
1986: Compact of Free Association.
$150 million settlement. Independence.
1988: Nuclear Claims Tribunal established.
2000: Tribunal awards $2.3 billion.
US Congress: has not appropriated the funds.
2024: Compact III renegotiated.
Nuclear claims: still unresolved.
The Runit Dome: cracking.
The Pacific: warming.
The dome: in the warming Pacific.
