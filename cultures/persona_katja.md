# Persona: Katja
## 51. Choir director. Dresden.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Choir director

## Projection
Katja is [Saxon](culture_german_saxon_position.md).
She directs a community choir of forty-two members
in a Dresden suburb.
The choir has been running since 1958.
It was a works choir under the GDR.
Now it is a community choir.
The membership has changed twice over.
The repertoire has not changed as much as you might expect.
There are three members who joined in 1975
and have not left.
She considers them the living archive.

## Action
A local radio station invites the choir
to record a special programme
marking the 35th anniversary of the Wende.
The producer wants the choir
to sing songs from 1989.
Katja knows the repertoire.
She also knows that two of her 1975 members
do not wish to perform
as if the GDR period can be summarised in an occasion.
She negotiates a programme
that includes 1989 material, pre-1989 material,
and post-reunification material.
She does not explain the negotiation to the producer.
She presents it as the artistic programme.
The producer accepts.
The recording is made.
The three 1975 members sing the whole thing.

## Shadow
She believes that the choir
is more honest than any occasion
that tries to fix what it means -
that forty-two people from different parts
of the same history
singing the same notes
is the actual document of what happened here.
What she cannot see:
the programme she negotiated
is also a smoothing.
The 1975 members did not want to be in an occasion.
She put them in a better occasion
and called it artistic judgement.
The discomfort they carried
was not resolved.
It was made singable.
She is good at this.
She is not sure this is the same as honest.

## Tell
When the radio producer thanks her
for the thoughtful programming -
says the choir decided.
The choir decided what she proposed.
She does not add the second sentence.
