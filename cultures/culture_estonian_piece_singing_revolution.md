# Piece: The Singing Revolution
## How Estonia left the Soviet Union with songs.

## Owner
- **Place:** Tallinn

## Place
Tallinn. The Song Festival Grounds.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and Estonian efficiency loses its emotional ground.
The Singing Revolution explains both the stillness and the speed:
the songs held the identity during occupation,
then ended the occupation.

## Apparent
The Song Festival Grounds, Tallinn. Capacity 100,000 standing.
June 1988: 300,000 people - a quarter of the Estonian population.
Singing Estonian songs in front of Soviet authorities
who cannot arrest 300,000 people for singing.

## Yearbook
1940: Soviet occupation.
1941–1944: German occupation.
1944: Soviet re-occupation.
The Song Festival continues, in modified form,
throughout the occupation.
The songs are the thread.
1987: the phosphorite protests. Estonians refuse
Soviet mining plans. The first public resistance.
1988: the Song Festival. 300,000 people.
The Singing Revolution begins.
1989: the Baltic Chain.
Estonia, Latvia, Lithuania: two million people
hold hands across 675 kilometres.
1991: Estonian independence.
August 20. Soviet tanks on the streets of Tallinn.
The parliament votes for independence anyway.
The tanks withdraw.
Today: Estonia is the most digitally advanced
country in the world.
E-governance, digital ID, online voting.
The speed of transformation is the other side
of the 50 years of suppression.
The songs held the identity.
The technology moves it forward.
