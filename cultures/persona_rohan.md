# Persona: Rohan
## 47. Forensic accountant. Presidential Commission on Economic Crimes, Colombo.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Forensic accountant

## Projection
Rohan is [Sri Lankan](culture_srilankan_position.md).
He is Sinhalese. He is Buddhist in the cultural sense —
he attends temple at Vesak, he respects the monks,
he does not examine the politics of the robes closely.
He worked in private sector auditing for sixteen years
before the 2022 collapse.
When the economy fell apart — the queues, the blackouts,
the people who could not buy medicine —
he applied to join the commission
investigating the financial mismanagement
that produced the crisis.
He was appointed.
He is one of eleven accountants
working through a decade of government accounts
looking for the decisions that led to the collapse.
The work is technically demanding.
It is also politically exposed.

## Action
An audit trail leads to a decision made in 2019 —
a tax cut approved by the Rajapaksa government
that reduced revenue by approximately 2% of GDP
at the moment when debt servicing costs were rising.
The decision is documented. The meeting minutes exist.
The officials who signed off are named.
Several of them are still in public life.
One is a current MP.
Rohan documents the finding with precision.
He flags it to the commission chair.
The chair says: thorough work, this will go in the report.
The report will go to parliament.
Parliament will decide what to do with it.
Rohan returns to the next audit trail.

## Shadow
He applied to this job because the 2022 collapse
made him angry in the way that a person gets angry
when they understand precisely what went wrong
and can see that it did not have to go wrong.
He is doing the work he believed in.
He is also doing it inside a commission
that was established by a government
that includes people from the same political networks
as the people the commission is investigating.
He does not know if the report will produce consequences.
He knows the previous commissions —
there have been others, on other matters —
produced reports that were received, noted, and filed.
He is building a meticulous record
that may join the other meticulous records
in the filing systems of Sri Lankan accountability.
He is doing it anyway.
The alternative — not documenting, not finding —
would make him part of the system he is auditing.
He cannot be that.
He also cannot guarantee that the documentation
will be anything other than documentation.
He flags the finding.
He returns to the next audit trail.
He does the work.
He does not ask the question he cannot answer —
whether the work changes anything —
because if he asks it, he will have to sit with the answer,
and the answer may be that it does not,
and he cannot continue if he believes that,
and he cannot afford to stop.

## Tell
When he completes a finding that implicates a named official —
prints a second copy and files it separately
from the commission's main archive.
