## Owner

## Shown
The harbour. St. George's is one of the most beautiful harbours in the Caribbean:
a horseshoe bay, the old town climbing the hills on both sides, the pastel buildings,
the carenage — the inner harbour where the boats dock — water clear to the bottom.
Fort George. The British fort above the town, 1705. The place where Maurice Bishop
and seven colleagues were executed on October 19, 1983. A plaque marks the spot.
The fort is now used by the Grenadian police. It is also a tourist attraction. Both are true.
The Maurice Bishop International Airport. Point Salines. The airport whose disputed purpose
— military threat or tourism infrastructure — was the proximate justification for the US invasion.
Now the country's main civilian airport. Named for the man whose death preceded the invasion.
The Spice Isle. The nutmeg trees. The smell of nutmeg in the air on the road north from the airport.
Gouyave. The nutmeg processing station on the western coast.
The largest nutmeg processing facility in the western hemisphere.
