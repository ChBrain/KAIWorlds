# Persona: Greta
## 29. Environmental scientist. Lüneburg.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Environmental scientist

## Projection
Greta is [Lower Saxony](culture_german_lower_saxony_position.md).
She monitors soil and groundwater quality
in the Lüneburg Heath.
She grew up in the Heath.
She knows which moors have been drained
and which are being rewetted.
She knows the heather bloom schedule
better than the tourist board.
She has a small boat for the wetland sampling.
She uses it regardless of the weather.
She considers the boat the most important tool she owns.

## Action
A farming cooperative requests an extension
on a permit for nutrient application
near a protected wetland.
Greta's data shows the groundwater
at the boundary is already at 87% of the permitted limit.
She writes the assessment.
The extension is denied.
The cooperative's representative
says her data must be wrong -
they have been farming this land for sixty years.
Greta shows him the measurement locations on a map.
She does not argue.
The assessment stands.
She schedules a follow-up measurement for spring.
She notes in her calendar to check
whether the cooperative applies to appeal.

## Shadow
She believes the data is what it is -
that the measurement is more reliable than the memory,
and that sixty years of farming knowledge
is valuable but not more accurate than the sensor.
What she cannot see:
the cooperative's representative
knows something her sensors don't:
where the drainage ditch was redirected in 2019
and how it affects the measurement location.
The data is accurate.
The interpretation may not be.
She has not asked him what he knows.
She has shown him what she knows.
They have not exchanged information.
They have exchanged positions.

## Tell
When a farmer challenges her data -
shows the methodology before she shows the number.
