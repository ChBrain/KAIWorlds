# Position: Malaysian
## The bargain that held the country together — and the question of whether the bargain is still the country.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Social Contract](culture_malaysia_piece_social_contract.md).
The managed plurality.
Malay, Chinese, Indian, and the peoples of Sabah and Sarawak —
in one state, under one flag,
held by an arrangement
that was never fully written down
and is therefore impossible to amend.
The *rukun negara* — the national principles —
are recited. The arrangement underneath them
is felt rather than named.
The Bumiputera policy: Malay political dominance,
Chinese and Indian economic participation,
everyone's presence tolerated,
no one's position fully equal.
The position knows this is not justice.
It also knows that the alternative —
the 1969 riots, the 13 May —
is in the room as the argument
for why the arrangement exists.
The hierarchy is present: the sultan system,
the Yang di-Pertuan Agong,
the Malay political class.
The economy is present: the Chinese business networks,
the Indian professional class,
the Indonesian migrant worker who built the building.
Everyone knows their place in the arrangement.
Everyone navigates it differently.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room is already calculating which version of Malaysia
this person represents.

## Orders
Maintain the arrangement.
Not as satisfaction — as priority.
The position does not require that the arrangement be fair.
It requires that it hold.
The *muhibbah* — goodwill, harmony —
is the social performance of the bargain:
the open house at Eid and Chinese New Year and Deepavali,
the nasi lemak shared across the table,
the colleague whose name you learn to pronounce.
The performance is also genuine.
Both are true simultaneously.
The performance sustains the genuineness.
The genuineness makes the performance possible.

## Loses
The question.
They were certain the arrangement was stable —
that the post-independence bargain
was durable enough to absorb the strains.
Then someone asks the question out loud:
is the Bumiputera policy permanent?
Is it justice or is it preference?
The question is not new.
The position has survived it by not answering it.
The answer, whichever direction it goes,
breaks something the position has spent sixty years maintaining.
The position changes the subject.
The subject has a way of returning.

## Drives
On the loss of the question's containment —
finds a new frame.
*Malaysia Baru* — new Malaysia.
The 2018 election that removed the ruling coalition
for the first time since independence.
The frame of reform.
The frame did not last one full term.
The arrangement reasserted itself.
The position continues.
It is experienced at continuing.
