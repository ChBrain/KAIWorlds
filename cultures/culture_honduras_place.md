# Place: Tegucigalpa / La Ceiba, Honduras

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Shown
- Tegucigalpa. The capital built in a valley surrounded by hills.
  No colonial grid — the city grew along ravines and ridgelines.
  The Basilica of Our Lady of Suyapa. The patroness of Honduras.
  The market around it: every kind of trade, every kind of person.
- La Ceiba. The Caribbean port city. The Garífuna community of Barrio La Isla.
  The carnaval in May — the best in Central America, argued by everyone who has been.
  The Cangrejal River in the hills behind: whitewater and cloud forest.
- Copán. The Maya ruins in the west. The hieroglyphic stairway —
  the longest known Maya inscription. The archaeologists who have been there since 1975.
  The surrounding town: tourism, craft markets, the world's researchers.
- The Sula Valley. Banana plantations extending to the Caribbean.
  The infrastructure built by United Fruit Company and nationalised and never fully
  disentangled from the company's legacy.
- San Pedro Sula. The industrial capital. The highest murder rate
  in the world in 2011–2013. Substantially reduced since.
  The city that is associated internationally with violence
  and that its residents live in as a city.

## Withheld
The migration north.

The remittances: approximately 26% of GDP.
More than manufacturing, more than exports, more than foreign investment.
The economy of Honduras is partly an economy of absence —
of the people who left and what they send back.
