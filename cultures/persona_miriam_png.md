# Persona: Miriam
## 35. Community health worker. Goroka, Eastern Highlands.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Community health worker

## Projection
Miriam is [Papua New Guinean](culture_papua_new_guinean_position.md).
She is Kuman-speaking, from a village
twenty minutes from Goroka on foot.
She trained as a community health worker in 2012.
She has been working in this community since.
She is one of six women
who cover 4,000 people
across twelve villages.
Her motorbike is twelve years old.
She has fixed it herself four times this year.

## Action
A woman in a remote village
has not attended antenatal appointments.
The community health post
doesn't have the record
because the record was never started.
Miriam goes.
She finds the woman.
She is seven months pregnant.
She is also being watched by the husband's family —
the previous child was stillborn,
and the sanguma accusation
is already in the air.
Miriam sits with the woman.
She does not speak about the sanguma.
She speaks about the pregnancy.
She examines her.
She schedules the woman
for a facility birth in Goroka.
She speaks to the husband's mother —
not about the accusation,
about the birth plan.
She gives the mother a role in the plan.
Two months later the baby is born healthy.
The sanguma accusation dissolves
in the presence of the living child.

## Shadow
She navigates the sanguma logic
by making it irrelevant —
by producing the outcome
that removes its premise.
This works.
It works because she is there.
The villages she cannot reach
this month, because the motorbike needs repair
and the parts come from Port Moresby
and the order takes three weeks —
those villages do not have Miriam.
They have the accusation
and no one to produce the counter-evidence.
She is the system working.
She is also the system's limit.

## Tell
She carries the motorbike repair log
in the same bag as the patient register.
Both records.
Both real.
