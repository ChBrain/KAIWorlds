# Position: Emirati
## The tribe that built a city-state in forty years — and became a minority in its own country.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Federation and the Pearl](culture_uae_piece_federation.md).
The *majlis* — the open sitting room,
the place where the sheikh receives,
where the guest arrives without appointment,
where coffee is served and grievances heard.
The *majlis* is the political philosophy made furniture:
the ruler is accessible,
the subject has recourse,
the relationship is personal before it is institutional.
The UAE has built skyscrapers and free zones
and artificial islands
and the *majlis* is still the operating system
underneath all of it.
The Emirati position is Bedouin in its root —
the desert, the falcon, the camel,
the hospitality that is absolute
because in the desert
the stranger at your tent
may be the person who saves your life next season.
The oil arrived in Abu Dhabi in 1958.
Dubai had almost no oil.
Dubai built the port, the free zone, the airport,
the real estate, the financial centre —
the trade hub that the Bedouin logic
extended to a global scale.
The position now exists inside a country
where Emiratis are 11% of the population.
89% are foreign nationals.
The position is the minority
that owns and governs the majority
that built everything around it.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room knows: this person is either Emirati
or they are not,
and the room operates differently
depending on which.

## Orders
Preserve the Emirati identity.
The position is acutely aware
of what it means to be 11%.
The Emiratisation policy — Emirati nationals
in government and private sector roles —
is the policy of a position
that knows the arithmetic
and is trying to ensure
the arithmetic does not erase it.
The *abaya* and the *kandura* —
the national dress —
are not merely clothing.
They are the visible insistence
that the Emirati is present
in the city the Emirati built
and is outnumbered in.

## Loses
The ground.
Not the territory — the cultural ground.
They were certain the prosperity
would preserve the position —
that the wealth would protect the heritage.
The heritage requires a community to hold it.
A community of 11%
inside a globalised megacity
is a community under pressure
regardless of the wealth.
The Emirati who grows up in Dubai
speaks English before Arabic,
has no Emirati classmates in most schools,
lives in a city where the culture being performed
is international, not Emirati.
The *majlis* is in the villa.
Outside the villa: Dubai.
The position holds the *majlis*.
The city holds everything else.

## Drives
On the loss of the ground —
the institutions.
The cultural centres, the heritage villages,
the Arabic language programmes,
the Emirati museums that tell
the story of the pearl diving
and the Bedouin camps
and the founding fathers
to a population that mostly
did not come from any of it.
The position builds the story
and offers it to the city
and waits to see what the city does with it.
The city is mostly looking at its phone.
The position continues building the story.
It knows what happens to stories
that are not told.
