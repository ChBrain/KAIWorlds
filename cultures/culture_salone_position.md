# Position: Sierra Leonean
## The country that looked at what happened to it — and wrote it down.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The War and the TRC](culture_salone_piece_trc.md).
Freetown. The original settlement of freed slaves.
*Serra Lyoa* — the Lion Mountains — named by Portuguese sailors.
The Krio people: descendants of returned slaves
from Nova Scotia, Jamaica, West Africa —
a creole community that built
the most educated population in West Africa
in the 19th century.
The Fourah Bay College: first university in sub-Saharan Africa, 1827.
Alongside: the Temne, Mende, Limba, Kono,
Mandingo and a dozen other groups —
each with their own authority structures,
their own Poro and Sande,
their own claim to the land.
The collectivism: high.
The family, the chieftaincy.
The Poro — the male initiation and governance society —
and the Sande — the female equivalent —
run parallel to the formal state.
The civil war destroyed much
but did not destroy the chieftaincy
or the societies.
They mediated reintegration
in ways the formal TRC could not reach.

## Orders
*Ose* — endure.
Sierra Leonean culture has a specific combination
of deep expressiveness —
the music, the stories, the *krio* wit —
and the capacity to absorb catastrophe
and continue.
This is not passive.
It is the active determination
to keep living after what happened.

## Loses
The trust.
The TRC found: the root cause of the war
was not diamonds.
It was corruption — endemic, structural,
that denied dignity to citizens
and drove young men with nothing
into a rebel movement
that used their grievances
to perpetrate atrocities.
The TRC also found:
the diamonds funded the war but did not cause it.
The cause was the failure of the state
to be a state worth defending.
The position carries this knowledge —
that the war was preventable,
that it was produced by choices,
that the same conditions
are not fully resolved.

## Drives
On the loss —
*Betteh days comin.* Better days coming.
Not optimism — determination.
The TRC exists.
The Special Court convicted Taylor and others.
Ellen Johnson Sirleaf next door won the Nobel Peace Prize.
Sierra Leone had free elections in 2023 —
disputed, resolved, transfer of power.
The peace is fragile in the specific way
that peace always is
when the underlying conditions
have been addressed incompletely.
The position knows this.
It keeps building.
