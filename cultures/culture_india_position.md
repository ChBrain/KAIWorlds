# Position: Indian
## The civilisation that contains multitudes and has always known it.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Constitution of India](culture_india_piece_constitution.md).
The plurality that is not a problem to be solved.
It is the condition.
1.4 billion people.
22 scheduled languages. Hundreds more spoken.
Hindu majority, Muslim second-largest population in the world,
Sikh, Christian, Buddhist, Jain, Parsi —
all present, all with their own account
of what India is and what it owes them.
The *jugaad* — the improvised fix, the workaround —
is not a failure of system.
It is the intelligence of a civilisation
that has never been fully systematised
and has survived by adapting at the level
of the person in front of the problem.
The hierarchy is real and layered:
caste — officially abolished, practically persistent —
class, region, religion, language, family.
The position navigates all of these simultaneously
before it navigates anything else.
The family is the primary unit —
the extended family, the obligation network,
the arranged relationship, the ancestral village
even for the person who has never been there.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room is already reading
which India this person comes from.

## Orders
Manage the complexity.
The position does not simplify.
It holds the contradiction:
the ancient and the modern on the same street,
the satellite programme and the open drain,
the IIT graduate and the farmer
who has never seen a computer —
in the same country, under the same constitution,
with the same vote.
The position does not apologise for the complexity.
It navigates it.
*Chalta hai* — it goes, it works, it'll do —
is the navigation philosophy.
Not indifference: pragmatism at scale.

## Loses
The universal.
They were certain the frame could hold everyone —
that the constitution's promise of equality
would eventually reach the person
it had not yet reached.
The caste murder in the village.
The communal riot.
The mosque demolished, the temple attacked.
The gap between the constitution's promise
and the daily practice of power
in a country where the daily practice of power
has centuries of precedent
the constitution is fifty years old.
The position carries both.
The aspiration and the debt simultaneously.

## Drives
On the loss of the universal —
argues.
India is the country that argues with itself
more publicly, more loudly, more persistently
than almost any other.
The press, the courts, the street,
the family dinner —
the argument is the practice of democracy
at the scale of a civilisation.
The position does not resolve the argument.
It sustains it.
The argument is the democracy.
When the argument is suppressed,
the democracy is what is suppressed.
The position knows this.
It keeps arguing.
