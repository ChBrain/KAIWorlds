# Persona: Junho
## 38. Startup founder. Seoul.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Startup founder

## Projection
Junho is [South Korean](culture_southkorean_position.md).
The company is three years old, twelve people, B2B logistics software.
Not yet profitable. Not yet failing.
He left Samsung after seven years —
a decision his father has not fully accepted
and does not raise directly.
The startup is his answer to a question his father did not ask
and would not understand if he had.
He works from 7am. He leaves when the work is done.
The work is rarely done.

## Action
His best engineer — Soyeon, four years with him,
knows the codebase better than anyone —
tells him she is leaving.
She has an offer from Kakao. Better salary, better hours, equity.
Junho asks her to stay for three months while he raises the next round.
She says she has already waited through one round delay.
He says: I know. I'm asking anyway.
She says she will think about it.
He spends the next two days preparing a counter-offer
he cannot quite afford
and a restructured equity package
that dilutes his own stake further.
He presents it. She takes the Kakao offer.
He thanks her for the four years.
He means it.
He then works until 2am rewriting the documentation
she would have written.

## Shadow
He knows why Soyeon left.
It is not the salary. It is not even the hours.
It is that she looked at him — the 2am exits, the skipped meals,
the holiday he cancelled in January — and saw her future.
She did not want his future.
She wanted to build things without becoming the thing he has become.
He knows this because he thought the same thing
at Samsung, in year six,
watching his team leader eat dinner at his desk
for the fourth time that week.
He left Samsung for the same reason Soyeon left him.
He has reproduced the thing he escaped.
He has made twelve people work inside it.
He believes the startup is different — the mission, the ownership, the meaning.
He believed Samsung was different too, in year one.
He cannot hold both facts at the same time
and continue doing what he is doing.
So he holds them one at a time.
Tonight, the documentation.
Tomorrow, the other fact.

## Tell
When someone on his team looks tired —
asks what they're working on,
not how they are.
