# Piece: The Assassination of Lumumba
## Seventy-one days — and the sixty years built on the rubble of what was killed.

## Owner
- **Place:** [Kinshasa](culture_drc_place_kinshasa.md)

## Place
Kinshasa. Then Léopoldville.
The city Lumumba governed for seventy-one days.
The house where he was placed under arrest.
The airport where he was flown out in chains.
Katanga province. Élisabethville.
January 17, 1961.
Shot. His body dissolved in acid
by Belgian officers.
The acid because they feared
what his grave would become.
They were right to fear it.
The grave they prevented
exists anyway — in every Congolese
who was ever told
that the country's potential
was foreclosed before it had a chance.

## Load Bearing
Remove Lumumba's murder
and the DRC has a different first Prime Minister —
perhaps a federalist, perhaps a regionalist,
certainly someone more palatable to the West.
With it: the DRC has Mobutu.
Specifically: the DRC has Mobutu because
the CIA and Belgium needed Lumumba removed,
and Mobutu was the instrument available.
Mobutu governed for thirty-two years.
He stole between four and fifteen billion dollars —
estimates vary.
He renamed the country Zaire.
He renamed himself: Mobutu Sese Seko Kuku Ngbendu Wa Za Banga —
the warrior who goes from conquest to conquest.
He renamed the river.
He renamed the cities.
He renamed Lumumba's birthplace Kananga
and let the memory rot.
What was not renamed:
the extraction logic.
Leopold had extracted rubber and ivory
through terror.
Mobutu extracted copper and cobalt and political loyalty
through the same mechanism, different instruments.
The IMF and World Bank funded him
because he was the anti-communist alternative.
When he fell in 1997 —
Laurent Kabila's rebels marching from the east —
the country he left had no functional institutions.
No roads. No courts. No reliable currency.
A civil service that had not been paid in years.
The two Congo Wars — 1996 to 2003 —
killed an estimated five to six million people.
More than any conflict since World War II.
Remove the piece and this chain of causation
looks different.
With it: the murder of Lumumba
is the load-bearing event
not only of Congolese history
but of Central African history —
the proof of concept for what the West
would permit in Africa
during the Cold War,
and therefore the template
for what followed across the continent.
*History will one day have its say.*
He was right. It does.
The history he predicted
is still being written
in cobalt mines
in South Kivu.

## Apparent
June 30, 1960: independence.
Lumumba's independence day speech — unscripted,
direct, naming Belgian atrocities.
The Belgian king in the front row.
July 5: army mutiny. Belgian troops intervene.
July 11: Katanga secedes under Tshombe.
Belgian mining interests intact.
Lumumba requests UN troops.
The UN arrives. Does not reunify the country.
Lumumba requests Soviet transport.
The CIA flags this as Communist alignment.
September 5: President Kasa-Vubu dismisses Lumumba.
September 14: Mobutu's first coup — bloodless.
Lumumba under house arrest.
UN troops guard the perimeter.
Mobutu's troops guard the outer ring.
December 1: Lumumba escapes.
Attempting to reach Stanleyville.
December 2: captured at Lodi.
Flown back to Léopoldville.
Beaten on the plane.
January 17, 1961: executed in Katanga.
Belgian officers present.
CIA informed.
His body dissolved in acid.
A tooth retained as a trophy by a Belgian officer.
That tooth was returned to his family
by the Belgian government
in 2022.
Sixty-one years.
1965: Mobutu seizes full power.
1971: country renamed Zaire.
1997: Mobutu falls. Laurent Kabila takes power.
1998: Second Congo War begins.
2001: Laurent Kabila assassinated.
His son Joseph Kabila takes power at twenty-nine.
2016: Joseph Kabila's term expires.
He refuses to step down.
2019: elections. Félix Tshisekedi declared winner.
The result negotiated, not entirely clean.
2023: Tshisekedi re-elected.
Eastern DRC still in conflict.
M23 rebels — backed by Rwanda — hold territory.
The cobalt leaves.
The phones are made.
Lumumba's tooth is in a box
in Kinshasa.
