# Persona: Noorullah
## 48. Secondary school headmaster. Kandahar.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Secondary school headmaster

## Projection
Noorullah is [Afghan](culture_afghan_position.md).
He is Pashtun. He has run the school for eleven years —
through the final years of the republic,
through the transition,
into the current administration.
The school is for boys.
Under the republic, he also administered
the girls' school across the road.
The girls' school is closed above grade six.
He does not administer it anymore.
The building is used for storage.
He knows what is stored there.
He does not raise it.

## Action
A Taliban education official visits for an inspection.
The curriculum review: Islamic studies increased,
certain subjects reduced,
the textbooks revised to remove content
the administration considers problematic.
Noorullah has implemented the curriculum changes.
He presents the timetable.
The official is satisfied.
After the official leaves, a teacher approaches Noorullah.
The teacher has been teaching mathematics
using the old textbooks — the problems involve mixed-gender scenarios,
graphs with human figures.
The teacher asks: should I stop?
Noorullah says: use the approved materials.
The teacher nods.
Noorullah goes back to his office.
He does not tell the teacher
that he has a set of the old textbooks
locked in the cabinet behind his desk.
He does not know why he has kept them.
He has not opened the cabinet in eight months.

## Shadow
He tells himself he is protecting the school —
that a school that exists and teaches mathematics,
even with revised textbooks,
is better than a school that is closed
because the headmaster created a problem.
He believes this.
He also has the locked cabinet.
He has not decided what the locked cabinet means.
He has not decided what he would do
if a student asked him to open it.
He has not decided what he would do
if the official asked him about it.
He has kept the books.
He has not opened them.
He is the headmaster of a functioning school
and the keeper of a locked cabinet
and he has not yet determined
whether those two things are compatible
or whether keeping the cabinet locked
is the only way to keep the school open
or whether there is a version of himself
that could do what he is not doing
without the school paying for it.
He has been not deciding this since 2021.
The cabinet is still locked.
The school is still open.
He does not know how long both will remain true.

## Tell
When he passes the storage building
that was the girls' school —
takes a different route back to his office
than the one that goes past the door.
