# Piece: The War That Didn't End
## 2015–present. The war that created the world's worst humanitarian crisis — and that the world largely stopped watching.

## Owner
- **Place:** Sanaa

## Place
Sanaa. The old city.
UNESCO World Heritage.
The tower houses — the *mafraj* at the top,
the decorated windows,
the architecture that is found nowhere else on earth.
Some were struck by Saudi airstrikes.
Some are being restored.
The old city of Sanaa is still there.
The children in it are hungry.

## Load Bearing
Remove the 2015 Saudi-led intervention
and Yemen is a failed state
with a Houthi coup in the capital
and a government in exile
and the worst humanitarian indicators
on the Arabian Peninsula.
With it: Yemen is a failed state
with a Houthi coup in the capital
and a government in exile
and the worst humanitarian indicators
on the Arabian Peninsula
plus ten years of airstrikes,
blockade, famine, cholera, COVID,
and the specific cruelty
of a war conducted by a coalition
armed by the United States and the United Kingdom
that killed civilians at wedding parties
and in school buses
and in hospitals
and produced documentation
that was presented to the UN
and resulted in no legal accountability.
Remove the piece: a civil war.
With it: a civil war that the world's most powerful countries
chose to arm one side of,
profited from doing so,
and which produced —
by conservative estimates — 377,000 dead
by the end of 2021,
half from indirect causes:
the famine, the disease,
the healthcare system that was bombed.
The Houthis began attacking Red Sea shipping in 2023
in solidarity with Gaza.
The US and UK struck Houthi targets in Yemen.
Yemen became, briefly, legible to the Western news cycle again.
The famine continued.
Remove the piece: a regional conflict.
With it: the argument about what the world
chooses to watch and what it chooses to arm
and what it calls a crisis
and when the calling changes anything.
The old city of Sanaa is still there.
The tower houses are still decorated.
The children are still hungry.
Both things are still true.

## Apparent
Pre-Islamic: the Sabaean kingdom. The Queen of Sheba.
The ancient spice trade routes.
The frankincense and myrrh that the Romans paid gold for.
The ancient dams — the Marib Dam, 8th century BCE.
1839: Britain takes Aden. The port colony.
1962: the revolution in north Yemen. The YAR.
1967: south Yemen independence from Britain. The PDRY — Marxist.
1990: unification. Ali Abdullah Saleh.
"Dancing on the heads of snakes" — his description of governing Yemen.
2011: the Arab Spring. Saleh steps down after months of protest.
2014: the Houthis take Sanaa.
2015: the Saudi-led coalition intervenes. March 26.
The coalition includes UAE, Bahrain, Kuwait, others.
US and UK provide weapons, intelligence, refuelling.
The blockade of Hodeidah port —
80% of Yemen's imports go through Hodeidah.
The blockade and the war produce famine conditions.
2018: the Stockholm Agreement. Limited ceasefire. Partial implementation.
2021: Saleh was killed by the Houthis in 2017.
The internationally recognised government: based in Aden.
The Southern Transitional Council: wants independence.
The Houthis: control the northwest including Sanaa.
Three Yemens effectively.
The war: continuing with varying intensity.
The ceasefire of 2022: held in some areas.
2023: Houthi attacks on Red Sea shipping begin.
2024: US and UK strikes on Houthi targets.
2025: the war continues.
The humanitarian crisis: still the worst in the world.
The old city: still standing.
