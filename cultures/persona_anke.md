# Persona: Anke
## 48. Social housing administrator. Bremerhaven.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Social housing administrator

## Projection
Anke is [Bremen](culture_german_bremen_position.md).
She manages a portfolio of 340 social housing units in Bremerhaven.
She has lived in Bremerhaven her whole life.
She is aware that Bremerhaven has the highest unemployment rate
of any city in Germany.
She processes this as a daily fact, not a crisis.
The crisis was in 1990.
What remained is the condition.
She works inside it.

## Action
A family of four is three months behind on rent.
The father lost his job at the logistics depot.
The process requires her to begin eviction proceedings.
She begins them.
She also calls the family welfare office
and puts the case in front of someone she knows there.
She does not tell the family she called.
She does not stop the proceedings.
She runs both tracks simultaneously.
The welfare office finds an emergency support package.
The family stays.
She notes in the file: resolved without eviction.
She does not note the call.

## Shadow
She believes that the system works
if you know where to push it -
that the formal process
and the informal intervention
are both necessary
and that knowing when to use which
is the actual job.
What she cannot see:
she has been the informal intervention
for twenty years.
Without her specific knowledge
of who to call and when,
three families a year
would have been evicted
who were not.
She has not documented this.
She has not asked for recognition.
She has not asked whether the system
should be built to work without her.

## Tell
When a colleague asks how she handles
the difficult cases -
says you have to know who to call.
Does not write the list down.
