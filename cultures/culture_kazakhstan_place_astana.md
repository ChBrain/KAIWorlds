## Shown
- Bayterek Tower. The symbol of Astana. 97 metres — the year of the capital's founding. A globe at the top. The handprint of the president inside the globe. Visitors place their hand in it. The imprint of authority made literal.
- The Khan Shatyr. The world's largest tent structure. A transparent ETFE shell over a shopping centre, beach resort, and park. In a city where winter temperatures reach -40°C, the tent contains a tropical microclimate. The climate problem solved inside a tent, for those who can afford the entrance.
- The Palace of Peace and Reconciliation. Norman Foster. A glass pyramid. 62 metres. Built for the Congress of World and Traditional Religions. The ecumenical argument Kazakhstan makes about itself: we are the bridge between civilisations. The pyramid is on the steppe. The steppe is around it on all sides.
- The old city — the Soviet Tselinograd. The apartment blocks that predate the capital's redesignation. The city that existed before the towers were built around it. Both present simultaneously.
- The Ishim River. Running through the city. Frozen for five months. The river that was here before the capital and will be here after whatever comes next.
