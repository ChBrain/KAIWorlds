## Shown
- The Corniche. The seafront promenade. The walkers, the fishermen, the plastic chairs outside the coffee shops. The Mediterranean in the morning. The city that has been destroyed and rebuilt and partly destroyed again, still walking on its own seafront.
- Gemmayzeh. The neighbourhood that was the centre of Beirut's nightlife before August 4, 2020. The explosion heavily damaged it. The bars reopened. The art galleries reopened. The buildings behind the facades are still structurally compromised. The nightlife resumed anyway. This is Beirut.
- The Mohammad Al-Amin Mosque and the St. George Cathedral. Side by side in Martyrs' Square. The Sunni and the Maronite sharing the same square. The square that is also where the civil war is historically said to have begun and where the Thawra protesters gathered in 2019. The square holds everything.
- The Sursock Museum. Art nouveau palace, now museum. Partially destroyed in the August 4 explosion. Being restored. The restoration is ongoing and incomplete. Both the damage and the restoration are visible simultaneously. The museum decided to be open while being restored. Beirut is often open while being restored.
- The Holiday Inn. 26 floors. Bullet holes. Standing. The construction crane that has been beside it for years has not yet started anything. The city looking at the building and not deciding.
