## Shown
- Independence Square — Mustaqillik Maydoni. The Soviet-era Lenin Square repurposed. The globe of Uzbekistan at the centre. The monuments to independence and to the soldiers who died in Afghan and Soviet wars. The history layered and renamed.
- The Chorsu Bazaar. The blue-domed market in the old city. Spices, dried fruit, bread, meat. The *mahalla* economy made visible. The largest bazaar in Central Asia. Open every day regardless of what is happening in the government district.
- The Hazrat Imam complex. The Islamic library and mosque in the old city. The Uthman Quran — one of the oldest Qurans in the world, possibly stained with the blood of the caliph Uthman, killed while reading it. Displayed. The Islamic heritage the Soviet period suppressed and the post-Soviet state now presents as national treasure.
- The metro. Soviet-built, 1977. The stations decorated with Uzbek national motifs — mosaics, carved plasterwork, Soviet heroism in Central Asian aesthetic. The Soviet and the national fused in the underground. Air-conditioned. Clean. Still the most reliable transport in the city.
- The new Tashkent City district. Glass towers rising. International hotels. The future the current government is selling to foreign investors. Located beside the old city. The future and the old city do not look at each other directly.
