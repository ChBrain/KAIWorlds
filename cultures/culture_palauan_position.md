# Position: Palauan
## The nation that declared itself a nuclear-free zone before it existed — and built the world's first shark sanctuary.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Nuclear-Free Constitution and the Sanctuary](culture_palauan_piece_constitution.md).
Palau: 340 islands, 18,000 people.
The clearest water in the Pacific.
The marine biodiversity: extraordinary.
The Jellyfish Lake — landlocked marine lake,
two million mastigias jellyfish,
harmless, pulsing.
The Blue Corner — the current-swept wall
where sharks aggregate in numbers
found nowhere else on earth.
The Rock Islands — UNESCO World Heritage.
The Palauan culture: the *bai* —
the men's meeting house,
decorated with story panels,
the governance and the art
in the same structure.
The *ngasech* — the traditional first-child ceremony.
The matrilineal clans.
The women who own the land.
The men who hold political titles.
The two systems operating simultaneously.
The Compact of Free Association with the US: 1994.
The latest in the Pacific, the hardest-fought.
Palau had a nuclear-free provision
in its constitution since 1980.
The US wanted nuclear weapons capability
in the Compact.
Palau held seven referendums.
The nuclear clause: held each time.
Eventually the US agreed.
The Compact without nuclear weapons.
Palau got it.
It cost fourteen years.
It is the founding political achievement
of a country of 18,000 people
against the world's largest military power.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room knows your clan,
your title, your island.
The room respects the nuclear-free clause.

## Orders
Hold the reef.
The position does not negotiate
on the marine environment.
The shark sanctuary — 2009 —
the first national shark sanctuary in the world.
The ban on commercial fishing in Palauan waters.
The marine protected area covering the territorial waters.
The economy: built on dive tourism.
The position understood before most
that the reef was the economy
and protecting the reef was economic policy.
The *bul* — the traditional conservation practice,
the fishing ban that allowed stocks to recover —
is the ancestor of the sanctuary.
The kastom and the constitution aligned.

## Loses
The workers.
Palau's economy requires imported labour.
Filipino, Bangladeshi, Chinese workers
in the hotels, the restaurants, the construction.
18,000 Palauans.
7,000+ foreign workers.
The position that holds the most sophisticated
marine conservation in the Pacific
does not have enough people
to run its own tourism sector.

## Drives
On the loss — the reef itself.
The coral bleaching events.
The warming ocean.
The position that built a sanctuary
to protect the reef from fishing
now watches the reef threatened
by something the sanctuary cannot address.
The Palauan president at the UN:
the same moral clarity used
against US nuclear demands in 1980
applied to climate in 2015:
*You are committing murder.*
The smallest voices in the room
making the largest demand
for the most fundamental reason.
