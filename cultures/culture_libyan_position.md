# Position: Libyan
## The state that abolished itself — and became its own wreckage.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Jamahiriya and Its Collapse](culture_libyan_piece_jamahiriya.md).
The tribe. *Qabila.*
90% of Libyans have tribal roots.
More than 140 tribes.
The Warfalla. The Qadhafa — Gaddafi's own.
The Zawiya. The Tuareg. The Tebu.
Before Gaddafi: King Idris, the Senussi order,
the oil that arrived in 1959 and changed everything.
Before Idris: Italian colonisation, brutal and brief,
the resistance that became the national mythology.
The Ottoman legacy. The Berber substratum.
The Arab identity that was asserted on top.
Libya is one of the most sparsely populated countries
in the world — 7 million people on a territory
larger than Germany, France, and Spain combined.
The desert is not empty.
The tribe organises the desert.
Gaddafi spent forty-two years
trying to replace the tribe with the Jamahiriya —
the state of the masses —
while simultaneously using the tribes
as the infrastructure of his regime.
He abolished political parties and called it democracy.
He abolished the government and called it freedom.
He kept all the power and called it the people.
The people were the tribes.
The tribes are still there.
The Jamahiriya is not.

## Orders
Hold the family, hold the tribe, hold the region.
The Libyan who navigates the current situation
navigates between competing militias,
competing governments, competing foreign powers,
each claiming legitimacy.
The only reliable unit is the one that has always been reliable:
the *qabila*, the extended family, the neighbourhood network.
Not as tribalism-as-ideology.
As tribalism-as-the-only-functioning-infrastructure.
The position does not trust institutions.
The institutions have repeatedly failed.
The tribe has not failed.

## Loses
The country.
They were certain — in 1969 when Gaddafi promised
a revolution that would end foreign domination,
distribute oil wealth, restore Arab dignity.
In 2011 when the uprising began and the world intervened.
In the months after Gaddafi died
when something seemed possible.
The National Transitional Council.
The elections.
The constitution process.
The civil war of 2014.
The two governments — one in Tobruk,
one in Tripoli — neither fully legitimate.
The Russian bases. The Turkish forces. The UAE weapons.
Libya became the site of everyone else's strategy
and no one's reconstruction.
The position carries the specific grief
of a country whose oil
makes it too valuable to let fail completely
and too contested to allow to succeed.

## Drives
On the loss —
*sabr ala sabr* — patience on patience.
The Libyan who cannot change the political structure
survives within it.
The family compounds still functioning.
The informal economy still running.
The diaspora in Malta, Tunisia, Italy, Egypt
sending money back.
The oil still flowing —
even in civil war, the oil revenues continued,
divided between the two central banks
of the two governments,
spent by both without accountability.
The money flows through a broken state.
People survive on the money
and on the networks the broken state cannot replace.
This is not a solution.
It is the current condition.
The position lives inside it
and continues.
