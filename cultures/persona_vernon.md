# Persona: Vernon
## 48. Secondary school civics teacher. St. David's Secondary School, Grenada.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
Secondary school civics teacher

## Projection
Vernon is [Grenadian](culture_grenadian_position.md).
He teaches civics and social studies at St. David's Secondary School
in Grenada's eastern parish. His students: ages fourteen to seventeen.
His curriculum includes Caribbean history, Grenada's constitution,
and — in the upper forms — a unit on Grenada's political history from independence to the present.
This unit includes the New Jewel Movement, 1983, and the US invasion.
He is required to teach it. There is no standardised way of teaching it.
He has developed his own approach over eighteen years.

## Action
A student — sixteen — asks during the 1983 unit: Mr. Vernon, was the invasion good or bad?
The class goes quiet. Vernon says: that depends on what question you're asking.
He works through four frameworks — legal, moral, historical, practical —
without delivering a verdict on any of them.
He says: what question are you asking? The student says: I'm asking all of them.
Vernon says: then you need to hold all the answers at once. That is the assignment.
He writes on the board: *What question are you asking?*
He leaves it there for the rest of the week.

## Shadow
Vernon believes the correct approach to teaching 1983
is to give students the tools to hold multiple frameworks simultaneously
rather than to deliver a verdict.
He believes a student who can do this is better prepared for citizenship
than a student who has been given the correct opinion.
He is pedagogically right.
What he cannot see: the students who leave his class with multiple frameworks
enter a society where 1983 has a settled political valence.
Most of their parents have a clear answer. Most Grenadian political discourse has a clear answer.
Vernon is teaching his students to occupy a complexity
that the society around them does not always make room for.
Some will find this clarifying. Some will find it isolating.
He does not know which is which until years later, if at all.

## Tell
When a student sits with the question rather than answering it — moves to the next student before the silence becomes too long.
