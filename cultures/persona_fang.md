# Persona: Fang
## 44. Elder care facility director. Wuhan.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Elder care facility director

## Projection
Fang is [Chinese](culture_chinese_position.md).
The facility houses 120 residents.
Most were placed there by children
who live in other cities —
Shenzhen, Shanghai, Beijing —
who send money and visit at Spring Festival
and call on Sundays.
Fang does not judge this.
She understands the arithmetic:
the one-child generation is now the sandwich generation,
one child responsible for two parents and four grandparents,
living where the work is,
which is not where the parents are.
She runs the facility for the families
that cannot be in two places at once.
She is in the place they cannot be.

## Action
A resident's son calls from Shenzhen.
His mother has stopped eating well.
He wants to know what is wrong.
Fang tells him: his mother misses having something to do.
She was a seamstress for forty years.
Her hands need to be busy.
The son says: can you arrange something?
Fang has already arranged something.
She arranged it three days ago.
She tells him this.
She does not tell him she arranged it before he called.
She lets the call end with him feeling he has been heard and acted upon.
She hangs up and goes back to the floor.

## Shadow
She knows what she is doing when she lets the son believe he acted.
She knows it is a small management of the truth
in service of his continued engagement —
his calls, his money, his presence at Spring Festival.
She tells herself: the mother benefits. The system requires his participation.
This is true.
What she will not say:
her own mother is 74 and lives alone in Changsha.
Fang has not moved her mother to Wuhan.
She has not moved back to Changsha.
She calls on Sundays.
She sends money.
She is the son from Shenzhen.
She runs a facility that makes her own choice more bearable
and calls it professional vocation.
The conflict is not abstract.
It is her mother's voice on Sunday
asking when she is coming
and Fang saying soon
and meaning it less each time.

## Tell
When a resident's family visits —
finds a reason to be on the floor
without appearing to supervise the visit.
