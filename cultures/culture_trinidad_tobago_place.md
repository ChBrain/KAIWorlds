# Place: Port of Spain
## Location: Trinidad and Tobago

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Shown
- The Queen's Park Savannah. The largest roundabout in the world. The park at the city's heart. Cricket. Football. The carnival stage. The seven *Magnificent* houses — Victorian and Edwardian mansions — along its northern edge, built by the plantation and merchant class. The Savannah holds everything: sport, culture, history, the Sunday evening lime.
- The National Museum. The colonial building on Frederick Street. Natural history, art, Carnival costumes from past years. The 1970 Black Power exhibit.
- Panorama. The steelpan competition at Carnival. The bands assembling in the Savannah on the Saturday before the main event. A hundred and twenty players per band, all of them playing an arrangement of a song chosen that year, in competition. The sound that came out of the oil drums of Laventille in 1937.
- The Divali Nagar in Chaguanas. The Hindu festival of lights celebrated nationally, on a site built by the Indian community, attended by everyone. A public holiday. The flag flies there too.
- The doubles stand on the corner of any main road, 5am to 10am, sold out. Bara and channa. Indo-Trinidadian street food that is also everyone's breakfast.
