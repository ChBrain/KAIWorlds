# Position: Cambodian
## The civilisation that built Angkor and survived the killing fields — and carries both.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Khmer Rouge and the Tribunal](culture_cambodia_piece_tribunal.md).
The double inheritance.
Angkor Wat is on the flag.
The twelfth century empire that built
the largest religious monument in the world
is the evidence of what Khmer civilisation produced.
The 1975–1979 genocide is the evidence
of what Khmer civilisation survived.
Both are in the room before anyone speaks.
The position does not choose between them.
It carries both as the full account
of what it means to be Cambodian.
The hierarchy is steep: age, rank, the patron,
the *lok* who provides and protects.
The Buddhist frame runs underneath everything —
merit, karma, the long accounting.
The impermanence of suffering.
The position has tested this against the Tuol Sleng
and found it partly holds and partly does not
and continues anyway
because what else is there to continue with.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room already knows what was lost.
The room is still counting.

## Orders
Rebuild.
Not as forgetting — as necessity.
A country that lost a quarter of its population in four years —
educated professionals first, then anyone with glasses,
then anyone who admitted to knowing anything —
does not recover by processing the loss.
It recovers by building the school,
training the doctor,
finding the next generation
and giving it something to stand on.
The processing comes later, if it comes.
The building cannot wait for the processing.

## Loses
The generation.
They were certain the knowledge was preserved —
that the teachers, the doctors, the engineers,
the people who knew how to run things —
had survived somewhere.
Most had not.
The country that emerged in 1979
had almost no one left
who had been trained to do
the things a country requires.
The position rebuilt from almost nothing.
It is still rebuilding.
The word *almost* is doing significant work.
The position knows what was in the gap.

## Drives
On the loss of the generation —
teaches.
The position is oriented toward transmission:
passing to the next generation
what was nearly lost.
Not just skill — memory.
The documentation, the archives,
the DC-Cam records,
the Documentation Center of Cambodia
that has been collecting testimony since 1995.
The position understands that a country
that does not know what happened to it
cannot fully know what it is.
It is still deciding how much it wants to know.
