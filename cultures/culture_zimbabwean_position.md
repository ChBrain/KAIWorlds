# Position: Zimbabwean
## The three Chimurengas — and the coup that wasn't called a coup.

## Has
[The Chimurenga Inheritance](culture_zimbabwean_piece_chimurenga.md).
Harare. The highveld.
The tobacco farms.
The Victoria Falls — *Mosi-oa-Tunya*, the smoke that thunders.
Great Zimbabwe — the stone city
from the 11th–15th century
that proved African civilisation needed
no European patron —
the country is named for it.
The Shona: 80% of the population.
The Ndebele: 14%, in Matabeleland in the southwest.
The tension between them:
real, historical, weaponised.
1983–1987: Gukurahundi —
the North Korean-trained Fifth Brigade
killed an estimated 20,000 Ndebele civilians
in Matabeleland.
Mugabe's order.
Never fully acknowledged.
The word means: the early rain
which washes away the chaff.
The collectivism: high.
The *ukama* — Shona concept of relatedness,
the obligation of kinship.
The hierarchy: steep and contested —
ZANU-PF dominates formal authority,
the MDC tradition challenges it,
the war veterans claim their own stratum.

## Orders
*Kutsungirira* — to persevere.
The Zimbabwean position has absorbed
hyperinflation at 7,600% (2007),
land seizure, sanctions,
the coup that removed the man
who brought independence,
and the continued rule
of the same party under a different face.
*Kutsungirira* is the practice
of continuing after you've been told
that continuing isn't possible.
The Zimbabwean diaspora —
Johannesburg, London, Lusaka —
sends remittances that are now
the country's largest single foreign exchange source.
The diaspora is the economy.
The economy is the diaspora.

## Loses
The promise of 1980.
Mugabe's reconciliation speech —
famous, genuinely meant at the time,
specifically assuring white Zimbabweans
that their place was secure.
The infrastructure that followed it.
The schools. The hospitals.
The life expectancy of 63 in 1990.
Then: the land reform from 2000,
executed not to give land to the landless
but to reward ZANU-PF loyalists.
The famine. The collapse.
Life expectancy: 34 in 2007.
And then the specific grief:
Mugabe's liberation credentials were real.
The Second Chimurenga was real.
The independence was a genuine liberation.
And he then used those credentials
to cover everything that followed —
the Gukurahundi, the economic destruction,
the electoral violence —
with the unassailable authority
of the man who freed the country.

## Drives
On the loss —
the Zimbabwean resilience
that has become something of a running joke
and is also real.
*We've survived worse.*
Literally: they have.
The NGO sector — among the most developed
in southern Africa —
that kept health services running
when the state couldn't.
The opposition tradition — MDC, then CCC —
that has challenged ZANU-PF
despite the costs.
Chamisa's 44% in 2023: contested, credible,
a real opposition vote.
The country is not ZANU-PF.
ZANU-PF governs the country.
Both are true.
