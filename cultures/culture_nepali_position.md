# Position: Nepali
## The country that was never colonised and never quite became what it could have been.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The End of the Shah Dynasty](culture_nepal_piece_shah.md).
The vertical country.
Not as metaphor — as geography.
From the Terai plains at 100 metres
to Everest at 8,849 metres,
all within 200 kilometres.
The position was shaped by altitude —
the village that is three days' walk from the road,
the district headquarters that cannot be reached in monsoon,
the country that is internally more diverse
than many continents.
The Brahmin-Chhetri hill elite,
the Madhesi of the Terai plains,
the Janajati — the indigenous nationalities,
the Sherpa, the Gurung, the Tamang, the Tharu —
and the Dalit, at the bottom of every structure.
The Hindu kingdom is gone since 2008.
The federal democratic republic is new and contested.
The political parties that won the republic
have governed it with the same elite networks
that ran the kingdom.
The position knows the difference between
the form changing and the substance changing.
It is still watching which has happened.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room is still deciding
what it is now.

## Orders
Endure the distance.
The distance between the capital and the village,
between the promise and the delivery,
between the constitution and its implementation —
this is the condition.
The position has endured it across every government.
It endures it in the current one.
The remittance economy sustains it:
the young men in Qatar and Malaysia and South Korea
sending money home
so the family can endure the distance
while the state remains distant.

## Loses
The young.
They were certain the 2006 peace,
the end of the Maoist insurgency,
the new republic, the new constitution —
would give the young a reason to stay.
The young are leaving faster than ever.
500,000 per year to foreign labour.
The ones who leave are the ones who could build.
The position watches them go
and does not have an answer
that competes with the salary in Doha.
The political class that runs the republic
did not build the answer.
It built the same networks as before.
The young noticed.
They left.

## Drives
On the loss of the young —
the remittance.
The money that comes back
is what the state cannot provide.
The house in the village.
The daughter's school fees.
The hospital bill.
The position does not celebrate the remittance.
It is what fills the gap
that should have been filled differently.
It fills the gap.
The gap remains.
The money flows.
The young keep leaving.
The position endures.
It has endured before.
It will endure again.
It is not sure endurance is the same as living.
