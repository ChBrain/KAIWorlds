# Place: Basseterre / Charlestown, Saint Kitts and Nevis
## The capital and the smaller capital, three miles apart across the channel.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas
- **Position:** [Kittitian and Nevisian](culture_sknv_position.md)

## Shown
Basseterre: the capital of Saint Kitts. The Independence Square: the central square,
formerly the slave market. The National Museum.
The port: the cruise ships, the inter-island ferry to Nevis — forty-five minutes across the channel.
The sugar estates: the windmills (processing towers), now ruins.
The Brimstone Hill Fortress: UNESCO listed, eighteenth century, built with enslaved labour,
the "Gibraltar of the West Indies." The sugar closed in 2005.
The land is now golf courses and hotel developments.
Charlestown: the capital of Nevis. Small, quiet, on the leeward coast.
The Museum of Nevis History in the birthplace of Alexander Hamilton —
Nevis-born, American founding father.
The Nevis Peak: the central volcano, clouds at the summit.
The Nevis Island Assembly: a small parliamentary building in Charlestown.
Eleven members. Its own Premier. The constitution that gives it the right to call a referendum.
The channel between the islands. Three miles of Caribbean Sea.
The ferry crosses it. On a clear day you can see both islands simultaneously.
They are close enough to misunderstand each other precisely.

## Offers
The room where the Kittitian and Nevisian position does its daily work.
*The Channel* — the three miles of water between Basseterre and Charlestown;
close enough to maintain, wide enough to feel separate;
runs here as the space in which the federation's dynamic plays out.

## Withheld
The specific weight of being both the same country and two different places
that know this about themselves.
The room shows both capitals.
It does not display the routine negotiation between them —
the appointments, the revenue distributions, the quiet management of the constitutional threat —
that keeps the federation running.
Both parties know the threat is always available.
Neither exercises it casually.
The room holds the channel.
