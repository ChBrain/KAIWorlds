## Shown
- The sea wall. Around the entire perimeter of the island. Concrete tetrapods. The ocean on the other side. The wall is everywhere. You adjust to it the way you adjust to a fact that cannot be changed.
- The Friday Mosque — Hukuru Miskiy. Built 1656. Coral stone. The oldest mosque in the Maldives. The Friday prayers. The island stopping for the call.
- The fish market. The tuna. Maldivian cuisine is tuna — smoked, dried, fresh. The fishermen returning. The market open before dawn.
- The streets. Malé has no traffic lights and almost no space. Motorbikes everywhere. The island is 2 square kilometres and 200,000 people. The density is the daily condition.
- Hulhumalé. Visible across the channel. The new island, built from dredged sand, rising 2 metres above sea level — higher than Malé. The future being constructed on engineered ground. The bridge connecting them opened 2018. The first bridge in the Maldives.
