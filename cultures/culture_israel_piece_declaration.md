# Piece: The Declaration and the Occupation
## 1948 and 1967. The state that was born and the territory that was captured — and the seventy-seven years between.

## Owner
- **Place:** Jerusalem / Tel Aviv

## Place
Jerusalem. The Western Wall.
The last remnant of the Second Temple.
The holiest accessible site in Judaism.
Also: the contested city.
The capital Israel declares.
The capital most of the world does not recognise as such.
The wall that holds everything.
The wall that is contested.
Both are the same wall.

## Load Bearing
Remove the Declaration of Independence (1948)
and there is no Israel.
Remove the 1967 occupation of the West Bank and Gaza
and the Israeli position exists without
the defining moral and political burden
of the last fifty-seven years.
With both: Israel is the country
that declared independence in a founding document
that promised complete equality of social and political rights
to all its inhabitants irrespective of religion, race or sex —
and that has governed 5 million Palestinians
in the West Bank and Gaza
without granting them those rights
for fifty-seven years.
The gap between the declaration and the occupation
is the load-bearing contradiction
of Israeli political life:
every Israeli political argument —
left, right, religious, secular —
is an argument about what to do with the gap.
Remove the Declaration: no state.
Remove the Occupation: no contradiction of this scale.
With both: a democracy that is also an occupation,
a state that was founded on a promise of equality
and has not delivered it to everyone under its control,
a position that holds its founding idealism
and its daily practice
in the same two hands
and cannot put either one down.
The October 7, 2023 attack and the Gaza war that followed
did not create the contradiction.
They compressed it.

## Apparent
1897: the First Zionist Congress. Basel. Herzl.
The political project of Jewish statehood.
1917: the Balfour Declaration. Britain supports a Jewish homeland in Palestine.
The Arab majority in Palestine: not consulted.
1920s–1940s: Jewish immigration to Mandatory Palestine.
Arab-Jewish violence escalates.
1947: the UN Partition Plan. Arab states reject it.
May 14, 1948: Israel declares independence.
The War of Independence / the Nakba.
700,000 Palestinians displaced.
Israel survives. Expands.
1967: the Six-Day War. Israel captures the West Bank, Gaza, Sinai, Golan Heights.
The occupation of the West Bank and Gaza begins.
It has not ended.
1973: the Yom Kippur War. Near-defeat. The trauma.
1978: the Camp David Accords. Peace with Egypt.
1993: Oslo. The handshake. The two-state framework.
The framework that has not produced a state.
2005: Israel withdraws from Gaza.
Hamas takes control of Gaza in 2007.
October 7, 2023: the Hamas attack. 1,200 killed. 251 taken.
The Gaza war. Tens of thousands of Palestinian dead.
The hostages: some released, some still held.
The two-state solution: discussed by everyone,
implemented by no one.
The wall: still standing.
The declaration: still in force.
The occupation: still continuing.
The argument: still unresolved.
