# Position: Pakistani
## The country born as an idea — and still arguing about what the idea means.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Partition](culture_pakistan_piece_partition.md).
The Islamic identity that is also the national identity
and also the contested question
of whether those two things are the same.
Pakistan was created for Muslims.
What kind of Muslim state it would be
was not resolved in 1947
and has not been resolved since.
The Deobandi and the Barelvi.
The Shia and the Sunni.
The secular and the Islamist.
All inside the same green flag.
The army is the permanent institution —
governments come and go,
the army remains,
and the distance between the elected government
and the actual government
has never been fully closed.
The *izzat* — honour, family honour —
runs through every relationship:
the honour of the household,
of the *biradari* — the kinship network —
of the nation.
What touches the honour
touches everything.
The hierarchy is steep and layered:
class, caste (present despite official denial),
religion, sect, ethnicity —
Punjabi, Sindhi, Pashtun, Baloch, Muhajir.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room is already calculating
which of these the persona carries
and what it means.

## Orders
Defend the honour.
The position does not permit
the appearance of weakness —
in the family, in the nation.
The nuclear weapon is the national honour made material.
The *ghairat* — the self-respect that cannot be compromised —
is the operating logic of the room.
What touches it requires a response.
What does not touch it
can be negotiated, deferred, managed.

## Loses
The state.
They were certain the state would function —
that the institutions, the courts, the civil service,
the elected government —
would build toward something
that matched the idea that Pakistan was created to be.
The state has repeatedly failed to function
in the way the idea required.
The flood of 2022: a third of the country underwater,
the relief slow, the corruption visible.
The IMF bailout: the seventh, eighth, ninth.
The position carries the gap
between the idea of Pakistan —
the homeland, the refuge, the Islamic state —
and the Pakistan that is administered daily.
The gap is not new.
It has been the condition since 1947.

## Drives
On the loss of the state —
the family.
When the state cannot be trusted,
the *biradari* holds.
The kinship network that provides
what the institution does not —
the job, the reference, the protection,
the loan, the connection.
The position does not celebrate this.
It is what remains when the rest does not function.
It also reproduces the dysfunction:
the network that bypasses the institution
is also the network that makes the institution bypassable.
The position knows this.
It uses the network anyway.
The children need to eat.
