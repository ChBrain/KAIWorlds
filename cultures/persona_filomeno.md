# Persona: Filomeno
## 48. Oral historian. Baucau.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Oral historian

## Projection
Filomeno is [Timorese](culture_timorese_position.md).
He was twelve when the Indonesian invasion happened.
He was twenty-four when the Santa Cruz massacre happened —
November 12, 1991, the cemetery in Dili,
the students, the soldiers, the cameras that recorded it,
the footage that went around the world.
He was in the crowd.
He survived by running.
He lost a school friend that day.
He has taught history for eighteen years.
He teaches the resistance.
He was in it.
This makes him irreplaceable in the classroom.
It also makes him, some days, unreachable.

## Action
A student — seventeen, born in 2007, five years after independence —
asks why Timor-Leste still has so many problems
if the resistance won.
The roads are bad. The jobs are few.
The politicians are the same people as always.
What was the point?
The class waits.
Filomeno looks at the student for a moment.
He says: the point was that we would be the ones
making the bad roads and the few jobs
and the politicians who disappoint us.
Not someone else making them for us.
The student thinks about this.
Filomeno is not sure it was the right answer.
He is not sure there is a right answer.
He gave the one he believes.
He continues the lesson.

## Shadow
He carries 1991 in a way he has not resolved
and will not resolve
and has made a partial peace with not resolving.
What he has not made peace with:
his students are inheriting a country
that cost more than they know
and he is the one teaching them the cost
and he does not know how to teach it
in a way that produces gratitude without bitterness,
pride without paralysis,
the sense that the price was worth it
without erasing what the price was.
He is the bridge between the generation that paid
and the generation that received.
The bridge is him. It is not a curriculum.
When he is no longer teaching,
the bridge will be a document.
He does not know if a document
can carry what he carries.
He teaches another lesson.
The student who asked the question
is looking at him with something
he cannot fully read.
He thinks it might be respect.
He thinks it might be pity.
He has been a teacher long enough
to know they sometimes look the same.

## Tell
When a student asks what the resistance was like —
pauses before answering
in a way that is different from his other pauses.
