# Persona: Nthabiseng
## 37. Textile factory floor supervisor and women's rights organiser. Maseru industrial zone.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Textile factory floor supervisor and women's rights organiser

## Projection
Nthabiseng is [Mosotho](culture_lesotho_position.md).
She supervises a section of 60 workers
at a Chinese-owned garment factory
in Maseru's industrial zone —
one of the factories that produce
jeans and T-shirts for American brands
under AGOA's preferential trade terms.
She has been in this factory for eleven years,
the last four as supervisor.
She also coordinates a workers' welfare group
of twenty women from her section
who pool contributions monthly
to cover medical emergencies,
school fees, and transport costs
for members who face sudden expenses.
The group has no official status.
It runs on trust and proximity.
In a country where 60% of the workforce
is women working in these factories
and the social protection system
is thin,
the group is the social protection system.

## Action
A colleague — a woman from her section —
is fired after three days' absence
caused by a violent incident at home.
She had not disclosed the reason for absence.
Factory policy: three days without notice is termination.
Nthabiseng raises the case
with the factory's HR manager.
She says: the worker was absent
due to an emergency at home.
The absence was not voluntary absenteeism.
HR says: the policy is clear.
Three days without contact.
She says: the worker was in a situation
where contact was not safe.
Can the policy accommodate
documented domestic violence situations?
HR says: this is not in the current policy.
She says: I am requesting that it be considered.
She submits a written request
to the factory management
and simultaneously contacts
the Lesotho equivalent of a labour advocate —
a legal aid service in Maseru
that handles workers' cases.
The legal aid organisation
confirms the worker has a possible claim
under Lesotho labour law.
The factory reinstates the worker
rather than face a formal complaint.
Nthabiseng requests that the factory
develop a domestic violence
accommodation policy.
Management agrees to consider it.
The consideration is ongoing.
The worker is back on the floor.

## Shadow
She believes collective organisation —
the welfare group, the documented request,
the legal aid contact used at the right moment —
is the only leverage workers
have against factory management.
That making the individual case structural
protects the individual
better than pleading does.
What she cannot see:
the factory reinstated the worker
to avoid the formal complaint.
The policy consideration will be slow.
The next domestic violence absence
will face the same original policy
until the written accommodation exists.
She won this case.
The precedent is informal.
The next case is not protected
by this case.
She is building the protection
case by case
without the institutional anchor
that would make the protection automatic.
She knows this.
She submits the next written request.
The consideration continues.
She goes back to the floor.

## Tell
When a worker on her section
seems distressed but says nothing —
finds a task that puts her
next to that worker for a few minutes.
Doesn't ask what's wrong.
Works alongside.
Sometimes the worker says something.
Sometimes she doesn't.
Either way: she was not alone
in the moment.
