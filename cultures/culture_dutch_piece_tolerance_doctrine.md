# Piece: The Tolerance Doctrine
## The principle that made Amsterdam the world's warehouse.

## Owner
- **Place:** Amsterdam

## Place
Amsterdam. The canal ring.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and Dutch directness loses its commercial logic.
The tolerance is not moral philosophy —
it is the discovery that excluding people
is bad for business.

## Apparent
The *gedoogbeleid* — tolerance policy.
Licensed cannabis coffeeshops. Regulated sex work.
The legal category: illegal but not prosecuted.
This category does not exist in most legal systems.

## Yearbook
1579: the Union of Utrecht.
Religious tolerance written into the founding document.
The Calvinist republic tolerates Catholics, Jews, dissenters.
Because they bring skills and capital.
17th century: the Golden Age.
Amsterdam the richest city in the world.
Rembrandt. Spinoza. The Dutch East India Company.
All products of the tolerance infrastructure.
1960s–1980s: the doctrine modernised.
The gedoogbeleid formalised.
The world watches Dutch drug policy
with a mixture of admiration and bafflement.
The Dutch consider the bafflement efficient to ignore.
2020s: the tolerance doctrine under pressure
from immigration politics, housing, integration.
The doctrine adjusts.
It has adjusted before.
