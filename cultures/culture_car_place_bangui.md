## Location: Central African Republic

## Shown
- The Ubangi River.
  The southern border with DRC.
  Wide, brown, slow.
  The dugout canoes.
  The women washing.
  The city's edge.
  The river is the city's most reliable feature —
  it has not moved, it has not been seized,
  it has not changed governments.
  Bangui looks to it
  the way a city looks to what endures.
- The Marché Central.
  Corrugated iron and daylight.
  The vendors selling everything.
  The baguettes on wire baskets.
  The sellers moving through
  making commerce work
  in a city that has been through
  four governments, two civil wars,
  and a Russian mercenary occupation
  since 1990.
  The market ran through all of it.
  It is running now.
- The Palais de la Renaissance.
  The presidential palace.
  Built by Bokassa for his coronation.
  Extravagant, partially run-down,
  still the seat of the presidency.
  The building that Bokassa built
  with a third of the national budget
  to crown himself
  is the building where Touadéra governs.
  The CAR uses what is there.
  It is not in a position to rebuild from scratch.
  Nothing here is built from scratch.
- The Cathédrale Notre-Dame.
  Where displaced civilians took shelter in 2013
  when Séléka took Bangui.
  Where bodies were found in the grounds.
  Where mass is held every Sunday.
  The church as the institution
  that outlasts the government.
  The Central African church
  — Catholic, Protestant, evangelical —
  is the most consistent social infrastructure
  the country has.
  It runs clinics. It runs schools.
  It provides what the state withdraws from.
  This is not admirable. It is necessary.
  The state's withdrawal is the condition.
- The Dzanga-Sangha entrance.
  500 kilometres to the southwest.
  Where the forest begins properly —
  the dense equatorial forest
  with its forest elephants and baka communities.
  Where international conservation NGOs operate
  alongside the armed groups
  and the Wagner remnants
  and the community hunting traditions
  that predate all of them.
  The forest exists.
  It is contested.
  It is still the forest.
