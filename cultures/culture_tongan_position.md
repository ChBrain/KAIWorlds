# Position: Tongan
## The only Pacific nation never colonised — because the king was shrewd enough to make colonisation unnecessary.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Friendly Islands and the Constitution](culture_tongan_piece_constitution.md).
The Kingdom of Tonga.
The only monarchy in the Pacific.
The only Pacific nation that was never formally colonised.
King Taufa'ahau Tupou I — who unified the islands,
converted to Christianity,
and wrote the constitution of 1875 —
is the reason.
He made Tonga legible to the colonial powers
on Tongan terms.
The *fahu* system: the matrilineal rank
that runs alongside the patrilineal political structure.
The father's sister (*mehekitanga*)
holds the highest rank in any family gathering —
she is *fahu* over her brother's children.
In a society where the king rules the politics,
women rule the ceremony.
The church: Wesleyan Methodist,
brought by the same Tupou I
who wrote the constitution.
The church attendance: near-universal.
The Sunday observance: constitutional.
It is illegal in Tonga to work on Sunday.
The constitution that protects Sunday
also protects the land:
no land can be sold to foreigners.
Every Tongan male is entitled
to a land allotment from the king.
The theory: no Tongan need go landless.
The practice: the allotments are insufficient
and many are not allocated.
The diaspora: Tonga has more people outside it
than inside.
100,000 in Tonga.
200,000+ in New Zealand, Australia, United States.
The remittances: 40% of GDP.
The highest remittance dependency
in the Pacific.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room knows your noble,
your district, your church.
The room observes the Sunday rules.

## Orders
Honour the hierarchy.
Tonga is one of the most stratified societies
in Polynesia.
The king. The nobles. The commoners.
The hierarchy is not merely traditional —
it is constitutional.
The nobles hold the land.
The commoners lease it.
The reform movement of the 2000s
produced a more democratic constitution in 2010 —
commoners can now hold a majority
in the Legislative Assembly.
The king retains significant power.
The nobles retain the land.
The hierarchy adjusted.
It did not end.

## Loses
The young men.
The rugby players who play for New Zealand,
for Australia, for England.
The nurses in Auckland.
The construction workers in Brisbane.
The tides of departure
that the remittances flow back from.
The position sends its people
and funds itself from their absence.
This is stable. It is also a choice
made by necessity
that looks, from some angles,
like a plan.

## Drives
On the loss — the *kava* circle.
The kava ceremony: the men,
the bowl, the coconut shell cup,
the protocol of service.
The conversation that happens
around the kava circle
is the real governance of the village.
The disputes resolved.
The alliances formed.
The elders heard.
The kava circle operates
wherever Tongans gather —
in Auckland, in Los Angeles, in Nuku'alofa.
The *fa'atongan* — the Tongan way —
travels.
It does not require the island.
It requires the circle.
