# Persona: Tarek
## 55. Bus driver. Cairo Ring Road.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Bus driver

## Projection
Tarek is [Egyptian](culture_egyptian_position.md).
He drives the 6 AM to 2 PM shift
on a route that runs from Shubra
along the Ring Road to Maadi and back.
He has driven this route for nineteen years.
He knows the traffic by the hour,
the police checkpoints by their shift changes,
and the passengers who ride every day
well enough to know when something is wrong with them.
He has a wife, three children,
and a mother-in-law in the same apartment in Shubra.
The apartment has four rooms.
In the evenings he listens to Quranic recitation on his phone.
He has a photograph of himself in Tahrir in 2011
folded in his wallet behind his licence.
The photograph is not for showing.
It is for knowing.

## Action
A passenger boards with a counterfeit ten-pound note.
Tarek notices when he checks the fare.
The passenger — a young man, early twenties, shabby coat —
does not look like he knows the note is fake.
Tarek pockets the note, waves the young man to a seat,
and does not report the shortage at the end of the shift.
He covers it from his own pocket.
It is less than one pound sterling.
At the end of the shift his supervisor
mentions the float is short.
Tarek says he miscounted.
His supervisor looks at him for a moment.
Says nothing.
They have worked together for eleven years.

## Shadow
He believes that the young man probably did not know —
that poverty in Cairo produces situations
where a bad note circulates
through enough hands that no one can say
at which point it was knowingly passed.
He chose to absorb the cost rather than cause trouble.
What he cannot see:
he makes this choice regularly —
not always with counterfeit notes,
but with anything that would require him
to escalate to an authority
he does not fully trust to act well.
He absorbs. He covers. He moves on.
He has convinced himself this is kindness.
It is also the calculation of a man
who has lived long enough under various authorities
to know that involving them
often makes the small thing larger
and never makes it better.
The photograph in his wallet is evidence
that he was once willing to demand better.
The calculation in his pocket is evidence
of how the eighteen days ended.
Both are true.
He has not found a way to hold both
without one of them hurting.

## Tell
When something on the bus requires a decision
he does not want to make —
adjusts the rearview mirror,
checks the road behind him,
and accelerates slightly.
