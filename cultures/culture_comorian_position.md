# Position: Comorian
## The archipelago that has had more coups than elections — and more Islam than the continent that surrounds it.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Coups and the Diaspora in Marseille](culture_comorian_piece_coups.md).
Three islands — Grande Comore, Anjouan, Mohéli —
and a fourth, Mayotte, that voted to remain French
and which Comoros has claimed ever since.
The smallest country in Africa by area.
One of the poorest in the world.
Arabic, French, Shikomori — the Comorian language,
Bantu with Arabic and French woven through it.
The Islam: nearly 100% Sunni.
The most Islamic country in sub-Saharan Africa.
The *Anda* — the grand marriage —
the ceremony that defines social status:
a man who has not performed the *Anda*
is not fully an adult in Comorian society,
regardless of age or achievement.
The *Anda* requires years of saving,
of contribution from the extended family,
of the accumulated social capital
of a community that does not forget
who gave what.
The ceremony can cost more than a house.
The position performs it anyway.
The politics: twenty coups or coup attempts
since independence in 1975.
Including one led by a French mercenary, Bob Denard,
who converted to Islam, took a Comorian wife,
and ran the islands effectively for a decade.
The coups are not chaos —
they are the form that political competition takes
in a country where the formal institutions
have never been strong enough
to channel it otherwise.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room knows which island.
The room knows whether the *Anda* has been performed.

## Orders
Hold the family and prepare the *Anda*.
The position does not navigate Comorian society
without the network of obligation and reciprocity
that the *Anda* system produces.
The man in Marseille who sends money home
for his brother's *Anda*
is not just meeting a social obligation —
he is maintaining his position in the network
that will receive him when he returns,
that will support his children when they leave,
that will bury him when the time comes.
The *Anda* is expensive.
The *Anda* is the price of belonging.

## Loses
The fourth island.
Mayotte voted French in 1974 — twice.
Comoros declared independence including Mayotte.
France kept Mayotte.
The UN has passed resolution after resolution
calling for Comorian sovereignty.
France vetoes or ignores them.
Mayotte is now a French department,
the richest territory in the region,
a magnet for migrants from Comoros and Madagascar.
The position carries the specific irredentism
of the flag that shows four stars
for four islands
when only three are governed from Moroni.
The fourth star is the claim.
The claim is fifty years old.
It shows no sign of resolution.

## Drives
On the loss — the diaspora.
The Comorians of Marseille:
the largest Comorian community outside the archipelago,
concentrated in the northern districts of the city,
maintaining Shikomori, maintaining the *Anda* across the water,
sending money home, returning for the ceremony.
The diaspora does not dissolve into France.
It maintains Comoros in France.
This is not failure of integration.
It is the Comorian response to dispersal:
the network holds even when the island cannot.
The remittances are 25% of GDP.
The *Anda* still happens.
The fourth star is still on the flag.
