# Persona: Ibrahim
## 41. Resort island operations manager. North Malé Atoll.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Resort island operations manager

## Projection
Ibrahim is [Maldivian](culture_maldivian_position.md).
He manages the operations of a luxury resort —
water villas, reef snorkelling,
$1,200 per night, full occupancy nine months of the year.
The guests come from Germany, China, the UK, the Gulf.
They come to see the reef.
He has worked in tourism for sixteen years.
He manages 140 staff, the supply chain,
the water desalination plant,
the diesel generators that power the island,
the waste management,
the coral restoration project
that the resort funds and markets.
The coral restoration project is real.
It restores approximately 200 coral fragments per month.
The 2024 bleaching event killed approximately
60% of the reef surrounding the resort.
200 fragments per month.
He knows the mathematics.

## Action
A German journalist visits for a feature
on sustainable luxury tourism.
She asks about the coral restoration programme.
Ibrahim walks her through it —
the coral nurseries, the fragments, the survival rate,
the sections of reef that are recovering.
She asks: is it enough?
He says: every fragment matters.
She asks again: is the resort's net impact on the reef positive?
He pauses.
He says: the restoration programme is the most active
in the northern atolls.
She writes this down.
He has answered a different question than she asked.
He knows this.
She may know it too.
The article will say the resort has the most active
coral restoration programme in the northern atolls.
This will be true.

## Shadow
The resort he runs is powered by diesel generators
that burn approximately 800,000 litres of fuel per year.
The guests fly here — long-haul, mostly —
producing emissions that dwarf
the resort's coral restoration offset.
The resort markets itself as sustainable.
The sustainability certification is real
and covers what it covers.
It does not cover the flights.
The flights are in someone else's column.
Ibrahim is Maldivian.
His country is sinking because of the emissions.
He runs a business that attracts people
who produce emissions to visit a sinking country
to see a reef that is dying because of emissions.
He has framed this many ways over sixteen years.
The framing that allows him to continue:
the tourism funds the government
that funds the sea wall
that gives his country more time.
The tourists' presence makes the Maldives visible
in the rooms where climate decisions are made.
The visibility is leverage.
He believes this.
He also knows that 200 coral fragments per month
is not leverage.
It is the thing he does
so that when the journalist asks
he has something to show her
that is true
and not the whole truth
and allows everyone to continue.
He walks her to the nursery.
The fragments are healthy.
He is proud of the fragments.
He is not proud of the column
the fragments are in.

## Tell
When a guest asks if the reef will survive —
answers about what the resort is doing
before answering about the reef.
