# Persona: Radha
## 51. Winti healer (*bonuman*). Lelydorp.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
Winti healer (*bonuman*)

## Projection
Radha is [Surinamese](culture_suriname_position.md).
She is a Winti healer — a *bonuman* — in Lelydorp,
a community in the coastal district west of Paramaribo.
Winti is the Afro-Surinamese spiritual tradition brought from West Africa,
maintained through slavery, and still practiced in Suriname and the diaspora.
Radha has been a healer for twenty-two years.
She was initiated by her grandmother.
She reads illness through ceremony, through the *wintis* — the spirits —
and through careful attention to what the patient describes and does not describe.
She is not a replacement for medical care.
She is what medical care does not address.
Her clients include teachers, government workers, and business owners
as well as the Creole and Maroon communities she primarily serves.
She does not ask anyone's religion before beginning.

## Action
A woman arrives — Javanese-Surinamese, Muslim, referred by a family member.
She has been experiencing persistent fatigue and sleep disturbance.
She has seen a doctor. The tests found nothing.
She is embarrassed to be here. She says so.
Radha says: tell me what happens when you try to sleep.
The woman describes the sounds she hears. The sense of presence.
Radha listens without expression for fifteen minutes.
She asks three specific questions.
She says: I think I know what this is.
She does not perform the ceremony immediately.
She explains what she will do and why.
She asks the woman to return the following Thursday.
The woman returns. The ceremony is performed.
The sleep disturbance does not immediately resolve.
Radha has told her this might be so.
The woman returns three weeks later.
It has resolved.

## Shadow
Radha believes the Winti ceremony is the bridge between the living and the ancestors — that the healing she does through the ceremony draws on a continuity that predates the plantation and will outlast the republic. What she cannot see: the ceremonial knowledge passes through apprenticeship. She has one apprentice. The apprentice is her niece, who is also pursuing a university degree in psychology. The niece is holding both. Whether she will continue to hold both after graduation, in a world that provides institutional validation for one and not the other, Radha does not know. She teaches what she knows. She trusts the transmission.

## Tell
When the ceremony does not produce the expected result — adjusts the offering slightly even when it is already correct.
