# Piece: The Gas and the Ambition
## The North Field. The largest natural gas reserve in the world. And the decision of what to do with it.

## Owner
- **Place:** Doha

## Place
Doha. The Corniche.
The dhow harbour in the foreground.
The West Bay skyline behind it —
the glass towers that went up
in fifteen years.
The Museum of Islamic Art
on its artificial peninsula,
I.M. Pei's last major commission.
The dhow and the towers and the museum.
The three Qatars in one frame.

## Load Bearing
Remove the North Field gas reserve
and Qatar is the smallest Gulf state —
11,000 square kilometres,
no oil to speak of,
dominated by its larger neighbours.
With it: Qatar has the third largest natural gas reserves
in the world (some calculations put it first).
The LNG export programme —
the liquefied natural gas that Europe
scrambled for after the Ukraine invasion —
made Qatar indispensable to European energy security.
Indispensability is Qatar's strategy.
The gas is the foundation of the strategy.
The strategy has produced:
Al Jazeera (1996) — the media arm.
Qatar Foundation (1995) — the education arm.
Education City — Cornell Medicine, Georgetown, Northwestern, others.
The Hamas political bureau in Doha — the diplomatic arm
that makes Qatar the necessary address
for any Gaza negotiation.
The World Cup 2022 — the visibility arm.
The $300 billion sovereign wealth fund — the financial arm.
Each arm serves the strategy: be too useful to ignore.
Remove the piece: a small Gulf emirate
with ambitions it cannot fund.
With it: a small Gulf emirate
that turned its gas into the most sophisticated
small-state foreign policy operation
in the world —
and that will spend the next thirty years
deciding whether the strategy
was wisdom or the specific hubris
of a position that confused
being indispensable
with being immune.

## Apparent
1971: independence from Britain.
1995: Sheikh Hamad takes power in a bloodless coup against his father.
Al Jazeera founded. The education and investment policy begins.
2003: the US military's Central Command moves to Qatar.
2006: the Asian Games. The first major international event.
2010: Qatar wins the FIFA World Cup bid.
2017: the blockade. Saudi Arabia, UAE, Bahrain, Egypt sever relations.
The demand: close Al Jazeera, expel the Muslim Brotherhood connections,
align foreign policy with Saudi Arabia.
Qatar refuses.
2021: the blockade ends. Qatar did not change its policies.
2022: the World Cup. 1.4 million visitors. 
The kafala investigations. The death toll of migrant workers:
disputed, significant.
The World Cup went ahead.
2023: Qatar mediates for Gaza hostages.
The ceasefire negotiations that produced partial releases.
The negotiations that produced the January 2025 ceasefire.
Qatar as the address for Hamas-Israel negotiations.
The gas: still flowing to Europe.
The ambition: still projecting.
