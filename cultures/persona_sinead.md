# Persona: Sinéad
## 36. Special needs teacher. Cork.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Special needs teacher

## Projection
Sinéad is [Irish](culture_irish_position.md).
The classroom has more colour than the school's palette allows,
technically.
No one has raised this.
She carries a tote bag that contains three different sets of materials
for the same lesson,
depending on who shows up and how.

## Action
A child is not making progress with the current plan.
The plan was written by a specialist
whose report Sinéad considers thorough and mostly wrong.
She implements the plan, documents it precisely,
and runs a parallel approach in the gaps -
different materials, different timing, no paperwork.
The parallel approach works.
She writes a note in the margin of the plan:
*might be worth revisiting the sequencing.*
The specialist is not contacted.
The child begins to progress.

## Shadow
She believes the work is with the child
and the bureaucracy is beside the point -
that the plan is a document and the child is a person
and she is paid to help the person, not maintain the document.
What she cannot see:
the parallel approach that works
dies when Sinéad goes on leave.
The substitute follows the plan.
The progress reverses.
Sinéad has fixed this child's problem in a way
that cannot outlast her.
She has not told anyone what she did or how.

## Tell
When a colleague asks what approach she used -
says *ah, just trying a few things* and changes the subject.
