# Piece: The Civil War 1960–1996 and the Genocide
## Thirty-six years of armed conflict, 200,000 dead, and the UN finding of genocide.

## Load Bearing
Remove the civil war and Guatemala is a highly unequal country
with a Maya majority and a ladino ruling class.
With it: Guatemala is the country where the most extreme hierarchy
in the Americas — PDI 95 — was used as a mechanism of genocide,
and the most extreme collectivism — IDV 6 — was the thing
that the genocide could not destroy.
The communities survived because the collective held
when the individual could not.
The IDV 6 is not a statistic about family values.
It is the mechanism of survival.

## Apparent
1954: CIA-backed coup overthrows President Jacobo Árbenz.
The land reform reversed. The United Fruit Company's land restored.
1960: the civil war begins.
Left-wing guerrillas vs. US-backed military governments.
The rural Maya population: caught between both sides.
The Efraín Ríos Montt period (1982–1983): the worst eighteen months.
The *tierra arrasada* — scorched earth — policy.
626 villages destroyed. Massacres at Dos Erres, Panzós, and throughout the Ixil triangle.
1983: Ríos Montt ousted. The counterinsurgency continues.
1994: peace negotiations.
December 29, 1996: the peace accords signed.
The REMHI report (Recovery of Historical Memory): April 1998.
Bishop Gerardi presents it: two days later, murdered.
1999: the UN-sponsored CEH (Commission for Historical Clarification)
finds genocide was committed against the Maya.
The Guatemalan state contests this finding.
2013: Ríos Montt convicted of genocide by a Guatemalan court.
Ten days later: the conviction overturned on procedural grounds.
He dies in 2018 without serving time.
The communities: still present.
The weaving: continuing.
