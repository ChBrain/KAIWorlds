# Piece: The Homeland War
## The war Croatia fought to exist as it had chosen to exist.

## Owner
- **Place:** Zagreb

## Place
Zagreb. The Croatian War of Independence Memorial. Also: Vukovar.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Croatian position's westward choice becomes a preference.
The Homeland War is the cost at which the choice was made —
the western orientation tested under fire and held.

## Apparent
The Vukovar water tower. Shrapnel holes preserved.
Official war memorial designation.
The town rebuilt around the deliberate ruin.

## Yearbook
June 25, 1991: Croatia declares independence.
June 27: the Yugoslav People's Army moves.
The war begins.
1991: Vukovar besieged for 87 days. Falls in November.
The city becomes a symbol.
1992: international recognition.
The war continues.
1995: Operation Storm. Croatian forces retake the Krajina.
The war ends.
The Dayton Agreement.
Croatia exists as it declared in 1991.
1996–2013: reconstruction, EU accession negotiations.
2013: EU membership.
2023: Schengen area. Euro currency.
The western integration complete.
The Vukovar water tower still standing with its holes.
The choice confirmed.
The scar kept visible.
