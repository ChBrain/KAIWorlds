# Place: Kingstown / Baliceaux, Saint Vincent and the Grenadines
## The capital and the uninhabited island the diaspora returns to.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas
- **Position:** [Vincentian](culture_vincentian_position.md)

## Shown
Kingstown: the capital. The Botanical Gardens — established 1765,
the oldest botanical gardens in the western hemisphere.
The breadfruit trees: brought from Tahiti by Captain Bligh on the *Providence* in 1793,
after the *Bounty* mutiny. The breadfruit that now grows across the Caribbean arrived here first.
The leeward coast. The villages that faced the Atlantic side.
Where the Black Carib settlements were. Where the wars were fought. Where Chatoyer was killed.
Baliceaux. The small island visible from Bequia on a clear day.
Uninhabited except for cattle. No permanent structures.
The beach where the Garifuna pilgrims land each March.
The Tobago Cays. The southern Grenadines — five uninhabited islets
surrounded by a horseshoe reef. A marine park. Sea turtles nesting.

## Offers
The room where the Vincentian position does its daily work.
*The March Pilgrimage* — the annual Garifuna return to Baliceaux;
runs here each March as the boats go out and the diaspora arrives.

## Withheld
The specific weight of being the island that the Garifuna call the motherland —
and the relationship between the contemporary Afro-Vincentian majority
and the Garifuna identity that was expelled from the island 228 years ago.
The room shows Baliceaux. It does not perform the complexity.
