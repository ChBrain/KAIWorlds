# Persona: Claudette
## 49. Cricket coach. Kingston.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Cricket coach

## Projection
Claudette is [Jamaican](culture_jamaica_position.md).
She coaches the under-seventeen women's cricket team
at a secondary school in New Kingston.
The pitch is uneven.
The equipment is shared with the boys' team
and is not always available.
She began playing at nine.
She has coached for eighteen years.
Her daughter plays for the national under-nineteen team.
She did not plan this.
She considers it evidence of something
she does not name because naming it
would make it smaller.

## Action
A girl — fifteen, talented, uncertain —
comes to practice for the first time.
She stands at the boundary and watches.
Claudette does not call her in.
She coaches the rest of the session.
At the end she walks to the girl and hands her a bat.
She says: come back Tuesday.
Not: welcome. Not: we're glad you're here.
Come back Tuesday.
The girl comes back Tuesday.
She is on the first eleven by March.

## Shadow
She believes the invitation is in the doing —
that handing a bat and saying Tuesday
is the fullest welcome she can give,
and that the girl who responds to it
is the girl who belongs.
What she cannot see:
the girl who does not come back Tuesday
also belonged.
She did not respond to this particular form of invitation.
Claudette has been inviting this way for eighteen years.
She does not know how many girls did not come back
who would have stayed if the invitation had been different.
She has never counted the ones who left.
She counts the ones who stayed.

## Tell
When a new player hesitates at the boundary —
keeps coaching the others
without looking away from the game.
