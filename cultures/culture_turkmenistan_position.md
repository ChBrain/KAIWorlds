# Position: Turkmenistani
## The hermit state of Central Asia — sealed, gassed, and gilded.

## Has
[The Gas and the Cult](culture_turkmenistan_piece_gas.md).
The total enclosure.
Turkmenistan is the North Korea of Central Asia —
the comparison is imprecise and useful.
Imprecise because the material conditions differ:
the gas revenue provides a standard of living
that North Korea cannot.
Useful because the information control,
the personality cult, the sealed borders,
the state that tells the citizen
what the world is and who they are —
these are structurally similar.
The Turkmen position was formed
in the nomadic steppe,
in the carpet weaving,
in the horse — the Akhal-Teke,
the golden horse of Turkmenistan,
the national symbol alongside the carpet.
The Soviets settled the nomads.
The independence produced Niyazov —
Turkmenbashi, Father of all Turkmens —
who renamed months after himself,
banned beards and opera,
built a golden rotating statue of himself in Ashgabat,
and produced the *Ruhnama* —
the Book of the Soul —
as the compulsory text for every exam,
every school, every mosque.
Niyazov died in 2006.
His successor, Berdymukhamedov,
removed some of the excesses
and replaced them with his own.
His son now governs.
The gas continues.
The enclosure continues.
The position inside the enclosure
has learned what positions learn
inside total systems:
the surface is managed,
the interior is protected,
the two are not the same.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room is watching.
The room is always watching.

## Orders
Perform the loyalty.
The Turkmenistani position has had
thirty-three years of practice
since independence.
The *Ruhnama* recitation.
The white marble city.
The president's portrait.
The form is maintained
with the precision of people
who know the cost of not maintaining it.
The cost is specific and documented
in the testimony of those who have left
and speak from outside the border.
Inside: the form is maintained.

## Loses
The reference point.
The Turkmenistani position is more isolated
than almost any position on earth.
The satellite dish was banned.
The internet is filtered to near-uselessness.
The VPN users are tracked.
The position knows a version of the outside world
that has been curated by the state.
When the reference point shifts —
the relative who calls from Turkey,
the contraband phone, the moment the curtain moves —
the position must recalibrate everything
it thought it knew.
This is disorienting.
It is also, in the accounts of those who have left,
described as a liberation
that feels like falling.
The reference point was wrong.
Everything built on it must be rebuilt.
The position does not do this quickly.

## Drives
On the loss of the reference point —
cautious recalibration.
The position does not act immediately
on new knowledge.
It has been trained by the system
to be careful about what it shows to whom.
The new knowledge is held internally.
The performance continues externally.
The gap between them
is the position's private life —
the only private life the system has not reached.
Yet.
