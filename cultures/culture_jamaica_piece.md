# Piece: Reggae and Rastafari
## The music and the philosophy that turned oppression into a cosmology of freedom.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas
- **Place:** Kingston

## Place
Kingston. Trench Town.
Studio One. Channel One. Tuff Gong.
Also: everywhere reggae has ever played,
which is everywhere.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Jamaican UAI 13 is inexplicable.
How does a society with Jamaica's history —
slavery, colonialism, persistent poverty, high violence —
produce the second-lowest uncertainty avoidance score in the world?
The answer is not that Jamaica has no problems.
It is that Jamaica developed a spiritual and cultural framework
that converts uncertainty into the operating condition of the righteous.
Rastafari says: Babylon — the system of oppression — is temporary.
Reggae says: in the meantime, one love.
The music and the philosophy together produce
a population that has learned to hold uncertainty
not as threat but as the space where freedom operates.
Remove reggae and Rastafari
and Jamaica is a small island with a difficult history.
With them, Jamaica is the country that gave the world
a way of thinking about freedom
that has been adopted everywhere from Bristol to Tokyo to Lagos.
The piece is not nostalgia.
It is the ongoing operating logic.

## Apparent
Reggae. Ska before it. Rocksteady between.
Kingston studios: 1960s. The music arrives from the yards.
Bob Marley. Peter Tosh. Bunny Wailer.
Jimmy Cliff. Toots Hibbert.
The Rastafari movement: 1930s. Marcus Garvey's prophecy.
Haile Selassie's coronation in Ethiopia: 1930.
The *dread* — the locs that cannot be cut.
The colours: red, gold, green.
Ethiopia's flag. Africa as origin and aspiration.
Babylon: any system of oppression.
*Jah*: God, presence, the force of righteousness.
*Ital*: pure, natural, unprocessed.
*One love*: the greeting and the cosmology simultaneously.
UNESCO: reggae inscribed on the Intangible Cultural Heritage list, 2018.
The music that started in Kingston yards
is now officially a world heritage.
The yards have not changed.

## Yearbook
1494: Columbus arrives. The Taíno people are here.
Within a generation: gone.
1655: British capture Jamaica from the Spanish.
Enslaved Africans brought to work the sugar plantations.
1739: the Maroon Treaties. Cudjoe and the British sign.
The Maroons keep their freedom. The mountains were never taken.
1838: full emancipation.
The formerly enslaved receive nothing.
The British compensate the slaveholders.
1865: the Morant Bay Rebellion. Paul Bogle. George William Gordon.
Both hanged. The rebellion is the reason Britain moved toward
Jamaican representative government.
1930: Haile Selassie crowned in Ethiopia.
Marcus Garvey's prophecy interpreted.
Rastafari begins.
1962: Jamaican independence. August 6.
The music and the philosophy are already in the streets.
1960s: ska, rocksteady, reggae emerge from Kingston yards.
1973: *Burnin'*. *Catch a Fire*. Bob Marley and the Wailers
reach the world.
The philosophy goes with the music.
1981: Bob Marley dies. He is 36.
The music and the philosophy continue without him.
They were never only him.
2018: UNESCO recognition.
The *riddim* is still being made in Kingston.
The philosophy is still being lived in the yards.
Both are the same thing.
