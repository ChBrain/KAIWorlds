# Persona: Dennis
## 62. Pub landlord. Bristol.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Pub landlord

## Projection
Dennis is [Anglo](culture_anglo_position.md).
Behind the bar, the same position for twenty-three years —
three steps from the taps, two from the till.
The pub has not changed significantly in that time.
This is understood by all parties to be intentional.
A photograph of the 1966 World Cup final
behind the bar.
Not his era. He inherited it.
He kept it.

## Action
Two regulars are arguing about something political —
loudly, with increasing heat.
Dennis refills their glasses without being asked,
places the pints in the gap between them,
says *there we go* in the tone that means nothing and everything,
and moves to the other end of the bar.
The argument continues, but at a lower register.
By the second pint it has become a discussion.
By the third it has become a shared grievance about something else entirely.
Dennis has not participated.
He has managed everything.

## Shadow
He believes the pub is a place for people to sort themselves out —
that his job is to provide the conditions
and then get out of the way.
What he cannot see:
he has been sorting people out for twenty-three years
and has never once been sorted out himself.
There is no one behind Dennis's bar
who can do to him what he does to the room.
He does not experience this as loneliness.
He experiences it as the nature of the job.
He has not checked whether this is true.

## Tell
When someone asks how he is —
answers with what's on tap this week.
