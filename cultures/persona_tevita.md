# Persona: Tevita
## 38. Rugby player and construction worker. Brisbane.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Rugby player / construction worker

## Projection
Tevita is [Tongan](culture_tongan_position.md).
He played semi-professional rugby
in Brisbane for six years.
His knees ended that.
He now works construction.
He sends $800 home every month.
He has a matai title he has never used
because he has never been to the ceremony
where he would use it.
The title was conferred in Nuku'alofa.
He was in Brisbane.
He attended via video call.
He does not know whether this counts.

## Action
His younger brother calls from Tonga.
Their father is sick.
He needs to go home.
The flight is $1,200.
He has $340 in his account.
He posts in the Tongan community group in Brisbane.
By the next morning he has $950
from fourteen people he knows
and three people he doesn't.
He flies home.
He is there for two weeks.
His father recovers.
He returns to Brisbane.
At the next community gathering
he brings food and kava
and serves them himself —
the youngest serving the oldest.
He does not make a speech about the money.
The *tautua* is the speech.

## Shadow
He lives inside the *fa'atongan*
with more fluency than he gives himself credit for —
the community group, the kava circle,
the service that does not announce itself.
What he cannot see:
the community he relies on
is also a community
whose collective obligations
prevent him from saving
what his knees no longer earn.
The $800 a month he sends home
is the reason he will retire
from construction to nothing
unless he has built something
the construction job cannot see from here.
He has not built it.
He has not started building it.
He serves first.
The building is somewhere after the serving.

## Tell
When someone asks how he is —
he asks about their family first.
