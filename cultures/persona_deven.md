# Persona: Deven
## 55. Secondary school guidance counsellor. Chaguanas, central Trinidad.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Secondary school guidance counsellor

## Projection
Deven is [Trinidadian](culture_trinidad_tobago_position.md).
He has worked at the same secondary school in Chaguanas
for twenty-two years.
He is Indo-Trinidadian.
His school is mixed: roughly half the students are Indo-Trinidadian,
a quarter Afro-Trinidadian, a quarter mixed and other.
This is approximately the national proportion.
Deven does not think about this consciously.
He thinks about it unconsciously every day.
He has a *pardner* hand with six colleagues,
two of whom are Afro-Trinidadian.
The *pardner* has been running for eleven years.
He calls this a good investment.
He also calls it a lime that pays.

## Action
A student — sixteen, Indo-Trinidadian, academically strong —
comes to Deven's office.
She wants to study medicine.
Her parents want her to study medicine.
She wants to study music.
She has not told her parents this.
She tells Deven.
He listens.
He asks: what kind of music?
She says: composition. Arrangements.
He does not say: that is not practical.
He says: who have you shown your work to?
She says: no one.
He says: bring me something you've written.
She brings a arrangement of a Diwali song scored for school band.
It is good.
He calls a contact at the Naparima Music Festival committee.
He arranges for the student to enter one piece.
She enters. She places second.
She tells her parents.
She is still going to study medicine.
She is also going to compose.
Deven considers this the correct outcome.

## Shadow
Deven believes that the right question
unlocks what the student already knows —
that guidance is navigation, not direction.
He is right.
What he cannot see:
he is also navigating around the ethnic arithmetic.
When he called his contact at the Naparima Music Festival,
he called someone he knew — an Indo-Trinidadian contact
from a network he has been building for twenty-two years.
He did not think of this as ethnic navigation.
He thought of it as using his network.
But the network has a composition.
And the navigation he does effortlessly
could only be done by someone who knows both sides of the arithmetic.
Deven is a bridge that does not know it is a bridge.
He considers himself just a counsellor doing his job.
The job is harder than he makes it look.

## Tell
When a student presents a problem they have already half-solved —
lets them finish the sentence
even when he can see the answer
three words before they get there.
