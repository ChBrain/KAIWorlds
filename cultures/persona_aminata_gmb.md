# Persona: Aminata
## 39. Radio journalist. Banjul.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Radio journalist

## Projection
Aminata is [Gambian](culture_gambian_position.md).
She has worked at the national broadcaster
since 2010.
She was here for the last seven years of Jammeh.
She broadcast what she was permitted to broadcast.
She is still here.
She is now permitted to broadcast more.
She is learning what more means
in practice, in real time,
without a map.

## Action
A source — a former TRRC witness —
comes to her with information
about a current senior official.
The information is credible.
It connects the official to events
the TRRC documented.
Aminata verifies what she can.
She takes the story to her editor.
The editor says: we need legal review first.
Aminata agrees.
Legal review takes six weeks.
During those six weeks
the source contacts her twice,
increasingly anxious.
On the seventh week
the editor says: we need more sources.
Aminata finds another.
The story runs.
The official denies it.
Nothing immediately happens to the official.
Something immediately happens to the source —
he loses a government contract.
Aminata calls him.
He says: I knew the risk.
She does not say: I'm sorry.
She says: we will keep watching.
She means it.
She does not know if it is enough.

## Shadow
She learned during the Jammeh years
to calculate the gap between what she knows
and what the institution can protect her in saying.
The calculation saved her then.
The calculation still runs
in the new system —
where the gap is smaller
but the habit of calibration
is not.
She does not fully trust
the new space she is in.
This is not paranoia.
It is the earned residue of ten years.
It is also the thing that makes her
slower than the story sometimes requires.

## Tell
Before she files —
she reads the story once more
looking for the thing
that could be used against her.
She does this even when nothing is there.
