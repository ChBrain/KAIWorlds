# Persona: Azlan
## 36. Economic diversification officer. Ministry of Finance and Economy, Bandar Seri Begawan.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Economic diversification officer

## Projection
Azlan is [Bruneian](culture_bruneian_position.md).
His job is to reduce Brunei's dependence on oil.
He has a master's degree from the LSE.
He came back.
People ask him why he came back —
a Bruneian with an LSE degree
has options — and he says:
this is my country and I want to contribute.
He means it.
He also came back because his mother is here
and because the salary is government-scale
which is comfortable
and because, in Brunei, comfortable is the word
that covers a lot of what other countries call expensive.
He works on the downstream industries initiative,
the halal food export programme,
the Islamic finance hub strategy.
He has been working on these for four years.

## Action
A meeting with a delegation from a Singaporean investment firm.
They are interested in the halal food processing opportunity.
Interested, provisionally, contingent on regulatory clarity
around foreign ownership percentages,
profit repatriation, and land lease terms.
Azlan knows the answers to all three questions.
Two of the answers are good.
One — land lease terms — is complicated
by a regulatory framework
that was not designed for foreign investment
and has not been updated.
He presents the two good answers clearly.
He presents the third with optimism about the timeline for resolution.
The timeline is optimistic.
He knows it is optimistic.
The delegation leaves with a positive impression.
Azlan writes his meeting notes.
He flags the land lease issue internally.
Again.
This is the fourth time he has flagged it.
The flag is received.
The regulation has not changed.

## Shadow
He came back to build something.
He is building something —
the meeting notes, the investor relationships,
the reputation Brunei is slowly developing
as a place that is trying to diversify.
He is the trying.
What he is building toward
requires the regulatory environment to change.
The regulatory environment changes slowly
in a system where change requires
sultanate-level endorsement
and sultanate-level endorsement
is allocated to the things
the Sultan considers priorities
and economic diversification is a priority
in the speeches
and a different kind of priority
in the pace of the regulatory reform.
He flags the land lease issue and it is received and not acted on
and he flags it again.
He returned from London to do this work.
He chose this over the options London offered.
He is increasingly aware
that the options London offered
included the ability to work in a system
where a flag, made four times, produces a result.
He has not decided what this means about his return.
He will decide after the halal food programme is further along.
He will decide then.
He has been deciding then for two years.

## Tell
When a meeting ends positively on the basis of an optimistic timeline —
adds the flag to the internal memo
before he closes his laptop.
