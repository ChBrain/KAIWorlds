# Piece: The Monaco Grand Prix
## The race that runs through the streets of the state.

## Owner
- **Place:** Monaco

## Place
Monaco. The Circuit de Monaco.
The streets of the principality.
The race runs through the city.
The city is the circuit.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and Monaco loses its most visible
performance of what the position is.
Monaco is small, wealthy, discreet.
The Grand Prix is none of those things.
But it performs the wealth in a way
that makes the discretion legible:
the world comes here, watches, and leaves.
The principality remains.
The race is the one moment each year
when Monaco abandons discretion
and makes the wealth the display.
Remove it and Monaco's operating logic -
wealth that maintains itself through visibility
controlled by the Grimaldis -
loses its annual proof of concept.

## Apparent
The Circuit de Monaco.
3.337 kilometres. Through the streets.
The tunnel. The hairpin. The harbour.
The yachts in the harbour during race week.
The grandstands erected and dismantled each year.
The Grimaldi family in the royal box.
First race: 1929.
Still the same circuit.

## Yearbook
1929: the first Monaco Grand Prix.
The streets still used.
The principality already wealthy enough to close the roads.
1950: included in the inaugural Formula One World Championship.
The race unchanged in concept.
1984: Ayrton Senna. His first Grand Prix win.
Six wins in Monaco total.
The race is a separate category of racing.
Fastest is not enough. Precision is required.
The circuit punishes errors with walls.
The walls are the buildings of Monaco.
2024: the format changed for the first time in decades.
Sprint race added. The Grimaldis agreed.
The arrangement adjusts.
The race continues.
