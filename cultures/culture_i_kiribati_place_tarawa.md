# Place: South Tarawa
## Location: Kiribati

## Owner
- *

## Shown
- The causeway. The road connecting the islets.
  Built by the Japanese during WWII.
  Now the main road of the capital.
  The ocean visible from both sides.
  At high tide: the road is sometimes wet.
  At king tide: the road is sometimes flooded.
  The infrastructure of daily life
  also being the measure of sea level rise.
- The *maneaba* of Bikenibeu.
  The large meeting house.
  The thatched roof.
  The open sides.
  The *unimwane* in their positions.
  The protocol: you enter from the side
  assigned to your clan.
  You sit in your designated place.
  You wait for the elder to speak.
  The maneaba is the constitution
  in operation.
- The WWII sites.
  The bunkers on the beach.
  The rusted tank in the lagoon.
  The memorial to the US Marines.
  5,500 dead in 76 hours of fighting.
  The island that was a Pacific crossroads
  in one war
  and is now a Pacific crossroads
  in the climate emergency.
  Both crossroads on the same sand.
- The taro pits.
  The traditional freshwater taro cultivation.
  The pits dug into the freshwater lens.
  The saltwater intrusion.
  The pits that are still producing
  and the pits that are not.
  The food security argument
  visible in the soil.
