## Shown
- The port. Europe's third largest.
  Container ships visible from the city centre.
  The Elbphilharmonie on its warehouse base.
  The new building on the old structure.
  This is how Hamburg builds.
- The Speicherstadt. Red brick warehouses on oak piles.
  The smell of coffee and spices in the wet air
  is a memory now, mostly.
  The function changed. The smell stayed a little.
- The Alster. Two lakes inside the city.
  The Inner and Outer Alster.
  Sailboats in summer.
  The city arranged itself around the water
  the way merchant cities do -
  facing it, not turning away from it.
- The Handelskammer. Since 1665.
  The chamber where Hamburg commerce
  governs itself.
  The chamber that predates the German state
  by two hundred years.
- The Reeperbahn. St. Pauli.
  The other Hamburg.
  Not contradicting the Ehrbare Kaufmann -
  present since the ships arrived
  and the sailors needed somewhere to go.
  The port city contains both.
  It always has.
- The Elbphilharmonie. Opened 2017.
  Seven years late. Ten times over budget.
  Hamburg complained throughout.
  Hamburg goes.
  It is now the most visited building in Germany.
