## Shown
- The Port Vila market.
  The kastom market on the waterfront.
  The women from the islands with their produce:
  laplap, kava, coconut, taro.
  Each woman from a different island.
  Each island a different language.
  All of them in Bislama to the buyer.
  The linguistic density made daily commerce.
- The kava bars. *Nakamals.*
  Open from dusk.
  The green kava in the coconut shell.
  The custom: you drink, you sit quietly,
  you do not talk loudly,
  you let the kava work.
  The kastom space in the modern town.
  The ni-Vanuatu and the expats
  and the tourists
  all in the same nakamal,
  all following the same protocol.
  The kastom as the equaliser.
- The Vanuatu Cultural Centre.
  The collection of kastom objects.
  The archive of languages.
  The fieldworkers programme —
  trained ni-Vanuatu who document
  their own kastom for the national archive.
  The most systematic kastom documentation
  in the Pacific.
  The building that holds
  what the Condominium didn't destroy.
- The view across the harbour.
  The islands visible.
  The volcano on Tanna — Yasur —
  the most accessible active volcano on earth.
  Tourists walk to the rim.
  The John Frum devotees
  on the other side of the island
  still wait for the American return.
  Both things are Tanna simultaneously.
