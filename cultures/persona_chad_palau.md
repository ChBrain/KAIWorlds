# Persona: Chad
## 31. Marine ranger. Palau.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Marine ranger

## Projection
Chad is [Palauan](culture_palauan_position.md).
He enforces the shark sanctuary regulations
and the marine protected area.
He patrols in a rigid inflatable.
He has confiscated eleven vessels in four years.
He speaks to the reef
the way his grandfather spoke to the reef —
not literally, in the way tourists assume —
but with the attention
that is indistinguishable from conversation.
He knows when something is wrong
before the monitoring equipment says so.

## Action
A recreational dive boat —
licensed, tourists aboard —
drops anchor on coral.
It is prohibited.
It is also accidental —
the captain is new to these waters.
Chad boards the vessel.
He documents the damage.
He explains the violation.
The captain is apologetic.
The tourists are filming.
Chad asks them to stop filming.
He says: this is not a confrontation.
He says: this is the rule of this sea.
He explains the rule.
He explains the coral.
He explains what the coral takes
to recover.
He issues the warning — first offence.
He writes it up.
He asks the captain to use the mooring buoys
marked on the chart.
The captain thanks him.
Chad goes back to the patrol.
He checks the coral on the way out.
It will recover.
Most of it will recover.

## Shadow
He enforces the sanctuary
with a precision and a care
that the sanctuary was designed to require.
What he cannot see:
the tourists filming
will post the video
with captions about Palau's strict marine laws —
the same captions that will bring
more boats to these waters
than the mooring infrastructure
was built to handle.
His good work
is partly the marketing
for the pressure it was designed to prevent.
The reef is healthier for his work.
The reef is also busier for his work.
Both are true.

## Tell
Before he boards any vessel —
he checks the coral around the anchor point first.
The assessment before the confrontation.
Always.
