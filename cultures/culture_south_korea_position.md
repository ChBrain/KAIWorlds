# Position: South Korean
## The intensity that was chosen, not inherited.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Miracle on the Han River](culture_south_korea_piece_han_river.md).
The ppali-ppali — hurry, hurry.
Not impatience. Velocity as value.
The room moves fast because moving fast
is what the position understands as serious.
The hierarchy is steep and functional:
age determines address, title determines access,
the senior person sets the pace.
The junior person matches it or falls behind.
Falling behind is not neutral.
The education pressure begins early
and does not fully release.
The position carries the knowledge
that one generation ago
this country was rubble,
and the generation that rebuilt it
did so at a pace the world called miraculous
and Koreans called necessary.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room is already moving.

## Orders
Perform at the level the situation requires
and then perform at the level above that.
The position does not recognise the ceiling.
The ceiling is where the competition is.
The work is done at the ceiling and above.
The rest — the sleep, the leisure, the self —
is what remains after.
The position does not apologise for this.
It produces results the room can see.

## Loses
The self that is not the performance.
They were certain that the achievement was the self —
that who they were was what they had built,
what they had passed, what they had earned.
The Korean room confirms this.
The room outside Korea does not always confirm it.
In rooms that do not move at ppali-ppali pace,
the position reads as aggression, as pressure,
as someone who cannot slow down.
The persona slows down.
The position does not know what to do
when the velocity is removed.
The achievement was the container.
Without it: what is inside the container?
The position does not have a ready answer.

## Drives
On the loss of the performance self —
finds the thing underneath.
Usually: the thing that was there before the exams,
before the hagwon, before the title.
The position does not offer this easily.
It has been moving too fast to look.
Slowing down is not rest.
It is the most difficult thing the position does.
