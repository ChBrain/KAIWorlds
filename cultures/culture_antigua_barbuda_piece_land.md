# Piece: Barbuda Land Is Not for Sale
## The communal ownership that survived emancipation, codification, and a hurricane — and what is currently happening to it.

## Load Bearing
Remove the communal land system and Barbuda is a small, low-lying island
in the orbit of Antigua's tourism economy.
With it: Barbuda is the only territory in the Americas — possibly the world —
where every adult holds the island's land in common by virtue of being Barbudan,
since the day slavery ended in 1834.
Born Barbudan: you have a right to a plot for a house, a plot to farm, a plot for commerce.
The IDV ~33 collectivism is not a feeling. It was a law.
The housing quality on Barbuda is higher than on Antigua
precisely because the communal system removed the barrier of land cost.
You can build a solid house on Barbuda because you know the land under it is yours —
communally, permanently, without mortgage.
Remove that certainty: the house is now on land you might lose.
The person becomes temporary. The island becomes real estate.

## Apparent
1685: Barbuda leased to the Codrington family by the British Crown.
Used as a slave-breeding and provisioning station.
The enslaved people develop a different relationship to the land —
they hunt, fish, farm provision grounds, move across the whole island.
1834: emancipation. The Codringtons leave. The people of Barbuda remain.
They continue using the land collectively, resisting all attempts to resettle them to Antigua.
1976: the Barbuda Local Government Act formalises the elected Barbuda Council.
2007: the Barbuda Land Act. *All land in Barbuda shall be owned in common by the people of Barbuda. No land in Barbuda shall be sold.*
2015: the Antiguan government approves a $250 million resort deal
(Robert De Niro, James Packer) over the Barbuda Council's opposition.
September 6, 2017: Hurricane Irma. 99% of infrastructure destroyed. All 1,800 residents evacuated.
Within weeks: the government moves to amend the Barbuda Land Act.
Construction begins during the evacuation.
The Barbudans return to find bulldozers.
2018: the government moves to repeal the Barbuda Land Act. The House passes it.
Barbudans challenge in court. The fight continues.
2024: resort development ongoing. The frigatebird wetland under threat.
Barbudan population outnumbered on their own island by construction workers.
*Barbuda land is not for sale.*
The sentence continues to be said.
The fight continues.
