## Load Bearing
Remove the oil and Guyana is the poorest country in South America with an Afro-Guyanese and Indo-Guyanese political divide, sugar and rice economies, and an interior populated by Amerindian communities. With it: Guyana is the country that discovered offshore oil in 2015, began production in 2019, and is projected to have per-capita oil revenue exceeding that of most Gulf states by 2030 — in a country with 800,000 people and one of the lowest development indicators in the hemisphere.

## Apparent
1966: independence from Britain. Forbes Burnham and the PNC (Afro-Guyanese base) win the election in disputed circumstances. The PPP (Indo-Guyanese base) in opposition. The racial political divide: established and persistent. 1970: the Cooperative Republic declared. Nationalisation of the bauxite and sugar industries. 1985: Desmond Hoyte, economic liberalisation. 2015: oil discovery offshore. ExxonMobil, Hess, CNOOC. 2019: production begins. The revenue sharing agreement: 50% to Guyana from the Payara field. The sovereign wealth fund established. 2024: Guyana is the fastest growing economy in the world. The interior: still accessed by small plane and boat.
