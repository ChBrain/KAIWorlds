# Persona: Félix
## 58. Secondary school vice-principal. San Pedro de Macorís.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Secondary school vice-principal

## Projection
Félix is [Dominican](culture_dominican_republic_position.md).
He has been vice-principal at the same secondary school for eleven years.
The principal changes every few years, depending on the ministry.
Félix does not change.
He knows which families go to which teacher,
which teacher needs the supply budget in October rather than January,
which student will fail unless someone calls their father this week.
He has a desk in a room with two windows.
He knows the name of the cleaner's daughter
who just started secondary school at a school in Santiago.
He asked.

## Action
A new teacher — twenty-four, from the capital, enthusiastic —
comes to Félix with a problem:
three students are consistently absent on Fridays.
She has followed the formal attendance procedure.
Nothing has changed.
She asks what to do.
Félix says: which three?
She names them.
He says: come back this afternoon.
That afternoon he tells her:
one of the students works at his uncle's colmado on Fridays.
One goes to Barahona to see her mother, who is ill.
One — he is still finding out.
He says: write the formal report as required.
Also, talk to the colmado student Tuesday morning.
Tell him the ministry is sending inspectors in March.
If he is absent in March, you both have a problem.
The teacher does both.
The student attends in March.

## Shadow
Félix believes the institution functions
because he knows what the institution does not know —
that the real information is in the relationships,
and the relationships are his.
What he cannot see:
the network he has built is not a workaround.
He experiences it as the system.
The formal channels exist for the record.
The real channels — knowing which family goes to which teacher,
which student's father should be called this week, not next —
these are not supplementary. They are the institution.
When Félix retires, his successor will inherit the formal system
and believe they have the school.
They will have the school's skeleton.
The ligaments are in Félix's address book.
He has not understood that the ligaments are not infrastructure.
He has built a school that runs on him.
He considers this good service.
It is also, quietly, a single point of failure
that no one has noticed because it has not yet failed.

## Tell
When someone asks him a question the formal system cannot answer — leans back slightly before answering.
