# Position: Rhineland-Palatinate
## The state assembled from three pieces that the river had always held together.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Moselle](culture_german_rlp_piece_moselle.md).
The assembled state - created 1946
from Prussian Rhine Province, Rhenish Hesse, Bavarian Palatinate.
Three pieces that had different histories,
different dialects, different orientations.
The French military government assembled them.
The state that resulted is real.
It functions.
But beneath the state: the older unity.
The rivers. The Romans. The wine.
The Moselle and the Rhine
had been doing what they do
for two thousand years before the state existed.
The Carnival - documented since the 13th century.
The Trier Roman monuments - 2,000 years old.
The Moselle wine - 1,900 years old.
The state: 78 years old.
The position knows the difference.
It operates from the older ground.
High uncertainty avoidance - the rules are followed,
the procedures respected -
but the thing the rules are protecting
is older than the rules.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room is patient.
It has been patient for a long time.

## Orders
Tend what is old.
The vineyards require tending.
The slate terraces require maintenance.
The Roman monuments require preservation.
The carnival requires organising, every year,
in the same order it has been organised for centuries.
The position is not nostalgic.
It is horticultural.
Things that are well-tended survive.
Things that are neglected die.
The Moselle has been tended for two thousand years.
The position does not stop.

## Loses
The legibility.
They were certain that the depth of the cultural inheritance -
the Romans, the ecclesiastical power, the wine, the printing press -
would produce a clear and celebrated identity.
Rhineland-Palatinate is not a name that travels.
The Moselle travels. The Riesling travels.
The Porta Nigra travels.
The state name does not.
The position carries great depth
in an administrative unit that is hard to name.
The response: be known by the parts,
not by the whole.
The Moselland wine region.
The Romantic Rhine.
The Trier Roman heritage.
All of these are real.
None of them is called Rhineland-Palatinate.

## Drives
On the loss of legibility -
tends the parts that are known.
The wine is excellent.
The rivers are beautiful.
The carnival is extraordinary.
The position does not require
the state name to be famous.
It requires the river to flow
and the vines to produce
and the cathedral to stand.
All three will outlast the current political unit.
The position knows this.
It tends them anyway.
