# Piece: The Captains Regent
## Two heads of state, six-month terms, since 1243.

## Owner
- **Place:** San Marino

## Place
San Marino. The Palazzo Pubblico.
The Captains Regent are sworn in here
on April 1st and October 1st of each year.
The ceremony has occurred, with interruptions,
since 1243.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and San Marino loses its governing mechanism
and its reason for existing as a republic.
The Captain-Regent system is not a historical curiosity.
It is the operational government.
Two serve simultaneously - to prevent tyranny.
Neither can serve consecutive terms - to prevent accumulation.
The design is 780 years old.
It still works.
Remove it and the oldest republic in the world
is no longer a republic.

## Apparent
Two Captains Regent. Sworn in twice yearly.
The ceremony: the Grand Council.
The sceptres of office presented.
The outgoing Captains hand over to the incoming.
The building: the Palazzo Pubblico, 1256.
The three towers on the mountain above.
The ceremony has the weight of 780 years.

## Yearbook
301 AD: the founding legend - Saint Marinus the stonemason.
1243: the first recorded Captains Regent.
The system formalised.
1463: San Marino acquires additional territories.
Still holds them.
1503: Cesare Borgia seizes San Marino.
Holds it for months.
Returns it.
The republic resumes.
Napoleon: does not conquer San Marino.
Garibaldi takes refuge here.
Mussolini: San Marino declares neutrality.
World War II: San Marino bombed by the British by accident.
The republic survives.
1988: joins the Council of Europe.
1992: joins the United Nations.
2023: EU accession talks begin.
The Captains Regent still sworn in April 1st and October 1st.
