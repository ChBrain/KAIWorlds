## Shown
- The Liberation Tower. 372 metres. The telecommunications tower that became the national symbol. The argument the skyline makes.
- The Grand Mosque. Completed 1986. The largest mosque in Kuwait. The call to prayer carrying across the flat city.
- The Souq Mubarakiya. The old market. Named for the Mubarak the Great. Gold, spices, traditional clothing, electronics. The market that was here before the oil and is still here after it.
- The *diwaniyya* rooms. Not visible from the street — they are in the ground floors of houses, in private compounds. But present everywhere in the social architecture. The city that runs on meetings that don't appear on official schedules.
- The Corniche. The seafront drive. The Kuwaiti Towers — the three towers with the water storage spheres, the symbol of pre-invasion Kuwait on every souvenir. Still standing. The Gulf beyond them.
