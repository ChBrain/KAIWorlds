## The policy that made not-having-a-single-identity into a national identity.

## Place

## Load Bearing
Remove the 1971 policy and Canada is a bilingual British Commonwealth country
with significant immigration.
With it: Canada is the country that formally declared
that cultural diversity is not a problem to be solved
but a condition to be maintained.
The first country in the world to adopt multiculturalism as official state policy.
This is load-bearing because the Canadian PDI 39, IDV 80, UAI 48, MAS 52
do not produce this policy automatically.
The policy is a decision — a particular answer
to the question of what holds a country together
when the country is assembled from fragments
that do not share a single origin.
Remove it: Canada is a country managing diversity.
With it: Canada is a country whose managing diversity *is* the identity.

## Apparent
1971: Prime Minister Pierre Trudeau introduces the policy.
Canada becomes the first country in the world to adopt multiculturalism as official state policy.
The policy: the government of Canada recognises the cultural contributions
of all ethnic groups and will assist all Canadian cultural groups
to grow and contribute to Canada.
1982: the Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms entrenches equality rights.
1988: the Canadian Multiculturalism Act gives the policy statutory force.
The policy does not resolve the tensions: the French-English linguistic divide,
the status of Indigenous peoples, the question of what Canadian culture is.
It provides a framework for holding the tensions without requiring their resolution.
The Indigenous critique: multiculturalism was adopted
without consultation with the peoples on whose land
the multicultural project was being built.
The policy names many cultures. It does not restore what was taken.
Both facts are present. The policy is real. The debt is real.
