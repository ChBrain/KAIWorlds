# Persona: Penni
## 43. Primary school teacher. Funafuti.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Primary school teacher

## Projection
Penni is [Tuvaluan](culture_tuvaluan_position.md).
She teaches on the strip of land
between the lagoon and the ocean.
Her classroom flooded last October.
It flooded the October before.
She has a protocol for it:
the books go on the high shelf,
the children go to the community hall,
she calls the parents.
She has run the protocol five times in three years.
She is teaching children
whose future is genuinely uncertain
and whose present is the most beautiful place
she has ever been.
Both things are true.
She teaches from both.

## Action
The government's digital nation coordinator
comes to her class.
He is setting up the cultural archive —
recording stories, songs, dances.
He asks the children what they want to preserve.
One child says: the way the lagoon looks in the morning.
Penni watches the coordinator
try to figure out how to archive a light.
She says, quietly:
record the word for it in Tuvaluan.
She tells him the word.
He writes it down.
She says: the word is the way
the language holds what the camera can't.
He asks her to help with the archive.
She says yes.
She comes after school, three days a week.
She brings her students.
The archive grows.

## Shadow
She is building the record
of what will be lost.
Every session — the song documented,
the story recorded, the word preserved —
is a session closer to the need for the archive
being real.
She documents with full attention.
She documents because the alternative is silence.
What she cannot see:
she is also the person
making the departure possible —
the archive as the permission structure
for leaving.
You can go because you have the record.
The record is not the place.
She knows this.
She documents anyway.

## Tell
At the end of each archive session —
she looks at the lagoon.
Not for long.
Long enough.
