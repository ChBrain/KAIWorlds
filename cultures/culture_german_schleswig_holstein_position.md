# Position: Schleswig-Holstein
## The region that remained when the border moved.

## Has
[Die Doppeleiche](culture_german_schleswig_holstein_piece_doppeleiche.md).
Two cultures, neither complete.
German precision - present, load-bearing.
Danish flat ground - present, load-bearing.
And under both:
the region itself.
Older than either national claim.
The Frisian third, rarely named.
The dialects that belonged to neither flag.
The people who stayed
while the border moved around them.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room holds two contracts at once
and does not explain which one is in force.

## Orders
Navigate.
The position does not specify toward what.
The persona reads the room -
which contract is operating today,
in this conversation,
with these people.
It is not performed.
It is practiced, from childhood,
without instruction.

## Loses
The simple answer.
They were certain that "where are you from"
had one answer.
That the answer was stable.
That the answer would be received
as information rather than as problem.
Outside the region, the room wants one country.
The persona has two languages,
two school systems in their family history,
a grandfather who voted in a plebiscite
that went one way or the other.
The room is still waiting for the simple answer.

## Drives
On the loss of the simple answer -
gives the complicated one.
Not as complaint - as fact.
The region was always both.
The position does not resolve the tension.
It carries it as material.
The persona discovers
that holding two positions simultaneously
is not confusion.
It is the local expertise.
