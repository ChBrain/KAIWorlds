# Piece: The Oil and the Wahhabis
## The 1744 pact and the 1938 discovery — two founding events that made Saudi Arabia what it is.

## Owner
- **Place:** Riyadh

## Place
Riyadh. The Masmak Fortress.
The mud-brick fort where ibn Saud
retook Riyadh in 1902,
beginning the unification of the kingdom.
The door still has the spear tip
embedded in it from the battle.
Twenty minutes away:
the Kingdom Tower, 302 metres.
The crescent moon at the top.
The hole in the crescent —
a sky bridge, a viewing platform,
the argument the kingdom makes
about where it is going.
Both structures are the same country.

## Load Bearing
Remove the 1744 pact between Al Saud and Wahhabism
and Saudi Arabia is one of several competing principalities
in the Arabian Peninsula
without the religious legitimacy
that unified them.
Remove the 1938 oil discovery at Dammam
and Saudi Arabia is the guardian of the holy cities
with limited revenue to exercise global influence.
With both: Saudi Arabia is the state
that controls Islam's holiest sites,
funds the global spread of Wahhabi doctrine
with oil revenue,
projects influence from Morocco to Indonesia,
and sits on 17% of the world's proven oil reserves
while hosting 2.5 million hajj pilgrims annually.
The two founding events are inseparable:
the pact gave the oil discovery its frame —
the wealth as God's gift, the custodianship as obligation —
and the oil gave the pact its reach —
the mosques built in Europe and Africa and Asia,
the scholarships, the madrassas,
the particular interpretation of Islam
exported with the petrodollars.
Remove either piece: a different country.
With both: the country that shaped
global Islam more than any other
in the twentieth century
and is now deciding whether to reshape itself
before the oil that funded the shaping runs out.
The crown prince is thirty-eight.
The oil peak is estimated for the 2030s.
The mathematics is clear.
The pact is being renegotiated.
The clerics have less power than in 2017.
The women are driving.
The concerts are running.
The spear tip is still in the door.
The Kingdom Tower crescent is still open to the sky.

## Apparent
1744: Muhammad ibn Saud meets Muhammad ibn Abd al-Wahhab. The pact.
Religious legitimacy for political authority. Political authority for religious legitimacy.
1902: ibn Saud retakes Riyadh. The unification begins.
1932: the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia proclaimed.
1938: oil discovered at Dammam Well No. 7.
1945: Roosevelt meets ibn Saud on the USS Quincy. The oil-for-security deal.
The US-Saudi relationship established.
1973: the oil embargo. OPEC. The price quadruples.
The petrodollars flow. The mosque-building begins globally.
1979: the Siege of Mecca. Juhayman al-Otaybi. Grand Mosque seized.
The Saudi state agrees to stricter Wahhabi enforcement
in exchange for clerical support to retake the mosque.
The religious conservatism deepens.
1990: the Gulf War. US troops in Saudi Arabia.
Osama bin Laden's break with the family.
2001: fifteen of the nineteen September 11 hijackers: Saudi.
The relationship with Wahhabism becomes a liability.
2015: Muhammad bin Salman rises.
2017: the Ritz-Carlton arrests. 200 princes and businessmen detained.
The consolidation of power.
2018: Jamal Khashoggi killed in the Saudi consulate in Istanbul.
The global reckoning. The position absorbs it and continues.
2021: Vision 2030 accelerates. The entertainment sector opens.
The religious police: powers curtailed.
The building: everywhere.
The oil: still flowing.
The spear: still in the door.
