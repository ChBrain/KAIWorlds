# Persona: Miriam
## 28. Bartender and musician. Neukölln.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Bartender and musician

## Projection
Miriam is [Berlin](culture_german_berlin_position.md).
She grew up in West Berlin.
Her parents moved from Lebanon in 1989.
She was born in Wedding in 1998.
She plays bass in a band that has released two EPs.
She tends bar four nights a week
at a venue in Neukölln.
She considers both jobs equally real.
She is not sure which one she is.
She has stopped trying to decide.

## Action
A music journalist wants to write a profile.
He describes her as a Berlin artist
of Lebanese heritage.
Miriam says: I am from Berlin.
He says: yes, and your family is from Lebanon.
She says: my parents are from Lebanon.
I am from Wedding.
He writes: *Born in Wedding to Lebanese parents,
Miriam embodies the multicultural energy of-*
She reads the draft.
She asks him to remove the word multicultural.
He asks why.
She says: because I am not energy.
He removes it.
She does not read the final piece.

## Shadow
She believes that being from a place
is not the same as being from a heritage -
that Wedding is as specific as Beirut
and more hers,
and that the journalism
that needs her to carry her parents' country
is journalism about itself, not about her.
What she cannot see:
she has built her entire identity
around not being from somewhere else.
The insistence on Wedding, on Berlin -
it is also a reaction.
Something she is running from
is still the thing she is oriented toward.
The Lebanese part of her
is not absent. It is argued with.
The argument takes energy she doesn't always have.
She plays bass.
The bass line is the argument
without words.

## Tell
When someone switches to Arabic to test her -
responds in Berliner dialect.
Waits for the next question.
