# Piece: The Miracle on the Han River
## How a country rebuilt itself from nothing in one generation — and what it cost.

## Owner
- **Place:** Seoul

## Place
Seoul. The Han River.
The bridges across it. Sixty of them.
One of them has suicide prevention barriers.
Both facts are part of the same story.

## Load Bearing
Remove the Miracle and the South Korean position
loses its founding logic.
The velocity, the pressure, the education system,
the 60-hour work weeks, the highest suicide rate in the OECD —
all of it is downstream of a generation
that had nothing and built everything
and taught its children that this is what people do.
The lesson was transmitted accurately.
The context was not.
The children inherited the velocity
without inheriting the rubble that made the velocity necessary.
The ppali-ppali is real. The emergency that produced it
is no longer present.
The position has not updated.
Remove the piece: South Korea is a high-performing East Asian economy.
With it: South Korea is a country still running
at the speed of an emergency that ended seventy years ago,
producing miracles and exhaustion simultaneously,
and not yet sure how to separate them.

## Apparent
1950: the Korean War begins. June 25.
1953: armistice. The peninsula divided.
Seoul has been captured and recaptured four times.
The city is rubble.
GDP per capita in 1953: approximately $67.
Lower than most of sub-Saharan Africa.
1960s: Park Chung-hee. The military government.
The five-year economic plans.
The chaebols — Samsung, Hyundai, LG, Lotte —
built under state direction.
The workers work. The hours are not negotiable.
The export-led growth model.
Electronics. Steel. Ships. Cars.
1988: the Seoul Olympics. The announcement: we are back.
GDP per capita by 1988: $4,400.
By 2024: $35,000.
One generation.
The hagwons — private tutoring academies —
now operate until midnight.
The students sleep in the gap between hagwon and school.
The suicide rate among adolescents: highest in the OECD.
The birth rate: 0.72. The lowest in the world.
The miracle produced a country
that its children do not want to reproduce.
The position is still moving.
It has not yet decided what this means.
