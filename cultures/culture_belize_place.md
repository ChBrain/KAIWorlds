# Place: Belmopan / Belize City, Belize

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Shown
- Belize City. The old capital on the coast — still the commercial hub,
  vulnerable to hurricanes, built below sea level on reclaimed mangrove.
  The colonial architecture. The swing bridge over Haulover Creek, hand-cranked.
  The Creole street culture. The smell of the sea in every street.
- Belmopan. The planned capital built after Hurricane Hattie destroyed Belize City in 1961.
  Functional, uncrowded, government buildings in a ring around a central plaza.
  The city that knows it exists as a consequence of a hurricane.
- The Barrier Reef. Second largest in the world. Blue Holes.
  The reason most people who are not Belizean know the country exists.
- The Cayo district. The interior. Maya ruins — Caracol, Xunantunich.
  The Mennonite communities of Spanish Lookout producing most of Belize's dairy and grain.
  The jungle that has not been cleared.
- The border with Guatemala. Contested since 1821.
  Guatemala withdrew its territorial claim only in a 2019 referendum.
  The border is marked on the ground. The claim is in the history.
