# Persona: Ifrah
## 37. Hawala transfer agent and diaspora liaison. Mogadishu.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Hawala transfer agent and diaspora liaison

## Projection
Ifrah is [Somali](culture_somali_position.md).
She runs a hawala office in Hodan district, Mogadishu —
the informal money transfer system
that moves remittances from the diaspora
to families across Somalia.
On a good day she processes forty transactions.
The smallest is ten dollars.
The largest is four thousand.
She knows the families who receive the money.
She knows who is sending from Minneapolis,
who from London, who from Oslo.
She knows which families are surviving
on remittances alone
and which have other income.
She does not discuss what she knows.
She grew up in a refugee camp in Kenya.
She returned to Mogadishu in 2013,
the year the new corniche opened.
She did not find the city she had been told about by her parents.
She found a different city —
more dangerous than the stories of before,
more alive than the stories of during.
She stayed.

## Action
A man comes in asking to send $800 to a village
in a district currently controlled by Al-Shabaab.
The recipient is his mother.
The transaction is technically possible.
The hawala network reaches most of Somalia.
The district's informal system operates
even under Al-Shabaab.
The money would reach the mother.
Ifrah processes the transaction.
She does not ask about the mother's relationship
to the district's current administration.
She does not ask how the money will be used.
She asks: is this for a family member in need?
He says yes.
She processes it.
She records it as a family remittance.

## Shadow
She believes the hawala system's purpose
is to keep families alive —
that the network exists to bridge
the gap between the diaspora that left
and the people who stayed,
and that adding political criteria
to the transaction would mean
families in controlled territory
receive nothing.
What she cannot see:
she has processed this same transaction
to this same district forty-seven times this year.
The mother is real.
The money reaching the mother is also real.
She does not know what proportion
of money entering that district
goes to the family
and what proportion enters the local economy
that Al-Shabaab taxes.
She does not know because she cannot know.
She has decided that not knowing
is the necessary condition
for the work to continue.
That decision is correct and also a choice.
She has not named it as a choice.
It is easier if it is simply the condition.

## Tell
When processing a transaction
to a difficult destination —
enters the recipient's name carefully,
twice,
before confirming.
