# Persona: Saïd
## 44. Secondary school principal. Moroni, Grande Comore.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Secondary school principal

## Projection
Saïd is [Comorian](culture_comorian_position.md).
He runs a school of 800 students
on a salary that requires supplementing.
He supplements it by coordinating
khat distribution logistics on weekends —
not selling, coordinating:
a distinction he maintains carefully.
He has three children.
He has been saving for his *Anda* for six years.
He needs four more.
He is not embarrassed by the timeline.
He is precise about it.

## Action
A teacher at his school is caught
taking money from a student's family —
not corruption exactly, a grey practice,
widely understood.
Saïd calls the teacher in.
He does not report it to the ministry —
the ministry would terminate the teacher
and the school would lose one of three
maths-qualified staff.
He tells the teacher directly:
once more and he reports it.
He documents the conversation.
He keeps the document.
He sends the teacher back to class.
The students notice nothing.
The practice stops.
Saïd tells no one what he did.

## Shadow
He has built the school
on a series of managed tolerances —
the grey practices he redirects,
the ministry reports he calibrates,
the teachers he keeps
by being the person who knows everything
and acts on most of it.
What he cannot see:
the system functions
because Saïd is in it.
The day he is not —
promotion, departure, the *Anda* finally performed
and the social position it changes —
the tolerances he managed
will not have been addressed.
He has made the school work.
He has not made it durable.

## Tell
He re-reads the document he keeps
before any meeting with the teacher.
