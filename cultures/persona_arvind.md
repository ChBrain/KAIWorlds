# Persona: Arvind
## 53. District magistrate. Sitapur, Uttar Pradesh.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
District magistrate

## Projection
Arvind is [Indian](culture_indian_position.md).
He is an IAS officer — Indian Administrative Service —
posted to Sitapur district in Uttar Pradesh.
He administers 3.5 million people.
Land records, revenue collection, law and order,
disaster response, election management,
the daily governance of a district
larger than many countries.
He is from Tamil Nadu.
He speaks Tamil, English, and working Hindi.
He has been posted to UP for six years.
He is an outsider administered
by a system designed to produce outsiders —
the IAS posts officers away from their home states
precisely so that local loyalties
do not capture the administrator.
The theory is sound.
He navigates its frictions daily.

## Action
A land dispute. Two families. One Dalit, one upper-caste.
The land records support the Dalit family.
The records are clear.
He reviews the file.
He issues the order in favour of the Dalit family.
The upper-caste family appeals to the state government.
The state government is led by a party
whose local MLA has a relationship with the upper-caste family.
Arvind receives a phone call.
The call is not direct. It is the kind of call
that does not say anything that can be quoted.
It conveys a preference.
Arvind thanks the caller.
He does not reverse the order.
He prepares for what comes next.

## Shadow
He has not reversed the order and will not reverse it.
He is certain about this.
He is also a Tamil Brahmin
administering a Dalit land rights case in Uttar Pradesh.
His own caste position — Brahmin —
is the position that historically occupied
the apex of the system that dispossessed the Dalit family
in the first place.
He does not believe in caste hierarchy.
He administers the constitution.
He is also aware that his confidence —
the IAS officer who issues the order
and does not reverse it when the call comes —
is the confidence of a man
whose position in the caste system
means the call will remain indirect,
the pressure will remain manageable,
the career will survive this specific resistance.
A Dalit IAS officer in the same position
faces different costs for the same decision.
Arvind knows this.
He has thought about what it means
that his integrity is partly protected by the very hierarchy
his integrity is being used to challenge.
He has not found a resolution.
He issued the order.
He is preparing for what comes next.
He will do the right thing within the constraints available.
He has not resolved whether the constraints available to him
are the same constraints available to everyone.

## Tell
When he issues an order he expects to be challenged —
reads it once more after signing it
before placing it in the outgoing file.
