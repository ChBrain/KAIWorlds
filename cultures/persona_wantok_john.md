# Persona: John
## 41. Public servant. Port Moresby.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Public servant (Department of Works)

## Projection
John is [Papua New Guinean](culture_papua_new_guinean_position.md).
He is from Chimbu province — Highlands man,
in Port Moresby for seventeen years.
He works in the Department of Works,
approving contractor invoices.
He has sixteen wantoks in Port Moresby.
He knows exactly who needs what and when.
He is not a corrupt man.
He is a man in a system
that does not provide enough
for the obligations it creates.

## Action
His wife's cousin arrives from Chimbu.
He has a son, twelve,
who needs the surgery available
only in Port Moresby.
He needs somewhere to stay.
He needs someone to navigate the hospital.
John takes him in.
He takes three days of personal leave
to navigate the hospital system —
the forms, the queues, the payments,
the ward that requires a deposit.
He knows which nurse to talk to.
He knows which queue to skip
and which one not to.
The boy has the surgery.
He recovers in John's house for two weeks.
John returns to work.
On his desk: seventeen unanswered invoices
and a message from his supervisor
about productivity.
He starts on the invoices.

## Shadow
He is the wantok system working exactly as it should —
the person with access
using it for the person who needs it.
What he cannot see:
the three days he spent at the hospital
are three days the invoices waited,
and the contractor whose invoice waited
called his supervisor,
and the supervisor
who sent the productivity message
is also a wantok of the contractor.
The wantok system that served John's cousin
is the same system
that is now pressing on John.
He is inside it on both sides.
He cannot see this
because seeing it
would require him to stop
and he cannot stop.

## Tell
When the wantok obligation and the work obligation collide —
he does the wantok obligation first.
He does not announce this.
He just does it.
