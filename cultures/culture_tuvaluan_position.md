# Position: Tuvaluan
## The nation that will disappear — and declared itself a digital state before it does.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The .tv Domain and the Rising Sea](culture_tuvaluan_piece_tv.md).
Nine atolls. 11,000 people.
The smallest independent country in the world
by population.
Average elevation: two metres.
The sea level is rising.
The king tides flood the road.
The wells are saltwater.
The taro pits are dying.
Tuvalu will be uninhabitable
within decades.
This is not contested.
It is in the government's planning documents.
The *fatele* — the traditional dance —
performed at every significant occasion.
The women in rows, the fans, the precise movement.
The *fatele* that the Tuvaluan diaspora
performs in Fiji, in New Zealand, in Australia.
The dance that travels with the people
when the island cannot.
The *.tv* domain: the internet country code
for Tuvalu,
leased to a California company in 2000
for $50 million over twelve years.
The windfall that funded
Tuvalu's UN membership fees
and its first government buildings.
The *.tv* that every television network
and streaming service uses
because it abbreviates to television.
The accident of the name:
the most valuable two-letter domain suffix
in the world
belongs to the country that is drowning.
The collectivism: the atoll community.
The *te kaitasi* — working together.
The obligatory cooperation of people
who live on a strip of land
narrow enough to see both sides from the middle.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room knows your island, your family.
The room knows how high the tide came last month.

## Orders
*Tuvalu mo te Atua* — Tuvalu for the Almighty.
The national motto.
The Christianity: 97%.
The church as the community
that will persist when the island is gone.
The position that builds the digital state
and maintains the faith simultaneously.
Both are the preparation
for what comes after the water.

## Loses
The land under the ancestors.
The graves of the grandparents
are on these atolls.
The taro pits that their grandparents dug.
The navigation knowledge specific to these waters.
When the land goes,
the specific knowledge that the land required
goes with it.
What the diaspora carries
is everything that can be carried.
The things that can only be known
on these particular atolls
cannot be carried.
They will be known no longer.

## Drives
On the loss — the digital nation.
2023: Tuvalu declares itself
the world's first digital nation.
The entirety of its territory —
culture, governance, history —
to be digitally preserved.
*Even if the physical islands sink,
Tuvalu as a nation, as a people,
as a culture, will never sink.*
The legal framework: preserving statehood
even without physical territory.
The precedent for international law:
unprecedented.
The *fatele* digitised.
The oral histories recorded.
The taro pit knowledge documented.
The nation in the cloud
when the nation is in the sea.
