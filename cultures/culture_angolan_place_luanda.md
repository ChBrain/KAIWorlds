## Shown
- The *musseques*.
  The informal settlements on Luanda's periphery.
  Six million people in greater Luanda.
  The majority in the musseques.
  The corrugated iron and the red earth.
  The water sellers.
  The informal economy — *zungueiras* (street vendors),
  mechanics, carpenters, traders —
  that runs alongside the oil economy
  without being part of it.
  The musseques are the city's majority.
  The glass towers of the bay
  are the city's face.
  The face is not the body.
- The Fortaleza de São Miguel.
  The Portuguese fort from 1576.
  The oldest European building in sub-Saharan Africa.
  Where the Portuguese controlled the bay.
  Where slaves were shipped.
  Where independence happened.
  The building that contains 400 years
  of what Luanda has been.
  Now a military museum.
  Managed by the state.
  Attendance: low.
  The building is more important
  than the visits.
- The Ilha de Luanda.
  The spit of land in the bay.
  The restaurants. The nightlife.
  The *kuduro* clubs.
  Where Luanda's middle class goes on Friday night.
  Where the expat oil workers go.
  Where the music is.
  *Kuduro* — the rhythm that came from the musseques
  and travelled to Lisbon and Rio and Paris
  before Luanda's own middle class
  fully claimed it.
  The music is back now.
  It never left the musseques.
- The Central Bank building.
  The new Luanda.
  The glass and steel of the oil reconstruction.
  The buildings that went up
  between 2002 and 2015
  when the oil money flowed.
  Now: some unfinished.
  The oil revenue declined.
  The construction stopped at different levels.
  The buildings are the record
  of where the boom was
  when it ended.
- Lopitanga, Bié Province.
  Not Luanda — but present in Luanda's political geography.
  Where Savimbi was finally buried
  on June 1, 2019.
  Where the Ovimbundu memory
  was formally acknowledged
  17 years after the war ended.
  The burial that the MPLA delayed
  because Savimbi's grave
  would have been a shrine.
  Then organised
  when the cost of not doing it
  exceeded the cost of doing it.
  The grave is in Bié.
  Its presence is in every MPLA-UNITA negotiation since.
