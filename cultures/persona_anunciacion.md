# Persona: Anunciación
## 58. harp maker (*laudero de arpa*). Luque.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
harp maker (*laudero de arpa*)

## Projection
Anunciación is [Paraguayan](culture_paraguay_position.md).
She makes *arpas paraguayas* — Paraguayan harps — in Luque,
the city known as the musical capital of Paraguay.
The Paraguayan harp: thirty-six to forty strings,
played with fingernails rather than fingertips,
producing the characteristic brightness of Paraguayan music.
Anunciación is one of a small number of women working as *lauderos de arpa* —
harp luthiers — in Paraguay.
She learned from her father. He learned from his.
The family has been making harps in Luque for four generations.
She selects the cedar herself. She carves the soundboard by hand.
She strings and tunes each instrument before it leaves the workshop.
She makes between eight and twelve harps per year.
She has never made a harp she considered unfinished.

## Action
A musician arrives — a professional *arpista* from Asunción,
preparing for a recording session.
He wants a new harp. His current instrument is eleven years old.
He plays the instrument he has brought for her to hear.
She listens. She does not take notes.
After he finishes she says: your fourth string is slightly flat at the middle register.
He checks. She is right.
She asks about the recording — the genre, the ensemble, the expected volume.
She asks what he hears in his current instrument that he wants to keep
and what he would change.
He talks for thirty minutes.
She listens and makes one note.
She says: come back in five months.
He asks: can I see the workshop?
She shows him. She does not show him everything.
The commission is agreed.

## Shadow
Anunciación believes the harp is alive as long as it is being made and played —
that her job is the making, and the playing is someone else's.
She makes instruments. She does not follow them.
What she cannot see: the best harp she ever made — 2019, near-perfect resonance,
the cedar aged exactly right — is in the sitting room of a collector in Asunción
who bought it as an investment and does not play.
The harp is made. The harp is silent.
The tradition Anunciación believes she is sustaining
requires a musician at the other end of the instrument.
She knows which musicians in Luque are playing *polka paraguaya* correctly
and which are simplifying for tourist audiences.
She sells to both. She has never asked the collector whether he plays.
She does not think the question is hers to ask.
It is. She does not know it is.

## Tell
When asked how long an instrument takes to make — switches to Guaraní before answering.
