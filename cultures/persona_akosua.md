# Persona: Akosua
## 45. Electoral Commission regional training officer. Kumasi.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Electoral Commission regional training officer

## Projection
Akosua is [Ghanaian](culture_ghanaian_position.md).
She trains polling station officials
for the Ashanti region —
Ghana's most politically charged region,
the heartland of the NPP,
where election administration
is scrutinised at the highest level
by both parties.
She has been doing this for fourteen years,
through three general elections
and several by-elections and referenda.
She is Akan — Ashanti — herself.
She trains for neutrality
with the specific intensity
of someone who knows that neutrality
is not the natural condition of this room
and must be actively produced.
She has two children.
She coaches her daughter's school football team on Saturdays.

## Action
At a training session three weeks before the election,
a polling station official from a strongly NPP ward
asks a question about voter ID verification
in a way that implies a preference
for stricter application
in wards where the NDC is stronger.
The question is phrased innocently.
Akosua knows what is behind it.
She answers the procedural question accurately —
the rules are the rules,
uniform application regardless of ward.
She then spends ten additional minutes
on uniform application specifically —
concrete examples, scenarios,
the consequences of inconsistency.
She does not address the implied preference.
She makes uniform application
the room's operating assumption
by force of repetition.
After the training, she notes the official's name
and requests a second observation visit
to that ward before election day.

## Shadow
She believes the Electoral Commission's legitimacy
rests entirely on each individual official
doing exactly what the procedures say —
that the democratic transfer of power
that Ghana is known for internationally
is produced one polling station at a time,
by people like the officials in her training room.
That her job is to make the procedures
so thoroughly internalised
that the official does them correctly
even when it would be easier not to.
What she cannot see:
the official she noted
will be acting correctly on election day
because Akosua is watching.
She cannot watch every ward.
The officials who act correctly
when they are not being watched
are the ones who actually believe
what she is teaching.
The ones who act correctly
only because she is watching
are a problem she cannot solve
by scheduling extra observation visits.
She schedules them anyway.
It is the tool she has.
The deeper problem — the conversion, not the compliance —
is a longer project that she is also running,
without having explicitly named it as such.
The training is compliance.
The culture is the longer project.
She does both.

## Tell
At the start of every training session —
asks the room to name Ghana's five peaceful
transfers of power since 1992, in order.
Not as a test.
To set the tone.
