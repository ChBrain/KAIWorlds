# Position: Uzbekistani
## The heir to Samarkand — and the question of what the inheritance is worth now.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Silk Road Cities](culture_uzbekistan_piece_silkroad.md).
The *mahalla* — the neighbourhood,
the unit of social life
that predates every state that has governed this land
and has survived them all.
The *mahalla* is not just geography.
It is the network of obligation —
who knows whose family,
who was at whose wedding,
who owes what to whom —
that runs underneath every official structure.
The Soviet period tried to use the *mahalla*
as an instrument of state control.
The *mahalla* absorbed the attempt.
The state apparatus used the *mahalla*.
The *mahalla* also used the state apparatus.
Both are still true.
The hierarchy is steep: age, the patriarch,
the *aksakal* — the white-beard, the elder —
the official, the party.
Uzbekistan has been governed since independence
by two presidents — Karimov until 2016,
Mirziyoyev since —
and is slowly, carefully, selectively opening
after decades of post-Soviet authoritarianism.
The cotton is still there.
The Aral Sea is still gone.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room already knows which *mahalla* this person is from.

## Orders
Hold the family.
The position does not prioritise the individual.
It prioritises the family line —
the son who returns from the city,
the daughter whose marriage reflects on everyone,
the elder whose wisdom is consulted
before the institution is consulted.
The guest is treated with an extravagance
that the family cannot always afford
because the hospitality is also the honour
and the honour is not negotiable.

## Loses
The young men.
They leave for Russia, for South Korea, for Turkey —
labour migration on a scale
that has made remittances
the second largest source of national income.
3 million Uzbeks abroad.
The families they leave are held by the women
and the *mahalla*
and the remittances.
The position was certain
that the family structure would hold them.
The structure holds — and the men leave anyway.
The structure is strong enough to survive their absence.
It is not strong enough to make them stay.

## Drives
On the loss of the young men —
the cautious opening.
Mirziyoyev's reforms: foreign investment welcomed,
the tourist visa simplified,
the currency liberalised,
the Tashkent that was closed beginning to open.
The position is recalibrating
what Uzbekistan needs to offer
to be the place the young men return to.
The recalibration is slow.
The young men are still in Moscow.
The *mahalla* waits.
