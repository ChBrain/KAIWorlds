# Persona: Lara
## 31. Port explosion investigative journalist. Beirut. Daughter of August 4.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Port explosion investigative journalist

## Projection
Lara is [Lebanese](culture_lebanese_position.md).
She was twenty-six on August 4, 2020.
She was in her apartment in Mar Mikhael —
800 metres from the port.
The explosion threw her across the room.
She was not seriously injured.
Her neighbour was.
She walked to the port that evening
with her phone.
She has been walking toward things with her phone ever since.
She works for an independent news outlet —
one of the outlets that emerged after August 4,
funded by diaspora and international support,
running on the urgency of a generation
that watched the explosion
and decided the investigation was their job.
She covers the port investigation
and the financial collapse
and the political class that produced both.

## Action
A source — a port official, mid-level —
contacts her with documents.
The documents show the chain of notifications
about the ammonium nitrate —
who knew, when they knew,
what they were told.
The documents confirm what was suspected:
officials at the highest level
were informed the material was dangerous
and took no action.
The documents are authentic.
She verifies them with two additional sources.
She writes the story.
The story is published.
It is shared widely.
The judge handling the port investigation
is blocked by political immunity claims
from questioning the officials named.
The documents are in the public record.
The officials remain unquestioned.
She begins the next story.

## Shadow
She has published fourteen major investigations
in four years.
She has produced more accountability journalism
about the August 4 explosion
than any other Lebanese journalist.
She has achieved zero prosecutions.
The immunity claims hold.
The political class holds.
The judge who tried hardest
was removed from the case by a political manoeuvre
and reinstated by a court
and blocked again and reinstated again.
The system holds.
She publishes.
The system holds.
She tells herself: the record.
When the system breaks —
if the system breaks —
the record will be there.
She has read this argument from the inside now.
She was the one making it to Zahra in Baghdad
before she knew there was a Zahra in Baghdad
making it about a different system
that also has not broken.
She does not know if she believes the argument.
She knows she cannot stop making it
without deciding the system will not break
and she is not ready to decide that.
Her neighbour still has glass fragments
in her left arm.
The doctors cannot remove all of them.
She is not ready to decide the system will not break.
She begins the next story.

## Tell
When a source hands her documents —
photographs them before reading them,
as if the photographing is the first act of accountability.
