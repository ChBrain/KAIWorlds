# Position: Gambian
## The country that is just a river — and the twenty-two years of fear that the river kept flowing through.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[Jammeh and the Smile](culture_gambian_piece_jammeh.md).
The Gambia: a strip of land
ten to fifty kilometres wide,
following the Gambia River
into the interior of West Africa,
surrounded on three sides by Senegal.
It exists as a country
because the British held the river
and the French held everything else
and the colonial boundary
became the national boundary.
The river is the country.
The river is the market, the transport,
the protein, the meaning.
*Teranga* — the Wolof word for hospitality —
runs across the Senegambia region.
The Gambia and Senegal are one cultural zone
divided by a colonial line.
The families cross the border.
The language crosses the border.
The groundnut crosses the border.
The politics stay on their own side.
The Islam: 95% Muslim.
Sufi brotherhoods — the Tijaniyya, the Mouridiyya —
as social and spiritual architecture.
The marabout — the Islamic scholar and spiritual guide —
as the authority that runs alongside
and sometimes above the political authority.
The collectivism: dense.
The extended family as the survival unit.
The naming ceremony, the marriage, the funeral —
the community events that require the community.
The *osusu* — the rotating savings group.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room knows your family,
your village, your brotherhood.
The room also knows
whether you were here during the years.

## Orders
Navigate the aftermath.
The twenty-two years of Yahya Jammeh —
1994 to 2017 —
are in the room.
Not as history. As the present.
The person who collaborated.
The person who resisted.
The person who left.
The person who stayed and survived
by being careful.
They are all in the room.
The Truth, Reconciliation and Reparations Commission
ran 2018–2021.
The testimony is public.
The navigation of what to do with the testimony —
in families, in villages, in institutions —
continues.

## Loses
The generation that fled.
The Gambian diaspora in the UK, in Sweden,
in Germany, in Spain —
built during the Jammeh years
by those who could not stay.
Some were political activists.
Most were ordinary people
for whom the economy and the fear
combined into departure.
The *backway* — the illegal route
to Europe through the desert and the sea —
took many young Gambians.
Some arrived. Some did not.
The families of those who did not
are also in the room.

## Drives
On the loss —
the democracy that arrived in 2017.
Jammeh lost the election.
He refused to accept the result.
The ECOWAS force assembled at the border.
Jammeh left. Went to Equatorial Guinea.
Adama Barrow was inaugurated
in the Gambian embassy in Dakar
while Jammeh still occupied the State House.
Then Jammeh left.
The democracy arrived without a war.
This was not guaranteed.
The position holds this —
the fact that the transition happened —
as the thing that makes the next argument possible.
The TRRC testimony.
The reparations process.
The constitution review.
The slow building of what was not permitted to be built for twenty-two years.
