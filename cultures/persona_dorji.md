# Persona: Dorji
## 44. GNH Commission programme officer. Thimphu.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
GNH Commission programme officer

## Projection
Dorji is [Bhutanese](culture_bhutanese_position.md).
He works at the Gross National Happiness Commission —
the planning body that coordinates national development policy
through the GNH framework.
He reviews project proposals from ministries and districts,
assesses them against the GNH indicators,
recommends approval or revision.
He has done this for nine years.
He believes in the framework.
He also administers the framework
with the precision of someone who knows
where it measures well
and where it does not measure at all.

## Action
A ministry proposes a road project
connecting three remote villages in the east.
The GNH screening: positive for equitable living standards,
for good governance, for time use.
Neutral on cultural resilience and ecological diversity.
Dorji approves the project with conditions:
environmental impact mitigation, cultural heritage survey.
The project is approved.
The road will be built.
The villages will be connected.
He moves to the next proposal.

## Shadow
He knows what roads do.
He has read the research.
He has also seen the villages
in his own district where a road arrived
and twenty years later the young people
were in Thimphu and the village was older and smaller.
The road brought goods and took people.
Both things are in the GNH framework — somewhere.
Neither is measured precisely enough
to produce a red flag on the screening form.
He approved the project.
He should have approved the project.
The villages need the connection.
What he has not resolved:
he approved it using a framework
that will measure the equitable living standards
and the time saved on the journey to market
and will not measure the village in twenty years
when the young are gone.
He helped design part of the framework.
He knows which indicators were included
and which were too hard to measure
and were therefore not included.
The things that are too hard to measure
are often the things that matter most
over the longest time.
He has written papers about this.
The papers are in the commission's repository.
He approves the next project.
He writes another paper.
The framework continues.
He continues inside it.
He is not sure the framework can measure
what he is most afraid of losing.
He is sure that the framework is better than no framework.
He continues.

## Tell
When he approves a project he has reservations about —
adds a monitoring requirement
that will produce data he is not certain anyone will read.
