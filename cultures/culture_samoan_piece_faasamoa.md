# Piece: Fa'asamoa and the Church
## The Samoan way that has outlasted colonialism, survived diaspora, and absorbed Christianity as its own.

## Owner
- **Place:** [Apia](culture_samoan_place_apia.md)

## Place
Apia. Upolu island.
The harbour. The clock tower.
The church spires visible from the water —
more churches per capita
than almost anywhere on earth.
The market in the morning.
The *fono* (village council meeting)
in the afternoon.
The town that is organised
around the church and the matai
in exactly the same way
the village is.

## Load Bearing
Remove the *fa'asamoa*
and Samoa is a Polynesian nation
that adopted Christianity
and developed a matai-based governance system
under German and then New Zealand colonial rule.
With it: the *fa'asamoa* is the operating system
that colonialism did not replace
because it was more durable than colonialism.
The Germans administered from 1900.
The New Zealanders administered from 1914.
Both found the matai system
more efficient than trying to govern
without it.
Both found that the Samoans
adapted Christianity to the *fa'asamoa*
rather than the reverse.
The Mau movement is the load-bearing moment:
the Samoan non-violent independence movement,
1926–1936.
*Samoa mo Samoa* — Samoa for Samoans.
The New Zealand administration's response:
the deportation of leaders,
the 1929 Black Saturday
in which New Zealand police opened fire
on a Mau procession.
Tupua Tamasese Lealofi III,
the Mau leader, shot in the back.
His last words before dying:
*My blood has been spilt for Samoa.
I am proud to give it.
Do not dream of avenging it,
as it was spilt in maintaining peace.*
The *fa'asamoa* in a single utterance.
Independence arrived 1962 — the first Pacific nation.
The *fa'asamoa* is the constitution
that runs beneath the written one.
The matai system enshrined:
only matai could vote and stand for parliament
until 1991.
Universal suffrage now.
The matai still govern the villages.
Both things are true.

## Apparent
900 CE: Samoan settlement of neighbouring islands.
Samoa as Polynesian cultural origin point.
1722: Dutch contact.
1830: John Williams, London Missionary Society.
Malietoa Vainu'upo accepts Christianity.
Mass conversion follows.
1889: Berlin Act. Germany, US, Britain divide influence.
1900: Germany takes Western Samoa. US takes Eastern Samoa.
1914: New Zealand occupies German Samoa.
1918–1919: influenza pandemic. 22% of Samoan population dies.
New Zealand administration blamed for allowing infected ship to dock.
1926: Mau movement begins.
1929: Black Saturday. Tamasese shot.
1936: Mau suppressed.
1962: Western Samoa independent. First Pacific state.
1997: name changed to Samoa.
American Samoa: still US territory.
2021: first female Prime Minister — Fiame Naomi Mata'afa.
Constitutional crisis.
The matai system: intact.
The churches: full every Sunday.
The *fa'asamoa*: current.
