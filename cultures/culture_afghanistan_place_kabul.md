## Shown
- The Arg. The presidential palace. White walls, watchtowers. The flag. A different flag now. The palace is occupied as it has always been occupied. By whoever controls Kabul.
- Chicken Street. The old bazaar district. Carpets, lapis lazuli, antiques, the objects of a country that has been selling its heritage for forty years to whoever would buy. Still open. Still selling.
- The Kabul River. Running through the city, mostly dry in summer, a waste channel. The city built beside a river that the city has slowly killed.
- Wazir Akbar Khan. The neighbourhood where the embassies were. The blast walls. Most embassies closed in 2021. The blast walls remain. Empty compounds behind them.
- The mountains above the city. Always visible. The Hindu Kush. Snow on the peaks in winter. The city is at 1,800 metres. The mountains go to 7,000. Kabul is already high. The mountains are higher. The mountains have been there for every government.

## Withheld
- The women's rooms. Since August 2021 the women of Kabul have been progressively removed from public space — from universities, from most workplaces, from parks without a male guardian, from their own faces in public. The room shows Chicken Street and the mountains. It withholds the specific weight of what it means to be a woman in Kabul today — the room within the room that has been locked, from the outside, by a government that calls it protection. The locked room is present in every unlocked room in the city.
