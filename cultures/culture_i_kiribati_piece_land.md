# Piece: The Drowning Land
## The first nation to purchase land in another country as a sovereign insurance policy against its own disappearance.

## Owner
- **Place:** [South Tarawa](culture_i_kiribati_place_tarawa.md)

## Place
South Tarawa. The capital atoll.
The strip of land between the lagoon and the ocean.
In places: 200 metres wide.
The road that runs its length.
The overcrowding: half of Kiribati's population
on this strip.
The ocean on both sides.
The king tide: visible from the road.

## Load Bearing
Remove the climate crisis
and Kiribati is a remote Pacific microstate
navigating the usual post-colonial development questions.
With it: Kiribati is the most vivid proof
that climate change is not future tense.
The land is going now.
The wells are saltwater now.
The crops are dying now.
The graves are washing away now.
This is load-bearing for the entire Pacific
and for the global climate negotiation:
Kiribati is the argument
that cannot be answered with projections
because it is the current evidence.
The Fiji land purchase is the second piece:
2014, the Kiribati government purchases
5,460 acres on Vanua Levu, Fiji.
For food security and potential relocation.
The first time a sovereign government
has purchased land in another country
as insurance against its own physical disappearance.
The legal complexity: unprecedented.
The moral urgency: unambiguous.
The I-Kiribati who will move to Fiji
will be the first climate refugees
with a planned destination
and a purchased home.
They will also be the people
who carry the *te katei ni Kiribati*
to an island that is not Kiribati
and must decide what the Kiribati way means
when there is no Kiribati land beneath it.

## Apparent
3000 BCE: Micronesian settlers arrive.
1606: Spanish contact.
1820s: British and American whalers.
1892: British protectorate.
1942–1943: Battle of Tarawa.
US Marines vs. Japanese. 5,500 dead.
One of the bloodiest battles per square metre of WWII.
The bones: still in the sand.
1979: independence as Republic of Kiribati.
1981: UN Law of the Sea — Kiribati's vast ocean territory recognised.
The EEZ: one of the largest in the Pacific.
Fishing licence revenue: the main income.
1999: sea level data shows accelerating rise.
2002: President Teburoro Tito warns the UN.
2008: Anote Tong elected. Climate as foreign policy.
2012: Tong addresses the UN.
"Migrate with dignity."
2014: Fiji land purchase.
2015: Paris Agreement. Kiribati co-chairs small island states.
1.5°C target specifically because of Kiribati and Tuvalu.
2019: Taneti Maamau elected. More cautious migration stance.
The land: still here.
Still going.
