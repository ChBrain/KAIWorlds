# Position: Kazakhstani
## The steppe nation that inherited an empire's infrastructure and is deciding what to build with it.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Soviet Inheritance](culture_kazakhstan_piece_soviet.md).
The vast flat.
1.7 billion hectares — the ninth largest country in the world,
18 million people.
The space is not emptiness.
It is the condition that shaped the position:
the nomadic logic, the *aul* — the village,
the extended family that moves together,
the *zhuz* — the clan confederation —
Great, Middle, and Little —
that still determines who trusts whom
before any institution is consulted.
The Soviet period gave Kazakhstan:
the industrial cities, the nuclear test sites,
the Baikonur Cosmodrome,
the Russian-speaking population
that is still 15–20% of the country,
the Cyrillic alphabet it is now converting to Latin,
the infrastructure of a socialist republic
repurposed for a petro-state.
The oil arrived in the 1990s.
The position has been deciding
what to do with the oil
ever since.
The *Nursultan* — the capital renamed for the first president —
renamed back to Astana in 2022
after the January protests shook the system —
is the answer the position gave:
build a capital from scratch,
on the steppe,
in extreme cold,
with oil money,
as the argument that Kazakhstan exists
and is serious.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room is calculating
which *zhuz* this person belongs to.

## Orders
Build the nation.
The position was invented in 1991 —
Kazakhstan had never been an independent state
before Soviet dissolution.
The nation-building is not metaphor.
It is the active project:
the language policy, the history curriculum,
the statues of Kazakh heroes
who had been Soviet heroes the decade before,
the repatriation of ethnic Kazakhs
from Mongolia and China and Russia —
the *Oralman* — to populate the Kazakh majority.
The project is unfinished.
It is making progress.

## Loses
The precedent.
They were certain the January 2022 protests —
fuel price rises, then political demands,
the largest uprising since independence —
would produce a reckoning.
Tokayev called in Russian troops.
The protests were suppressed.
The reckoning did not come.
Nazarbayev's network was partially dismantled.
The system continued.
The position learned again
that the steppe absorbs.
The steppe absorbed the Soviet system.
The steppe absorbed the uprising.
The steppe is patient.
The position does not know
whether the patience is wisdom or numbness.
It has not resolved the question.
It continues.

## Drives
On the loss of the precedent —
the pivot.
Kazakhstan pivots — toward China, toward the West,
away from Russia when Russia invades Ukraine,
maintaining equidistance with precision
that smaller countries cannot afford
but Kazakhstan, with the oil and the size,
can manage.
The multi-vector foreign policy
is the steppe logic applied internationally:
do not commit to any single direction,
read the weather, move accordingly.
It has kept the country sovereign.
Whether it has kept it free
is the question the position
is still not answering directly.
