# Position: Gabonese
## One family. Fifty-six years. The oil, the French, and the palace coup that ended it — from within.

## Has
[The Bongo Dynasty and the Coup That Kept It](culture_gabonese_piece_bongo.md).
Libreville. Named *Free Town* —
the name French sailors gave
the place where they freed enslaved people
from a Brazilian ship in 1849.
A country of 2.3 million people.
One of the highest GDPs per capita in Africa —
$17,000 per person.
One-third of the population below the poverty line.
The forest: 80% of Gabon is forested.
The largest intact tropical forest in Africa.
The gorillas. The forest elephants.
The Ogooué River.
The Fang, the Myénè, the Nzebi,
the Punu, the Tsogo —
forty-five ethnic groups
in a country smaller than France.
The collectivism: high.
The hierarchy: steep,
with a specific feature —
the hierarchy operated through
one family for 56 years.
The PDG party. The oil. The French.
Elf. Then Total.
The French paratroopers at Camp Charles de Gaulle
outside Libreville —
still there in 2026.
Gabon is the most reliably stable country
in Central Africa.
This stability was not free.

## Orders
*Prudence* — careful navigation.
The Gabonese position operates
in a country where power has always been concentrated,
where the relationship with France is the given,
where the oil will run out and is running out —
production peaked in 1997,
has been declining since —
and where the transition
is still being defined.
The prudent person learns the new patron's requirements
while maintaining the relationships
that predate the patron.

## Loses
The promise of the oil wealth.
The resource that produced
one of the highest per capita GDPs in Africa
and one-third poverty simultaneously —
the clearest possible demonstration
of the resource curse
is Gabon: the money was there,
it did not reach the people,
it went to the family,
to France, to the party,
to the 39 properties and 70 bank accounts
French police found in 2007.
The position carries the knowledge
that the wealth existed
and was redirected
for 56 years —
that what the country has now
is what 56 years of kleptocracy left.

## Drives
On the loss —
the forest.
Gabon is the lung.
The carbon that Gabon's forests sequester
is one of the country's genuine leverages —
international carbon finance,
conservation agreements,
the debt-for-nature swaps
that give Gabon financial relief
in exchange for forest preservation.
The coup leader Oligui Nguema
has maintained the forest commitments.
The constitution passed by referendum in 2024.
Elections expected.
The Gabonese position does not know
what the transition will produce —
it is still being produced.
It watches. It participates.
It holds its prudence.
