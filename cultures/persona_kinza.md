# Persona: Kinza
## 38. Teacher. Nauru.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Teacher (secondary school)

## Projection
Kinza is [Nauruan](culture_nauruan_position.md).
She teaches at Nauru Secondary School.
She was born the year the phosphate company
posted its first operating loss.
She grew up watching the topside
expand and the trust fund shrink.
She knows both histories with precision —
the boom from her parents,
the bust from the window.
She teaches both to her students.
She teaches them because
if they don't know the history,
they will only have the present,
and the present requires the history to make sense.

## Action
A student asks her in class:
why are we so poor if we were so rich?
The room goes quiet.
The question is the question.
Kinza says: let's find out.
She puts the trust fund figures on the board —
$1 billion in 1991.
$13 million in 2002.
She asks: what happened in between?
The class spends an hour on it.
Bad investments. Bad advice.
The musical that ran two weeks.
The properties bought high and sold low.
At the end the student says:
so other people made money from our money?
Kinza says: yes.
She says: and we let them, because we didn't know enough.
She says: this is why you are in this class.

## Shadow
She teaches the history as a warning.
The warning is true.
What she cannot see:
the lesson — know enough so you can't be exploited —
is a lesson that leads
the best students out of Nauru,
because knowing enough
requires going somewhere
that knowledge can be used.
She is teaching the students
who will apply the lesson
in Brisbane, in Auckland, in Taipei.
The students who stay
will have the lesson and nowhere to use it.
She is right to teach it.
The teaching and the staying
are in tension she does not examine.

## Tell
She writes the numbers on the board
before she explains them.
The numbers first.
Always the numbers first.
