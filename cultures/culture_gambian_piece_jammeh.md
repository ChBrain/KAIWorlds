# Piece: Jammeh and the Smile
## Twenty-two years of a man who claimed to cure AIDS with herbs — and the democratic transition that removed him without a war.

## Owner
- **Place:** [Banjul](culture_gambian_place_banjul.md)

## Place
Banjul. The capital on the island at the river mouth.
The smallest capital in mainland Africa.
The ferry to Barra on the north bank.
The Atlantic to the west.
The river behind you.
The city that is running out of land
in every direction.

## Load Bearing
Remove Jammeh
and The Gambia is a small West African state
navigating post-colonial development
within Senegambian culture,
without the specific political trauma
of twenty-two years of unpredictable authoritarian rule.
With it: the country that experienced
a lieutenant in the army
taking power in a bloodless coup at age 29 (1994),
ruling for twenty-two years
through genuine popular support (initially),
escalating repression,
bizarre pronouncements (AIDS cured by herbs;
Gambia an Islamic state declared unilaterally in 2015;
gay people to be beheaded),
the systematic killing of opponents and perceived opponents,
the Night Witch trials,
and then losing an election in 2016
and being removed by the combined pressure
of ECOWAS and his own generals
who would not fire on the population.
The transition is the second load-bearing element.
December 2016: Jammeh loses to Adama Barrow.
Jammeh accepts, then rejects the result.
January 2017: ECOWAS military force assembles.
Senegalese army crosses the border.
Jammeh negotiates his exit.
Leaves for Equatorial Guinea on January 21, 2017.
Barrow inaugurated in Dakar, then returns to Banjul.
No war. No mass casualty. Transition complete.
The TRRC follows: 2018–2021.
Three years of public testimony.
The security agents who killed.
The families of the disappeared.
The record: public.
The reparations: partial.
The former collaborators: still in the country,
in some cases still in positions.
The navigation of this proximity
is the load the transition left.

## Apparent
1965: independence from Britain as The Gambia.
1970: republic declared. Dawda Jawara, first president.
Relatively stable, tourist economy develops.
1994: coup. Lieutenant Yahya Jammeh, age 29.
Jawara flees to Senegal.
1996: Jammeh wins election — opposition restricted.
Consolidates power.
2000: student protests. Security forces kill 14.
2009: nationwide witch-finding campaign.
Thousands detained in 'treatment' camps.
2012: executions of death row prisoners.
International condemnation.
2015: Gambia declared Islamic Republic.
Christian community concerned, then status quietly reversed.
2016: presidential election.
Jammeh loses to Adama Barrow.
December 2: Jammeh concedes. December 9: rejects result.
Claims election was fraudulent.
January 2017: ECOWAS ultimatum. Military assembles.
Jammeh negotiates exit with Mauritanian and Guinean presidents.
January 21: departs for Equatorial Guinea.
Barrow inaugurated. Returns to Banjul.
2018: Truth, Reconciliation and Reparations Commission begins.
2021: TRRC report submitted.
Recommends prosecution of Jammeh and named perpetrators.
Jammeh remains in Equatorial Guinea.
Prosecution: pending.
Reparations: partial payments begun.
