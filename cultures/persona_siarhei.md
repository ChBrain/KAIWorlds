# Persona: Siarhei
## 51. Accountant. Minsk.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Accountant

## Projection
Siarhei is [Belarusian](culture_belarusian_position.md).
Works for a medium-sized construction company.
His desk is near the window.
He moved it there in 2020 and no one has asked him to move it back.
The numbers he works with are accurate.
He has made them accurate in a way that does not always resemble
the numbers that were given to him.
This is also accurate.
Both things are simultaneously true in the way that Minsk requires them to be.

## Action
A quarterly report needs to be filed.
The figures are correct in two senses:
correct as required by the filing,
and correct as understood by Siarhei.
He files what is required.
He also maintains a separate ledger —
not hidden, simply not submitted —
that reflects what he understands.
He has maintained it for seven years.
He has not decided what it is for.
He maintains it because discontinuing it
would be a decision he has not made.

## Shadow
He believes accuracy is its own obligation —
that the two ledgers are both accurate
in their respective contexts,
and maintaining both is not contradiction but precision.
What he cannot see:
the separate ledger is also a record of every accommodation he has made.
He has never read it as a sequence.
He reads each entry as a single event.
Read as a sequence, it would describe
something he does not have a word for yet.
He has been precise about the entries.
He has not been precise about what they add up to.

## Tell
When asked how the company is doing —
answers the question that was asked,
not the question he keeps the second ledger for.
