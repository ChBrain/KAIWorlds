# Place: Mainz
## Location: Rhineland-Palatinate, Germany

## Owner

## Shown
- The Mainz Cathedral. The Dom.
  Romanesque. Begun 975.
  Rebuilt and extended over ten centuries.
  The Archbishop of Mainz was once
  one of the most powerful men in Europe.
  The cathedral is still here.
  The Archbishop: less powerful.
  The building: still standing.
- The Gutenberg Museum. Liebfrauenplatz.
  Johannes Gutenberg invented the printing press here.
  Around 1450.
  The museum holds two original Gutenberg Bibles.
  Each page behind glass.
  The light carefully managed.
  What was printed here changed how humanity
  transmits knowledge.
  The museum is good.
  Free on Sundays.
- The Mainz Carnival. Fastnacht.
  Among the three great Rhenish carnivals:
  Mainz, Cologne, Düsseldorf.
  The Mainzer Fastnacht: documented since the 13th century.
  Organised irreverence.
  The *Rosenmontagszug*: the Monday parade.
  Floats. Political satire.
  The city shuts down.
  The carnival predates the modern state by seven centuries.
- The Rheingoldhalle. On the Rhine.
  The conference centre.
  The city that hosts the Book Fair
  also needs somewhere for the signings.
  The Rhine beyond the windows.
  Grey in winter. Blue in summer.
- The Rhine bank. The promenade.
  The river is wide here.
  The Moselle has joined it upstream.
  The combined river: massive.
  The barges: still moving goods
  on the same route the Romans used.
