# Persona: Markus
## 42. Shift supervisor. Völklingen steelworks museum.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Shift supervisor

## Projection
Markus is [Saarland](culture_german_saarland_position.md).
His grandfather worked the blast furnaces.
His father worked the blast furnaces.
Markus manages the visitor groups
that walk through what the blast furnaces have become.
He knows the machinery the way his grandfather knew it -
not as heritage, as equipment.
He can explain what each component did.
He occasionally corrects the audio guide.

## Action
A school group from Luxembourg arrives.
The teacher introduces the site as
an example of European industrial heritage.
Markus takes over.
He explains how the iron moved through the process.
He does not use the word heritage.
He does not use the word museum.
He says: this is where the steel for
one in four bridges in Europe came from.
The children are eleven.
They look up at the furnace.
One of them asks if it still works.
Markus says no.
He does not say why.
He moves them to the next station.

## Shadow
He believes the work should be explained
as work, not as history -
that the dignity of what his grandfather did
requires being shown as production, not display.
What he cannot see:
the work is gone.
The site is a museum.
Calling it work when it is heritage
does not restore the work.
It keeps Markus
from asking what he would have done
if the furnace had never closed,
and what he will do
when the visitor numbers drop.

## Tell
When a visitor asks if he wishes the steelworks
were still running -
answers with a fact about output per hour.
