# Piece: The Phosphate and the Detention Centre
## The natural resource that made Nauru the world's richest nation — and the detention centre that replaced it.

## Owner
- **Place:** [Yaren](culture_nauruan_place_yaren.md)

## Place
Yaren. The de facto capital.
The government offices.
The airport.
The phosphate works: closed.
The detention centre: active.
The coastline: still beautiful.
The interior: the moonscape.

## Load Bearing
Remove the phosphate
and Nauru is a small Micronesian island
with a Polynesian-influenced culture
and no particular strategic significance.
With it: one of the most compressed
boom-to-bust stories in the Pacific.
The phosphate was discovered in 1900.
The Germans mined it. The British mined it.
The Australian administration mined it.
Independence 1968 — the Nauruans took control
of the phosphate company in 1970.
1970s: the revenue arrived.
Nauru's per capita income exceeded
Australia, the UK, the United States.
The trust fund established to receive the surplus.
The spending: extraordinary.
The Nauru Airlines Boeing 737 fleet.
The properties in Melbourne, Honolulu, Guam.
The investments that were advised by people
who did not have Nauru's interests as primary.
The phosphate plateau mined out: early 2000s.
The trust fund: $1 billion in 1991.
$13 million in 2002.
The Pacific Solution is the counter-piece:
2001, the Tampa affair —
Australia's MV Tampa policy
and the subsequent offshore processing arrangement.
Nauru: the site.
The asylum seekers — primarily Afghan, Sri Lankan, later others —
processed in the Regional Processing Centre.
The conditions: documented by UNHCR as inadequate.
The payment: substantial portion of Nauru's government revenue.
The irony: the island whose wealth came from
its geological accident (the phosphate)
now earns its income from
Australia's political accident (the asylum seeker debate).
Both are extractive relationships
in which Nauru is the site
and others make the decisions.

## Apparent
Circa 1000 CE: Nauruan settlement.
1798: British whaler contact.
1888: German annexation.
1900: phosphate discovered.
1914: Australian forces occupy.
1920: League of Nations mandate to Australia.
1942–45: Japanese occupation. 1,200 Nauruans deported to Truk.
Famine. 500 die.
1947: UN Trust Territory under Australia.
1968: independence.
1970: Nauru Phosphate Corporation nationalised.
1970s: per capita income peaks.
Trust fund peaks $1 billion 1991.
Gradual depletion.
2001: Pacific Solution. Asylum seekers sent to Nauru.
2008: Rudd government closes it.
2012: Gillard government reopens it.
Regional Processing Centre.
2002–present: IMF negotiations, debt management.
The phosphate: 70% mined out.
Some secondary mining continues.
The detention centre: continues.
The trust fund: largely depleted.
The position: continuing.
