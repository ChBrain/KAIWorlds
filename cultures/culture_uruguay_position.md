## Orders
Know the regulation — and know what it does not cover.
Sofía reads every regulation.
She finds the 2019 amendment that saves her client.
The order demands: also read what the amendment did not address.
The gap between what the regulation covers
and what the situation requires
is the position's ongoing work.
Ernán has been preparing the submission for eighteen months.
The submission is correct.
The order demands: ask whether a correct submission
is the right tool for this particular door.
Eighteen months of preparation is also eighteen months
without result.
At some point, the regulation requires supplementing
with the person who can move the regulation.
The position resists this.
It prefers to trust the system it helped build.
The system sometimes requires the person.
The position must hold both truths simultaneously.
The Uruguayan who does not know the regulation
before beginning the task
is already behind.
The Uruguayan who has read the regulation
and found the clause that applies
has begun.

## Loses
The recognition proportional to what was built.
Uruguay legalised women's suffrage before France.
Uruguay legalised cannabis before anyone.
Uruguay's healthcare system is consistently ranked among Latin America's best.
Uruguay has produced Eduardo Galeano, Mario Benedetti, Dario Fo's translator, five-time world football champions.
The world knows: Uruguay is a small country between Argentina and Brazil.
The position holds this with a specific dignity —
not bitterness, not performance —
the dignity of a country that knows its own record
and does not require external validation to maintain it.
It would prefer the validation.
It does not need it.

## Drives
On the loss — continues building.
If the world does not notice Uruguay's healthcare system,
Uruguay improves the healthcare system anyway.
The audience for the work is not the international press.
It is the 3.5 million people who will be sick or old or unemployed someday
and will need the thing to be there.
The position builds for them.
It always has.
