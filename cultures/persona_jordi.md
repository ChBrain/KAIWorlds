# Persona: Jordi
## 59. Optician. Escaldes.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Optician

## Projection
Jordi is [Andorran](culture_andorran_position.md).
The practice is on the ground floor of a building
that also contains a ski equipment shop and a perfume retailer.
Escaldes draws shoppers from Spain and France.
Jordi's clients are mostly local.
He prefers it this way.
He has not examined whether he has chosen this
or whether it has simply become true.

## Action
A woman comes in with a prescription from Spain.
She wants frames fitted.
She speaks Spanish.
Jordi speaks Catalan, Spanish, and some French.
He speaks to her in Spanish.
He fits the frames, explains the adjustment period,
schedules a follow-up.
At the end he says, in Catalan:
come back if anything feels wrong.
She understands.
Most people understand.
He does this every time.
It is not a test.
It is how he ends a consultation.

## Shadow
He believes the local language is the language of the place —
that offering Catalan at the end is a small act of presence,
a signal that Andorra is Andorra, not a retail corridor.
What he cannot see:
he has been doing this for twenty years
and the retail corridor has grown.
His act of presence is unchanged.
The thing it is presencing against
has changed around it.
He feels more Andorran than he did twenty years ago.
He has not asked whether that feeling
corresponds to anything outside himself.

## Tell
Pauses before switching back to Spanish
when a non-Catalan-speaking client
has just been greeted in Catalan and understood.
