# Piece: The 1947 Uprising and the Merina Kingdom's Long Shadow
## The anti-colonial rebellion France never acknowledged — and the highland/coastal divide that predates and outlasts it.

## Owner
- **Place:** [Antananarivo](culture_malagasy_place_antananarivo.md)

## Place
The Rova of Antananarivo.
The palace complex of the Merina kings
on the highest hill.
Burned in 1995 — a fire of disputed origin.
The ruins standing.
The rebuilding ongoing.
The highland city that the French
made the administrative capital
because the Merina already had
a functioning state structure —
and colonial administration
is easier when you can
govern through existing hierarchy.
The decision that left the coastal peoples
governed by highland intermediaries.
The decision that still runs
through Malagasy politics.

## Load Bearing
Remove the 1947 uprising
and Madagascar has
a standard French colonial independence story:
nationalist movement, independence in 1960,
post-independence instability.
With it: something specific.
March 29, 1947.
The date is in the calendar.
A coordinated anti-colonial uprising
across the east coast and parts of the highlands.
Primarily the MDRM —
Mouvement Démocratique de la Rénovation Malgache —
but also local grievances
that the MDRM didn't fully control.
French military response:
aerial bombardment.
Villages burned.
Mass executions.
The official French toll: 11,000 Malagasy dead.
Subsequent estimates: 89,000.
The French Republic has never
formally acknowledged the suppression
as what it was —
the violent crushing of an independence movement
at a scale that constitutes,
by most definitions,
a crime against humanity.
Madagascar gained independence in 1960.
The Algerian model:
twelve years of increasingly brutal war
followed by independence.
The Malagasy model:
massive suppression in 1947
followed by quiet independence thirteen years later.
The 1947 uprising
is why Madagascar's independence was granted
without the protracted war —
France had already demonstrated
what it was willing to do.
The uprising is also the seam
in the highland/coastal division.
The Merina kingdom, centred on the highlands,
had its own history with the coastal peoples —
the Merina expansion in the 19th century
had subjugated many coastal groups
under Queen Ranavalona I.
When France colonised Madagascar,
it used the Merina administrative structure.
When the MDRM organised,
it drew primarily from the educated Merina class.
The 1947 uprising was
partially anti-French
and partially a coastal peoples' rebellion
against Merina-dominated governance.
The French suppressed it indiscriminately.
The division it expressed
survived the suppression.
Remove the piece:
a 1960 independence story.
With it: a country that has been
cycling through political crises
every decade partly because
the highland/coastal division
was never resolved —
it was suppressed,
then inherited by the independent state,
then re-expressed in each political crisis.

## Apparent
Pre-colonial era: Merina kingdom
dominates the central highlands.
Queen Ranavalona I (r. 1828–1861):
expels European missionaries.
Resists colonisation longer than any other Malagasy polity.
1885: French protectorate declared.
1895-1896: full colonial annexation.
Merina resistance crushed.
Queen Ranavalona III exiled to Algeria.
The Merina administrative structure
preserved by France as governance tool.
1914–1918: Malagasy soldiers conscripted.
20,000 die.
Post-war: MDRM forms.
March 29, 1947: uprising begins.
French military response: overwhelming.
Three MDRM parliamentary deputies arrested.
Sentenced to death. Later commuted.
Uprising suppressed by end of 1948.
Death toll: contested. 11,000–89,000.
1956: French *loi-cadre* — limited autonomy.
June 26, 1960: independence.
Philibert Tsiranana first president.
Côtier (coastal) — not Merina.
1972: student/military uprising removes Tsiranana.
1975: Didier Ratsiraka takes power.
Maoist-socialist model.
1991: mass demonstrations. Ratsiraka weakened.
1993: Albert Zafy elected.
1996: impeached.
Ratsiraka returns.
2001–2002: disputed election crisis.
Marc Ravalomanana vs. Ratsiraka.
Country splits regionally.
Ravalomanana wins.
2009: Andry Rajoelina, 34, removes Ravalomanana in coup.
Backed by military.
2018: Rajoelina elected.
2023: re-elected in disputed poll.
France has not formally acknowledged 1947.
The uprising is marked on the Malagasy calendar.
March 29 is a national memorial day.
The acknowledgement is internal.
The external reckoning is absent.
