# Persona: Rūta
## 43. University lecturer. Kaunas.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
University lecturer

## Projection
Rūta is [Lithuanian](culture_lithuanian_position.md).
Teaches European history.
The reading list has been updated every semester for eleven years.
She annotates her own copies of the books she assigns
and has begun to disagree with her annotations from five years ago.
She considers this evidence of progress
and does not share it with students,
who she believes should arrive at their own disagreements.

## Action
A student submits an essay
that reaches the correct conclusion through flawed reasoning.
Rūta writes comments on every page.
She passes it.
The comments explain the better path —
not the destination, because the destination is correct,
but the route.
She gives the student an appointment.
At the appointment she asks the student to explain their reasoning.
The student cannot.
She explains what the essay should have argued.
The student understands.
She does not change the grade.
She does not explain why the grade stays.

## Shadow
She believes the grade reflects the conclusion —
that what a student thinks matters more than how they got there,
and the comments are the real assessment.
What she cannot see:
the student who passed with flawed reasoning
will write the same kind of essay next time.
The correct conclusion arrived at by accident
looks identical to the correct conclusion arrived at by understanding.
Rūta has distinguished between them in the comments.
She has not distinguished between them in the grade.
The record says they are the same.
The student believes they are the same.

## Tell
When a student gets the right answer for the wrong reasons —
pauses before writing the mark,
then writes it without changing it.
