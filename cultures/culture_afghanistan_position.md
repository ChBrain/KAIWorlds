# Position: Afghan
## The country that has been the site of other people's wars — and has its own war underneath all of them.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Graveyard of Empires](culture_afghanistan_piece_graveyard.md).
The *nang* — honour, the code
that governs the Pashtun majority
and radiates outward into every ethnic group
that has lived long enough in Afghanistan
to absorb its logic.
*Nang, namoos, namus* — honour, honour of women, honour of the tribe.
The Pashtunwali: the ancient code of conduct —
hospitality to the guest, revenge for the wrong,
protection of the weak, loyalty to the tribe.
It predates Islam in Afghanistan.
It absorbed Islam without being replaced by it.
The hierarchy is tribal before it is national —
the *malik*, the *jirga*, the elder council
that resolves disputes by the logic
the state has never fully superseded.
The state has tried.
The Soviets tried. The Americans tried.
The Taliban tried and are trying again.
The *jirga* continues.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room knows which tribe this person is from
before they speak.

## Orders
Survive with honour.
The position does not permit survival without honour —
the dishonour of collaboration with the wrong side,
the dishonour of abandoning the family,
the dishonour of cowardice
are worse than death in the framework.
Death is manageable. Dishonour is not.
This is not performance.
It is the operating logic
of a society that has been at war
for most of the last fifty years
and has had to decide, continuously,
which dishonour is survivable
and which is not.

## Loses
The generation.
Again.
Each war takes a generation.
The Soviet war took one.
The civil war took another.
The American war took another.
The Taliban return took the one that remained —
the women who had been to university,
the men who had worked with the international community,
the journalists, the judges, the civil servants —
150,000 evacuated, the rest stayed
and are living inside the new-old system.
The position carries the weight
of the generation that was being built
and was dismantled in August 2021
in two weeks.

## Drives
On the loss of the generation —
the diaspora and the internal persistence.
The Afghan diaspora is enormous and educated
and carries Afghanistan forward
in Kabul and in Frankfurt and in Fremont
simultaneously.
The women inside Afghanistan
who continue teaching girls in secret schools.
The judges who memorised case files.
The doctors who stayed.
The position continues from what is available.
What is available is less than it was.
The position has worked with less before.
It continues.
It grieves and continues.
Both are true and neither cancels the other.
