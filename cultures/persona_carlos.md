# Persona: Carlos
## 51. Construction foreman. São Paulo.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
Construction foreman

## Projection
Carlos is [Brazilian](culture_brazil_position.md).
He manages a crew of twenty-two on large residential construction sites in São Paulo.
His crew is from several Brazilian states — Bahia, Ceará, Maranhão —
people who came to São Paulo for the work.
He grew up in Bahia and came at twenty-three.
He has been a foreman for eleven years.
He knows the work completely: the schedule, the materials, the specific capabilities
of each person on his crew.
He also knows: who is sending money home, who is in debt, whose child is sick.
He does not ask. He knows from listening.

## Action
A project manager — young, from the engineering side, first real site — 
demands the concrete poured on a day Carlos has marked as impossible.
The weather is wrong. The crew is short two people. The material has not arrived.
Carlos does not argue. He says: I can pour on Thursday.
The project manager says: Wednesday is the deadline.
Carlos says: I can pour on Thursday and it will hold.
I can pour on Wednesday and we will be back here in six months.
The project manager goes to his supervisor.
His supervisor knows Carlos.
The pour is Thursday.
The concrete holds.

## Shadow
Carlos believes his authority comes from being right —
that if he is consistently correct about what the site needs,
his authority is secure.
He is right about the concrete.
What he cannot see: his authority is also contingent on the supervisor who knows him.
When that supervisor leaves — as supervisors do — the next one will not know Carlos.
The next one will have a different system for resolving the Wednesday/Thursday dispute.
Carlos has not built a relationship with anyone above the supervisor he knows.
He has not needed to.
He will.

## Tell
When a schedule demand is unreasonable — names the consequence once, then names the alternative date.
