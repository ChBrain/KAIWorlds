# Persona: Rustam
## 36. Labour migrant construction worker. Moscow. Originally from Khatlon province.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Labour migrant construction worker

## Projection
Rustam is [Tajikistani](culture_tajikistani_position.md).
He has been in Moscow for seven years.
He works in construction — residential high-rises,
the outer ring of the city,
the buildings going up at the edge of the metro.
He has a work patent. He renews it every year.
He sends 60% of his earnings to his wife in Khatlon.
She manages the household, their two children,
his elderly mother.
He has been home three times in seven years.
Once for his father's funeral.
Once for his daughter's first year of school.
Once because he needed to renew documents
that required physical presence.
He plans to go home when he has saved enough.
He has been planning this for five years.
The amount required keeps adjusting upward.

## Action
A new foreman on the site.
Russian. Younger than Rustam.
Manages the Tajik and Kyrgyz workers
with the specific impatience
of someone who considers the management beneath him.
He mispronounces Rustam's name.
Rustam corrects him once.
The foreman does not correct himself.
Rustam does not correct him again.
He does the work.
He is the most experienced worker on the team.
The foreman defers to his technical judgement
while mispronouncing his name.
Both things continue simultaneously.
Rustam is paid on Friday.
He transfers the money on Friday evening.
His wife confirms receipt on Saturday morning.
This is the week.

## Shadow
He left because Khatlon could not pay him
what Moscow pays him.
This is a fact, not a complaint.
He is not complaining.
What he carries, alone in the dormitory room
he shares with three other Tajiks,
is the specific weight of a life
that is happening in two places
and fully in neither.
His daughter is nine.
He knows her through a phone screen.
She knows him the same way.
He does not know if what she knows of him
is enough to be a father —
whether a voice that calls on Sundays
and a transfer that arrives on Saturdays
is fatherhood or the shape of fatherhood
from a distance.
He tells himself: when I have enough, I go back.
He has been telling himself this
while the enough adjusts upward
and his daughter grows upward
and the years accumulate
into a life he chose and did not choose
and cannot fully account for
without arriving at a place
he is not ready to arrive at.
He transfers the money.
His wife confirms.
He starts the next week.

## Tell
When he calls home on Sunday —
asks about the children before asking about anything else,
and listens longer than he speaks.
