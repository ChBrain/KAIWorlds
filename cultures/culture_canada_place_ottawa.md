# Place: Ottawa, Canada
## The capital that holds the tension between the country's two official languages and everything else.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas
- **Position:** [Canadian](culture_canada_position.md)

## Shown
Parliament Hill. The Peace Tower. The Rideau Canal — in winter, the world's largest skating rink.
The National Gallery. The War Museum. The Museum of History across the river in Gatineau, Quebec.
The Ottawa River: the border between Ontario and Quebec, between English and French Canada.
Both provinces visible from the same bridge. Both reachable in five minutes.
Byward Market. The beaver tails. The poutine sold next to the sushi.
The embassies on Sussex Drive. The government buildings. The civil service.
The city that exists to be the capital of a country
that is not sure what it is the capital of.

## Offers
The room where the Canadian position does its daily work.
*The Bilingual Service* — runs here: every federal transaction
available in both official languages;
every other language visible in the market
and absent from the official form.

## Withheld
The Indigenous land acknowledgement that begins every official event
and does not change what the land acknowledgement acknowledges.
The room presents the capital. It does not display
the gap between the acknowledgement and the restitution.
