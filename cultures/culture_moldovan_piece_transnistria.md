# Piece: Transnistria
## The frozen conflict on Moldovan soil since 1992.

## Owner
- **Place:** Chișinău

## Place
Chișinău. The Parliament of the Republic of Moldova.
Also: the Nistru River - the eastern boundary of Moldova proper.
Transnistria is east of the river.
Transnistria is not administered from Chișinău.
It is administered from Tiraspol.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Moldovan position loses its
most concrete evidence of the eastern pull.
Transnistria is not a political abstraction.
It is a territory on Moldovan maps
under a different government,
with Russian peacekeepers,
powered by Russian gas until 2024.
The western direction Moldova has chosen
is chosen with Transnistria on the left.
The load bearing is the weight that does not dissolve.

## Apparent
A sliver of territory. 4,163 square kilometres.
East of the Nistru River.
Self-declared republic since 1990.
Recognised by: no UN member state.
Russian peacekeepers: present since 1992.
The hammer and sickle on the flag.
Soviet-era iconography maintained.
The border between Moldova and Transnistria:
not a hard border. Not a soft border.
A line that is present when it needs to be.

## Yearbook
1990: Transnistria declares sovereignty.
1992: the War of Transnistria.
Five months. 700–1,500 dead.
Ceasefire. Russian 14th Army - peacekeepers - remain.
Moldova internationally recognised.
Transnistria internationally unrecognised.
Both persist.
2014: Moldova signs EU Association Agreement.
Transnistria is within the agreement's territory.
Transnistria does not cooperate.
2022: Moldova applies for EU. Candidate status granted.
Russian gas through Transnistria: cut January 2025.
Moldova reroutes energy supply.
The frozen conflict is still frozen.
The EU application continues regardless.
