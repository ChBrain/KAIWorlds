## Shown
Derek Walcott Square. The central square in Castries, named 2017 after the poet's death.
The enormous saman tree — the largest in the Caribbean — shades the square.
The tree is older than the square's current name.
The Minor Basilica of the Immaculate Conception: French Catholic, British colonial, Caribbean present — all in the same space.
The Morne Fortune. The hill above Castries. The old British and French fortifications.
The view: the entire harbour, the island's green mountains, the Atlantic beyond.
The banana fields. The Cul de Sac valley south of Castries.
The Windward Islands bananas that once filled British supermarkets.
The fields are smaller now — the WTO ruling ended the EU preference arrangement.
The Pitons. Gros Piton and Petit Piton — the twin volcanic spires on the southwest coast.
UNESCO World Heritage Site. The most photographed thing in Saint Lucia.
St. Mary's College. The school where Arthur Lewis and Derek Walcott both studied. Still operating.
