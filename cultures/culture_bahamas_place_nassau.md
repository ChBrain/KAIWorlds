# Place: Nassau, Bahamas
## Location: Bahamas

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas
- **Position:** [Bahamian](culture_bahamas_position.md)

## Shown
Bay Street. The commercial strip that gave the Bay Street Boys their name.
Straw Market. Rawson Square — where independence was declared, July 10, 1973.
The pink colonial buildings of Parliament Square.
The Supreme Court. The Senate building.
All of them built for a colonial order. All of them now operating under Bahamian governance.
The contrast is visible and unremarked upon by anyone who lives here.
The cruise ship terminals: several. The ships: enormous.
The ratio of cruise ship visitors to overnight tourists to residents
shifts the character of Bay Street by the hour.
At 8am: the city belongs to itself. By 10am: it is working for visitors.
The Fish Fry at Arawak Cay: what the city eats when not performing for tourists.
Fried snapper. Conch fritters. Cracked conch. Peas and rice.
The Junkanoo Shack off West Bay Street:
where the Valley Boys costume workshop operates year-round.
The workshop is not a tourist attraction.
It is where the work happens.

## Offers
The room where the Bahamian position does its daily work.

the city organises itself around it without announcing that this is what it is doing.

## Withheld
The Out Islands — the 700 cays and islands that are not Nassau.
The Bahamas is an archipelago. Nassau is where 70% of the population lives.
The room shows Nassau. It does not show Andros, or Long Island, or Inagua.
Those are different rooms, carrying different weights.
The flamingos on Inagua have no relationship to Bay Street.
Both are the Bahamas.
