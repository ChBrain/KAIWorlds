# Position: Mozambican
## The liberation front that became the state — and the province that fought hardest for it now fights against it.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[FRELIMO, the Forgotten North, and the Cabo Delgado Insurgency](culture_mozambican_piece_frelimo.md).
Maputo. The coast.
The Indian Ocean.
10,000 kilometres of coastline.
The Zambezi River.
The Makonde plateau in the north —
where the liberation war began in 1964,
where FRELIMO's first fighters came from,
where the gas is now.
The Tsonga, Makua, Makonde, Sena, Ndau, Shona —
over 40 ethnic groups.
Portuguese as the official language.
The *língua franca* that colonial rule imposed
and independence kept
because no single indigenous language
would serve as neutral ground.
The collectivism: high.
The *ubuntu* variant of mutual obligation
runs through the family and community structures.
The hierarchy: real in both formal
and traditional senses —
the *régulo* (traditional chief)
and the FRELIMO district secretary
both exercise authority
in the same village.

## Orders
*Calma* — calm, steadiness.
The Mozambican position navigates
one of the world's poorest countries
with one of Africa's most promising
offshore gas deposits,
a liberation party that has governed
since 1975,
a civil war that ran from 1977 to 1992,
and an insurgency in the north
that has been running since 2017.
All simultaneously.
Calma is not indifference.
It is the practice of not being destroyed
by what cannot be immediately resolved.

## Loses
The liberation promise.
FRELIMO was not just a party.
It was the only organisation
that unified Mozambique's independence movement,
fought the Portuguese for ten years,
and built the first institutions
of an independent state.
The promise was: liberation for all.
The delivery: a party that became
the only legal political entity,
then a nominally multiparty state,
then an increasingly authoritarian government
that ran a secret $2 billion debt
without parliamentary approval
and let the north — Cabo Delgado,
the province that bled most for liberation —
rot in poverty while the gas deals were signed.
The insurgency that erupted in 2017
in Cabo Delgado
erupted in FRELIMO's heartland.
That is the position's deepest loss.

## Drives
On the loss —
the coastline.
The beaches that have no rival in East Africa.
The music — *marrabenta*, the coastal rhythm
that runs from Maputo to the diaspora.
The Mozambican civil society —
fragile, underfunded, persistent —
that documented the hidden debt,
that advocates in the courts,
that trains the journalists.
The offshore gas — if managed for the country
rather than the party —
could still change the arithmetic.
The *if* is doing a great deal of work.
The position holds it
while working on the conditions
that would make it less speculative.
