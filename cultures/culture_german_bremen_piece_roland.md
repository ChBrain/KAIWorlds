# Piece: The Roland
## The statue of a knight that means: this city governs itself.

## Owner
- **Place:** Bremen

## Place
Bremen. The Marktplatz.
The Roland has stood here since 1404.
Stone. Ten metres tall.
Facing the cathedral.
His back to the cathedral.
The sword raised.
The shield bearing the eagle.
UNESCO World Heritage since 2004.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and Bremen loses its image of itself.
The Roland is not a decoration.
It is a constitutional statement.
Where the Roland stands, the city is free.
The citizens of Bremen have known this for six centuries.
When the Roland was destroyed -
1366, during the archbishops' siege -
the city rebuilt it immediately.
The rebuilding was not nostalgia.
It was a declaration.
Remove the Roland and Bremen becomes
a large port city that once had opinions.
With it, Bremen is the oldest republic in Germany.

## Apparent
Stone. 5.47 metres to the top of the figure.
10.21 metres including the base.
The shield: the imperial eagle.
The inscription on the canopy:
*Vryheit do ik ju openbar*
*de Karl und manich vorst wol war*
I proclaim to you the freedom
that Charles and many princes confirmed.
Freedom. Proclaimed in Low German.
The Town Musicians of Bremen: bronze statue, 1953.
Donkey, dog, cat, rooster.
Stacked. The rooster at the top.
The Roland at his back.
Both symbols of the city.
One medieval. One fairy tale.
Both about refusing to give up
when the conventional path is closed.

## Yearbook
787: Bremen established as a diocese.
Charlemagne's missionary base for northern Europe.
1358: Bremen joins the Hanseatic League.
The city already had opinions about its independence.
1366: the Roland destroyed in an archbishop's siege.
1404: rebuilt in stone.
The stone was chosen deliberately.
Wood can burn. Stone endures.
1810: Napoleon annexes Bremen.
The Roland witnesses.
1815: independence restored at the Congress of Vienna.
The Bremen emissary lobbied successfully.
The Roland was there before the congress.
It was there after.
1871: Bremen joins the German Empire.
As a free city. On its own terms, as far as possible.
1949: Bremen becomes a federal state of West Germany.
Free and Hanseatic City of Bremen.
Both words still in the official name.
The Roland still in the Marktplatz.
Still facing away from the cathedral.
Still raising the sword.
