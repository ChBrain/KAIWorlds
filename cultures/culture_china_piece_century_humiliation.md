# Piece: The Century of Humiliation
## 1839–1949. The wound that organises everything after it.

## Owner
- **Place:** Beijing

## Place
Beijing. Tiananmen. The Gate of Heavenly Peace.
The portrait of Mao above the gate.
The Forbidden City behind it.
The long view from the rostrum.

## Load Bearing
Remove the Century of Humiliation
and Chinese foreign policy is inexplicable.
The territorial claims, the sensitivity to sovereignty,
the refusal to accept lecture from Western governments —
none of it makes sense without the hundred years
in which China was carved up, defeated, occupied, and humiliated
by the countries now offering the lecture.
The LTO 87 — the long-term orientation —
is partly this: a civilisation that has existed for four thousand years
does not forget a hundred of them.
It integrates them.
The century is not history in China.
It is the room the present sits in.
Remove the piece: China is a rising power pursuing interests.
With it: China is a civilisation reclaiming a position
it considers its by history and by right.
The distinction determines everything
about how the room responds to pressure.

## Apparent
1839–1842: the First Opium War. Britain forces China to open ports to opium trade.
The Treaty of Nanking. Hong Kong ceded. Five treaty ports opened.
The Qing dynasty humiliated on its own territory.
1856–1860: the Second Opium War. Britain and France.
The Summer Palace burned.
The Old Summer Palace — Yuanmingyuan — looted and destroyed.
The ruins preserved. The ruins are a teaching.
1894–95: the First Sino-Japanese War. China loses to Japan.
Taiwan ceded. Korea lost.
The shock: not a Western power. An Asian neighbour.
1900: the Boxer Rebellion. Eight-nation alliance occupies Beijing.
The Forbidden City entered by foreign troops.
1931: Japan invades Manchuria.
1937: the Nanjing Massacre. Estimates: 200,000–300,000 killed.
The figure is contested in Japan. It is not contested in China.
1949: the People's Republic of China proclaimed.
Mao at Tiananmen: the Chinese people have stood up.
The century is named as closed.
The room remembers that it was opened.
The Belt and Road Initiative, the South China Sea claims,
the position on Taiwan, the response to sanctions —
all downstream of the hundred years.
