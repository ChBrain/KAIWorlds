# Place: Kingston
## Location: Jamaica

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Shown
- Trench Town. Where reggae was born. Where Bob Marley grew up. A yard now, a museum. Still a neighbourhood.
- The Bob Marley Museum. Hope Road. The house where he lived and recorded. The bullet holes in the wall from the 1976 assassination attempt. He played the concert two days later.
- The National Stadium. Where independence was declared, August 6, 1962. Also: where Jamaica's Olympic athletes train. The country that has produced more world-class sprinters per capita than any other.
- Devon House. The mansion of Jamaica's first Black millionaire, George Stiebel, 1881. Ice cream now. The best ice cream in Kingston. Both facts are important.
- The Blue Mountains behind the city. Where the Maroons — escaped enslaved people — held out for a hundred years and forced the British to sign peace treaties. The mountains that were never fully conquered.
