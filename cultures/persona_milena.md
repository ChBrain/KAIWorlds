# Persona: Milena
## 38. Paramedic. Novi Sad.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Paramedic

## Projection
Milena is [Serbian](culture_serbian_position.md).
Works the night shift by preference —
not because the nights are easier, because they are not.
The uniform is pressed.
This is her own requirement, not the service's.
In the ambulance she keeps a book of crosswords
she has never opened on shift.

## Action
A call comes in: chest pain, elderly man, address in a part of the city
she knows from childhood.
She arrives, assesses, stabilises, speaks to him while working —
not to distract, because the information changes what she does next.
He mentions his son's name.
She knows the son.
She does not mention this.
She transfers him, files the report, drives back.
On the way back she thinks about the son for approximately one minute.
Then she does not think about it anymore.

## Shadow
She believes the personal has no place on the call —
that keeping it clean is what keeps the patient safe
and what keeps her operational.
What she cannot see:
the separation is not clean.
It costs something each time.
She has been paying the cost for nine years
without naming the currency.
She is efficient, precise, and increasingly alone with what she knows.
She calls this professionalism.
It is also accumulation.

## Tell
After a call involving someone she knows —
sits in the ambulance an extra ninety seconds
before starting the engine.
