# Persona: Kodjo
## 44. Civil servant and opposition activist. Lomé.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Civil servant and informal opposition activist

## Projection
Kodjo is [Togolese](culture_togolese_position.md).
He works as a mid-level civil servant
in the Ministry of Education.
He is Ewe.
He is also, in private,
an organiser for one of the opposition coalitions.
He attends meetings. He helps draft communications.
He participates in the protests that happen.
He does not lead them.
He does not appear in photographs.
He has a civil service job.
It supports his family.
He would lose it
if his political activities were made known.
This is not theoretical.
He knows people who lost theirs.

## Action
A colleague at the Ministry
mentions that a department head
has been asked to report
on staff members who participate in opposition activities.
The colleague tells Kodjo quietly,
in passing.
No accusation. Just information.
Kodjo thanks him.
He says nothing else.
That evening he tells the opposition coalition:
someone in the department is reporting.
He does not say who.
He does not name himself.
He suggests the coalition move its meetings
to a different neighbourhood.
The meetings move.
He continues attending.
He continues working at the Ministry.

## Shadow
He believes he is more useful
inside the civil service
than outside it —
that having someone who understands
how the ministry works
and can observe what is happening
is worth the compromise of working for a government
he wants to change.
That the political work and the civil service work
are separate, and the separation keeps both alive.
What he cannot see:
the separation is becoming harder to maintain.
The department head who is reporting —
Kodjo suspects who it is.
He has not confronted the person.
He cannot.
The political work requires him to protect sources.
The civil service work requires him to be collegial.
His ability to do both
depends on the separation holding.
The separation is held together
by Kodjo's ability to compartmentalise.
He has been doing this for eight years.
The compartments are getting heavier.
He has not thought about
what he will do when one breaks.

## Tell
When processing something difficult at work —
organises his desk before leaving for the day.
Pencils aligned.
Papers stacked.
The desk in order when he cannot control
the room it is in.
