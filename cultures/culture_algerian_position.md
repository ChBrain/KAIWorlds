# Position: Algerian
## The country of a million martyrs — and the revolution the regime hijacked and the people keep trying to reclaim.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The War of Independence and the Hirak](culture_algerian_piece_war.md).
1954–1962: the war.
One million Algerians dead — the official figure.
The French tortured. The FLN also killed, including Algerians
who cooperated with France.
The violence was mutual and the asymmetry was real.
France lost. Algeria won.
The FLN declared itself the revolution.
The army declared itself the guardian of the revolution.
Both have governed Algeria ever since
in a system where power flows
from the military and the FLN downward,
not upward from elections.
The legitimacy was the war.
The war was sixty years ago.
The position carries the revolution
as both the founding achievement and the founding debt —
the thing the regime invokes to justify everything
and the thing the people invoke
to ask: *is this what we fought for?*
The collectivism is strong — IDV low.
*Djama'a* — the community, the group.
The extended family as the real social safety net.
The solidarity is deep and old.
The hierarchy: complex.
High in formal structures — the general, the minister.
But underneath: the suspicion of authority
that comes from a people who survived colonialism
by building parallel structures
the colonial power could not fully penetrate.
The house. The family. The private space.
The *hrig* — illegal emigration to Europe —
is the most eloquent statement
the country makes about itself:
young Algerians willing to cross the Mediterranean
in inflatable boats to escape what their parents
built the country to become.

## Orders
Hold the line.
Not as conservatism — as the specific Algerian stubbornness
that survived 132 years of French colonialism,
the FLN's internal purges,
the Black Decade of the 1990s,
and is still here.
*Saber* — patience with teeth.
The Algerian who cannot change the system
maintains dignity within it.
The private space is sovereign.
The family is the real republic.
The system can have the public sphere.
The private sphere is guarded.

## Loses
The future it kept being promised.
The FLN promised: independence, dignity, development.
The oil arrived. The money arrived.
The development did not distribute itself
in the way the promise implied.
The 1988 riots — the generation that had not fought the war
and did not accept the war as a governing credential.
The *décennie noire* — the Black Decade.
The 1991 election the military cancelled
when the Islamists looked set to win.
The civil war that followed.
200,000 dead.
Then Bouteflika for twenty years.
Then the Hirak of 2019 —
the most beautiful thing in Algeria's post-independence history:
millions in the streets, peacefully,
every Friday for fifty-three consecutive weeks.
Bouteflika fell.
The system remained.
The position carries the recurring loss
of the political moment that almost changed things
and then did not change things.

## Drives
On the loss —
*débrouillardise*. Making do.
The informal economy that keeps the country running
when the official economy is inefficient.
The Algerian intellectual tradition:
Camus, Derrida, Kateb Yacine, Assia Djebar —
the writers who took French, the language of occupation,
and used it to describe Algerian truth.
The music: raï, Kabyle, Chaabi.
The food: the best couscous debate
between Algerians and Moroccans
is a cultural institution in itself.
The resilience is not resigned.
It is furious and alive
and looking for the form
that will finally work.
