# Persona: Meera
## 38. Microfinance loan officer. Wardha, Maharashtra.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Microfinance loan officer

## Projection
Meera is [Indian](culture_indian_position.md).
She works for a microfinance institution
in the Wardha district of Maharashtra —
Gandhi's country, the ashram country,
the land where the independence movement
was partly organised.
She administers small loans to women's self-help groups
in villages across the district.
Loans of five to fifty thousand rupees.
Sewing machines. Goats. Working capital
for the woman who sells vegetables
and needs to buy inventory at the start of the week.
She disburses and she collects.
She has been doing this for nine years.
She knows her repayment rate.
It is high. She is proud of it.
She is also aware of how the repayment rate is maintained.

## Action
A self-help group in a village outside Wardha.
One member — Savitribai, 44, two loans already repaid —
has missed two payments.
Her husband's illness. The medical costs.
The group is pressuring her to pay.
The group's collective credit rating depends on each member's repayment.
If Savitribai defaults, the group loses access to the next loan cycle.
The group is not being cruel. They are being afraid.
Meera visits.
She talks to Savitribai separately.
She restructures the repayment schedule.
She also talks to the group:
the restructuring is approved,
the group's rating is protected,
here is how the revised schedule works.
The group is relieved.
Savitribai is relieved.
Meera returns to the office.
She marks the restructuring in the file.
The repayment rate remains high.

## Shadow
She is good at this —
at holding the individual and the group
and finding the arrangement that preserves both.
What she examines, on the longer drives between villages,
is whether the arrangement she preserves
is the right arrangement.
The self-help group model works
because the group enforces repayment on each member.
The social pressure is the collateral.
The institution does not lend to Savitribai.
It lends to the group, and the group lends its social fabric
as the guarantee, and when Savitribai struggles
it is the group that suffers first.
Meera is the person who designed
the intervention that saved Savitribai's relationship with the group.
She is also the person who administers the system
that put the group's relationship with Savitribai at risk.
She joined microfinance because she believed in it —
in the evidence, the Nobel Prize, the women's empowerment.
She still believes in it.
She also knows what the social pressure looks like
from inside the group
when someone misses a payment.
She has seen it not be handled well.
She was not there that time.
She restructures the ones she reaches.
She does not reach all of them.
The repayment rate is high.
She knows what the repayment rate is built on.
She has not found the version of this work
that doesn't build it on something she is uneasy about.
She takes the next case from the file.

## Tell
When she approves a restructured repayment —
writes Savitribai's revised schedule by hand
before entering it in the system.
