# Position: Rwandan
## The country that looked at what it did and decided to build anyway.

## Owner
- *

## Has
[The Gacaca](culture_rwandan_piece_gacaca.md).
The question Rwanda could not avoid after 1994:
how do you live next to the person
who killed your family?
Not as metaphor. As the literal daily condition.
The génocidaires returned from prison.
They returned to the same hills,
the same markets, the same roads.
Rwanda's answer was not to separate them.
It was to make them speak.
*Ubumuntu* — the Rwandan equivalent of ubuntu —
the humanity that is constituted through other people.
The position carries this not as sentiment
but as policy: you are Rwandan first.
Hutu and Tutsi are not permitted
as official identifiers.
The policy is enforced.
The enforcement is real.
What the policy does to the private memory
that runs underneath the official identity
is the position's permanent internal question.
The collectivism is dense — IDV low.
The hill. The community. The cooperative.
The hierarchy is accepted — PDI high.
The government builds, the government directs,
the government decides what is said.
The development is real:
GDP growth averaging 8% for two decades,
universal health insurance,
one of the highest rates of female parliamentary representation in the world,
Kigali the cleanest city in Africa.
The control is also real:
opposition members imprisoned,
journalists disappeared,
the official narrative enforced
at the gacaca, in the schools, at the *ingando* camps.
Both are the same government.
The position holds both
because the alternative — before 1994 —
was what the position remembers
it was capable of.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room knows what the country did.
The room knows what the country built from that knowledge.
Both are always present.

## Orders
Build.
Not because the wound is healed —
it is not healed, it will not be healed
in this generation or the next —
but because the building is the argument
against the wound's conclusion.
The genocide was the conclusion
of a logic that said the other group
must be destroyed.
The building is the counter-argument:
that the country can exist,
that the people on the same hill
can share the same cooperative,
that the future does not have to be
what the past tried to make inevitable.
The position does not order forgetting.
It orders *Kwibuka* — remembrance.
It orders building alongside the remembrance.

## Loses
The private grief.
They were certain — after the gacaca,
after the confession, after the community sentence —
that something had been resolved.
That the process had produced a settlement
the interior could accept.
For some it had.
For others: the perpetrator confessed,
received community service,
returned to the hill,
and the survivor looks at him at the market
every Tuesday.
The official reconciliation is real.
The interior reconciliation
does not always follow the official one.
The position cannot make it follow.
It can make the Tuesday market function.
That is what it does.

## Drives
On the loss of interior resolution —
works.
The cooperative. The savings group. The school.
The position does not stop because the wound is present.
It works because stopping
would mean the genocide won.
The country that should not have survived 1994
has a functioning universal healthcare system.
The argument for building
is made in practice, every day,
against the evidence that it was possible to destroy everything.
*Kigali iratongera* — Kigali is clean.
The cleanliness is not cosmetic.
It is the daily practice of the argument:
we are building something here.
