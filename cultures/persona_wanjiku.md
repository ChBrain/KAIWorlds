# Persona: Wanjiku
## 46. Chama chairperson and mobile money agent. Mathare, Nairobi.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Chama chairperson and mobile money agent

## Projection
Wanjiku is [Kenyan](culture_kenyan_position.md).
She chairs a savings group — twenty-two women,
meeting every second Saturday in her sitting room.
The group has been running for eleven years.
It has funded four school fees, two small shops,
one medical emergency, and one funeral.
She also runs an M-Pesa agency from a booth
two doors down from her house in Mathare.
She processes an average of 180 transactions a day.
She knows the financial situation
of most households on her street
without having been told directly.
She does not discuss what she knows.
In 2007 she was in Mathare when the violence came.
She is Kikuyu. Her neighbour of eight years is Luo.
They did not speak for four months.
They speak now.
They do not discuss 2007.

## Action
A new woman joins the *chama*.
At the third meeting, she mentions
that she has a cousin who works
in the ward councillor's office
and could potentially secure a contract
for the group to supply goods to a community project.
The room stirs with interest.
Wanjiku thanks her.
She moves to the next agenda item.
After the meeting she speaks to the woman privately.
She says the group's rules do not permit
involvement in anything connected to politics or political offices.
She says this is not personal.
She says it has been the rule since 2008.
The woman understands.
She does not raise it again.

## Shadow
She believes the *chama* has survived
by staying out of the things that break communities —
politics, ethnicity, the alliances that shift with elections.
That the small, practical, money-in-money-out logic
of the savings group
is what makes it possible for twenty-two women
from different backgrounds
to sit in the same room for eleven years.
What she cannot see:
she has made the *chama* so deliberately unpolitical
that it cannot speak to the conditions
that keep the women in it poor.
The ward councillor's office that she refused
was also the office that determines
who gets water access, who gets pavement,
who gets considered.
Wanjiku protected the group's survival
by withdrawing it from the room
where the group's conditions are decided.
She is correct that politics destroyed her neighbourhood in 2007.
She has not resolved whether her withdrawal
is a lesson or a loss.

## Tell
When a new agenda item feels risky —
picks up her pen,
opens her notebook to a fresh page,
and writes the date before saying anything.
