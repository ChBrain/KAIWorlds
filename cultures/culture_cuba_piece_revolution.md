# Piece: The Cuban Revolution 1959
## The revolution that gave Cuba universal education and healthcare — and took something else.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas
- **Place:** [Havana, Cuba](culture_cuba_place_havana.md)

## Place
[Havana, Cuba](culture_cuba_place_havana.md)

## Load Bearing
Remove 1959 and Cuba is a US client state with extreme inequality,
a tourist economy serving American capital, a Batista government.
With it: Cuba is the country that demonstrated that a small Caribbean island
could defeat a US-backed dictator, nationalise its economy, build universal healthcare
and one of the highest literacy rates in the world —
and pay for this with sixty years of embargo, isolation, and internal constraint.
The position holds both: the achievement and the cost.
Remove the revolution: Cuba is interesting but unremarkable.
With it: Cuba is the ongoing argument about what was worth it.

## Apparent
1953: Fidel Castro attacks the Moncada Barracks. Fails. Imprisoned.
1956: the *Granma* lands. The guerrilla war in the Sierra Maestra.
January 1, 1959: Batista flees. The Revolution wins.
1961: the Bay of Pigs. CIA-backed invasion fails.
1962: the Missile Crisis. Thirteen days. The world watches.
The embargo: US economic blockade from 1962 to present.
The literacy campaign: 1961. 100,000 young volunteers. Illiteracy near-eliminated.
The healthcare system: free, universal, among the best outcomes in Latin America.
The *balsero* crisis: 1994. 35,000 Cubans cross the Florida Strait on rafts.
The Special Period: Soviet Union collapses 1991. Cuba loses its primary trade partner.
Extreme scarcity. The *resolver* culture intensifies.
2008: Raúl Castro. Reforms. Private business partially allowed.
2021: the July 11 protests. The largest protests since 1959.
Fidel: died 2016. The institutions he built continue.
The revolution is 65 years old. It continues in its current form.
