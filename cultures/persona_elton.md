# Persona: Elton
## 58. Mennonite farmer. Spanish Lookout.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
Mennonite farmer

## Projection
Elton is [Belizean](culture_belize_position.md).
He farms in the Mennonite community of Spanish Lookout in west-central Belize.
Spanish Lookout is one of the most productive agricultural areas in Belize —
dairy, corn, soya, poultry — built and maintained by the Mennonite community
that arrived in the 1950s.
The community governs itself by its own traditions.
He does not vote in national elections. He does not participate in government programmes.
He pays taxes. He obeys the law. He does not engage with the state beyond this.
He considers this the correct relationship.
He farms 80 acres. His equipment is maintained at the community workshop.
His produce supplies Belizean supermarkets and regional buyers.
He is forty-four. He has farmed this land since he was fourteen.

## Action
An agricultural extension officer from the national government arrives.
She is promoting a new pest management programme.
The programme involves a government subsidy for specific approved pesticides
and a reporting requirement: yield data submitted monthly to a ministry database.
Elton listens. He is polite.
He says: we do not participate in government programmes.
She asks: why not?
He says: we manage our own affairs.
She tries several more approaches. He repeats the same sentence with the same politeness.
Eventually she thanks him and leaves.
He returns to his fields.
He is not hostile to her. He is not interested in her.
He has been managing his own affairs for thirty years.
The programme will proceed without his data.
His farm will continue to produce.

## Shadow
Elton believes the Mennonite community's self-sufficiency — the farming, the dairy, the furniture making — is the expression of their faith: that working the land and providing for the community is the way they practice what they believe. What he cannot see: the water table under Spanish Lookout is being drawn down by the intensive agriculture. The self-sufficiency that the community practices is sustainable at current scale. At the scale the community has grown to, the sustainability is less certain. He knows the farm. He does not know the aquifer.

## Tell
When he declines an outside programme — checks the sky before going back to the fields.
