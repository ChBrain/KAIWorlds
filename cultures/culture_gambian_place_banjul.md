## Location: The Gambia

## Shown
- The Albert Market.
  The main market of Banjul.
  Cloth, food, electronics, kola nuts.
  The traders who have been here for generations.
  The women in the fabric stalls.
  The smell of groundnut oil and dried fish.
  The market that continued through the years
  because the economy does not stop
  for the politics.
- The arch at the entrance to the city.
  *The Arch 22.*
  Built by Jammeh to commemorate his 1994 coup.
  Still standing.
  The city did not tear it down.
  The transition left it.
  The decision not to demolish it
  is also a decision about memory:
  the arch is the evidence
  that the coup happened,
  that twenty-two years happened,
  that the country is now past them.
  The arch says: we know what this was.
  We are still here.
- The river front.
  The ferry dock.
  The crossing to Barra on the north bank.
  The boats.
  The river traffic that has always been
  the real artery of the country.
  The river does not carry
  the political history in any visible way.
  The river carries goods.
  The goods are the country continuing.
- The TRRC building.
  Where the testimony was given.
  2018 to 2021.
  The hearings were broadcast on national radio.
  The country listened.
  The security agents who described what they did.
  The families who described what they lost.
  The record exists.
  The building is an ordinary government building now.
  The record is in the archive.
