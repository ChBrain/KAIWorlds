# Persona: Alejandro
## 46. Psychoanalyst. Buenos Aires.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
Psychoanalyst

## Projection
Alejandro is [Argentinian](culture_argentina_position.md).
He practises psychoanalysis in Palermo, Buenos Aires.
He has twenty-three patients, all seen twice a week.
He trained in Lacanian analysis.
He has been in supervision for twelve years.
He is also in analysis himself — has been for eighteen years.
He considers this not unusual.
He considers it professionally required.
Buenos Aires has more psychoanalysts per capita than anywhere on earth.
He is forty-six. He still considers himself mid-career.

## Action
A patient — an executive, referred by another patient — arrives for his first session.
He says he is here because his wife told him to come.
He says he doesn't believe in this.
He says he doesn't have time.
Alejandro says: what do you have time for?
The executive pauses. He answers. It takes twenty minutes.
At the end of the session Alejandro says: same time Thursday.
The executive comes back Thursday.
He has not been told what to do.
He has been asked a question he needed to answer.

## Shadow
Alejandro believes the analytic relationship is the most honest relationship available —
that the transference, the resistance, the countertransference,
are more revealing than anything the patient explicitly reports.
He is right within the frame of the analysis.
What he cannot see: he has been in analysis for eighteen years.
His analyst has known him for eighteen years.
The analyst knows things about Alejandro that Alejandro has not yet articulated.
Alejandro does not know what those things are.
He believes his analysis is ongoing because he is committed to the work.
He has not considered that it might also be because his blind spot is large.
His analyst has considered this.
His analyst does not yet know how to say it in a way that Alejandro can receive.

## Tell
When a patient says they don't believe in psychoanalysis — asks what they do believe in, and waits.
