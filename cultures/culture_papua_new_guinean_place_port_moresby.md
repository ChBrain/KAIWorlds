# Place: Port Moresby
## Location: Papua New Guinea

## Shown
- The Parliament House. 1984.
  Designed by Cecil Hogan.
  The Haus Tambaran aesthetic —
  the spirit house architecture of the Sepik River
  translated into the national parliament.
  The building that says: this state
  is built from the cultures it contains.
  The cultures it contains:
  800 languages' worth.
  The building is genuine.
  The translation is ongoing.
- The LNG terminal at the harbour.
  The tankers.
  The infrastructure that ExxonMobil built.
  The gas flowing from the Highlands
  to the ships.
  The royalties flowing to the treasury.
  The treasury allocating to provinces.
  The provincial hospitals running out of drugs.
  The pipeline visible from the city.
  The gap visible from everywhere.
- The settlements. *Settlements.*
  The informal housing on the hills.
  The corrugated iron.
  The wantok networks that organise them —
  Highlands people in one settlement,
  Sepik people in another,
  the language geography of the village
  reproduced in the urban margin.
  The settlements that are the real story
  of Port Moresby's population
  and the last part of the city
  mentioned in development reports.
- The National Museum and Art Gallery.
  The masks. The canoes. The bilums.
  The *sing-sing* photographs.
  The biodiversity specimens.
  The record of what the country is
  before it became a state.
  The record is partial.
  800 languages means 800 material cultures.
  The museum holds some.
  The villages hold the rest.
