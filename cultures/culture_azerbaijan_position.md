# Position: Azerbaijani
## The country at the crossroads that learned to play all sides — and won Karabakh back.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[Karabakh and the Oil](culture_azerbaijan_piece_karabakh.md).
The Turkish-Islamic-secular triangle.
Azerbaijan is majority Shia Muslim
and majority secular in practice —
a combination that confuses outsiders
and the position navigates without confusion.
The Islam is the inheritance.
The secularism is the Soviet legacy
that the position decided to keep.
The Turkish kinship — "one nation, two states" —
is the regional alliance.
The Iranian border to the south
and the 15–20 million ethnic Azerbaijanis in Iran
who are more Azerbaijanis than in Azerbaijan itself —
is the unresolved demographic fact
the position does not press too loudly.
The Aliyev dynasty: Heydar from 1993,
his son Ilham from 2003.
The oil funded the dynasty.
The dynasty funded the army.
The army won Karabakh in 44 days in 2020.
The position is not modest about this.
It was not modest before.
After the victory, it is less modest still.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room knows: we won.

## Orders
Project strength.
The position that was frozen for thirty years —
the Karabakh conflict locked,
the displaced persons in camps,
the territory occupied —
is now the position that resolved
what the world said could not be resolved militarily.
It resolved it militarily.
The lesson the position drew:
strength works when diplomacy did not.
The position now applies this lesson
to every room it enters.
The confidence is genuine.
It is also the confidence of a position
that has not yet learned
what winning produces next
when the winning was harder than the waiting.

## Loses
The critique.
They were certain the victory would produce recognition —
that winning back the territory,
resettling the displaced,
rebuilding the cities would be seen as restoration.
The rooms in Brussels and Washington
saw also: the displacement of Armenians from Karabakh,
the 100,000 who left in September 2023.
The position does not accept this framing.
It has a counter-framing that is also true:
the thirty years the Azerbaijanis were displaced,
the 800,000 who lived in camps.
Both displacements are real.
The position presents its own.
It does not present the other.
This is the position's current limit.

## Drives
On the loss of the critique —
the economy.
Baku is being rebuilt with oil and gas revenue
and the ambition of a country
that wants to be taken seriously
as more than a pipeline.
The COP29 hosting in 2024 —
an oil state hosting the climate summit —
is the position at its most characteristic:
playing all sides, sitting at every table,
defining itself as the bridge
and making sure the bridge is essential.
The position knows it is running out of oil.
It is building the next argument
before the oil runs out.
It is not certain the next argument is ready.
It builds it anyway.
