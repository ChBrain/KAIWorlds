# Piece: The Federal Charter
## The oath sworn on a meadow in 1291 that Switzerland keeps renewing.

## Owner
- **Place:** Bern

## Place
Schwyz. The Federal Charter Archive.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and Swiss consensus loses its reason.
The confederation chose to stay together when apart was easier
and has been choosing it again every generation since 1291.
The Charter is the record of that choice.

## Apparent
A sheet of parchment. Latin, mostly. Approximately 400 words.
Three forest cantons: Uri, Schwyz, Unterwalden.
The original ink faded.
The original in the Schwyz archive.
On display once a year: August 1st.

## Yearbook
1291: the three cantons swear the oath.
August 1st is now the national holiday.
1315: Battle of Morgarten. The Swiss defeat the Habsburgs.
The confederation holds under pressure.
1648: Peace of Westphalia. Swiss independence recognised
by the great powers of Europe.
The neutrality institutionalised.
1848: the modern federal constitution.
The 1291 charter does not become obsolete.
It becomes origin myth.
Origin myths are more durable than constitutions.
1971: women granted the vote at federal level.
The consensus took until 1971.
This is also part of the piece.
2023: Switzerland holds its 733rd national day.
The meadow in Rütli where the oath was sworn
is accessible by boat only.
Every August 1st, people go by boat.
