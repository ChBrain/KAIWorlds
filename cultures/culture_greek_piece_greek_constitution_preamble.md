# Piece: The Orthodox Preamble
## The constitution that opens in the name of the Trinity.

## Owner
- **Place:** Athens

## Place
Athens. The Hellenic Parliament.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and Greek argument loses its theological ground.
The Orthodox preamble is not decoration -
it is the frame that survived three thousand years
of intersection between East and West, ancient and modern.

## Apparent
The preamble: *In the name of the Holy
and Consubstantial and Indivisible Trinity.*
The Archbishop of Athens present at every presidential swearing-in.
*Christos Anesti* - Christ is Risen - exchanged as greeting
by people who may not attend church the other 364 days.

## Yearbook
1821: the Greek War of Independence.
The Orthodox Church organises resistance.
The Bishop of Patras raises the flag at a monastery.
1833: independent Greece. First constitution.
Orthodox Christianity the established religion.
Every constitution since: the same preamble.
1967–1974: the military junta.
The church navigates.
1974: democracy restored. New constitution.
Same preamble.
2015: Syriza government. Secular left.
The preamble unchanged.
The argument about the church's role in the state:
ongoing since 1833.
The preamble: unchanged for nearly 200 years.
The argument is the Greek position.
The preamble is what the argument is about.
