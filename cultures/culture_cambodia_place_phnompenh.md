## Shown
- The Royal Palace. The Silver Pagoda. The Throne Hall. Gold and white on the riverfront. Sihanouk's ashes in a stupa in the grounds. The king is present. The city is arranged around his presence.
- Tuol Sleng — S-21. The school that was a prison. The photographs on the walls. The rooms unchanged. The silence that visitors arrive in and leave in. Open every day.
- The Mekong and Tonle Sap confluence. The rivers meet here. The Tonle Sap reverses direction twice a year — the only river in the world that flows backward. The Khmer built their civilisation on this reversal, the flood that fertilises the plains.
- The Central Market — Phsar Thmei. The art deco dome, 1937. The stalls inside: gold, electronics, clothing, food. The city's commercial heartbeat, unchanged in function across every government.
- The riverside at dusk. The families, the students, the food vendors. The city that was emptied in 1975 and is full again. The fullness is the argument.
