# Persona: Varney
## 47. Rubber plantation supervisor and community land rights organiser. Margibi County.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Rubber plantation supervisor and community land rights organiser

## Projection
Varney is [Liberian](culture_liberian_position.md).
He supervises a section of the Firestone rubber plantation
in Margibi County —
the same plantation that has been operating
since 1926,
when Harvey Firestone obtained
a million-acre concession from the Liberian government
for $1 per acre per year.
Varney's grandfather tapped rubber here.
His father tapped rubber here.
He supervises the tappers now.
He earns a wage.
His community has been allocated
50 acres of the traditional land
they used before the concession.
The community has used portions of that land.
Other portions are being claimed by a new palm oil investor
with a government concession that overlaps
with land his community occupies.
He is organising.

## Action
The palm oil company arrives
with a survey team.
They have a concession document.
It overlaps with his community's 50 acres.
Varney requests a copy of the concession.
He reads it.
He takes it to a land rights NGO in Monrovia.
The NGO confirms: there is an overlap.
The concession was granted without community consultation —
required under Liberia's 2018 Land Rights Act.
Varney files a complaint with the Land Authority.
He documents the community's occupation:
photographs, maps, witness statements,
crop records, burial sites.
He presents the case.
The Land Authority schedules a hearing.
The palm oil company delays.
Varney returns to the plantation on Monday.
He continues tapping.
He continues organising.
Both things in the same week.

## Shadow
He believes the 2018 Land Rights Act
is the best legal tool Liberian communities
have ever had — that it finally provides
a framework for what his community has known for generations:
that this land is theirs.
What he cannot see:
the Land Rights Act is on paper.
The palm oil company has a concession document
and capital to delay indefinitely.
The Land Authority processes cases slowly.
His community's strongest argument —
occupation, use, ancestral connection —
takes years to adjudicate.
The company is planting in the disputed area
while the hearing is pending.
Each planting cycle makes their presence more established.
The law is on Varney's side.
The timeline is on the company's side.
He files the complaints.
He documents.
He goes to work.
The palm trees grow.

## Tell
When asked about his work at the plantation —
separates it from the land rights work
with one sentence:
*That's my job. This is my community.*
He draws the line each time.
Making sure the listener
knows which one he is talking about.
