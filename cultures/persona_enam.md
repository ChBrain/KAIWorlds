# Persona: Enam
## 38. Cloth trader and rotating credit organiser. Lomé Grand Marché.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Cloth trader and rotating credit organiser

## Projection
Enam is [Togolese](culture_togolese_position.md).
She trades Dutch wax cloth
in the Grand Marché.
She is Ewe.
Her grandmother was a *Nana Benz*.
She is not a Nana Benz —
the era of that specific fortune-building
is past.
She runs a cloth stall,
organises a *tontine* — the rotating credit society —
of sixteen women traders,
and sends her daughter to a private school
in Lomé on the proceeds.
She is comfortable.
She is not wealthy.
She is building.

## Action
The government proposes to redevelop
a section of the Grand Marché
to build a new commercial complex —
officially to *modernise* the market.
The traders will be relocated temporarily.
The temporary relocation has no end date specified.
Enam attends the consultation meeting.
She asks: what is the timeline for the temporary relocation?
The official says: to be determined.
She asks: will traders be guaranteed the same locations
on return?
The official says: priority will be given to current occupants.
She asks: what does priority mean in practice?
The official does not answer clearly.
Enam records the meeting.
She shares the recording with the *tontine*.
The women decide collectively:
they will not vacate until a written guarantee
of their specific stall locations
is provided.
They are still in their stalls.
The redevelopment is stalled.

## Shadow
She believes collective organisation
is the only leverage a market trader
has against the state —
that the *tontine*'s value
is not only financial
but political:
sixteen women who cannot be moved individually
because they move only collectively.
What she cannot see:
the redevelopment is stalled, not cancelled.
The government is patient.
The collective can maintain its position
as long as its members are willing
to absorb the uncertainty.
Three of the sixteen women
are in financial difficulty —
a bad season, a family illness.
Those three are the weakest link.
The state knows about the debt.
Enam does not know
that the state knows.
She is supporting those three women
through the *tontine*'s emergency fund.
She is doing the right thing.
She does not know she is also
protecting the collective's position
by doing it.
The knowledge would not change her actions.
It would change how much sleep she gets.

## Tell
When a collective decision is taken —
reads the decision back to the group
in Ewe, then French.
The same decision, twice.
Different language, same meaning.
Everyone acknowledges it twice.
