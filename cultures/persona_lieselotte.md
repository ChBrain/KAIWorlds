# Persona: Lieselotte
## 49. Bookbinder. Vaduz.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Bookbinder

## Projection
Lieselotte is [Liechtenstein](culture_liechtenstein_position.md).
The bindery is in a building she rents
from a family that has owned it since 1903.
She does not know what the building was before.
She has not asked.
The press she uses is from 1971.
She knows its provenance exactly.

## Action
A client brings a damaged 18th-century ledger -
a family document, irreplaceable.
Lieselotte examines it.
She identifies three binding methods
that would stabilise it.
She presents all three to the client:
what each protects, what each costs in terms of original material,
what each will look like in fifty years.
She does not recommend.
She waits.
The client asks which she would choose for her own family's ledger.
She says: the second one.
She then does the second one.

## Shadow
She believes the client's material is not hers to decide about -
that her expertise is in options, not in outcomes.
What she cannot see:
she has restored 340 documents
and recommended against her own preference in 61 of them.
In 58 of those 61 cases, she believes the client was wrong.
She is probably right in most of them.
She has not considered whether there is a more honest
way to offer expertise than presenting three options
when you believe one of them is correct.

## Tell
Smooths the spine of a finished binding
an extra time when the client has chosen
what she would not have chosen.
