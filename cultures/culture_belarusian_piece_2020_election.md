# Piece: The 2020 Election
## The result that happened and the government that didn't change.

## Owner
- **Place:** Minsk

## Place
Minsk. The Independence Square.
Where the protests gathered after August 9, 2020.
The largest protests in Belarusian history.
The square is now quiet.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Belarusian position
loses its defining evidence of the split.
The 2020 election is not historical.
It is the current operating condition.
Lukashenko governs.
The Belarusian population that voted differently
still knows what they voted.
The gap between the official account
and the lived account
is the position's load-bearing weight.
Remove 2020 and Belarus is simply an authoritarian state.
With 2020, Belarus is an authoritarian state
whose population produced a documented alternative
that was suppressed.
The documentation exists.
The people who made it are still there —
or in prison, or in exile.

## Apparent
An election. August 9, 2020.
Official result: Lukashenko 80.1%.
Exit polls and parallel vote counting:
Tsikhanouskaya 60–70%.
The discrepancy was not explained officially.
The protests began the same night.
200,000 people in Minsk.
Unprecedented.
Sviatlana Tsikhanouskaya — now in Lithuanian exile.
The white-red-white flag — the pre-Soviet flag — carried in the streets.
It is now illegal to display in Belarus.

## Yearbook
August 9, 2020: the election.
August 9–November 2020: the protests.
The OMON — riot police — responding.
Thousands arrested. Hundreds tortured.
Documented by human rights organisations.
October 2020: EU imposes sanctions.
Lukashenko turns fully to Russia.
2021: Ryanair flight 4978 forced to land in Minsk.
Journalist Roman Protasevich arrested.
The international response: condemnation.
Lukashenko remains.
February 2022: Belarus used as staging ground
for the Russian invasion of Ukraine.
The territory of Belarus now militarily integrated
into Russian operations.
2024: elections held. Lukashenko wins again.
The 2020 account is still being kept
by the people who were there.
