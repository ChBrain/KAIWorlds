# Piece: January 23 — Two Nobel Prizes from One Island
## The two men who were born on the same day, attended the same school, and defined their fields in relation to the Caribbean.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas
- **Place:** [Castries, Saint Lucia](culture_saintlucia_place_castries.md)

## Place
[Castries, Saint Lucia](culture_saintlucia_place_castries.md)

## Load Bearing
Remove the Nobel prizes and Saint Lucia is a Windward Island with tourism, bananas,
a French-British colonial history visible in its place names and language, and the Pitons.
With them: Saint Lucia is the small island that established
that the Caribbean can produce work of global significance —
not as exception, not as surprise,
but as what happens when the capacity that is here is given the conditions to develop.
Lewis's dual-sector model of economic development: still taught, still used,
still the foundation of development economics discourse.
Walcott's *Omeros*: the retelling of Homer in the landscape of Saint Lucia —
the fisherman Achille, the Pitons in the background,
the colonial wound and the beauty of the place held simultaneously.
The two prizes together constitute a claim: this island is a place where this happens.
Not happened once. Happens. The festival each January 23 is the claim renewed.

## Apparent
Arthur Lewis: born January 23, 1915, Castries. Nobel Prize in Economics, 1979 —
the year Saint Lucia became independent. The dual-sector model of economic development.
Died 1991. Buried at the Sir Arthur Lewis Community College, Saint Lucia.
Derek Walcott: born January 23, 1930, Castries. Nobel Prize in Literature, 1992 —
"for a poetic oeuvre of great luminosity, sustained by a historical vision,
the outcome of a multicultural commitment." *Omeros* (1990). Died 2017, Cap Estate, Saint Lucia.
Both attended St. Mary's College, Castries.
Same birthday. Same school. 238 square kilometres. 180,000 people.
The annual Nobel Laureate Festival: every January 23.
The wreath-laying at St. Mary's College. The lectures. The *Kwéyòl* performances.
2024 Paris Olympics: Saint Lucian sprinter Julien Alfred wins the women's 100m —
the first Olympic gold for Saint Lucia.
The capacity: continues.
