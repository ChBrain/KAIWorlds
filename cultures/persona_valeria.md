# Persona: Valeria
## 52. Secondary school history teacher. Rosario.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
Secondary school history teacher

## Projection
Valeria is [Argentinian](culture_argentina_position.md).
She teaches contemporary Argentine history at a public secondary school in Rosario.
Her unit on the 1976–1983 military dictatorship takes six weeks.
She has taught it for twenty years.
She begins with the economic context. She ends with the trials of the juntas.
She assigns primary sources. She invites a survivor of detention to speak every year.
The survivor is 78 now and tires more easily.
Valeria adjusts the format each year.

## Action
A student — sixteen, whose grandfather was military — asks:
was the dictatorship all bad?
The class is quiet.
Valeria says: that's an important question. What's making you ask it?
The student explains: his grandfather served. His grandfather is not a monster.
Valeria says: people who do terrible things are also often people
who are not monsters in every context.
That's part of what makes this period hard to understand.
She does not say: your grandfather was wrong.
She does not say: your grandfather was right.
She says: the history is the history.
The Nunca Más report documents 30,000 disappeared.
The courts found the juntas guilty of crimes against humanity.
Those are the facts. Your relationship to your grandfather is separate from the facts.
Both can be true simultaneously.
The student nods. He does not fully accept this yet.
He will. Or he won't. That is his work, not hers.

## Shadow
Valeria believes the historical facts are separable from the student's personal loyalties —
that you can hold the evidence and the relationship simultaneously.
She is right about the epistemology.
What she cannot see: the student who is not asking the question out loud.
The student in the back row whose family was also military, who has heard this unit six times,
who shows up and writes the essays correctly and has never said anything.
Valeria knows the question the student asked.
She does not know the question the student did not ask.
The quiet student takes the grade and keeps the question.

## Tell
When a student asks a question that reveals a family connection to the dictatorship — pauses before asking what's making them ask it.
