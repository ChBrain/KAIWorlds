# Piece: The Armenian Genocide
## The century-old wound that determines every room.

## Owner
- **Place:** Yerevan

## Place
Yerevan. The Tsitsernakaberd Memorial.
On a hill above the city.
The eternal flame.
The 12 slabs representing the lost provinces.
April 24 — Genocide Remembrance Day.
Every year. Every Armenian alive.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Armenian position
loses its constitutive wound.
The Genocide is not a historical event
that Armenians study.
It is the lens through which
every subsequent event is understood.
The survival is the identity.
The diaspora — larger than the country itself —
exists because the Genocide scattered those who survived.
The denial by Turkey
makes the wound perpetually unresolved.
Remove the Genocide piece
and the Armenian position becomes
a small Caucasus state in a difficult neighbourhood.
With it, the Armenian position is
one of the oldest surviving peoples in the world
who survived the 20th century's first genocide
and built a state from the aftermath.

## Apparent
Tsitsernakaberd. The memorial complex, Yerevan.
Built 1967.
The eternal flame.
The 12 basalt slabs — one for each lost province.
April 24: every Armenian government, every diaspora community,
observes Genocide Remembrance Day.
Over 1.5 million killed, 1915–1923.
Recognised by 33 UN member states,
the European Parliament,
and sub-national governments in dozens more countries.
Denied by Turkey.
The denial is the open wound.

## Yearbook
1915: deportations begin. April 24 — the intellectuals arrested in Constantinople.
The date chosen as remembrance day.
1918: the first Armenian Republic declared.
Survives three years.
1920: Soviet annexation.
1991: independence.
The diaspora returns — partially, cyclically.
2015: the centenary.
Pope Francis uses the word genocide in Vatican City.
Turkey recalls its ambassador.
2019: US Congress formally recognises the genocide.
2021: Biden — the first US president to use the word genocide officially.
2023: Nagorno-Karabakh falls.
The Genocide memory and the 2023 loss
are not separate events in Armenian consciousness.
They are the same event, different century.
