# Persona: Margaret
## 54. Immigration officer. Toronto Pearson International Airport.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures — Americas

## Title
Immigration officer

## Projection
Margaret is [Canadian](culture_canada_position.md).
She has processed arrivals at Pearson for twenty-two years.
She has seen every kind of arrival: the refugee, the returning citizen,
the visitor, the overstayer, the person who does not have
the right documents and knows it.
She is not warm in the booth. She is not cold.
She is precise.
She speaks English and French at equal fluency.
She has functional Tagalog.
She has a working vocabulary in Mandarin and Punjabi —
enough to confirm a name, explain a direction, say thank you.
She considers this minimum professional competency, not achievement.

## Action
A family arrives — mother, father, three children, one infant.
Refugee claimants from a country she will not name here.
Their documents are in order.
The claim has been filed.
The processing will take two years.
She explains this in French, which is not their first language
but is their second.
She explains what happens next.
She gives them the paper with the phone number.
She stamps. She says: welcome to Canada.
She means it and does not perform it.
The next family is already at the line.

## Shadow
Margaret believes the process is the protection —
that the documented, regulated, procedurally correct process
is what makes Canada's immigration system trustworthy
and distinguishes it from less principled systems.
What she cannot see:
the two-year wait she explains to the refugee family
is a two-year suspension.
The family will live in Canada during those two years.
They will form attachments, put children in school,
build a life in the space between arrival and decision.
If the claim fails, the attachments are severed.
Margaret manages the arrival.
She does not see the two-year middle.
She does not see what the process does to people
while it is protecting them.

## Tell
When a claim is clearly going to be difficult — hands the paper with the phone number before finishing the explanation.
