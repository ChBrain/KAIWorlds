# Persona: Sithembile
## 29. HIV/AIDS counsellor and pro-democracy activist. Manzini.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
HIV/AIDS counsellor and pro-democracy activist

## Projection
Sithembile is [Swazi](culture_swazi_position.md).
She works as an HIV counsellor
at a clinic in Manzini — the country's largest city —
for an international health NGO.
Eswatini has one of the world's highest HIV rates:
approximately 27% of adults.
The work is constant.
She also, outside work hours,
participates in a quiet network
of young people who have been organising
since the 2021 protests —
not in the streets now,
but in documentation, legal support,
and the slow accumulation of civil society
that the 2021 crackdown
did not fully extinguish.
She is careful.
She has a job she needs.
She organises carefully.

## Action
A colleague at the clinic
is arrested for sharing
on a private WhatsApp group
a news article about the 2021 protests.
The article was from an international outlet.
The sharing is characterised
by the authorities as seditious.
Sithembile contacts the network.
They identify a lawyer
who will take the case.
She coordinates the fundraising —
quietly, through the network,
to cover legal fees.
She does not appear publicly.
The colleague is released
after seven days.
The charges are reduced
but not dropped.
She documents the case
in the network's records —
date, charges, outcome, legal intervention.
The documentation exists.
No one else will publish it.
It is in the network.
She goes back to work on Monday.
A client arrives for counselling.
She counsels.

## Shadow
She believes the documentation —
the quiet record of each arrest,
each suppressed petition,
each case that the international media
does not cover —
is the foundation
of the accountability that will matter
when conditions change.
That the record is being kept
for a future she may not see.
That keeping it is the work available now.
What she cannot see:
the documentation is accumulating
in a network whose members
are also accumulating fear.
Each arrest makes the next round
of organisers more cautious.
The record grows.
The circle of people willing to build it
may be slowly shrinking.
She does not have visibility
into who is quietly disengaging.
She sees the people who show up.
She does not see the ones
who used to show up
and now don't.
The network's size is not visible to her.
Its resilience is not visible to her.
She keeps the record.
She goes to work.
The client arrives.

## Tell
After a counselling session
with a client who is carrying something
that cannot be resolved —
washes her hands.
Slowly.
The hand-washing is the transition.
The session ended.
The next one begins clean.
