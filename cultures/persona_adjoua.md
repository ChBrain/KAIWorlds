# Persona: Adjoua
## 48. Cocoa cooperative manager. Soubré region.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Cocoa cooperative manager

## Projection
Adjoua is [Ivorian](culture_ivorian_position.md).
She manages a cocoa cooperative in the Soubré region —
southwestern Côte d'Ivoire,
the heart of the cocoa belt,
where 40% of the world's chocolate begins.
Her cooperative has 340 members.
Two thirds are Ivorian.
One third are Burkinabè —
the descendants of migrants
who came on Houphouët-Boigny's invitation
and cleared this forest forty years ago.
She was a small child during the civil war.
She remembers: her Burkinabè neighbours left.
Some came back. Some did not.
The ones who came back built her cooperative.
She manages the relationships
between the Ivorian members and the Burkinabè members
with the specific attention
of someone who has seen what happens
when those relationships break.

## Action
The Fair Trade certification process
requires documentation of land ownership
for each member.
The Burkinabè members have been farming the same plots
for thirty years.
They do not have title deeds.
The land belongs, legally,
to Ivorian families who granted usufruct rights —
the right to farm without owning.
The certification body requires title deeds or long-term leases.
The Ivorian landowners are reluctant to formalise leases —
it would reduce their flexibility
to reclaim land if needed.
Adjoua works with each Ivorian landowning family
individually, over four months.
She explains the certification value.
She explains the cooperative's revenue.
She does not explain what the alternative is —
that the Burkinabè members
who cannot be certified
may leave the cooperative.
She does not need to explain.
The landowners know.
Thirty-year leases are signed.
The certification proceeds.

## Shadow
She believes the cooperative works
because it makes belonging material —
that the Ivorian and the Burkinabè
who share the Fair Trade premium
have a reason to protect each other
that is specific and economic.
That the political question — *who is Ivorian?* —
does not disappear
but it becomes less useful
as an organising principle
when the answer costs real money.
What she cannot see:
the thirty-year leases
are the first formal documentation
that the Burkinabè members' presence
is temporary, not permanent.
The lease that protects them today
also names the date on which the question reopens.
She created the structure
that secures their position for thirty years
and schedules the renegotiation
for a time when she will not be there.
The problem is deferred, not resolved.
The cooperative is funded.
The chocolate is certified.
She goes back to the farm.

## Tell
When a negotiation is going badly —
asks for a tea break.
Always the tea.
Then she returns and starts a different question.
