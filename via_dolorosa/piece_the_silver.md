# Piece: The Silver
## The price paid before the road existed.

## Owner
- **Project:** Via Dolorosa

## Place
Not on the road.
The temple floor before Station I.
The potter's field after the road ends.
The Silver never touches the Via Dolorosa.
Its work was done before the road began.

## Load Bearing
Without it there is no betrayal.
Without the betrayal there is no arrest.
Without the arrest there is no Praetorium.
Without the Praetorium there is no road.
The Silver precedes every piece on the Via Dolorosa.
Including the Cross (piece_the_cross).

## Apparent
Thirty pieces. Silver.
The price of a slave under Mosaic law.
Paid to one man.
Returned by that man to the temple floor.
Refused.
Small enough to hold in two hands.
Heavy enough to end a road before it begins
and a life before the road ends.

## Yearbook
Counted out before the road existed.
Paid at the moment of betrayal.
Carried by one man to the temple.
Thrown on the temple floor.
The priests would not take it back.
Blood money, they said.
Left on the floor.
Collected by the priests.
Used to buy the potter's field.
The field named Akeldama. Field of Blood.
The man who threw it there
buried in the ground it purchased.
The road ends at the Tomb (place_station_xiv_the_tomb).
The Silver ends in the field.
Neither the road nor the Silver
returns to where it started.
