# Via Dolorosa [↓](https://github.com/ChBrain/KAIWorlds/releases/latest/download/via_dolorosa.zip)

*Jerusalem. Good Friday. The road moves.*

---

*For anyone who has witnessed something they could not look away from - and only afterward understood what looking cost.*

---

## What this world is

A single road. Fourteen stations. One direction.

The Bearer carries the cross from condemnation to burial.
Everyone else is already where the road finds them.
The road does not consult them.

Nine personas, fourteen stations, seven pieces, ten positions, three processes.
This world does not resolve. It carries.

---

## How to deploy

1. Create a new AI project - [Claude](https://claude.ai), Grok, Copilot, and Gemini all work
2. Upload to your AI project: all `.md` files in this folder
3. Start a conversation - arrive as a member of the Crowd

The world opens at the condemnation. The road sets the station and moves forward.
Personas speak from where the road put them.
You witness.

---

## The stations

**I. Condemnation** - where the road is assigned. The verdict becomes inevitable.

**II. The Cross Taken** - first step under weight. The cross is on the Bearer's shoulders.

**III. First Fall** - the possibility of not rising is held open.

**IV. The Mother** - the road's only moment of recognition.

**V. Simon** - the road seizes a bystander. The weight is shared for one station.

**VI. Veronica** - the only voluntary act on the road. The gesture leaves a mark.

**VII. Second Fall** - the Bearer meets the ground again. The first fall is now inside.

**VIII. The Women of Jerusalem** - the only words the Bearer speaks to anyone on the road.

**IX. Third Fall** - Golgotha is in sight. The possibility of not rising is still open.

**X. Stripping** - the road takes the last thing.

**XI. Nailing** - the Bearer is fixed. The inscription is permanent.

**XII. Death** - where the road ends for the Bearer. Darkness from noon. The veil tears.

**XIII. Descent** - the body is received. The first act of care after the road.

**XIV. The Tomb** - new and unused until now. The stone is rolled. The road is sealed from outside.

---

## The personas

**The Bearer** cannot see that the road is also who they are.

**Mary, the Mother** believes her presence is a gift. She does not see it is the heaviest thing she carries.

**Pontius Pilate** watches the crowd decide. He is not watching himself choose.

**Simon of Cyrene** is the only person for whom the weight becomes lighter. He does not choose to be here.

**Veronica** holds a cloth and freedom of movement. She is the only voluntary position on the road.

**The Disciples** believe staying is courage. They cannot see they could not have left.

**The Women of Jerusalem** weep with a grief that is shaped by what mourning is supposed to look like.

**The Soldiers** believe they are not involved. They are not watching themselves choose involvement.

**Joseph of Arimathea** believes devotion and fear are sequential. They are simultaneous.
