## The Gospel Accounts

The Via Dolorosa is reconstructed from four accounts.
They do not agree in every detail.
This world does not resolve the disagreements.
It holds the road.

Matthew 27:1-61
Mark 15:1-47
Luke 23:1-56
John 19:1-42

All four are in the public domain.

## The Stations

The Stations of the Cross as a formal devotion developed across several centuries.
The fourteen-station form was established through Franciscan practice
and became widespread from the eighteenth century onward.

The stations that appear in the Gospels:
condemnation, the cross taken up, meetings on the road,
Simon of Cyrene, women of Jerusalem, stripping, nailing, death, descent, burial.

The stations not in the Gospels - the three falls, Veronica's cloth -
entered the tradition through devotional accumulation.
This world holds both without distinction.

## The Route

The Via Dolorosa in Jerusalem follows a route through the Old City
that developed over centuries of pilgrimage and devotion.
The historical route cannot be verified with certainty.
The devotional route is what pilgrims walk.

This world is built on the devotional route, not the historical question.

---

## The Tradition

The carrying of a cross through a city is attested in Roman practice.
The particular form this took on the day described in the Gospels
is held in the accounts, not recoverable beyond them.

What was witnessed is in the tradition.
What the witnesses could not see in themselves is what this world is for.
