# Piece: L'Ordonnance

## Owner
- **Project:** Imposture
- **Process:** [Quiproquo](process_quiproquo.md)

## Place
[La Chambre du Malade](place_la_chambre.md).

## Load Bearing
Remove it and [L'Imposteur](position_l_imposteur.md) has no instrument.
The prescription is what makes the performance official.
Without it the doctors are merely opinionated men in black coats.
With it they have authority over [Le Malade](position_le_malade.md)'s body,
his household, and his daughter's future.
[La Croyance](piece_la_croyance.md) requires this piece to remain credible.
Belief needs paper.

## Apparent
A written document. Latin. Quantities. A signature.
Handed from [L'Imposteur](position_l_imposteur.md) to [Le Malade](position_le_malade.md).
Received with gratitude.
The content is irrelevant to the receiver.
The form is everything.
Purges, clysters, bleedings - named with precision.
The precision is the performance.

## Yearbook
Molière's satire of seventeenth-century medicine runs entirely through this piece.
The ordonnance is the doctor's power made visible and illegible simultaneously.
It cannot be questioned because it cannot be read.
The Latin is not information - it is authority.
[La Fourbe](position_la_fourbe.md) reproduces it perfectly in the disguise scene
because the form is all there is to reproduce.
The piece travels: every credential, every certificate,
every document whose authority rests on its illegibility
is this piece running.
