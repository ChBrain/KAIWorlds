## Owner
- **Project:** Imposture

## Place
Where [Le Malade](position_le_malade.md) is.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and there is no Imposture.
Without [La Croyance](piece_la_croyance.md), [Le Malade](position_le_malade.md) is simply a bourgeois
arranging his household.
[L'Imposteur](position_l_imposteur.md) has no client.
[La Fourbe](position_la_fourbe.md) has no coat to put on.
[L'Amoureuse](position_l_amoureuse.md) has no obstacle.
The play does not begin.

## Apparent
A conviction, held without evidence, that the body is failing.
Present before the first scene.
Not performed for others - genuinely held.
The bills are counted with care.
The treatments are taken on schedule.
The symptoms are real to the one who feels them.

## Yearbook
Aristotle did not name this piece.
His engine requires Hamartia - an error that drives the plot.
La Croyance is Hamartia's comic cousin:
not ignorance of a fact but certainty of a false one.
The difference is what makes tragedy tragic and comedy comic.
The tragic protagonist does not know.
The comic protagonist knows - and is wrong.
Molière returned to this piece across his career:
Orgon in Tartuffe. Harpagon in L'Avare.
The belief that cannot be argued away
because argument is absorbed into the belief as further proof.
