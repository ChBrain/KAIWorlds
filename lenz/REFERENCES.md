# References

## Georg Buchner (1813-1837)

Born in Goddelau, Hesse. Studied medicine and natural sciences. Wrote *Lenz* in 1835 in Strasbourg, based on a handwritten copy of Oberlin's diary given to him by his friend August Stoeber. Never finished it. Dead at twenty-three of typhus. The manuscript was published posthumously in 1839.

**The work this world is built on:**

## Secondary sources

**Johann Friedrich Oberlin** - *Herrn L. Aufenthalt in Waldbach* (diary, 1778)
The primary source Buchner drew from. das Protokollbuch in this world. Published in full 1839. Available in the Sieburth bilingual edition alongside the novella.

**Richard Sieburth** - translator's introduction and notes in *Lenz* (Archipelago Books, 2004)
Essential for understanding the manuscript history, the free indirect speech, and what Buchner made from Oberlin's record.

**Jan-Christoph Hauschild** - *Georg Buchner: Biographie* (1993)
The definitive German biography. For understanding who Buchner was at twenty-two, writing from inside.
