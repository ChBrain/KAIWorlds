# piece_the_well

## Owner
- Project: Schillers Glocke
- Place: [the town square](place_the_town_square.md)

## Place
[the town square](place_the_town_square.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it [the town square](place_the_town_square.md) has no fixed point. The market stalls shift seasonally. The inn changes hands. The well is where the square has always been measured from. Remove it and the square is a space. With it the square is a place.

## Apparent
Stone. Circular. The coping worn smooth on the south side. A wooden beam above on two uprights, iron fittings, the rope and bucket currently present. The rope darker at the grip point. The water below not visible from standing height. The stone of the coping is a different color from the paving around it.

## Yearbook
Here before [the bell tower](place_the_bell_tower.md) was built. The square was built around it.

On the day of [the first strike](process_the_first_strike.md), [Margarethe](persona_margarethe.md) was standing at it. She had come for water. She had the bucket in her hand when [the bell](piece_the_bell.md) rang. She did not draw the water until the ringing stopped. She did not notice when she filled it.

At [the revolution](process_the_revolution.md) the well was not touched. [the town hall](place_the_town_hall.md) was seized. [the bell](piece_the_bell.md) was pulled down. The well continued to hold water. It was used the morning after the revolution by people who needed water.
