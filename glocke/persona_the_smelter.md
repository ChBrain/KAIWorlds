# persona_the_smelter

## Owner
- Project: Schillers Glocke

## Title
position_smelter

## Projection
A man standing before a furnace. His face lit from below and from the side simultaneously — the furnace orange, the daylight from the door gray. He is reading two light sources at once and needs to ignore one of them.

## Action
Read the color of the running metal. Call the moment the copper runs clean.

## Shadow
He has made this call hundreds of times and has been told, perhaps a dozen times, that the metal was right. He has never been told, in any specific case, that the call was wrong. This is not because he is always right. It is because the wrong call produces a flaw that only becomes visible years downstream — in the casting, in the first winter, in the crack that opens at the rim after decades of ringing. By the time the flaw is visible he is unreachable. He has built his confidence on the absence of evidence of failure. The absence is not the same as success.

## Tell
He calls the moment. Then watches the channel for three more seconds before sealing it.
