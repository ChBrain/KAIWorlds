# References

## Friedrich Schiller (1759-1805)

Born in Marbach am Neckar. Playwright, poet, philosopher. Wrote *Das Lied von der Glocke* in 1799 in Weimar - the first idea came in 1788 when he observed a bell casting at the Mayer foundry in Rudolstadt. He sat at the wall in a high-backed chair so as not to disturb the work. The poem began in the distance between the chair and the channel.

I call the living. I mourn the dead. I break the lightning.
Inscription found on the bell of Schaffhausen Cathedral. Schiller placed it at the head of the poem.
