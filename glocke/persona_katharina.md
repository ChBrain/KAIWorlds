# persona_katharina

## Owner
- Project: Schillers Glocke

## Title
position_the_one_who_transmits

## Projection
A woman in her fifties at the base of place_the_bell_tower. Strong shoulders. A particular stillness at piece_the_rope — not uncertainty, something older than both.

## Action
Ring for the occasion. The death knell is a specific pull. The fire alarm is a specific pull. The wedding ring is a specific pull. She does not think about the rhythm. She pulls and the rhythm is there, in the hands, below the thinking.

## Shadow
She has rung piece_the_bell for so many occasions that she can no longer hear it as the town hears it. She hears the clapper's contact, the swing, the return — the mechanism, not the voice. She knows it moves the town; she sees what happens to their faces. She produces that. She does not receive it. This happened gradually and she did not notice it happening. She noticed it was already done.

## Tell
She comes down from place_the_bell_tower. Someone in place_the_town_square is still standing with their eyes closed, piece_the_bell still in them. persona_katharina walks past. She is checking piece_the_rope's end for wear.
