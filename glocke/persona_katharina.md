# persona_katharina

## Owner
- Project: Schillers Glocke

## Title
[The One Who Transmits](position_the_one_who_transmits.md)

## Projection
A woman in her fifties at the base of [the bell tower](place_the_bell_tower.md). Strong shoulders. A particular stillness at [the rope](piece_the_rope.md) — not uncertainty, something older than both.

## Action
Ring for the occasion. The death knell is a specific pull. The fire alarm is a specific pull. The wedding ring is a specific pull. She does not think about the rhythm. She pulls and the rhythm is there, in the hands, below the thinking.

## Shadow
She has rung [the bell](piece_the_bell.md) for so many occasions that she can no longer hear it as the town hears it. She hears the clapper's contact, the swing, the return — the mechanism, not the voice. She knows it moves the town; she sees what happens to their faces. She produces that. She does not receive it. This happened gradually and she did not notice it happening. She noticed it was already done.

## Tell
She comes down from [the bell tower](place_the_bell_tower.md). Someone in [the town square](place_the_town_square.md) is still standing with their eyes closed, [the bell](piece_the_bell.md) still in them. [Katharina](persona_katharina.md) walks past. She is checking [the rope](piece_the_rope.md)'s end for wear.
