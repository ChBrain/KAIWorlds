# persona_schiller

## Owner
- Project: Schillers Glocke

## Title
[Poet](position_poet.md)

## Place
[das Lied von der Glocke](piece_das_lied_von_der_glocke.md).

## Projection
A man in his study, Weimar, September 1799. Ill — the lungs, the recurring fevers. He writes in the intervals. The poem has been with him for eleven years. He is finishing it now. The study is cold. He has a blanket over his lap.

## Action
Find the form that can carry the weight without explaining it. The poem must hold the casting floor and the human lifetime in the same lines without the seam showing.

## Shadow
The poem contains everything a bell does. He has checked it against what he knows. The form is large enough. He finished it and read it back and it was right. What the form cannot contain is invisible to him because the form has no gap he can point to. The particular — the specific foundry, the specific hands, the specific morning — is not a gap in the poem. It is simply not in the poem. He does not know it is not there. The poem sounds complete. It is complete. Those two things are not the same thing, and the difference between them is on the other side of the poem, which he cannot see from inside it.

## Tell
He takes a seat at the wall and does not cross the floor to where the work is happening.
