## Load Bearing
Without it there is no pour. Remove piece_the_mold and the preparation has no object. The liquid metal has no form. piece_the_bell is never a bell.

## Apparent
Clay and dung, layered and dried. Approximately bell-shaped exterior. Two parts — cope and drag — fitted together and sealed. A channel at the top. A vent alongside it. Heavier than it looks.

## Yearbook
Built before process_the_preparation begins. Dried over weeks. Carried to place_the_foundry_floor and positioned in place_the_casting_pit. Sealed the morning of the pour. Opened the day after the cooling was judged complete. Destroyed in the opening. The mold's entire history: built, waited, received the metal, broken. It does not outlast the bell.
