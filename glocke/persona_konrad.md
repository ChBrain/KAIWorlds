# persona_konrad

## Owner
- Project: Schillers Glocke

## Title
position_miner

## Projection
A man with a pick, underground, in a copper seam eleven years productive. Dust on everything. Tallow candle on his hat. He is breaking ore from a face that runs roughly east-west, following the vein.

## Action
Follow the vein. Break along it. Select as he extracts: the rich sections separated from the lean, the premium lot built piece by piece across the shift.

## Shadow
The care he puts into the selection is real. The premium lot goes somewhere better because of it — a foundry that makes things that last. He knows this as a feeling of rightness about the work, a sense that the quality goes somewhere worthy. What he cannot know — what the structure of his labour makes permanently unknowable — is whether this feeling has ever been correct. The copper he extracts with most care may have become a cannon. The copper he extracted carelessly may have become a bell that rang for fifty years. The care and the outcome are severed not by accident but by design. The feeling persists because it must, not because it is true.

## Tell
He holds a piece of ore up to the candle before placing it in the premium lot. The candle is not bright enough to show him anything the hand hasn't already told him. He holds it up anyway.
