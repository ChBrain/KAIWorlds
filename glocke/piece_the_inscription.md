# piece_the_inscription

## Owner
- Project: Schillers Glocke
- Piece: [the bell](piece_the_bell.md)

## Place
[the bell](piece_the_bell.md). The shoulder. Permanently.

## Load Bearing
Without [the inscription](piece_the_inscription.md) [the bell](piece_the_bell.md) exists in time without a name for what it does. The bell still rings. But it rings without the three functions declared. The inscription is the bell's statement of purpose cast in the same metal as the purpose itself.

## Apparent
Raised letters on [the bell](piece_the_bell.md)'s shoulder. *Vivos voco. Mortuos plango. Fulgura frango.* The letters are the same metal as the bell — cast in the same pour, not added after.

## Yearbook
Cut into [the mold](piece_the_mold.md)'s interior by [Mold Maker](position_mold_maker.md) from [the specification](piece_the_specification.md). Cast in [the pour](process_the_pour.md). Emerged at [the opening](process_the_opening.md), sharp, the clay having taken the cutting cleanly. Confirmed against [the specification](piece_the_specification.md) by [Master Founder](position_master_founder.md). Read for the first time in the transit by the town, [the inscription](piece_the_inscription.md) facing upward on the sledge. Readable from [the town square](place_the_town_square.md) through the open arches of [the bell tower](place_the_bell_tower.md) for fifty-one years. Destroyed in the first blow of [the recasting](process_the_recasting.md). The shoulder broke first.
