# place_the_bell_tower

## Owner
- Project: Schillers Glocke

## Shown
Stone. Four stories. The bell chamber at the top: open on all four sides through arched gaps, no glass. [the bell](piece_the_bell.md) hanging from the iron yoke bolted to the main beams. The clapper visible from below through the chamber floor gap. [the rope](piece_the_rope.md) descending through that gap, down through the stairwell, to the ground floor entrance hall. The staircase: stone, narrow, worn at the inside edge. A door at the base of the stair, currently open, giving onto [the town square](place_the_town_square.md). [the town hall](place_the_town_hall.md) hall behind the stair door: long table, the Town Council's seal above the entrance.

## Holds
[the rope](piece_the_rope.md). [the bell](piece_the_bell.md).

## Offers
[Bellringer](position_bellringer.md) can pull [the rope](piece_the_rope.md) from the ground floor entrance hall. [The One Who Transmits](position_the_one_who_transmits.md) can reach the bell chamber by the staircase.

## Withheld
The wear pattern on the staircase inside edge. It records the accumulated descents — pace, weight, urgency. The stone does not say which occasion produced which wear.
