# piece_das_lied_von_der_glocke

## Owner
- Project: Schillers Glocke
- Persona: persona_schiller

## Place
Where persona_schiller is. The poem is his place.

## Load Bearing
Without it the world has no author. The world was built from it. Remove it and the commission has no inscription, persona_brenner has no marked margin, the world has no question it was built to answer. The world is the particular this poem cannot reach. Both exist because the other exists.

## Apparent
A manuscript, then a printed broadsheet in the Musen-Almanach, 1799. 430 lines. The title at the top. The inscription in Latin before the first stanza: *Vivos voco. Mortuos plango. Fulgura frango.* The lines move between the casting floor and a human lifetime without announcement. The form holds both simultaneously.

## Yearbook
The first idea: 1788. persona_schiller at the Mayer foundry in Rudolstadt. He sat at the wall in a high-backed chair so as not to disturb the work. The casting master was not pleased about the disruption. persona_schiller watched from the periphery. He did not cross the floor. The poem began in that chair, in the distance between the chair and the channel.

Set aside. Returned to 1793 in Ludwigsburg. Set aside again. In September 1799, in Weimar, finished quickly. Published in the Musen-Almanach that year.

persona_brenner read it. Marked three lines in the margin in ink that was already fading. The three lines left the poem and entered piece_the_specification and then piece_the_bell.

The poem remained. The three lines are still in it. They are also in piece_the_bell — in bronze, raised, sharp, from the pour to the first blow of process_the_recasting. The poem held the words in ink. piece_the_bell held them in bronze. piece_the_bell is gone. The poem remains.

The world built from this poem contains place_the_foundry_floor. place_the_foundry_floor contains the south face variance in the cooling, persona_anna whose breath is audible before the silence, persona_konrad holding ore up to a candle that cannot show him anything his hand has not already told him. None of this is in the poem. The poem cannot reach it. The poem is the reason it exists.
