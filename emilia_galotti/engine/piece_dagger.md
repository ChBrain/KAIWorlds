# Piece: The Dagger

## Owner
- **Project:** Emilia Galotti
- **Place:** [The Summer Residence](place_residence.md)

## Place
Where [The Discarded](position_discarded.md) is — until it is not.
The piece does not stay.
It moves through the hands the play requires
in the order the play requires them.
It arrives in [The Final Room](place_final_room.md) last.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and [Honour as Procedure](process_honour.md) has no instrument.
The code runs to its final answer and finds nothing to hold.
The ending the play has been building toward
since the moment Marinelli was given permission
cannot happen.
Not a different ending — no ending.
The architecture requires a specific object
passing through specific hands in a specific sequence.
This is that object.

## Apparent
A short blade.
Plain handle, no ornament.
Small enough to be carried in a woman's dress
or held inside a coat without showing.
The edge is maintained.
It has been kept.

## Yearbook
Carried into [The Summer Residence](place_residence.md) by [The Discarded](position_discarded.md).
She did not bring it for Emilia.
She brought it for the prince.
She gives it to [The Father](position_father.md) instead —
in the moment she understands
that her hand is not the hand the play requires.

[The Father](position_father.md) holds it through the scene with the prince.
Does not use it there.
The code does not permit it yet.
He carries it into [The Final Room](place_final_room.md).

[The Daughter](position_daughter.md) asks for it.
[The Father](position_father.md) takes it back.
What she asked for and what he does
are not the same act.
She asked to end herself.
He ends her.
The distinction is everything.
The piece does not carry the distinction.
It only passes.

[The Executioner](position_executioner.md) drops it at the prince's feet.
It lies on the floor of [The Summer Residence](place_residence.md).
The play ends.
The piece remains.
