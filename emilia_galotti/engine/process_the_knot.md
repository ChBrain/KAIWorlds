# Process: The Knot

## Owner
- **Project:** Emilia Galotti

## Initiated by
[The Prince](position_prince.md) has seen Emilia and wants her.
[The Father](position_father.md) has arranged a marriage that will place her beyond reach.
The wedding is today.
Marinelli is given permission to do whatever is necessary.
The knot begins the moment permission is granted —
not the moment it is used.

## Direction
Toward the point where every action taken to prevent the marriage
has made the marriage permanently impossible
by making Emilia permanently unavailable to everyone,
including [The Prince](position_prince.md).

## Lever
Carrying force: the plot's internal logic.
Each move produces the next by necessity.
The attack on the carriage requires a place of safety.
The place of safety requires a pretext.
The pretext requires the removal of [The Father](position_father.md).
The removal of [The Father](position_father.md) produces the scene
in which [The Discarded](position_discarded.md) reaches him first.
No move was improvised. Each was the only available move
once the one before it had been made.

Resistance: [The Father](position_father.md)'s code.
It does not bend. It does not negotiate.
It produces a man who will not be bought, threatened, or reasoned with.
The knot tightens against this resistance.
The code is not strong enough to stop the plot.
It is strong enough to produce [The Executioner](position_executioner.md).

## Echo
[The Daughter](position_daughter.md) is inside the residence.
Count Appiani is dead.
[The Father](position_father.md) knows what has been done and by whom.
[The Dagger](piece_dagger.md) is in the building.
What the next process inherits:
a room in which the only remaining question
is what [The Father](position_father.md)'s code will produce
when it finally has no room left to move.
