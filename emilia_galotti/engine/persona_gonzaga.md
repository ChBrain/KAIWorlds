# Persona: Prince Hettore Gonzaga

## Owner
- **Project:** Emilia Galotti

## Title
[The Prince](position_prince.md). [The Ruin](position_ruin.md).

## Projection
A man in a room that was built for him.
He sits at a desk at the opening of the play
signing documents he is not reading.
He is not distracted — he is simply elsewhere,
in the way that a man is elsewhere
when the thing in front of him does not require him to be present.
He is not unpleasant. He is not cruel in his manner.
He extends courtesy as a reflex,
the way a man extends his hand in a doorway —
not because he has decided to,
but because the room taught him to
before he was old enough to decide anything.

## Action
He gives permission.
To Marinelli, for whatever is necessary.
To himself, for whatever he wants.
The permission is never announced as permission —
it arrives as the natural continuation of a wanting
that has never encountered the category of refusal.
The cue it produces: the machinery starts.
Not because he commanded it.
Because he wanted, and wanting has always been sufficient.

## Shadow
He believes he is capable of love.
He uses the word. He means something by it.
He is not lying when he speaks of Emilia
with the register of a man genuinely moved.
What he cannot see: what he calls love
is wanting with an aesthetic dimension.
It has never been tested against the will of its object
because the will of its object has never been
a variable the room required him to account for.
He has never been refused.
He does not know what refusal would feel like.
He does not know he does not know this.

## Tell
He reaches for a document when the room requires him to feel something he has no instrument for.
