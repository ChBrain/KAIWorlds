# References

## Gotthold Ephraim Lessing (1729-1781)

Born in Kamenz, Saxony. Son of a Lutheran pastor. Studied theology in Leipzig, then abandoned it for theatre and criticism. Worked as a playwright, dramaturge, and critic across Berlin, Hamburg, and Wolfenbuttel. He was the first major critic to argue that German theatre should learn from Shakespeare rather than from French classicism.

He spent the last decade of his life as librarian at Wolfenbuttel. He died in 1781, seven years after completing *Nathan the Wise*, his final play.

## Emilia Galotti (1772)

First performed 13 March 1772 at the court theatre in Brunswick, on the birthday of the Duchess of Brunswick. Lessing had been working on the subject for twenty years before completing it.

The immediate source is the story of Verginia from Livy's *Ab Urbe Condita* (Book III): a Roman plebeian father kills his daughter to prevent her from being enslaved as the concubine of a decemvir. Lessing transposed the action from republican Rome to an unnamed Italian principality, replacing the political conflict between plebeians and patricians with a social conflict between the bourgeoisie and the nobility.

The play is classified as a *burgerliches Trauerspiel* - a bourgeois tragedy - a genre Lessing had helped define in *Miss Sara Sampson* (1755). The subject of the genre is the private life of non-aristocratic characters treated with the full tragic weight previously reserved for royalty.

The question the play poses: whether Emilia's death is an act of honour or an act of violence, and whether the distinction holds when a father is the one who decides. Lessing does not answer it. He structures the final scene so that both readings are simultaneously true.

## Secondary sources

**Hugh Barr Nisbet** - *Lessing: A Biography* (2008). The standard English-language biography. Essential for the intellectual and political context of the plays.

**F.J. Lamport** - *Lessing and the Drama* (1981). Close readings of the major plays. The chapter on Emilia Galotti is the most precise analysis of the play's structural ambiguity.

**Edward Dvoretzky** - *The Enigma of Emilia Galotti* (1963). On the question of Emilia's role in her own death - the central interpretive problem of the play.

## On Orsina

Countess Orsina is not in the Verginia source. She is Lessing's addition. She arrives in Act IV with a letter that proves Marinelli's role in Appiani's murder - information that arrives too late to change the outcome but early enough to confirm that what is about to happen was not inevitable. Her function in the play is to be the person who knows the truth and cannot use it. Her position in this world carries that function intact.
