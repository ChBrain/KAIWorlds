# References

## On alcoholism and the dry drunk

The term "dry drunk" describes a person who has stopped drinking
but has not addressed the underlying patterns that drove the drinking.
The absence of alcohol does not produce recovery.
Recovery is a different process. It may or may not initiate.

The distinction matters because the house is the same house.
The weather is the same weather.
The held breath is the same held breath.
What has changed is only the bottle.

## On growing up inside it

The child who grows up in a house with alcoholism
does not receive the wound directly.
The wound lands elsewhere. The child receives the shape of the house.
The way rooms change when someone enters.
The taxonomy of silence.
The knowledge of what is not being said,
built without knowing it is being built.

This is the mechanism this world is built around.
The Thing The Child Carries is not named.
By the time it has a name, it is the shape of how she moves.

---

## On what this world does not contain

A resolution. A recovered person who speaks about their recovery.
A therapeutic arc.
The world contains [Process: Recovered](process_recovered.md).
It does not contain what follows it.
What follows it is a life. Lives are not built here.
