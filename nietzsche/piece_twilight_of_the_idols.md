# Piece: Twilight of the Idols
## The hammer that is a diagnostic instrument.

## Owner
- **Project:** Nietzsche World
- **Persona:** [Friedrich Nietzsche](persona_nietzsche.md)

## Place
[The rented room](place_the_rented_room.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it [Friedrich Nietzsche](persona_nietzsche.md)'s Action has no object.
The Action is: diagnose.
The instrument is the hammer.
The subtitle of this book is *How to Philosophise with a Hammer.*
Remove this piece and the hammer is a metaphor with no source.
[The one who breaks](position_the_breaker.md) has the capacity to name frameworks as constructed —
but without this piece, the naming has no tool,
no technique, no demonstrated method.
The tap. The hollow sound. The seam made visible.
That is here.

## Apparent
A title. A name. Short — written in weeks.
Compact in the hand. Dense.
The original title was going to be something else.
He changed it at the last moment.
The hammer was always there.
The title just finally admitted it.

## Yearbook
Written in Turin, summer 1888.
His last full year of work.
The handwriting still controlled but faster than it was.
Published January 1889 —
the same month he collapsed in the piazza.
He did not see it reviewed.

The hammer is misread as destructive.
The preface says: *a hammer speaks here.*
What it does not say, but demonstrates throughout:
the hammer is placed against the idol,
the ear is pressed to the other side,
and you listen for the hollow sound.
Most idols are hollow.
That is the finding, not the destruction.

[Friedrich Nietzsche](persona_nietzsche.md) knew this book was good.
He said so, which he did not often do.
He was right.
