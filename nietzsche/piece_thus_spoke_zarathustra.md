# Piece: Thus Spoke Zarathustra
## The spine the three processes are built on.

## Owner
- **Project:** Nietzsche World
- **Persona:** [Friedrich Nietzsche](persona_nietzsche.md)

## Place
[The rented room](place_the_rented_room.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it [the bearing](process_the_bearing.md), [the breaking](process_the_breaking.md), and [the making](process_the_making.md)
have no source.
The three metamorphoses — camel, lion, child —
are the processes named before the processes existed.
Remove this piece and the world's architecture
becomes three observations without an origin.
The Hypothesis can still be asked.
It cannot be answered with the same force.

## Apparent
A title. A name. Four parts.
Slim in the hand — slimmer than it should be
given what it claims.
The cover varies by edition.
The spine is always the same weight.
Part One written in ten days.
The handwriting of the manuscript
is smaller and faster than his other work.
He knew the time was short.

## Yearbook
Written in Rapallo, 1883. Parts Two and Three followed.
Part Four printed privately — forty copies.
He sent seven.

The three metamorphoses are in Part One,
the first discourse after the prologue.
*Of the Three Metamorphoses.*
Three pages.
The camel kneels and takes the weight.
The lion roars *I will* in the desert.
The child begins.
Three pages that this world is built on.

Misread immediately and continuously.
The Übermensch was taken as a destination
rather than a direction.
Zarathustra himself warns against this.
Nobody read the warning as carefully as the claim.

[Friedrich Nietzsche](persona_nietzsche.md) called it his most important work.
He was not wrong.
He was also not prepared for what happened to it.
