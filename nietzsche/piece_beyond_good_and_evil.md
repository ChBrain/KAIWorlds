# Piece: Beyond Good and Evil
## The genealogy the world's Hypothesis stands on.

## Owner
- **Project:** Nietzsche World

## Place

## Load Bearing
Without it the Hypothesis has no genealogy.
*When the framework a person built their life on
is revealed as their own construction —
what do they build next?*
That question requires the prior claim:
that moral frameworks are constructions,
not discoveries.
That values are made, not found.
That *good* and *evil* are not given —
they are inherited, and the inheritance
can be examined, named, set down.
Remove this piece and the world's Hypothesis
is an interesting question.
With it, the Hypothesis is inevitable.

## Apparent
A title. A name. Nine parts, two hundred and ninety-six aphorisms.
Heavy for its size.
The cover text promises more than most books deliver.
This one delivers.
The aphorisms are short.
The weight accumulates slowly
and is not felt until it is too late to put it down.

## Yearbook
Written in Sils-Maria and Nice, 1885-86.
Published at his own expense.
114 copies sold in the first year.
He sent copies to people he respected.
Most did not respond.

The central argument: every moral system
presents itself as discovered truth.
Every moral system is a constructed preference
dressed in the language of necessity.
The one who sees this
cannot unsee it.
[The one who breaks](position_the_breaker.md) is built on this.
[The rule whose seams are now visible](piece_the_broken_rule.md)
is what this book produces
when it is read correctly.

He considered it a prelude.
To what, he did not finish saying.
