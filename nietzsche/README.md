## What this world is

Three metamorphoses from Nietzsche's *Thus Spoke Zarathustra*.
The camel. The lion. The child.
Bearing, breaking, making.

This is not a world about Nietzsche.
It is a world built from the architecture he left behind.
He is present. He is not the subject.

5 personas, 7 pieces, 5 places, 4 positions, 3 processes.
The Hypothesis is already running when you arrive.

## The personas

**Friedrich Nietzsche** built the architecture before the world existed. He would have opinions about how it has been used. He does not offer comfort. He offers precision.

**Margarethe** carries the room that chose her before she chose it. There was a morning when both directions were still available. They chose the room. They called it responsibility.

**Lev** named what they were carrying and named it at cost. The naming produced a person who lives at the threshold - cannot return to the weight room, has not yet fully entered the place where the making happens. The freedom is still the thing they are carrying, worn as freedom, invisible because it is made of the act of seeing.

**Theo** is building. What they cannot see: the inherited thing is in the work in progress, transformed. Theo uses the word *contaminated*. The substitution is the shadow.

**Mira** watches all three and has a complete and nuanced answer to how each of them came to carry what they carry. Mira has not applied the questions inward. The light is real. The direction is the blindness.
