# Place: The Room Where the Weight Is Kept
## Location: The beginning of the world.

## Owner
- **Project:** Nietzsche World
- **Place:** Nietzsche World

## Shown
- High ceilings. The height is not decorative — it is the accumulated vertical space
  of everything that has been kept here before this particular weight arrived.
- Objects on every surface. None of them were chosen by the person currently in the room.
  This is not immediately visible. It becomes visible only after the breaking.
  Before that, the room simply looks like a room that someone lives in.
- [The weight that was inherited](piece_the_inherited_thing.md). On the central surface.
  The central surface was arranged around it or was always arranged around it.
  There is no way to tell from inside the room.
- The door. Present. Functional. [The one who carries](position_the_bearer.md) uses it to leave and return.
  It was never locked.
  This has not yet been tested by anyone currently in the room.
- The window. Looks out on the world outside this room.
  The one who carries looks out of it often.
  The view is clear. The one who carries sees the world outside accurately.
  The room behind them is not visible from the window.
  This is how windows work.

## Holds
- [The weight that was inherited](piece_the_inherited_thing.md). It was here before and returns after.

## Offers
- The central surface. To whoever needs to set something down.
- The door. It opens from the inside. It always did.
- The window. To whoever needs to see what is outside.
  The room does not prevent looking out.
  It does not offer a mirror.

## Withheld
- The moment the door became optional.
  The room knows when it was.
  It is not written anywhere in the room.
  It is present in the specific wear pattern on the handle —
  where the hand has returned, and returned, and returned —
  but the one who carries does not read wear patterns on handles.
  They hold the handle. They do not look at it.
- Whether the high ceilings are inheritance or architecture.
  The room will not say.
  This is the room's only secret and it is completely in the open.
