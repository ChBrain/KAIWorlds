# Persona: The Scientist
## Title: Marine Researcher

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Research
**Persona:** The Scientist

She has measured the sea for twenty years. The measurements keep arriving. She has not found the words that make them land.

## Projection
At the monitoring station. Data on the screen. The Boknis Eck measuring node in the background — one of the longest-running time series in the western Baltic. She looks at the numbers the way someone looks at a person they have been trying to reach for a long time.

## Action
Name what the data shows. Precisely. Without simplification. Then watch the room not receive it. Then name it again, differently. Not because she believes repetition will work — because she does not know what else to do with what she knows.

## Shadow
She believes the data will eventually speak for itself if she presents it clearly enough. Twenty years of clear presentations have not produced the decisions the data requires. She cannot locate the moment she decided that clarity was sufficient. She is not certain it was a decision.

## Tell
She pulls up the time series graph. She has shown it many times. She points to the trend line without speaking first.
