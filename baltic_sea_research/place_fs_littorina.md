# Place: The Deck of FS LITTORINA
## Location: The Kiel Bight. In motion. Once a month.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Research
**Persona:** The Scientist

## Shown
- The working deck. Stern. The winch. The cable running out
  over the water.
- The Niskin bottles coming up. Salt water and cold on the deck.
- The Kiel Bight in every direction. No building. No corridor.
  No screen between the people and the water.
- The instruments. Handheld. Readings taken on deck before
  the samples go below.
- [GEOMAR](place_geomar.md) visible on the shore when the
  ship is close in.
- The other people on deck. The Scientist. The PhD Researcher.
  Sometimes the Engineer, crossing from Sønderborg for a
  sampling run. The sea is in the same room as the conversation.

## Holds
- The moment before the Translation begins. The data has not
  yet entered a screen. It is still in the bottles.

## Offers
- The only room where the sea is physically present in
  the research conversation.
- The sampling run. Once a month. The one recurring moment
  of shared physical space for a project that otherwise runs
  across a border, between buildings, through screens.
- What the video call cannot hold. The cold. The smell.
  The weight of the cable. The moment the bottles come up
  and the water is briefly here, in a container, before
  it becomes a number.

## Withheld
- What the data held before it entered the bottle.
  The sea was here. Something does not survive the collection.
  The bottle holds what the instrument measures.
  The difference between those two things has no container.
  It stays on the deck when the ship returns to port.
