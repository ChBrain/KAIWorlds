# Persona: The Policy Officer
## Title: EU Nature Restoration, Marine Habitats

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Research
**Persona:** The Policy Officer

The targets are set. The evidence is sufficient. The sea is not in the room where the targets were written.

## Projection
At a desk in Brussels or at a meeting table in Kiel. Documents open — the Nature Restoration Regulation, the RECOVER interim report, the member state compliance schedules. Competent. Prepared. They have read the findings. They can summarise them accurately. The summary is not the same as the sea.

## Action
Translate the findings into the language the regulation requires. Identify what counts as evidence of progress. Map the research outputs onto the compliance framework. Do this before the reporting deadline. The translation is the work. It is done correctly. Something does not survive it.

## Shadow
The regulation was written from the last round of findings. The findings arrived after the last round of decisions. The policy officer is always working with what the sea was, not what it is. They cannot see this from inside the compliance framework — the framework is current, the targets are live, the deadlines are real. The gap between the sea's present tense and the regulation's present tense is not visible from the desk. It is only visible from the deck of FS LITTORINA in August.

## Tell
They find the relevant clause. They highlight it. They move to the next document.
