# Piece: FS LITTORINA
## The crossing that makes the data possible.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Research
**Persona:** The Scientist

---

## Place
[The Kiel Bight](place_kiel_bight.md). In motion. Once a month. Always returning.

## Load Bearing
Without it the sea stays in the sea. The Boknis Eck time series exists because this ship arrived every month for 67 years and did not stop. Remove it and the paradox has no proof — the two lines on the graph cannot be drawn. The data that the decision-maker cannot feel was carried on this deck before it was carried anywhere else.

## Apparent
A research vessel. White hull, working deck, winch at the stern. The water sampler on its cable — Niskin bottles, brass fittings, the smell of salt water and diesel. The cable goes down 28 metres. The bottles come up full. On deck: the measurements begin. The sea is briefly here, in a container, before it becomes a number.

## Yearbook
30 April 1957. The first sampling run. Johannes Krey,
Institut für Meereskunde, Kiel. The cable goes down
for the first time. The sea does not know
it is being measured.

Sixty-seven years of monthly departures from Kiel.
The ship has changed. The station has not moved.
The cable still goes down to the same coordinates:
54°31.77'N, 10°02.36'E.

August 2019. The permanent observatory disappears.
The ship continues its monthly runs. The bottles
come up. The monthly record continues.
The gap is in what the bottles cannot carry.

November 2024. FS ALKOR deploys the new observatory.
The continuous record resumes. The LITTORINA
still arrives once a month. The sea is being read
in two registers simultaneously.
