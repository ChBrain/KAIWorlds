# Place: The Kiel Bight
## Location: Western Baltic Sea, between Kiel and the Danish coast.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Research
**Persona:** The Scientist

## Shown
- Water. 25 metres average depth. Shallower on the shelves, deeper in the channels.
  The surface reflects the sky. The sky does not see what is below.
- The thermocline. Where warm surface water meets cold deep water.
  Below it: less oxygen. The organisms that need oxygen cannot cross it.
- The Boknis Eck monitoring node. Fixed to the seafloor.
  Recording since 1957. Temperature, salinity, oxygen, nutrients.
  The longest continuous record in the western Baltic.
- The coast to the west: Kiel, Eckernförde, Schleswig-Holstein.
  Agriculture behind it. Fertiliser runoff reaching the sea through rivers and groundwater.
- The coast to the north: Flensburg Fjord. The border above it.
  The water does not stop at the border.
- The open connection east: to the central Baltic, to the Belts, to the North Sea.
  What enters here travels. What is depleted here affects what travels.
- [The Deck of FS LITTORINA](place_fs_littorina.md). On the water once a month.

## Holds
- [The Microalgae](piece_microalgae.md). Everywhere. Invisible until the bloom.
- The oxygen record. 67 years of measurements. The trend is visible.

## Offers
- The time series. What the Boknis Eck node has recorded since 1957.
  Available to anyone who asks. Rarely asked for in the room where decisions are made.
- The bloom. When the conditions are right. Not always. More often than before.

## Withheld
- What the Bight held before the nitrogen arrived.
  The seagrass beds. The cod populations. The oxygen in the deep basins in August.
  The Bight knows what it held. It has not been asked to compare.
