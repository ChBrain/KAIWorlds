# Place: University of Southern Denmark — Alsion
## Location: Alsion 2, Sønderborg. On the Alssund.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Research
**Persona:** The Engineer

## Shown
- The Alsion building. On the Alssund. The water of the strait
  directly outside every window facing east.
- [The Mads Clausen Institute](place_mads_clausen_institute.md).
  Sensors. Prototypes. The algae signal being translated into data.
- The Mærsk Mc-Kinney Møller Institute. AI models and visualisation
  tools. The data being translated into something a policy officer
  can read.
- The Department of Political Science. Studying societal acceptance
  of restoration measures. Whether the instrument will be trusted,
  running as a research project two floors from the bench where
  the instrument is being built.
- A concert hall. The South Denmark Philharmonic. In the same
  building. Culture and science sharing a structure on the water.
- The border. On a map in the corridor. The water in Sønderborg
  today was in Flensburg Fjord yesterday and in Kiel Fjord
  the day before. The map marks where the governance stops.
  The water does not stop.
- The connection to [GEOMAR](place_geomar.md).
  Across the border. 90 kilometres by road.
  The water makes it continuous.

## Holds
- Three different parts of the same question. In three institutes.
  The convergence has not been scheduled.

## Offers
- The full chain. Algae signal to sensor to AI model to
  policy-legible visualisation — under one roof.
- The view. The Alssund. The water the instruments are being
  built to read is outside every eastward window.

## Withheld
- Whether the three institutes will converge before the project
  closes. The sensor produces the data. The AI processes it.
  The political science department studies whether anyone will
  act on it. The three rooms have not yet fully talked
  to each other.
