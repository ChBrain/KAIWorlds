# Piece: The Microalgae
## The first thing that knows before anyone measures it.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Research
**Persona:** The Scientist

---

## Place
[The Kiel Bight](place_kiel_bight.md). Everywhere in the water column. Invisible until the bloom.

## Load Bearing
Without them the ecosystem has no early warning system. The microalgae respond to nutrient loading before the oxygen collapses, before the fish die-offs, before the measurements that trigger the reports that trigger the decisions. Remove this piece and the world has data but no signal. The signal arrives before the data. The data arrives before the decision. The decision arrives after the cost.

## Apparent
Single-celled. Microscopic. Invisible to the decision-maker looking at the sea. Visible from satellite when the bloom has already spread across the surface — green and blue-green, kilometres wide, toxic in some species. By the time it is visible it has already been growing for weeks.

## Yearbook
They have been here longer than the Baltic itself — carried in when the sea formed after the last ice age. They are the foundation of the food web. The herring eat them. The cod eat the herring.

When the nitrogen arrived — agricultural runoff, the 20th century — they bloomed larger. The blooms consume the oxygen when they die and sink. The deep basins empty. The cod move. The herring change.

RECOVER is testing them as health indicators. They are the right indicator. They knew before RECOVER arrived.
