# Place: The Mads Clausen Institute
## Location: University of Southern Denmark, Alsion, Sønderborg.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Research
**Persona:** The Engineer

## Shown
- A lab bench. Sensor prototypes. Wiring, circuit boards,
  calibration equipment. The smell of electronics and salt water
  — the salt water brought in for testing.
- Screens showing algae signal data. Not the sea. The signal
  the sea is sending, translated into numbers, tested against
  numbers the sea actually produced.
- The window. The Alssund visible. The water the instruments
  are being built to read is outside the window.
- The door to the corridor. Connects to the AI team, to the
  visualisation team, to the political science department
  at SDU. The building holds three different parts of
  the same question.

## Holds
- The sensor prototypes. Low-cost, robust. Built to be used
  by trained citizens as well as researchers.
- The algae samples. In containers. The signal source the
  instruments are being calibrated against.

## Offers
- The crossing. The sensor is the instrument that closes
  part of the gap between what the algae know and what
  the decision-maker can act on.
- Real-time data. When the sensors are deployed. Not yet.
  Being built toward.

## Withheld
- Whether the sensor is measuring the right thing.
  The instrument is calibrated against what the algae produce.
  The question of whether what the algae produce is the right
  proxy for what the decision room needs to act on is not
  on the bench. It is in the gap between the instrument
  and the question it was built to answer.
  The bench cannot see that gap from inside the calibration.
