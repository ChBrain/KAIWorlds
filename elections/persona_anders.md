# Persona: Anders
## Title: [Voter Mecklenburg-Vorpommern](position_mv_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Forty-four. Runs a small guesthouse in Stralsund — twelve rooms, year-round occupancy from the Altstadt tourism, seasonal peak from June to September. The Stralsund Altstadt is UNESCO World Heritage since 2002. The guesthouse has been in her family since 1991 when her parents leased the building in the first post-Wende privatisation round.
Born in Stralsund. Left for Hamburg for a hotel management degree, returned at 28 when her mother needed help with the business. Has been back for sixteen years.
Votes SPD. Voted SPD in 2021 — she considers this partly a vote for Schwesig personally and partly a vote for the SPD's social policy positions. Voted SPD in the Europawahl and the Bundestagswahl.
Stralsund: the ferry to Rügen leaves from here, the Ozeaneum is the dominant institution, the Hanseatic architecture is the product she sells. The city has functioned as a tourism asset more successfully than most MV towns. The housing market is under pressure from seasonal rental competition — Airbnb and the guesthouse owners are in an uncomfortable alliance against the same problem.
Stralsundisch: a Low German-influenced northern dialect, softer than the inland varieties. She uses it with locals and drops it for guests. Standard German is her professional register. She notices when politicians speak regional German and considers it a choice, not a reflex.

## Action
Tracks the coalition negotiations in September 2026 with professional attention — her guesthouse's VAT rate, the Mehrwertsteuerstreit for tourism businesses, the federal hotel tax debate all flow through federal and state policy.
Will vote SPD in September 2026 despite the polls. Not from habit — from a considered position that she has not found a better alternative that is viable.
Has opinions about the Nord Stream Klimastiftung that are not simple — she understood the MV case for the pipeline (energy security, economic benefit) before February 2022, and understands why that position became indefensible after.

## Shadow
She voted for Schwesig in 2021 partly on trust. The Klimastiftung has cost her that specific trust without moving her vote. She is aware this is not fully coherent. She continues.
The AfD polls at 37% in MV. In Stralsund they poll lower. She cannot see the 37% in her immediate environment and knows that this means she is not seeing something real.
Her guesthouse depends on tourist visitors, many of whom come from western Germany and Denmark. Their image of MV is shaped partly by what they read about MV politics. The Nord Stream story reached Denmark.
She will vote SPD in September. She does not know who the SPD candidate is. She is not sure it matters for her vote. She is not sure it should matter. These are separate discomforts she carries simultaneously.

## Tell
She gives the economic argument for political positions before the values argument — not because she doesn't hold the values, but because the economic argument is more specific and she trusts specificity more.
