## Place
[Rotes Rathaus Berlin](place_be_rotes_rathaus.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it the CDU has no governing partner.
Without it the SPD's choice — to abandon the R2G majority and enter as junior partner to the CDU — has no formal ground.
Without it Wegner cannot govern. 52 CDU seats. Majority: 80. Without SPD's 34 the coalition does not exist.

## Apparent
A bound document. Signed 26 April 2023.
CDU and SPD. A grand coalition — Große Koalition, GroKo — of the two largest parties.
The existing R2G coalition (SPD-Greens-Linke) retained a mathematical majority after the 2023 election. The SPD's membership voted 54.3% to enter the CDU coalition instead. Giffey, the outgoing Regierende Bürgermeisterin, proposed it despite knowing she would lose her title. She lost it. Wegner leads.
Five Senatsverwaltungen for each party. Ten Senators total.

## Yearbook
April 2023. CDU and SPD sign the coalition agreement. Each takes five Senatsverwaltungen.
27 April 2023. Wegner elected Regierender Bürgermeister — third ballot, 86 votes. First and second ballots failed: some coalition members withheld their vote. The secret ballot obscures who. The coalition has 86 seats. Exactly 86 votes. No margin.
2023–2025. Coalition governs. Housing, transport, public order, school reform are the contested areas. Berlin's chronic fiscal deficit continues — Länderfinanzausgleich dependency, federal infrastructure investment, housing costs rising.
Current polls (April 2026): CDU 23%, AfD 16%, Linke 16%, SPD 16%, Greens 15%. The coalition has no majority in current polling. The next election is 20 September 2026.
