# Position: Voter Brandenburg

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election.md)

## Has
Two votes on Landtag polling day — Erststimme for a direct candidate, Zweitstimme for a party list.
Minimum voting age: 16.
One vote in the federal election. One vote in the European election.
The [election record](piece_bb_election_record.md) as the accumulated history.
Geography as political identity — sharper in Brandenburg than almost anywhere. Potsdam and its surroundings vote like a western city. The Lausitz, Uckermark, Spree-Neiße vote like the eastern periphery they are. The same ballot produces different worlds depending on which side of Berlin the polling station stands.
The knowledge, in 2024, that the election was a referendum on whether the SPD could hold Brandenburg against the AfD. Woidke said so explicitly. The position voted on that framing.

## Orders
Vote or don't.
In 2024: 72.9% did — highest since 1990. The Woidke referendum mobilised Brandenburg in a way no normal Landtag election had. People who had not voted since 1994 came out to vote SPD. Or to vote AfD. The turnout benefited both leading parties.

## Loses
The Linke as an option. Gone from the Landtag for the first time since 1990. The voter who voted Linke for thirty-four years and cast their last vote for a party that disappeared overnight. The Linke took 3.0% — outside the chamber. Their voters went to BSW, SPD, AfD, and Nichtwähler in roughly equal measure.
In the Lausitz: the voter who voted SPD in 1994 at 54% and voted AfD in 2024 at 39% in Spree-Neiße. The Zeche did not close overnight. The exodus did not happen at once. The vote moved in one direction across thirty years in small annual steps that the aggregate made visible.

## Drives
On the loss of the Linke as an option —
votes in the knowledge that the 2024 election was a referendum framed by Woidke personally.
The position that turned out at 72.9% — highest since 1990 — was mobilised by that framing.
The position holds the vote as the answer to a specific question: whether Brandenburg remains the eastern state that held.
It voted. The answer, for now, is yes.
