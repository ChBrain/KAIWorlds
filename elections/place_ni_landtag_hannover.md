## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Shown
Leineschloss, Hannah-Arendt-Platz 1, Hannover. A baroque palace on the Leine river, converted to parliament use in 1962. The building was destroyed in the war, rebuilt. The parliament of a state that was itself created in 1946 from multiple Prussian provinces and the Free State of Brunswick sits in a rebuilt baroque palace beside a river named for the region.
146 seats currently. SPD 57, CDU 47, Greens 24, AfD 18. Majority: 74.
SPD-Green coalition holds 81 — a working majority, seven above threshold.
6 Bundesrat seats — the maximum, shared with Bayern and NRW.
Voting age: 18.
Next election: 2027.

## Offers
Legislation for 8.1 million people — Germany's second-largest Flächenstaat by area.
Election of the Ministerpräsident.
Six Bundesrat seats — the maximum. On industrial policy, labour law, VW-related legislation: Niedersachsen's votes are followed closely by Wolfsburg.
The Leine outside. Hannover as Germany's largest trade fair city — the Hannover Messe, the IAA (before it moved to Munich). The state's economic identity is built partly on exhibition and logistics.

## Withheld
The VW supervisory board vote. The state holds 20% of VW shares. The Ministerpräsident sits on the supervisory board. Every major VW restructuring — including the 2024 plant closure discussions — runs through this chamber indirectly.
The AfD's 18 seats — entering in 2017 at 6.2%, rising to 10.9% in 2022. Current polls show them at 21% nationally. What that means for 2027 in Niedersachsen is not yet visible.
The Emsland and Vechta voters — the Catholic western triangle that votes CDU at 40–46% while Hannover votes SPD at 35%. The same Landtag holds both.
