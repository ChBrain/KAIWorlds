# Piece: Election Record Sachsen-Anhalt

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Place:** [Landtag Magdeburg](place_st_landtag_magdeburg.md)

## Load Bearing
Without it Haseloff's 2021 triumph — CDU at 37%, AfD pushed back to 21% — has no scale against the current polls showing AfD at 38% with no equivalent counter.
Without it the 2026 election, five months away, has no historical context — the only eastern state where the AfD is tracking to become the largest party without a Woidke or Kretschmer to hold them.
Without it the Magdeburger Modell — a SPD minority government tolerated by the PDS in the 1990s — has no place in the architecture.
Remove it and Sachsen-Anhalt looks like a generic eastern state. With it, it looks like the state that tried every coalition format and is now facing the one no democratic party will enter.

## Apparent
Sachsen-Anhalt: 2.2 million inhabitants. 4 Bundesrat seats. Capital: Magdeburg. Founded 3 October 1990. The state between the Harz and the Elbe — Martin Luther's territory, Wittenberg, Lutherstadt Eisleben, Quedlinburg.
Current: CDU-SPD-FDP Deutschland-Koalition. Haseloff Ministerpräsident — but not standing in 2026.
Next election: **6 September 2026.** Current polls: AfD 38%, CDU 25%. No viable democratic majority visible without a significant shift.
Voting age: 18. 4 Bundesrat seats.

## Yearbook
**2026** — 6 September. Next Landtag election. Haseloff not standing. No named CDU successor with comparable personal authority. AfD polling at 38% — largest party. CDU at 25%. No democratic majority achievable without BSW, Linke, or significant CDU recovery.

**2021** — 6 June. CDU **37.1%** — up 7.4 points. AfD 20.8% — down 3.4 points, first decline for AfD in an eastern state election. Linke 11.0%. SPD 8.4% — worst ever in Sachsen-Anhalt. FDP 6.4% — returns after ten years. Greens 5.9%. Turnout 60.3%. CDU won 40 of 41 direct constituencies. Deutschland-Koalition: CDU-SPD-FDP — first at any German state level. Haseloff re-elected on second ballot. Greens excluded despite being arithmetically available — CDU preferred FDP. The Umweltministerium was abolished in the new government.

**2016** — 13 March. CDU 29.8%. AfD **24.3%** — first entry, immediately second largest party. Linke 16.3%. SPD 10.6%. Greens 5.2%. FDP 4.9% — barely outside. Turnout 61.1%. Kenia-Koalition: CDU-SPD-Greens — first in Germany. Haseloff. The Kenia coalition was a direct response to the AfD's strength — no two-party majority existed that excluded AfD.

**2011** — 20 March. CDU 32.5%. SPD 21.5%. Linke 23.7%. Greens 7.1%. FDP 3.8% — outside. Turnout 51.2%. CDU-SPD coalition. Haseloff first term as Ministerpräsident.

**2006** — 26 March. CDU 36.2%. Linke 24.1%. SPD 21.4%. FDP 6.7%. DVU 3.0% — outside. Turnout 44.4% — historic low. CDU-SPD coalition. Wolfgang Böhmer Ministerpräsident.

**2002** — 21 April. CDU 37.3%. SPD 20.0%. Linke/PDS 20.4%. FDP 13.3% — strong FDP result. DVU 1.8% — outside. Turnout 56.5%. CDU-FDP coalition. Böhmer.

**1998** — 26 April. CDU 22.0%. SPD 35.9% — SPD peak in Sachsen-Anhalt. Linke/PDS 19.6%. DVU **12.9%** — far-right surge. FDP 4.2% — outside. Greens 3.2% — outside. Turnout 71.6%. SPD-PDS coalition. Reinhard Höppner Ministerpräsident — controversial red-red coalition in 1994 had already established the precedent.

**1994** — 26 June. SPD **34.0%**. CDU 34.4% — CDU fractionally largest. PDS 19.9%. FDP 3.6% — outside. DVU 6.9%. Greens 5.1%. Turnout 54.8%. CDU-FDP tried but FDP below threshold — SPD-minority government tolerated by PDS. The Magdeburger Modell. Werner Münch (CDU) was already Ministerpräsident; Höppner (SPD) formed the minority. A contested transition.

**1990** — 14 October. CDU **39.0%**. SPD 26.0%. PDS 12.0%. FDP 13.5%. Greens/Bündnis 90 5.3%. Turnout 65.5%. CDU-FDP coalition. Gerd Gies (CDU) briefly Ministerpräsident, then Werner Münch.
