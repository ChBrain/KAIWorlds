## Load Bearing
Without it the CDU has no governing partner — 40 seats, majority 49.
Without it the first Deutschland-Koalition at any German state level has no record.
Without it the Greens' exclusion — despite being arithmetically available — has no context.

## Apparent
CDU, SPD, and FDP. Signed September 2021. Deutschland-Koalition — the first at any German state level, a name borrowed from the federal Black-Red-Yellow combination that had never actually existed in Berlin.
58 of 97 seats — working majority.
The Greens were arithmetically available for a Kenia-II coalition but CDU preferred FDP. The Umweltministerium was abolished — its functions distributed to other ministries.
Haseloff elected Ministerpräsident 16 September 2021 — failed first ballot (48 votes needed 49), won second (no absolute majority required in second round). Seven CDU members did not support him in round one.

## Yearbook
August 2021. CDU, SPD, FDP agree coalition terms.
September 2021. Coalition signed. Greens excluded. Umweltministerium dissolved.
16 September 2021. Haseloff elected on second ballot.
2021–2026. Deutschland-Koalition governs. Stable majority. CDU dominant.
2024 Kommunalwahl and Europawahl: AfD receives most votes in Sachsen-Anhalt — both times. The federal mood is different from 2021.
April 2026. Haseloff announces he will not stand in September.
Current polls: AfD 38%, CDU 25%. The coalition's combined share (CDU+SPD+FDP) would be approximately 34% — not a majority.
6 September 2026. The coalition agreement ends. What replaces it is the central political question in Sachsen-Anhalt.
