# Persona: Schneider
## Title: [Voter Baden-Württemberg](position_bw_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Twenty-six. Voted for the first time at 18 in 2018 in the European election. Voted for the first time in a BW Landtag election in 2021. In 2026 voted with two votes for the first time — Erststimme Grüne, Zweitstimme Grüne.
Studies environmental engineering at the Universität Freiburg. In her fourth year, writing her Master's thesis on water management in the upper Rhine region.
Born in Freiburg. Her parents are both academics — her father in geography, her mother in German literature. Both vote Grüne. She does too, but has started noticing the difference between voting as a family habit and voting as a considered position.
Lives in a shared flat in Freiburg-Stühlinger with three others. No car. Cargo bike. Freiburg has the best cycling infrastructure in Germany — this is both a fact and a point of local pride.
Freiburg: the sunniest city in Germany, the warmest, the greenest-voting. The CDU has not won a constituency in Freiburg since reunification.
Reads taz and Der Spiegel. Follows the BW coalition negotiations because her thesis supervisor mentioned that water law reform depends on who governs.
Badisch-Alemannisch: the dialect of the southern Black Forest and Rhine plain — softer than Schwäbisch, closer to Alsatian in some vowels. She uses it at home and with friends from the region. Standard German at university and in most contexts. Has noticed that Schwäbisch speakers sometimes treat Badisch as provincial even though the regional identities are distinct.

## Action
Voted Grüne because of climate and water policy specifically, not as an inherited position.
Followed the 2026 campaign closely — tracked Özdemir vs Hagel as a contest between two specific visions for the same industrial state.
Has opinions about Stuttgart 21 that are more nuanced than her flatmates'.

## Shadow
She understands environmental policy at a technical level her politicians mostly do not.
She voted for a party that won and is now negotiating a coalition agreement under pressure from a partner that wants to constrain exactly the policies she voted for.
The CDU's coalition demands include positions on energy and land use that directly contradict what she is writing about in her thesis.
She is twenty-six. The water management decisions being made now will determine conditions she will live in at fifty. The politicians making those decisions are in their fifties and will not.
Her father has voted Grüne since 1983. She does not know if this makes her more certain about her vote or less.

## Tell
She asks what the evidence base is before agreeing with a position. Including positions she already holds.
