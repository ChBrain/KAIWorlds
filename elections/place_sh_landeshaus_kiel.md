# Place: Landeshaus Kiel
## Where the state mandate is held and exercised.
## Runs: [the election process](process_election.md) · [the governing process](process_governing.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Shown
The former Royal Marine Academy. Built 1888 on the western shore of the Kieler Förde.
Red brick. Three storeys. A building that was designed to project imperial authority and has since been asked to project democratic transparency.
The glass plenary chamber faces the water — added in 2003, visible from the Förde. The old stone building behind it, the new glass in front.
69 seats arranged in a semicircle. Five party groupings in the current legislature: CDU, Greens, SPD, FDP, SSW.
The public gallery above. Visitors can watch.
The chamber opens onto a roof terrace above the water.
The Staatskanzlei is two streets away — separate building, separate logic.
The Förde outside. Ships pass. The view is Baltic.

## Offers
Legislation in the state's domains: education, culture, police, administrative law.
Election of the Minister-President.
Parliamentary scrutiny of the state government.
The public record of what the coalition said it would do and what it has done.
A room where the opposition speaks. What is said here is on the record.

## Withheld
What was decided before the session opened.
The conversations in the corridor that resolved what the chamber then formally ratified.
The distance between the vote on the floor and the agreement that preceded it.
How often the Bundesrat business in Berlin shapes what is or is not brought to this chamber.
