# Position: Ministerpräsident Baden-Württemberg

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election.md)
- **Process:** [the governing process](process_governing.md)
- **Place:** [Staatsministerium Stuttgart](place_bw_staatsministerium.md)

## Has
The mandate from the [Landtag Stuttgart](place_bw_landtag_stuttgart.md) — not yet formally awarded. Coalition agreement still under negotiation.
The Grüne's 56 seats — exactly matched by the CDU's 56. No seat advantage. Every cabinet decision requires CDU agreement.
The inheritance from Kretschmann — fifteen years of the position defined by one person's style: Catholic, conservative-Green, Swabian, personally popular beyond his party, the Landesvater model.
Five Bundesrat seats in Berlin.
The largest industrial economy in Germany outside NRW. Mercedes-Benz. Bosch. Porsche. The Mittelstand of the Neckar valley and the Black Forest.
Stuttgart 21.

## Orders
Govern a state where the coalition partner has identical seat count and extensive structural demands.
Succeed Kretschmann without imitating him — Özdemir is from Stuttgart by choice, not by birth, a national figure coming home to govern locally.
Hold five Bundesrat seats aligned while the coalition negotiates every policy position from scratch.
Manage the transition of BW's industrial economy — away from combustion engines, toward what comes next — without losing the companies or the workers who built the state.

## Loses
The Landesvater position as it existed under Kretschmann.
Kretschmann governed from personal authority — his approval ratings exceeded his party's vote share substantially. The position under Özdemir must be rebuilt from the coalition agreement up.
The CDU's structural demands are a claim that the position cannot simply inherit Kretschmann's authority. The CDU insists on procedural checks that Kretschmann never needed because his majority was real.
The 56-56 result is the electoral message: the state did not endorse the Grüne, it chose between two evenly matched options and the Grüne prevailed by 0.5 points. The position governs with half a mandate.

## Drives
On the loss of the clear mandate —
governs as a coalition manager rather than a Landesvater.
Builds authority through delivery — school reform, housing, infrastructure — rather than through personal popularity.
Uses the national profile as a resource: Özdemir is one of the most recognisable German politicians. The position gains national visibility without the position requiring it.
