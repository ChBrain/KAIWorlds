# Piece: Bundesrat Delegation Hessen

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Place
[Bundesrat Berlin](place_bundesrat_berlin.md).
And simultaneously: [Staatskanzlei Wiesbaden](place_he_staatskanzlei_wiesbaden.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it 6.4 million people and the European financial centre they host have no Bundesrat representation.
Without it Hessen's specific interests — financial market regulation, Frankfurt airport infrastructure, Rhine-Main metropolitan planning — have no voice in the federal chamber.

## Apparent
Five seats. Appointed from CDU-SPD cabinet. Voted as a bloc.
Hessen's five votes align broadly with CDU federal positions — centre-right on fiscal policy, on migration, on internal security. SPD modulates on social policy.
The financial centre creates specific Bundesrat positions: Hessen lobbies for federal financial market regulation that does not disadvantage Frankfurt relative to London, Paris, Amsterdam post-Brexit. Hessen's Bundesrat position on EU financial regulation is watched by the banking sector.

## Yearbook
January 2024. CDU-SPD cabinet appointed. Delegation follows.
2024–2028. Five votes cast from a CDU-dominant coalition. On financial market legislation, Hessen often leads Bundesrat initiatives or opposes federal measures that threaten Frankfurt's regulatory advantage.
The Finanzplatz Frankfurt lobby — Deutsche Bank, Commerzbank, Deutsche Börse, the ECB as indirect stakeholder — does not vote in Hessen elections but shapes the political environment in which the Bundesrat delegation operates.
