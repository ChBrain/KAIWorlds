# Place: Staatskanzlei Sachsen Dresden
## Runs: [the governing process](process_governing.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Shown
Archivstraße 1, Dresden. A classical building in the Innere Neustadt. The executive seat of the Ministerpräsident and the Sächsische Staatsregierung.
Kretschmer governs from here — minority government, 50 seats, assembling case-by-case majorities in a chamber of 120.
Four Bundesrat seats coordinated from here. Sachsen's position on federal legislation is shaped by a CDU-SPD minority that must negotiate every vote.
Sachsen receives net from the Länderfinanzausgleich. The structural funds — EU Kohäsionsfonds, federal East Germany investment — are managed through ministries answering to this building.
The hard Schuldenbremse: Sachsen's state constitution has one of the strictest debt brakes in Germany, restricting deficit spending even during downturns.

## Offers
Executive governance from a position of structural weakness — minority government in the state where the CDU has never lost.
Four Bundesrat votes — cast from a coalition that needs Greens, Linke, or AfD abstentions to pass legislation in its own Landtag.
The Strukturwandel: Sachsen's transformation from a DDR industrial economy — cameras (Praktica), typewriters (Erika), textiles — to TSMC's semiconductor fab outside Dresden, the BMW plant in Leipzig, the logistics corridor along the A4. The Staatskanzlei manages the continuity of this transformation across governments.

## Withheld
Whether the minority government survives to 2029. Each budget vote, each legislative package, each constitutional decision requires a case-by-case majority.
What Kretschmer conceded to the Linke for their six investiture votes in December 2024.
The demographic emptying of the Erzgebirge, the Vogtland, eastern Sachsen toward the Polish border — populations that left in the 1990s and 2000s and did not return, replaced by AfD vote shares above 40%.
