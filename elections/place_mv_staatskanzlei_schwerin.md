## Owner

## Shown
Schloßstraße 2-4, Schwerin — the Schweriner Schloss. The 19th-century castle on a lake island, restored after DDR use as a teachers' college. The Landesregierung moved in in 1990. One of the most architecturally striking seats of any German state government — a neo-gothic and baroque fantasy on water.
Schwesig governs from here. SPD-Linke coalition. Three Bundesrat seats coordinated from here.
MV receives net from the Länderfinanzausgleich — one of the larger recipients, a structurally weak economy despite three decades of EU Kohäsionsmittel investment.
The Schloss faces the Landtag across the Schweriner See. Parliament and government look at each other across the water.

## Offers
Executive governance of Germany's least densely populated Flächenstaat — 69 inhabitants per square kilometre.
Three Bundesrat votes aligned with SPD-led left positions — on housing, fiscal transfers, social policy. On energy: complicated by the Nord Stream legacy.
The tourism infrastructure coordination — Baltic resorts, island ferry services, national park management. The Bodden coast is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The Schloss itself is a tourist destination.

## Withheld
The Nord Stream Klimastiftung established from this building — the decision, who made it, what it was intended to accomplish, what it actually did. The foundation was wound up in 2022 after international pressure. The political damage is ongoing.
Whether Schwesig governs from here after October 2026. If the AfD wins 37% and no democratic majority exists, the Staatskanzlei is in constitutional uncertainty.
The lake in winter — grey, empty, the Schloss lit against the cold. The political temperature of a state that empties toward the cities and is governed from a castle.
