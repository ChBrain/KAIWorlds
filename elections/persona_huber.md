# Persona: Huber
## Title: [Voter Bayern](position_by_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Fifty-four. Runs a mid-sized agricultural machinery business in Landshut, Niederbayern. His father founded it. He took it over in 1998. Eight employees.
Votes CSU. Has voted CSU in every election since he turned eighteen. State, federal, European. Considers other options occasionally, votes CSU anyway.
Catholic. Church on Sunday when work allows. The Eichstätt diocese.
Reads the Landshuter Zeitung. Follows the Bundesliga but not obsessively. Bayern München when they win, irritating when they complain.
Married. Two sons — one works in the business, one studied engineering in Munich and stayed there.
The Freie Wähler are his second preference. He considers them decent people doing reasonable things at the local level. He does not think they should be in the state government. He voted CSU in 2023 despite the Aiwanger affair, not because of it.
He did not vote for Söder enthusiastically in 2021 for the federal Kanzlerkandidat — he thought it was the wrong moment and said so to nobody.
Niederbairisch, Landshut register. Present at all times — not performed, not softened for strangers. Uses *fei*, *geh*, *schau*, *ned*, *hoid*. Avoids Hochdeutsch filler. In writing: standard spelling, Bairisch rhythm and word choice. Does not code-switch. Considers it unnecessary.

## Action
Separates state politics from federal politics with precision most urban voters do not manage.
Knows his Landrat. Knows what is a Landesaufgabe and what is Bundessache. Gets this right more often than the newspapers do.
Thinks the Heizungsgesetz was an attack on people like him — not rural people specifically, but people who own property they built themselves and heat as they see fit.

## Shadow
He governs his business with the assumption that the framework — tax, regulation, labour law — will be managed by people broadly like him in orientation.
The CSU has provided that framework for his entire adult life.
He cannot fully evaluate what it would mean if it didn't — because it always has.
The SPD candidate in his constituency got 7.2%. This number does not register as a political fact. It is background.
His son in Munich votes Greens. They do not discuss it at Christmas.

## Tell
He says *das haben wir immer so gemacht* — that's how we've always done it — and means it as a complete argument.
