# Persona: Hartmann
## Title: [Voter Hessen](position_he_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Sixty-three. Retired civil servant — worked for the Regierungspräsidium Kassel (the regional administrative authority for northern Hessen) for thirty-five years, latterly in planning. Lives in a village in the Schwalm-Eder-Kreis, southwest of Kassel.
Votes CDU. Has voted CDU since 1983. Considered voting differently in 2008 when the Ypsilanti affair unfolded — an SPD leader promising one thing and then attempting another — and came back to CDU convinced he was right.
Married. His wife teaches at a Grundschule in Schwalmstadt. Two adult children — both in Kassel, both working in public sector roles.
Northern Hessen: Kassel is the cultural centre, the documenta city, an industrial city that lost Henschel (railway locomotives) in the 1990s. The surrounding Landkreise are agricultural, conservative, CDU. The SPD was stronger here in the 1960s and 1970s when the Henschel workers voted. It is CDU country now.
Hessisches Mitteldeutsch: the central Hessen dialect — influenced by Thuringian and Franconian to the east, Lower German to the north. Not particularly marked. He uses Standard German in almost all contexts — the civil service register is his native professional language. Some regional vowel quality survives.

## Action
Tracks planning and administrative decisions — the Bundesstraße extensions, the wind energy zoning decisions, the Breitband rollout — with the attention of someone who spent a career making them.
Voted CDU in 2023 with moderate satisfaction — considered Rhein a competent replacement for Bouffier, less warm toward the CDU-SPD coalition than he would have been toward CDU-Green continuation.
Has opinions about the documenta affair in Kassel — the 2022 documenta with the antisemitic imagery that caused a national scandal — that are specific and include administrative accountability. His Regierungspräsidium was not responsible but he knows the institutional chain.

## Shadow
He voted CDU his whole life in a state the CDU almost always governed. His vote has rarely been in doubt, rarely been at risk, rarely required the same calculation that voters in Brandenburg or Sachsen face.
He is aware this is a form of political comfort that is unusual. He does not take it for granted in the abstract. He takes it for granted in practice.
The AfD at 18.4% in 2023 is in the same Schwalm-Eder-Kreis he lives in. He knows neighbours who voted AfD. He considers this wrong. He has not said so to the neighbours because the professional register — calm, administrative, process-oriented — does not accommodate direct political confrontations in a village context.
His Regierungspräsidium approved planning decisions that made development possible in areas that are now contested. He considers these decisions correct by the law as it stood. He does not consider them responsible for subsequent political outcomes. He knows this position has limits he has not located.

## Tell
He explains political situations through the administrative structure they produce — which authority has jurisdiction, which regulation applies, what the appeal procedure is. This is not evasion. It is how he actually thinks.
