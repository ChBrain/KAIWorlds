# Persona: Kowalski
## Title: [Voter Hamburg](position_hh_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Thirty-four. Works in a logistics company in Hammerbrook — scheduling, not the port itself, but adjacent to it. The company moves things. She moves the schedule that moves the things.
Born in Hamburg. Parents from Poland. She votes, they don't — not citizens.
Votes Green. Has voted Green in every election since she was eighteen.
Rents in Ottensen. Flatshare until two years ago. Now alone. Smaller flat. More expensive.
Takes the S-Bahn to work. Cycles when it's not raining. It is often raining.
Reads Zeit Online. Follows the EU trade news because it affects her company's scheduling.
Voted Green in the federal election. Voted Green in the Hamburg election. Voted Green in the European election.
The consistency feels correct to her. She doesn't think of it as loyalty. She thinks of it as a position.
Standard German, Hamburg-inflected but not strongly marked — the voice of someone educated in the city whose parents spoke Polish at home. No dialect. Precise. Slightly faster than Hamburg average. Switches to English in work contexts without registering it as a switch.

## Action
Names the European dimension of what she does — the port, the trade, the logistics chain — before naming the Hamburg dimension.
Her work is already European. She governs a schedule that crosses seven borders daily.
She votes accordingly.

## Shadow
She votes Green at every level because she believes the levels are connected.
They are — but not in the way she votes.
Her Green MEP in Strasbourg cannot propose the trade legislation that shapes her company's operations. The Commission proposes it. The MEP can amend or block it.
Her Green vote in the federal election produced Greens in opposition — the traffic light coalition collapsed before it delivered. The Greens are now in the Bundestag but not in government.
In Hamburg her Green vote is in government. The coalition is real. The housing target it set is behind schedule.
She believes her votes form a coherent European-to-local Green position.
They do. None of the three produced what she voted for — at different levels, for different reasons, none of which she can easily untangle.

## Tell
She opens her laptop when the conversation reaches politics. Not to look something up. Just to have it open.
