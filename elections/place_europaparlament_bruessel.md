# Place: Europaparlament Brüssel
## Where the work happens. Where the MEPs actually are.
## Runs: [the Europa governing process](process_governing_europa.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Shown
The Espace Léopold complex, Brussels, Belgium.
The Paul-Henri Spaak building at its centre — the Brussels hemicycle. Smaller than Strasbourg. Used for committee work, mini-plenaries, extraordinary sessions.
Committee rooms across multiple buildings. Twenty permanent committees. Each with a secretariat of eight to twelve staff.
Political group offices. Each of the eight groups has rooms, staff, researchers, coordinators.
The Commission is nearby — the Berlaymont building, visible from the Parliament complex.
The Council is nearby. The European Council is nearby.
Three weeks out of four, this is where the MEPs are.
The fourth week: Strasbourg.

## Offers
Committee work — where legislation is actually shaped before the Strasbourg vote.
Political group coordination — where MEPs negotiate across national lines.
Access to the Commission and Council — the institutions that propose and implement.
The rapporteur role — the MEP assigned to shepherd a specific piece of legislation through committee. The most influential position available to an MEP.
The informal trilogue — the three-way negotiation between Parliament, Council, and Commission where most legislation is actually agreed.

## Withheld
The right of initiative. The Commission proposes. The Parliament can request. The Commission decides whether to respond.
What the Council of Ministers agrees behind closed doors before it arrives here.
The distance between what an MEP campaigns on at home and what their political group requires of them in committee.
That most Europeans do not know who their MEP is, what committee they sit on, or what they voted for last month in Strasbourg.
