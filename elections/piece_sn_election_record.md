# Piece: Election Record Sachsen

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Place:** [Landtag Dresden](place_sn_landtag_dresden.md)

## Load Bearing
Without it the CDU's unbroken winning streak has no architecture — eight elections, eight CDU victories, three of them with absolute majorities that needed no coalition.
Without it the AfD's 30.6% in 2024 has no trajectory — they were at 9.7% in 2014 and zero before that.
Without it the NPD's ten years in the Landtag 2004–2014 has no place in the record — the far-right presence that prefigured the AfD.
Without it the Brombeer failure — CDU, BSW, and SPD all in the room, BSW walking out — has no context.
Remove it and Sachsen looks like any CDU-governed state. With it, it looks like the state where the German right tested its limits before anyone else.

## Apparent
Sachsen: 4.0 million inhabitants. 4 Bundesrat seats. Capital: Dresden. Founded 3 October 1990. Also known as the Freistaat Sachsen — a constitutional designation from 1920 reclaimed in 1990.
Sachsen is the only German state where the same party has won every Landtag election since 1990.
Current: CDU 40 seats, AfD 40 seats, BSW 15 seats, SPD 10 seats, Greens 7 seats, Linke 6 seats, Freie Wähler 1 seat. 120 seats total. Majority: 61.
CDU-SPD minority government — 50 seats, 11 short of majority. Kretschmer elected Ministerpräsident in second ballot on 18 December 2024 with 69 votes — Linke voted for him to prevent a constitutional crisis.
Next regular election: 2029.

## Yearbook
**2029** — Next regular Landtag election.

**2024** — 1 September. CDU 31.9%. AfD **30.6%**. BSW 11.8% — first entry. SPD 7.3%. Greens 5.1% — barely above threshold. Linke 4.5% — below threshold but saved by two Leipzig direct mandates (Grundmandatsklausel). Freie Wähler 1 direct mandate. Turnout 74.4%. BSW broke off Brombeer (CDU-BSW-SPD) negotiations in November over the Friedensformel. CDU and SPD formed minority government — 50 seats. Kretschmer failed first investiture ballot (55 votes, needed 61 absolute majority), won second (69 votes — Linke supported him to avoid Neuwahl deadline). Kabinett Kretschmer III.

**2019** — 1 September. CDU 32.1%. AfD 27.5% — surged from 9.7%. Linke 10.4%. Greens 8.6%. SPD 7.7%. FDP 4.5% — outside. NPD 0.0% — finally out after fifteen years. Turnout 66.5%. CDU-Greens-SPD Kenia coalition. Kretschmer second term.

**2014** — 31 August. CDU 39.4%. Linke 18.9%. SPD 12.4%. AfD 9.7% — first entry. Greens 5.7%. FDP 3.8% — outside. NPD 4.95% — barely outside after ten years. BVB/FW 5.7%. Turnout 49.2% — historic low. CDU-SPD coalition. Tillich third term.

**2009** — 30 August. CDU 40.2%. Linke 20.6%. SPD 10.4%. FDP 10.0% — strong result. Greens 6.4%. NPD 5.6% — second term. Turnout 52.2%. CDU-FDP coalition. Tillich second term.

**2004** — 19 September. CDU 41.1%. Linke/PDS 23.6%. SPD **9.8%** — first time any party in Sachsen fell below 10%. FDP 5.9%. NPD **9.2%** — first entry, almost as large as the SPD. Greens 5.1%. Turnout 59.6%. CDU-SPD coalition — absolute majority ends after fourteen years. Milbradt. The NPD's near-parity with the SPD in Sachsen was a national shock.

**1999** — 19 September. CDU **56.9%** — third absolute majority. PDS 22.2%. SPD 10.7%. Turnout 61.1%. CDU governs alone. Biedenkopf third term.

**1994** — 11 September. CDU **58.1%** — best CDU result ever at any German state election. PDS 16.5%. SPD 16.6%. Turnout 58.4%. CDU absolute majority. Biedenkopf second term. CDU won all 60 direct constituencies for the second consecutive time.

**1990** — 14 October. First Landtag election. CDU **53.8%** — absolute majority. SPD 19.1%. PDS-Linke Liste 10.2%. Bündnis 90 5.6%. FDP 5.3%. Turnout 72.8%. Kurt Biedenkopf Ministerpräsident — came from the West as CDU Spitzenkandidat. CDU governs alone. The SPD in Sachsen starts as a permanent minority and never escapes it.
