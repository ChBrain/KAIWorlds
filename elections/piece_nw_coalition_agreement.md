## Load Bearing
Without it CDU has no governing partner — 76 seats, majority 98.
Without it the first Schwarz-Grün coalition in NRW history has no programme.
Without it six Bundesrat seats have no unified position.

## Apparent
Title: *Zukunftsvertrag für Nordrhein-Westfalen*. Signed 23 June 2022.
CDU and Greens. First Schwarz-Grün coalition in NRW's seventy-six-year history.
The SPD-FDP coalition had been the natural NRW formation for decades. The CDU had governed with FDP as recently as 2017–2022. The Greens had governed with SPD 2010–2017. CDU and Greens had never governed together.
115 of 195 seats. Wüst elected Ministerpräsident 28 June 2022 with 106 votes.
The SPD held a formal offer open to form an alternative coalition with Greens and FDP — an Ampel in NRW — but the SPD's 26.7% gave them no credibility as the leading party of such a coalition. The Greens chose CDU.

## Yearbook
June 2022. Coalition signed. CDU and Greens divide the ministries. Greens take Environment, Climate, Agriculture, and Building as their core domains.
The coal transition is the structural challenge that shapes the whole coalition — NRW is the last operating coal state. The federal Kohleausstieg (coal exit) by 2038, potentially 2030, affects NRW more than any other state. The Greens pushed 2030. The CDU resisted. The Staatskanzlei manages the tension.
2022–2026. Coalition governs with a stable majority. The AfD opposition with 12 seats — a presence in the chamber, concentrated in Ruhrgebiet constituencies, limited legislative influence.
Current polls (April 2026): CDU 35%, SPD 20%, AfD 15%, Greens 13%. The coalition parties poll above their 2022 combined result. Next election: spring 2027.
