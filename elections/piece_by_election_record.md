# Piece: Election Record Bayern

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Place:** [Maximilianeum München](place_by_maximilianeum.md)

## Load Bearing
Without it the CSU's position has no depth — it is not merely dominant, it has been dominant for seventy years while the political landscape around it transformed entirely.
Without it the SPD's collapse from 35% to 8% has no arc.
Without it the 1954 Viererkoalition — all parties except the CSU governing together — has no echo in 2023.
Remove it and Bavaria looks like a one-party state by accident. With it, it looks like a one-party state by sustained construction.

## Apparent
A state that rebuilt its democratic institutions under American occupation in 1945.
The CSU has governed Bavaria since 1966 without interruption. Before that, coalition instability. After that, structural dominance.
203 seats in the current Landtag. Majority: 102.
CSU-Freie Wähler coalition: 122 seats. Markus Söder Minister-President.
The Bundesrat delegation: 6 seats — the largest of any state except NRW.

## Yearbook
**2023** — 8 October. CSU 37.0% — historically weak but still dominant. Freie Wähler 15.8% — best ever. AfD 14.6% — best ever in Bayern. Greens 14.4%. SPD **8.4%** — worst ever in Bayern. FDP 3.0% — outside. Turnout 73.3%. CSU-Freie Wähler coalition renewed. Söder re-elected Minister-President 31 October with 120 to 76 votes. AfD becomes new opposition leader in the Maximilianeum. All Ampel parties lost votes — federal government punished in state election. Freie Wähler benefited from Aiwanger antisemitic flugblatt affair — controversy increased rather than damaged their support.

**2028** — Next regular Landtag election. Due by October 2028.

**2018** — 14 October. CSU 37.2% — then their worst result since 1950. Freie Wähler 11.6%. Greens 17.6% — best ever. SPD 9.7%. AfD 10.2% — first entry into Bavarian Landtag. FDP 5.1%. Turnout 72.4%. CSU loses absolute majority. Forms coalition with Freie Wähler for first time. Söder becomes Minister-President, replacing Seehofer who left for Berlin as federal Interior Minister.

**2013** — 15 September. CSU **47.7%** — absolute majority of seats restored. SPD 20.6%. Greens 8.6%. FDP 3.3% — outside. Turnout 63.6%. CSU governs alone again. Seehofer Minister-President.

**2008** — 28 September. CSU **43.4%** but loses absolute majority of seats for the first time since 1966. SPD 18.6%. Greens 9.4%. FDP 8.0%. Freie Wähler 10.2% — first entry into Landtag. AfD does not yet exist. Turnout 57.9%. CSU-FDP coalition. Beckstein briefly Minister-President then Seehofer.

**2003** — 21 September. CSU **60.7%** — absolute majority of votes, largest result since 1974. SPD 19.6%. Greens 7.7%. FDP outside. Turnout 57.1%. Edmund Stoiber governs alone. Stoiber had run for Bundeskanzler against Schröder in 2002 and nearly won — 38.5% vs 38.5%, decided by 6,000 votes in eastern Germany. He returned to Bayern and won this landslide.

**1998** — 13 September. CSU 52.9%. SPD 28.7%. Greens 5.7%. FDP outside. Turnout 69.8%. Stoiber governs alone. SPD nearly broke 30% — their high-water mark of the late Stoiber era.

**1994** — 25 September. CSU 52.8%. SPD 30.0%. Greens 6.1%. FDP outside. Turnout 67.7%. Stoiber begins his tenure as Minister-President, succeeding Streibl who resigned due to the Amigo affair — accepting gifts from businessman Schörghuber.

**1990** — 14 October. CSU 54.9%. SPD 26.0%. Greens 6.4%. FDP 5.2%. Turnout 65.9%. Max Streibl Minister-President. First post-reunification election. Bavaria is now a border state no longer — the Iron Curtain that ran along its eastern edge with Czechoslovakia has dissolved.

**1986** — 12 October. CSU 55.8%. SPD 27.5%. Greens 7.5%. FDP 3.8% — outside. Turnout 69.8%. Strauß governs alone — his last election, he died in office October 1988.

**1982** — 10 October. CSU **58.3%**. SPD 31.9%. Greens 4.6% — outside. FDP 3.5% — outside. Turnout 74.0%. Strauß absolute majority. The Greens do not yet enter the Landtag.

**1978** — 15 October. CSU **59.1%**. SPD 31.4%. Turnout 74.1%. Strauß governs alone. The CSU's structural peak approaching.

**1974** — 27 October. CSU **62.1%** — the highest result ever for the CSU, the highest ever for CDU/CSU in any German state election. SPD 30.2%. Turnout 75.3%. Alfons Goppel governs alone.

**1970** — 22 November. CSU 56.4%. SPD 33.3%. Turnout 70.0%. Goppel governs alone. The CSU begins its long ascent past 50%.

**1966** — 20 November. CSU 48.1% — regains absolute majority. SPD 35.8% — their best ever result in Bayern. Turnout 77.3%. Goppel governs alone for the first time. The era of coalition instability ends. From this point until 2008 the CSU governs Bayern without a coalition partner.

**1962** — 25 November. CSU 47.5%. SPD 35.3%. BP 5.1%. Turnout 75.5%. CDU minority government, tolerated.

**1958** — 23 November. CSU 45.6%. SPD 30.8%. BP 8.1%. FDP 5.6%. Turnout 79.8%. CSU-SPD-FDP coalition. The BP (Bayernpartei) still present, declining.

**1954** — 28 November. CSU 38.0% — their weakest postwar result. SPD 28.1%. BP 13.2%. BHE 10.2%. FDP 7.2%. Turnout 82.5%. The **Viererkoalition**: SPD, BP, BHE, FDP form a government together, explicitly excluding the CSU despite it being the largest party. Wilhelm Hoegner (SPD) becomes Minister-President. The only time in postwar Bavarian history that a non-CSU government governed. It lasts three years.

**1950** — 26 November. CSU 27.4% — their lowest ever postwar result. SPD 28.0% — the only election in which the SPD outscored the CSU. BHE 17.9% — the refugees' party, enormous in Bavaria which absorbed millions of Heimatvertriebene from Bohemia and Silesia. BP 12.3%. Turnout 77.0%. Coalition chaos. CSU eventually leads a government.

**1946** — 1 December. CSU 52.3%. SPD 28.6%. WAV 7.4% (Wirtschaftlicher Aufbau-Vereinigung). FDP 5.7%. Turnout 75.7%. Hans Ehard (CSU) Minister-President. The Bavarian constitution is adopted by referendum on the same day. The CSU has an absolute majority from its first free election. It will hold that majority — with one three-year interruption — for the next seventy-seven years.
