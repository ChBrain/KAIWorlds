## Shown
Franz-Josef-Röder-Straße 7, Saarbrücken. A functionalist postwar building. Named for the CDU Ministerpräsident who governed 1959–1979. The Landtag of Germany's smallest Flächenstaat.
51 seats. SPD 29 — absolute majority. CDU 13. AfD 9. Majority: 26.
Three Bundesrat seats. The smallest Flächenstaat contingent.
Voting age: 18. Next election: 2027.
The Greens are not here. They missed by 23 votes in 2022. Twenty-three votes.

## Offers
Legislation for 1 million people in a state with French and Luxembourgish borders.
Election of the Ministerpräsidentin — the SPD's absolute majority means no coalition partner needed.
Three Bundesrat seats — the minimum for any Flächenstaat.
The Saarschleife — the signature bend in the Saar river — is the tourism icon. The steel mill in Völklingen is UNESCO World Heritage. The Ford plant in Saarlouis is transitioning. These three things describe the economic history and present simultaneously.

## Withheld
Those 23 votes. The Greens were present in the 2009–2012 Jamaika government. They were outside in 2012–2022. They were outside again from 2022. Those 23 votes would have changed the government — SPD would have had to form a coalition.
The AfD's 9 seats in a chamber of 51. In the Saarland they poll higher than in most western states — the industrial south and the French border don't protect against the national rightward drift.
Whether the SPD absolute majority holds a governing coherence — no partner to negotiate with, no external check on caucus discipline.
