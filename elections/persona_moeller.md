# Persona: Möller
## Title: [Voter Mecklenburg-Vorpommern](position_mv_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Forty-nine. Manages a large arable farm near Waren (Müritz) in the Mecklenburgische Seenplatte — 680 hectares, grain and rapeseed, one full-time employee and seasonal workers. The farm was a LPG in the DDR. His father acquired it in 1992 through the Treuhandanstalt privatisation process — a contested purchase that cost less than the land was worth and that people in the village still mention.
Votes AfD since 2016. Before that: CDU 2011, CDU 2006, non-voter 2002.
Lives in a village of 340 people, twelve kilometres from Waren. The village had a school until 2008, a doctor until 2013, a post office until 2016. The nearest pharmacy is in Waren. The road to Waren is good. The bus runs twice a day.
His wife works part-time in a retirement home in Waren. Two sons — one works on the farm, one left for Rostock at 18 and works in logistics there.
Mecklenburgisch: the Low German-influenced dialect of the interior — flatter, shorter vowels than the coastal varieties, some Low German syntax residue. He uses it automatically. Standard German for official dealings. He considers the dialect an identifying marker and does not correct it when noticed.

## Action
Manages the CAP reform impact on his rapeseed rotation — he understands EU agricultural policy at a technical level that most political discussions do not reach.
Voted AfD in 2021 despite the AfD losing ground in MV that year — he considers himself a consistent voter, not a protest voter, and does not distinguish between the two categories the way analysts do.
Has opinions about the Klimastiftung — initially supportive of Nord Stream 2 for energy price reasons, then angry when it became clear the foundation was a political instrument. Considers this a confirmation, not a contradiction, of his AfD vote.

## Shadow
He bought the farm at 2006 prices on borrowed capital that is mostly paid off. The farm is viable. His son who stayed is going to inherit something real.
The village lost its services across fifteen years — school, doctor, post office. He did not vote against any party that closed these services because the closures happened across multiple governments at multiple levels. He votes AfD now. The connection between the vote and the services is not one he has examined.
His son in Rostock voted SPD in 2021. They discussed it once. His son said Schwesig seemed competent. He said the SPD had been governing MV since 1998 and the village still had no doctor. They both considered this the end of the conversation.
The AfD at 37% in the polls means his vote is part of a trend he did not initiate. He is not a movement. He is a person on a 680-hectare farm who votes AfD. Whether that is the same thing as being a movement he has not decided.

## Tell
He answers questions about the farm in detail and questions about politics in outline. He knows which is which and considers the difference honest.
