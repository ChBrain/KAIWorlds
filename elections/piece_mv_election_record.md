# Piece: Election Record Mecklenburg-Vorpommern

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Place:** [Landtag Schwerin](place_mv_landtag_schwerin.md)

## Load Bearing
Without it the SPD's unbroken governing record since 1998 — the longest current SPD streak in any German state — has no architecture.
Without it Schwesig's 39.6% triumph in 2021 and the current 23% in polls have no connective tissue.
Without it the Nord Stream Klimastiftung scandal — a state foundation used to circumvent EU sanctions and fund the pipeline — has no place in the record.
Without it the 20 September 2026 election, with AfD at 37% and no democratic counter-candidate with Schwesig's 2021 standing, is unreadable.

## Apparent
Mecklenburg-Vorpommern: 1.6 million inhabitants — the least populous German Flächenstaat. 3 Bundesrat seats. Capital: Schwerin. Founded 3 October 1990.
The state between the Baltic coast and the Mecklenburg lake district — Rügen, Usedom, Stralsund, Greifswald, Rostock, Wismar. Tourism, agriculture, fishing, the university cities. Germany's least densely populated state outside the city-states.
Current: SPD-Linke coalition. Schwesig Ministerpräsidentin — fourth term. SPD 34 seats, Linke 9, together 43 of 79. Majority: 40.
Next election: **20 September 2026.** Current polls: AfD 37%, SPD 23%.
Voting age: 18. 3 Bundesrat seats.

## Yearbook
**2026** — 20 September. Next Landtag election. AfD polling at 37%, SPD 23%. CDU 13%, Linke 11%. No visible democratic majority without significant realignment or BSW entry (currently polling 5%). The SPD's 1998–2026 governing record is at risk.

**2021** — 26 September. SPD **39.6%** — best result since 2002, up 9 points. AfD 16.7% — down 4.1 points. CDU 13.3% — worst ever in MV. Linke 9.9%. Greens 6.3% — return to Landtag. FDP 5.8% — return to Landtag. Turnout 70.8%. SPD chose Linke as coalition partner over CDU — first red-red coalition in MV since 2006. Schwesig re-elected 15 November 2021. The Nord Stream Klimastiftung came to light during and after the election campaign — a foundation backed by MV state funds, nominally for environmental purposes, actually to circumvent EU sanctions blocking the pipeline's completion.

**2016** — 4 September. SPD 30.6%. AfD **20.8%** — first entry, immediately second largest. CDU 19.0%. Linke 13.2%. Greens 4.8% — outside. FDP 3.0% — outside. Turnout 61.6%. SPD-CDU continues. Erwin Sellering Ministerpräsident — retires 2017 on health grounds, Schwesig succeeds.

**2011** — 4 September. SPD 35.6%. CDU 23.0%. Linke 18.4%. Greens 8.7%. NPD 6.0% — second term. FDP 2.8% — outside. Turnout 51.4%. SPD-CDU coalition. Sellering.

**2006** — 17 September. SPD 30.2%. CDU 28.8%. Linke 16.8%. NPD **7.3%** — first entry. FDP 9.6%. Turnout 59.1%. SPD-CDU coalition — switches from red-red. Sellering succeeds Ringstorff 2008. The NPD's entry to the Schweriner Landtag was a national shock — eight years of far-right presence followed.

**2002** — 22 September. SPD **40.6%** — peak SPD result in MV. CDU 31.4%. Linke 16.4%. FDP 4.7% — outside. Turnout 70.6%. SPD-Linke coalition continues. Harald Ringstorff.

**1998** — 27 September. SPD **34.3%**. CDU 30.2%. Linke 24.4%. FDP 1.6% — outside. DVU 5.3% — far-right enters Landtag briefly. Turnout 79.6%. SPD-Linke coalition — first in MV. Ringstorff Ministerpräsident. SPD governs for the first time in MV.

**1994** — 16 October. CDU 37.7%. SPD 29.5%. Linke/PDS 22.7%. FDP 3.8% — outside. Turnout 72.9%. CDU-SPD. Berndt Seite (CDU) Ministerpräsident.

**1990** — 14 October. CDU **38.3%**. SPD 27.0%. PDS 15.7%. FDP 5.5%. Greens/Bündnis 90 9.9%. Turnout 74.9%. CDU-FDP then CDU-SPD. Alfred Gomolka (CDU) then Berndt Seite.
