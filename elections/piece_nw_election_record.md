# Piece: Election Record Nordrhein-Westfalen

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Place:** [Landtag Düsseldorf](place_nw_landtag_duesseldorf.md)

## Load Bearing
Without it the SPD's claim to NRW as heartland has no ground — the CDU governed from 1947 to 1966 before the SPD first won.
Without it the 39 years of SPD government 1966–2005 have no scale — the longest unbroken run for any party in any German state.
Without it the 2022 result has no shock value — the SPD at 26.7% in a state where it once scored 50%.
Without it the six Bundesrat seats have no political history — who sends them, and from what tradition.
Remove it and NRW looks like a CDU state that sometimes votes SPD. With it, it looks like a state where the question of who governs is the most consequential single question in German federal politics.

## Apparent
Nordrhein-Westfalen: 18 million inhabitants — the most populous German state. 6 Bundesrat seats — the maximum. Landtag in Düsseldorf.
Founded 23 August 1946 by British military ordinance. The merger of the Prussian Rhine Province and the Province of Westphalia, plus the state of Lippe (1947).
Three political geographies: the Ruhrgebiet — Dortmund, Essen, Bochum, Duisburg, Gelsenkirchen, Herne, former coal and steel — historically SPD. The Rhine cities — Düsseldorf, Cologne, Bonn — mixed, increasingly CDU and Green. Münsterland and the Rhineland countryside — CDU.
Current: CDU 76 seats, SPD 56, Greens 39, FDP 12, AfD 12. CDU-Green coalition. Hendrik Wüst Ministerpräsident. Majority: 98 of 195.
Next regular election: spring 2027.

## Yearbook
**2027** — Next regular Landtag election. Due spring 2027.

**2022** — 15 May. CDU **35.7%**. SPD 26.7% — worst SPD result ever in NRW. Greens 18.2% — best ever. FDP 5.9%. AfD 5.4%. Turnout **55.5%** — historic low. CDU-Green coalition: first Schwarz-Grün in NRW history. Wüst elected Ministerpräsident with 106 of 181 votes on 28 June. The CDU won despite fewer absolute votes than 2017 — the turnout collapse hurt SPD far more than CDU. 310,000 former SPD voters went to Nichtwähler.

**2017** — 14 May. CDU 33.0%. SPD 31.2%. FDP 12.6%. Greens 6.4%. AfD 7.4% — first entry. Turnout 65.2%. CDU-FDP coalition. Laschet Ministerpräsident. SPD loses power after seven years. Laschet wins the CDU Kanzlerkandidatur in 2021 and leaves NRW — Wüst becomes Ministerpräsident October 2021.

**2012** — 13 May. SPD 39.1%. CDU **26.3%** — worst CDU result ever in NRW. Greens 11.3%. FDP 8.6%. Pirates 7.8%. Turnout 59.6%. SPD-Green coalition. Hannelore Kraft Ministerpräsidentin — first woman to lead NRW. The Landtag dissolved itself in 2012 after a minority SPD-Green government lost a budget vote.

**2010** — 9 May. CDU 34.6%. SPD 34.5% — separated by 0.1 percentage points. Greens 12.1%. FDP 6.7%. Turnout 59.3%. SPD-Green minority government. Hannelore Kraft. The CDU and FDP together held no majority despite being larger combined — SPD and Greens formed a minority that then won a confidence vote.

**2005** — 22 May. CDU 44.8%. SPD 37.1%. Greens 6.2%. FDP 6.2%. Turnout 63.0%. CDU-FDP coalition. Jürgen Rüttgers Ministerpräsident — ends 39 years of SPD government in NRW. The SPD's worst result in NRW to that point.

**2000** — 14 May. SPD 42.8%. CDU 37.0%. Greens 7.1%. FDP 9.8%. Turnout 56.7%. SPD-Greens. Wolfgang Clement then Peer Steinbrück Ministerpräsidenten.

**1995** — 14 May. SPD 46.0%. CDU 37.7%. Greens 10.0%. FDP 4.0% — outside. Turnout 64.0%. SPD absolute majority ends — SPD-Greens. Johannes Rau then Wolfgang Clement.

**1990** — 13 May. SPD 50.0% — first time any party clears 50% in NRW. CDU 36.7%. Greens 5.0%. Turnout 71.8%. SPD absolute majority. Johannes Rau. The reunification election year — NRW votes in May, before the Wall questions have fully resolved.

**1985** — 12 May. SPD 52.1% — SPD peak in NRW. CDU 36.5%. Greens 4.6% — outside. Turnout 74.6%. SPD absolute majority. Rau.

**1980** — 11 May. SPD 48.4%. CDU 43.2%. FDP 4.98% — barely outside. Greens 3.0%. Turnout 77.0%. SPD governs alone. Rau begins his long tenure. The FDP falls below 5% for the first time.

**1975** — 4 May. SPD 45.1%. CDU 47.1% — CDU is larger but SPD-FDP coalition continues. FDP 6.7%. Turnout 82.3%. Heinz Kühn continues. The SPD governs NRW with FDP despite CDU being nominally larger.

**1970** — 14 June. SPD 46.1%. CDU 46.4% — CDU fractionally larger, SPD-FDP coalition continues. FDP 5.5%. Turnout 83.0%. Kühn. The tightest result in NRW history. CDU larger by 0.3 points, SPD governs.

**1966** — 10 July. SPD **49.5%** — narrowly misses absolute majority. CDU 42.8%. FDP 7.4%. Turnout 76.5%. SPD becomes stärkste Partei for the first time. Heinz Kühn Ministerpräsident. The end of nineteen years of CDU government. The SPD will govern NRW for the next 39 years. The trigger: the Kohlekrise — the coal crisis that began in 1959 with closures and job losses in the Ruhrgebiet, the core SPD territory.

**1962** — 8 July. CDU 46.4%. SPD 43.3%. FDP 6.9%. Turnout 73.0%. CDU-FDP coalition. Franz Meyers Ministerpräsident.

**1958** — 6 July. CDU **50.5%** — absolute majority. SPD 39.2%. FDP 7.1%. Turnout 79.5%. Meyers. CDU absolute majority — their only one in NRW history.

**1954** — 27 June. CDU 41.3%. SPD 34.5%. FDP 11.5%. Zentrum 4.2%. KPD 4.0%. Turnout 79.5%. CDU-FDP-Zentrum. Karl Arnold continues.

**1950** — 18 June. CDU 36.9%. SPD 32.3%. FDP 12.1%. Zentrum 8.3%. KPD 5.5%. Turnout 72.6%. CDU-Zentrum coalition. Arnold.

**1947** — 20 April. First Landtag election. CDU 37.5%. SPD 32.0%. KPD 14.0%. Zentrum 9.8%. FDP 5.9%. Turnout not fully recorded — large numbers of soldiers not yet home. Karl Arnold (CDU) Ministerpräsident — all-party coalition including KPD. "Wir sind das soziale Gewissen Deutschlands" — we are the social conscience of Germany. The state is eight months old. The Ruhrgebiet is rubble and coal dust. The Zeche Prinz Regent is still operating. The closure comes in 1960.
