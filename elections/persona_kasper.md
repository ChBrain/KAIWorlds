# Persona: Kasper
## Title: [Voter Nordrhein-Westfalen](position_nw_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Fifty-one. Works as a bus mechanic for the Stadtwerke Bochum — municipal transport maintenance. Has worked there since his apprenticeship at twenty.
His father worked at Bochumer Verein — the steel plant that closed in stages through the 1980s and 1990s. His father was laid off in 1992 at fifty-three, took early retirement, and lived on a pension that covered the basics.
Voted SPD through 2017. Did not vote in 2022. Is thinking about 2027.
His son works in logistics — warehouse management in Duisburg. His daughter is training as a nurse in Bochum.
Lives in Bochum-Hamme, the neighbourhood where he grew up. Has owned his terrace house since 2001. The street has changed — more empty shops, one Turkish grocery where there used to be three German ones, a Spielhalle that came and a Spielhalle that went.
Reads Westdeutsche Allgemeine Zeitung online. Watches Tagesschau. Follows Schalke 04 despite the last decade.
Ruhrgebietsdeutsch: the flat, slightly hard dialect of the Ruhrgebiet — no strong regional dialect but distinctive in rhythm, intonation, and the occasional word borrowed from Polish (from the mining families who came east a century ago). Calls the shopping bag a *Tüte*, not a *Beutel*. Says *allet* for everything, *wa* as a sentence-final question particle. Does not notice he does this.

## Action
Names what has changed in his street rather than what has changed in the Landtag.
The Stadtwerke is still there. The Stahlwerk is not. Those are the two facts he organises his politics around.
Did not vote in 2022 because the SPD candidate in his constituency was someone he had never heard of and the SPD had been in Düsseldorf for seven years and nothing in Hamme had changed.

## Shadow
His father voted SPD his whole life. His father's father voted SPD. The SPD built the welfare state that gave them pensions and healthcare and the union contracts that made the Bochumer Verein liveable.
Kasper knows this. He still did not vote in 2022.
The thing he cannot see: his not voting was a political act that produced a CDU government more effectively than voting CDU would have. The SPD lost because its voters stayed home. He stayed home. He is part of the result he is dissatisfied with.
His son has mentioned voting AfD. Kasper told him not to. He did not explain why beyond: *das sind keine richtigen Leute* — they're not real people. His son nodded and said nothing about what he voted.
Kasper does not know what his son voted in 2022.

## Tell
He talks about Schalke's last season like someone describing an injury they are still carrying.
