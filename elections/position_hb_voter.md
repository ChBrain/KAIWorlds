# Position: Voter Bremen

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election.md)

## Has
Two votes — Erststimme and Zweitstimme. Voting age: 16.
One vote in the federal election. One vote in the European election.
The [election record](piece_hb_election_record.md) as the accumulated history.
Two distinct experiences depending on which city: the Bremen voter and the Bremerhaven voter cast ballots in the same state election with the same parties, but their political environments are different enough that the same party can represent different things in each city.
The knowledge that the AfD was absent in 2023 — their votes went to BiW. In 2027 the AfD returns. The BiW voter faces a choice.

## Orders
Vote or don't.
In 2023: 57.0% voted — down from 64.1% in 2019. The turnout decline hurt the parties of the left more than the right. The position that stayed home in 2023 is part of the current coalition's precarious poll numbers.
Voting age 16: younger voters can vote in state elections. Bremen's political culture has been more willing to experiment with electoral structures — the split Wahlbereich system, the 16-year voting threshold — than most German states.

## Loses
The certainty of SPD governance.
For 80 years the Bürgermeister has been SPD. Current polls show CDU ahead. The position cannot take the SPD's tenure as a natural condition — the 2019 election showed it is contingent, and the 2027 election will show it again.
The BiW voter who found a right-populist home in 2023 without the AfD. In 2027 that voter has the AfD available again. Whether they return or stay with BiW is a question about what the vote was actually expressing.
The Bremerhaven voter whose city is one of the most economically challenged in Germany, whose vote is counted in a separate Wahlbereich, and whose political priorities are consistently less well-served by the Bremen-centric coalition that governs both.

## Drives
On the loss of SPD as permanent —
votes in a city that has had an SPD Bürgermeister since 1945 and whose current polls show CDU ahead.
The position in Sachsenhausen and the position in Bremerhaven-Lehe cast ballots in the same election and inhabit different Bremens.
The position votes for its own Bremen. It knows the other position voted too. The sum of both is the state they share.
