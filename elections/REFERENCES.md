# References

## Electoral Law - Federal

Bundeswahlgesetz (BWahlG) — the law governing Bundestagswahlen.
The 2023 electoral reform introduced a hard cap of 630 seats
and removed the guarantee that winning a direct constituency produces a seat
if the party's Zweitstimme total does not support it.
23 direct winners did not enter the Bundestag in 2025 as a result.

https://www.bundeswahlbeauftragter.de/

Bundeswahlleiter — official results, full historical data.
https://www.bundeswahlleiterin.de/

## Electoral Law - State

Each of the sixteen states has its own Landeswahlgesetz.
State elections operate independently of the federal cycle.
Results produce Landtag seats and trigger coalition formation.
The coalition formed appoints the Bundesrat delegation.
The state election and its federal consequence arrive in the same result.

---

## The Bundesrat

Bundesrat — the federal chamber representing the sixteen states.
69 votes distributed by population (3-6 per state).
Delegations must vote as a bloc. Abstentions are possible.
The Bundesrat can delay or block federal legislation.

https://www.bundesrat.de/

---

## The European Parliament

European Parliament elections take place every five years across all EU member states.
Germany elects 96 MEPs — the largest national delegation.
No constituency system. National party lists. Proportional.
No threshold in European elections for Germany.
The Tierschutzpartei received 1.4% in 2024 and holds one seat.

https://www.europarl.europa.eu/
