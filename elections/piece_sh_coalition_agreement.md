## Place
[Staatskanzlei Kiel](place_sh_staatskanzlei_kiel.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it the government has no programme.
Without it the opposition has no measure.
Without it the voter has nothing to hold the coalition against.
Remove it and the five years of governing are ungoverned — activity without commitment.

## Apparent
A bound document. Several hundred pages.
Signed by the leaders of two parties that previously governed in opposition to each other.
Divided into chapters: economy, climate, education, infrastructure, interior.
Each chapter contains targets. Timelines. Named responsibilities.
Some targets are specific. Some are directional. Some are aspirational.
The distinction between these categories is not made explicit in the document.
Dated June 2022.

## Yearbook
June 2022. The CDU and the Greens sit down. The previous coalition — CDU, Greens, FDP — ends because the FDP declined to continue. The new coalition is the CDU's choice, not the Greens'.
The agreement is negotiated over several weeks. Compromises are made in both directions.
The Greens accept constraints on climate targets they would not have accepted in a stronger position.
The CDU accepts environmental commitments it would not have initiated alone.
The document is signed. The Minister-President presents it to the Landeshaus.
From this day forward the agreement is two things simultaneously: a programme to govern by, and a record to be judged against.
Every year the distance between the two grows — not because either party governs badly, but because governing always produces the gap between what was written and what the world requires.
By 2027 the agreement will be history. The question it leaves is: what closed, and what didn't, and who decided.
