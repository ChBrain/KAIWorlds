# Position: Voter Nordrhein-Westfalen

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election.md)

## Has
Two votes on Landtag polling day — Erststimme for a constituency candidate, Zweitstimme for a party list.
128 of the 181 minimum seats come from direct constituencies — a higher proportion than most states.
Minimum voting age: 18.
One vote in the federal election. One vote in the European election.
The [election record](piece_nw_election_record.md) as the accumulated history.
Geography as political identity: a vote in Gelsenkirchen-Mitte means something different from a vote in Düsseldorf-Mitte. The same ballot, the same parties, different weight of experience behind the choice.

## Orders
Vote or don't.
In 2022: the turnout collapsed to 55.5%. 310,000 former SPD voters went to Nichtwähler rather than another party. The CDU won because its voters came out and SPD voters stayed home. The position's choice not to vote was as decisive as the choice to vote CDU.

## Loses
The assumption that NRW produces SPD governments.
From 1966 to 2005 the SPD governed. That is the living memory of anyone over 40 in the Ruhrgebiet. The assumption that NRW belongs to the SPD survived longer than the reality — SPD voters went home rather than to another party, because the alternative was unacceptable, not because they changed their view.
The Ruhrgebiet voter who voted SPD in 1985 at 52% and whose children vote AfD in 2022 at 7% in Gelsenkirchen. The same constituency, different answers. The same loss underneath.

## Drives
On the loss of the SPD as reliable governing force —
votes in the knowledge that 310,000 former SPD voters went home in 2022 rather than choosing another party.
The position that stays and votes is already doing something the majority of its political cohort did not do.
That act — the continued vote — is the minimum form of civic presence. The position holds it.
