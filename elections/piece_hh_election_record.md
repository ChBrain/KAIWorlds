## Apparent
Date: 2 March 2025. Eight days after the federal Bundestag election.
Turnout: 67.7% — up from 63% in 2020.
SPD: 33.5% — clear winner, down from 39.2% in 2020 but still dominant.
CDU: 19.8% — strongest gains of any party, up from 11.2%.
Greens: 18.5% — down from 24.2%. Behind CDU for the first time in years.
Linke: 11.2% — best ever result in Hamburg.
AfD: 7.5% — up from 5.3%, but far below their 20.8% in the federal election one week earlier.
FDP: 2.3% — failed to enter the Bürgerschaft.
BSW: 1.8% — failed to enter the Bürgerschaft.
Volt: 3.2% — failed to enter the Bürgerschaft.
121 seats. Majority: 61.
SPD + Greens: 70 seats. Coalition renewed.
Ten votes per voter. Complex ballot. Most voters used it as a simple party endorsement.
Tschentscher elected Erster Bürgermeister for a third term on 7 May 2025.

## Yearbook
**2025** — 2 March. Eight days after the federal election. SPD 33.5%, CDU 19.8%, Greens 18.5%, Linke 11.2%, AfD 7.5%. Turnout 67.7%. SPD-Green coalition renewed. Tschentscher elected for third term. AfD 13 points below their federal result one week earlier.

**2020** — 23 February. SPD 39.2%, Greens 24.2%, CDU 11.2% — historic low. AfD 5.3% — barely in. FDP 4.97% — barely out. Turnout 63%. SPD-Green coalition formed. Tschentscher's first full term as Erster Bürgermeister.

**2015** — 15 February. SPD 45.6%, CDU 15.9%, Greens 12.3%, FDP 7.4%, AfD 6.1% — first entry into Bürgerschaft. Linke 8.5%. Turnout 63%. SPD-Greens. Olaf Scholz continues as Erster Bürgermeister.

**2011** — 20 February. SPD 48.4%, CDU 21.9%, Greens 11.2%, Pirates 6.4%, FDP 6.7%. Turnout 57.3%. SPD-Greens. Scholz becomes Erster Bürgermeister. CDU era ends after a decade.

**2008** — 24 February. CDU 42.6%, SPD 34.1%, Greens 9.6%, Linke 6.4%, FDP 4.8%. Turnout 63.5%. CDU-SPD grand coalition. Von Beust continues.

**2004** — 29 February. CDU 47.2%, SPD 30.5%, Greens 12.3%. Turnout 68.7%. CDU-Greens. Ole von Beust Erster Bürgermeister — unusual CDU-Green coalition.

**2001** — 23 September. SPD 36.5%, CDU 26.2%, **Schill 19.4%**, Greens 8.6%. Turnout 71.4%. Populist Ronald Schill's party enters Bürgerschaft with 19.4% — unprecedented. CDU-Schill-FDP coalition. Von Beust becomes Erster Bürgermeister. SPD loses Hamburg for the first time since 1957.

**1997** — 21 September. SPD 36.2%, CDU 30.7%, Greens 13.9%, DVU 4.98%. Turnout 74.1%. SPD-Greens. Henning Voscherau continues, then Ortwin Runde.

**1946-1993** — SPD dominated throughout. Near-total majority in 1946. Gradual narrowing through the 1970s and 1980s. The Greens enter in 1982. The Statt-Partei protest vote in 1993 (5.6%) signals the first cracks in SPD dominance.
