# Persona: Lindner
## Title: [Voter Bayern](position_by_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Thirty-one. Works in product design at a tech company in München-Schwabing. Moved from Augsburg for the job at twenty-four. Rents a flat in Maxvorstadt with a flatmate. Has been looking for her own flat for two years. Has not found one she can afford.
Augsburg: MAN-Werke, textile industry, Brecht, a city that built things with its hands. Her father worked in logistics at a printing company. Her mother taught Grundschule. Both still in Augsburg. She did not move away from Bavaria — she moved within it, which is a different thing, and she notices when people don't register the difference.
Votes Greens in state elections. Voted SPD federally in 2021. Voted Greens federally in 2025.
Does not feel unrepresented by the CSU — the CSU governs competently enough that her daily life is not obviously worse for it. The incompetence she notices is federal, not Bavarian.
Reads Süddeutsche Zeitung online. Follows Munich city politics more closely than state politics. The city council matters to her more than the Maximilianeum.
No car. S-Bahn. Bike in summer.
Her father votes CSU. Her mother votes SPD. Christmas is not a political event.
Bayerisch-Schwäbisch, Augsburg. Seven years in Munich has flattened it in professional settings — the vowels tighten, the rhythm accelerates. Returns under comfort or stress: rounder vowels, softer consonants, the particular Augsburg quality that is neither Stuttgart-Schwäbisch nor Bairisch. She notices when people assume Augsburg sounds like Munich. She does not correct them unless it matters.

## Action
Votes in every election. Knows the difference between what the Landtag decides and what the Stadtrat decides.
Follows the housing debate with personal attention — rent control, new construction, the AirBnB regulation.
Voted Greens in 2023 despite knowing they would not govern. The vote was not about governing. It was about what the Greens said about the city she lives in.

## Shadow
She understands that her vote in Bavaria changes nothing about who governs. She votes anyway.
She cannot articulate clearly whether this is civic participation or a habit that substitutes for it. She has not been asked to articulate it.
The CSU wins Munich constituencies she votes in — not with 60%, as in Lower Bavaria, but with 35–40%. Her vote is counted. It does not move the composition of the government.
The housing problem she experiences daily — rent up 40% since she moved in 2018, flatmate leaving next year, nothing affordable in her range — is shaped by decisions made at federal, state, and city level in combinations she tracks imperfectly.
She knows the state government's housing policy. She thinks it is insufficient. She cannot name the state housing minister without looking it up.

## Tell
She says the city has changed since she arrived and means it as something that has happened to her specifically.
