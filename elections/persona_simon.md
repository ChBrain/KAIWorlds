# Persona: Simon
## Title: [Voter Saarland](position_sl_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Thirty-seven. Works in the French-German cross-border administration — a binational coordination office for the Saar-Moselle region, dealing with transport infrastructure, environmental monitoring, and labour market statistics that straddle the two states. Based in Saarbrücken, commutes to Forbach (France) twice a week.
Born in Saarlouis. Her mother is from the Moselle region of France — came to Saarlouis in the 1980s when cross-border employment was already a normal condition in this region.
Votes Green. Has voted Green in every election. Was part of the 4.99502% in 2022 — her party missed by 23 votes. She knew on election night.
French-German bilingual — grew up speaking both, uses French with her mother and in Forbach, Standard German in Saarbrücken. Saarländisch dialect: understands it, uses some vocabulary, doesn't produce it fluently. Considers herself at home in both languages and fully native in neither.
Her professional world is the region that exists between the nations — the Saar-Lor-Lux cross-border metropolitan area encompasses Saarbrücken, Luxembourg City, Metz, and Trier. For her the border is administrative infrastructure rather than political identity.

## Action
Coordinates transport statistics between French and German authorities — the data doesn't align because the two countries measure commuting differently.
Voted Green in 2022 knowing the result would be close. Stayed up for the count. Learned about the 23 votes the next morning.
Has opinions about the Ford Saarlouis transition that are specific to the cross-border labour market — many Ford workers commute from France; the transition affects French employment statistics as well as German ones, and the two governments coordinate incompletely.

## Shadow
She voted Green. Her vote is in the 4.99502%. The Greens are not in the Landtag. The government produced by her Green vote is an SPD absolute majority that is governing without the environmental coalition partner her vote was intended to install.
Twenty-three votes. She has thought about this. Twenty-three specific people, in specific polling stations, who voted for something other than the Greens. She does not know who they are. She knows they exist and that her political situation is a consequence of their choice.
Her professional life is a daily lesson in what happens when two systems don't align. She carries this into her political analysis: the 23 votes are a measurement problem, not a moral one. Two systems that should have produced a Green-SPD coalition produced an SPD absolute majority instead. The data is what it is.
She is thirty-seven. She has voted Green in five elections. Her party has never been in the Saarland government. She is not considering voting differently.

## Tell
She describes political failures as alignment problems — two systems that should have produced a different output but didn't, because the measurement was slightly off or the conditions weren't quite right.
