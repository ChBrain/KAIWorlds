## Owner

## Shown
Hegelstraße 42, Magdeburg. A postwar building in the Altstadt. The executive seat of the Ministerpräsident.
Haseloff governs from here — last months of his tenure. He announced he will not stand in 2026. The office is occupied by a Ministerpräsident in the final act of a long tenure, knowing the state he leaves is not the state he found.
Four Bundesrat seats coordinated from here.
Sachsen-Anhalt receives net from the Länderfinanzausgleich. The chemical industry corridor — BASF in Schwarzheide, the Bitterfeld-Wolfen chemical park — is the economic backbone. The Harz has tourism. The universities in Halle and Magdeburg are the knowledge economy. The flat agricultural north is sparsely populated and economically marginal.

## Offers
Executive governance of a state with 2.2 million people and a demographic trajectory that is one of the steepest in Germany — the birth rate is low, emigration to the west has continued across three decades, the remaining population is older.
Four Bundesrat votes — aligned with CDU federal positions, cautious on energy and climate, assertive on fiscal equalisation.
The Lutherjahr calendar — Wittenberg, Lutherstadt Eisleben, Quedlinburg are UNESCO heritage sites that generate tourism and diplomatic visits. Cultural soft power from a state that is otherwise economically peripheral.

## Withheld
The successor question. Haseloff was the only CDU politician in Sachsen-Anhalt with approval ratings that exceeded his party's vote share. Without him the 37% of 2021 has no personal foundation. The CDU at 25% in current polls is the CDU without Haseloff.
What happens in October 2026 when a new Ministerpräsident governs from this room. Whether that person is CDU. Whether a CDU candidate can be elected without AfD votes if no democratic majority exists.
The seven CDU investiture abstentions in 2021 — they are still in the CDU caucus.
