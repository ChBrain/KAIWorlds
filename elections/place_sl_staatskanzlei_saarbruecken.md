# Place: Staatskanzlei Saarland Saarbrücken
## Runs: [the governing process](process_governing.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Shown
Am Ludwigsplatz 14, Saarbrücken. The Staatskanzlei in the city centre, near the Saar river.
Rehlinger governs from here — SPD absolute majority, no coalition. Three Bundesrat seats coordinated from here.
Saarland is a net recipient from the Länderfinanzausgleich — a small state with structural economic challenges receives more than it pays in.
The French border is fifteen minutes from this building. The Moselle region of France, Luxembourg, the Saarland, and the western Rhineland-Palatinate form a cross-border labour market where workers commute across three countries. This shapes the Staatskanzlei's employment policy in ways that no other German state faces identically.

## Offers
Executive governance of Germany's smallest Flächenstaat — 1 million people, 2,571 square kilometres.
Three Bundesrat votes — the minimum allocation. SPD-aligned on social and economic policy.
The structural transformation portfolio: Ford Saarlouis is phasing out combustion engine production; the replacement investment — an EV battery plant — is being negotiated. Saarstahl (steel) is transitioning to hydrogen-based production. Both transitions are being managed from this building.

## Withheld
Whether the Ford transition produces the promised jobs or produces a smaller, different workforce in a different factory.
The cross-border workers — French citizens who work in Saarland, Saarland citizens who work in France and Luxembourg — whose interests sit in multiple administrative systems and no single political chamber.
What it means to govern a state of 1 million as an absolute majority. No opposition to negotiate with that has constitutional weight. No coalition partner to constrain decisions. The accountability is entirely electoral.
