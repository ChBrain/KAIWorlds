## Runs: [the governing process](process_governing.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Shown
Heinrich-Mann-Allee 107, Potsdam. A postwar building in the southern district. The working seat of the Ministerpräsident and the Brandenburg state government.
Woidke governs from here — fourth term, smallest coalition, one seat above majority.
The four Bundesrat seats are coordinated from here. Brandenburg votes with the SPD-BSW coalition position.
Brandenburg receives net from the Länderfinanzausgleich — a smaller state with structural gaps, dependent on federal transfers. The Staatskanzlei negotiates the terms.
The coal transition: the Lausitz in southeast Brandenburg is the last German lignite mining region. The Kohleausstieg by 2038, possibly accelerated — this is the largest structural challenge the Staatskanzlei manages.

## Offers
Executive governance of 2.5 million people across a state with extreme regional variation — Potsdam booming, the Lausitz emptying.
Four Bundesrat seats — Brandenburg votes as a bloc with the SPD-BSW coalition position. On coal transition, infrastructure, migration: positions shaped by a state that is simultaneously losing population in the east and gaining it near Berlin.
The Lausitz structural fund negotiations — billions in federal and EU transition money that the Staatskanzlei manages for the mining region.

## Withheld
Whether the SPD-BSW coalition holds to 2029. The coalition has one seat above majority. A single defection from BSW and the budget fails.
The AfD's 30 seats as permanent veto threat on constitutional matters.
What Woidke conceded to BSW — on Friedenspolitik, on migration, on the coal timeline — that is in the agreement but not announced.
