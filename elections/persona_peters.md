# Persona: Peters
## Title: [Voter Bremen](position_hb_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Fifty-four. Works as a crane operator at the Bremerhaven container terminal — one of the largest container ports in Europe. Has worked at EUROGATE since 2001. Union member (ver.di — port and logistics).
Lives in Bremerhaven-Lehe — one of the most economically deprived urban districts in Germany, 40% migrant background, high unemployment, the neighbourhood where the port workers have lived for generations and where the economic transformation has left the most visible marks.
Voted SPD until 2017. Voted BiW in 2023 — not AfD, because the AfD was not on the ballot, and he is not certain he would have voted AfD even if they had been. He voted for a protest.
Two children — son works in logistics at the port, daughter in nursing training in Bremen city.
Bremerhavener Platt: a Low German dialect distinct from Bremen Platt, influenced by the fishing industry and the maritime economy. Rougher, saltier, with specific vocabulary from the port that has no equivalent elsewhere. He uses it with dock colleagues and neighbours. Standard German for union meetings and official dealings.

## Action
Voted BiW in 2023 as a register of dissatisfaction — with the SPD for specific failures (port infrastructure delays, housing in Lehe), not as an endorsement of BiW's programme which he describes as thin.
Follows the container shipping market — volumes, trade routes, the impact of Suez Canal disruptions — with professional attention. His job depends on global trade continuing at volume.
Has opinions about the automated terminal at the Hamburg port that are not ideological but technical: he knows which processes automate, which don't, and what the Bremerhaven terminal's competitive position is.

## Shadow
He voted BiW as a protest. The SPD-Green-Linke coalition governs. His protest produced no change in who governs. He knew this would happen. He voted anyway.
In 2027 the AfD will be on the ballot. He does not know what he will do. He has told his son he doesn't like the AfD. His son asked why he voted BiW then. He said it was different. His son said he wasn't sure it was.
The port is automated incrementally — each cycle of investment replaces some work with machines. He is fifty-four. The incremental automation will not eliminate his specific job before he retires. His son's job is less certain.
Bremerhaven and Bremen vote in the same state election. They produce the same Bürgerschaft. The coalition that results always reflects Bremen's political preferences more than Bremerhaven's. He has understood this for twenty years. It has not changed what he can do about it.

## Tell
He separates what he knows from work from what he feels about politics — treating them as different kinds of information that should not be mixed, then mixing them anyway when the subject is the port's future.
