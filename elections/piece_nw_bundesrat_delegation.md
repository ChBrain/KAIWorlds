## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Place
[Bundesrat Berlin](place_bundesrat_berlin.md).
And simultaneously: [Staatskanzlei Düsseldorf](place_nw_staatskanzlei_duesseldorf.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it 18 million people — more than the entire population of the Netherlands — have no voice in federal legislation.
Without it NRW's structural interests — coal transition, Duisburg port, Ruhrgebiet infrastructure, Rhine waterways — have no formal presence in the chamber that shapes the laws governing them.
Without it the most consequential six votes in the Bundesrat are uncast. NRW and Bayern together hold 12 of 69 seats — more than enough to form a blocking minority on consent legislation if they align.

## Apparent
Six seats. The maximum. Shared with Bayern only.
Appointed from the CDU-Greens cabinet. Cast as a bloc.
The delegation votes on federal legislation that shapes NRW more directly than any other state — the Kohleausstieg law, infrastructure investment, asylum policy, fiscal equalisation.
The Bundesrat sits in Berlin. The delegation travels. NRW has its own permanent representation in Berlin — the Landesvertretung — to manage the continuous presence.
Six votes from a state that has 18 million people but votes with one voice. The bloc discipline holds even when the CDU and Greens disagree in Düsseldorf.

## Yearbook
June 2022. CDU-Greens cabinet appointed. Bundesrat delegation follows.
The delegation has voted on the federal Kohleausstieg implementation, on infrastructure packages, on asylum reform. The CDU position and the Greens position on some of these bills differ — the bloc vote is the outcome of internal coalition negotiation, not external visibility.
The six seats swing federal votes when the Bundesrat is closely divided. NRW under SPD governments voted consistently for left-majority positions. Under CDU governments since 2017, the alignment shifted — but the Greens as coalition partner introduce a moderating element that makes NRW's position unpredictable on climate and social legislation.
Next delegation: appointed from whatever government forms after the spring 2027 election.
