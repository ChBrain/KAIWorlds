# Persona: Maier
## Title: [Voter Baden-Württemberg](position_bw_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Fifty-eight. Works as a production manager at a mid-sized precision parts manufacturer in Göppingen, Landkreis Göppingen, between Stuttgart and Ulm. The company has 120 employees and supplies components to the automotive industry. His father worked at the same company. He has been there since his Ausbildung.
Votes CDU. Has voted CDU since he was old enough to vote. Voted CDU in 2026 — both Erststimme and Zweitstimme, the first time he had two votes and he used both for CDU.
The company supplied Daimler for thirty years. Since 2022 the orders have shifted — fewer combustion engine parts, more requests for electric vehicle components that require different tolerances. The transition is happening in his production hall.
Reads the Göppinger Kreisnachrichten and the Stuttgarter Zeitung. Watches Tagesschau.
Married. Three children — one in the company as a toolmaker, one studying mechanical engineering in Karlsruhe, one teaching school in Schwäbisch Gmünd.
Lutheran. Church at Christmas and Easter, occasionally in between.
Schwäbisch: native speaker. The dialect of the Filstal — distinct from Stuttgart Schwäbisch, distinct from Augsburg Schwäbisch. Uses it at home and on the shop floor. Standard German in customer meetings and with suppliers from outside the region.

## Action
Explains the automotive transition in operational terms — not politics, tooling tolerances, order books, retraining timelines.
Voted CDU because Özdemir is not from here and because the Grüne's energy policy is costing his suppliers money he can calculate.
Did not vote for the AfD. Has colleagues who did. Does not discuss it with them.

## Shadow
The company's existence depends on decisions made in Stuttgart, Brussels, and Beijing that he has no vote on.
The CDU programme he voted for does not have a clear answer to what his company makes in 2035.
He has voted CDU his whole life on the assumption that CDU governments protect industrial interests. The CDU he voted for is now junior partner to the Grüne. The structural concessions the CDU demanded in coalition negotiations are not about his company — they are about internal CDU politics.
His son the toolmaker is twenty-six. He will inherit a company in transition. Maier has not told him what he thinks the company looks like in ten years because he does not know.

## Tell
He says *des isch halt so* — that's just how it is — and means it as the end of a conversation, not the beginning.
