# Piece: Bundesrat Record

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Place:** [Bundesrat Berlin](place_bundesrat_berlin.md)

## Load Bearing
Without it the 69 state votes and their distribution have no institutional history.
Without it the Bundesrat's role as the chamber that forced the Ampel to negotiate on migration, energy, and fiscal legislation has no record.
Without it the shift from SPD-led majority (2021–2025) to CDU-led majority (2025 onwards) has no visibility at the federal level.

## Apparent
69 seats distributed by state population: Bayern 6, NRW 6, Baden-Württemberg 6, Niedersachsen 6, Hessen 5, Sachsen 4, Rheinland-Pfalz 4, Berlin 4, Schleswig-Holstein 4, Brandenburg 4, Sachsen-Anhalt 4, Thüringen 4, Hamburg 3, Mecklenburg-Vorpommern 3, Saarland 3, Bremen 3.
Simple majority: 35. Two-thirds majority: 46 (for constitutional amendments and certain federal laws).
Current composition (April 2026): CDU/CSU-led states hold the majority following recent state elections. SPD-led states (NI, MV, BB, SL, HB, HH) hold approximately 22 votes.
The Bundesrat does not dissolve — it is a permanent body. State governments are appointed after elections; their delegation changes accordingly. A Bundesrat without SPD-led states would still exist with CDU-led delegations from every Land.

## Apparent composition by state government (April 2026)
CDU/CSU-led: BY (CSU alone), BW (Grün-Schwarz — CDU junior), HE (CDU-SPD), SH (CDU alone), ST (CDU-SPD-FDP — transitioning after Sep 2026 election), NW (CDU-Greens), SN (CDU-SPD minority), TH (CDU-BSW-SPD Brombeer)
SPD-led: NI (SPD-Greens), MV (SPD-Linke), BB (SPD-BSW), SL (SPD alone), HB (SPD-Greens-Linke), HH (SPD-Greens)
RLP: CDU-SPD coalition forming — transitioning from SPD-Ampel to CDU-SPD, Bundesrat votes shifting direction.
BE: CDU-SPD coalition.

## Yearbook
2021–2025. SPD-led Ampel in Berlin. Bundesrat increasingly CDU-led at state level — friction between federal government and Bundesrat on migration, energy, and fiscal legislation drove compromise in the Ampel's final years.
February 2025. Bundestagswahl. CDU/CSU wins. Bundesrat now aligned with federal government direction — CDU-led majority at both levels for first time since 2013.
2025 onwards. CDU-SPD federal GroKo and CDU-dominant Bundesrat. Consent legislation passes more easily. Opposition to federal legislation now requires coordination within the SPD-led states — a minority bloc.
Critical Bundesrat decisions upcoming: state pension reform consent, defence infrastructure (Sondervermögen), energy transition legislation.
