## Shown
Lennéstraße 1, Schwerin. On the Schweriner See — the parliament building directly overlooks the lake and faces the Schweriner Schloss, the 19th-century castle on its own island. A new parliament building opened 2000 after the state's first decade in a converted building.
79 seats currently. SPD 34, AfD 14, CDU 13, Linke 9, Greens 5, FDP 4. Majority: 40.
SPD-Linke hold 43 — working majority but narrow. Three Bundesrat seats.
The Schloss across the water is the seat of the Landesregierung — the parliament and the government face each other across the lake.
Voting age: 18.
Next election: 20 September 2026 — the Landtag that assembles after that vote may look unrecognisable from this one.

## Offers
Legislation for 1.6 million people — the least populous German Flächenstaat.
Election of the Ministerpräsidentin.
Three Bundesrat seats in Berlin.
The Baltic coast economy: tourism (Rügen, Usedom, the Hansestädte), agriculture, fishing, port logistics at Rostock, the universities in Greifswald and Rostock.

## Withheld
The Nord Stream Klimastiftung — a state-backed foundation nominally for climate purposes, actually used to circumvent EU sanctions on Nord Stream 2 construction. Schwesig established it. Its legacy shapes MV's political reputation beyond the state.
The AfD's 14 seats — their presence since 2016, their decline in 2021, their current polling at 37%. The trajectory since the chamber was built has gone in one direction.
What the chamber looks like after 20 September 2026. If AfD wins 37% there is no visible democratic majority. The SPD at 23% cannot govern without partners who may not have enough seats.
