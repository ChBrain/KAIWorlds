## Shown
Bernhard-von-Lindenau-Platz 1, Dresden. On the Elbe bank, beside the Zwinger and the Semperoper. A modernist building opened 1994, designed to contrast deliberately with the baroque city around it. Glass and concrete in a city of sandstone.
120 seats. Majority: 61. CDU 40, AfD 40, BSW 15, SPD 10, Greens 7, Linke 6, Freie Wähler 1.
CDU and AfD hold exactly the same number of seats. The minority CDU-SPD government has 50 — eleven short of majority. Every vote is a negotiation.
The AfD's 40 seats give it more than a third — Sperrminorität on constitutional decisions. Combined with CDU's refusal to govern with the AfD, the chamber is structurally ungovernable by any democratic majority without the AfD's passive tolerance.
Voting age: 18. Four Bundesrat seats.
Sachsen is the only German state in which the CDU has won every election since 1990. This building has never seen a government without a CDU Ministerpräsident.

## Offers
Legislation for 4 million people — education, police, culture, industrial policy for a state rebuilding its economy across three decades.
Election of the Ministerpräsident — in December 2024 the most complicated investiture vote in German state politics since Thüringen 2020.
Four Bundesrat seats. Sachsen votes with the CDU-SPD minority position — which requires assembling majorities case by case.
The view from the public gallery: CDU and AfD sitting as mirror images across the chamber. The same number of seats. Different worlds.

## Withheld
The Linke's six votes that elected Kretschmer in the second ballot to prevent a constitutional Neuwahl crisis. What the Linke required or received in exchange. Whether this was a precedent.
The AfD's 40 seats as permanent veto — no constitutional change is possible without either CDU accepting AfD votes or AfD abstaining.
What it means to govern Sachsen as a minority from a chamber where the largest opposition force is classified as gesichert rechtsextrem by the Verfassungsschutz.
Dresden's particular weight within Sachsen — the city that gave Germany Pegida in 2014, that voted AfD at 25% in 2024 while Leipzig voted them at 18%.
