# Position: Voter Sachsen-Anhalt

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election.md)

## Has
Two votes — Erststimme and Zweitstimme. Voting age: 18.
One vote in the federal election. One vote in the European election.
The [election record](piece_st_election_record.md) as the accumulated history.
The knowledge that the next election is in September 2026 — five months away. The polls show AfD at 38%. The position has not yet voted in that election. What it will do is the live question.
In 2021: many voted CDU not from conviction but to prevent AfD first place. Haseloff mobilised non-voters and cross-party voters for a single-election purpose. That purpose ends in September 2026 — Haseloff is not standing.

## Orders
Vote or don't.
In September 2026: the position faces the same structural question as 2021 but without Haseloff as the answer. The CDU candidate is unknown. The AfD is 13 points ahead in polls. The Woidke gambit — stake everything on personal credibility — has no equivalent in Sachsen-Anhalt.

## Loses
The reliable anchor.
The SPD voter of 1998 (35.9%) and the SPD voter of 2021 (8.4%) cast the same type of vote with radically different effects. The erosion was not an event — it was a direction.
The voter who mobilised for Haseloff in 2021 and has no equivalent reason to mobilise in 2026. The polls show where that voter went — or did not go.
In the Altmark, Mansfeld-Südharz, Burgenlandkreis — AfD above 40% at the 2024 Europawahl. These are the territories where the vote moved furthest and earliest.

## Drives
On the loss of Haseloff as the anchor —
votes in September 2026 without the personal credibility that in 2021 moved the result from 26% to 37%.
The position that mobilised for Haseloff specifically must now decide whether to mobilise for the CDU generically.
The ones who do hold the vote as a principle. The ones who don't become the gap between the 2021 result and whatever comes next.
