## Shown
Platz des Landtags 1, Düsseldorf. On the Rhine bank, north of the Altstadt. Built 1988. A low, circular building — the Plenarsaal visible from outside through glass, facing the river.
195 seats currently. Majority: 98. CDU 76, SPD 56, Greens 39, FDP 12, AfD 12.
The most consequential state parliament in Germany for federal politics. NRW's 6 Bundesrat seats are the maximum. Every federal coalition needs to account for what NRW sends to Berlin.
The SPD governed from this building for 39 years — 1966 to 2005. The CDU returned in 2005. The SPD returned from 2010 to 2017. The CDU returned in 2017. The swing seat of German federal politics.
128 of the 181 minimum seats come from direct constituencies — a higher proportion than most German states. The Ruhrgebiet returns SPD direct candidates even in SPD bad years.
Voting age: 18.
