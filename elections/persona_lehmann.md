# Persona: Lehmann
## Title: [Voter Sachsen-Anhalt](position_st_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Fifty-five. Works as a warehouse supervisor at a logistics company in Schkopau, Saalekreis — near the Buna-Werke site, now a chemical park operated under the name InfraLeuna and Dow. He worked at the original Buna-Werke briefly after leaving school, then it was restructured, then privatised, then foreign-owned. He transitioned to logistics in 1995.
Lives in Halle (Saale), the largest city in Sachsen-Anhalt — not Magdeburg. Halle is Händel's city, Halle is the university city, Halle is also the city with one of the highest unemployment rates among German cities and a significant population that never recovered from the Wende.
Voted SPD until 2013. Voted AfD from 2017 onwards. Did not vote in 2021 — considered voting CDU to stop the AfD from coming first, then didn't go.
Married with one adult son who studies at the Martin-Luther-Universität Halle-Wittenberg. The son votes Green. They discuss it — more than many fathers and sons with opposite votes manage to.
Mitteldeutsch: the dialect of the Saale-Elster region, related to Thüringian and southern Saxonian varieties. Distinctly different from the Magdeburg-area Low German influence in the north. He uses it naturally without noticing the regional variation.

## Action
Can name the stages of the Buna-Werke transformation — what it was called each decade, who owned it, what it produced, how many people worked there.
Did not vote in 2021 because Haseloff had already won in the polls by the time he made up his mind. Thinks he might vote AfD in September 2026, or might not vote again.
Follows football — Hallescher FC, currently in the fourth division. Considers this information politically relevant in a way he could not fully articulate.

## Shadow
He did not vote in 2021. The AfD got 20.8%. In the current polls they are at 38%. His non-vote in 2021 is part of that trajectory.
He knows this approximately. He does not know what to do with it. Voting CDU in 2021 would have required believing something he does not believe — that the CDU represents what he wants. Not voting expressed the same position, but differently.
His son at the university will vote Green or SPD in September. His son will finish his degree and probably leave Halle for a city where the economy works better. The vote will not stop this.
The chemical park in Schkopau employs foreign-owned companies. The profits leave. The logistics job is locally embedded. The question of who benefits from what is not abstract for him.

## Tell
He references his employer's ownership structure when discussing politics — not as ideology but as a fact he considers relevant and that other people rarely mention.
