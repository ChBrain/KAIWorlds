# Piece: Coalition Agreement Mecklenburg-Vorpommern 2021

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Place
[Staatskanzlei Schwerin](place_mv_staatskanzlei_schwerin.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it the SPD has no governing partner — 34 seats, majority 40.
Without it the switch from SPD-CDU (2006–2021) to SPD-Linke has no record — a deliberate realignment away from the party that had been junior partner for fifteen years.

## Apparent
SPD and Linke. Signed 13 November 2021. Red-red coalition — the second in MV history, the first since 2006.
43 of 79 seats — narrow majority, three above threshold.
CDU was arithmetically available but Schwesig chose the Linke after eight rounds of negotiation. The CDU's historic low of 13.3% may have made them a less attractive anchor.
The Nord Stream Klimastiftung was established before the election — it is not in the coalition agreement. It became a political liability for the coalition from 2022 onwards when Russia invaded Ukraine and the foundation's purpose became indefensible internationally.

## Yearbook
October–November 2021. Schwesig conducts parallel soundings with CDU, Linke, and Ampel options.
13 October 2021. Schwesig announces SPD will enter talks with Linke.
5 November 2021. Coalition agreement concluded.
13 November 2021. Coalition agreement signed.
15 November 2021. Schwesig re-elected with 41 votes, 35 against, 3 abstentions.
February 2022. Russia invades Ukraine. Nord Stream 2 project dead. The Klimastiftung's purpose is exposed. International pressure on MV grows.
2022. Klimastiftung wound up under pressure. Schwesig accepts political responsibility but does not resign.
Current polls (April 2026): AfD 37%, SPD 23%. The coalition's combined share would be approximately 34% — below majority. The 2021 majority of 43 seats is not replicable on current polling.
