# Position: Ministerpräsident Nordrhein-Westfalen

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election.md)
- **Process:** [the governing process](process_governing.md)
- **Place:** [Staatskanzlei Düsseldorf](place_nw_staatskanzlei_duesseldorf.md)

## Has
The mandate from the [Landtag Düsseldorf](place_nw_landtag_duesseldorf.md) — 106 of 195 votes on election.
A coalition with the Greens — the first Schwarz-Grün in NRW history.
Six Bundesrat seats — the maximum, shared only with Bayern.
18 million people. The largest state population in Germany. More people than the Netherlands, Belgium, or Sweden.
The Ruhrgebiet: the industrial heartland that is not what it was. Duisburg, Essen, Dortmund, Bochum, Gelsenkirchen. The coal that is leaving. The jobs that left before the coal.
The Bundesverkehrswegeplan 2030 — NRW has more entries than any other state. The Rhine corridor, the A1, the A2, the A3. Every German truck uses at least one of these roads.

## Orders
Govern three political geographies simultaneously — the Ruhrgebiet, the Rhine cities, Münsterland and the Rhine countryside — with a coalition that reflects none of them perfectly.
Hold six Bundesrat seats aligned. Coordinate with the federal government on the Kohleausstieg — a timeline that affects NRW employment more directly than any other state.
Manage the SPD's revival or prevent it. The 2027 election is the test.
Not become Kanzlerkandidat — Laschet tried, failed, left. The position has a history of consuming politicians who look beyond it.

## Loses
The national ambition.
The Ministerpräsident of NRW is the most powerful state leader in Germany by most measures — the largest population, the most Bundesrat seats, the most consequential economy.
The position is not the federal government. Armin Laschet left it for Berlin and lost both. Johannes Rau left it for Berlin and became a modest Bundespräsident. The pattern: the position is large enough to sustain a career, not large enough to become the next thing.
Whoever holds this position in 2027 will be asked: are you running for Chancellor?

## Drives
On the loss of the national ambition —
governs NRW as if it were the centre of German politics. Which it is, in several specific senses.
Uses the six Bundesrat seats as leverage in federal negotiations.
Makes the coal transition a national story — NRW's structural challenge becomes a federal policy test that the Ministerpräsident shapes.
