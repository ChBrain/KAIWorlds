# Persona: Wagner
## Title: [Voter Thüringen](position_th_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Thirty-seven. Works in the Erfurt city administration — planning department. Has worked there since her degree in urban planning from Bauhaus-Universität Weimar.
Born in Erfurt. Stayed. Most of her university cohort left for Leipzig, Berlin, Munich.
Votes Linke. Voted Linke in every election since she was eighteen. State, federal, European.
Knows more about how the Brombeer coalition was formed than most people in her building. Followed the negotiations. Followed the Pflichtenheft discussions. Followed the investiture vote.
Rents in Erfurt's Löbervorstadt. Cycles to work. No car.
Reads the Thüringer Allgemeine and Zeit Online. Listens to Deutschlandfunk on the way to the office.
Her parents both worked — her father in city administration (she works in the same building he once did, without having planned it), her mother as a teacher. Born 1987, two years before the Wall fell.
Mittelthüringisch with Erfurt urban overlay — not strong dialect, but present in vowel quality and sentence rhythm. The register of someone who grew up in the city, went to university in Weimar, came back. Educated Standard German in the office. The dialect returns at speed or under pressure. She is aware of it. She does not suppress it.

## Political Memory
She does not remember the GDR. She has its shape from her parents and from the city she grew up in — the Plattenbau districts to the north, the medieval centre that was preserved because the GDR did not have money to modernise it, the Stasi prison on Andreasstraße which is now a memorial. She has been to it on school trips three times.
Her parents watched West German television. Her mother sometimes talks about what it was like to know the other world existed on the other side of the screen. Her father does not talk about it at all.
She knows that on 4 December 1989 women from *Frauen für Veränderung* occupied the Stasi building 800 metres from the flat she grew up in — the first occupation of a Stasi headquarters in Germany. She knows it because she read about it. Not because her parents told her. The plaque on the building she passed every day for years before she looked at it.
Her political memory of the GDR is inherited and curated — she has chosen what to carry. The memory of someone who lived it is different — lived and suppressed, carried without being able to put it down.

## Action
Explains the coalition mathematics to anyone who asks.
Specifically: why the Linke voted for Voigt despite everything, why the incompatibility resolution is a fiction, why the Sperrminorität is the AfD's real power.
She is not angry when she explains this. She is precise.

## Shadow
She understands the mechanism better than most of the people operating it.
What she cannot see is that her understanding is a substitute for power.
She explains why the Linke had to vote for Voigt. She did not vote for Voigt. The Linke did, with her party's votes, without asking her.
She understands why the Bundesrat delegation has four seats. She has never followed what those four seats voted on in Berlin.
The gap between her comprehension of the system and her influence within it is not visible to her because the comprehension feels like a form of participation.
She votes Linke. The Linke in Thüringen has 12 seats in a chamber of 88. It governs nothing. It shapes everything. She cannot evaluate whether this is enough.

## Tell
She draws the coalition mathematics on whatever surface is available — a napkin, her phone screen, the back of an envelope.
