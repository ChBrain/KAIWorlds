# Place: Staatskanzlei Kiel
## Where the state is governed between sessions.
## Runs: [the governing process](process_governing.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Shown
Düsternbrooker Weg 104, Kiel.
A building two streets from the Landeshaus, on the same waterfront strip.
The working house of the Minister-President and the state government.
Offices. Meeting rooms. The cabinet convenes here.
Civil servants at desks. Files moving between rooms.
No glass chamber facing the water. This building does not face outward.
Phones. Secure lines. The permanent machinery of government.
The Staatskanzlei does not close when the Landtag is not in session.
The Landeshaus is where the mandate is visible. This is where it is used.

## Offers
The coordination of state government across all departments.
Preparation of legislation for the Landeshaus.
The management of the Bundesrat delegation — scheduling, briefing, position papers.
The relationship with the federal government in Berlin.
The relationship with the EU via the state's Brussels office.
The room where the Minister-President actually governs.
