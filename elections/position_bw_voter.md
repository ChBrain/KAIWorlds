# Position: Voter Baden-Württemberg

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election.md)

## Has
From 2026: two votes — Erststimme for a constituency candidate, Zweitstimme for a party list. First time BW voters have had two votes. Every other German state had this already.
Minimum voting age: 16 from 2026. Previously 18.
One vote in the federal election. One vote in the European election.
The [election record](piece_bw_election_record.md) as the accumulated history.
The memory — generational in much of the state — of CDU dominance as the natural condition. The expectation, now broken, that the CDU governs BW.
For 16 and 17-year-olds voting for the first time in 2026: the first ballot, the new system, and a result so close that the winner was declared in the early hours of the morning.

## Orders
Vote or don't.
In 2026 for the first time: choose between Erststimme and Zweitstimme independently. The CDU won 56 of 70 direct constituencies. The Grüne won the party vote. The split produced 56 seats each — an outcome the old one-vote system would not have produced.

## Loses
The clarity.
Under the old one-vote system the result was the result. Under the new system the same voter can send contradictory signals — and did. CDU won the constituencies; Grüne won the lists.
The expectation that BW produces stable CDU governments has been broken since 2011. The expectation that BW produces stable Grüne governments has now been broken too.
The FDP voter of 2021 — 10.5% — became the FDP non-voter of 2026 — 4.4%. The FDP has not been outside the BW Landtag since the state was founded. That voter cast the same type of vote and produced the first-ever FDP exclusion.

## Drives
On the loss of certainty about who governs —
votes with the new two-vote system for the first time in 2026, experiencing the split result: CDU won the constituencies, Grüne won the lists, both got 56 seats.
The position that voted CDU with both votes and the position that split them produced the same chamber arithmetic.
The position learns what the vote actually does by watching the result disaggregate.
