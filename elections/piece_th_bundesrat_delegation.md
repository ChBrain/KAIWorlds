# Piece: Bundesrat Delegation Thüringen

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Place:** [Bundesrat Berlin](place_bundesrat_berlin.md)

## Place
[Bundesrat Berlin](place_bundesrat_berlin.md).
And simultaneously: [Staatskanzlei Erfurt](place_th_staatskanzlei_erfurt.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it Thüringen has no voice in federal legislation.
Without it 2.1 million people — including the 32.8% who voted AfD and have no representation here — have no formal presence in the chamber that shapes federal law.
Remove it and Thuringia is governed federally by others entirely.

## Apparent
Four seats. Appointed from the Brombeer coalition cabinet — CDU, BSW, SPD ministers.
Cast as a bloc. The coalition must agree on a position before the vote.
A coalition that cannot always agree internally must still vote as one bloc.
If the coalition splits on a federal bill, the four votes become an abstention.
An abstention counts as opposition in a chamber requiring 35 for a majority.
Four votes from a state whose government has no majority. The delegation is appointed by that government regardless.

## Yearbook
December 2024. Cabinet Voigt appointed. Bundesrat delegation follows. CDU, BSW, and SPD ministers represent Thüringen in Berlin.
The delegation votes on federal bills — migration law, infrastructure, energy. Each vote requires a prior agreement within a coalition that is itself dependent on Linke support for its own legislation.
The Sperrminorität the AfD holds in Erfurt has no equivalent in Berlin — the Bundesrat delegation represents the governing coalition only.
The 32 AfD members in the Thüringer Landtag have no seat in the Bundesrat. Their voters have no voice in Berlin until 2029 changes the government — or the government falls before then.
