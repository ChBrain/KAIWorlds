# Position: Ministerpräsident Bayern

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election.md)
- **Process:** [the governing process](process_governing.md)
- **Place:** [Bayerische Staatskanzlei München](place_by_staatskanzlei.md)

## Has
The mandate from the [Maximilianeum München](place_by_maximilianeum.md) — the largest state mandate in Germany by population outside NRW.
The coalition agreement with the Freie Wähler — a working majority of 122 of 203 seats.
Six Bundesrat seats in Berlin — Bavaria's weight in every federal bill.
The Landesvertretung network — offices in Washington, Brussels, Beijing, and six other cities.
The CSU party leadership — in Bavaria the Ministerpräsident and the CSU Landesvorsitzender are the same person. The position carries both mandates simultaneously.
The expectation, held by the CSU and much of the Bavarian electorate, that Bavaria is governed by the CSU. The CSU has held this position for all but three years since 1946.

## Orders
Govern the largest German state by area, second by economy.
Hold the coalition with the Freie Wähler — a partner that negotiates from a growing position and whose leader survived a national controversy to increase his vote share.
Cast six Bundesrat votes as a bloc — coordinate with the cabinet before each federal bill.
Manage the CSU's dual role: state party with absolute focus on Bavaria, and federal partner of the CDU with national ambitions the position sometimes pursues beyond the state border.

## Loses
The national mandate.
The position holds the largest state government in Germany. It is not the federal government. The gap between these two things has defined every occupant since Strauß.
Strauß ran for Chancellor in 1980. Lost. Stoiber ran in 2002. Lost by 6,000 votes. Söder sought the candidacy in 2021. Did not receive it. Each time the position returned to Bayern.
The federal ambition is not available to this position. Bavaria is too large to leave and too small to be the whole.

## Drives
On the loss of the national mandate —
turns Bayern into a sovereign actor within the federal system.
International offices. Constitutional court challenges to federal legislation. Bundesrat blocking minorities.
Makes the position as large as the federal system allows without becoming the federal government.
