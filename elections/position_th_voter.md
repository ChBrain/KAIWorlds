# Position: Voter Thüringen
## Two votes. Three elections. One state where the largest party cannot govern.

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election.md)

## Has
Two votes on Landtag polling day — Wahlkreisstimme for a constituency candidate, Landesstimme for a party list.
Minimum voting age: 18. Unlike Hamburg, Bremen, Schleswig-Holstein and others — no vote at 16 in Thüringen.
One vote in the federal election. One vote in the European election.
The [the election record](piece_th_election_record.md) as the accumulated history.
The knowledge — widely shared in Thüringen — that the vote they cast may produce a government that cannot govern.
No SSW equivalent. No minority exemption. Every party must clear 5%.

## Orders
Vote or don't.
In Thüringen the consequence of the vote is more visible than in most states — the mathematical impossibility of a stable majority has been public knowledge since at least 2019.

## Loses
The expectation that the vote produces a government.
In most states the election produces a coalition and the coalition governs.
In Thüringen the election produced a result in which the largest party is excluded from government, the government has no majority, and every piece of legislation requires a negotiation the coalition agreement didn't anticipate.
The voter voted. The vote landed. The consequence is a governing process unlike any other in Germany.
The 32.8% who voted AfD produced the largest faction in the chamber and zero representation in the government and zero seats in the Bundesrat.

## Drives
On the loss of the expectation —
votes on what they can see: Ramelow's tenure, the cost of living, the sense that the west still doesn't understand the east, the AfD as the available vehicle for registering that.
The governing consequence of the vote is managed by others in rooms the voter does not follow.
