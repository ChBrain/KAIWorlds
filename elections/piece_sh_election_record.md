## Place
[Landeshaus Kiel](place_sh_landeshaus_kiel.md).
Filed. Public. Permanent.

## Load Bearing
Without it the coalition has no mandate.
Without it the Bundesrat delegation has no legitimacy.
Without it the governing process has no beginning.
Remove it and the five years between elections have no ground.

## Apparent
A document and a set of numbers.
Printed in the Federal Gazette and on the Landeshaus wall.
CDU: 43.4%. The largest result in nearly forty years.
SPD: 16.0%. Their worst ever in this state.
Greens: 18.3%. Their best. Second place.
FDP: 6.4%. Inside the chamber, barely.
AfD: 4.4%. Outside the chamber. Below threshold for the first time.
SSW: 5.7%. The Danish minority party. Exempt from the threshold. Present.
Date: 8 May 2022.
Turnout: 60.4%.

## Yearbook
**2022** — 8 May. CDU 43.4%. SPD 16.0% — worst ever in SH. Greens 18.3% — best ever, second place. FDP 6.4%. AfD 4.4% — below threshold, first time. SSW 5.7% — best since 1947. Turnout 60.4%. CDU-Green coalition formed. Minister-President Günther re-elected. Bundesrat delegation changes.

**2017** — 7 May. CDU 32.0%. SPD 27.2%. Greens 12.9%. FDP 11.5%. AfD 5.9% — enters Landtag for first time. SSW 3.3%. Three-way Jamaica coalition: CDU, FDP, Greens. Günther becomes Minister-President for first time. Narrow result — governing without the SPD for the first time in years.

**2012** — 6 May. SPD 30.4%. CDU 30.1%. Greens 13.2%. FDP 8.2%. SSW 4.6%. Pirates 8.2% — new force. SPD-Greens-SSW coalition. Torsten Albig Minister-President.

**2009** — 27 September. CDU 31.5%. SPD 25.4%. FDP 14.9%. Greens 12.4%. SSW 4.3%. CDU-FDP coalition. Peter Harry Carstensen continues as Minister-President.

**2005** — 20 February. CDU 40.2%. SPD 38.7%. Greens 6.2%. FDP 6.6%. SSW 3.6%. Grand coalition CDU-SPD. Carstensen Minister-President.

**2000** — 27 February. SPD 43.1%. CDU 35.2%. FDP 7.6%. Greens 6.2%. SSW 4.1%. SPD-FDP coalition. Heide Simonis Minister-President — first female Minister-President in SH history.

**1996** — 24 March. SPD 39.8%. CDU 37.2%. FDP 5.7%. Greens 8.1%. SSW 2.5%. SPD minority government tolerated by SSW and Greens. Heide Simonis Minister-President. First minority government in SH history.

**1992** — 5 April. SPD 46.2%. CDU 33.8%. FDP 5.6%. Greens 4.97% — narrowly outside. SSW 1.9%. SPD-FDP coalition. Björn Engholm Minister-President. SPD at its postwar peak in SH — the Barschel dividend still running.

**1988** — 8 May. Snap election after Barschel's death. SPD **54.8%** — absolute majority. CDU 33.3%. FDP 4.4% — outside. Greens 2.9% — outside. Engholm Minister-President. The largest SPD result in SH history. The CDU destroyed. The scandal had done what thirty years of opposition could not.

**1987** — 13 September. CDU 42.6%. SPD 45.2% — SPD largest party. FDP 4.4% — barely in. SSW holds one seat. Result: exact parity. CDU+FDP: 37 seats. SPD+SSW: 37 seats. Barschel refuses to resign initially. Gives his *Ehrenwort* — word of honour — on live television that he knew nothing of the dirty tricks campaign. Dies in Geneva on 11 October 1987 under circumstances never fully clarified. Snap election follows.

**1983** — 13 March. CDU **49.0%** — absolute majority of mandates. SPD 43.7%. FDP 2.2% — outside. Greens 3.6% — outside. SSW present. Barschel consolidates CDU dominance. His best result. Four years later the scandal will end his career and his life.

**1979** — 29 April. CDU 48.0%. SPD 41.7%. FDP 5.9%. SSW present. Greens enter German politics nationally — in SH as *Grüne Liste Schleswig-Holstein* with 2.4%, outside the chamber. CDU governs alone. Stoltenberg continues.

**1975** — 13 April. CDU **51.9%** — absolute majority. SPD 41.0%. FDP 3.8% — outside. SSW present. SPD had broken 40% for the first time since 1947 — and still lost. CDU governed alone comfortably. Stoltenberg continues.

**1971** — 25 April. CDU 51.9%. SPD 38.0%. FDP 7.4%. SSW present. CDU absolute majority. Gerhard Stoltenberg becomes Minister-President — begins the long CDU era. Helmut Lemke steps down.

**1967** — 23 April. CDU 46.0%. SPD 35.6%. FDP 14.3%. SSW present. CDU-FDP coalition. Helmut Lemke Minister-President. The FDP at its high-water mark in SH.

**1962** — 23 September. CDU 45.0%. SPD 35.0%. FDP 9.0%. SSW present. BHE/expellees' parties fading. CDU minority government tolerated. Kai-Uwe von Hassel continues until January 1963 when he becomes federal Defence Minister. Helmut Lemke succeeds him.

**1958** — 28 September. CDU 43.5%. SPD 35.8%. FDP 6.4%. BHE/GB 4.4% — outside. SSW present. CDU-FDP coalition. Kai-Uwe von Hassel Minister-President from 1954, continues.

**1954** — 12 September. CDU 48.0%. SPD 36.0%. BHE (expellees) 14.5%. FDP/SHB 5.1% — barely in. SSW **3.5%** — falls below threshold for first time. Excluded from Landtag. SSW contested the 5% threshold as discriminatory. Federal Constitutional Court ruled the 7.5% threshold unconstitutional — threshold reduced to 5%. SSW still fell short. The minority exemption was restored in 1955 following the Bonn-Copenhagen Declarations. CDU-BHE-FDP coalition. Friedrich Wilhelm Lübke Minister-President, then Kai-Uwe von Hassel.

**1950** — 9 July. SPD 27.5%. CDU-FDP-DP Wahlblock 19.8% combined. BHE (expellees/refugees) **23.4%** — earthquake result. The state had absorbed nearly a million refugees after the war — they now voted as a bloc. CDU collapsed to 19.7%. Coalition chaos followed. Multiple failed investiture votes. Walter Bartram (CDU) eventually forms coalition with BHE, DP, FDP. SSW enters as SSW for the first time.

**1947** — 20 April. First democratic election in SH. British occupation zone. SPD 43.8% — absolute majority of seats despite 44% of votes, due to the electoral system heavily favouring the largest party. SPD won 34 of 40 constituency seats. CDU 34.0%. SSW as Südschleswigscher Verein (SSV) — the Danish minority's first political presence. Otto Selenka (SPD) then Bruno Diekmann Minister-President. The state is five months old. Half its population are refugees. The Landeshaus is not yet its home.

**2027** — Next regular Landtag election. Due by May 2027.
