# Piece: Election Record Rheinland-Pfalz

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Place:** [Landtag Mainz](place_rp_landtag_mainz.md)

## Load Bearing
Without it the SPD's 35-year governing record — 1991 to 2026 — has no context against the CDU's 45 years before that.
Without it the 2026 result — CDU reclaims first place after 35 years, AfD at 19.5% best in any western state, FDP out after two terms in government at 2.1% — has no trajectory.
Without it the Ampel coalition that RLP invented in 2016 — the first SPD-Green-FDP state coalition in Germany — has no history.

## Apparent
Rheinland-Pfalz: 4.1 million inhabitants. 4 Bundesrat seats. Capital: Mainz. Founded 1946 by French military ordinance.
Current: CDU-SPD coalition forming. Gordon Schnieder (CDU) incoming Ministerpräsident. Coalition negotiations ongoing as of April 2026.
CDU 31.0%, SPD 25.9% — together a large majority. AfD 24 seats as opposition. Greens 10 seats.
Next regular election: 2031.
Voting age: 18. 4 Bundesrat seats.

## Yearbook
**2031** — Next regular Landtag election.

**2026** — 22 March. CDU **31.0%** — stärkste Partei for first time since 1987. SPD 25.9% — worst ever in RLP, down nearly 10 points. AfD **19.5%** — best AfD result in any western German state. Greens 7.9%. FDP **2.1%** — out after two terms in government. Freie Wähler 4.2% — out. Linke 4.4% — out. Turnout 68.5%. Four parties in new Landtag. CDU-SPD coalition forming. Schweitzer (SPD) leads opposition negotiations after 35 years of SPD-led government ends.

**2021** — 14 March. SPD 35.7%. CDU 27.7% — worst ever in RLP at that point. Greens 9.3% — near-doubled. AfD 8.3% — down. FDP 5.5%. Freie Wähler 5.4% — first entry. Turnout 64.4%. Ampel-Koalition (SPD-Green-FDP) continues. Dreyer third term. Dreyer retires July 2024, succeeded by Alexander Schweitzer.

**2016** — 13 March. SPD 36.2%. CDU 31.8%. AfD 12.6% — first entry, immediately third. FDP 6.2%. Greens 5.3%. Turnout 70.4%. First Ampel-Koalition in Germany — SPD-Green-FDP. Dreyer second term.

**2011** — 27 March. SPD 35.7%. CDU 35.2% — separated by 0.5 points. Greens 15.4%. Turnout 61.8%. SPD-Green coalition. Dreyer becomes Ministerpräsidentin in 2013 (Beck retires).

**2006** — 26 March. SPD **45.6%** — near absolute majority. CDU 32.8%. FDP 8.0%. Turnout 58.3%. SPD governs alone. Kurt Beck Ministerpräsident.

**2001** — 25 March. SPD 44.7%. CDU 35.3%. FDP 7.8%. Turnout 62.1%. SPD-FDP coalition. Beck.

**1996** — 24 March. SPD 39.8%. CDU 38.7%. FDP 8.9%. Turnout 73.9%. SPD-FDP. Beck.

**1991** — 21 April. SPD **44.8%** — SPD wins Rheinland-Pfalz for the first time since 1971. CDU 38.7%. FDP 6.9%. Turnout 83.8%. Rudolf Scharping Ministerpräsident — the first SPD government in RLP in twenty years.

**1987** — 17 May. CDU **44.7%** — CDU last stärkste Partei before 2026. SPD 38.8%. FDP 7.3%. Turnout 79.7%. Bernhard Vogel (CDU) then Carl-Ludwig Wagner.

**1983** — 6 March. CDU 51.9%. SPD 39.6%. Turnout 84.5%. CDU absolute majority. Vogel.

**1979** — 18 March. CDU 50.1%. SPD 42.3%. FDP 6.4%. Turnout 82.6%. CDU. Helmut Kohl had been here — left for Bonn in 1976. Bernhard Vogel succeeds.

**1971** — 21 March. CDU **50.0%** — CDU absolute majority. SPD 40.7%. Turnout 83.1%. Kohl first full term as Ministerpräsident. The CDU had governed RLP without interruption since 1946. SPD never won here in the first 45 years.

**1946** — 1 December. CDU 47.2%. SPD 34.3%. KPD 8.7%. FDP 7.4%. Turnout 74.0%. CDU from the start. Wilhelm Boden Ministerpräsident.
