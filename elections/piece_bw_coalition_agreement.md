# Piece: Coalition Agreement Baden-Württemberg 2026

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Place:** [Staatsministerium Stuttgart](place_bw_staatsministerium.md)

## Place
[Staatsministerium Stuttgart](place_bw_staatsministerium.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it the Grüne have no governing partner — 56 seats, majority 79.
Without it the CDU has no ministerial positions — 56 seats, same as the Grüne, no path to govern alone or with AfD.
Without it the five Bundesrat seats have no unified position.
Remove it and the only viable coalition in BW collapses.

## Apparent
Not yet signed — negotiations ongoing as of April 2026.
Grüne and CDU. The same coalition as 2016 and 2021, but now with identical seat counts rather than a Green majority. The power balance has shifted.
Cem Özdemir Ministerpräsident-designate. First Ministerpräsident with Turkish heritage. First Ministerpräsident not from BW.
CDU conditions on entering negotiations included: procedural limits on the Ministerpräsident's authority within cabinet, the Landtagspräsident post for CDU, the CDU programme to be reflected "one to one", mechanisms to prevent CDU from being outvoted in cabinet. CDU Spitzenkandidat Hagel declined to resign after losing — CDU Landesvorstand rejected his offered resignation.

## Yearbook
8 March 2026. Result: Grüne 56, CDU 56. No other coalition viable without AfD. Grün-Schwarz the only option.
March–April 2026. CDU enters negotiations with extensive demands. Özdemir offers "Partnerschaft auf Augenhöhe" — partnership at eye level. CDU demands structural constraints suggesting they do not accept the meaning of "eye level" as the Grüne understand it.
Coalition agreement: not yet finalised as of 03.04.2026.
