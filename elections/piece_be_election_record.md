# Piece: Election Record Berlin

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Place:** [Abgeordnetenhaus Berlin](place_be_abgeordnetenhaus.md)

## Load Bearing
Without it the 2023 result has no context — it was a rerun of a voided election, not an ordinary cycle.
Without it the SPD's choice to enter a CDU-led coalition despite holding a R2G majority has no explanation.
Without it the division of the city — West Berlin democratic elections, East Berlin Scheinwahlen, 1946–1989 — has no presence in the record.
Remove it and Berlin looks like a normal city-state. With it, it looks like a city that had to reconstitute its democratic identity twice: once in 1948 when the East split off, once in 1990 when it reunified.

## Apparent
Berlin: city-state, federal capital, 3.7 million inhabitants. 4 Bundesrat seats.
The Abgeordnetenhaus, not Landtag. The Senat, not Landesregierung. The Regierende Bürgermeister, not Ministerpräsident.
Minimum voting age: 16.
Current composition: CDU 52 seats, SPD 34, Greens 34, Linke 22, AfD 17. CDU-SPD coalition. Kai Wegner Regierender Bürgermeister.
Next regular election: 20 September 2026.

## Yearbook
**2026** — Next regular Abgeordnetenhauswahl. 20 September 2026. Current polls: CDU 23%, AfD 16%, Linke 16%, SPD 16%, Greens 15%. CDU-SPD coalition has no majority in current polls.

**2023** — 12 February. Wiederholungswahl — rerun of the voided 2021 election. CDU **28.2%** (+10.6). SPD 18.4% — worst result in Berlin history. Greens 18.4% — separated from SPD by 53 votes. Linke 12.2%. AfD 9.1%. FDP 4.6% — outside. Turnout 62.9% — over 12 points lower than 2021, which was held with the Bundestagswahl. The existing R2G coalition retained a mathematical majority. The SPD chose instead to form a CDU-led grand coalition. Wegner elected Regierender Bürgermeister on 27 April — third ballot, 86 votes. The first and second ballots failed when some coalition members withheld their votes.

**2021** — 26 September. Held parallel to Bundestagswahl. SPD 21.4%, Greens 18.9%, CDU 18.0%, Linke 14.1%, AfD 8.0%, FDP 7.1%. Turnout 75.4%. R2G coalition formed. Franziska Giffey (SPD) Regierende Bürgermeisterin. **The election was declared fully invalid** by the Berliner Verfassungsgerichtshof on 16 November 2022 — polling chaos during the Berlin Marathon, insufficient ballots, voters turned away, hours-long queues after poll closing. A full rerun was ordered within 90 days. The Volksentscheid "Deutsche Wohnen & Co enteignen" was not rerun — no constitutional complaint was filed against it.

**2016** — 18 September. SPD 21.6%, CDU 17.6%, Linke 15.6%, Greens 15.2%, AfD 14.2% — first entry into Abgeordnetenhaus. FDP 6.7%. Turnout 66.9%. R2G coalition: SPD-Linke-Greens. Michael Müller (SPD) Regierender Bürgermeister.

**2011** — 18 September. SPD 28.3%, CDU 23.4%, Greens 17.6%, Linke 11.7%, Pirates 8.9% — first entry. FDP 1.8% — outside. Turnout 60.2%. SPD-CDU grand coalition. Klaus Wowereit (SPD) continues as Regierender Bürgermeister. The Pirate Party's 8.9% is the highest result for a new party in Berlin history. It disappears entirely by 2016 (1.9%).

**2006** — 17 September. SPD 30.8%, CDU 21.3%, Linke 13.4%, Greens 13.1%, FDP 7.6%. Turnout 58.0% — lowest in Berlin history to that point. SPD-Linke coalition continues. Wowereit re-elected.

**2001** — 21 October. Vorgezogene Wahl after the Bankenskandal — the CDU-SPD coalition collapsed over the Berliner Bankgesellschaft affair, a €21 billion banking scandal. SPD 29.7%, PDS 22.6%, CDU 23.8%. Turnout 68.1%. SPD-PDS coalition. Klaus Wowereit (SPD) becomes Regierender Bürgermeister — the first openly gay head of government in Germany. His remark "Ich bin schwul, und das ist auch gut so" — "I am gay, and that is a good thing too" — is said at the SPD party conference before this election and becomes one of the most quoted political sentences in modern German history.

**1999** — 10 October. CDU 40.8%, SPD 22.4%, PDS 17.7%, Greens 9.9%, FDP 2.2%. Turnout 65.8%. CDU-SPD grand coalition. Eberhard Diepgen (CDU) Regierender Bürgermeister — his third term. This is the high point of CDU dominance in post-reunification Berlin.

**1995** — 22 October. CDU 37.4%, SPD 23.6%, PDS 14.6%, Greens 13.9%. Turnout 68.6%. CDU-SPD. Diepgen continues.

**1990** — 2 December. First free all-Berlin election since 1946. CDU **40.4%**. SPD 30.4%. PDS 9.2%. FDP 7.1%. Greens/AL 4.4% — just outside. Turnout 80.8%. Walter Momper (SPD) outgoing, Eberhard Diepgen (CDU) becomes Regierender Bürgermeister. The eastern and western halves of the city vote under a single ballot for the first time in 44 years. The PDS — successor to the SED — enters the Abgeordnetenhaus of the city the SED governed through Scheinwahlen for four decades.

## Political Memory: The Divided City (1948–1989)

*West Berlin held free democratic elections throughout. East Berlin held SED Scheinwahlen. The Abgeordnetenhaus of West Berlin is the continuous institution. East Berlin's SED Stadtverordnetenversammlung is a different thing entirely.*

**1946** — 20 October. First postwar Berlin election — the whole city. SPD 48.7%. CDU 22.2%. SED 19.8%. LDP 9.3%. Turnout 92.3%. The SED's poor result in Berlin — despite Soviet pressure — is one reason the Soviets begin restricting West Berlin. The city votes against the SED with 80% of its ballots. This result shapes everything that follows.

**1948** — 5 December. West Berlin only — the Soviet sector has split off. SPD **64.5%**. CDU 19.4%. FDP 16.1%. The SPD's best result anywhere in postwar German history. Ernst Reuter (SPD) becomes Oberbürgermeister. The Berlin Blockade is ongoing — Soviet forces have cut road, rail, and canal links to West Berlin since June. The Western Allies are supplying the city by air. The vote is an act of political identity under siege.

**1950–1989** — West Berlin only. The SPD dominates through the 1950s and 1960s — absolute majorities from 1958 to 1971. Willy Brandt is Regierender Bürgermeister 1957–1966. The CDU takes over through the 1970s and 1980s. Ernst Reuter, Otto Suhr, Willy Brandt, Heinrich Albertz, Klaus Schütz, Dietrich Stobbe, Hans-Jochen Vogel, Richard von Weizsäcker, Eberhard Diepgen — each Regierender Bürgermeister governing half a city with an open wound running through it.

**1961** — 13 August. The Wall is built. Berlin loses its function as the escape route out of the GDR overnight. The wall runs through the city — through streets, through gardens, through family lines. West Berlin becomes an island surrounded by the GDR. The Abgeordnetenhaus continues to function. The elections continue. The wall is outside the window.

**1963** — SPD 61.9% — second-best result in Berlin history. Willy Brandt in his third term. Kennedy has visited. "Ich bin ein Berliner" is six months old. The SPD governs a city half of which cannot vote.

**1989** — 29 January. Last West Berlin election before reunification. CDU 37.7%, SPD 37.3%, AL (Greens) 11.8%, FDP 3.9%, Republikaner 7.5%. Turnout 80.3%. Grand coalition CDU-SPD. Diepgen. Nine months later the Wall falls. Fourteen months after this election the city holds its first free all-Berlin vote.
