# Persona: Petersen
## Title: [Voter Schleswig-Holstein](position_sh_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Forty-four. Works the reception desk at the Flensburg ferry terminal — the Scandlines crossing to Denmark.
Born in Flensburg. Grew up hearing Danish at home, German at school. Reads both without thinking about it.
Her mother voted SSW. Petersen votes CDU. This has never been discussed directly in the family.
She knows the SSW exemption exists — the Danish minority party doesn't need five percent to enter the Landeshaus. She has never had to explain this to anyone who grew up here. Everyone here already knows.
Rents a flat ten minutes from the harbour. Takes the bus. Makes coffee at home before the shift.
Watches the news in the evening. Turns it off before the political analysis begins.
Moves between German and Danish without marking the transition — a sentence can start in one and end in the other. Her German carries a slight Danish rhythm: longer vowels, less final consonant sharpening. In professional settings she straightens it. At the ferry terminal she doesn't bother.

## Action
Votes in every election. State, federal, European.
Fills in the ballot carefully. Folds it correctly. Drops it in.
Goes home.

## Shadow
She believes the border she lives on is settled — 1955, the Bonn-Copenhagen Declarations, minority rights on both sides, stability ever since.
She votes to keep things stable. CDU is stable. The state is stable. The border is stable.
What she cannot see: the things that make her border stable — the minority exemption, the cross-border framework, the European structures that underpin both — are federal and European decisions, not state ones.
Her vote produces a state government.
The state government produces a Bundesrat delegation.
The delegation votes on the federal legislation that holds the framework her stability depends on.
She voted for the state. The state went somewhere she didn't follow.
She doesn't know there's a gap because nothing has broken yet.

## Tell
She says *hier ist das immer so gewesen* — it's always been like that here — and means it as a complete answer.
