## Shown
Am Markt 20, Bremen. The Bürgerschaft meets in a building on the Marktplatz — the UNESCO-listed medieval square with the Roland statue and the Rathaus. The parliament of the smallest German state meets beside the oldest symbols of Hanseatic mercantile freedom.
87 seats. SPD 33, CDU 25, BiW (Bürger in Wut) 10, Greens 13, Linke 12, FDP 4. Majority: 44.
SPD-Green-Linke holds 58 — fourteen above majority.
The AfD is not present — not admitted in 2023 due to competing lists.
Voting age: 16. Three Bundesrat seats.
Two Wahlbereiche: Bremen city (about 80 seats) and Bremerhaven (about 7 seats), each with its own 5% threshold. A party can enter the Bürgerschaft by passing 5% in either city alone.
The Bürgerschaft, not the Landtag. The head of government is the Bürgermeister (President of the Senate), not the Ministerpräsident.

## Offers
Legislation for 0.7 million people in Germany's smallest state.
Election of the Bürgermeister — the SPD has held this office since 1945.
Three Bundesrat seats.
Bremen's Hanseatic identity — merchant republic, free city, Schütting (the merchants' house), the Roland, the Rathaus. A city that governed itself as a trading power and still governs through the forms that merchant culture produced.

## Withheld
The BiW's 10 seats — a local right-populist party that exists because the AfD was excluded. When the AfD re-enters in 2027, what happens to BiW is unclear. Whether the AfD voter and the BiW voter are the same person making the same choice in different packaging is the question.
Bremerhaven: a separate city with its own distinct political culture — poorer, more working-class, more AfD-adjacent, voting differently from the university and media city of Bremen. The two Wahlbereiche share a state but not always a political direction.
The Roland statue. He has been standing on the Marktplatz since 1404. The Bürgerschaft has been meeting beside him in various forms for nearly as long. He is not in the coalition.
