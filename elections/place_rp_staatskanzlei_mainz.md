## Owner

## Shown
Peter-Altmeier-Allee 1, Mainz. Named for Peter Altmeier — CDU Ministerpräsident 1947–1969, the architect of CDU dominance in RLP for its first four decades.
Currently in transition — the SPD government of Alexander Schweitzer concluded on 22 March 2026 with the election result. Gordon Schnieder (CDU) and his team are in coalition negotiations. The Staatskanzlei is occupied by an outgoing government and preparing to receive an incoming one.
4 Bundesrat seats will be reappointed with the new CDU-SPD cabinet.
RLP pays into and receives from the Länderfinanzausgleich in roughly neutral terms — the wine sector, tourism, BASF at Ludwigshafen, Bosch, and federal military installations (US military bases in the Palatinate) give the state a mixed economic structure.

## Offers
Executive governance of a state that spans Rhine corridor, Moselle wine region, Eifel uplands, the Palatinate.
Four Bundesrat seats — transitioning from SPD-led Ampel position to CDU-led coalition position. The shift will affect federal votes on climate, fiscal policy, and migration.
The Nürburgring, the Moselle, the Gutenberg Museum — cultural assets that generate tourism and diplomatic identity.

## Withheld
What the building looks like under a CDU Ministerpräsident. No one currently working there has experienced that.
The Dreyer era's end — Malu Dreyer retired July 2024 after eleven years; Schweitzer held the position for eight months before the election; the transition from the Dreyer consensus to whatever comes next was not completed before the electoral defeat.
Whether the CDU-SPD coalition — large majority, 70 of 105 seats — produces the stability its arithmetic suggests. Two parties that have governed as opponents for 35 years.
