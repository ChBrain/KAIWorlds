## Shown
Domplatz 6, Magdeburg. In the shadow of the Magdeburger Dom — the oldest Gothic cathedral in Germany, begun 1209. The Landtag building is a converted baroque palace from the 18th century, rebuilt after wartime destruction. The cathedral survived. The old city did not. The parliament sits in a reconstruction.
97 seats currently. CDU 40, AfD 23, Linke 14, SPD 11, FDP 7, Greens 7. Majority: 49.
CDU-SPD-FDP hold 58 — a working majority, but fragile. The Greens are not in government.
4 Bundesrat seats.
Next election: 6 September 2026 — five months away. Current polls: AfD 38%, CDU 25%. The chamber after September 2026 will look very different from this one.
Voting age: 18.

## Offers
Legislation for 2.2 million people — a shrinking population in a state with significant rural depopulation and one of the lowest population densities in Germany.
Election of the Ministerpräsident — in 2021 Haseloff failed the first ballot and won the second. In September 2026 the chamber will elect someone new.
Four Bundesrat seats — cast from a Deutschland-Koalition position, centre-right on fiscal policy, cautious on energy transition.

## Withheld
What the chamber looks like after 6 September 2026 if the AfD wins 38% and the CDU 25%. Which democratic parties will be in the room. Whether a majority exists without BSW or against the AfD.
The Umweltministerium that was abolished in 2021. What environmental policy looks like without a dedicated ministry in a state with significant chemical industry heritage.
The seven CDU votes that did not support Haseloff in the first investiture ballot. Where those votes went. Whether they go to the AfD in September.
