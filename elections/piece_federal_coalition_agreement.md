# Piece: Coalition Agreement Bundesrepublik Deutschland 2025

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Place:** [Reichstag Berlin](place_reichstag_berlin.md)

## Load Bearing
Without it CDU/CSU has no governing coalition — 208+44 seats, majority 316.
Without it the SPD's choice to govern with CDU/CSU at 16.4% — worst ever federal result — has no record.
Without it the first CDU/CSU-SPD coalition since 2021, and the fourth GroKo in German history, has no institutional record.

## Apparent
CDU/CSU and SPD. Signed 5 May 2025. Friedrich Merz Bundeskanzler.
328 of 630 seats — simple majority. CDU/CSU: 252 seats. SPD: 120 seats. 12 above the 316 threshold.
17 ministers: 7 CDU (including Foreign Affairs, Economics, new Digitalisierung/Staatsmodernisierung), 7 SPD (including Finance, Justice, Defence), 3 CSU (including Interior).
Merz was elected Bundeskanzler on 6 May 2025 in the second ballot — failed first (310 votes, needed 316). Won second (325 votes). Six coalition members did not support him in the first round.

## Yearbook
23 February 2025. Election. CDU/CSU 28.5%, AfD 20.8%, SPD 16.4%.
February–April 2025. CDU/CSU-SPD coalition negotiations. SPD membership vote required and conducted.
9 April 2025. Coalition agreement finalised.
5 May 2025. Coalition agreement signed by party leaders.
6 May 2025. Bundestag elects Merz Bundeskanzler on second ballot.
Current: Kabinett Merz governing. The Schuldenbremse controversy — calls for defence and infrastructure spending exceeding constitutional debt limits. The AfD as the largest opposition party in parliament with 152 seats — a structural presence in every Bundestag debate.
Next regular election: autumn 2029.
