## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Shown
Planckstraße 2, Hannover. The executive seat of the Ministerpräsident and the Niedersächsische Landesregierung.
Weil governs from here — third term, SPD-Green coalition, 2022–2027.
Six Bundesrat seats coordinated from here — maximum allocation, alongside Bayern and NRW. On industrial and labour legislation Niedersachsen's six votes carry significant weight.
Niedersachsen is a net contributor to the Länderfinanzausgleich — the VW revenues, the agricultural sector, the chemical industry at Salzgitter and Wolfsburg contribute to a state that pays into the federal equalisation system.
The VW Gesetz: the state holds 20% of VW shares and a veto right on major corporate decisions under a special law that has survived EU challenges. The Staatskanzlei is not Wolfsburg's employer, but it is Wolfsburg's largest single shareholder.

## Offers
Executive governance of Germany's second-largest state by area. The North Sea coast, the Harz, the Lüneburg Heath, the Emsland, the Weser Uplands — a state of extreme geographic variation.
Six Bundesrat votes — cast from a SPD-Green coalition that is one of the few functioning red-green governments in Germany. On climate, energy, and industrial transition: Niedersachsen's positions are watched carefully because they have to reconcile VW's interests with Green coalition commitments.
The port of Wilhelmshaven — Germany's only deepwater container port. LNG import terminal added post-2022.

## Withheld
The VW plant closure crisis of 2024 — negotiations over Niedersachsen's role as shareholder in a company restructuring that would eliminate German jobs. The Staatskanzlei's position between shareholder interest and labour interest is the permanent structural tension of governing this state.
Whether Weil runs for a fourth term in 2027. He has not said. The question shapes the coalition's final two years.
What six votes from a SPD-Green government mean in a Bundesrat increasingly shaped by CDU-led state governments after the Ampel collapse.
