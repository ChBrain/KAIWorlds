# Position: Voter Niedersachsen

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election.md)

## Has
Two votes — Erststimme and Zweitstimme. Voting age: 18.
One vote in the federal election. One vote in the European election.
The [election record](piece_ni_election_record.md) as the accumulated history.
Geography as political identity — more pronounced in Niedersachsen than in most western states. The Emsland voter and the Hannover voter cast ballots in the same Landtag election and inhabit different political worlds: Catholic west, SPD-industrial centre, rural conservative south, North Sea coast.
The VW voter — in Wolfsburg, Salzgitter, Emden, the company's factories — for whom the automotive transition is not an energy policy question but a job question.

## Orders
Vote or don't.
In 2022: 60.3% voted — lower than 2017 but not historically low. The SPD held against the Ampel headwind; the CDU collapsed. The position's individual vote was part of a pattern that Weil's personal standing produced.
In 2027: the position votes with current polls showing CDU and SPD at 27%/26% — nearly equal, with AfD at 21%. A different kind of election from 2022.

## Loses
The VW certainty.
The Wolfsburg voter whose employment security was underwritten by the political consensus that Niedersachsen's 20% stake protected — that consensus is under pressure from the automotive transition in ways that had no equivalent in previous elections.
The SPD as a governing federal force. The position votes SPD in Niedersachsen where it often produces SPD governments. At the federal level, the SPD just lost badly. These are the same voters, the same position, producing different results at different altitudes.

## Drives
On the loss of VW certainty —
votes knowing the 20% shareholder's seat on the Volkswagen supervisory board is also a political position that the Landtag controls indirectly.
The Wolfsburg voter who votes SPD is voting partly for the party of codetermination.
The Emsland voter who votes CDU is voting partly for the enclave that insulates local political culture from the state result.
Both know what they are doing. Neither is wrong.
