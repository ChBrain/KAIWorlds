# Piece: Coalition Agreement Sachsen 2024

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Place:** [Staatskanzlei Dresden](place_sn_staatskanzlei_dresden.md)

## Place
[Staatskanzlei Dresden](place_sn_staatskanzlei_dresden.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it CDU has no governing partner — 40 seats, majority 61.
Without it the SPD has no leverage in a state where it has never exceeded 19%.
Without it Sachsen has no government — constitutional deadline was 3 February 2025.

## Apparent
CDU and SPD. Minority government — 50 of 120 seats. Signed December 2024.
The Brombeer-Koalition (CDU-BSW-SPD, 66 seats) failed in November 2024 — BSW left negotiations over the Friedensformel and migration disputes after BSW members voted with the AfD in the Landtag on a Corona investigation committee.
CDU then negotiated minority government with SPD only. The partners agreed to "stärkere Einbindung des Parlaments" — stronger parliamentary involvement — as a structural acknowledgement that they cannot pass legislation alone.
Kretschmer elected 18 December 2024 in second ballot with 69 votes. First ballot: 55 votes (below 61 absolute majority needed). Second ballot: Linke's 6 votes gave him the 69. No formal agreement with Linke published.

## Yearbook
September 2024. CDU wins narrowly. Brombeer negotiations begin.
October 2024. SPD pauses talks after BSW votes with AfD.
November 2024. BSW ends negotiations. Cites Friedensformel and migration.
November–December 2024. CDU-SPD minority coalition negotiated.
14 December 2024. CDU party congress approves coalition agreement. SPD membership vote: 78.1% approve (37.1% turnout).
18 December 2024. Kretschmer fails first ballot (55 votes). Wins second ballot (69 votes — Linke provides margin).
Current: minority government assembling case-by-case majorities. Budget, legislation, and constitutional decisions all require partners beyond the 50-seat coalition.
