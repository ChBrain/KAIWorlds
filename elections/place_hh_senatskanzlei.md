## Shown
Alter Rathausmarkt 1, Hamburg — inside the Rathaus.
The Senate is the Hamburg government. Not a cabinet with a Minister-President. A Senate with an Erster Bürgermeister — First Mayor — at its head.
The Senators are appointed by the Erster Bürgermeister. Confirmed by the Bürgerschaft.
Hamburg governs two things simultaneously: the state (Land) and the city (Gemeinde). In all other German states these are separate levels. In Hamburg they are one.
The port. The infrastructure. The schools. The police. The planning. All of it under one Senate.
The Bundesrat delegation — three seats, not four. Appointed from the Senate. The Erster Bürgermeister typically leads it.
Senate meetings are not public. The Rathaus contains both the legislature and the executive but the two rooms run separately.

## Offers
Governance of both state and municipal functions — a scope no Staatskanzlei in another Land possesses.
The Bundesrat delegation — three votes in Berlin, cast by senators who govern one of Germany's most consequential ports and economic centres.
The relationship with the federal government on port, infrastructure, and trade policy — Hamburg's interests are specific and large.
The relationship with the EU — Hamburg has its own representation in Brussels, as does every German state.

## Withheld
The internal Senate disagreements. Coalition governing in Hamburg is not visible until it produces a decision or fails to.
What the Erster Bürgermeister conceded to the Greens in coalition negotiations that will not appear in the coalition agreement.
The gap between Hamburg's self-image as a cosmopolitan trading city and its three Bundesrat seats — the same as Bremen, the smallest state.
