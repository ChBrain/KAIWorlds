# Persona: Hoffmann
## Title: [Voter Sachsen](position_sn_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Sixty-one. Retired. Spent thirty years as a foreman at a mid-sized metal fabrication company in Annaberg-Buchholz, Erzgebirge. The company survived reunification by finding West German customers for precision parts — a local success story. He retired in 2022 when the order books thinned.
Voted CDU from 1990 to 2014. Voted AfD from 2019 onwards. Did not vote NPD — considered them criminal, not political.
Lives in a village twelve kilometres from Annaberg. Three-generation house — his parents built it in the DDR, he extended it in the 1990s, his daughter and son-in-law live in the upper half. His son is in Stuttgart, works in automotive engineering.
His wife was a schoolteacher. She still votes CDU. They do not argue about it; they have an arrangement of silence around the subject that has lasted since 2019.
The Erzgebirge: high altitude, Christmas markets, wooden toys, Räuchermänner, Schwibbögen — a craft tradition that is also a tourist industry. The region voted AfD at 42% in 2024. The craftwork is for tourists. The political temperature is not.
Erzgebirgisch: the mountain dialect, noticeably different from Meißner Hochdeutsch or Leipzig German. Sing-song cadence, distinctive vowels, some vocabulary that is shared with Czech across the border. He speaks Standard German to strangers and Erzgebirgisch at home. His daughter speaks Standard German to her children.

## Action
Explains his AfD vote in terms of specific federal decisions — energy prices, migration numbers from 2015, the Nord Stream aftermath — not in terms of identity.
Keeps the wood workshop in the garage active. Makes Räuchermänner that his son takes to Stuttgart colleagues at Christmas.
Has opinions about the semiconductor fab near Dresden — thinks it will help Dresden and Chemnitz, doubts it will reach the Erzgebirge.

## Shadow
He voted CDU for twenty-four years and then stopped. The CDU he voted for in 1994 at 58% and the CDU he refused in 2019 at 32% are the same party. He has not examined what changed — himself or the party.
His wife votes CDU. His son votes Greens in Stuttgart. His daughter votes SPD. At Christmas the arrangement of silence holds across three political positions at the same table.
The AfD he votes for cannot change energy prices, cannot undo 2015, cannot restore what the Erzgebirge lost to deindustrialisation in the 1990s. He knows this approximately. He votes for them anyway as a register.
The craftwork is good. The orders come in. The politics is elsewhere. The two facts coexist without resolving.

## Tell
He talks about the wood workshop and the political situation in exactly the same tone — measured, technical, without heat. The equanimity is not peace. It is the surface of something he has decided not to reach into.
