# Piece: Election Record Bremen

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Place:** [Bürgerschaft Bremen](place_hb_buergerschaft_bremen.md)

## Load Bearing
Without it the SPD's unbroken tenure as the party of the Bürgermeister since 1945 — nearly 80 years — has no architecture.
Without it the 2019 shock — CDU became stärkste Partei for the first time since the Second World War — and the 2023 recovery have no connective tissue.
Without it the BiW (Bürger in Wut) — a local right-populist alternative to the AfD — has no context as a specifically Bremer phenomenon.
Without it the dual Wahlbereich structure — Bremen city and Bremerhaven voting separately, each with its own 5% threshold — has no record.

## Apparent
Freie Hansestadt Bremen: 0.7 million inhabitants — the smallest German state, city-state, two cities. 3 Bundesrat seats. Capital and largest city: Bremen. Second city: Bremerhaven (110,000 people, 60 km north on the Weser estuary). Founded as a state 1947.
The parliament is the Bremische Bürgerschaft, not a Landtag. The head of government is the Bürgermeister (President of the Senate), not a Ministerpräsident.
Current: SPD-Green-Linke coalition. Bovenschulte Bürgermeister. SPD 33 seats, Greens 13, Linke 12 — together 58 of 87. Majority: 44.
Voting age: 16.
Next regular election: 2027. Current polls: CDU 24%, SPD 22%, AfD 15%, Greens 15%, Linke 13%.

## Yearbook
**2027** — Next regular Bürgerschaft election.

**2023** — 14 May. SPD **29.8%** — recovers from 2019 shock. CDU 26.2% — retains near-2019 level. Greens 11.9% — heavy losses. Linke 10.9%. **Bürger in Wut (BiW) 9.4%** — right-populist local party, formerly marginal, surges as AfD substitute. FDP 5.1%. **AfD: not admitted** — submitted two competing lists. Turnout 57.0% — down from 2019's 64.1%. SPD-Green-Linke coalition continues. Bovenschulte second term.

**2019** — 26 May. **CDU 26.7%** — stärkste Partei for the first time since WWII. SPD 24.9% — worst ever. Greens 17.4%. AfD 6.1% — first entry. FDP 5.9%. Linke 11.3%. Turnout 64.1%. SPD-Green-Linke coalition — first left coalition without GroKo option, first Linke participation in a western German state government. Carsten Sieling resigns; Andreas Bovenschulte elected Bürgermeister.

**2015** — 10 May. SPD 32.8%. CDU 22.4%. Greens 15.1%. Linke 9.5%. AfD 5.5% — first entry to Bremen. FDP 6.6%. Turnout 50.2%. SPD-Green-FDP coalition.

**2011** — 22 May. SPD 38.6%. CDU 20.4%. Greens 22.5%. FDP 6.5%. Linke 5.6%. AfD did not exist. Turnout 55.5%. SPD-Green coalition. Jens Böhrnsen Bürgermeister.

**2007** — 13 May. SPD 36.7%. CDU 25.6%. Greens 16.4%. FDP 6.0%. DVU 2.7%. Turnout 57.6%. SPD-Green coalition. Böhrnsen.

**2003** — 25 May. SPD 29.8%. CDU 29.8% — tied. Greens 12.8%. FDP 4.2% — outside. DVU 5.1%. Schill 4.0% — outside. Turnout 60.5%. SPD-CDU GroKo. Henning Scherf then Böhrnsen.

**1999** — 6 June. SPD 42.6%. CDU 37.1%. Greens 9.0%. FDP 2.5% — outside. DVU 3.0% — outside. Turnout 60.3%. SPD-CDU GroKo. Peter Sodann — no, Scherf.

**1995** — 14 May. SPD 33.3%. CDU 32.6%. Greens 13.1%. DVU 2.5%. FDP 3.4% — outside. Turnout 66.0%. SPD-CDU. Scherf.

**1991** — 29 September. SPD 38.8%. CDU 30.7%. Greens 11.4%. FDP 9.5%. Turnout 73.3%. SPD-FDP-Greens "Ampel" — first Ampel coalition in Germany. Klaus Wedemeier Bürgermeister.

**1987** — 13 September. SPD 50.5% — SPD absolute majority. CDU 23.4%. Greens 10.2%. FDP 10.0%. Turnout 71.3%. SPD alone. Hans Koschnick then Wedemeier.

**1983** — 25 September. SPD 46.1%. CDU 28.7%. FDP 7.9%. Greens 5.1%. Turnout 76.9%. SPD alone. Koschnick.

**1947** — First postwar election. SPD established as governing party of Bremen — an unbroken line from here to the present.
