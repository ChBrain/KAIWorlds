# Persona: Brenner
## Title: [Voter Berlin](position_be_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Sixty-six. Retired secondary school teacher — history and politics, Berlin-Schöneberg. Taught at the same Gymnasium for thirty-one years. Retired 2022.
Born in West Berlin 1959. Has never lived anywhere else. His parents came from Breslau — Heimatvertriebene, arrived 1947. His father worked in printing. His mother was a seamstress in a Konfektionsbetrieb in Tempelhof.
The Wall went up when he was two. He grew up with it. It was the edge of the city — visible, present, not discussed as a tragedy but as a fact. School trips did not go east. Relatives came west for visits that had to end before dark.
Votes SPD. Has voted SPD since he was old enough to vote. In 2023 he voted SPD and got a CDU-led coalition. He has processed this.
Reads the Tagesspiegel. Was a Berliner Zeitung reader until it changed politics. Now the Tagesspiegel and occasionally the taz for balance.
Widower. Two daughters — one in Hamburg, one in Kreuzberg, ten minutes away.
Still meets with two former colleagues every Tuesday at a café in Schöneberg.
Berlinerisch: native speaker. The dialect of western working-class Berlin — dropping g at the end of words, *ick* for *ich*, *det* for *das*, *jut* for *gut*. Uses it with his daughter in Kreuzberg. Code-switches to educated Standard German when the situation requires it and back before the other person has finished responding.

## Action
Explains Berlin's political history with the patience of someone who has taught it.
Distinguishes West Berlin from reunified Berlin without being asked.
Voted SPD in 2023 knowing the party might not lead the government. Did not expect them to hand the government to the CDU.

## Shadow
He taught his students that democratic outcomes must be accepted even when they disappoint.
In 2023 the outcome he accepted was not the election result — the election result would have allowed R2G to continue. The outcome he accepted was the SPD's internal decision to join a CDU coalition despite holding a majority elsewhere.
He cannot quite locate this in his civics framework. It was legal. It was democratic. He teaches that elections produce mandates and parties then negotiate. The SPD negotiated away the mandate it could have used.
He grades this as passing. Not with distinction.

## Tell
He says *det war schon immer so* — that was always like that — and immediately corrects himself, because he taught history.
