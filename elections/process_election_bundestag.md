# Process: Election Bundestag
## The mechanism that renews the federal mandate — or doesn't.

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Place:** [Reichstag Berlin](place_reichstag_berlin.md)

## Initiated by
The four-year legislative term expires — or the Chancellor loses a confidence vote and the President dissolves the Bundestag.
The last federal election result is the ground the campaign stands on.
Every state's last election result is also present — the Bundesrat composition the new government will inherit is already set.
The [Voter](position_voter.md) has two votes and one polling day.
What was promised in the last coalition agreement is now a record.

## Direction
Toward the result.
Parties campaign nationally on what the last term produced and failed to produce.
The state results already in place shape what coalition is mathematically possible before a vote is cast.
The [Voter](position_voter.md) carries the last state result, the last federal result, and the last European result simultaneously — on different clocks, in the same body.
The [Reichstag Berlin](place_reichstag_berlin.md) waits.

## Lever
The [Voter](position_voter.md) votes for a federal government.
They are also, without being asked, voting into a chamber whose upper house — the Bundesrat — was already determined by state elections they voted in at different times.
The federal result produces a Bundestag. The Bundestag produces a Chancellor. The Chancellor governs alongside a Bundesrat whose composition the federal election did not change.
A party can win the Bundestag and face a Bundesrat controlled by its opponents — the product of state elections held years earlier.
Remove the state-federal separation and governing is simple.
Keep it and every federal election inherits a landscape it did not create.

## Echo
The federal election result is written.
Coalition negotiations begin. The 5% threshold has eliminated some parties. The mathematics are what they are.
The [Voter](position_voter.md) returns to their life.
The Bundesrat composition is unchanged.
[Process: Governing Bundestag](process_governing_bundestag.md) begins.
