# Process: Governing Europaparlament
## The period between the European mandate and the European reckoning.
## The institution that governs reactively — by design.

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Place:** [Europaparlament Brüssel](place_europaparlament_bruessel.md)
- **Place:** [Europaparlament Strassburg](place_europaparlament_strassburg.md)

## Initiated by
The Commission President is approved by the Parliament.
The College of Commissioners is confirmed after individual hearings.
The committees are constituted. Rapporteurs are assigned.
Three weeks in Brussels. One week in Strasbourg. Twelve times a year.
The Commission's work programme arrives. It contains what the Parliament will be asked to respond to.
What the Parliament wanted to initiate is a request, not a right.

## Direction
Toward the next European election result.
The Parliament works in [Europaparlament Brüssel](place_europaparlament_bruessel.md) — committees, political groups, trilogue negotiations with the Council and Commission.
Legislation proposed by the Commission arrives. The rapporteur shapes the Parliament's position.
The trilogue runs — Parliament, Council, Commission negotiate the final text.
Twelve times a year the Parliament moves to [Europaparlament Strassburg](place_europaparlament_strassburg.md) to vote formally.
All votes must happen in Strasbourg. This is written into the treaty.
Most of the work that produced the vote happened in Brussels.
The [Voter](position_voter.md) at home does not follow this. The results arrive in their life — as regulations, as standards, as funding — without the process being visible.

## Lever
The Parliament can block. It can amend. It can reject the Commission President.
It cannot propose.
The Commission has the near-monopoly on legislative initiative.
The Parliament can request legislation. The Commission must explain if it refuses — but it can refuse.
89% of MEPs want a general right of initiative. The treaty change this requires needs unanimity from all 27 member states.
Remove the initiative gap and the Parliament is a co-equal legislature.
Keep it and the Parliament governs the space between what arrives and what it will accept — influential, reactive, structurally constrained.
The MEPs from SH — Herbst, Burkhardt, Andresen — sit in this space. They shape what they can. They cannot set the agenda.

## Echo
Five years have run.
The Parliament has co-decided hundreds of pieces of legislation.
The CAP subsidies that reach Hansen's farm in Dithmarschen passed through this process.
The framework that holds the border Petersen grew up on was shaped here.
Neither of them followed it.
The European election result is now history.
[Process: Election Europaparlament](process_election_europa.md) begins again.
