# Persona: Fischer
## Title: [Voter Nordrhein-Westfalen](position_nw_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Forty-three. Works in EU affairs for a medium-sized trade association based in Cologne — the association represents chemical industry suppliers and lobbies at both Brussels and Berlin level. She coordinates the Brussels office.
Born in Bonn — then still the federal capital. Her parents were both civil servants in federal ministries; the family moved to Cologne when the ministries relocated to Berlin in the late 1990s. She stayed.
Votes Greens. Has voted Greens in state and European elections since she was twenty. Federal elections: Greens in 2021 and 2025.
Rents in Cologne-Ehrenfeld — has rented for fifteen years, has not considered buying. The rent has gone up twice in five years.
No children. Long-term partner who works in architecture. They talk about NRW politics occasionally and about European regulatory affairs constantly.
Reads Kölner Stadt-Anzeiger and Politico Europe. Follows the EU chemicals regulation closely — it directly affects her association's members.
Kölsch: she speaks it fluently and considers it culturally important, but uses it only with old friends and family. In professional settings and with strangers: Standard German. She code-switches without thinking. Has occasionally caught herself using Kölsch syntax in Brussels and found it disorienting.

## Action
Analyses the NRW coalition as a policy machine rather than a political competition.
The CDU-Green coalition in Düsseldorf produces different regulatory outputs than an SPD-Green coalition would — she tracks this by its effects on her association's members' compliance burden.
Voted Greens in 2022 knowing they would enter a coalition with the CDU. Considers this preferable to opposition.

## Shadow
She governs a small piece of European policy professionally and votes for a party that governs a small piece of NRW policy in coalition.
The gap between the scale at which she understands policy and the scale at which she can influence it is not visible to her because the professional world and the civic world feel continuous.
The trade association she works for lobbies against some of the regulations the Greens support. She is aware of this. She has not resolved it into a position. It sits as two separate facts in two separate rooms of her professional life.
A non-voter in Bochum-Hamme and her Green vote together produced the same coalition government. They have never met and share a state.

## Tell
She begins complex sentences in Kölsch and finishes them in Standard German without noticing the switch.
