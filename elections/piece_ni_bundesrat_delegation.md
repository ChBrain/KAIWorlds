## Place
[Bundesrat Berlin](place_bundesrat_berlin.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it 8.1 million people have no voice in federal legislation.
Without it Niedersachsen's structural interests — VW and the automotive transition, North Sea wind energy, port infrastructure at Wilhelmshaven and Bremen, the Hannover Messe — have no Bundesrat representation.
Without it one of the three maximum-seat delegations in the Bundesrat is absent. Niedersachsen, Bayern, and NRW together hold 18 of 69 seats — more than a quarter. When they align on consent legislation, the rest of Germany cannot block them.

## Apparent
Six seats. Appointed from the SPD-Green cabinet. Voted as a bloc.
Niedersachsen's six votes are among the most watched in the Bundesrat — the state's combination of industrial weight, labour tradition, and a Green coalition partner makes its position on energy and industrial policy non-predictable from party affiliation alone.
The VW-Gesetz has been defended in the Bundesrat against European Commission challenges. Niedersachsen votes consistently to preserve it.

## Yearbook
November 2022. SPD-Green cabinet appointed. Delegation follows.
2022–2026. Votes on federal energy legislation with the Green coalition's climate orientation, modified by VW interests on automotive transition timelines.
On the Kohleausstieg, on LNG infrastructure (the Wilhelmshaven terminal), on the Heizungsgesetz: Niedersachsen's six votes occupied the centre-left position of a state that must reconcile competing industrial and environmental obligations.
2027 onwards: delegation follows the new government.
