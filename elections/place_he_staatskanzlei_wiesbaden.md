## Owner

## Shown
Georg-August-Zinn-Straße 1, Wiesbaden. The executive seat of the Ministerpräsident. Named for Georg August Zinn — SPD Ministerpräsident 1950–1969, the longest-serving in Hessen's history, whose era of SPD governance is now a historical memory.
Rhein governs from here. CDU-SPD coalition. Five Bundesrat seats coordinated from here.
Hessen is a net contributor to the Länderfinanzausgleich — Frankfurt's financial industry taxes contribute significantly to a state that funds weaker states.
The ECB is in Frankfurt, not under Hessen jurisdiction, but the regulatory environment for German banks — Bafin is in Frankfurt and Bonn — intersects with state economic policy.

## Offers
Executive governance of the state with Germany's highest GDP per capita among the larger Flächenstaaten.
Five Bundesrat votes — cast from a CDU-SPD coalition on the centre-right, with SPD modulation on social policy.
The Frankfurt airport — the largest in Germany, the third largest in Europe. Hub of Lufthansa. Coordinate of the state's global connectivity and economic identity.
The Rhine-Main region: Frankfurt, Wiesbaden, Darmstadt, Offenbach — one of the most economically productive metropolitan areas in Europe.

## Withheld
The Zinn nameplate on the building — governing from a building named after the last significant SPD Ministerpräsident (1969), with the SPD now as a junior partner at 15.1% of the vote.
Whether the CDU-SPD coalition holds to 2028. Both parties are polling below their 2023 results. The AfD at 21% in current polls is consolidating its western German presence.
What Frankfurt's financial centre workers vote. Many commute from outside Hessen. The ECB staff are European citizens who cannot vote in state elections. The city's politics and the city's economy are not governed by the same people.
