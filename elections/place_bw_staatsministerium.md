## Shown
Richard-Wagner-Straße 15, Stuttgart. The Villa Reitzenstein — a neoclassical villa built 1910–1913, converted to government use after 1945. Set on a hillside above the city with a view across the Neckar valley.
Seat of the Ministerpräsident and the Staatsministerium — the core executive of BW.
Kretschmann governed from here for fifteen years. The rooms are the same. The occupant is changing.
Five Bundesrat seats are coordinated from here — BW votes as a bloc on federal legislation.
BW pays significantly into the Länderfinanzausgleich. Bayern and BW are the two largest net contributors. This shapes every Bundesrat vote on fiscal matters.

## Offers
Executive governance of a state of 11 million — Germany's third largest by population, second by economic output.
The five Bundesrat votes — significant in federal legislation, particularly on economic, energy, and fiscal policy.
The Landesregierung: Ministerpräsident plus cabinet ministers from both coalition parties.
International economic representation — BW has its own representation in Brussels and maintains export relationships as the home of major German industrial companies.

## Withheld
The coalition agreement not yet signed. The CDU's demands — including procedural constraints on the Ministerpräsident's authority, the Landtagspräsident position, programme commitments — are still under negotiation as of April 2026.
Whether Özdemir can govern as a Landesvater in the Kretschmann mode — pragmatic, Catholic, conservative-Green — or whether he will be read as a national Grüne figure in a state that just punished the national Grüne.
The Autobahn that runs through BW connects Stuttgart to Munich to the south and to Frankfurt and the Rhine-Ruhr to the north. The industrial economy that built this state is transforming — from combustion engines to something not yet named.
