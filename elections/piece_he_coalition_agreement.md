## Place
[Staatskanzlei Wiesbaden](place_he_staatskanzlei_wiesbaden.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it CDU has no governing partner — 52 seats, majority 67.
Without it the first CDU-SPD government in Hessen's history has no record.
Without it the SPD's choice to govern with CDU at 15.1% — the worst result in the party's Hessen history — has no context.

## Apparent
CDU and SPD. Signed December 2023. Motto: "Eine für alle."
74 of 133 seats — seven above majority.
CDU chose SPD over Greens after sounding both. SPD has been in opposition since 1999 — twenty-four years. This is the first time SPD joins a CDU-led Hessen government. Faeser, who lost her direct constituency, negotiated the coalition as SPD Landeschefin in parallel to continuing as Bundesinnenministerin.
The Greens were arithmetically available for Schwarz-Grün continuation — CDU with Greens had governed 2014–2024. CDU chose the switch. Reasons: Greens' losses in 2023 reduced their coalition value; SPD's willingness to make concessions was higher after their poor result.
SPD gets vice-Ministerpräsident and several ministries including social and justice.

## Yearbook
8 October 2023. Election. CDU 34.6%, SPD 15.1%.
October–December 2023. CDU conducts parallel soundings with SPD and Greens. Chooses SPD.
18 December 2023. Coalition agreement signed.
18 January 2024. New Landtag constitutes. Rhein re-elected Ministerpräsident.
2024–2026. Coalition governs. Both partners polling below 2023 results. AfD consolidating at 21%.
Current coalition polling: CDU 31%, SPD 15% — coalition holds a theoretical majority but CDU has not grown.
Next election: 2028.
