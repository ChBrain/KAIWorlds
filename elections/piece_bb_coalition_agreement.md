## Place
[Staatskanzlei Potsdam](place_bb_staatskanzlei_potsdam.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it SPD has no governing partner — 32 seats, majority 45.
Without it the only viable democratic majority in Brandenburg has no programme.
Without it Woidke cannot govern despite winning the election.

## Apparent
SPD and BSW. Signed November 2024.
The smallest possible coalition in Brandenburg's capped chamber — 46 of 88 seats. One above majority.
BSW's first Brandenburg government. Second BSW entry into any state government (after Thüringen).
The Friedenspolitik clause — BSW demanded language on opposing US missile stationing in Germany. The dispute echoed Thüringen. Woidke reached a compromise.
The CDU declined to participate — CDU Spitzenkandidat Redmann said his party would go into opposition and prepare for 2029.

## Yearbook
September 2024. Woidke announces SPD will seek coalition with BSW. CDU declines. No other viable option exists without AfD.
November 2024. Coalition agreement signed. SPD and BSW form government. Woidke re-elected Ministerpräsident.
The Lausitz coal transition is the coalition's largest structural challenge — the federal Kohleausstieg timeline, the retraining funds, the infrastructure investment for a region that will lose 20,000 jobs.
Current polls (April 2026): AfD 34%, SPD 24%, CDU 14%, BSW 8%. The coalition polls well below its 2024 combined result. The AfD has strengthened. The one-seat majority is precarious.
