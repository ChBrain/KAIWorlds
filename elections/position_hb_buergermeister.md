# Position: Bürgermeister Bremen

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election.md)
- **Process:** [the governing process](process_governing.md)
- **Place:** [Rathaus Bremen](place_hb_rathaus_bremen.md)

## Has
The mandate from the [Bürgerschaft Bremen](place_hb_buergerschaft_bremen.md) — elected Bürgermeister and President of the Senate.
An SPD-Green-Linke coalition with 58 of 87 seats.
Three Bundesrat seats.
The SPD's unbroken tenure as the party of the Bürgermeister since 1945 — nearly eighty years, through reconstruction, through the decline of the port-based economy, through structural transformation.
The Rathaus — the oldest governing building in continuous use in Germany. The position governs Germany's smallest state from Germany's most historic town hall.
The harbour. The steel plant. The two cities that are one state.

## Orders
Govern a state that is net dependent on federal transfers. Every major investment, every structural programme, every infrastructure project requires federal and EU funding that the position negotiates rather than commands.
Hold the left coalition together — SPD, Greens, and Linke have significant policy differences that are managed inside the coalition, not aired outside it.
Navigate the AfD's expected return in 2027. In 2023 the AfD was absent. In 2027 they will be present. The Bürger in Wut seats will be competed for directly. The political arithmetic changes.
Manage Bremerhaven — the second city, poorer, more alienated, more right-leaning, represented through the same state government that governs the university city and media city of Bremen.

## Loses
The comfortable majority.
Current polls show SPD at 22% — below its 2023 result, with CDU ahead. The position governs at the edge of its own mandate on current numbers. 2027 will require rebuilding the majority.
The Sieling precedent — the previous SPD Bürgermeister who lost his position after the 2019 shock. The position knows what happens when the SPD's long tenure ends badly.
The two-city problem. Bremerhaven is part of the state. Its poverty, its post-industrial decline, its distance from Bremen's cultural and university identity — these are the position's responsibility and its most difficult governance challenge.

## Drives
On the loss of comfortable majority —
governs through delivery — housing, port investment, school reform — rather than through ideology.
Uses Bremen's Hanseatic identity as a frame: merchant republic, self-governing, trading city. Positions the smallest German state as a laboratory for urban social policy that scales upward rather than downward from a capital.
Holds the left coalition together by keeping the Linke inside the governance structure — the precedent of 2019 (first Linke in western government) is maintained, creating a governing norm rather than an exception.
