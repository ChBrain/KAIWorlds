# Position: Voter Schleswig-Holstein
## Three mandates on three clocks. One person.

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election.md)

## Has
The right to vote in three separate elections: state, federal, European.
Each on a different cycle. Each for a different institution. Each producing a different result.
Two votes on polling day in the state election — first vote for a constituency candidate, second vote for a party list.
The [the election record](piece_sh_election_record.md) as the last record of what this position produced collectively.
No direct vote on the Bundesrat delegation — that appointment follows from the state result.
No direct vote on the European Commission — that appointment follows from the European Parliament result.
The position casts votes. It does not control what the votes produce beyond the chamber they enter.

## Orders
Vote or don't.
There is no third option on polling day.

## Loses
The connection between the vote and the consequence.
The position was certain that voting in the state election was voting on state matters.
The state vote also produces the Bundesrat delegation — four federal votes the position did not directly cast.
The federal vote produces a Bundestag whose legislation the Bundesrat then processes — using the delegation the state vote produced.
The European vote produces a parliament that cannot initiate legislation.
The position cast three votes. The chain of consequence from each is longer and less visible than the ballot paper suggested.

## Drives
On the loss of the direct connection —
forms a view from what is visible: the record, the campaign, the candidate in the constituency.
The Bundesrat consequence is rarely part of that view.
The position votes on what it can see.
The consequences it cannot see arrive anyway.
