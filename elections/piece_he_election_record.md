# Piece: Election Record Hessen

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Place:** [Landtag Wiesbaden](place_he_landtag_wiesbaden.md)

## Load Bearing
Without it the CDU's near-unbroken dominance since 1946 — interrupted only by eight years of SPD-Green government 1991–1999 — has no scale.
Without it Frankfurt's weight as the financial centre of continental Europe has no political context: the state that hosts the ECB, the Bundesbank, the Deutsche Börse, the largest airport in Germany has voted CDU in almost every election.
Without it the 2023 result has no resonance: AfD at 18.4% — at the time the best result in any western German state — and the SPD becoming CDU's junior partner for the first time in Hessen's history.
Without it the red Hessen myth — the industrial south and Frankfurt labour movement that made the SPD competitive — has nothing to stand against.

## Apparent
Hessen: 6.4 million inhabitants. 5 Bundesrat seats. Capital: Wiesbaden. The financial capital is Frankfurt, seat of the European Central Bank, the Deutsche Bundesbank, Deutsche Börse, the major German and European banks.
Current: CDU-SPD coalition. Rhein Ministerpräsident. CDU 52 seats, SPD 22, together 74 of 133. Majority: 67.
Next regular election: 2028.
Voting age: 18. 5 Bundesrat seats.

## Yearbook
**2028** — Next regular Landtag election.

**2023** — 8 October. CDU **34.6%** — up 7.6 points. AfD **18.4%** — up 5.3 points, best AfD result in any western German state to that date. SPD 15.1% — worst ever in Hessen. Greens 14.8% — down 5 points. FDP 5.0% — barely in. Linke 3.1% — out after 15 years. Turnout 66.0%. CDU chose SPD over Greens as coalition partner — first CDU-SPD government in Hessen history. SPD Spitzenkandidatin Faeser (also Bundesinnenministerin) lost her direct constituency. Rhein elected Ministerpräsident January 2024.

**2018** — 28 October. CDU 27.0%. SPD 19.8%. Greens **19.8%** — tied SPD. AfD 13.1% — first entry to Hessen Landtag. FDP 7.5%. Linke 6.3%. Turnout 67.3%. CDU-Green coalition. Volker Bouffier Ministerpräsident, then Boris Rhein (from May 2022).

**2013** — 22 September. CDU 38.3%. SPD 30.7%. Greens 11.1%. FDP 5.0%. AfD 4.1% — outside. Turnout 73.2%. CDU-Green coalition. Volker Bouffier Ministerpräsident.

**2009** — 18 January. CDU 37.2%. SPD 23.7%. Greens 13.7%. FDP 16.2% — strong FDP result. Linke 5.4%. Turnout 61.0%. CDU-FDP coalition. Roland Koch Ministerpräsident.

**2008** — 27 January. CDU 36.8%. SPD 36.7% — separated by 0.1 points. Greens 7.5%. FDP 9.4%. Linke 5.1%. Turnout 64.0%. No majority for CDU-FDP. SPD's Andrea Ypsilanti attempted red-red-green coalition — collapsed when four SPD members refused to support a Linke-tolerated government. New election January 2009.

**2003** — 2 February. CDU **48.8%** — near-absolute majority. SPD 29.1%. Greens 7.9%. FDP 7.6%. Turnout 64.6%. CDU-FDP coalition. Roland Koch.

**1999** — 7 February. CDU 43.4%. SPD 39.4%. Greens 7.2%. FDP 5.1%. Turnout 66.2%. CDU-FDP coalition. Koch defeats Schröder-era headwind. Ends eight years of SPD-Green government.

**1995** — 19 February. CDU 39.2%. SPD 38.0%. Greens 11.2%. FDP 7.4%. Turnout 66.0%. CDU-FDP coalition under Hans Eichel (SPD minority) then CDU. Contested transition.

**1991** — 20 January. CDU 40.2%. SPD **40.8%** — SPD narrowly largest. Greens 8.8%. FDP 7.4%. Turnout 65.7%. SPD-Green coalition. Hans Eichel Ministerpräsident. The only time in Hessen's history the SPD governed — eight years, 1991–1999.

**1987** — 5 April. CDU 42.1%. SPD 40.2%. Greens 9.4%. FDP 7.8%. Turnout 77.2%. CDU-FDP. Walter Wallmann.

**1983** — 25 September. CDU 45.6%. SPD 46.2% — SPD largest but CDU-FDP continues with FDP. Greens 5.9%. Turnout 80.3%. Holger Börner (SPD) then Walter Wallmann (CDU).

**1978** — 8 October. CDU 46.0%. SPD 44.5%. FDP 6.6%. Turnout 87.0%. CDU-FDP. Alfred Dregger era.

**1974** — 27 October. CDU 47.3%. SPD 43.2%. FDP 7.4%. Turnout 88.6%. CDU-FDP.

**1970** — 8 November. SPD 45.9%. CDU 39.7%. FDP 10.1%. Turnout 83.4%. SPD-FDP. Albert Osswald.

**1966** — 6 November. CDU 26.4%. SPD **51.0%** — SPD absolute majority for the first and only time in Hessen. FDP 10.4%. Turnout 74.6%. Georg August Zinn then Holger Börner SPD govern.

**1946** — 1 December. CDU 30.9%. SPD 42.7%. LDP 15.7%. KPD 10.7%. Turnout 73.4%. SPD-CDU coalition under Christian Stock (SPD) — but within two decades the CDU builds its dominance.
