## Shown
Jürgen-Fuchs-Straße 1, Erfurt.
The Plenarsaal of the Thüringer Landtag. 88 seats.
Five factions in the current chamber: AfD (32), CDU (23), BSW (15), Linke (12), SPD (6).
The AfD sits as the largest faction. It does not govern. It cannot be part of the governing majority. It holds more than a third of the seats.
Majority required: 45 votes.
The governing coalition holds 44.
The chamber that produced Kemmerich in February 2020 and Voigt in December 2024.
The Alterspräsident — the oldest member, by tradition — opens the first session of each new Landtag. In September 2024 the AfD held this role and used it as a political act. The Thüringer Verfassungsgerichtshof had to intervene before a Landtag president could be elected.
Erfurt. Population 215,000. Capital of a state of 2.1 million. The most intact medieval city centre in Germany — the Krämerbrücke, the Petersberg, the Dom and Severikirche facing each other across the Domplatz.

## Withheld

The distance between the coalition agreement and what can actually be legislated.
The Sperrminorität in action: the AfD blocking constitutional court appointments, blocking Landtag dissolution, attending every vote knowing its abstention is often enough.
What Höcke says in faction meetings that never reaches the record.
The weight of 2020 in every investiture vote.
