# Piece: Election Record Saarland

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Place:** [Landtag Saarbrücken](place_sl_landtag_saarbruecken.md)

## Load Bearing
Without it Rehlinger's 43.5% absolute majority has no scale — the SPD had been at 29.6% five years before.
Without it the CDU's 23 consecutive years of government (1999–2022) — Müller, Kramp-Karrenbauer, Hans — have no arc.
Without it the Greens missing by 23 votes has no weight.
Without it the Saarland's structural identity — steel, coal (ended 2012), automotive, the French border — has no political expression.

## Apparent
Saarland: 1.0 million inhabitants — second smallest German Flächenstaat after Bremen. 3 Bundesrat seats. Capital: Saarbrücken. France borders on two sides. Luxembourg to the north. The state was returned to West Germany in 1957 after a period of semi-independence under French administration.
Current: SPD absolute majority government. Rehlinger Ministerpräsidentin. 29 of 51 seats. AfD 9 seats, CDU 13 seats.
Next regular election: 2027.
Voting age: 18. 3 Bundesrat seats.

## Yearbook
**2027** — Next regular Landtag election.

**2022** — 27 March. SPD **43.5%** — absolute majority. CDU 28.5% — worst since 1955. AfD 5.7% — in, barely. Greens 4.99502% — out by 23 votes. FDP 4.8% — out. Linke 2.6% — out. Turnout 61.4%. SPD governs alone — first SPD majority government since 1994. Rehlinger Ministerpräsidentin.

**2017** — 26 March. CDU **40.7%**. SPD 29.6%. Linke 12.8%. AfD 6.2% — first entry. Greens 4.0% — outside. Turnout 69.7%. CDU-SPD Große Koalition. Tobias Hans Ministerpräsident from March 2018 (Kramp-Karrenbauer leaves for Berlin).

**2012** — 25 March. CDU 35.2%. SPD 30.6%. Pirates 7.4% — peak Piraten moment. Linke 16.1%. Greens 5.0%. FDP 1.2% — outside. AfD did not yet exist. Turnout 61.6%. CDU-SPD GroKo. Peter Müller then Annegret Kramp-Karrenbauer.

**2009** — 30 August. CDU 34.5%. SPD 24.5%. FDP 9.2%. Greens 5.9%. Linke 21.3% — with Oskar Lafontaine as leading figure, peak Linke. Turnout 67.6%. Jamaika-Koalition (CDU-FDP-Greens) — first in Germany. Collapsed 2012 when FDP and Greens withdrew. Müller.

**2004** — 5 September. CDU **47.5%**. SPD 30.8%. Linke/PDS 2.4% — outside without Lafontaine. FDP 5.2%. Turnout 55.5%. CDU-SPD. Müller.

**1999** — 5 September. CDU **45.5%**. SPD 44.4% — separated by 1.1 points. FDP 2.6% — outside. Greens 2.2% — outside. Turnout 68.7%. CDU governs — ending 13 years of SPD government. Müller. The only time CDU won with SPD within 1.1 points.

**1994** — 16 October. SPD **49.4%** — near-absolute majority. CDU 38.6%. Greens 5.5%. Turnout 83.5%. SPD governs alone. Oskar Lafontaine Ministerpräsident. Lafontaine leaves for Berlin federal politics in 1998.

**1990** — 28 January. SPD 54.4% — absolute majority. CDU 33.4%. Greens 2.5% — outside. Turnout 83.5%. SPD. Lafontaine Ministerpräsident.

**1985** — 10 March. SPD 49.2%. CDU 37.3%. FDP 10.0%. Turnout 81.9%. SPD-FDP then SPD alone. Johannes Hoffmann then Lafontaine.

**1980** — 27 April. SPD 45.4%. CDU 43.7%. FDP 6.4%. Turnout 85.4%. SPD-FDP. Hoffmann.

**1975** — 4 May. CDU **49.1%**. SPD 41.8%. FDP 7.4%. Turnout 84.8%. CDU-FDP. Franz Josef Röder. CDU had governed since 1959 — twenty years.

**1970** — 14 June. CDU 47.8%. SPD 40.8%. FDP 8.3%. Turnout 80.0%. CDU-FDP. Röder.

**1965** — 4 July. CDU 42.7%. SPD 40.5%. FDP 8.3%. Turnout 82.9%. CDU-FDP. Röder.

**1960** — 5 June. CDU 36.6%. SPD 30.0%. FDP 13.6%. CVP 6.9%. Turnout 85.9%. CDU coalition. Hubert Ney then Röder.

**1955** — 18 December. First Landtag election after return to West Germany. CDU 25.4%. SPD 14.3%. CVP 24.5% (Catholic regional party). DPS 23.9% (pro-German, anti-France). Turnout 88.0%. Coalition. The Saarland returns to West Germany 1 January 1957.
