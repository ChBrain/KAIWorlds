# Position: Voter Saarland

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election.md)

## Has
Two votes — Erststimme and Zweitstimme. Voting age: 18.
One vote in the federal election. One vote in the European election.
The [election record](piece_sl_election_record.md) as the accumulated history.
A state that was not Germany until 1957 — the Saar Statute, the 1955 referendum, the return to West Germany on 1 January 1957. The oldest voters carry this in lived memory. Everyone carries it in the specific identity of the Saarland as a place that was briefly not here.
The industrial memory: coal ended in 2012 with the closure of Saar Ferngas's last mine. The steel restructuring of the 1990s. The Ford Saarlouis plant that has been the largest private employer and is now transitioning.

## Orders
Vote or don't.
In 2022: 61.4% voted. The SPD's landslide was produced partly by cross-party Rehlinger support — voters who normally voted CDU or Linke chose SPD because of the candidate, not the party. This is a specific Saarland phenomenon: a small state where candidate recognition is higher than in larger states.

## Loses
The certainty of the CDU.
From 1999 to 2022 the CDU governed. For voters who were children in 1999, the Saarland had always been CDU. 2022 was the first election where that was no longer true.
The Linke. From 2009 onwards the Linke had over 12%, partly because of Lafontaine's regional following. By 2022 it was at 2.6% — gone. The Linke voter who cast the same type of vote in 2009 and 2022 experienced twenty percentage points of attrition in a single lifetime.
Those 23 votes. If you voted Green in 2022 your vote is in the 4.99502%. You voted correctly by your values. Your party is not in the Landtag. The government you produced by your Green vote is an SPD absolute majority. The arithmetic of small states.

## Drives
On the loss of structural certainty —
votes in the smallest German Flächenstaat where 23 votes in 2022 separated a two-party SPD absolute majority from a three-party coalition.
The position that voted Green knows what 4.99502% means.
The position that voted SPD governs through an absolute majority it did not choose — it voted for a party, not for solo rule.
Both positions vote again in 2027. The 23 votes are always somewhere. The position that notices this votes more carefully.
