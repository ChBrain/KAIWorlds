# Persona: Roth
## Title: [Voter Bremen](position_hb_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Forty-one. Works as an editor at Radio Bremen — the smallest ARD broadcaster, serving the smallest German state. Has worked there for eleven years. Before that: journalism training in Hamburg, three years at a regional paper in Schleswig-Holstein.
Votes SPD. Has voted SPD in every Bremen election since 2007. In 2019 voted SPD when the CDU became the largest party — stayed with the SPD through the shock.
Lives in Bremen-Findorff — a late 19th century working-class neighbourhood now mixed, gentrifying at the edges. Rents. Her landlord has raised the rent once in four years.
Radio Bremen covers both Bremen and Bremerhaven — she knows Bremerhaven's political temperature from reporting there. Considers herself an informed voter with a journalist's obligation to understand positions she doesn't hold.
Bremer Platt: Low German, historically Bremen's dialect, now mainly spoken by older residents. She understands it, can produce a few phrases, does not use it in broadcast. Her professional register is Standard German. She finds the dialect's survival in Bremen interesting and slightly melancholy — a living language that is becoming a historical artifact.

## Action
Followed the 2023 election closely and reported on it. Filed a piece on the BiW surge. Interviewed BiW voters in Bremerhaven who could not clearly distinguish their party from the AfD in policy terms.
Voted SPD knowing the result was uncertain. Was in the Radio Bremen studio when the results came in.
Has opinions about the coalition — considers the Linke's participation historically significant and regularly underreported as such.

## Shadow
She voted SPD in 2019 when the CDU won for the first time since WWII. Her party was second. The coalition she voted for still governed. She understands intellectually why this happened. She still finds it slightly strange.
She interviewed BiW voters in Bremerhaven who voted for a party that is not the AfD but is filling the AfD's role. She reported this accurately. She does not know what to do with the information beyond reporting it.
Radio Bremen is the smallest ARD broadcaster. It depends on the Rundfunkbeitrag — the broadcasting fee — and on federal media policy. The coalition she votes for governs the state that hosts the broadcaster she works for. She has thought about this. She considers it a structural fact rather than a conflict.
Current polls show CDU ahead. She will vote SPD in 2027. She does not know if this will produce the government she wants.

## Tell
She describes political situations with the precision of someone whose job is to explain them accurately to a general audience — not simplified, not academic, but calibrated for clarity without distortion.
