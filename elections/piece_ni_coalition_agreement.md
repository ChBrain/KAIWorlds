## Place
[Staatskanzlei Hannover](place_ni_staatskanzlei_hannover.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it the SPD has no governing partner — 57 seats, majority 74.
Without it the return to red-green after five years of SPD-CDU Große Koalition has no record.
Without it the VW conflict — shareholder interest versus Green coalition commitments on the combustion engine transition — has no governing framework.

## Apparent
SPD and Greens. Signed early November 2022. Weil third term.
81 of 146 seats — seven above majority.
The coalition was arithmetically available from election night — SPD 57, Greens 24. The SPD-CDU Große Koalition that had governed since 2017 ended with the CDU's worst result since 1955 and Althusmann's immediate resignation.
The VW tension is structural: the Greens support accelerated combustion engine phase-out; Niedersachsen holds 20% of VW and has labour obligations to Wolfsburg that run in the opposite direction. The coalition agreement manages this by supporting electrification timelines while protecting Niedersachsen's shareholder position.

## Yearbook
9 October 2022. SPD and Greens have majority. Weil announces coalition talks.
Late October 2022. Coalition agreement concluded in three weeks — one of the fastest state coalition formations.
8 November 2022. Weil elected Ministerpräsident for third time.
2024. VW plant closure discussions — Volkswagen announces potential German plant closures, triggering Niedersachsen's shareholder role and labour conflict. The coalition's structural tension becomes publicly visible.
Current polls (April 2026): CDU 27%, SPD 26% — nearly equal. AfD 21%. Greens 10%. The red-green majority would not be reproduced on current polling.
Next election: 2027.
