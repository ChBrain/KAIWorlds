# Piece: Election Record Baden-Württemberg

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Place:** [Landtag Stuttgart](place_bw_landtag_stuttgart.md)

## Load Bearing
Without it the Kretschmann era has no scale — 15 years, three terms, the longest-serving Green Ministerpräsident in German history, in a state the CDU considered its own for sixty years.
Without it the 2026 result has no context — the first election under new rules, the first without Kretschmann, Özdemir winning by 0.5 percentage points.
Without it the CDU's twenty years of absolute majority looks like normal politics rather than the structural dominance it was.
Remove it and BW looks like a Green state. With it, it looks like a CDU state that became something else.

## Apparent
Baden-Württemberg: 11.1 million inhabitants. 5 Bundesrat seats. Landtag in Stuttgart.
Founded 25 April 1952 from three predecessor states: Württemberg-Baden, (Süd-)Baden, Württemberg-Hohenzollern.
Until 2026: one vote only — uniquely among German states. Since 2026: Erststimme and Zweitstimme. Voting age 16 from 2026.
Current: Grüne 56 seats, CDU 56 seats, AfD 35 seats, SPD 10 seats. Majority: 79. Grün-Schwarz coalition forming. Cem Özdemir Ministerpräsident-designate.
Next regular election: 2031.

## Yearbook
**2031** — Next regular Landtag election.

**2026** — 8 March. First election with two votes and voting age 16. Grüne **30.2%** — 56 seats. CDU 29.7% — 56 seats. AfD **18.8%** — nearly doubled from 2021, best AfD result in any western German state. SPD **5.5%** — worst SPD result in any German state ever. FDP 4.4% — outside for the first time since the state's founding in 1952. Linke 4.4% — outside. Turnout 70.2%. Kretschmann did not stand — end of fifteen years. Özdemir (Grüne) vs Hagel (CDU) — Özdemir won by 0.5 points. Both parties received exactly 56 seats. A Grün-Schwarz coalition is the only viable option. CDU made extensive demands before entering negotiations.

**2021** — 14 March. Grüne **32.6%** — best Green result ever in any German state. CDU 24.1% — worst CDU result ever in BW. SPD 11.0%. FDP 10.5%. AfD 9.7%. Linke 3.6% — outside. Turnout 63.8%. Kretschmann third term. Grün-Schwarz coalition continues. The CDU spitzenkandidatin Eisenmann did not enter the Landtag — BW then had no party list, only constituency mandates, and she lost her seat. The old electoral system had no safety net for losing candidates.

**2016** — 13 March. Grüne 30.3%. CDU 27.0%. AfD 15.1% — first entry. SPD 12.7%. FDP 8.3%. Turnout 70.4%. Kretschmann second term. Switches coalition partner — drops SPD, takes CDU. First Grün-Schwarz coalition in Germany.

**2011** — 27 March. Grüne **24.2%** — first time Greens lead a German state government. CDU 39.0% — loses power after 58 years. SPD 23.1%. FDP 5.3%. Turnout 66.3%. Kretschmann becomes the first Green Ministerpräsident in German history. Grün-Rot coalition with SPD. The trigger: Stuttgart 21 — the controversial underground railway project that brought hundreds of thousands onto the streets and defined BW politics for years.

**2006** — 26 March. CDU 44.2%. SPD 25.2%. Greens 11.7%. FDP 10.7%. Turnout 53.4% — historic low. CDU-FDP coalition. Günther Oettinger Ministerpräsident.

**2001** — 25 March. CDU 44.8%. SPD 33.3%. Greens 7.7%. FDP 8.1%. Turnout 62.6%. CDU-FDP. Erwin Teufel Ministerpräsident.

**1996** — 24 March. CDU 41.3%. SPD 25.1%. Greens 12.1%. FDP 9.6%. Republicans 9.1% — far-right party, in Landtag 1992–2001. Turnout 67.5%. CDU-SPD then CDU-FDP. Teufel.

**1992** — 5 April. CDU 39.6%. SPD 29.4%. Greens 9.5%. FDP 5.9%. Republicans **10.9%** — extreme right, first entry. CDU absolute majority ends after twenty years — Republicans split the conservative vote. CDU-SPD grand coalition. Teufel.

**1988** — 20 March. CDU 49.0%. SPD 32.0%. Greens 7.9%. FDP 5.9%. CDU absolute majority. Lothar Späth Ministerpräsident.

**1984** — 25 March. CDU 51.9%. SPD 32.4%. Greens 8.0%. CDU absolute majority. Späth.

**1980** — 16 March. CDU 53.4%. SPD 32.5%. Greens 5.3% — first entry into Landtag. FDP 8.3%. CDU absolute majority. Späth.

**1976** — 4 April. CDU **56.7%** — best CDU result ever in BW, one of the highest results for any party in any German state election. SPD 33.3%. CDU absolute majority. Hans Filbinger Ministerpräsident.

**1972** — 23 April. CDU 52.9%. SPD **37.6%** — best SPD result ever in BW. CDU absolute majority begins — twenty unbroken years. Filbinger.

**1968** — 28 April. CDU 44.2%. SPD 29.0%. FDP/DVP 14.4%. CDU-SPD grand coalition. Kurt Georg Kiesinger then Hans Filbinger.

**1964** — 26 April. CDU 45.8%. SPD 27.7%. FDP/DVP 13.1%. CDU-SPD. Kiesinger.

**1960** — 15 May. CDU 46.9%. SPD 28.3%. FDP/DVP 18.7%. CDU-FDP. Gebhard Müller then Kiesinger.

**1956** — 4 March. CDU 41.8%. SPD 28.2%. FDP/DVP 13.0%. BHE 8.4%. CDU-FDP/DVP. Müller.

**1952** — 9 March. Verfassunggebende Versammlung. CDU 36.1%. SPD 28.2%. FDP/DVP 18.0%. BHE 9.1%. CDU largest party but FDP's Reinhold Maier forms the first government — SPD-FDP-BHE coalition, explicitly excluding the CDU. The only time in BW history the CDU won the election and did not lead the government. CDU governs from November 1953 under Gebhard Müller.
