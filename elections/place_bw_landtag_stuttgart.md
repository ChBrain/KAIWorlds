## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Shown
Konrad-Adenauer-Straße 3, Stuttgart. Built 1957–1961. Designed by Kurt Viertel. A functionalist building with a circular plenary chamber — the round form chosen deliberately to avoid any sense of a government side facing an opposition side across a divide.
157 seats currently — 56 Grüne, 56 CDU, 35 AfD, 10 SPD. Majority: 79.
From 1952 to 2026: one vote only per voter, no party list. A unique system in Germany. From 2026: Erststimme and Zweitstimme, voting age 16, matching the Bundestag model.
Five Bundesrat seats. Appointed from the Landesregierung.
The circular chamber held twenty years of CDU absolute majorities, fifteen years of Kretschmann, and now the first election result where Grüne and CDU have identical seat counts.

## Offers
Legislation in Baden-Württemberg's domains — education, police, culture, agriculture, regional planning.
Election of the Ministerpräsident.
Five Bundesrat seats in Berlin — BW is the fourth largest state by population, a net contributor to the Länderfinanzausgleich, and a significant industrial economy: Mercedes-Benz, Bosch, Porsche, SAP, Carl Zeiss are all headquartered here.
Stuttgart 21 is still under construction.

## Withheld
The 56-56 deadlock as a governing condition — two parties with identical seat counts must agree on everything before anything can pass. The CDU's post-election demands for structural protections against being outvoted.
What the AfD's 35 seats mean in a chamber where Grün-Schwarz has 112 — enough for a majority but not enough for constitutional changes requiring two-thirds.
The distance between the BW that voted CDU at 56% in 1976 and the BW that voted AfD at 18.8% in 2026. Both are the same electorate, fifty years apart.
