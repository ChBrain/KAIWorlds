# Position: Ministerpräsident Niedersachsen

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election.md)
- **Process:** [the governing process](process_governing.md)
- **Place:** [Staatskanzlei Hannover](place_ni_staatskanzlei_hannover.md)

## Has
The mandate from the [Landtag Hannover](place_ni_landtag_hannover.md) — won in third investiture, 8 November 2022.
A SPD-Green coalition with 81 of 146 seats — seven above majority.
Six Bundesrat seats — the maximum. The most consequential state executive position outside NRW by seat count.
A seat on the Volkswagen supervisory board as the 20% shareholder's representative. The position sits between its shareholders' obligation to VW's profitability and its coalition obligation to the Greens' automotive transition commitments. These are not always reconcilable.
The SPD's strongest western state — Niedersachsen has voted SPD in most elections since 1959. The position is the institutional expression of that alignment.

## Orders
Govern the second-largest German state by area across three distinct economic geographies: the industrial north (Hannover, Wolfsburg, Salzgitter), the agricultural east and south, the Catholic west (Emsland, Vechta) that votes CDU at 40–46%.
Navigate the VW restructuring — plant closure discussions, the combustion engine transition, the 20% shareholder's obligations when the company and the coalition point in different directions.
Hold six Bundesrat seats aligned on a SPD-Green coalition position in a Bundesrat that now has a CDU-led majority after the Ampel's collapse.
Decide whether to run for a fourth term in 2027.

## Loses
The red-green majority in the national direction of travel.
The Ampel fell in 2024. The SPD governs in Berlin as junior partner to CDU now. The position's six Bundesrat votes support policies from a SPD-Green state that are increasingly isolated in a CDU-led federal government's direction.
The VW seat as a clean position — holding 20% of a company undergoing restructuring, with a Green coalition partner, with 15,000 Wolfsburg jobs at stake, produces decisions that cannot satisfy all obligations simultaneously. Whatever the position decides on the supervisory board, something is being conceded.

## Drives
On the irreconcilable obligations —
governs by sequencing — not resolving the VW-Green tension but managing when each obligation takes precedence in which specific decision.
Uses the six Bundesrat seats as the primary federal instrument — when the state cannot move the coalition nationally through government, it moves legislation through the Bundesrat.
The Hannover Messe as a symbol: a state that invented the trade fair as a form of economic diplomacy can hold contradictions without resolving them, and call this normal.
