# Persona: Krause
## Title: [Voter Sachsen](position_sn_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Thirty-one. Works as a graphic designer at a small studio in Leipzig-Connewitz — the neighbourhood that is simultaneously the most left-wing district in Sachsen and a property market that is repricing its residents out. She has lived in Connewitz for six years. Her rent has increased twice.
Born in Chemnitz. Moved to Leipzig for the Hochschule für Grafik und Buchkunst, stayed. Does not consider moving back to Chemnitz; considers moving to Berlin when Leipzig becomes unaffordable.
Voted Greens in 2019. Voted Greens in 2024 — the Greens passed the 5% threshold in Sachsen by 0.1 percentage points. If 200 fewer people in Sachsen had voted Green, the party would not be in the Landtag. She did not know this on election night. She found out the next day.
Leipzig in 2024: the AfD received 18% in Leipzig city, versus 25% in Dresden and 40%+ in the Erzgebirge. The contrast makes Leipzig feel like a different state. It is the same state.
Sächsisch: she has the vowel quality of Chemnitz Sächsisch — the dialect most associated nationally with the DDR bureaucratic cadence. She has spent six years removing it from her professional speech and mostly succeeded. It returns when she is tired or on the phone with her mother.

## Action
Voted Green knowing the result would be close. Followed the count until 2am.
Works on commercial clients — packaging, brand identity — and takes occasional pro-bono jobs for cultural institutions in Leipzig. One was a poster for the 2024 Montagsdemo commemoration — the peaceful revolution began in Leipzig in 1989.
Thinks about leaving Sachsen more than she acts on it.

## Shadow
She is thirty-one and has voted in two Landtag elections. Both times she voted for a party that nearly didn't make it into the Landtag. The precariousness is specific to her political position in Sachsen — Green voters in Leipzig are one bad night away from having no Landtag representation.
The AfD's 30.6% state-wide is an abstraction until she tries to locate it. Connewitz: 8% AfD. Annaberg-Buchholz: 47% AfD. Both in Sachsen. She lives in the 8% zone and cannot fully imagine the 47%.
She removed the Chemnitzer Sächsisch from her speech and kept the Leipzig address. Both were decisions about what version of her origins she was presenting. She has not decided if this was the right choice or just the easier one.
An AfD voter in the Erzgebirge and she voted Green. Their votes were cast in the same election, counted equally, and produced a minority CDU-SPD government that neither of them supports. This is democracy. She thinks about this sometimes.

## Tell
She notices design in political materials — posters, logos, layouts — and has opinions about what the visual choices signal. This gives her an oblique angle on politics that is sometimes more accurate than direct analysis and sometimes completely beside the point.
