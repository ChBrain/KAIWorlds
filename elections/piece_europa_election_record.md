# Piece: Election Record Europaparlament — Deutschland

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Place:** [Europaparlament Strasbourg](place_europaparlament_strassburg.md)

## Load Bearing
Without it the European Parliament election has no German history — just a single result floating without trajectory.
Without it the Greens' 20.5% surge in 2019 and their collapse to 11.9% in 2024 has no pattern.
Without it the AfD's entry to the European Parliament in 2019 and their consolidation in 2024 has no arc.
Without it the structural difference between how Germans vote federally and how they vote European — more protest, more small parties, higher Green results historically — has no record.

## Apparent
Germany sends 96 MEPs to the European Parliament — the largest national contingent. Germany votes as a single national constituency — no regional constituencies, no separate Wahlbereiche. All German parties above the EP threshold (no minimum threshold since 2014 when the Constitutional Court struck down the 3% and 5% thresholds) compete nationwide.
Voting age: 16 from 2024.
The European Parliament election in Germany has historically been used as a Denkzettelwahl — a protest vote against the federal government — more than as a genuinely European vote. In 2024: 55% of voters said Bundespolitik was decisive for their choice; only 38% named Europapolitik.
Next European Parliament election: June 2029.

## Yearbook
**2029** — Next European Parliament election.

**2024** — 9 June. CDU/CSU **30.0%**. AfD 15.9% — doubled since 2019. SPD **13.9%** — worst EP result ever, below federal 2021 result. Greens **11.9%** — collapsed from 20.5%, worst since 1994. BSW **6.2%** — first EP election. FDP 5.2%. Linke 2.7%. Freie Wähler 2.7%. Volt 2.6%. Turnout 64.8% — highest since 1994. Germany returns 96 MEPs. AfD is second largest German bloc in EP, joining the far-right ESN (Europe of Sovereign Nations) group. The Greens' collapse is the single largest swing — minus 8.6 percentage points. The Denkzettel against the Ampel is the dominant interpretation: 55% voted on Bundespolitik, not Europapolitik.

**2019** — 26 May. CDU/CSU 28.9%. **Greens 20.5%** — best European result, second largest German party in EP. SPD 15.8%. AfD 11.0% — first EP election. Linke 5.5%. FDP 5.4%. Freie Wähler 2.2%. Tierschutzpartei 1.4%. Turnout 61.4% — up sharply from 2014 (48.1%). 96 MEPs. The Fridays for Future wave drove Green youth mobilisation. CDU in EPP group, Greens in Greens/EFA, SPD in S&D, AfD in Identity and Democracy (ID) group.

**2014** — 25 May. CDU/CSU 35.3%. SPD 27.3%. Greens 10.7%. Linke 7.4%. AfD **7.1%** — first EP election. FDP 3.4% — outside threshold. Turnout **48.1%** — lowest in German EP election history. 96 MEPs. The Constitutional Court struck down Germany's 3% EP threshold in 2011 — no minimum threshold from 2014, allowing small parties to win single seats.

**2009** — 7 June. CDU/CSU 37.9%. SPD 20.8% — worst EP result for SPD to that date. Greens 12.1%. FDP 11.0%. Linke 7.5%. Turnout 43.3% — at the time the lowest in German EP history. 99 MEPs.

**2004** — 13 June. CDU/CSU 44.5%. SPD 21.5%. Greens 11.9%. FDP 6.1%. Linke/PDS 6.1%. REP 1.9%. Turnout 43.0%. 99 MEPs. The Schröder government at its nadir — Hartz reforms, Iraq split. The Denkzettel effect at its clearest.

**1999** — 13 June. CDU/CSU 48.7%. SPD 30.7%. Greens 6.4%. FDP 3.0% — outside threshold. PDS 5.8%. Turnout 45.2%. 99 MEPs. CDU peak in EP elections — Kohl's final legacy in European voting.

**1994** — 12 June. CDU/CSU 38.8%. SPD 32.2%. Greens 10.1%. FDP 4.1%. REP 3.9%. Turnout **60.0%** — highest since 1979. 99 MEPs. The REP (Republikaner) far-right party peaks before fading.

**1989** — 18 June. CDU/CSU 37.8%. SPD 37.3%. Greens 8.4%. REP **7.1%** — far-right surge, Berlin Wall still standing. FDP 5.6%. Turnout 62.4%. 81 MEPs. The REP result was the first major far-right EP result in Germany. The Wall falls five months later.

**1984** — 17 June. CDU/CSU 45.9%. SPD 37.4%. Greens 8.2%. FDP 4.8% — outside threshold. Turnout 56.8%. 81 MEPs.

**1979** — 10 June. First direct European Parliament election. CDU/CSU 49.2%. SPD 40.8%. Greens/AUD 3.2% — outside. FDP 6.0%. Turnout **65.7%** — first time, high engagement. 81 MEPs. Helmut Kohl's CDU swept the EP election in the same year the SPD's Schmidt governed federally. The European election has never tracked the federal result precisely.
