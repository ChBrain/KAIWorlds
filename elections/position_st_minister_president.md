# Position: Ministerpräsident Sachsen-Anhalt

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election.md)
- **Process:** [the governing process](process_governing.md)
- **Place:** [Staatskanzlei Magdeburg](place_st_staatskanzlei_magdeburg.md)

## Has
The mandate from the [Landtag Magdeburg](place_st_landtag_magdeburg.md) — won on second ballot in 2021.
The Deutschland-Koalition: CDU-SPD-FDP, 58 of 97 seats.
Four Bundesrat seats.
The Haseloff brand — the personal approval that in 2021 pushed the CDU from 24% to 37% by mobilising voters who came out specifically to prevent AfD first place. Haseloff is not running again.
A state with 2.2 million people and population decline in most of its territory outside Magdeburg and Halle. Luther's landscape, the Harz, the chemical industry — these are the resources.

## Orders
Govern to September 2026 and hand over to whoever follows.
Manage the transition question — no CDU successor has Haseloff's personal standing. The position's final months are spent finding a candidate who can hold CDU votes and prevent the AfD from winning outright.
Hold four Bundesrat seats aligned on eastern state priorities — infrastructure, fiscal transfers, the coal transition.

## Loses
The succession.
The position ends in September 2026. It does not choose its successor. The CDU must find a candidate who can replicate what Haseloff did — but the personal authority was not transferable by appointment.
The seven CDU members who did not vote for Haseloff in the first ballot in 2021. They are still in the caucus. They will vote in September. Their votes will go somewhere.
The AfD at 38% in current polls is the measure of what five years of stable government in Sachsen-Anhalt produced: the CDU fell twelve points. Stability was not the answer the voters were looking for.

## Drives
On the loss of the succession —
governs symbolically — making the state legible as something worth governing democratically.
The Luther connection is not ironic: Haseloff has invoked the moral weight of the Reformation territory repeatedly. The position understands itself as defending something older than the party.
Leaves the Staatskanzlei with a balanced budget, a working coalition record, and an open question about what comes next.
