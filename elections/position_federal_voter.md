# Position: Voter Bundesrepublik Deutschland

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election_bundestag.md)

## Has
Two votes in the Bundestagswahl — Erststimme for a direct candidate, Zweitstimme for a party list. Voting age: 18.
One vote in the European Parliament election.
The [federal election record](piece_federal_election_record.md) as the accumulated history of the republic — 1949 to 2025.
Geography as political identity: the east-west divide produces different party systems in the same election. The AfD at 20.8% nationally scored over 30% in Sachsen, Thüringen, Sachsen-Anhalt, and Brandenburg. CDU/CSU at 28.5% nationally scored over 35% in Bavaria. SPD at 16.4% nationally scored above that only in a handful of western states.
The knowledge that the BSW missed the Bundestag by 13,000 votes — the vote that was cast for a party that did not enter the chamber is the vote whose absence made the CDU/CSU-SPD majority possible.

## Orders
Vote or don't.
In 2025: 82.5% voted — the fourth consecutive increase in turnout since the historic low of 70.8% in 2009. The Ampel's collapse, the vorgezogene Neuwahl, the AfD's surge, and Elon Musk's interventions on X all contributed to a mobilisation that exceeded any election since 1998.
The Zweitstimme decides the government. The Erststimme sends a person to Berlin. The 2023 electoral reform means winning a constituency no longer guarantees a seat if the party's Zweitstimme doesn't support it — 23 direct winners did not enter the Bundestag in 2025.

## Loses
The FDP.
In 2021 the FDP got 11.4% and entered government. In 2025 it got 4.3% and left government and the Bundestag. The same party, many of the same voters, four years apart. The governing responsibility destroyed what the opposition position had built.
The Ampel.
Three parties formed a government in 2021 that 59% of voters had chosen. The government collapsed in 2024. The parties that survived electorally (SPD, Greens) survived damaged. The party that triggered the collapse (FDP) did not survive at all.
The certainty of the two-party system.
In 1957 the two largest parties took 82% of the vote. In 2025 they took 44.9%. The rest — AfD, Greens, Linke — hold 323 seats between them. The Bundestag of 2025 is a six-party parliament where the governing majority is arithmetically fragile and the opposition includes a party classified as partially right-wing extremist.

## Drives
On the loss of the two-party field —
votes in a six-party Bundestag where 44.9% goes to the two governing parties and 55.1% goes elsewhere.
The position that voted BSW at 4.97% sent no one to Berlin and made the CDU/CSU-SPD majority possible.
The position that voted Tierschutzpartei at 1.4% sent one MEP to Brussels but no one to Berlin.
The position holds the vote as the one act it controls in a system it does not control.
It votes. It counts. The counting is not the same as governing. The position knows this and votes anyway.
