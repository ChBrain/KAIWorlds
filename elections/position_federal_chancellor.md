# Position: Bundeskanzler

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election_bundestag.md)
- **Process:** [the governing process](process_governing_bundestag.md)
- **Place:** [Reichstag Berlin](place_reichstag_berlin.md)

## Has
The mandate from the Bundestag — won in second ballot, 6 May 2025.
A CDU/CSU-SPD coalition with 328 of 630 seats — 12 above majority.
The Kabinett: 17 ministers, CDU/CSU-SPD divided.
The AfD as permanent structural opposition — 152 seats, the largest opposition bloc in Bundestag history.
The Bundesrat — now CDU-led at the state level, aligned with the federal government for the first time since 2013. Consent legislation can pass without the SPD-led state opposition blocking it.
The Schuldenbremse — the constitutional debt brake that limits federal deficit spending, now under intense pressure from defence and infrastructure investment demands following Ukraine and NATO commitments.
The longest-governing democracy in German history with an unbroken record since 1949. The position is the 76-year institutional heir to a constitutional founding that began as an emergency measure.

## Orders
Govern the Federal Republic from a coalition that commands 52% of Bundestag seats — working majority, not comfortable.
Hold the Schuldenbremse or modify it — the pressure from defence spending (NATO 2% target, Ukraine commitments), infrastructure investment (Sondervermögen debates), and energy transition costs runs directly against the constitutional debt limit. The position has committed to fiscal discipline while the coalition agreement includes investment programmes that test it.
Navigate the AfD's 152 seats — the largest opposition bloc ever, classified as gesichert rechtsextrem at the federal level, present in every committee, visible in every debate. The position governs without the AfD and cannot govern with it.
Hold the SPD as a junior coalition partner at 16.4% — the worst federal SPD result in history produces a partner with limited leverage but with finance, justice, and defence ministries as compensation.

## Loses
The first-ballot majority.
Six coalition members did not vote for Merz in the first investiture ballot. He needed 316 and got 310. He won the second. The six are still in the coalition. Their votes are needed in every budget and every major legislative vote.
The AfD containment.
The position has constructed a Brandmauer — a firewall — against governing with or through AfD votes. In January 2025 (pre-election) the CDU passed a migration motion with AfD votes in the outgoing Bundestag, producing a national crisis. The position owns that decision. The Brandmauer is now the primary political commitment, not a policy position but an institutional one.

## Drives
On the loss of the first-ballot majority —
governs through the formal coalition structure, not through personal authority.
Uses the Bundesrat alignment — CDU-led majority at state level — as a governance accelerator. Federal legislation that previously required years of Bundesrat negotiation can now proceed faster.
The Sondervermögen for defence and infrastructure — a special fund mechanism that allows borrowing outside the Schuldenbremse for specific designated purposes — as the financial instrument for reconciling constitutional constraint with investment need.
