## Shown
Platz der Republik, Berlin.
Built 1884–1894. Burned 1933. Divided 1945. Restored 1999 by Norman Foster.
The inscription above the west portal: *Dem Deutschen Volke*. Added in 1916. It took decades of argument to get there.
630 seats in the plenary chamber. Reichstag blue — chosen in the 1990s for political neutrality. No party had blue as its colour at the time.
The glass dome above the chamber. Open to the public. Visitors walk a spiral ramp and look down into the plenary below. The people above the government — by design. The mirrors in the cone reflect light down into the chamber and sight lines up to the gallery.
The Bundestag sits here since 1999. For fifty years it sat in Bonn. The move was decided by 338 votes to 320.
299 constituencies across Germany. 11 of them in Schleswig-Holstein.
