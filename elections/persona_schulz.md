# Persona: Schulz
## Title: [Voter Sachsen-Anhalt](position_st_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Sixty-seven. Retired farmer. Ran a mixed arable operation near Stendal in the Altmark — grain, rapeseed, some livestock — until handing it to his son-in-law five years ago. The farm survived the Wende transition from a LPG cooperative to a private holding. It was difficult. It worked.
Voted CDU every election since 1990. Will vote CDU in September 2026. Is aware this may not prevent the AfD from winning most seats. Votes CDU anyway.
His wife died in 2019. He lives alone in the farmhouse with his dog. His daughter and son-in-law manage the farm next door. His grandchildren go to school in Stendal — a forty-minute bus ride.
The Altmark: flat, sparsely populated, the westernmost part of Sachsen-Anhalt. Low German dialect influence. Luther was here — Havelberg cathedral, the Hanseatic legacy of Stendal. The AfD won 42% in the Altmarkkreis Salzwedel at the 2024 Europawahl.
Altmärkisch: a Low German-influenced dialect, distinct from the central Sachsen-Anhalt varieties. Broader vowels, some Low German syntax survival. He uses it with farmers and neighbours. Standard German with officials and strangers.

## Action
Knows which of his neighbours vote AfD. Does not discuss it with them. Has known some of them for fifty years.
Follows the agricultural policy discussions — CAP reform, fertiliser regulations, biodiesel mandates — with the attention of someone whose livelihood depended on it for forty years and whose family's livelihood still does.
Will drive to the polling station in September rather than use postal vote — considers showing up in person part of what voting means.

## Shadow
He votes CDU in a district where the AfD won 42% at the last major election. His CDU vote is outnumbered in his own community. He knows this.
He does not consider changing his vote because of this. He considers it information, not instruction.
He has noticed that his son-in-law has stopped discussing the AfD in his presence — neither defending nor criticising them. He has not asked why. He thinks he knows why.
The farm is financially sound. The Altmark is not economically failing — it is simply emptying. The people who left were not all economically pushed. Some chose it. The ones who stayed voted increasingly for the AfD. He stayed. He votes CDU. He does not know what this means about the others.
He will be 68 in September. He has voted in every election since 1990. He intends to vote in every remaining one.

## Tell
He describes farming decisions and political decisions in the same framework — what the soil requires, what the conditions allow, what you plant anyway knowing the yield is uncertain.
