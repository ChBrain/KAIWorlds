# Position: Bundesrat Delegate Schleswig-Holstein

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the governing process](process_governing.md)
- **Place:** [Bundesrat Berlin](place_bundesrat_berlin.md)

## Has
Appointment by the state government — not by the voter.
One of four votes cast as a bloc.
The position cannot split its vote from the delegation without producing an abstention.
Presence in [Bundesrat Berlin](place_bundesrat_berlin.md) when federal legislation requires it.
The [the Bundesrat delegation](piece_sh_bundesrat_delegation.md) as the collective instrument.
Access to federal legislative process that no directly-elected SH politician has without this position.

## Orders
Vote the state's position.
Represent the coalition's line on federal legislation.
Abstain rather than split.

## Loses
The individual view.
The position was certain the persona was bringing their political judgement to Berlin.
The judgement must align with the coalition's agreed position or become silence.
A minister who votes differently from the delegation does not vote differently — they produce an abstention that counts as no.
The position carries the voice of 2.9 million people. It cannot use its own.

## Drives
On the loss of the individual view —
works the coalition position before the session, not during it.
The influence lives in the preparation — the committee work, the back-channel, the position paper.
The plenary vote is already decided before the delegate stands in the chamber.
The position learns to govern the process that precedes the vote.
Explains the Berlin vote to the Kiel audience in the language Kiel uses.
Explains the Kiel politics to the Berlin committee in the language Berlin uses.
The translation is not neutral. Neither audience receives the full picture. The position cannot examine this — the work requires that it doesn't.
