# Position: Regierender Bürgermeister Berlin

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election.md)
- **Process:** [the governing process](process_governing.md)
- **Place:** [Rotes Rathaus Berlin](place_be_rotes_rathaus.md)

## Has
The mandate from the [Abgeordnetenhaus Berlin](place_be_abgeordnetenhaus.md) — obtained in the third ballot with exactly the coalition's seat count. No margin.
The Senat: ten Senators, five CDU, five SPD.
Four Bundesrat seats in Berlin — a short walk from the Rotes Rathaus.
The title: not Ministerpräsident. Regierender Bürgermeister — governing mayor. The title encodes the dual nature of the position: mayor of a city that is also a state.
The fiscal reality: Berlin is a net recipient of the Länderfinanzausgleich. The position governs a city that spends more than it earns and has done so for decades.
The symbolic weight: governing the capital of Germany, the city where the Wall ran, where the Reichstag burned, where the Basic Law was written in a city that was then divided.

## Orders
Govern a city-state of 3.7 million in which housing costs, public transport, schools, and migration are simultaneously the most contested political questions and the most visible daily experiences of the population.
Maintain the coalition — 86 seats, majority 80, no margin for defection.
Cast four Bundesrat votes as a bloc from the same city where the Bundesrat meets.
Represent Berlin's interests at the federal level without confusing them with federal governance — the Bundeskanzler governs from Berlin; the Regierende Bürgermeister governs Berlin.

## Loses
The confusion.
Every visitor to Berlin, every journalist, every diplomat who arrives in the federal capital assumes that the most powerful political figure in the city is the federal government. The position is not the federal government. The Bundeskanzleramt is three kilometres up the Spree.
The position governs housing and the BVG and the Philharmoniker. The Bundeskanzler governs foreign policy and defence and the federal budget. They share a city. They do not share a government.
The prestige is inverted: the federal capital's head of government has less formal power than the Ministerpräsident of NRW, fewer Bundesrat seats than Bayern, and a smaller budget than Baden-Württemberg.

## Drives
On the loss of that confusion —
uses the symbolic weight of the capital to punch above the position's formal weight.
Berlin's Regierende Bürgermeister is the most internationally visible state head of government in Germany. Willy Brandt held it. Klaus Wowereit held it. The position carries history the title alone does not explain.
