## Shown
Niederkirchnerstraße 5, Berlin-Mitte. Former Prussian Landtag building, 1897. Adjacent to the Topographie des Terrors — the documentation centre on the site of the Gestapo and SS headquarters. The Martin-Gropius-Bau is across the street. The Potsdamer Platz is a five-minute walk.
Not called Landtag. Not called Parlament. Abgeordnetenhaus — house of deputies.
Minimum 130 seats. Current: 159 seats with Überhang and Ausgleich. CDU 52, SPD 34, Greens 34, Linke 22, AfD 17.
Majority: 80.
CDU-SPD coalition: 86 seats. Wegner elected in third ballot — 86 votes — on 27 April 2023.
Voting age: 16. One of six German states where 16-year-olds vote in state elections.
The 2021 election held here was declared fully invalid by the Berliner Verfassungsgerichtshof — the only time a German state election has been annulled and rerun in full. The rerun took place 12 February 2023.
4 Bundesrat seats. Sent from the Senat.

## Offers
Legislation in Berlin's state domains — education, police, housing law, public transport, culture.
Election of the Regierender Bürgermeister.
The four Bundesrat seats — Berlin's voice in federal legislation, cast from the Senat.
The scrutiny function. Committee work. The public gallery.
The building itself: the former Prussian Landtag, where the Nazis were legally empowered in 1933, now housing a state parliament 90 years later. The Topographie des Terrors is visible from the windows.

## Withheld
The Federal Republic's institutions — Bundestag, Bundesrat, Bundesregierung, Bundespräsident — are all in Berlin but none of them are of the Abgeordnetenhaus. They share the city without sharing governance.
What it means to govern a city that is also the capital of the country it is part of — Berlin's state interests and Germany's federal interests are not the same and sometimes conflict directly.
The distance between West and East Berlin within the city — voting patterns still diverge significantly along the old border thirty-five years after reunification.
