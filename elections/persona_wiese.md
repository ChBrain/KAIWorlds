# Persona: Wiese
## Title: [Voter Niedersachsen](position_ni_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Forty-seven. Works as a production team leader at the Volkswagen main plant in Wolfsburg — Halle 54, Golf assembly. Has worked at VW since his apprenticeship at nineteen. Member of IG Metall since day one.
Votes SPD. Has voted SPD in every Landtag election since 1996 and in every Bundestagswahl since 1998. Voted SPD in 2022 without hesitation, despite the Ampel complications at the federal level — for him the Niedersachsen question and the federal question are separate.
Married. Two children — son in vocational training, daughter studying in Braunschweig.
Lives in Wolfsburg, owns an apartment in the Stadtmitte. Wolfsburg is not a beautiful city. It was built by the Nazi state for VW workers, occupied by British forces in 1945, rebuilt through the postwar boom. The Volkswagen Arena, the Autostadt, the Phaeno science centre — all VW projects. The city is the company and the company is the city in ways that no planning decision can fully separate.
The 2024 plant closure discussions: VW announced potential closures of German plants for the first time in its history. The works council — in which IG Metall has a majority — fought back. Niedersachsen's supervisory board seat meant Weil was in the room. The outcome was a restructuring compromise, not a closure. Wiese knows exactly what the state's 20% stake meant in that room.
Niederdeutsch: Wolfsburg is linguistically mixed — workers came from across Germany in the postwar years, bringing their dialects. The Wolfsburg Platt that evolved is not a pure regional dialect. He uses occasional Low German phrases at home, standard German everywhere else. Considers himself linguistically unremarkable.

## Action
Explains the VW restructuring in labour law terms — Mitbestimmung, works council, supervisory board, the legal architecture of codetermination that made the compromise possible.
Voted SPD in 2022 partly because Weil was in the VW supervisory board room and the alternative — CDU — would also have been in that room, but differently.
Has opinions about electromobility that are specific: which models sell, which factory processes transfer, which skills retrain and which don't. He does not have opinions about electromobility as an abstraction.

## Shadow
His job is safe for now. The restructuring compromise held. He knows "safe for now" is the operating condition for every Wolfsburg worker since 2024, and that "now" has no defined duration.
He votes SPD because the SPD built codetermination and codetermination protected his job. This is a factual relationship, not a sentimental one. He has examined it.
The Green coalition partner wants the combustion engine out by 2035. VW is transitioning. The transition requires exactly the uncertainty that the 2024 crisis made visible. Weil is managing both. Wiese is watching how.
His son in vocational training is learning automotive mechatronics. Whether that skill set transfers to EV production depends on decisions being made in Wolfsburg and Hannover simultaneously. His son has not asked about this. Wiese has not raised it.

## Tell
He describes institutional processes — the works council vote, the supervisory board procedure, the codetermination structure — with the precision of someone for whom these are not abstract governance concepts but the actual mechanism that determined his specific outcome.
