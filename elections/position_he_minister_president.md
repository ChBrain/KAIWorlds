# Position: Ministerpräsident Hessen

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election.md)
- **Process:** [the governing process](process_governing.md)
- **Place:** [Staatskanzlei Wiesbaden](place_he_staatskanzlei_wiesbaden.md)

## Has
The mandate from the [Landtag Wiesbaden](place_he_landtag_wiesbaden.md) — elected January 2024.
A CDU-SPD coalition with 74 of 133 seats.
Five Bundesrat seats.
The CDU's near-unbroken Hessen governing record — interrupted only 1991–1999 — as institutional inheritance.
Frankfurt as the state's economic anchor: the ECB, the Bundesbank, Deutsche Börse, Deutsche Bank, the largest German airport. The state's fiscal strength flows from the financial centre's tax revenues.
The Georg-August-Zinn-Straße address — governing from a building named after the longest-serving SPD Ministerpräsident, now with SPD as a junior partner.

## Orders
Govern Hessen through the coalition's 2024–2028 term without the personal authority of a Bouffier or the CDU's historic absolute majorities.
Navigate the SPD as coalition partner — a party at its worst-ever Hessen result, willing to accept CDU terms, but with its own agenda on social policy and housing.
Hold five Bundesrat seats aligned on a CDU-led coalition position in a federal chamber where the CDU is now in government — Hessen's position is no longer opposition to the federal government.
Manage Frankfurt's relationship with the state — the financial centre generates the taxes that fund Hessen's Länderfinanzausgleich contributions, but its workforce commutes, its regulation is federal and European, and its political interests are not always the same as the state's.

## Loses
The Bouffier era's quiet authority.
Volker Bouffier governed for twelve years — methodical, reliable, never a national story. The position Rhein inherited is the same institution but without the accumulated weight. Rhein is still building his version of CDU Hessen leadership.
The Greens as coalition partner — a decade of Schwarz-Grün, stable, known, predictable. The CDU-SPD coalition is new territory for both parties in Hessen. The working relationship has no established precedent.
The myth of "rotes Hessen" — the SPD is now a 15% junior partner. The industrial labour movement that made Frankfurt SPD country is the past. The Frankfurt of banking towers and ECB architecture is the present. The position governs the transition between them.

## Drives
On the loss of the Bouffier era —
builds authority through delivery rather than biography.
Uses Frankfurt's European profile — ECB, Deutsche Börse, post-Brexit financial centre consolidation — as a state-building narrative that exceeds party politics.
Manages the CDU-SPD coalition through the practical — housing in the Rhine-Main region, school investment, policing — where the parties' policy overlap is real and the disagreements are manageable.
