## Shown
Franz-Josef-Strauß-Ring 1, München. Adjacent to the Residenz, facing the Hofgarten.
The working headquarters of the Minister-President and the Bavarian state government.
The building is a postwar construction — the original Staatskanzlei was destroyed. The current structure incorporates a surviving Renaissance rotunda from the former Army Museum, flanked by modern wings. An unusual combination of eras.
The Bundesrat delegation operates from here — six seats in Berlin, the largest of any state except NRW.
Bavaria is the only German state with a permanent representation in Washington D.C. and offices in Brussels, Beijing, Cairo, Mumbai, New York, São Paulo, and Tokyo. The Staatskanzlei coordinates all of it.
Söder governs from here. The CSU-Freie Wähler coalition runs from here.

## Offers
State governance across Bavaria's extensive domains — the largest German state by area, 13 million people.
The six Bundesrat votes — cast as a bloc by the coalition, decisive in federal legislation on asylum, infrastructure, energy, and fiscal equalisation.
The Landesvertretung network — Bavaria projects state power internationally in a way no other German state does.
The constitutional court appointments. The police law. The school system. The beer purity law, if it comes to that.

## Withheld
The running tension between Bavaria's self-presentation as a sovereign actor — with its own foreign offices, its own fiscal demands, its own constitutional identity — and the fact that it remains one of sixteen states in a federal republic.
What the CSU's federal ambitions cost in domestic policy — Söder ran for Chancellor in 2021, did not get the nomination, returned to Bayern.
The calculation behind every Bundesrat vote — whether Bavaria's six seats will swing a federal bill, and whether that is the point.
