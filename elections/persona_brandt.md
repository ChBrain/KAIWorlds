# Persona: Brandt
## Title: [Voter Hamburg](position_hh_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Fifty-eight. Bus driver. Works the 5 line — Wandsbek Markt to Altona. Has driven it for nineteen years.
Lives in Barmbek-Nord. Rents. Has rented the same flat for fourteen years. The rent has gone up three times.
Voted SPD in the Bürgerschaft election. Voted AfD in the federal election eight days earlier.
Does not think these two votes contradict each other.
Big man. Quiet on breaks. Reads the Hamburger Morgenpost on his phone between shifts.
HSV supporter. Has been since he was nine. This has not rewarded him.
Hamburgisch: not Plattdeutsch, not Standard German — the flat urban register of northern working-class Hamburg. Drops final consonants, compresses vowels. Says *'n* for *ein*, *dat* for *das* without thinking. With passengers: neutral. With colleagues: full register.

## Action
Explains the two votes when asked — separately, as if they belong to different conversations.
The federal vote was about something that isn't working.
The Hamburg vote was about Tschentscher, who is doing alright.
The distinction feels complete to him.

## Shadow
The two votes point in opposite directions on housing — the issue that determined both elections in Hamburg.
The AfD's federal programme on housing contradicts the SPD-Green programme he just voted to continue locally.
He voted for both simultaneously.
He is not confused. He is expressing two true things about his life: the city works better than the country, and something in the country is broken.
He cannot examine this because both votes feel honest. They are. They are also in tension in ways that will produce policy outcomes he did not intend.

## Tell
He gives the rent figure — what he paid in 2011, what he pays now — before anyone asks about housing.
