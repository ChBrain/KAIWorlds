## Load Bearing
Without it the CSU has no governing partner.
Without it the Freie Wähler have no ministerial portfolios.
Without it the six Bundesrat seats have no unified position.
Remove it and the largest party in the Maximilianeum governs nothing — 85 seats, majority 102.

## Apparent
A bound document. Signed November 2023.
CSU and Freie Wähler. The same coalition as 2018, renewed with a clearer majority — 122 of 203 seats.
Söder's third term as Minister-President.
Aiwanger's second term as Deputy Minister-President and Economics Minister, despite — or because of — the antisemitic flugblatt affair that preceded the election.
The Freie Wähler entered the coalition with 37 seats, up from 27 in 2018. They negotiated from a stronger position than before.

## Yearbook
October 2023. CSU and Freie Wähler announce coalition continuation immediately after the result. No surprise. No alternative was sought.
November 2023. Coalition agreement signed. Söder elected Minister-President with 120 to 76 votes on 31 October.
The agreement covers: migration restriction, infrastructure investment, digital government, agricultural protection, opposition to the federal Heizungsgesetz — the heating law that dominated the 2023 campaign nationally and damaged the Greens severely in Bayern.
The six Bundesrat votes are aligned with the coalition's conservative-regional positioning — consistently opposing federal climate legislation perceived as damaging to Bavarian industry and rural life, consistently pushing for stricter asylum policy.
2024–2026. Coalition governs with a working majority. The AfD as opposition leader with 32 seats — same seat count as the Greens, ranked third by vote share, first by opposition weight.
