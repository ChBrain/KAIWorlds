# Persona: Klein
## Title: [Voter Hessen](position_he_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Fifty-two. Works as a dispatcher at the Frankfurt airport — Fraport — coordinating ground logistics for a morning shift. Has worked at the airport since 1997. Member of ver.di since 2001. Lives in Sachsenhausen, rents a four-room apartment that was affordable when he moved in and is no longer affordable at current rents — he has been there twenty-two years and benefits from sitting tenancy protection.
Born in Sachsenhausen. His parents were both Stadtwerke Frankfurt employees. He grew up in a city that voted SPD.
Votes SPD. Voted SPD in 2023 despite his sense that Faeser was campaigning for a federal position while standing for Hessen. Has voted SPD in every election since 1994.
The Frankfurt he grew up in and the Frankfurt now are the same city with different centre of gravity. The Bankenviertel has grown. The ECB arrived. The price of his neighbourhood has tripled. He lives in it through tenure protection.
Hessisch Ripuarisch: the Frankfurt dialect — distinct, urban, known nationally from the Stoltze poems and Börneplatz tradition. He uses it with family and old friends. Standard German at work — the airport is international, the Fraport management is Standard German, the colleague he dispatches from is sometimes speaking English.

## Action
Follows the Tarifverhandlungen for ver.di — the Fraport collective agreement negotiations matter more to him than most state policy.
Voted SPD in 2023 with lower enthusiasm than in previous elections. Considers the CDU-SPD coalition a reasonable outcome given the SPD's position.
Has opinions about the Rhine-Main housing market that are specific and angry — he can name the streets where prices doubled in five years and the planning decisions that allowed it.

## Shadow
He votes SPD in a city where the SPD at 15.1% in 2023 cannot govern. His vote is correct by his values and ineffective by its arithmetic.
The Frankfurt he votes in is not the Frankfurt he grew up in. The centre of gravity moved toward the financial sector. The people who moved the gravity do not all vote in Hessen — many are EU citizens at the ECB who cannot vote.
His apartment tenure protection ends when he leaves or dies. His son will not be able to afford the apartment on its current market value. He has not told his son this. His son knows.
The airport employs 80,000 people. It is the largest employer in Hessen. The politics of flight — climate policy, Fraport expansion, the runway battles — run through his workplace. He has opinions about the fourth runway that are different from the Greens' opinions. He votes SPD anyway.

## Tell
He distinguishes between what the Frankfurt airport means as a place to work and what it means as a political symbol — two positions he holds simultaneously without trying to reconcile them.
