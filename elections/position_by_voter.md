# Position: Voter Bayern

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election.md)

## Has
Two votes on Landtag polling day — Stimmkreisstimme for a direct candidate, Wahlkreisstimme for a party list.
Minimum voting age: 18.
One vote in the federal election. One vote in the European election.
The [the election record](piece_by_election_record.md) as the accumulated history.
The expectation, widely shared, that the CSU will govern Bayern. This expectation shapes what the vote means — it is often a vote about how the CSU governs, not whether.
A second layer: the Freie Wähler, the Greens, the SPD, and now the AfD compete for the space around the CSU rather than against it for power.
The structural asymmetry of the Bavarian ballot — a vote for any non-CSU party in most constituencies produces a CSU winner.

## Orders
Vote or don't.
In Bayern the consequence of the vote is more predictable than almost anywhere else in Germany — the CSU will lead the government. The question the vote answers is: with whom, and how large.

## Loses
The expectation of alternation.
In most German states governments change. In Bayern they do not. The CSU has governed since 1966 without a break. The opposition votes accumulate but do not produce a change of power.
The SPD voter of 1966 — 35.8% — and the SPD voter of 2023 — 8.4% — cast votes in the same state under the same structural conditions. The arc of that decline is built into the position.
The 14.6% who voted AfD in 2023 produced 32 seats — the same as the Greens, who got 14.4%. Neither governs. The AfD leads the opposition. The Greens sit beside them.

## Drives
On the loss of CSU dominance as natural condition —
votes in the knowledge that Bayern's political geography is internally contested — Munich against Straubing, university city against Erzgebirge village equivalent.
The position that voted CSU at 56% in 1974 and votes CSU at 37% in 2023 is the same position holding a smaller share of the outcome.
It votes anyway. The vote is still counted.
