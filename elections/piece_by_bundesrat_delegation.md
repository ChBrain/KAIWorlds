# Piece: Bundesrat Delegation Bayern

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Place:** [Bundesrat Berlin](place_bundesrat_berlin.md)

## Place
[Bundesrat Berlin](place_bundesrat_berlin.md).
And simultaneously: [Bayerische Staatskanzlei München](place_by_staatskanzlei.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it Bavaria has no formal voice in federal legislation.
Without it 13 million people have no representation in the chamber that shapes federal law on asylum, infrastructure, taxation, and energy.
Remove it and Bavaria's weight in the federal system — its longest border, its largest area, its largest economy outside NRW — disappears from Berlin entirely.

## Apparent
Six seats. The maximum allocation outside NRW.
Appointed from the CSU-Freie Wähler cabinet. Ministers of both parties hold the seats.
Voted as a bloc. The coalition must agree a position before the vote.
Six votes that have helped block or delay federal legislation — on the Heizungsgesetz, on asylum reform, on fiscal equalisation — in every legislative period since 2018.
Bavaria pays into the Länderfinanzausgleich — the fiscal equalisation system — more than any other state. This shapes every Bundesrat vote on tax and finance.

## Yearbook
2023. Cabinet Söder III appointed. Bundesrat delegation confirmed. CSU and Freie Wähler ministers hold the six seats.
The delegation votes consistently against federal climate legislation perceived as rural-hostile, against any softening of asylum law, and for infrastructure investment weighted toward Bavaria's transport needs — the Bundesverkehrswegeplan 2030 lists 325 Bavarian projects.
Bavaria's six votes, combined with other conservative-led states, form a blocking minority on consent legislation when the political alignment favours it.
The delegation is the instrument through which Bayern's state government shapes federal law between elections. It has more seats than Thüringen, Sachsen-Anhalt, Mecklenburg-Vorpommern, and Bremen combined.
