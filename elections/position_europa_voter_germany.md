# Position: Wähler Europäisches Parlament — Deutschland

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the Europa election process](process_election_europa.md)

## Has
One vote — Zweitstimme only, no Erststimme. Germany votes as a single national constituency. No geographic Wahlkreis.
Voting age: 16 from 2024 — lower than federal elections (18).
The [European election record](piece_europa_election_record.md) as the accumulated history — 1979 to 2024.
No minimum threshold — the Constitutional Court struck down the 3% threshold in 2011. Every vote above zero contributes proportionally. Tierschutzpartei, Volt, and Die Partei enter the EP with single digits nationally.
The ability to vote for parties that do not exist at state or federal level — or to vote for a national party differently from the federal vote. The European election is the one election where the protest is most affordable structurally.

## Orders
Vote or don't.
In 2024: 64.8% voted — the highest German turnout for an EP election since 1994. The Denkzettel against the Ampel drove engagement. But 55% of those who voted said Bundespolitik shaped their choice, not Europapolitik. The position voted in a European election that was partly about something else.
No runoff, no coalition negotiation that follows directly from the vote. The EP vote produces MEPs who join European groups that negotiate in Strasbourg and Brussels. The connection between the vote and the legislative outcome is longer and less visible than in state or federal elections.

## Loses
The direct feedback.
In a state election the voter can see who governs their state within weeks. In the EP election the voter sends MEPs into a 720-person chamber in two cities, where they join groups containing politicians from 26 other countries, and pass legislation that arrives as EU Regulation or Directive three to seven years later. The accountability loop is real but long.
The geographic anchor.
Every German voter in every other election has a specific geography — a state, a Wahlkreis, a Bundesland identity. In the EP election all 64 million eligible German voters cast the same type of vote for the same national list. The position has no regional expression of political identity distinct from the national one.
The certainty of outcome.
In 2024 the Greens collapsed from 20.5% to 11.9% — the position that voted Green in 2019 from conviction found their party's representation cut nearly in half. The position that voted BSW in 2024 sent Sahra Wagenknecht to the EP at 6.2%. The position that voted Tierschutzpartei at 1.4% sent one MEP to Brussels. The no-threshold system means the vote matters proportionally at every level — but the consequences are harder to predict than in a binary state government election.

## The Denkzettel question.
55% of German EP voters in 2024 voted on Bundespolitik. This is structurally rational — the EP election has lower stakes for the position's daily life than the Bundestagswahl or the Landtagswahl, so using it as a protest is lower cost. But the EP shapes the Agricultural Policy that determines what food costs. It shapes the GDPR that governs how every website treats the position's data. It shapes the AI Act, the Chemical Regulation, the Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism. The position that votes on Bundespolitik is simultaneously shaping Europapolitik in ways it may not have intended.

## Drives
On the loss of the direct feedback loop —
votes knowing the result takes years to become visible in daily life as EU regulation.
The position that voted on Bundespolitik in 2024 shaped Europapolitik it may not have intended to shape.
The position that voted on Europapolitik shaped the EP groups that negotiate the legislation that arrives as CAP reform or GDPR amendment three years later.
Both voted correctly by their own logic. The legislation does not distinguish between them.
