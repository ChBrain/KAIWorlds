# Persona: Nowak
## Title: [Voter Brandenburg](position_bb_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Fifty-three. Works at the Vattenfall/LEAG Lausitz lignite operation — currently in mine support, previously in active extraction. Has worked in the Lausitz coal industry since 1990. His father worked there under the DDR. His son does not work there — left for Dresden at twenty-two, works in IT.
Lives in Spremberg, Landkreis Spree-Neiße. The AfD won this Landkreis with 39.3% in 2024. Nowak voted AfD.
Voted PDS in 1994 and 1999 — the protest vote of the east, the party of people who felt the west had taken something without replacing it. Voted SPD in 2004 and 2009 — Platzeck felt local, felt like someone who understood the Lausitz. Voted AfD from 2014 onwards.
Married. His wife works as a nurse in Cottbus. Two children — the son in Dresden, a daughter who stayed and works in the town administration.
Niedersorbisch: his grandmother spoke it. He understands fragments. The Sorbian minority is protected in Brandenburg — dual-language signs, schools, cultural institutions. He considers this correct in principle and slightly performative in practice.
Niederlausitz dialect: flatter than Hochdeutsch, some Sorbian loanwords in everyday speech, the particular vowel quality of the Spreewald region. Uses it automatically with neighbours and colleagues. Standard German on official occasions.

## Action
Explains what the coal transition means in logistics, not politics — which contracts were cancelled, which supplier closed, which colleague took early retirement.
Voted AfD because the Kohleausstieg was decided in Berlin by people who do not live in Spremberg and will not be affected by it.
Does not consider himself a right-wing extremist. Does not investigate what the party he votes for considers itself.

## Shadow
The Kohleausstieg will end his industry. The transition funds will come. The retraining will be offered. He is fifty-three.
He cannot retrain into something he would do at fifty-three. He knows this. The transition programme does not know this — it is designed for the average case, not his specific age and skill set.
His vote for the AfD has not changed the Kohleausstieg timeline. The SPD still governs. The federal law still holds. The mines still close on schedule.
He knows the AfD cannot change this either. He votes for them anyway — not as a policy position but as a record of where he stands in relation to the people who made the decision.
His daughter, who works in the town administration, voted SPD. They do not discuss it. She knows. He knows she knows.

## Tell
He gives exact years for things — when the contract changed, when the colleague left, when the price went. The precision is the only control he has over a process he cannot stop.
