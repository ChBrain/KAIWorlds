# Position: Ministerpräsident Brandenburg

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election.md)
- **Process:** [the governing process](process_governing.md)
- **Place:** [Staatskanzlei Potsdam](place_bb_staatskanzlei_potsdam.md)

## Has
The mandate from the [Landtag Potsdam](place_bb_landtag_potsdam.md) — won by 1.7 percentage points after a campaign staked entirely on personal credibility.
A coalition with BSW — 46 of 88 seats. One above majority.
The SPD's unbroken governing record in Brandenburg since 1990 — every Ministerpräsident since the state was founded.
Four Bundesrat seats in Berlin.
The Lausitz coal transition — 20,000 jobs, billions in federal funds, a 2038 deadline that may accelerate.
The AfD's 30 seats as permanent structural constraint on constitutional decisions.
The knowledge that he lost his own direct constituency by seven votes while winning the state.

## Orders
Govern with one seat above majority in a chamber with a 30-seat AfD Sperrminorität.
Hold the BSW coalition partner — a new party with uncertain discipline and a party leader in Berlin who intervenes in state negotiations.
Manage the Lausitz transition without losing the communities it will transform.
Keep Brandenburg the exception — the eastern state the SPD held.

## Loses
The comfort of a working majority.
Every budget, every constitutional measure, every structural reform requires not a single defection within the coalition. The position operates permanently at the edge of its own mandate.
Lost his constituency. The man who staked his career on Brandenburg not falling to the AfD lost his own seat by seven votes and governs via party list.
The position cannot examine what it means that his margin was seven votes in one constituency and 1.7 percentage points across the state. Both are true. Neither is comfortable.

## Drives
On the loss of the comfortable majority —
governs as if the next vote is the last one.
The personal credibility that won the election is the only resource that exceeds the institutional weakness of the position.
Stays in Brandenburg. Does not look toward Berlin — unlike every other eastern SPD politician of his generation who tried the federal level and failed.
Makes the Lausitz transition the defining project — structural legacy that outlasts the coalition arithmetic.
