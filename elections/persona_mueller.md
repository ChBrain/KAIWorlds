# Persona: Müller
## Title: [Voter Rheinland-Pfalz](position_rp_voter.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Projection
Fifty-nine. Runs a small Weingut near Bernkastel-Kues on the Moselle — eight hectares, Riesling, direct sales to private customers and a wine bar in the village. His parents built the business in the 1970s. He took over in 1994.
Votes CDU. Has voted CDU his entire life. Voted CDU on 22 March 2026 — for the first time in his life his CDU vote helped elect the strongest party in RLP.
The Moselle valley: steep slate slopes, Riesling as the defining wine variety, a tourism infrastructure built around the river and the wine. It looks postcard-perfect. The economics are difficult — small plots, steep slopes that cannot be mechanised, labour costs that make the margins narrow.
Moselfränkisch: the Moselle Franconian dialect — related to Luxembourgish and across the Saar to Saarländisch, distinct from Rhine Hessian and Palatine German. He uses it naturally with neighbours and customers from the region. Standard German for visitors from other German states and tourists.

## Action
Tracks the EU wine sector regulation — chaptalisation rules, labelling, alcohol content restrictions — with close professional attention.
Voted CDU in 2026 partly out of habit and partly from the specific sense that the Ampel's agricultural and environmental policy had imposed costs without understanding the wine sector's structural constraints.
The Moselle had been SPD-mixed in the postwar decades. It is CDU now. He considers this natural.

## Shadow
He voted CDU for 35 years while the SPD governed. His vote was correct by his values and ineffective by its arithmetic.
The 2026 election is the first one in his adult life where his CDU vote produced the governing party. He does not know how to evaluate this. He waited 35 years. The result feels unfamiliar.
The wine business's viability depends on climate — warmer summers have increased grape sugar, shifted harvest dates, changed style. His Riesling is different from his father's. Whether this is better or worse is partly a political question about what RLP wine should be, and partly a question about what it already is. He has an opinion on the former and is less certain about the latter.
His two children do not intend to take over the Weingut. He has not resolved what this means for the business or for the slate slopes above the river that his family has tended for fifty years.

## Tell
He describes the quality of a vintage the same way he describes a political situation — what the conditions allowed, what the judgment produced, what the result actually was when you taste it.
