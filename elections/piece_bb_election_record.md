# Piece: Election Record Brandenburg

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Place:** [Landtag Potsdam](place_bb_landtag_potsdam.md)

## Load Bearing
Without it Brandenburg's exceptionalism in the east has no ground — the only new state where the SPD won in 1990 and has governed every year since.
Without it Woidke's 2024 gamble — "I'll quit if the AfD wins" — has no weight.
Without it the Linke's disappearance from an eastern parliament for the first time has no arc — they were in every Landtag from 1990 to 2024.
Remove it and Brandenburg looks like a normal eastern state. With it, it looks like the one that held.

## Apparent
Brandenburg: 2.5 million inhabitants. 4 Bundesrat seats. Capital: Potsdam. Founded 3 October 1990.
The state surrounds Berlin without containing it. Berlin is a separate Bundesland inside Brandenburg's territory.
Voting age: 16. Maximum Landtag size: 110 seats — capped, unique in Germany.
Current: SPD 32 seats, AfD 30 seats, BSW 14 seats, CDU 12 seats. 88 seats total. Majority: 45.
SPD-BSW coalition. Woidke Ministerpräsident for the fourth term. Smallest possible working majority.
Next regular election: 2029.

## Yearbook
**2029** — Next regular Landtag election.

**2024** — 22 September. SPD **30.9%**. AfD 29.2%. BSW 13.5% — first entry. CDU 12.1% — worst ever in Brandenburg. Greens 4.1% — outside. Linke 3.0% — outside for the first time since 1990. Turnout 72.9% — highest since 1990. Woidke had declared he would resign if AfD became strongest party. He won by 1.7 points. Woidke lost his own direct constituency — Spree-Neiße I — by seven votes to the AfD. He entered the Landtag via party list. SPD-BSW two-party coalition — smallest majority possible in Brandenburg's capped chamber. First time Linke absent from any eastern German Landtag. All 44 direct mandates went to SPD and AfD only.

**2019** — 1 September. SPD 26.2%. AfD 23.5% — largest gain. CDU 15.6%. Linke 10.7%. Greens 10.8%. BVB/Freie Wähler 5.0%. Turnout 61.3%. SPD-CDU-Green Kenia coalition. Woidke third term. First time three parties needed to form a majority.

**2014** — 14 September. SPD 31.9%. CDU 23.0%. Linke 18.6%. AfD 12.2% — first entry. Greens 6.2%. BVB/FW 2.7%. Turnout 47.9% — historic low. SPD-Linke coalition continues. Woidke first full term as Ministerpräsident.

**2009** — 27 September. SPD 33.0%. Linke 27.2%. CDU 19.8%. FDP 7.2%. Greens 5.7%. DVU 1.2% — outside after ten years. BVB/FW 2.6%. Turnout 67.0%. SPD-Linke coalition — first red-red coalition in Brandenburg. Woidke enters cabinet. Platzeck Ministerpräsident until 2013, then Woidke.

**2004** — 19 September. SPD 31.9%. CDU 19.4%. Linke/PDS **28.0%** — first time PDS becomes second largest party in Brandenburg. DVU 6.1% — re-enters Landtag. FDP 3.3% — outside. Greens 3.6% — outside. Turnout 56.4%. SPD-CDU continues. Platzeck Ministerpräsident (succeeds Stolpe June 2002).

**1999** — 5 September. SPD **39.7%** — peak SPD result in Brandenburg. CDU 26.6%. PDS 23.3%. DVU **5.3%** — far-right enters Landtag for first time. FDP 1.9% — outside. Greens 1.9% — outside. Turnout 54.3%. SPD-CDU Koalition. Stolpe Ministerpräsident.

**1994** — 11 September. SPD **54.1%** — absolute majority. CDU 18.7%. PDS 18.7%. FDP 2.2% — outside. Greens/Bündnis 90 2.9% — outside. Turnout 56.3%. SPD governs alone. Stolpe. The Stasi-Kontakte controversy about Stolpe's past as a church official in the GDR broke the Ampel coalition — Bündnis 90 left in 1994. SPD won outright regardless.

**1990** — 14 October. First Landtag election. SPD **38.2%**. CDU 29.4%. PDS-Linke Liste 13.4%. FDP 6.6%. Bündnis 90 6.4%. Turnout 67.1%. Brandenburg was the only new eastern state where the SPD won in 1990. All others went CDU. Manfred Stolpe (SPD) Ministerpräsident — Ampel coalition with FDP and Bündnis 90. Stolpe had been Konsistorialpräsident of the Protestant church in East Berlin — a figure who had navigated the GDR as a church leader, not a dissident, not a party member. His Stasi contacts were contested for years. He governed regardless.
