# Piece: Election Record Bundesrepublik Deutschland

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Place:** [Reichstag Berlin](place_reichstag_berlin.md)

## Load Bearing
Without it the CDU/CSU's governing dominance through the Adenauer, Kohl, and Merz eras has no scale.
Without it the SPD's three chancellorships — Brandt, Schmidt, Schröder, Scholz — have no pattern.
Without it the AfD's doubling from 10.4% to 20.8% in one election has no trajectory.
Without it the Ampel's collapse — the first governing coalition to break before the end of its term since 1966 — has no context.
Without it the BSW missing the Bundestag by 13,000 votes has no weight.

## Apparent
Bundesrepublik Deutschland: 84 million inhabitants. 630 Bundestag seats. 69 Bundesrat seats. Federal capital: Berlin. Founded 23 May 1949. Reunified 3 October 1990.
Current: CDU/CSU-SPD Große Koalition. Friedrich Merz (CDU) Bundeskanzler. Koalitionsvertrag signed 5 May 2025. Merz cabinet: 17 ministers — 7 CDU, 7 SPD, 3 CSU.
Next regular election: autumn 2029.
Voting age: 18. The Bundestag has at least 630 seats under the 2023 electoral reform.

## Yearbook
**2029** — Next regular Bundestagswahl.

**2025** — 23 February. Vorgezogene Neuwahl after Ampel collapse. CDU **22.6%** / CSU 6.0% = 28.5% combined. AfD **20.8%** — doubled from 2021, now second largest party. SPD **16.4%** — worst ever in federal elections. Greens 11.6%. Linke 8.8% — benefited from BSW voters returning. BSW **4.97%** — out by ~13,000 votes. FDP **4.3%** — out. Turnout 82.5%. CDU/CSU: 208 seats, AfD: 152, SPD: 120, Greens: 85, Linke: 64, CSU: 44, SSW: 1. CDU/CSU-SPD coalition has 328 of 630 — simple majority. Merz failed first investiture (310 votes, needed 316). Won second ballot (325 votes). Kabinett Merz.

**2021** — 26 September. SPD 25.7%. CDU/CSU 24.1% — worst CDU result ever. Greens 14.7%. FDP 11.4%. AfD 10.4%. Linke 4.9% — outside threshold but in via 3 direct mandates. BSW did not exist. Turnout 76.4%. SPD-Greens-FDP Ampel coalition. Olaf Scholz Bundeskanzler. Coalition collapsed 6 November 2024 when Scholz dismissed FDP finance minister Lindner.

**2017** — 24 September. CDU/CSU 32.9%. SPD 20.5%. AfD **12.6%** — first entry to Bundestag. FDP 10.7%. Linke 9.2%. Greens 8.9%. Turnout 76.2%. CDU/CSU-SPD GroKo after four months of failed Jamaica talks. Angela Merkel fourth term.

**2013** — 22 September. CDU/CSU **41.5%** — near absolute majority. SPD 25.7%. Linke 8.6%. Greens 8.4%. AfD 4.7% — just outside. FDP **4.8%** — outside for first time since 1949. Turnout 71.5%. CDU/CSU-SPD GroKo. Merkel third term.

**2009** — 27 September. CDU/CSU 33.8%. SPD 23.0% — worst since 1949 at that point. FDP **14.6%** — best ever FDP result. Greens 10.7%. Linke 11.9%. Turnout 70.8% — lowest since 1949 at that point. CDU/CSU-FDP coalition. Merkel second term.

**2005** — 18 September. CDU/CSU 35.2%. SPD 34.2% — separated by 1 percentage point. Greens 8.1%. FDP 9.8%. Linke 8.7% — first federal appearance of refounded left. Turnout 77.7%. CDU/CSU-SPD GroKo. Merkel first term — first woman Bundeskanzlerin.

**2002** — 22 September. SPD 38.5%. CDU/CSU 38.5% — tied to the decimal point, SPD ahead by 6,027 votes. Greens 8.6%. FDP 7.4%. PDS 4.0% — outside. Turnout 79.1%. SPD-Greens. Schröder second term. The closest federal election in German history. The Oder flood and Iraq War shaped the campaign.

**1998** — 27 September. SPD **40.9%** — SPD peak result. CDU/CSU 35.1%. Greens 6.7%. FDP 6.2%. PDS 5.1%. Turnout 82.2%. SPD-Greens. Gerhard Schröder first term. Sixteen years of Kohl ended.

**1994** — 16 October. CDU/CSU 41.4%. SPD 36.4%. Greens 7.3%. FDP 6.9%. PDS 4.4% — outside threshold but 4 direct mandates. Turnout 79.0%. CDU/CSU-FDP. Kohl fourth term — narrowest victory in unified Germany.

**1990** — 2 December. CDU/CSU **43.8%** — first all-German election since 1932. SPD 33.5%. FDP 11.0%. Greens (West) 3.9% — outside. Alliance 90/Greens (East) 6.1% — in via eastern exception clause. PDS 2.4% — in via eastern exception. Turnout 77.8%. CDU/CSU-FDP. Kohl third term. The unification election.

**1987** — 25 January. CDU/CSU 44.3%. SPD 37.0%. FDP 9.1%. Greens 8.3%. Turnout 84.3%. CDU/CSU-FDP. Kohl second term.

**1983** — 6 March. CDU/CSU **48.8%**. SPD 38.2%. FDP 7.0%. Greens 5.6% — first entry to Bundestag. Turnout 89.1%. CDU/CSU-FDP. Kohl first full term after constructive vote of no confidence (October 1982) ended Schmidt's chancellorship.

**1980** — 5 October. SPD 42.9%. CDU/CSU 44.5% — CDU/CSU larger but SPD-FDP continues. FDP 10.6%. Turnout 88.6%. SPD-FDP. Schmidt second term.

**1976** — 3 October. CDU/CSU **48.6%**. SPD 42.6%. FDP 7.9%. Turnout 90.7%. SPD-FDP. Schmidt first full term. CDU/CSU largest party but could not form government.

**1972** — 19 November. SPD **45.8%** — SPD peak until 1998. CDU/CSU 44.9%. FDP 8.4%. Turnout **91.1%** — highest ever. SPD-FDP. Willy Brandt second term. The Ostpolitik election.

**1969** — 28 September. CDU/CSU 46.1%. SPD 42.7%. FDP 5.8%. Turnout 86.7%. SPD-FDP — the first change of government without an election producing the change. Brandt first term.

**1965** — 19 September. CDU/CSU 47.6%. SPD 39.3%. FDP 9.5%. Turnout 86.8%. CDU/CSU-SPD GroKo. Ludwig Erhard then Kurt Georg Kiesinger.

**1961** — 17 September. CDU/CSU 45.3%. SPD 36.2%. FDP 12.8%. Turnout 87.7%. CDU/CSU-FDP. Adenauer fourth term.

**1957** — 15 September. CDU/CSU **50.2%** — only time any German party passed 50% in a federal election. SPD 31.8%. FDP 7.7%. Turnout 87.8%. CDU/CSU alone. Adenauer third term.

**1953** — 6 September. CDU/CSU 45.2%. SPD 28.8%. FDP 9.5%. BHE 5.9%. Turnout 86.0%. CDU/CSU coalition. Adenauer second term.

**1949** — 14 August. First Bundestagswahl. CDU/CSU 31.0%. SPD 29.2%. FDP 11.9%. KPD 5.7%. DP 4.0%. Turnout 78.5%. CDU/CSU-FDP-DP coalition. Konrad Adenauer first Bundeskanzler — elected by one vote (his own).
