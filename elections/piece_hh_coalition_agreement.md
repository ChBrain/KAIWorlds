## Owner
- **Project:** Elections

## Place
[Senatskanzlei Hamburg](place_hh_senatskanzlei.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it the Senate has no programme.
Without it the second SPD-Green coalition in succession has no measure to be held against.
Without it the question — what changed between term one and term two — has no answer.
Remove it and the five years of governing are ungoverned.

## Apparent
A bound document. Signed 24 April 2025.
SPD and Greens. The same two parties. Some of the same senators. A new distribution of responsibilities — the Bezirke now fall under the SPD-led Finanzbehörde.
Chapters on housing, port, climate, mobility, education, social policy.
The Linke's 11.2% and their mietendeckel campaign are not in this document. The pressure they produced may be.
Tschentscher confirmed by 71 of 119 votes — 47 against, one abstention.

## Yearbook
24 April 2025. The SPD and Greens sign for a second consecutive term. The negotiation took four weeks — shorter than 2020. The ground was familiar.
The CDU was also in exploratory talks. Tschentscher chose the Greens. The CDU result of 19.8% was their best in decades. It was not enough.
The agreement is the second coalition agreement these two parties have signed together. The first produced a record. This agreement is being compared to that record before the ink is dry.
Housing is the central tension — the Linke's strong showing showed where Hamburg voters felt the coalition had fallen short. The new agreement addresses this. Whether it does so more than the last one did remains the question.
The Bundesrat delegation is appointed from the Senate on 7 May. Three seats. Hamburg's position on port infrastructure, trade policy, and climate legislation travels to Berlin.
By 2030 this agreement will be two records simultaneously: what was promised in 2025, and how the second term compared to the first.
