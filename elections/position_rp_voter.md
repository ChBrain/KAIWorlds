# Position: Voter Rheinland-Pfalz

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election.md)

## Has
Two votes — Erststimme and Zweitstimme. Voting age: 18.
One vote in the federal election. One vote in the European election.
The [election record](piece_rp_election_record.md) as the accumulated history.
The knowledge that the election twelve days ago ended something. Thirty-five years of SPD government ended in a single evening. CDU becomes stärkste Partei for the first time since 1987 — before the Berlin Wall fell.
Geography as political identity: the Rhine cities (Mainz, Koblenz, Trier) have different political profiles from the Eifel, the Westerwald, the Palatinate wine country. The AfD's 19.5% sits unevenly — higher in the rural uplands, lower in the university towns.

## Orders
Vote or don't.
In 2026: 68.5% voted — up from 2021. The election was framed as a referendum on the federal Ampel and on 35 years of SPD state government simultaneously. Both frames mobilised differently: anti-Ampel voters went to CDU and AfD; some SPD loyalists stayed home.

## Loses
The SPD as the natural governing force.
For 35 years the SPD governed RLP. Voters under 50 have never experienced a CDU Ministerpräsident in their state. The position's assumption about which party governs is now wrong.
The FDP voter of 2021 — 5.5%, coalition partner, in government. The FDP voter of 2026 — 2.1%, out of the Landtag. The vote produced opposite results with the same intent. The difference was a federal Ampel collapse that destroyed the party's credibility before the Mainzer voter could separate the state from the federal question.
The Ampel — Germany's first SPD-Green-FDP state coalition, invented in RLP in 2016, exported to Berlin in 2021. Both versions ended in 2025–2026.

## Drives
On the loss of 35 years of SPD government —
votes on 22 March 2026 in an election that ended something.
The position that voted SPD from inheritance now votes in a state where the SPD is second at 25.9%.
The position that voted CDU for the first time since 1987 cast a vote that produced the first CDU Ministerpräsident in living political memory.
Both positions hold the vote as a record of where they stood when something changed. The record stands.
