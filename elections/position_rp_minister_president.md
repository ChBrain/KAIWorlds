# Position: Ministerpräsident Rheinland-Pfalz

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election.md)
- **Process:** [the governing process](process_governing.md)
- **Place:** [Staatskanzlei Mainz](place_rp_staatskanzlei_mainz.md)

## Has
The mandate from the [Landtag Mainz](place_rp_landtag_mainz.md) — not yet formally awarded. Coalition negotiations ongoing.
An incoming CDU-SPD coalition with 70 of 105 seats.
Four Bundesrat seats — transitioning from Ampel to CDU-led position.
The CDU's return to power after 35 years — the first CDU Ministerpräsident since 1991 governs a state where the CDU had been in opposition for the entire adult life of most working-age voters.
The Altmeier nameplate on the Staatskanzlei — Peter Altmeier (CDU) built the state for CDU dominance 1947–1969. The building carries that legacy. The position that returns to it in 2026 carries none of it directly.

## Orders
Govern RLP for the first time as CDU in 35 years.
Negotiate a coalition agreement with the SPD — parties that governed as opponents since 1991 must now construct a working document together.
Hold four Bundesrat seats aligned on CDU-led position in a federal chamber where the CDU now also governs in Berlin.
Define what CDU RLP government looks like in 2026 — not the Vogel era, not the Kohl era, something new in a state that has changed substantially since 1991.

## Loses
The institutional memory.
The SPD governed RLP for 35 years. The institutional knowledge of how to run the Staatskanzlei, coordinate the Bundesrat, manage the wine sector lobby, negotiate with BASF — all of this is in SPD-trained civil servants. CDU politicians returning to government encounter an administrative culture built in opposition.
The AfD's 24 seats as permanent opposition — 19.5%, best in any western state. The position cannot ignore this. It cannot govern with them. It must explain why 19.5% of RLP voters are not represented in the government it leads.

## Drives
On the loss of institutional memory —
builds the new CDU administrative culture by hiring from CDU-governed states — picking up civil service knowledge from Hessen, Sachsen, the federal ministries.
Uses the large coalition majority (70 seats) as a governance resource — decisions that require constitutional majorities can be passed without depending on fragile partners.
Presents the CDU-SPD coalition as a stability argument: both large parties, large majority, stable government in a state where the Ampel had become associated with the instability of its federal counterpart.
