# Position: Ministerpräsidentin Saarland

## Owner
- **Project:** Elections
- **Process:** [the election process](process_election.md)
- **Process:** [the governing process](process_governing.md)
- **Place:** [Staatskanzlei Saarbrücken](place_sl_staatskanzlei_saarbruecken.md)

## Has
The mandate from the [Landtag Saarbrücken](place_sl_landtag_saarbruecken.md) — absolute majority, 32 investiture votes.
29 SPD seats — no coalition partner required.
Three Bundesrat seats.
The SPD's recovery narrative — from 29.6% in 2017 to 43.5% in 2022 — as the political foundation.
The industrial transformation portfolio: Ford Saarlouis (EV transition), Saarstahl (hydrogen), the cross-border labour market.
The smallest Flächenstaat in Germany — everything is closer here. The Ministerpräsidentin of 1 million people governs at a scale where decisions are legible to the people they affect.

## Orders
Govern alone — no coalition partner, no negotiating constraints, no shared authority.
Manage the Ford and steel transitions without losing the employment base that the state's economy and the SPD's coalition depend on.
Hold three Bundesrat seats on SPD positions in a Bundesrat increasingly shaped by CDU-led states.
Not be the Saarland's Lafontaine — the SPD leader who built the state and then left for national politics. The position has to be its own end.

## Loses
The pressure.
Governing alone means: no coalition discipline from a partner, no shared accountability, no external reason to compromise. Everything that goes wrong is the SPD's. Everything that goes right is the SPD's. The position is more exposed than any coalition government.
The 23 votes the Greens didn't get. If the Greens had entered the Landtag, the position would exist in a different form — leading a coalition, managing a partner, sharing authority. The 23-vote absence created the absolute majority. The position did not choose it; it happened to it.

## Drives
On the exposure of solo governance —
governs visibly — uses the smallness of the state as an asset. One million people, the Ministerpräsidentin can be in three towns in a day.
The industrial transition is the defining project — if Ford Saarlouis produces the promised EV jobs, the transformation is evidence. If it doesn't, the position owns the failure.
The cross-border identity: the first Saarland to be returned to Germany in 1957, the most French-adjacent German state, the position builds European regional policy as a governing frame that exceeds the state's size.
