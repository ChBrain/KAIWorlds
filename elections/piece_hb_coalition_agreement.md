## Load Bearing
Without it SPD has no governing coalition — 33 seats, majority 44.
Without it the continuation of the historic first western German red-green-red coalition has no record.
Without it the BiW's exclusion — the SPD chose to continue with left partners rather than bring the right-populist Bürger in Wut into government — has no context.

## Apparent
SPD, Greens, and Linke. Signed June 2023. Bovenschulte second term.
58 of 87 seats — 14 above majority.
The 2023 election gave the SPD options: SPD-CDU GroKo was arithmetically viable. SPD-Green-Linke continuation was also viable. Bovenschulte chose left continuity. The CDU was excluded despite being the second-largest party.
First established in 2019 — the first Linke participation in a western German state government. The 2023 coalition is its second term.

## Yearbook
14 May 2023. SPD wins. CDU second. BiW surge.
25 June 2023. SPD, Greens, Linke agree coalition continuation.
July 2023. Bovenschulte re-elected Bürgermeister.
2023–2027. Coalition governs. Current polls (April 2026): CDU 24%, SPD 22% — CDU polling ahead, SPD below 2023 result. AfD 15% — expected to be admitted in 2027. BiW trajectory unclear once AfD returns.
Next election: 2027.
