# Piece: Anagnorisis
## The recognition.

## Owner
- **Project:** Poetics
- **Process:** [Catastrophe](process_catastrophe.md)

## Place
[The Stage](place_the_stage.md). Fires at the hinge of [Catastrophe](process_catastrophe.md).

## Load Bearing
Remove it and [Catastrophe](process_catastrophe.md) is blind - the fortune reverses but no one understands why.
The audience feels the blow. The [Protagonist](position_protagonist.md) does not.
Anagnorisis is what makes the blow legible.
The [Protagonist](position_protagonist.md) passes from ignorance to knowledge.
That passage is the piece.

## Apparent
A change from ignorance to knowledge -
producing love or hate between persons
destined for good or bad fortune.
Not always self-recognition.
Sometimes: the identity of another person revealed.
Sometimes: the truth of a situation suddenly known.
Always: what was hidden is now seen.
Cannot be unseen.

## Yearbook
Aristotle considers it the mark of a superior tragedy when Anagnorisis causes [Peripeteia](piece_peripeteia.md) or arrives with it.
Oedipus learns who he is - that learning is the reversal.
The two pieces are distinct. In the finest plots they are inseparable.
The piece travels beyond tragedy: every twist, every reveal, every moment a character learns what the audience already knew - this piece is running.
