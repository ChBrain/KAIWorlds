# Piece: Peripeteia
## The reversal.

## Owner
- **Project:** Poetics
- **Process:** [Catastrophe](process_catastrophe.md)

## Place
[The Stage](place_the_stage.md). Fires at the hinge of [Catastrophe](process_catastrophe.md).

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the story is simple - a change of fortune without a turn.
Simple plots exist. They are inferior.
Peripeteia is what makes a complex plot complex.
The situation was moving in one direction.
It veers to its opposite.
The veer must arise from the logic of what came before -
not from coincidence, not from the gods arriving on a machine.

## Apparent
A change by which the action veers round to its opposite.
Most powerfully: from prosperity to misfortune.
The [Protagonist](position_protagonist.md) who thought the situation was resolving
finds it has resolved against them.
Fires together with [Anagnorisis](piece_anagnorisis.md) in the finest tragedies.
When they fire together the impact is compounded:
the world collapses at the exact moment the [Protagonist](position_protagonist.md) understands why.

## Yearbook
Aristotle names it alongside [Anagnorisis](piece_anagnorisis.md) as the most powerful elements of plot.
Oedipus Rex is his primary example - the messenger's news is both the reversal and the recognition in one stroke.
The piece travels across every plot type that followed Aristotle.
It is still the moment the audience gasps.
