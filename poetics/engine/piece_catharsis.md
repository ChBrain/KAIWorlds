## Load Bearing
Remove it and [Mythos](process_mythos.md) produces event without effect.
The story happened. Nothing was discharged.
Catharsis is why the story needed to be told at all.
Without it tragedy is suffering observed, not suffering processed.
The audience leaves the same as they arrived.

## Apparent
Purgation, purification, or clarification - the translation is disputed.
All three are present.
Pity and fear, built through [Desis](process_desis.md) and broken open in [Catastrophe](process_catastrophe.md),
are released here.
The audience reaches a stable, neutral emotional state.
Not numbness - resolution.
The emotions were real. They have now been used.

## Yearbook
Aristotle's most debated term.
Plato banned tragedy from his republic because it aroused pity and fear and made men weak.
Aristotle's answer: tragedy arouses them in order to discharge them.
The therapeutic reading held through the Renaissance.
The clarification reading holds now - the audience understands something they could not have understood without the story.
Both are correct. They are not in conflict.
The piece does not resolve the debate.
It produces the effect regardless.
