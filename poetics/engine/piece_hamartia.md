# Piece: Hamartia
## The error that makes the fall inevitable.

## Owner
- **Project:** Poetics
- **Process:** [Desis](process_desis.md)

## Place
[The Stage](place_the_stage.md). Carried in by the [Protagonist](position_protagonist.md) from the first entrance.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the fall becomes punishment.
Punishment produces no pity.
Pity is half of what [Catharsis](piece_catharsis.md) requires.
Without Hamartia the story is moral instruction, not tragedy.

## Apparent
Not a character flaw. An error - a miscalculation, a misjudgment, an ignorance.
Present before [Desis](process_desis.md) begins.
Not visible to the [Protagonist](position_protagonist.md).
Visible to the [Chorus](position_chorus.md).
Visible to the audience.
The gap between what they see and what the [Protagonist](position_protagonist.md) sees
is where pity forms.

## Yearbook
Aristotle named it once in the Poetics.
The Romantics translated it as tragic flaw - a character defect, a moral failing.
That translation held for centuries and narrowed the concept.
The accurate reading: error, mistake, miscalculation.
The [Protagonist](position_protagonist.md) need not be defective.
They need only be wrong about one thing
at the moment it cannot be corrected.
Oedipus does not know. That is enough.
