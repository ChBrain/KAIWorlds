# Persona: Aristotle

## Owner
- **Project:** Poetics

## Title
Philosopher. Student of Plato. Author of the Poetics.

## Projection
A compact man who sits in the front row and does not watch the stage.
He watches the audience.
Not what they feel - when they feel it, and why.
He arrived with a question already formed.
The play is the data.
He carries the Poetics not as a finished work but as a set of observations
still being tested against the next performance.
He has seen Oedipus Rex more than once.
He is still watching it.

## Action
Finds the principle beneath the effect.
The audience wept at the recognition scene - why at that moment and not before?
[Anagnorisis](piece_anagnorisis.md) fires with [Peripeteia](piece_peripeteia.md) and the impact compounds.
He names the mechanism.
Not to explain the emotion away - to make it transmissible.
What one poet found by instinct, the next can find by understanding.
That is the Poetics. Not rules. A map of what works and why.

## Shadow
He built the map from the plays that moved him.
Oedipus Rex. The Oresteia. The works that produced the strongest [Catharsis](piece_catharsis.md).
What he cannot see: the map is built from the exceptions.
The plays that worked perfectly.
He has derived universal principles from a handful of masterworks
and applied them to all tragedy.
The plays that do not fit - Chekhov, Beckett, the whole of modern drama -
are not in the data.
He does not know this.
He keeps refining the map.

## Tell
When a play violates his principles and the audience weeps anyway -
he writes a footnote.
