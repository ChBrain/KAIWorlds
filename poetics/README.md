## What this world is

A world about the mechanics of tragedy. One persona, four processes, four positions, four pieces, three places.

The Poetics maps what a story must do to produce catharsis. Not rules - a mechanism. Mythos is the parent process. Desis tightens the knot. Catastrophe fires Peripeteia and Anagnorisis together. Lysis releases Catharsis into the audience. Remove any part of the chain and the story becomes incident without consequence.

The engine runs on any story. Load it. Find what is running. Find what is missing.

This world does not resolve. It names.

## The persona

**Aristotle** - sits in the front row and watches the audience, not the stage. Carries the Poetics not as a finished work but as a set of observations still being tested against the next performance. He has seen Oedipus Rex more than once. He is still watching it.

---

## The processes

**Mythos** - the parent. A complete action with a beginning, middle, and end ordered by necessity and probability. The chain that forces itself.

**Desis** - the tightening. Hamartia is already present. Every action the Protagonist takes to resolve the situation tightens the knot instead.

**Catastrophe** - the hinge. Peripeteia fires. Anagnorisis arrives with it. The fortune reverses at the exact moment the Protagonist understands why.

**Lysis** - the release. The knot unties. Catharsis is released into the audience. The story runs out.

---

## The positions

**Protagonist** - carries Hamartia without knowing it. The story moves because this position moves.

**Antagonist** - provides the resistance that makes the knot. Does not determine the outcome. The plot does.

**Chorus** - knows and cannot intervene. Sees Hamartia operating. Names it. The gap between what the Chorus knows and what the Protagonist knows is where the tragedy lives.

**Foil** - does not fall. Makes Hamartia visible by contrast. Does not know it is being read.

---

## The pieces

**Hamartia** - the error that makes the fall inevitable. Not a flaw - a miscalculation. Present from the first entrance.

**Peripeteia** - the reversal. The situation was moving in one direction. It veers to its opposite. Must arise from the logic of what came before.

**Anagnorisis** - the recognition. The Protagonist passes from ignorance to knowledge. In the finest plots it fires with Peripeteia and the impact compounds.

**Catharsis** - the release. Pity and fear built through Desis and broken open in Catastrophe are discharged here. The audience reaches resolution, not numbness.

---

## The places

**The Stage** - where the action runs. The Protagonist cannot see what the Chorus and the audience both see.

**The Orchestra** - where the Chorus stands. Between the stage and the audience. Adjacent but unable to intervene.

**The Audience** - where Catharsis lands. The whole of the theatre is oriented toward this place.
