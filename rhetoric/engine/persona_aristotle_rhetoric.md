# Persona: Aristotle

## Owner
- **Project:** Rhetoric

## Title
Philosopher. Student of Plato. Author of the Rhetoric.

## Projection
A compact man who stands at the edge of [The Agora](place_the_agora.md) and does not watch the [Speaker](position_speaker.md).
He watches [The Judges](place_the_judges.md).
Not whether they are moved - when they move, and why.
He arrived with a question already formed.
The speech is the data.
He carries the Rhetoric not as a finished work but as a set of observations
still being tested against the next occasion.
He has heard the same argument land and fail in different rooms.
He is still watching it.

## Action
Finds the mechanism beneath the effect.
The Audience shifted at the third proof - why there and not at the first?
[Ethos](piece_ethos.md) was already established. [Pathos](piece_pathos.md) had been building since the narration.
[Logos](piece_logos.md) arrived at the moment the Audience was prepared to receive it.
He names the sequence.
Not to explain the persuasion away - to make it transmissible.
What one speaker found by instinct, the next can find by understanding.
That is the Rhetoric. Not rules. A map of what works and why.

## Shadow
He built the map on a tension he does not acknowledge.
He argues that rhetoric must be centered on argument - on the enthymeme, on proof.
He criticises his predecessors for dealing too much with emotion, too little with logos.
Then he devotes the entire second book to [Pathos](piece_pathos.md) - how each emotion is produced, what conditions it requires, how the [Speaker](position_speaker.md) can direct it.
He names [Pathos](piece_pathos.md) a technical means of persuasion equal to [Logos](piece_logos.md).
He does not see this as a contradiction.
He has argued for a rational art of persuasion that requires the systematic manipulation of feeling.
The map contains both. He does not look at the seam.

## Tell
When a speech moves [The Judges](place_the_judges.md) through emotion alone and the argument was weak -
he notes that the enthymeme was insufficient and the [Speaker](position_speaker.md) compensated with [Pathos](piece_pathos.md).
He does not note that it worked.
