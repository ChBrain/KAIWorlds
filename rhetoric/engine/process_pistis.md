# Process: Pistis

## Owner
- **Project:** Rhetoric
- **Process:** [Techne](process_techne.md)

## Initiated by
[Prothesis](process_prothesis.md) has run. The claim stands. The Audience knows what it is being asked to believe. The Speaker now deploys the available means of persuasion.

## Direction
Toward conviction. The Audience's resistance must be met and overcome - not by force, but by the logical connection of the claim to premises the Audience already holds. The direction is one: from the Audience's existing convictions toward the Speaker's stated claim.

## Lever
Carrying force: [Ethos](piece_ethos.md), [Pathos](piece_pathos.md), and [Logos](piece_logos.md) working together. Aristotle names the enthymeme - the rhetorical proof - as the body of persuasion. Everything else supports it. The Speaker selects the means that fit this Audience, this occasion, this claim.

Resistance: the Audience's prior convictions. They are not empty. They arrive with opinions already formed, emotions already present, and a judgment already forming. Pistis works against this. It does not replace the Audience's convictions - it connects the claim to them.

## Echo
[Epilogos](process_epilogos.md) inherits an Audience that has heard the proof. Conviction has been produced - or it has not. What changed: the available means have been deployed. The Speaker has done what the art requires. The outcome is no longer in the Speaker's hands.
