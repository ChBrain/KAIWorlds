# Piece: Ethos

## Owner
- **Project:** Rhetoric
- **Process:** [Prooimion](process_prooimion.md)

## Place
Where the [Speaker](position_speaker.md) is. Carried in from the first word.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the [Audience](position_audience.md) has no grounds for trust. [Pathos](piece_pathos.md) and [Logos](piece_logos.md) require a Speaker worth listening to. Without Ethos, argument and emotion arrive from nowhere. The [Audience](position_audience.md) does not receive them. They dismiss the source first.

## Apparent
The character of the [Speaker](position_speaker.md) as it appears in the speech itself. Not reputation brought from outside - that is not technical. What is technical: the demonstration of practical intelligence, virtue, and good will through what is said. Present from the first sentence. Cannot be added later.

## Yearbook
Aristotle names it the most effective means of persuasion the Speaker possesses - more effective than argument alone.
The Romantics reduced it to personal charisma. The accurate reading: a constructed demonstration, built in the speech, of why this Speaker on this occasion is worth trusting.
It travels beyond the spoken word: every byline, every author bio, every platform profile - Ethos is running.
On Substack, it arrives before the first paragraph. The reader has already seen the name.
