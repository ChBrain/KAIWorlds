# Piece: Pathos

## Owner
- **Project:** Rhetoric
- **Process:** [Pistis](process_pistis.md)

## Place
Where the [Audience](position_audience.md) is. Fires inside the Audience, not on the stage.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and [Pistis](process_pistis.md) produces argument without landing. The [Audience](position_audience.md) may follow the logic and still not move. Judgment is not purely rational. Aristotle notes that people judge differently depending on their emotional state. Pathos changes the state in which the judgment is formed.

## Apparent
The emotional condition of the [Audience](position_audience.md) as the [Speaker](position_speaker.md) shapes it. Not manipulation - orientation. The Speaker must understand what the Audience already feels, what it fears, what it desires. The piece works by connecting the claim to those existing states. It does not manufacture emotion from nothing. It finds what is already there and directs it.

## Yearbook
Aristotle devotes the whole of Rhetoric Book II Chapters 2-11 to the specific emotions - how each is produced and what conditions are required.
Plato's objection: Pathos bypasses reason and makes men weak. Aristotle's answer: it is a technical means of persuasion, not a manipulation, when deployed in service of a true claim.
The piece travels wherever an Audience must be moved to decide. Every fundraising letter, every closing argument, every product launch that tells a story before stating a price - Pathos is running.
