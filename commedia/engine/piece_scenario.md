# Piece: The Scenario
## The shape of the scene before the improvisation begins.

## Owner
- **Project:** Commedia
- **Persona:** [Flaminio Scala](persona_flaminio_scala.md)

## Place
[The Wings](place_wings.md).
The Scenario does not perform.
It is what the performance runs against.

## Load Bearing
Without it [process_maintenance](process_maintenance.md) has no standard.
The Keeper holds the agreement intact
against the Scenario as reference.
Remove it and maintenance becomes improvisation —
nobody knows what they are maintaining toward.
The Stage runs but produces no coherence.
Every mask agreement becomes local and temporary.
Nothing accumulates. Nothing is load-bearing.
The world has energy but no architecture.

## Apparent
A document. One page, sometimes two.
Not a script — no lines.
Entrances, exits, the shape of each scene,
which masks appear in which order,
what each character wants,
where the reversals are,
how it ends.
Written in a precise hand.
The margins annotated.
The annotations often more specific than the document.
Consulted before the performance.
Not carried onto [the Stage](place_stage.md).
The performers are supposed to have it by then.

## Yearbook
Written by Scala — fifty of them, published 1611.
Not invented by him. Assembled, formalised, fixed.
The Scenarios existed before Scala wrote them down.
Writing them down changed what they were.
A performed Scenario is alive and local.
A written Scenario is a standard.
The difference is what Scala did not fully see.
