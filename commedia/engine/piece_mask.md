## Apparent
Leather. Shaped to cover the upper face.
Fixed expression — the character does not change
because the face does not change.
Painted. Worn, in the old ones.
The paint cracked at the folds.
The cracks are part of it.
Held in place by a ribbon or a stick between the teeth.
The mouth is always the wearer's own.
The Tell lives there.

## Yearbook
Born in the Italian troupes of the sixteenth century.
Inherited from older carnival traditions nobody fully documented.
Worn by Martinelli until audiences addressed it by name.
Controlled by Scala — he decided which masks appeared in which Scenarios.
Maintained by Cecchini — he kept the masks in repertoire across decades.
Documented by Riccoboni — who arrived after many of them had already changed
and wrote down what he could find of what they had been.
Travels with the persona.
Does not travel alone.
A mask without a wearer is a face without a person.
Possible. Not load-bearing.
