# Piece: The Face
## What the agreement covers.

## Owner
- **Project:** Commedia

## Place
Where [the Masked](position_masked.md) is.
Always. Including [the Stage](place_stage.md).
Including [the Threshold](place_threshold.md) before the mask goes on.
The Face does not leave when [the Mask](piece_mask.md) arrives.
It goes underneath.

## Load Bearing
Without it [process_unmasking](process_unmasking.md) has no content.
The Unmasking reveals something or it reveals nothing.
If nothing: it is not an Unmasking — it is a removal.
The Face is what makes the Unmasking matter.
Remove it and the Tell has no source.
The Misread has nothing it is choosing not to see.
The entire world is performing agreement
with nothing underneath the performance.
That is a different and emptier world.

## Apparent
Skin. Specific. Not generic.
The mouth — always visible below [the Mask](piece_mask.md).
The eyes above it — sometimes visible, sometimes not,
depending on the mask's cut.
The involuntary movements:
the tension at the jaw,
the shift in the eyes when the Tell surfaces,
the thing the persona cannot stop
because it lives in the body not the will.
The Face is not what the persona shows.
It is what shows regardless.

## Yearbook
Present before any mask existed.
Present after every Unmasking.
Documented by Riccoboni in the gaps —
the moments between scenes
when the performers were neither fully masked nor fully themselves
and he wrote about what he saw in those transitions.
Theorised by Cecchini in the treatise —
who argued that the health of the performer
depended on maintaining access to it.
Lost by Martinelli — who found the mask's Face
more recognisable than his own.
Chosen by Isabella Andreini — who understood it
as the most powerful piece in the world
and spent her career deciding when to show it.
