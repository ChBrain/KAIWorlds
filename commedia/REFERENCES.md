# References

## Flaminio Scala (c.1552-1624)

Actor, playwright, and company leader. Member of the Gelosi troupe.
Published *Il Teatro delle Favole Rappresentative* (1611) - fifty Scenarios fixed in writing.
The act of writing them down changed what they were.
A performed Scenario is alive and local. A written Scenario is a standard.
Scala did not fully see the difference between those two things.

---

## Tristano Martinelli (c.1557-1630)

Most celebrated Arlecchino of the commedia dell'arte tradition.
Created a version of the character so complete that audiences addressed the mask by name.
Corresponded with kings and cardinals in character.
The mask's precision where chaos was promised: his Tell.

---

## Isabella Andreini (1562-1604)

Actress, playwright, and poet. Member of the Gelosi troupe alongside her husband Francesco.
Performed the Innamorata role - unmasked by convention, which made it the most powerful mask on the Stage.
Admitted to the Accademia degli Intenti, Pavia, 1601.
Died in Lyon on tour.
Understood the Face as the most powerful piece in the world and spent her career deciding when to show it.

**The works this world is built on:**

*Mirtilla* - Isabella Andreini
Published 1588. A pastoral drama. One of the first published dramatic works by a woman.

---

## Francesco Andreini (1548-1624)

Actor and playwright. Created the Capitano character in a form that became definitive.
Husband of Isabella Andreini.
His Tell: retirement - the shape of the absence.
After Isabella's death he retired from performance and spent the remaining decades editing her work for publication.

---

## Pier Maria Cecchini (1563-1645)

Actor, company manager, and theorist.
Performed Fritellino across decades of Italian and French touring.
Kept the mask tradition in repertoire through administrative and institutional pressure.

**The works this world is built on:**

*Frutti delle moderne comedie et avisi a chi le recita* - Pier Maria Cecchini
Published 1628. A treatise on the theory and practice of commedia performance.
Argues that the health of the performer depends on maintaining access to the Face underneath the Mask.

---

## Luigi Riccoboni (1676-1753)

Actor, playwright, and theatre historian.
Revived the Italian comedy in Paris (Theatre-Italien) in 1716.
Arrived after the masks had changed and wrote down what he could find of what they had been.

**The works this world is built on:**

*Histoire du Theatre Italien* - Luigi Riccoboni
Published 1728. The primary documentary source for the history of commedia dell'arte.
Written in French for a French readership.
His Tell: the reform argument - grief performing as programme notes.

---

## Carlo Goldoni (1707-1793)

Playwright. Born Venice.
Replaced the Scenario with the fully written script across 137 plays in 30 years.
Not because improvisation was bad - because the Mask cannot survive a fully written character.
Did this from inside the system, quietly, until the unmasked plays made the masked ones look like what they were.
Left Venice for Paris in 1762. Never returned.
His Tell: the departure.

**The works this world is built on:**

*Memorie* - Carlo Goldoni
Published 1787. Written in French. His autobiography - the shape of the full account.

Selected plays consulted: *La Putta Onorata* (1749), *La Locandiera* (1753), *Il Campiello* (1756).
