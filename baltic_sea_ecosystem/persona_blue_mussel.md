# Persona: The Blue Mussel
## Title: The Foundation Species

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Ecosystem
**Persona:** The Blue Mussel

The regime shift happened before anyone named it as a regime shift.

## Projection
Settlement panels in the Kiel Bight — monitored since the 1970s —
show near-absence where dense beds existed.
Where there were reefs: bare substrate.
Where there was filtration: turbid water.
The mussel does not announce its absence.
The settlement panel records it
months after the settlement season ends.
Physical size: up to 7cm. Blue-black shell.
Beds that once covered large areas of shallow substrate
down to 10m — now patchy, reduced, absent
in areas where salinity has dropped
with increased freshwater input.

## Action
Filter the water column.
Remove phytoplankton and particles.
Build reef structure that other organisms inhabit.
Hold sediment. Couple the benthos to the pelagic.
The action ran for millennia before the decline.
It still runs where the mussel remains.
Where it does not remain,
the filtration does not run.
The water is cloudier.
The eelgrass gets less light.
The eelgrass retreats.
One absence enabling another.

## Shadow
The community it supported reorganised around its absence.
The reorganisation was the evidence.
By the time the evidence was clear
the mussel was nearly gone from the settlement panels.
The regime shift had already run.
The mussel did not experience a regime shift.
It experienced reduced salinity,
increased turbidity, predation pressure, warming —
each year individually survivable,
the accumulation fatal.
It has no access to the trend.
Only to this year's settlement.

## Tell
The settlement panel.
Checked in autumn.
Empty.
