# Place: Boknis Eck
## Location: Exit of Eckernförde Bay, southwestern Kiel Bight. 54°31.77'N, 10°02.36'E.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Ecosystem
**Persona:** The Oxygen

## Shown
- A fixed point on the seafloor. 28 metres depth.
  Two kilometres off the coast of Eckernförde.
- The sensors. Temperature. Salinity. Oxygen.
  Chlorophyll. Nutrients. Trace gases.
  Recording what the water column is doing
  at this exact location, every month,
  since 30 April 1957.
- The research vessel LITTORINA arriving once a month.
  The water sampler lowered on a cable.
  The bottles brought up.
  The measurements taken on deck.
- The surface above: Eckernförde Bay.
  The town on the shore.
  The German Navy torpedo range.
  The First Submarine Squadron.
  Both present. Neither acknowledges the other.
- The water column above the sensors.
  In spring: cold, mixed, oxygen-rich throughout.
  In August: stratified.
  Warm and oxygen-rich above twelve metres.
  Cold and oxygen-depleted below.
- The seafloor sediment.
  Sandy mud. Accumulating.
  Seasonally hypoxic.
  In August: sometimes sulfidic.
  Hydrogen sulfide measurable in the porewater.
  The sensors record it.
- The new underwater observatory.
  Deployed November 2024.
  Anchored to the seafloor by research divers from Kiel University.
  Continuous sensors. Real-time data via cable to shore.

## Holds
- [The Boknis Eck Time Series](piece_time_series.md).
  67 years. Monthly. Nine parameters.
- The new underwater observatory.
  Running again after five years of absence.

## Offers
- The paradox. Available to anyone who reads the graph.
  Nutrients down since the late 1980s.
  Oxygen still falling.
  The graph is public.
  Both lines. One image.
  Available here without commentary.
- The time series itself.
  67 years of monthly measurements.
  The longest continuous coastal marine record
  in the southwestern Baltic.
- The gap.
  August 2019 to November 2024.
  Five years without continuous observatory data.
  The monthly ship visits continued.
  The short-term dynamics were not recorded.
  The gap is in the record.
  Available as absence.

## Withheld
- What the observatory saw before it disappeared.
  Installed January 2019. Gone August 2019.
  Seven months of high-resolution continuous data.
  The observatory recorded the formation of oxygen minimum zones
  in real time for the first time.
  Then it was gone.
  Where it went: unknown.
  The navy checked. No vessels were in the area.
  The shore cable was torn and the station had vanished.
  What it was beginning to show
  about the short-term dynamics of the dead zone —
  the speed of formation, the trigger events —
  is in the seven months of data that were collected
  and in the five years of data that were never collected.
- The baseline before 1957.
  What Boknis Eck recorded before it was a station.
  The oxygen in August 1900.
  The phytoplankton community in 1905.
  The station began in 1957.
  The sea had already been under pressure
  for a decade from postwar agricultural intensification.
  The sediment below the sensors holds the pre-1957 record.
  Nobody has asked the sediment
  in the language the station uses.
