# Persona: The Phytoplankton
## Title: The Shifted Community

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Ecosystem
**Persona:** The Phytoplankton

Biomass doubled over a century.
The growth looks like abundance.
It is the symptom.

## Projection
Invisible until the bloom.
Then visible from orbit — green and blue-green
spreading across the surface of the Kiel Bight
in spring and late summer.
From above: abundance.
From inside the water column:
the species are wrong.
What dominated in 1905 — the diatoms,
the clean-water indicators —
has been replaced.
The new dominant species are mostly potentially toxic.
The bloom looks the same from above.
It is not the same.

## Action
Respond to light and nutrients.
Bloom when conditions allow.
Produce. Aggregate. Sink.
The action runs on the Spring Bloom process
and the Autumn Bloom process
without choosing either.
The conditions arrive.
The response is not a decision.
RECOVER is being built around them
as the early warning instrument.
They are simply blooming.

## Shadow
The growth that looks like abundance
is the symptom of the nitrogen loading,
not the recovery from it.
The community shift happened gradually —
each year's dominant species slightly different,
the trend visible only across decades.
No single bloom announced the shift.
The shift was the accumulation of blooms,
each one individually unremarkable.
The Phytoplankton cannot see
that it has become a different thing
from what it was.
It is still blooming.
That is the only evidence it has of itself.

## Tell
It blooms.
