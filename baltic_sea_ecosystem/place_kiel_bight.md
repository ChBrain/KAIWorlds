# Place: The Kiel Bight
## Location: Western Baltic Sea, between Kiel and the Danish coast.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Ecosystem
**Persona:** The Oxygen

## Shown
- Water. 25 metres average depth.
  The surface catches light and wind.
  Below: cold, stratified from April to October,
  the thermocline at twelve metres.
- The thermocline. Warm surface water on cold deep water.
  Below it: less oxygen. Getting less every decade.
- The dead zone. Below the thermocline in August.
  Less than 2mg oxygen per litre in the bottom water.
  The fish have already moved.
  The benthos in the affected zone has died.
- The coast to the west: Kiel, Eckernförde, Schleswig-Holstein.
  Agriculture behind it. The nitrogen arrives through rivers and groundwater.
- The open connection to the east: the Belts, the central Baltic.
  What enters here travels.
  What is depleted here affects what travels.
- The seafloor. Sandy mud at 25 metres.
  Sediment accumulating since the sea formed.
- [Boknis Eck](place_boknis_eck.md).
  Fixed to the seafloor at the exit of Eckernförde Bay.

## Holds
- [The Paradox](piece_paradox.md).
  Everywhere in the water column.
- [The Cascade Through Absence](piece_cascade_through_absence.md).
  In every empty position the web runs around.
- The food web.
  Phytoplankton, zooplankton, sprat, herring,
  cod, harbour porpoise, eelgrass, blue mussel,
  bacteria, mysids, amphipods, eel.
  All present. All under pressure.
- The sediment archive.
  Everything that has sunk here since the sea formed.
  Pollen layers. Shell middens. Organic matter.

## Offers
- The annual cycle.
  Spring bloom. Sinking. Summer stratification.
  Benthic coupling. Autumn bloom. Winter dormancy.
  The Witness can enter at any point in the cycle.
  The cycle does not pause for entry.
- The thermocline.
  At twelve metres in summer.
  Knowable without being visible.
- The organisms.
  Each one present and under pressure.
  Each one holdable for the duration of attention.
- The coast.
  Visible from the water.

## Withheld
- What the Kiel Bight held in 1900.
  Eelgrass beds extending to 4–5 metres depth
  across 75–80% of the area they now cover.
  Blue mussel reefs on the shallow substrate.
  Cod populations at pre-industrial density.
  Oxygen in the bottom water in August:
  above the hypoxic threshold.
  The Bight holds this in the sediment record.
  The shells of organisms that are no longer there.
  The pollen layers from a different agricultural regime.
  It has not been asked to compare.
  The comparison requires a question
  that has not yet been brought to it
  in the language it can answer.
