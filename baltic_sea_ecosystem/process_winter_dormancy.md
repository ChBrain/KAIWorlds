# Process: The Winter Dormancy
## The accumulation that makes the next bloom inevitable.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Ecosystem
**Persona:** The Oxygen

## Initiates
Light fails. Temperature drops throughout the water column.
Mixing deepens. Nutrients from decomposition
accumulate in the now-homogeneous water column.
The phytoplankton are dormant or resting.
The positions are held by no one.
The web idles.

## Direction
Toward readiness. Nutrients accumulate.
Oxygen slowly recovers in the deep water
through winter mixing — but not fully.
Each year the recovery is fractionally less complete
than the year before.
The warming has shortened the mixing season.
What accumulates in winter is now the limit
of what next spring can produce.

## Lever
The mixing depth. How deep winter circulation reaches.
It carries oxygen down and nutrients up.
Warming reduces mixing depth.
A shallower mix means less oxygen delivered below,
less nutrient returned above.
The lever is weakening.

## Echo
Nutrients are loaded. Oxygen is partially restored.
The Spring Bloom is inevitable.
The baseline is lower than last year.
The system has not returned to where it was.
It never does.
