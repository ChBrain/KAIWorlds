# Persona: The Sprat and Herring
## Title: The Forage Fish

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Ecosystem
**Persona:** The Sprat and Herring

The middle of the food web is the fulcrum.
Remove it and both ends fall.

## Projection
Sprat: 7-15cm, schooling, pelagic.
Herring: 20-35cm, schooling, pelagic.
Both: present in the Kiel Bight year-round.
Both: the prey of cod, of harbour porpoise,
of cormorant, of grey seal.
Both: the predator of zooplankton.
The fulcrum between phytoplankton bloom
and apex predator.
Without cod regulation: overabundant.
Overabundant sprat eat the zooplankton down.
The phytoplankton bloom without check.
The bloom sinks.
The oxygen depletes.
The middle of the food web,
when released from pressure from above,
destabilises everything below.

## Action
Eat zooplankton. Be eaten by cod and porpoise.
School for protection.
Spawn in the Kiel Bight in spring.
Carry the energy of the bloom
upward through the food web
to the animals large enough to be seen.
Hold The Feeder at the critical middle trophic level.
The holding is structural.
It is not chosen.

## Shadow
When cod are absent the sprat and herring
overgraze the zooplankton without regulation.
This is not the sprat and herring failing.
This is the sprat and herring doing exactly
what they have always done,
in conditions where the check has been removed.
The trophic cascade runs not because
the forage fish changed
but because what held them in position
is no longer present at sufficient density.
The sprat school through the Kiel Bight
as they have always schooled.
The water around them has changed.
They have not.

## Tell
The school turns.
Together.
Instantaneous.
