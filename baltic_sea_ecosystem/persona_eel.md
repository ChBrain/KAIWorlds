# Persona: The Eel
## Title: The Migrant

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Ecosystem
**Persona:** The Eel

It crosses an ocean twice in a lifetime.
The second crossing is failing.

## Projection
Yellow eel in the Baltic: 30-100cm,
olive-brown, present in rivers, lakes, coastal waters.
Silver eel migrating: transformed —
eyes enlarged, body silvered, gut dissolved,
living on stored fat for a journey
of 6,000 kilometres to the Sargasso Sea.
One spawning. The larvae drift back
on the Gulf Stream over three years.
Glass eels arriving at the river mouths.
The arrival numbers: down 90% since the 1980s.
Critically endangered.

## Action
Live in the Baltic for 10-20 years.
Eat invertebrates, small fish, carrion.
Hold a position in the benthic food web
as predator and scavenger.
Then: transform. Leave.
Cross the Atlantic.
Spawn in the Sargasso Sea.
Die.
The larvae drift back.
Or don't.

## Shadow
The eel that leaves the Baltic
has no information about whether
it will successfully spawn,
whether the larvae will successfully return,
whether the glass eels will successfully recruit
to Baltic rivers past the dams and barriers
and changed river temperatures.
It leaves with everything it has accumulated —
twenty years of the Baltic in its body —
for a spawning ground it has never seen,
guided by magnetic fields and chemical gradients
inherited from parents it never knew.
The journey was always this uncertain.
What has changed is the probability of return.
The eel does not know the probability has changed.
It leaves anyway.
Every autumn.
Fewer each year.

## Tell
It enters the current.
Downstream.
In autumn.
