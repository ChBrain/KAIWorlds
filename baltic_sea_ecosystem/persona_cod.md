# Persona: The Cod
## Title: The Recoverable

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Ecosystem
**Persona:** The Cod

Recoverable in principle. Not yet recovered.

## Projection
Up to 1.5m, demersal — living and feeding near the bottom
in the Kiel Bight at 10-25m depth.
Spawns in late winter in the Kiel Bight area.
The eggs require cold, oxygen-rich water near the bottom.
The oxygen near the bottom in August
is below 2mg per litre.
The spawning ground and the dead zone
occupy the same water.
Western Baltic cod: distinct population,
managed separately from North Sea cod.
Stock: at historically low levels.
Recovery: modelled as possible within a decade
of ecosystem-based management.
That management: not yet implemented.

## Action
Eat sprat. Eat herring. Eat invertebrates.
Regulate the forage fish population.
Without the cod, sprat and herring
overgraze the zooplankton.
The zooplankton crash.
The phytoplankton bloom unchecked.
The bloom sinks.
The oxygen depletes.
The cod's absence runs through
the entire food web below it.
The action the cod performs
is the regulation that prevents cascade.
It is not performing it at sufficient population levels.

## Shadow
The recovery is possible. The model shows it.
Ecosystem-based management would rebuild the stock.
The management has not been implemented.
The cod holds the proof of what could happen
and the evidence of what has not.
It does not experience this as possibility.
It experiences the oxygen at the bottom
dropping below the threshold for its eggs.
It moves to shallower water.
The shallower water is where the fishing pressure is highest.
The cod does not know it is recoverable.
It knows: go where the oxygen is.

## Tell
It moves to shallower water.
In August.
