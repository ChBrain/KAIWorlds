# Piece: The Paradox
## Doing the right thing is no longer enough.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Ecosystem
**Persona:** The Oxygen

---

## Place
[The Kiel Bight](place_kiel_bight.md).
Everywhere in the water column.

## Load Bearing
Without it the world has a problem with a solution.
With it the world has a problem to which the solution
has already been applied — and the problem continues.
Remove it and the ecosystem becomes a case of neglect.
With it the ecosystem is something different:
a system that did the right thing
in conditions where the right thing
is no longer sufficient.
That difference is the entire pressure
this world exerts on whoever enters it.

## Apparent
Two lines on a graph.
Nutrients: declining from the late 1980s.
Oxygen at 25 metres: declining across the same period.
Both in the same dataset.
One moving down from the right cause.
One moving down from the wrong direction.
The graph fits on a single screen.

## Yearbook
Present in the data before it was named.
The nutrient reduction began in the late 1980s.
The oxygen continued to fall through the 1990s,
2000s, 2010s.
Each year the data collected.
Each year the paradox in the data.

2014: named. Biogeosciences.
The two lines examined together statistically
across the full record for the first time.
Still not in the room where decisions are made.

2024: mechanism confirmed. von Jackowski et al., *Scientific Reports*.
Warming has taken over from eutrophication
as the primary driver of oxygen decline.
The internal phosphorus load named as feedback —
oxygen minima release phosphorus from sediment,
phosphorus feeds the next bloom,
the next bloom sinks,
bacteria consume the oxygen,
oxygen minima deepen,
more phosphorus released.
The feedback cannot currently be interrupted.

The paradox does not resolve.
It deepens.
Each year the warming continues,
the paradox deepens.
