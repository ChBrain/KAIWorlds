# Persona: The Zooplankton
## Title: The Grazers

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Ecosystem
**Persona:** The Zooplankton

They arrive after the bloom has already committed.
The timing is the technique.
It has never quite worked.

## Projection
Copepods, cladocerans, ciliates.
Millimetres long. Present in their billions.
The spring copepod maximum follows the spring bloom
by days or weeks depending on temperature.
Warming shifts the bloom earlier.
The copepod maximum follows —
but not by the same margin.
The gap between bloom and grazer is widening.
Each spring the bloom has more time
to sink before it is grazed.

## Action
Graze the bloom while it is in the water column.
Control the sinking.
Hold The Grazer.
When the bloom sinks anyway —
follow it. Eat the particles in descent.
Migrate vertically: surface at night,
depth by day.
Carry what was pelagic toward
what is benthic in the body.

## Shadow
They are the check on the bloom
that the warming is making late.
Each decade the phenological mismatch
between bloom and grazer widens fractionally.
The Zooplankton do not experience this as drift —
they experience it as a single spring,
each year, in which they arrive
and the bloom is already past its peak.
They have no memory of the springs
when the timing was different.
They have only this spring.
This spring the bloom peaked early.

## Tell
It grazes what remains.
