# Piece: The Boknis Eck Time Series
## The 67-year record that makes the paradox legible.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Ecosystem
**Persona:** The Oxygen

---

## Place
[Boknis Eck](place_boknis_eck.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it the paradox has no proof.
The oxygen is falling despite the nutrients declining —
both facts are in this record.
Remove it and the world has concern but no evidence.
The record is the only reason the paradox is legible at all.
Without it every claim about this ecosystem
is a claim without the number that makes it a fact.

## Apparent
Monthly measurements. Nine parameters. 1957 to present.
Two breaks: 1976–78, 1983–85.
The dataset: rows of numbers.
Temperature. Salinity. Oxygen. Chlorophyll.
Nutrients. Trace gases.
Each row: one month. One station. One depth profile.
67 years of rows.
The graph drawn from them: two lines.
One image.

## Yearbook
30 April 1957.
Johannes Krey, Professor of Planktology,
Institut für Meereskunde, Kiel.
The first water sampler lowered.
The first measurements taken.
The sea already under nitrogen pressure
from postwar agricultural intensification.

1960: chlorophyll measurements added.

1979: nutrient data added.

Late 1980s: nutrient concentrations begin to decline.
The oxygen recovery expected.
The oxygen recovery does not arrive.

2014: first full statistical evaluation published.
Lennartz et al., Biogeosciences.
The paradox named.
Not yet in the room where decisions are made.

2016: permanent underwater observatory installed.
Continuous measurements for the first time.

August 2019: the observatory disappears.
Shore cable torn. Station gone.
Five years of continuous data never collected.

2024: warming confirmed as primary driver
overtaking eutrophication.
Internal phosphorus load feedback named.

November 2024: new observatory deployed from FS ALKOR.
The continuous record resumes.
The gap: 2019–2024.
Visible. Permanent. Unexplained.
