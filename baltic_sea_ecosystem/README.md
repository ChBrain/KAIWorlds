## What this world is

The western Baltic Sea holds a record of what it was and what it is becoming.
The Kiel Bight: 25 metres average depth. The Boknis Eck monitoring node
recording since 1957. The thermocline that separates the world above
from the world below. The organisms that have been here longer than
any measurement, longer than any border, longer than any decision
about what to do with them.

This world is elegiac. It runs on witness.

The audience enters to understand what exists before it doesn't.

## The personas

**The Oxygen** — present everywhere, declining in the deep basins.
Cannot announce its own absence.

**The Phytoplankton** — doubled in biomass over a century.
Shifted toward the toxic. Growth that looks like abundance
is the symptom, not the recovery.

**The Zooplankton** — arrives after the bloom has already committed.
The timing is the technique. It has never quite worked.

**The Blue Mussel** — foundational species. Near-disappeared from
Kiel Bight. The regime shift happened before anyone named it.

**The Eelgrass** — habitat-former, carbon sink, fish nursery.
Was here. Is retreating.

**The Cod** — recoverable in principle. Not yet recovered.

**The Sprat and Herring** — the middle of the food web is the fulcrum.
Remove it and both ends fall.

**The Harbour Porpoise** — the mammal. Visible. Endangered.
Arrives last in the chain of collapse.

**The Bacteria** — they run the process that runs the paradox.

**The Eel** — crosses an ocean twice in a lifetime.
The second crossing is failing.

## Terminology note

Position = Role throughout this world.
The term Position is used in place of Role to reflect
the ecosystem context — positions in the food web
are assigned by processes before any organism arrives,
the same way roles are assigned by institutions
before any persona arrives. The structure is identical:
Has / Orders / Loses / Drives.

## Architecture note

This world runs on five components simultaneously:
Processes assign Personas to Positions as they run.
Places exert pressure on whoever enters them.
Pieces travel and carry weight.
The interference condition is met —

seven Processes assigning them to Positions they did not choose.
