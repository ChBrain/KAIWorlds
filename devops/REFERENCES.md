# References

## The DevOps Chain

The chain itself is community property. No single author owns it.
Everyone has run it. Everyone has broken it. The stages were not invented — they were agreed upon.

---

## The Canonical Texts

**Gene Kim, Patrick Debois, John Willis, Jez Humble**
*The DevOps Handbook: How to Create World-Class Agility, Reliability, & Security in Technology Organizations*
IT Revolution Press, 2016.

The standard reference for the practices behind the chain. The handbook does not invent the loop — it names the failure modes that prevent it closing.

---

**Gene Kim, Kevin Behr, George Spafford**
*The Phoenix Project: A Novel about IT, DevOps, and Helping Your Business Win*
IT Revolution Press, 2013.

The failure mode as narrative. The plant floor logic applied to the software factory. The Three Ways as the principle beneath the chain.

---

**Jez Humble, David Farley**
*Continuous Delivery: Reliable Software Releases through Build, Test, and Deployment Automation*
Addison-Wesley, 2010.

The pipeline as engineering object. The deployment pipeline as the artefact of the entire chain.
