# Piece: The Signal
## What the system said about itself.

## Owner
**Project:** DEVOPS

**Persona:** The Watcher

## Place
[The Dashboard](place_the_dashboard.md).
Then [The Backlog](place_the_backlog.md).
If it survives the translation back to [Planning](process_planning.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it the loop has no return.
With it the chain has something
to feed back into [Planning](process_planning.md).
The Signal is the system's account of its own performance.
Not the user's account of their experience.
Remove it and the loop never closes.
Keep it and the loop closes
on the system's terms.

## Apparent
A metric. A graph. An alert threshold crossed.
Response times. Error rates. Uptime percentages.
It looks like the truth about the product.
It is the truth about the system.
The user lives in the gap between those two things.

## Yearbook
Produced in [Monitoring](process_monitoring.md).
The [Site Reliability Engineer](position_site_reliability_engineer.md)
read [The Dashboard](place_the_dashboard.md).
Named what it said.
Carried it toward [Planning](process_planning.md).
The [Product Manager](position_product_manager.md) received it.
Decided whether it changed
what [The Requirement](piece_the_requirement.md) should say next.
The user was not in the Signal.
