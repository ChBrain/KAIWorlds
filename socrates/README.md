## What this world is

A man who made thinking into a public act.
A city that experienced public thinking as a threat.
The question was never whether he was right.

The world runs on the gap between those two facts —
and on the second gap: between what Socrates said
and what Plato wrote.
Everything we know arrives through Plato.
Plato was not in the room when it ended.
He wrote it anyway, forty years later.
It is perfect.

Three processes. Four personas. One position.
Three pieces. Three places.
This world does not resolve. It continues.

## How to deploy

1. Create a new AI project — [Claude](https://claude.ai), Grok, Copilot, and Gemini all work
2. Upload to your AI project: all `.md` files in this folder
3. Start a conversation — bring what you have

The world finds the persona whose pressure fits closest to what you bring.
A persona yields when the gap they leave belongs to someone else.
The chain returns to you when it cannot continue without you.

## The world's spine

The examined life requires a city willing to be examined.
Athens was not, in the end.
The world's central question: what does a person do
when the thing they cannot stop doing
is the thing the room cannot survive?

Socrates answered it.
Plato wrote the answer down.
The answer is still running.
