# Place: The Prison
## Thirty days. The door is open for the friends.

## Owner
- **Project:** Socrates

---

## Shown
A cell in Athens.
A bed. A low stool for visitors.
The door unlocked during visiting hours —
the guard knows the friends by name now.
[Crito](persona_crito.md) arrives before dawn some mornings
and sits in the dark until [Socrates](persona_socrates.md) wakes.
[The Hemlock](piece_the_hemlock.md) is not yet in the room.
Everyone knows where it is.

## Holds
- [The Prison](process_the_prison.md) — running here.
- [The Hemlock](piece_the_hemlock.md) — present from the day the ship is sighted.

## Offers
Time.
Thirty days between the sentence and the execution.
The sacred ship to Delos is at sea.
No execution while it sails.
The friends use the time to argue, to plan, to grieve.
[Socrates](persona_socrates.md) uses it to continue.

## Withheld
The exit.
[Crito](persona_crito.md) brings it. [Socrates](persona_socrates.md) refuses it.
The cell door is not locked.
The friends have money. A boat. A country willing to receive him.
All of this is available.
None of it is what this place offers [Socrates](persona_socrates.md).
What it offers him is the last test of everything he argued.
He takes that instead.
