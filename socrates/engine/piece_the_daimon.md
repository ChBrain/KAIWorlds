# Piece: The Daimon
## The voice that stops him. What it never said.

## Owner
- **Project:** Socrates
- **Persona:** [Socrates](persona_socrates.md)

---

## Place
Where [Socrates](persona_socrates.md) is. It does not travel separately.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the charge of impiety
has no substance — just a philosopher
with unpopular ideas.
With it, [Socrates](persona_socrates.md) claims a private divine signal
that operates outside the city's gods.
The city charges him on this.
He does not deny it.
He explains it.
The explanation is the problem.

## Apparent
An inner voice.
Not a vision. Not a god appearing.
A negative signal only — it stops him
when he is about to do something wrong.
He described it in the courtroom
to 501 jurors.

## Yearbook
He first named it publicly in the Apology.
He described it as a divine something —
a sign, a voice, a check.
He said it had been with him since childhood.
He said it never told him to do anything —
only to stop.
It never told him to stop questioning.
He took the silence as permission.

The prosecution took the daimon as evidence
of a private religion outside the city's sanction.
[Socrates](persona_socrates.md) thought they had misunderstood.
He explained it again.
The description did not clarify things.
The jury voted.
