# Piece: What Plato Heard
## The distance between the voice and the text.

## Owner
- **Project:** Socrates
- **Persona:** [Plato](persona_plato.md)

---

## Place
Where [Plato](persona_plato.md) is. It does not live in any room.
It lives in the distance between rooms.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the world has a philosopher
whose words we possess completely.
With it, the world has a philosopher
whose words we possess entirely through one man
who loved him, who was not present at the end,
and who wrote the most detailed account of the end
forty years later.
Remove this piece and there is no problem.
The problem is the world's spine.

## Apparent
A gap.
Not visible in any single text.
Visible only when you ask:
is this what Socrates said,
or is this what Plato needed him to have said?
The question has no answer.
The gap is the answer.

## Yearbook
The Apology was written within a few years of the trial.
[Plato](persona_plato.md) was present. He names himself once.
The account is the closest to the event.
It is still a reconstruction.

The Phaedo was written forty years later.
It is the account of the death.
[Plato](persona_plato.md) was not present.
It is the most detailed, most composed,
most philosophically complete account
in the entire corpus.
Its perfection is the evidence.

Scholars have spent two thousand years
trying to locate the historical Socrates
beneath the Platonic Socrates.
The project has not succeeded.
Not because the scholars failed —
because the gap is not a problem to be solved.
It is the piece the world runs on.
