# Piece: The Hemlock
## The sentence, made concrete.

## Owner
- **Project:** Socrates

---

## Place
[The Prison](place_the_prison.md). A cup on a table.
Present from the moment the ship returns.

## Load Bearing
Without it the sentence is an idea.
With it the sentence is an object in the room,
visible to everyone,
requiring no explanation.
The philosophy either holds here or it doesn't.
[Socrates](persona_socrates.md) has been arguing toward this test for forty years.
He does not know that until now.

## Apparent
A clay cup.
Liquid the color of water, not quite.
On a table in [The Prison](place_the_prison.md).
Present from the morning the ship is sighted.

## Yearbook
The ship returned on the morning of the execution.
[Crito](persona_crito.md) confirmed this — he had been watching for it.

The friends were in the room.
[Plato](persona_plato.md) was not.
He says he was ill.

The guard who brought it had done this before.
He apologized before handing it across.
He said he had come to know Socrates
in these thirty days
and considered him the most noble man
who ever came to this place.
Then he turned and wept.
Socrates thanked him.

Socrates asked that the cup be brought
without waiting for evening.
He said he would only be laughing at himself
if he clung to life at this point.
He drank it.
He walked until his legs were heavy.
He lay down.
He said: *We owe a cock to Asclepius.*
The god of healing.
The debt of a man cured of something.

Nobody is certain what he meant.
