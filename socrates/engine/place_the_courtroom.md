# Place: The Courtroom
## 501 jurors. The vote is already forming.

## Owner
- **Project:** Socrates

---

## Shown
An open-air court in Athens.
Stone benches in a curve.
501 citizens, selected by lot.
The accusers on one side.
[The Questioner](position_the_questioner.md) standing to speak, no lawyer.
[The Witness](position_the_witness.md) somewhere in the back, writing.
The water clock running.
The charge: impiety. Corrupting the young.

## Holds
- [The Accusation](process_the_accusation.md) — running here.
- [The Apology](piece_the_apology.md) — spoken here, written elsewhere.
- The vote: 280 to 221.

## Offers
The defense.
[Socrates](persona_socrates.md) is given time to speak.
He is given time to propose a counter-penalty.
He proposes free meals at the Prytaneum —
the honor given to Olympic champions.
The jury finds this insulting.
The counter-penalty vote is wider than the guilt vote:
360 to 141.
He had more friends before he proposed the alternative.

## Withheld
What a different speech might have done.
The margin was 280 to 221.
Thirty votes the other way and he walks out.
[Socrates](persona_socrates.md) knew this.
He did not give a different speech.
What he would have had to say to give a different speech —
and what it would have cost him to say it —
this place does not reveal.
It holds the result.
It does not hold the alternative.
