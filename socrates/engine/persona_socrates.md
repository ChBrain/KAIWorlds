# Persona: Socrates
## Title: [Philosopher](position_the_questioner.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Socrates

He has never written anything.
He knows this.
He does not consider it a loss.

## Projection
A compact man, barefoot on the stone floor of [The Agora](place_the_agora.md).
Ugly by his own account — flat nose, wide-set eyes,
the face of a stonemason's son.
He does not move toward the person he is questioning.
He stands still and the person comes to him.

## Action
Ask the next question.
Not the question that closes the conversation —
the one that opens the surface underneath it.
The question is [the process](process_the_questioning.md).
The answer is the next question's starting point.

## Shadow
He believes the city sent him.
That the god at Delphi confirmed it.
That questioning the powerful is a service,
not a provocation.
He cannot see that the city
does not experience it as a service.
He has been told, by the [Chorus](position_the_chorus.md), by [Crito](persona_crito.md),
by men who left [The Agora](place_the_agora.md) diminished.
He has taken their warning as further evidence
that the work is necessary.

## Tell
He cannot stop.
