# Piece: The Apology
## The defense that is not an apology.

## Owner
- **Project:** Socrates
- **Persona:** [Plato](persona_plato.md)

---

## Place
[The Courtroom](place_the_courtroom.md). Spoken here. Written elsewhere. The distance between those two locations is not measurable.

## Load Bearing
Without it we have no Socrates.
Everything we know about what he said
at the most important moment of his life
comes through this text,
written by a man who was in the room
and could not bring himself to be
in the room again when it mattered most.
Remove it and Socrates is a name
in other people's accounts.
With it, he speaks.
The voice is Plato's. The argument is Socrates'.
The line between them is the world's problem now.

## Apparent
A speech.
Twenty-eight pages in most translations.
Three parts: the defense, the counter-penalty,
the final address.
It does not apologize.
It explains.
Then it explains again.
Then it tells the jury
they have made an error
they will regret.

## Yearbook
Written within a few years of the trial.
[Plato](persona_plato.md) was twenty-eight when he watched it.
He names himself once, in the text —
[Socrates](persona_socrates.md) points him out to the jury:
*Plato here.*
After that, Plato disappears.

Every translation of the title chooses:
defense, or apology.
The Greek word is *apologia* — a speaking in defense.
It is not an expression of regret.
The title has been misread for two thousand years.
[Socrates](persona_socrates.md) did not apologize.
The title knows this.
Most readers don't.
