# Place: The Agora
## The open space. Forty years of questions.

## Owner
- **Project:** Socrates

---

## Shown
The commercial and civic center of Athens.
Colonnades. Stalls. The smell of leather and fish.
People passing through on their way somewhere else.
[Socrates](persona_socrates.md) standing still in the middle of it.
Barefoot. No scroll. Nothing in his hands.
Waiting for the person whose certainty
is loudest today.

## Holds
- [The Questioning](process_the_questioning.md) — running here for forty years.

## Offers
A conversation.
With whoever stops.
The conversation is free.
[Socrates](persona_socrates.md) charges nothing.
He says this at the trial —
if he had charged, he would be less dangerous,
because only those who chose to pay
would have been affected.
He gave it away.
Everyone was affected.

## Withheld
The cost. And the accumulation.
The Agora looks like a place where things are exchanged.
Goods for money. Information for attention.
What [The Questioning](process_the_questioning.md) takes
does not look like a transaction.
The person who walks away knowing less
does not know yet what was taken.
They know by evening.
They remember in the morning.
The city's patience empties here, conversation by conversation,
and the place does not announce it.
