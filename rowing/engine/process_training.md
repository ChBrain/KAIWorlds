# Process: Training

## Owner
- **Project:** Rowing
- **Process:** [Stroke](process_stroke.md)

## Initiated by
The shell is on [flat water](place_flat_water.md) or [open water](place_open_water.md). The crew is assembled or the single sculler is alone. No race is scheduled for today. The [boathouse](place_boathouse.md) was unlocked before the water. The session has a plan — a distance, an interval, a drill — but the plan is not the race. What is being built today will not be used today.

## Direction
Toward a body that knows the stroke without being told. Technique, endurance, and timing are accumulated piece by piece across sessions. No single session completes the work. The direction is not toward today's finish — it is toward the rower that will exist in a month, a season, a year.

## Lever
Carrying force: repetition under attention. The [stroke](process_stroke.md) is run hundreds of times in a session. Each repetition is a chance to correct what the last one revealed — the catch that was a fraction late, the recovery where the hands dropped, the drive where the arms pulled before the legs had finished. The coach on the bank names what the rower cannot see from inside the boat.

Resistance: the body's tendency to revert. Under fatigue, the stroke shortens. The [slide](piece_slide.md) is rushed. The [blade](piece_blade.md) is extracted early. These are not failures of will — they are the body choosing efficiency over technique when technique costs more than the body has left. Training is the process of raising the threshold at which the reversion occurs.

## Echo
The session ends. The shell is returned to [the boathouse](place_boathouse.md). What changed is not visible today and will not be tested until the [race](process_race.md). The rower who comes back tomorrow is not the same rower who launched this morning. The difference is too small to measure and too real to doubt.
