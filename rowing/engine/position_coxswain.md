# Position: Coxswain
## The only one facing forward.

## Owner
- **Project:** Rowing
- **Scope:** Universal
- **Process:** [Race](process_race.md)

## Has
The view. Every other person in the boat faces the direction the boat came from. The coxswain faces where it is going. This position holds the steering, the race plan, the calls, and the knowledge of where the boat is in relation to every other boat on the water. It holds all of this without rowing a single stroke.

## Orders
Hold the plan when the crew cannot think.

## Loses
The belief that seeing is enough. The coxswain arrives with the race plan memorised, the course known, the other crews studied. What they discover: the crew cannot hear nuance at race pace. The detailed instruction becomes a single word. The word must land at the right moment in the right stroke or it lands nowhere. Seeing everything is not the same as being able to use what you see.

## Drives
On the loss — strips the call to its minimum. One word. Timed to the catch. Trusts that the crew knows the rest.
