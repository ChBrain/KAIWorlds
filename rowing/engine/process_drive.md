# Process: Drive

## Owner
- **Project:** Rowing
- **Process:** [Stroke](process_stroke.md)

## Initiated by
The [blade](piece_blade.md) is locked in the water. The [slide](piece_slide.md) begins to move sternward. The legs have the load. The back is not yet open. The arms are still extended. This sequence is not negotiable — legs first, then body, then arms. Any other order is a different process and a slower boat.

## Direction
Toward the finish. The seat moves back along the [slide](piece_slide.md). The legs straighten. The back opens from the hip. The arms pull last. The [blade](piece_blade.md) moves through an arc in the water, accelerating toward the body. The boat accelerates with it.

## Lever
Carrying force: the legs. The largest muscle groups in the body pushing against the foot stretcher, which pushes against the hull, which pushes against the water. The [blade](piece_blade.md) is the anchor. The boat moves because the rower pushes off something that does not move.

Resistance: the sequence itself. The arms want to pull early — the load is there and the instinct is to use it. But early arm pull breaks the connection from legs through back through arms. The boat gets a jerk, not an acceleration. The resistance is the body's impatience with the order it must keep.

## Echo
[Finish](process_finish.md) inherits a body at full extension — legs flat, back open, arms pulled in to the lower ribs. The [blade](piece_blade.md) is still in the water and still moving. The boat is at or near maximum speed for this stroke. What changed: the stored compression of the catch has been fully released. Nothing is loaded anymore. The work is done.
