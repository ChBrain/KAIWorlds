# Process: Finish

## Owner
- **Project:** Rowing
- **Process:** [Stroke](process_stroke.md)

## Initiated by
The legs are flat. The back is open and slightly behind vertical. The arms have completed their pull — handles drawn to the lower ribs, elbows past the body. The [blade](piece_blade.md) is at the end of its arc, perpendicular to the water, still under load. The boat is at maximum speed for this stroke. The rower is fully extended and there is nowhere left to go.

## Direction
Toward extraction. The hands press down and away from the body in one connected movement — down to lift the blade clear of the water, away to begin the recovery. The blade exits cleanly. The feather begins: the inside hand rolls the handle, turning the blade from perpendicular to parallel with the water surface.

## Lever
Carrying force: the boat's momentum. The shell is moving at its fastest. The extraction must not disturb that run — a clean finish preserves what the drive built.

Resistance: washing out. Lifting the hands too early or pulling the blade upward rather than pressing it out causes the blade to exit before the stroke is complete. The remaining load is lost. The [puddle](piece_puddle.md) is shallow and close — it shows the boat the stroke was short.

## Echo
[Recovery](process_recovery.md) inherits a [blade](piece_blade.md) above the water, feathered, and a body that is fully extended. The [puddle](piece_puddle.md) is on the water at the point of extraction — the stroke's only physical record. What changed: the [blade](piece_blade.md) is free. The boat runs on what was given to it. Nothing more can be added to this stroke.
