# Place: Flat Water

## Owner
- **Project:** Rowing

## Shown
A course marked by buoys. The water surface is still or near-still — wind and current are present but not determining. Lane markers run parallel from start to finish. The finish line is visible from the start. The distance is fixed: 2,000 metres for most races, shorter for some junior and masters events. The banks are set back. Nothing interrupts the line.

## Holds
- [Blade](piece_blade.md) — enters and exits cleanly here. The still surface reveals every fault in the catch and every failure at the finish.
- [Puddle](piece_puddle.md) — visible on flat water in a way no other surface allows. The puddles read the boat's run. Good spacing means the boat is gliding. Close puddles mean the recovery was rushed.
- [Slide](piece_slide.md) — operates at full function here. No need to brace against movement of the hull under the feet.

## Offers
A controlled environment. The flat water gives back exactly what was put in — no more, no less. A well-executed stroke produces visible, measurable distance. A compromised stroke produces a visible, measurable loss. The conditions do not excuse the rower and do not reward them. The water is neutral.

## Withheld
What the rower looks like from inside the boat. Flat water shows every consequence of the stroke — the puddles, the rate, the gap to the other crews — to everyone except the person making the strokes. The coach on the bank sees the catch that was late. The rower felt something but cannot name it. The place offers complete visibility of the stroke and withholds it from the only person who could use it to change what happens next.
