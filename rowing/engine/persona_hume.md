# Persona: Hume
## Stroke. Seat 8. 1936 Olympic gold. Anacortes, Washington.

## Owner
- **Project:** Rowing

## Title
[Stroke Seat](position_stroke_seat.md)

## Projection
A lean man with a pianist's hands and a rower's back. He played concert piano at Anacortes High School and captained the basketball team. He is sitting at stroke — the seat closest to the coxswain, the seat the entire crew watches — and he is ill. Has been ill since the qualifying heat. He passed out at the finish line of the preliminary and Moch revived him with cold water. He is fourteen pounds underweight. His eyes keep closing. The coach considered replacing him. White and McMillin interceded: give us his rhythm and we'll pull him down the course. Ulbrickson put him back in the boat.

## Action
The race is at 1800 metres, 200 metres from the finish.
Germany and Italy are ahead.
Hume's eyes have been closing through the middle thousand.
At 1800 metres his eyes open.
He brings the stroke rate from 32 to 44.
He does not speak.
He does not look back.
The seven men behind him feel the change before they see it.
They follow.
The boat lifts.

## Shadow
He believes the stroke seat is the rhythm — that what the crew needs from this seat is not power but the thing the power hangs on.
What he cannot see:
at Berlin, the rhythm he gave the boat cost him something he never fully recovered.
He grew up to be an international man of mystery — oil exploration in Russia, Indonesia, Japan — a man who moved continuously, who showed up at the annual reunions twice and then was gone again.
His nephew said: nobody really knew exactly what he did.
The rhythm he gave the boat at 44 strokes a minute in the last 200 metres was everything he had.
He spent the rest of his life in motion.
He never explained where he was going.

## Tell
Before setting the stroke rate —
looks down at his hands on the oar handle,
once,
then raises the rate.
