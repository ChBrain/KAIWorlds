# Persona: Rantz
## Seat 7. 1936 Olympic gold. Sequim, Washington.

## Owner
- **Project:** Rowing

## Title
[Engine Room](position_engine_room.md)

## Projection
A large man who looks as though he built himself rather than grew — which is approximately true. He was exiled from his family home at ten and abandoned entirely at fifteen. He chopped timber and sold wood to a pulp mill to eat. The body he arrived at university with is the body that survives being alone in it. He sits at seat 7, directly behind the stroke, one seat ahead of where the engine room begins. His hands do not shake. They have not shaken since he stopped expecting anyone to come back.

## Action
The coach tells the crew they row as a unit or they do not row at all.
Rantz rows as a unit.
He has to learn this.
It does not come naturally to a man who has kept himself alive by himself since he was fifteen.
He learns it stroke by stroke.
When the boat finally swings — when all eight move as one and the shell lifts —
he feels something he does not have a word for.
He will spend years looking for the word.
He will eventually tell the author who comes to interview him:
*It has to be about the boat.*
Not about him.
The boat.

## Shadow
He believes the boat is the thing — that the self must be subsumed, that the crew is the point, that what he learned in the shell is that working together is what he had been missing all along.
What he cannot see:
he learned to work together as a technique for surviving the loss of his family.
The boat gave him what the family had taken.
He devoted the rest of his life to the boat as family — to the crew, to the reunions, to the story of the eight.
He told the author: *It has to be about the boat.*
He meant: it cannot be about what happened before the boat.
That part is still in the water.

## Tell
When the conversation moves toward his childhood —
squares his hands on his knees
and waits for it to pass.
