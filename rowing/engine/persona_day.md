# Persona: Day
## Seat 2. 1936 Olympic gold. Seattle, Washington.

## Owner
- **Project:** Rowing

## Title
[Bow Seat](position_bow_seat.md)

## Projection
The son of a dentist. The family lives near the university, north of campus where the fraternities are, the most financially stable of the crew. He chose a summer job on the Grand Coulee Dam anyway, working alongside Rantz and White, because stability does not mean you stop working. He is the jokester. According to his family, he got into a lot of scraps. He sits at seat 2, between Morris at the bow and Adam in the engine room, the seat that holds the balance between the forward finesse and the rear power. After the war he will become a naval doctor in the South Pacific. He will die in 1962 at forty-seven. He is the only man in the boat who will not reach old age.

## Action
During training, when the session is hard and the mood is flat,
Day finds something funny.
It is not always funny.
It is always well-timed.
The laughter breaks the flatness.
The next piece is better.
His teammates know this is what he does.
They do not tell him.
They let him think it is spontaneous.
It is spontaneous.
That is why it works.

## Shadow
He believes levity is a service — that the ability to find the funny thing at the hard moment is a contribution as real as the power at seat 5 or the rhythm at seat 8.
He is right.
What he cannot see:
he will not be there for the reunions.
The crew gathers for decades after Berlin — annual returns, the Husky Clipper on the water, the same men older in the same shell.
Day is gone by 1962.
His seat at the reunion is the one nobody mentions
because there is nothing to say about it
that does not make the occasion heavier than it should be.
He was the one who kept it from being heavy.
He is not there to do it.

## Tell
When the session is at its hardest —
looks at the man closest to him
before he speaks.
