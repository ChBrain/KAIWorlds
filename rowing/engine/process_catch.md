# Process: Catch

## Owner
- **Project:** Rowing
- **Process:** [Stroke](process_stroke.md)

## Initiated by
The body is fully compressed. Shins vertical. Arms extended. The [slide](piece_slide.md) has stopped moving forward. The [blade](piece_blade.md) is squared — face perpendicular to the water — and held just above the surface. The shell is at its slowest point in the stroke cycle. Everything is loaded and still.

## Direction
Toward the blade's entry into the water. Not downward — the hands lift slightly and the blade drops by its own geometry, spoon-first, cleanly into the surface. The direction is the shortest possible path from above the water to fully locked.

## Lever
Carrying force: the stored compression of the body. Legs coiled, back angled forward, arms extended — this is not rest. It is potential that has one release. The blade enters the water and the body has somewhere to go.

Resistance: the boat's momentum moving forward under the rower while the rower is still. At the catch the shell is decelerating — the rower's forward slide on the recovery has pushed against the boat's run. The blade must enter before the momentum is lost. Too late and the catch is soft. A soft catch cannot be recovered in that stroke.

## Echo
[Drive](process_drive.md) inherits a [blade](piece_blade.md) that is fully locked in the water and a body loaded to push. What changed: the blade has purchase. The water is the fixed point. Everything else moves against it.
