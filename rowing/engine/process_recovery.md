# Process: Recovery

## Owner
- **Project:** Rowing
- **Process:** [Stroke](process_stroke.md)

## Initiated by
The [blade](piece_blade.md) is out of the water, feathered. The body is at full extension — legs flat, back open, arms extended away from the body. The boat is running on the momentum given to it by the drive. The rower is not adding to that momentum. This is the only moment in the stroke where the boat works and the rower rests.

## Direction
Toward the catch. The sequence reverses: arms move away first, then the body rocks forward from the hip, then — only then — the [slide](piece_slide.md) begins to move toward the stern. The hands carry the [blade](piece_blade.md) level above the water. Not too high, not dragging. The body rebuilds its compression for the next catch.

## Lever
Carrying force: the boat's run. The shell is gliding. Every smooth, unhurried movement forward on the slide preserves that run — the rower moves toward the stern without pushing the bow down, without checking the boat's speed.

Resistance: rushing. The instinct is to get back to the catch quickly — to not waste the glide, to begin the next stroke before the current one has fully given. But rushing the slide shifts the rower's weight forward against the boat's direction of travel. The boat checks. Speed is lost that no amount of power in the next drive can recover. The recovery is not rest — it is the preparation that makes the next catch possible. It cannot be shortened without cost.

## Echo
[Catch](process_catch.md) inherits a body fully compressed, a [blade](piece_blade.md) squared and ready, a [slide](piece_slide.md) at its forward limit. The boat has given what it can from the last stroke. What changed: the rower is loaded again. The entry condition of the stroke is restored. The [puddle](piece_puddle.md) from the last finish is already behind them and receding.
