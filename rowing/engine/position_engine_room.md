# Position: Engine Room
## The power that does not set the pace.

## Owner
- **Project:** Rowing
- **Scope:** Universal
- **Process:** [Stroke](process_stroke.md)
- **Process:** [Race](process_race.md)

## Has
The mass. The engine room seats — the middle of the boat — carry the strongest and heaviest rowers. The hull pitches and yaws least here. This position does not steer, does not call, does not set the rating. It drives. The boat accelerates because these seats apply the most force through the water. Remove this position and the boat moves — but it does not move fast.

## Orders
Match the timing before adding the load.

## Loses
The belief that more effort always produces more speed. The engine room arrives prepared to give everything and discovers that everything, given wrong, costs the boat. A pull that breaks the timing by a fraction of a second is felt by the entire crew as a check. Strength that is not synchronised is drag. What they were certain was their contribution — the power — is only a contribution when it is indistinguishable from everyone else's.

## Drives
On the loss — locks onto the back of seat seven. Matches the timing before adding the load. Lets the rhythm carry the power rather than the power forcing the rhythm.
