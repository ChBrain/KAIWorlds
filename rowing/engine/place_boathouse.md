# Place: Boathouse

## Owner
- **Project:** Rowing

## Shown
A long, low building at the water's edge. The doors are wide enough for an eight — the longest boat is the measure everything else is built around. Inside: racks holding shells, one above the other, their hulls facing down. Oars stacked upright or laid flat along the walls. The floor is concrete and often wet. The smell is fibreglass, rubber, and river mud. Cold. At the far end, the doors open to the water. Outside those doors: the dock, the launching point, the beginning and end of every session.

## Holds
- [Blade](piece_blade.md) — racked here between sessions. Counted before launch, counted after return.
- [Coastal shell](piece_coastal_shell.md) — stored here with all other hulls.
- [Slide](piece_slide.md) — maintained here. The work of the boat that is not rowing happens here.

## Offers
The ritual before the water. Carrying the boat out — hands on the hull, the weight distributed across the crew, the commands that keep eight people moving in one direction without dropping what cannot touch the ground — this is the first act of being a crew. The boathouse offers the work of getting ready, which is different from the work of rowing.

## Withheld
What the water will be. The boathouse is the last moment of not knowing. Wind and conditions are guessed at from inside. The plan exists but the water has not yet confirmed or refused it. The boathouse does not release this. Only the water does, after launch.
