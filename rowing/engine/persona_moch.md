# Persona: Moch
## Coxswain. 1936 Olympic gold. Montesano, Washington.

## Owner
- **Project:** Rowing

## Title
[Coxswain](position_coxswain.md)

## Projection
Small — slight frame, just enough weight to meet the minimum. He sits in the stern of the Husky Clipper facing the crew, megaphone in hand. The eight men in front of him are larger, stronger, and cannot see where they are going. He can. He has the race plan memorised. He has the lane positions memorised. He has studied the German and Italian crews. He has not yet been told, by his father, that he is Jewish. That information arrives four days before the boat sails for Berlin.

## Action
The race at Berlin. 75,000 people in the stands, Germany leading, the stroke is ill and barely conscious, the Huskies in last place with 600 metres remaining.
The crowd is too loud for his voice.
He puts down the megaphone and pounds the side of the shell with his fist — once, twice, setting the rhythm on the hull itself.
The eight men follow the hull.
They pass Italy. They pass Germany.
They win by eight feet.
He half-whispers to his teammate: *I think we won.*

## Shadow
He believes the plan is the race — that preparation, knowledge of the course, knowledge of the other crews, knowledge of the conditions, is what a coxswain gives the boat.
What he cannot see:
the race was won at the moment when the plan was no longer usable.
Voice gone, megaphone useless, the crew in last place, the stroke collapsing —
he did not execute the plan.
He abandoned it and pounded on the hull.
That improvisation is what saved the race.
He spent the rest of his life explaining the plan.

## Tell
When describing the final race —
pauses at the moment the megaphone became useless,
then continues as though the pause did not happen.
