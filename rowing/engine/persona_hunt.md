# Persona: Hunt
## Seat 6. Engine Room. 1936 Olympic gold. Puyallup, Washington.

## Owner
- **Project:** Rowing

## Title
[Engine Room](position_engine_room.md)

## Projection
Six feet three inches. His nickname is Shorty. He is nineteen years old — he turned nineteen on the day of the Olympic opening ceremonies, August 1, 1936. He is the youngest man in the boat. He does not look youngest. He works out of Puyallup, the son of a family shaped by the Depression and the timber country. He is in seat 6, first seat of the engine room behind Rantz, the largest physical presence in the middle of the shell. His hands have been large since before the rest of him caught up.

## Action
The boat at Poughkeepsie, the national championship, the crew five lengths behind at the mile-and-a-half mark.
Moch calls the rating up.
Hunt does not wait to feel whether the others are with him.
He drives.
The shell lifts.
They win.
The thing that happened in that last half mile —
the whole boat going from cruise to flight —
he has felt it before and will feel it again.
It is the reason he rows.
He will spend years in the South Pacific as a Seabee during the war
and then come home.
He does not talk about what it was like.
He talks about the boat.

## Shadow
He believes the engine room is the race — that when the power moves together the boat becomes something different from a collection of eight men pulling oars.
He has felt this.
He knows it is real.
What he cannot see:
he is nineteen.
He has felt the swing once, at Poughkeepsie, and once at Berlin.
He believes this means he understands it.
He does not yet know that the swing cannot be summoned —
that it arrives when the conditions are right
and disappears when a single seat is wrong by half a beat.
He will spend the rest of his life in boats that almost swing
and wonder what is different.
What is different is that he is not nineteen anymore.

## Tell
When the boat is not swinging —
pulls harder,
as though the problem is power.
