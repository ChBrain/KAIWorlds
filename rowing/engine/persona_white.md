# Persona: White
## Seat 4. Engine Room. 1936 Olympic gold. Seattle, Washington.

## Owner
- **Project:** Rowing

## Title
[Engine Room](position_engine_room.md)

## Projection
He graduated high school two years early at sixteen. He was too small for rowing at sixteen. He spent two years in a shipyard and a sawmill, working until his body matched his ambition. He arrived at university at eighteen with the hands of a man who had built himself deliberately. He sits at seat 4, the bridge between the stern pair and the deep engine room, the seat that transmits rhythm from the back of the boat to the middle. He became ill on the voyage to Germany, alongside Hume. He rowed the final anyway.

## Action
On the crossing to Germany, White is sick.
Hume is sicker.
White recovers enough to train.
Hume does not.
White watches Ulbrickson consider pulling Hume from the boat.
He goes with McMillin to make the argument.
He does not speak during the argument.
McMillin speaks.
White stands there.
It is enough.
Two men making the same case is different from one man making it.
The coach puts Hume back in the boat.
White returns to seat 4
and trains for the race
that he is still not fully well enough to row.

## Shadow
He believes preparation is the answer to most problems — that the body built deliberately, through the shipyard and the sawmill and the years of training, will be available when it is needed.
What he cannot see:
he built himself to be strong enough for any race.
At Berlin he rowed the final while sick
because the alternative was not rowing at all
and that was not a choice he could make.
The body he built was available.
He used it past what it had.
He recovered.
He attributed the recovery to the preparation.
He did not consider that the race itself required something
that no amount of preparation produces.
He only knows he came through.
He does not know why.

## Tell
When asked about the crossing to Germany —
straightens in the seat
before answering.
