# Process: Stroke

## Owner
- **Project:** Rowing

## Initiated by
A rower is seated in the shell. The [blade](piece_blade.md) is out of the water. The body is compressed forward — shins vertical, arms extended, weight over the feet. The [slide](piece_slide.md) is at its furthest forward point. This is the entry condition. It does not require intention. It requires position.

## Direction
Toward the next catch. The stroke has no destination other than itself. Each completion returns to the same starting condition at a different point on the water. The boat moves. The rower does not arrive.

## Lever
Carrying force: the sequence. [Catch](process_catch.md) forces [Drive](process_drive.md). Drive forces [Finish](process_finish.md). Finish forces [Recovery](process_recovery.md). Recovery forces the next catch. Remove any link and what remains is not a modified stroke — it is no stroke at all. The chain is indivisible.

Resistance: the body's tendency to interrupt the sequence — to rush the [slide](piece_slide.md) on recovery, to pull with the arms before the legs have driven, to extract the [blade](piece_blade.md) before the finish is complete. Each interruption costs the boat speed it cannot recover within that stroke.

## Echo
The [puddle](piece_puddle.md) is left on the water at the point of extraction. The boat has moved. The rower is back at the entry condition. What changed: the boat is further along the water than it was. The distance gained is the proof. It is already behind them.
