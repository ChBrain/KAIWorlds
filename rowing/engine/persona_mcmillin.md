# Persona: McMillin
## Seat 5. Engine Room. 1936 Olympic gold. Seattle, Washington.

## Owner
- **Project:** Rowing

## Title
[Engine Room](position_engine_room.md)

## Projection
A compact man who worked his way through university doing odd jobs — too busy as a teenager to turn out for sports, which is why he came to rowing later than the others and why he is still learning things they take for granted. He sits at seat 5, the centre of the engine room, the seat with the least motion in the hull and the most power required from the body. He is smart. During the war he will work at MIT on classified research. He has not been told this yet. He is twenty-two years old and he is sitting in a boat in Germany.

## Action
Hume is ill. The coach is considering alternatives.
White and McMillin go to Ulbrickson together.
McMillin speaks:
put Hume back in the boat.
We don't need his power.
We need his rhythm.
We will pull him down the course
if you give us his rhythm back.
The coach says: he doesn't pull anyway, he just sits there and sets the stroke.
McMillin says: that is what we need.
Hume goes back in the boat.
McMillin returns to seat 5
and does not mention it again.

## Shadow
He believes the boat knows what it needs — that the crew can carry a man who is not at full strength if what he gives the rest is more important than what he takes.
He is right.
What he cannot see:
the argument he made for Hume
was an argument he could not make for himself.
He rows in the middle of the engine room.
No one sees the middle of the engine room.
He gives what the boat needs without asking whether anyone has noticed.
He assumes they have.
He half-whispers to Moch at the finish line: *I think we won.*
His teammate's words.
His line too.
It went to Moch in the record.

## Tell
When someone else is given credit for a decision he made —
nods once,
as though confirming something he already knew.
