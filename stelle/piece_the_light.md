# Piece: The Light
## What the Ascent is made of. Not symbol. Substance.

## Owner
- **Project:** Stelle
- **Place:** [The Spheres](place_the_spheres.md)

## Place
[The Spheres](place_the_spheres.md).
It does not travel downward.
It is not present in the Circles or on the Mountain
except as memory of what is absent.

## Load Bearing
Without it the Ascent has no medium.
The Descent moved through geography - circles, ditches, ice.
The Climb moved through architecture - terraces, gates, fire.
The Ascent moves through light.
Remove the Light and the Spheres become
another kind of upward journey,
which is not what they are.
The Spheres are not places that contain light.
The Light is what the Spheres are.
The Traveller does not move through them.
The Traveller's capacity to receive the Light
determines how deep into it they go.
This is not the same as moving.
Remove the distinction and the Ascent collapses
back into a climb, which it is not.

## Apparent
Increasing. At first the Traveller can barely glance at it -
the [Intermediary](position_the_intermediary.md) redirects their gaze as mediator.
The Intermediary's eyes hold the reflection.
The Traveller looks at them looking at it.
Gradually the Traveller can hold more directly.
At the final sphere there is only light.
No geometry. No direction. No above or below.
The light does not hurt.
It did at first.
It no longer hurts because the Traveller
is no longer the same Traveller
who found it painful.

## Yearbook
Dante could not look at the sun at the start of Paradiso.
A brief glance stunned him.
He redirected his gaze to the one who loved him
and whose eyes held what he could not yet bear directly.
Her eyes were brighter than the sun.
He looked at her eyes.
By the end he could look at the Trinity directly -
three circles of light, each a different colour,
each the same dimension.
The language failed at that point.
He wrote it down anyway.
What he wrote is not the thing.
The thing cannot be written.
He wrote toward it and stopped
and said he stopped
and that stopping was not failure.
The Light is still there.
It does not need to be described to be real.
