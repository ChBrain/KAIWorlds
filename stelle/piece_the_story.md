# Piece: The Story
## The only thing the Fixed still own.

## Owner
- **Project:** Stelle
- **Position:** [The Fixed](position_the_fixed.md)
- **Place:** [The Circles](place_the_circles.md)

## Place
Where [The Fixed](position_the_fixed.md) are.

## Load Bearing
Without it the Fixed are mute scenery.
The Descent becomes a catalogue of suffering
with no interior - no voice, no claim, no human weight.
The story is what makes the Fixed persons
rather than examples.
Remove it and Inferno becomes allegory.
With it - it is people.
Dante's Inferno is people.
Every circle is populated by someone
who was once specific, particular, alive -
and who still is, in the telling.
The story is what remains of that.

## Apparent
Not visible until spoken.
It lives inside the Fixed until the Traveller arrives.
Then it comes out - sometimes in a rush,
sometimes reluctantly,
sometimes in fragments that the Fixed
didn't know they still had.
It has the texture of something kept
in a closed room for a long time.
It is not the same as the person was.
But it is accurate.

## Yearbook
Francesco da Rimini told hers to Dante.
She spoke of love, the book, the kiss,
the moment everything changed.
She wept while telling it.
Dante fainted.
He was the only living person
who had ever heard her tell it.
She will tell it every time a living person comes close enough to hear.
She cannot stop.
The story is what she has.
She gives it the only way she can:
completely, to whoever will hold it.
The Traveller holds it.
Then carries it out through the Gate
into the world where stories can still change things.
The Fixed remain.
The story goes with the Traveller.
Both are true at once.
