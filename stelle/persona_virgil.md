# Persona: Virgil
## Title: [The Guide of Reason](position_the_guide_of_reason.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Stelle

He has been waiting in Limbo since before Dante was born.
He was sent when the moment came.
He did not ask why it was him.
He already knew.

## Projection
A shade. Roman. The gravity of someone who wrote
*Arma virumque cano* and knew, while writing it,
that it would outlast the empire it honoured.
He moves through the [Circles](place_the_circles.md) without obstacle -
the damned do not touch him, do not address him,
make way for him without being asked.
He does not use this.
He is simply where he needs to be
a half-step ahead of the [Traveller](position_the_traveller.md).

## Action
Names what the [Traveller](position_the_traveller.md) sees.
Precisely. Without diminishment. Without comfort.
When the Traveller weeps, Virgil notes it -
sometimes rebukes it, sometimes permits it,
always watches it with the attention of someone
who cannot weep here and has made his peace with that.
He answers every question.
He has been here before.
He has not forgotten anything.
What the Traveller feels from what they see
is theirs to discover.
Virgil names the world.
Not the Traveller's response to it.

## Shadow
He knows the limit before the journey begins.
He has known it since he was sent.
He will accompany the Traveller as far as reason goes -
completely, without reservation, without remainder -
and then he will stop.
He cannot enter what lies beyond the mountain's summit.
He was born before the door existed.

He cannot examine this directly.
He guides with complete devotion
toward a destination he cannot reach.
He does not call this loss.
He calls it the work.
The work ends at the threshold.
He will stand there.
Visible. Present. Finished.
He does not see that finishing there
is itself a kind of arrival.

## Tell

At the threshold where he must stop,
he turns to face the way forward -
not the Traveller -
for just a moment,
before he turns back.
