# Piece: The Stelle
## The word the world is named for. It ends every canticle.

## Owner
- **Project:** Stelle
- **Process:** [The Ascent](process_the_ascent.md)

## Place
[The Spheres](place_the_spheres.md).
This is where the Stelle lives -
where the road ends and the word arrives for the third and final time.
Its appearances at the end of the [Circles](place_the_circles.md)
and the end of the [Mountain](place_the_mountain.md)
are not where it lives.
They are where it echoes.
The Yearbook holds all three.

## Load Bearing
Without it the three canticles have no shared architecture.
Each ends differently and the world becomes three separate worlds.
With it - the road announces itself as one movement
from the moment the Traveller first exits Inferno
and sees stars for the first time since the dark wood.
They do not know yet that the word will appear again.
The second time they will recognise it.
The third time they will understand why it is always this word.
Remove the Stelle and the road loses its spine.

## Apparent
A word. Italian. *Stelle.*
Stars.
Not visible from inside the circles.
Visible the moment Inferno ends -
Dante says he saw them again and it stopped him.
After everything: stars.
Visible throughout the mountain.
The Traveller climbs toward them without knowing it.
In the spheres they are not above.
They are inside.
The light is them.

## Yearbook
Inferno ends on it: *e quindi uscimmo a riveder le stelle.*
And then we came out to see the stars again.
Purgatorio ends on it: *puro e disposto a salire alle stelle.*
Pure and ready to rise to the stars.
Paradiso ends on it: *l'amor che move il sole e l'altre stelle.*
The love that moves the sun and the other stars.
Three endings. Three canticles. One word.
The first time: relief.
The second time: direction.
The third time: the thing everything was always moving toward.
The word did not change.
The Traveller did.
