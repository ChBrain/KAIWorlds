# Process: The Climb
## The road that only moves if you do.

## Owner
- **Project:** Stelle
- **Process:** [The Descent](process_the_descent.md)
- **Position:** [The Traveller](position_the_traveller.md)
- **Position:** [The Guide of Reason](position_the_guide_of_reason.md)
- **Position:** [The Moving](position_the_moving.md)

## Initiated by
The [Descent](process_the_descent.md) is complete.
The Traveller has emerged.
The [Mountain](place_the_mountain.md) is visible.
The [Guide of Reason](position_the_guide_of_reason.md) has not yet reached their limit.
The Traveller is moving upward.

The Climb is different from the Descent in one structural way:
the dead here are not fixed.
They are moving.
Slowly. With difficulty. But moving.
The Traveller is among those who are not yet finished.

## Direction
Upward. Outward.
Terrace by terrace, the mountain lightens.
What was carried into Inferno is not set down here -
it is transformed.
There is a difference.
The [Guide of Reason](position_the_guide_of_reason.md) knows the difference.
The Traveller feels it but cannot yet name it.

Movement stalls where the Traveller has something unfinished.
The mountain knows this before the Traveller does.
The stall is not punishment.
It is the process working correctly.

## Lever
The Traveller's will to ascend
pressing against their refusal to be changed
by what they already understand.
The [Guide of Reason](position_the_guide_of_reason.md) cannot resolve it for them.
Reason can name the obstruction.
Reason cannot move it.
What moves it is consent -
the moment the Traveller stops resisting
what the mountain is making of them
and the will aligns with it.
Not by deciding to consent.
By arriving at it.
The mountain knows the difference.
The stall ends when consent is genuine.
Not before.

## Echo
The Traveller has reached the summit.
The [Guide of Reason](position_the_guide_of_reason.md) has reached their absolute limit.
Not exhaustion. Boundary.
The Guide was born before what comes next existed.
The Guide cannot enter what the Traveller is about to enter.
The Guide knows this.
Has known it since the [Dark Wood](place_the_dark_wood.md).
Has not said so until now.

The [Crossing](process_the_crossing.md) inherits:
a Traveller who has climbed under their own weight
and a Guide who has arrived at the edge of what they can offer.
Both knew this was coming.
Only one of them could say so.
