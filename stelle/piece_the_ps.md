# Piece: The P's
## The marks of what must be shed before the summit.

## Owner
- **Project:** Stelle
- **Place:** [The Mountain](place_the_mountain.md)
- **Position:** [The Traveller](position_the_traveller.md)

## Place
On the [Traveller](position_the_traveller.md)'s forehead.
From the gate of Purgatory until the summit.

## Load Bearing
Without them the Climb has no visible measure of progress.
The Traveller does not know how much remains
until they notice one is gone.
They never feel it removed -
they only notice afterward, when it is lighter.
Remove the P's and the Climb becomes
an ascent with no feedback,
no confirmation that the consent given on one terrace
has registered in the structure of the world.
With them - the mountain keeps count.
The Traveller does not have to.

## Apparent
Seven letters. P, carved in ash on the forehead
by an angel's sword at the gate of Purgatory.
One P for each of the seven terraces.
Each removed by an angel as the terrace is cleared.
The Traveller cannot see them.
The Guide of Reason can.
*You have seven wounds upon your brow*,
Virgil says when they are first marked.
Each removal makes the Traveller lighter
without their understanding why they feel lighter.

## Yearbook
Marked on Dante's forehead at Purgatorio IX.
He did not feel the marking.
First removal at Purgatorio XII - the terrace of pride.
An angel's wing touched his forehead.
He felt the wing but not the erasure.
Virgil told him afterward: you have one less.
Dante reached up. Nothing there to find.
The absence was the confirmation.
By the summit all seven were gone.
He had not counted them leaving.
He knew they were gone because the weight was different.
The mountain had done its accounting.
He was free to enter the fire that remained.
