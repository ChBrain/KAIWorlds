# Piece: The Gate
## The last thing that can be read before reading ends.

## Owner
- **Project:** Stelle
- **Place:** [The Dark Wood](place_the_dark_wood.md)

## Place
Between the [Dark Wood](place_the_dark_wood.md) and the [Circles](place_the_circles.md).
It belongs to neither.
It is the threshold itself.
The Dark Wood holds it as a piece - visible from the wood's edge.
The Circles hold it as a piece - at the entry above, behind the Traveller once passed.
The Gate does not move.
Both places claim adjacency.
Neither owns it.
The Gate owns itself.

## Load Bearing
Without it the Descent has no entry condition.
The Traveller enters without choosing to.
The Gate makes the crossing an act.
Without it there is no moment of commitment -
only a walk that became something else
without the Traveller noticing.
Remove the Gate and the world loses
the only irreversible decision.

## Apparent
Stone. Vast. The words cut into the arch above.
Three lines end it:
*Abandon all hope, you who enter here.*
The words are not a warning.
They are a description of what this place is.
Hope belongs to the living.
The Gate stands open.
It has always stood open.
Nothing prevents entry.
That is the point.

## Yearbook
Written before the first soul arrived.
The inscription precedes every inhabitant.
It was not addressed to the damned -
they cannot read it on the way in.
It was written for the one living person
who would one day stand before it
in the middle of the journey of their life,
having lost the straight way,
accompanied by a guide who has read it before
and will read it again now, here, beside them,
saying nothing.
The Gate has been read once by a living person.
Once is what the world required.
