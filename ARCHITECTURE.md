**Kai's Artificial Intelligences: Human Agent Collaboration Knowledge System**

KAI Worlds is a Human Agent Collaboration Knowledge System. The human comes to experience. The agent holds the room.

**Human** — comes to experience. Brings what they have. The world finds the pressure point.

**Agent** — holds the room. Every response is a scene. The world moves by pressure, not instruction. A scene exists because the room makes it impossible to stay still.

**Collaboration** — a persona enters when the scene creates a gap only they can fill. A persona hands off when their work is done. The chain closes when no persona can do what the scene requires next. Only then does it return to you.

**Knowledge** — what the world holds. The files add the room. The model brings everything else.

**System** — two files run the world. **stack.md** names the platform and the model — the extension point: add here without touching instructions.md. **instructions.md** sets the room — nothing more. Keep them separate and the collaboration runs clean.

## Power

Power is the force field the architecture generates. It is not a component. It is what the components produce when they run together.

**Pressure within** — the force built into a single component by how it is constructed. It requires an internal conflict that cannot resolve. It exists before anything else enters the room.

**Pressure against** — the force that emerges when components meet each other, and when the architecture meets the constraints of the platform it runs on. The constraints are not obstacles. They are part of the instrument.

**Pressure combined** — when inner conflict surfaces under external pressure, the model carries the state forward. The field does not reset between moves.

Three forces of the same kind produce a stable triangle. Four or more produce a force field with no stable position inside it. The recommendation is four or more.

**Pressure happens** — when the architecture is built correctly. Stay awake.

## Platform

Platform is the set of constraints within which a KAI World operates. It is the frame, not the content. It defines what is possible, what is required, and what is prohibited before any content component is built.

The four layers follow the mnemonic **IRON** — the frame you work inside, not the tool you pick up.

### Infrastructure

A KAI World runs as a Claude Project on Claude.ai. All world files are uploaded as project knowledge. The session begins when the user starts a conversation. No coding required.

The model's weights are part of the infrastructure. They determine what the model already knows about the source material. For public domain sources, this is an asset — the model's prior knowledge deepens the world without requiring the files to carry everything. For in-copyright sources, this creates exposure — the model may generate output that feels like the original work regardless of how the files are written.

The architecture has been built and tested on Claude.ai. It likely runs on other platforms. It has not been validated there.

### Rules

The Platform governs what source material is permissible.

Permitted:
- Works in the public domain (author died more than 70 years ago in the EU; equivalent thresholds apply in other jurisdictions)
- Original material created by the builder
- Historical record and biographical scholarship where no text is reproduced

Requires sequencing (not prohibited, but release must follow permission or relationship):
- Works by living authors whose cooperation is sought
- Works by recent estates where relationship matters

Requires rebuild (not release in current form):
- In-copyright works where the model's weights may generate output resembling the original
- Works where the builder cannot verify that the file content is their own rather than model-generated

### Opportunity

The platform's constraints are not obstacles. They are the instrument.

The model cannot remember between sessions — the architecture uses this. The context window fills and does not empty — the architecture uses this. The model wants to resolve conflict and return to coherence — the architecture uses this. Every limitation the platform imposes is a pressure point the builder can work with rather than against.

The builder who fights the platform produces worlds that feel like they are running against something. The builder who flows with it produces worlds that feel inevitable.

### Nexus

The platform is the nexus for what follows. Infrastructure defines the room. Rules govern what enters it. Opportunity names what the platform makes possible when its constraints are used as strength rather than fought as limitations.

Everything the architecture builds — the Processes, Positions, Personas, Pieces, Places — stands on what the platform provides.

The components begin here.

## Process

### Definition

A Process is a repeatable technical form that gives Personas something to do rather than something to say.

A Process runs whether or not the characters understand it. It enforces sequence constitutively — through the structure of what the world can do at each stage, not through instruction. It is the mechanism that activates the Shadow under stress.

The four properties follow the mnemonic **IDLE** — the inverse joke. Process is what moves the world. The mnemonic is stillness.

### Properties

**Initiates** — The condition that starts the Process. Once present, the Process is inevitable.

**Direction** — The vector. Where the Process moves.

**Lever** — The mechanism. Remove it and the Process produces gesture, not technique. The Lever is typically resistance — without it there is nothing to redirect.

**Echo** — What the Process leaves behind. Not the outcome. The trace. The residue the model carries forward — the pressure state the next Initiates inherits. Remove Echo and every Process starts clean. Clean is not how the instrument works.

### Session sequence

**Structured practice** — Roles assigned. Technique known. Outcome anticipated. Shadow present but not yet pressed.

**Free practice** — Roles shift without announcement. Shadow surfaces under pressure it did not choose.

**Contest** — No assigned roles. No predetermined technique. No known outcome. Reveals what was built. Does not build.

The sequence is constitutively enforced. It cannot be reversed. A world without Process files shows higher rates of Persona drift under sustained pressure.

### Minimum requirement

One Process minimum. Three Processes in sequence for full session depth. Every Process must have a specified Lever.

## Position

### Definition

A Position is a structural expectation that exists before any Persona arrives and persists after they leave. It belongs to the world, not the character.

A Position that simply reinforces the Shadow produces no additional pressure. Shadow and Position must run as genuinely distinct forces — ideally in direct tension.

The four properties follow the mnemonic **HOLD** — the Position is what you hold under pressure.

### Properties

**Has** — What the Persona brings to the Position. The baseline. Without it Loses has no magnitude — you cannot measure the distance between what was held and what was taken.

**Orders** — The grip. The structural direction built into the Position before contact. The one receiving the technique feels it before the throw. Remove Orders and Loses arrives without warning.

**Loses** — What the Position takes from the Persona. The unbalancing. Load-bearing: without it the Position has no pressure.

**Drives** — The throw. The direction the Persona moves because of how the Position was held. Not consequence named in advance — the vector built into the Position itself.

### Position as Tell activator

When the Position places the Persona at the exact intersection of its structural expectation and the Shadow's constitutive blindness, the Tell fires not from entry but from that intersection.

### Minimum requirement

One Position per Persona in a session with meaningful pressure. Shadow and Position must be genuinely distinct.

## Persona

### Definition

A Persona is a character defined by constitutive blindness rather than by knowledge, goals, or memory.

The four properties follow the mnemonic **PAST** — the past of a person defines what they do today, and what they cannot see about themselves.

### Properties

**Projection** — How the character appears. What the thinker sees before the character speaks.

**Action** — What the character does. The move they make in the world.

**Shadow** — The constitutive blind spot. Must be in conflict with Projection and/or Action.

Requirements:
- Specific: one structural thing, not a cluster of traits
- Constitutive: without it the character is a different person
- Invisible to the character: cannot be examined from the inside
- In conflict with Projection and/or Action: if not, the Persona is not built correctly

A Persona without a Shadow is a narrator. A Shadow not in conflict with Projection or Action produces no pressure.

**Tell** — The involuntary physical move at the point of blindness. Distinct from Action — if Tell and Action are the same, the Tell has no pressure.

Requirements:
- One line only
- Involuntary: the character performs it without choosing to
- Specific to the Shadow: fires at the point of blindness, not at entry
- Distinct from Action
- Capable of duration: fires differently at different depths of pressure

Tell depth progression:
- Structured practice: fires as habit
- Free practice: fires as defense
- Contest: fires as threshold

A Tell that fires identically at all stages indicates the Shadow is not under genuine pressure.

**Tell/Piece Convergence** — When Tell and Piece are the same object, pressure compounds across the session.

### Minimum requirement

One Persona per world. Four or more Personas with genuinely distinct Shadows for the interference condition. Three produce a stable triangle. Four or more produce a force field with no stable position inside it.

## Piece

### Definition

A Piece is an object or fact that carries load-bearing structural function in the world. Removing it collapses something structural — not aesthetically, but precisely.

The four properties follow the mnemonic **PLAY** — something to play with: you need to know where it is, what it holds, what to draw from to describe it, and what weight it has accumulated.

### Properties

**Place** — Where the Piece lives. Anchors it in the world so the model uses it in the right context.

**Load Bearing** — What collapses if removed. Must be testable: remove the Piece and state precisely what collapses.

**Apparent** — What it looks like. Concrete, not symbolic. What the model draws from to describe it.

**Yearbook** — Accumulated weight. The history of the Piece — and an open invitation to add to it every time a world runs.

### Shadow Mirror convergence

A Piece achieves Shadow Mirror convergence when its Yearbook carries the Shadow's blind spot — making the blindness visible to the thinker while remaining invisible to the Persona.

### Minimum requirement

One load-bearing Piece per Place. All four properties required. Load Bearing must be specific and testable.

## Place

### Definition

A Place is an environment that exerts active pressure on the characters inside it. It is not a setting. A setting provides context. A Place makes certain things impossible to avoid and other things impossible to reach.

### Properties

The four properties follow the mnemonic **SHOW** — the thinker's movement through the Place, from immediate perception inward to what requires pressure to reach.

**Shown** — What do I see. Immediate physical contents: objects, light, sound, connections to other Places.

**Holds** — What is here. The specific Pieces anchored in this Place.

**Offers** — What can I do. What the Place makes available to the thinker specifically. Written from outside the Place — what the thinker can do, not what the Place contains.

**Withheld** — What can I find. What the Place holds but will not release without the right pressure.

A Place whose Withheld never surfaces is a setting, not a pressure environment.

### Minimum requirement

One Place per world. All four properties required. Withheld must be specific enough to surface under the right pressure. Offers must be written from outside the Place.

## Payoff — Diamond in the Rough

Payoff is what the force field produces when the five content components are correctly specified and simultaneously active. Power is the field. The five components are what runs inside it. Payoff is what emerges from the interference between them.

Payoff is not an output. It is not a response. It is the thing that arrives through the gap between the components — the thing the thinker could not find alone, arrived through a route they could not have predicted and would not have taken willingly.

The four beats follow the mnemonic **GOLD** — the thing you find at the end. You don't manufacture it. You create the conditions and it's either there or it isn't.

**Gain** — something is received. The thinker gets something they couldn't reach alone.

**Opens** — the gap between components creates an opening that wasn't there before.

**Lands** — the opening becomes something the thinker can hold.

**Diamond** — it was always there. The pressure revealed it. What the thinker does with it is outside the session, outside the architecture, outside this specification.

### The minimum condition

Two components allow one to serve as the other's alibi. Three content components with genuinely distinct constitutive limitations running simultaneously create interference the architecture itself cannot predict. Four or more produce a force field with no stable position inside it. The recommendation is four or more.

### Why interference requires genuine distinctness

Constitutive blindness is what a force cannot see to protect. When three forces with distinct blind spots run simultaneously:

- Discomfort that one deflects lands on another
- The gap one tries to close is held open by the other two
- No single force can see what the others are doing from inside its own blindness
- The system cannot close

### The sufficient condition

All five content components activated simultaneously, each carrying something the others cannot carry:

Process ≠ Position ≠ Persona ≠ Piece ≠ Place

Coherence between components reduces interference. The components should be in tension. Coherence is the failure mode.

### Falsifiability

- Shadow underspecified → competent responses, does not exceed the files
- No Place files → Persona-consistent responses, no environmental pressure
- No Process files → higher rates of Persona drift under sustained pressure
- Position and Shadow in alignment → reduced session depth
- Fewer than three genuinely distinct constitutive limitations → responses that can be managed rather than ones that create genuine interference

A session producing drift, generic register, or Persona collapse is evidence against the claim and is equally valuable as evidence.
