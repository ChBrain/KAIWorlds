## Load Bearing
Without it the argument stays at the conference table —
verbal, improvised, lost when the sessions end.
With it Einstein's challenge has a permanent form.
It is in the literature.
It cannot be dismissed.
It has to be answered.
Bohr answered it.
Einstein did not accept the answer.
The paper is still there.
The argument is still there inside it.

## Apparent
Six pages. Three authors: Einstein, Podolsky, Rosen.
The title: *Can Quantum-Mechanical Description of Physical Reality
Be Considered Complete?*
The answer Einstein wanted: no.
The answer the paper proves: that if quantum mechanics is complete,
reality is not local — two particles can affect each other
across any distance, instantaneously.
Einstein called this *spooky action at a distance.*
He meant it as a reductio ad absurdum.
It turned out to be true.
He did not live to see the experiments.

## Yearbook
Written Princeton, 1935.
Published Physical Review, May 1935.
Bohr's reply: September 1935.
The reply answered the paper.
Einstein read it. Wrote to Schrödinger:
*Bohr's answer does not get to the heart of the matter.*
Experiments confirmed spooky action at a distance, 1982.
Einstein was right that it was real.
He was wrong that it broke quantum mechanics.
The paper is still being cited.
It is still not settled in the way he needed it to be settled.
It will not be settled that way.
The question was real.
The answer he wanted does not exist.
