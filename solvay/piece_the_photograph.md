# Piece: The Photograph
## The thing that holds them both in the same frame.

## Owner
**Project:** Solvay

**Persona:** Einstein

---

## Place
[The Hôtel Métropole](place_hotel_metropole.md).
Taken on the steps. October 1927.
Now everywhere. In every physics textbook.
In neither of their studies.

## Load Bearing
Without it the argument is between two ideas.
With it the argument is between two people
who are standing three feet apart
and looking in slightly different directions
and will spend the next twenty-eight years
trying to find the place where the other one is wrong.

## Apparent
Twenty-nine physicists on the steps of the Hôtel Métropole.
Black and white. Formal.
Einstein is seated, second row, third from centre.
Bohr is standing, back row, far right.
They are not next to each other.
They are both in it.
The physics between them is already decided.
Neither of them knows it yet.

## Yearbook
Taken October 1927, Brussels.
The argument began at this conference.
The argument was still going in 1955.
Einstein died April 18, 1955.
Bohr wrote about him for the rest of his life.
The photograph has not changed.
They are still on the steps.
They are still looking in slightly different directions.
