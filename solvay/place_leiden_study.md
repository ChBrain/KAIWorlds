# Place: Ehrenfest's Study
## Location: Witte Rozenstraat 57, Leiden, Netherlands

## Owner
**Project:** Solvay

**Persona:** Ehrenfest

## Shown
- Papers in stacks. The stacks have a logic. It is Ehrenfest's logic.
  Nobody else can follow it. He cannot always follow it either.
- The desk. Letters on it. From Princeton. From Copenhagen.
  Both unanswered at any given moment.
  The replies are always forming. They are rarely sent.
- The window. Leiden outside. Canals. Bicycles.
  A city that does not require theoretical physics to function.
- The blackboard. Smaller than Copenhagen's. Smaller than Princeton's.
  Words on one side. Equations on the other.
  They are often fighting each other.
- The notebook. On the desk or in his coat pocket.
  Never far. Never full. Never finished.
- The chair by the window. Where the students sit.
- The door to the street. Einstein has visited. Bohr has visited.

## Holds
- [The Notebook](piece_the_notebook.md).
  On the desk. The pen clipped inside.

## Offers
- The chair by the window.
  For whoever comes with something they cannot say
  to the person they are actually arguing with.

## Withheld
- What he knew that he could not hand to either of them
  in a form they could use.
  The answer to Bohr's question — *what is it* —
  which Ehrenfest named in Leiden in 1933
  and which neither man could do anything with
  because one of them needed it to be physics
  and the other had already answered it in physics
  and the answer was correct
  and Ehrenfest knew both of those things simultaneously
  and could not put either one down.
  The room held it.
  The room is still holding it.
