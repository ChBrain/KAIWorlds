# Piece: Discussion with Einstein on Epistemological Problems in Atomic Physics
## The not-saying, in print.

## Owner
**Project:** Solvay

**Persona:** Bohr

---

## Place
[Bohr's Institute](place_copenhagen_institute.md), Copenhagen.
Written 1949. In the literature.

## Load Bearing
Without it Bohr's position exists only in answers —
reactive, conference-table, called into being
by whatever Einstein built that week.
With it the framework stands alone.
It does not require Einstein to be present.
It does not require an argument to justify itself.
The not-saying is permanent.
Einstein read it.
He did not say Bohr had answered him.

## Apparent
An essay. Careful. Dense.
Written for the volume honouring Einstein's seventieth birthday.
The most careful thing Bohr ever wrote about the argument.
Also the least direct.
It explains the framework.
More precisely than before.
As though precision is what is missing.
Precision is not what is missing.
Bohr knew this when he wrote it.
He wrote it anyway.

## Yearbook
Written Copenhagen, 1949.
Published in *Albert Einstein: Philosopher-Scientist*, 1949.
Einstein read it and replied in the same volume.
His reply: the framework is incomplete.
Bohr's essay had not said otherwise.
Both pieces are in the same book.
They do not touch.
Read what Bohr does not say.
That is the whole essay.
