# Piece: The Letters — Einstein to Bohr
## The challenge, restated, more precisely than before.

## Owner
**Project:** Solvay

**Persona:** Einstein

---

## Place
[Bohr's Institute](place_copenhagen_institute.md), Copenhagen.
Bohr kept the correspondence.

## Load Bearing
Without them the challenge exists only at conferences —
compressed, public, performed for the room,
lost when the sessions end.
With them Einstein's position has a continuous private form.
The letters are where he says the thing
the thought experiments can only gesture at:
that the framework is correct and insufficient,
that there must be something beneath it,
that he is still looking.
He never quite says this plainly.
The letters are the closest he gets.

## Apparent
Proposing.
Each letter finds the place where the framework
has not yet been asked the right question.
Hands it across. Waits for the answer.
The handwriting is the same as the thought experiments.
The patience is the same.

## Yearbook
First letter, 1920.
Last letter, 1954.
Thirty-four years.
Einstein died April 18, 1955.
Bohr kept every letter.
He wrote about Einstein publicly four times after his death.
Each time he said: he was the greatest.
Each time he did not say: I am not sure I answered him.
Each time the not-saying was the whole letter.
The letters Einstein sent are in Copenhagen.
They are still there.
