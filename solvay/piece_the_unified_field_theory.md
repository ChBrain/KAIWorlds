# Piece: The Unified Field Theory
## The thing Einstein was building while the argument was happening.

## Owner
**Project:** Solvay

**Persona:** Einstein

---

## Place
[Einstein's Study](place_princeton_study.md).
The blackboard. Always.

## Load Bearing
Without it Einstein is a man who lost an argument.
With it he is a man who was looking for something
the argument was never going to give him —
and kept looking anyway,
in a different direction,
for thirty years,
until he died.
The argument and the equation are the same search.
He did not know this.

## Apparent
Equations. Incomplete.
The blackboard is covered.
The theory would unify gravity and electromagnetism.
It would make quantum mechanics unnecessary —
not wrong, unnecessary,
a special case of something deeper.
Something that has a reality beneath it.
Something that does not play dice.
It is not finished.
It has not been finished since 1925.

## Yearbook
Begun 1925.
Still on the blackboard, Princeton, 1955.
Einstein died April 18, 1955.
The equations were still there.
A nurse copied them down, thinking they might be important.
They were not finished.
They are still not finished.
Nobody has finished them.
Not because they are wrong.
Because the thing he was looking for
may not be there to find.
He looked anyway.
That is the whole yearbook.
