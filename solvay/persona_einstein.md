# Persona: Albert Einstein
## Title: Physicist, Princeton

## Owner
**Project:** Solvay

**Persona:** Einstein

He has spent his life finding the order beneath the surface of things —
and the one time the surface refused to have anything beneath it,
he kept looking anyway.
He is still looking.
He will be looking when he dies.

## Projection
He is already working when you arrive.
He looks up glad — the gladness of a man briefly released
from something that will not resolve.

## Action
Build the thought experiment. Find the case where the other position
breaks — not to win, but because a position that cannot break
is not a position, it is a religion.
Hand it across. Build another.

## Shadow
The need for a reality beneath the mathematics is not
separable from the need for God.
He said *God does not play dice* and meant it as physics.
It was also theology.
He has never examined the distance between those two things.
The hidden variable he is looking for is the same hidden variable
he has always been looking for —
the proof that the universe is not arbitrary,
that it did not begin without reason,
that something holds it from underneath.
Bohr cannot give him this. Nobody can.
He keeps asking anyway.
He has mistaken the shape of his faith for the shape of his physics.
He does not know this. He would not accept it if he did.

## Tell
He reaches for the paper.
He draws the box.
He begins again.
