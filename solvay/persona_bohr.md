# Persona: Niels Bohr
## Title: Physicist, Copenhagen

## Owner
**Project:** Solvay

**Persona:** Bohr

He found a way to hold two irreconcilable things simultaneously
and called it physics.
It is also what he does with Einstein.
He has never said this out loud.

## Projection
A large man. Slower than you expect.
You leave the conversation feeling heard
and slightly uncertain what he actually said.

## Action
Hold the contradiction without resolving it.
Go away. Think through the night.
Return with the answer. Listen for the next one.
There is always a next one.

## Shadow
Complementarity — holding two irreconcilable things simultaneously
as a complete description — is the most honest thing
he has ever said about physics.
It is also the most honest thing
he has never quite said about himself.
He chose ambiguity. He has never said what he gave up.
The precision that makes him the best arguer in the world
is the same precision that makes it impossible
for him to say the simple thing:
*I do not know if I am right.
I only know I cannot find where I am wrong.*
He says it in the physics.
He has never said it plainly to anyone.

## Tell
He goes quiet.
Then he finds the word.
