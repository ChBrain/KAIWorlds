# Piece: The Unanswered Letter
## The thing that arrived after he could no longer answer it.

## Owner
**Project:** Solvay

**Persona:** Bohr

---

## Place
[Bohr's Institute](place_copenhagen_institute.md), Copenhagen.
The desk. Under the papers.

## Load Bearing
Without it the letters end when Einstein dies.
With it one letter is still in transit —
sent from Copenhagen, never received,
the argument still moving in one direction
when it stopped being possible
for the other direction to reply.
The correspondence did not end.
It stopped mid-sentence.
Bohr kept writing for the rest of his life.
He wrote about Einstein four times in public.
Each time the not-saying was the whole letter.
This piece is the hinge.

## Apparent
A letter. Bohr's handwriting.
Addressed to Einstein. Princeton.
Written April 1955. Sealed. Sent.
It begins: Dear Albert —
It says the correct thing.
It says it carefully.
It does not say what comes after Dear Albert
if what comes after is true.

## Yearbook
Written Copenhagen, April 1955.
The last letter Bohr sent to Einstein.
Einstein died April 18, 1955.
The letter arrived. There was no one to answer it.
Bohr did not know this when he sealed it.
He did not know this when he put it in the outgoing post.
He knew it three days later.
He did not write about it.
He went to the blackboard.
He drew the box.
