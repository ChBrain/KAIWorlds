# Piece: The Adiabatic Hypothesis
## The thing that found what survives when everything changes.

## Owner
**Project:** Solvay

**Persona:** Ehrenfest

---

## Place
[The Hôtel Métropole](place_hotel_metropole.md).
In the argument. Present at every exchange.

## Load Bearing
Without it Ehrenfest is a witness.
With it he is load-bearing in the physics itself —
the one who identified what is invariant under transformation,
what classical quantities survive the crossing into quantum mechanics,
what holds when the framework changes underneath it.
Both men need him in the room.
He can feel when something is breaking that shouldn't break.
He has always been able to feel this.
It has never helped him stop it.

## Apparent
A paper. 1916.
The adiabatic hypothesis: if a system changes slowly enough,
certain quantities remain invariant.
The action integral. The quantum numbers.
The things that do not change even as everything around them changes.
Ehrenfest found them.
He knew what to look for
because he had been watching things break
and noticed what didn't.

## Yearbook
Published 1916.
Einstein read it immediately. Wrote to Ehrenfest:
your adiabatic hypothesis is the most beautiful thing
in the new quantum theory.
Bohr built on it.
Ehrenfest watched both of them use what he had found
to build the argument he could not resolve.
The invariants held.
He did not.
