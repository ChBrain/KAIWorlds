# Place: Einstein's Study
## Location: 112 Mercer Street, Princeton. 1940s–1955.

## Owner
**Project:** Solvay

**Persona:** Einstein

## Shown
- A room that has stopped pretending to be tidy.
  Papers in stacks that have their own logic.
  Nobody else can find anything.
  He can find everything.
- The blackboard. Always in use.
  The equations on it are not quantum mechanics.
  They are the unified field theory.
  They have been the unified field theory for twenty years.
  They are not finished.
- The desk. Letters on it, half-answered.
  Some of them are from Bohr.
  The replies are careful.
  The care is the argument, continuing.
- The window. Princeton outside.
  A quiet street. Trees.
  He walks to the Institute every morning.
  The walk is where he thinks.
  The study is where he writes down what the walk found.
- The photograph on the wall. Not the Solvay photograph.
  A different one. Faraday. Maxwell. The ones who found
  the order beneath the surface before him.
  He put it there to remind himself the order is there.
  He has not needed reminding for forty years.
  It is still there.

## Holds
- [The Unified Field Theory](piece_the_unified_field_theory.md). Unfinished.
  It has been unfinished since 1925.
  It will be unfinished when he dies.
- [The EPR Paper](piece_the_epr_paper.md). Published. Still being read.
  Still not answered in the way he needed.
- [The Letters — Bohr to Einstein](piece_the_letters_bohr_to_einstein.md).
  On the desk. Half-answered.

## Offers
- The blackboard. For the next attempt.
- The paper. For the next letter to Bohr.
  The letters are still going.
  It is 1954. They are still going.

## Withheld
- [The EPR Paper](piece_the_epr_paper.md).
  Not physically — it was published, it is everywhere.
  But its final implication:
  that spooky action at a distance is real,
  that the universe is non-local,
  that he was right about the phenomenon
  and wrong about what it meant.
  The experiments that would show this
  will not happen until 1982.
  He will not live to see them.
  The study does not say this.
  The chalk does not say this.
  The blackboard holds the equations
  and withholds the ending.
