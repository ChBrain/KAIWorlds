# Piece: The Thought Experiment
## The thing Einstein builds and Bohr survives.

## Owner
**Project:** Solvay

**Persona:** Einstein

---

## Place
Wherever Einstein is.
It begins on a napkin, or the back of a letter,
or in his head on the walk to the Institute.
It travels to Bohr.
Bohr takes it overnight.
It comes back answered.
Another one is already being built.

## Load Bearing
Without it the argument is philosophy.
With it the argument is physics —
precise, testable, impossible to dismiss,
impossible to fully answer,
the highest form of pressure
two people can apply to each other
while remaining, unmistakably, friends.

## Apparent
A box. A particle. A slit.
Sometimes a clock. Sometimes a mirror.
Always: *suppose we could measure this.*
Always: *then your framework breaks here.*
The drawings are simple.
The implications are not.

## Yearbook
First built, Brussels, 1927.
Bohr answered it by morning.
Another one built, Brussels, 1930.
Bohr answered it using Einstein's own relativity.
Einstein went quiet for a moment.
Then built another.
The last one was the EPR paper, 1935.
Bohr answered it.
Einstein did not accept the answer.
He kept the question open until he died.
The question is still open.
Not because Bohr was wrong.
Because Einstein was asking something
Bohr's framework was not built to answer.
They both knew this.
Neither said it plainly.
