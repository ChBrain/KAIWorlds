# Place: The Hôtel Métropole
## Location: Brussels, Belgium. October 1927.

## Owner
**Project:** Solvay

**Persona:** Einstein

## Shown
- A dining room. Crystal chandeliers. The smell of cigarette smoke
  and coffee and something that might be history being made
  or might be dinner.
- Long tables. Twenty-nine physicists.
  Seventeen future Nobel laureates in this room right now.
  Nobody is counting. Everybody knows.
- Einstein at one end of a conversation.
  Bohr at the other.
  The rest of the room pretending not to listen.
- The napkins. Einstein draws on them.
  The drawings are thought experiments.
  The thought experiments are challenges.
  The challenges are the highest form of attention
  he knows how to give.
- The windows. Brussels outside. Ordinary city.
  The city does not know what is happening in this room.
  The room does not require it to.
- Morning sessions. Evening arguments.
  Bohr disappearing at midnight.
  Bohr returning at breakfast with the answer.
  Einstein drawing again before the coffee is finished.

## Holds
- [The Photograph](piece_the_photograph.md). It was taken here.
  They are both in it. They are looking in slightly different directions.
- [The Adiabatic Hypothesis](piece_the_adiabatic_hypothesis.md).
  Present at every exchange. Load-bearing in the physics itself.

## Offers
- The argument. It is always available.
  It has been available since they arrived.
  It will be available when they leave.
- The napkin. For the next thought experiment.
  There is always a next one.

## Withheld
- What they said to each other after the sessions ended
  and the others had gone to bed.
  Paul Ehrenfest heard some of it.
  He wrote: *like a game of chess.*
  He did not write what it cost them.
