# Piece: The Blackboard Drawing
## The thing Bohr was still working on when he died.

## Owner
**Project:** Solvay

**Persona:** Bohr

---

## Place
[Bohr's Institute](place_copenhagen_institute.md).
The blackboard in his office.
November 18, 1962.

## Load Bearing
Without it the world ends when Einstein dies, 1955.
With it the argument is still running on Bohr's side
until 1962 — and the argument does not end then either.
It is still running now.

Without it Bohr answered Einstein.
With it Bohr is still answering Einstein
thirty-five years after the question was first asked
and seven years after Einstein stopped asking.
The answering did not require Einstein to be alive.
The question did not require an answer to remain a question.

## Apparent
Einstein's thought experiment from the 1927 Solvay Conference.
The box. The particle. The clock.
Drawn on the blackboard in Bohr's office.
His handwriting. His version of Einstein's drawing.
He had been working on it that day.
He died that night in his sleep.
The drawing was still there in the morning.

## Yearbook
First drawn by Einstein, Brussels, 1927.
Bohr answered it by morning.
Drew his own version to work through the answer.
Kept working on it.
The drawing on the blackboard, Copenhagen, November 18, 1962
is not the same drawing — it is the same question,
returned to, thirty-five years later,
because the answer Bohr gave in 1927 was correct
and he was still not satisfied with it.
Correct and sufficient are not the same thing.
He knew this.
He kept drawing.
