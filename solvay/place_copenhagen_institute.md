# Place: Bohr's Institute
## Location: Blegdamsvej 17, Copenhagen, Denmark

## Owner
**Project:** Solvay

**Persona:** Bohr

## Shown
- A building he built for the argument.
  Not this argument specifically.
  The kind of argument where the best minds
  come to the same room and push until something gives.
  Nothing has given.
  The building is still standing.
- His office. First floor. The desk faces the window.
  The window faces the garden.
  The garden does not change between letters.
  This is its function.
- The blackboard. Always in use.
  Not equations — words.
  Bohr thinks in words more than numbers.
  The words are attempts at precision.
  Each one replaces the last.
  The last one is never quite right.
- The seminar room. Where the visitors come.
  Heisenberg came here. Pauli came here.
  Schrödinger came and argued for three days
  and left unconvinced.
  Einstein has never come.
  The argument happens by letter and by conference.
  It has never happened in this room.
  The room holds the shape of its absence.
- The smell: chalk, pipe smoke, the garden in summer.
  In winter the light goes early.
  He works through it.
- The door to the street. Copenhagen outside.
  Ordinary city. Bicycles. The harbour not far.
  The Institute is not the city.
  The city does not require the Institute.
  The Institute does not require the city.
  They coexist without explanation.

## Offers
- The overnight. When the letter arrives, or the thought experiment,
  he goes quiet. He goes away into the problem.
  He returns with the answer.
  The Institute is where the overnight happens.
  It has always been available.
  It is available now.

## Withheld
- The conversation he never had with Einstein in this room.
  Einstein was invited. Einstein did not come.
  The chair is still there.
  It has held many people.
  It has not held Einstein.
  The room knows the difference.
