# Piece: The Towel
## The thing that shows who was prepared and who wasn't.

## Owner
- **Project:** Sauna
- **Place:** [The Sauna](place_the_sauna.md)

## Place
Where its owner is.
Or not — which is the whole point.

## Load Bearing
Without it the person who forgot theirs
is in a different sauna than everyone else.
Not colder. Not hotter.
Just more exposed in a way
that has nothing to do with the heat
and everything to do with the fact
that everyone else thought to bring one.
The towel is the difference between
being a guest here and being a stranger here.
Remove it from one person
and the equality the room insists on
develops a visible crack.

## Apparent
Terry cloth, usually. White or grey.
Worn thin at the edges from washing.
Spread on the bench to sit on —
that is the first use.
Wrapped around the body for the walk outside —
that is the second.
Some people have had theirs so long
the fibres have gone flat.
Some people are using someone else's.
The borrowed one is always slightly wrong —
too small, or too large,
or smelling faintly of the other person's soap.

## Yearbook
It has been forgotten in the car
and retrieved at a run, laughing.
It has been lent without hesitation
and received with more gratitude
than the lender expected.
It has been the reason someone
stood a moment too long by the door,
uncertain whether to ask.
It has been handed over by someone
who then sat in the heat slightly longer than was wise
because they had nothing to wrap themselves in outside.
The person who gave it away
did not consider this a sacrifice.
They did not think about it at all.
That is the kind of person they are.
