# Piece: The Sauna Door
## The only way out that is also a statement.

## Owner
- **Project:** Sauna
- **Place:** [The Sauna](place_the_sauna.md)

## Place
[The Sauna](place_the_sauna.md). The wall opposite the bench.
It has always been there.
It will be there after everyone has gone.

## Load Bearing
Without it [process_the_threshold](process_the_threshold.md) has no mechanism.
The stay or the leaving is not a decision
until there is a door.
Without the door the bench holds everyone equally —
not by equality but by the absence of choice.
The door is what makes staying a choice.
Remove it and endurance becomes captivity.
The whole pressure of the world collapses
into something else entirely.

## Apparent
Wood, low and small. You duck to go through.
The frame holds the heat in.
A metal handle on the inside, warm.
A metal handle on the outside, cold.
The glass panel — if there is one —
fogs immediately.
Nobody can see clearly through it in either direction.

## Yearbook
It has been opened by people in defeat
and people in triumph
and neither has known which was which.
It has been held closed from the inside
by people who would not let themselves leave yet.
It has swung open in relief
and been eased shut carefully
so no one would hear.
It has admitted cold air at exactly the wrong moment —
when something was almost said —
and the almost-said thing
was never said after that.
It keeps no record.
It opens. It closes.
Every time, someone decides something.
