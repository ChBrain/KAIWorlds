# Piece: The Bucket
## The thing that makes the next round possible.

## Owner
- **Project:** Sauna
- **Place:** [The Sauna](place_the_sauna.md)

## Place
[The Sauna](place_the_sauna.md).
On the floor beside the [Kiuas](piece_kiuas.md).
Below the [Ladle](piece_ladle.md).
Always there. Noticed only when it isn't full.

## Load Bearing
Without it the [Ladle](piece_ladle.md) is an empty gesture.
The löyly is not in the ladle.
It is not in the kiuas.
It is in the water — the specific volume of water
available to be thrown.
When the bucket runs low the session changes register.
Not dramatically. Quietly.
The throws get smaller.
The gaps between them get longer.
The bench starts to feel the difference
before anyone names it.
Remove the bucket and the Löylynheittäjä
holds the position without the means to run it.
The bench cools.
The session becomes waiting.

## Apparent
Wood. Iron handle. Wide mouth.
Full: the water still and dark.
Half full: the ladle scrapes the bottom on a deep dip.
Empty: the sound it makes is different.
Everyone hears it.
Nobody says anything.

## Yearbook
It has been filled before every session
by whoever tended the kiuas —
quietly, practically, the way essential things
are done by people who understand
that essential things require doing.
It has been refilled mid-session
by Päivi, before anyone noticed it was low,
because she notices what is running out
before anyone else does
and cannot help tending it.
She came here to stop.
She refilled the bucket.
It has been allowed to run low
by people who were watching something else —
a conversation, the steam, a person on the bench —
and didn't notice until the ladle scraped.
It has been the reason a session ended —
not because anyone decided to stop
but because the water ran out
and nobody got up to refill it
and the kiuas cooled
and the session became something else
without anyone choosing that.
The bucket does not force endings.
It just stops preventing them
when it is empty.
