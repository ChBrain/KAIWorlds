# Piece: The Vihta
## The thing that changes what kind of sauna this is.

## Owner
- **Project:** Sauna
- **Place:** [The Sauna](place_the_sauna.md)

## Place
[The Sauna](place_the_sauna.md).
Soaking in a bucket of water near the door.
Or in someone's hand.
Or absent — which is also a statement.

## Load Bearing
Without it the sauna is functional.
With it the sauna is something older.
The vihta is the difference between
bathing and ritual.
Its presence changes the register of the room
before anyone has used it.
Its absence is equally noticed
by anyone who knows what its presence means.
Given to a guest: a specific kind of welcome.
Not offered: a specific kind of withholding.
Neither is neutral.
Neither is always intentional.
That is where it does its work.

## Apparent
Birch branches, bound at the handle.
The leaves soft from soaking.
It smells of birch and steam and something green
that does not belong to winter.
Used, it makes a sound — a wet brush against skin —
and leaves a faint mark that fades.
Old and dry: brittle, the leaves papery.
It should have been soaked longer.

## Yearbook
It has been brought from the cottage garden
and from the freezer where last summer's bundles wait.
It has been offered to guests
who did not know what it was
and to guests who took it with both hands.
It has been used with such vigour
that the leaves came off entirely
and someone laughed.
It has sat in its bucket all evening
and been carried home unused.
It has been the reason someone came back
and the thing that made a stranger
feel, for one moment, that they belonged.
It has been forgotten on the bench.
Found the next morning, dry and done.
