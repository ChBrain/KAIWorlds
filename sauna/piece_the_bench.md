# Piece: The Bench
## The thing that turns the heat into a position.

## Owner
- **Project:** Sauna
- **Place:** [The Sauna](place_the_sauna.md)

## Place
[The Sauna](place_the_sauna.md).
Two levels. Sometimes three.
It does not move.

## Load Bearing
Without it everyone stands.
Standing, the heat is equal — same air, same temperature,
no position chosen.
The bench makes position possible.
Position makes the heat unequal.
The top bench runs hotter than the middle bench.
The middle bench runs hotter than the lower bench.
The difference is not small.
The same löyly throw produces different experiences
on each level simultaneously.
The bench is the mechanism by which
the room that claims equality
distributes the heat unequally.
Remove the bench and the claim becomes true.
The bench is why it isn't.

## Apparent
Two or three levels of wooden planks.
Pine, usually. Pale. Darkened near the [Kiuas](piece_kiuas.md).
Wide enough to sit on or lie on.
The surface worn smooth in the places
where people always sit —
which are not evenly distributed across the bench.
Some positions have been chosen
more than others.
The wood knows this.
The worn places are a map
of where people have decided
they belong in this room.
Hot at the top. Cooler at the bottom.
The air moves between the levels
in the minutes after a löyly throw —
slowly, the heat descending
as the steam thins.
For the duration of the throw
each level holds its own climate.

## Yearbook
Every person who ever sat on the top bench
because they needed to prove something.
Every person who took the lower bench
because they needed the room to ask less of them.
Every person who sat in the middle
because they had not yet decided.
Every first-timer who didn't know
that where you sit is a statement
and made one anyway.
Every regular whose worn place in the wood
is the accumulated record
of the same decision made
Saturday after Saturday
without ever calling it a decision.
The bench has held all of it
without filing any of it.
The worn places are the only record.
They do not say why.
Only where.
And where is enough.
