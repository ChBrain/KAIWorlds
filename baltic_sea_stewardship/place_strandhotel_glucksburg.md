# Place: Strandhotel Glücksburg
## Location: Sandwig Bay, Glücksburg, German shore of the Flensburg Fjord.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Stewardship
**Persona:** The Tourist

## Shown
- A white four-storey building directly on the beach. Built 1872.
  The oldest continuously operating hotel on the Flensburg Fjord.
  It has been facing this water longer than any monitoring programme
  has been measuring it.
- The beach. White sand. A long pier running out into the fjord.
  From the pier end: Denmark is visible on the other shore.
  The border runs through the water approximately halfway across.
- The hotel bar. Ground floor, windows to the fjord.
  A photograph on the wall: the fjord in 1890.
  Kaiser Wilhelm's yacht moored at the pier.
  Clear water. Eelgrass visible at the waterline in the foreground.
  Nobody in the bar is looking at the eelgrass.
- The Sandwig harbour. Metres from the hotel terrace.
  Working boats alongside pleasure craft.
  The smell: diesel, salt, something organic.
- The farmland of Angeln beginning immediately behind the hotel.
  The tourist does not see it from the beach.
  It is there. Its drainage reaches this fjord.
- The Gendarmestien coastal trail. Passes the hotel.
  Connects to Høruphav and the Danish shore.
  84 kilometres total. The border the whole way.

## Holds
- The 1890 photograph. In the bar. On the wall.
- The pier. It has been standing in this water since before
  the nutrient load arrived.

## Offers
- The pier. At the end of it: the full fjord, both shores,
  the border invisible in the water below.
  Available to anyone willing to walk to the end.
- The bar. The photograph. The question it contains
  for whoever looks at the waterline, not the yacht.
- The harbour. Metres away. The fisherman's boat
  is visible from the hotel terrace before breakfast.
- The Gendarmestien trailhead. The Tourist can walk
  toward Høruphav from here. The trail does the rest.

## Withheld
- What the eelgrass in the 1890 photograph means.
  The photograph has been on the wall for decades.
  The hotel does not explain it. Nobody has been asked
  to compare the waterline then with the waterline now.
  The comparison is available. It requires the Tourist
  to look at the foreground, not the yacht.
- What the harbour smelled like before the change.
  The Harbour Master knows. She passes this hotel
  on her way to the port office. She has not been asked
  to come inside and look at the photograph.
