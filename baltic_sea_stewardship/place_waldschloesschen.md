# Place: Romantik Hotel Waldschlösschen
## Location: Schleswig, Schleswig-Holstein. Forest edge, five minutes by bicycle from the Schlei.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Stewardship
**Persona:** The Tourist

## Shown
- A low, sprawling hotel in a clearing between forest and meadow.
  65 years of family hospitality. The same family.
  Nordic ambience. Thatched outbuildings. An idyllic lake
  visible from the breakfast room.
- The bicycle rack at the entrance. Hire bikes available.
  The hotel map shows a cycling route to the Schlei.
  The route is marked in green. It passes through farmland.
  It passes a tile outfall where a drainage ditch meets
  a stream that meets the inlet. The map does not label this.
- The breakfast room window. The meadow beyond it.
  Beyond the meadow: fields. Beyond the fields: the Schlei,
  invisible from here, five minutes away.
- The restaurant. Herbs from the owner's own garden.
  Regional fish on the menu. The fish come from the Schlei
  and the Kiel Bight. The menu does not say what the stocks
  look like in the monitoring data.
- Haithabu visible twenty minutes by bike.
  The Viking trading settlement. One of the most important
  in northern Europe. On the Schlei. UNESCO World Heritage.
  The Schlei carried trade then. It carries nutrient load now.

## Holds
- The hotel cycling map. The route to the Schlei.
  The tile outfall is on the route.
  The map does not name it. The farmer knows where it is.

## Offers
- The bicycle. The route. The accidental arrival
  at the working Schlei rather than the tourist Schlei.
- The breakfast window. The meadow that looks like
  simply a meadow. The farmer whose field begins
  where the meadow ends is available if the Tourist
  cycles far enough.
- The evening meal. Regional fish. The question
  of where the fish came from and what the water
  looked like when they were caught.

## Withheld
- What the Schlei held before the postwar drainage
  infrastructure was laid. The sediment record at the
  bottom of the inlet holds it. The tourist cycling route
  passes above it without knowing it is there.
- The tile outfall. It is on the map as an unmarked
  point where the path crosses a drainage ditch.
  The farmer checks it after rain. He is sometimes there
  when the Tourist passes. Most tourists do not stop.
