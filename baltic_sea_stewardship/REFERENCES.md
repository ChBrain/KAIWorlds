## The 1864 War and Ludvig Hansen

Private Ludvig Hansen, no. 350, 10th Infantry Regiment, 2nd Company.
Born 13 April 1841 in Haderslev.
Killed 29 June 1864 at Høruphav, Als —
the last Danish soldier to fall in the battles on Als,
after the ceasefire had come into force.
Buried by the enemy at Høruphav.
Identity confirmed 1933 by museum director Jens Raben,
Sønderborg Castle.

Grave 1: Lambjerg Indtægt forest, c. 100m from Søndre Landevej,
just before Høruphav. Granite stone with black plate (1933).

Grave 2: Corner of Havbo and Havnevænget, Høruphav.
Cast iron fence. A second soldier from the same engagement.

Source: Danske krigergrave og mindesmærker 1848–50 og 1864.
http://gravminder.blogspot.com

## Adam Olearius

Adam Olearius (1599–1671). German scholar, mathematician,
geographer and librarian. Court mathematician and librarian
to Duke Friedrich III of Holstein-Gottorp at Gottorf Castle, Schleswig.

Built the Gottorf Globe, 1651–1664 — the first planetarium in the world.
Three metres in diameter. Twelve people could sit inside it.

Published the first comprehensive German-language description of Russia
and Persia (1647). Translated Saadi's Gulistan into German (1654).
Published a history of Holstein (1663).

Died at Gottorf Castle, 22 February 1671.
Buried in the Cathedral of Schleswig.

The restaurant at Romantik Hotel Waldschlösschen is named after him.

## Thomas Mann at Strandhotel Glücksburg

Thomas Mann (1875–1955) and his publisher Samuel Fischer
stayed at Strandhotel Glücksburg in the summer of 1919.

Buddenbrooks (1901) — novel about the four-generation decline
of a merchant family on the Baltic coast at Lübeck.
Nobel Prize for Literature, 1929.

Source: Strandhotel Glücksburg official history.
https://strandhotelgluecksburg.de/en/history/

## Strandhotel Glücksburg

Built 1872 as the Curhaus, Sandwig, Glücksburg.
Founded by the Aktiengesellschaft des Ostseebades Glücksburg.
Kaiser Wilhelm II visited with his yacht Hohenzollern, 1890.
Alfred Krupp was a regular guest.
Thomas Mann stayed, summer 1919.
https://strandhotelgluecksburg.de/en/history/
