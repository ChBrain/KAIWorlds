# Process: The Tending
## The daily attention that holds the water between research visits.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Stewardship
**Persona:** The Shore

## Initiates
Every morning before the instruments record anything.
Before the research vessel arrives.
Before the project meeting.
Before the decision cycle opens.
The Fisher checks the weather and the depth, in that order.
The Farmer walks the field boundary.
The Harbour Master opens the log.
The Elder sits on the harbour wall.
Nobody asked them to. They do it because the water
is theirs to tend, not because anyone assigned it.
The process initiates in the dark,
before the data cycle begins.

## Direction
Toward the water's continued existence
as a place someone is paying attention to.
Not governing. Not measuring. Not managing.
Tending. The attention is the act.
The tending does not require the water to respond.
It does not require acknowledgement.
It does not require the research to notice.
It runs regardless of whether it is sufficient.
It has never been sufficient.
It continues anyway.

## Lever
The daily return. Not the knowledge accumulated —
the act of returning to check.
Remove the daily return and the tending becomes
memory of tending. With it the water is witnessed
every day, in the gap between every monthly
research vessel visit, in the years between
every policy review, in the decades between
every governance reform.
The lever is the refusal to stop looking
even when looking is not enough.

## Echo
The water was witnessed today.
By someone who will witness it again tomorrow.
The record is not in any file.
It is in the accumulated attention of people
who cannot stop looking.
The tending ran.
The Tourist who passes through
receives a fraction of what the tending holds —
not the full record, but enough to feel
that the water has been cared about by someone
who was here before she arrived
and will be here after she leaves.
