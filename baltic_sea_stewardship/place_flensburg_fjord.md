# Place: The Flensburg Fjord
## Location: Western Baltic. German shore to the south. Danish shore to the north. 40 kilometres long.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Stewardship
**Persona:** The Shore

- The border. Running through the water
  approximately halfway along its length.
  Visible on maps. Invisible in the water.
  The fish do not cross it. The nutrients do not cross it.
  Neither registers it.

## Holds
- The Pre-1957 Record. Distributed across
  the four people who have been at this shore
  long enough to hold the comparison.
- The border marker. On the shore.
  Not in the water.
- The monitoring gap. The German stations
  measure the German shore. The Danish stations
  measure the Danish shore. The fjord
  is measured in two halves.
  Neither half says what the whole shows.

## Offers
- The view from the middle.
  Neither shore. Both shores.
  The border somewhere below.
  Available to anyone in a boat.
  Most Tourists do not go to the middle.
- The water itself. Swimming possible
  from both shores. The water
  does not distinguish between the swimmer
  on the German side and the swimmer
  on the Danish side.
  The eelgrass retreat is the same
  from both shores.

## Withheld
- What the fjord held before the border was drawn.
  The eelgrass beds at full extent.
  The cod population before the dead zone
  pushed them to shallower water.
  The oxygen in August before the thermocline
  strengthened with the warming.
  The fjord holds this in the sediment.
  It has not been asked to compare
  what it was with what it is.
  Nobody has brought the right instrument
  to both shores at the same time
  and read the whole record as one thing.
