# Place: Hotel Baltic
## Location: Høruphav, Als, Danish shore of the Flensburg Fjord.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Stewardship
**Persona:** The Tourist

## Shown
- A 19th-century hotel directly at the water.
  The marina in front of it: 300 berths,
  sheltered from all wind directions.
  Private harbour, owned by Høruphav Brolaug.
  Working boats and pleasure boats alongside each other.
- The Kegnæsstue. The warm room. Facing the harbour.
  Classic Danish home cooking. Local ingredients.
  The Tourist who has walked the Gendarmestien
  arrives here at the end of the last day.
  This is the first warm room after 84 kilometres
  of border trail.
- The Gendarmestien trail end. Marked outside the hotel.
  The trail has run the length of the Flensburg Fjord
  from Padborg to here. The Tourist has walked the border
  the gendarmes patrolled from 1920 to 1958.
  She has walked past farms, fishing villages,
  eelgrass shoreline, the line that the water
  has never respected.
- The harbour wall. Accessible from the hotel.
  In the morning before breakfast it is quiet.
  The fisherman's boat is in the harbour.
  The Village Elder sometimes sits on the wall.
- Høruphav has been a recreational area for
  Sønderborg's residents through generations.
  The people who come here have always come here.
  The Tourist is the only one who arrived this week
  for the first time.

## Holds
- The harbour log. Held by Høruphav Brolaug.
  Not in the hotel. At the harbour office.
  The Tourist does not know it exists.

## Offers
- The Kegnæsstue. The meal. The table next to
  whoever is already there.
  The fisherman comes in from the harbour.
  The Village Elder has his usual table.
  The Tourist sits down because she is tired
  and hungry and it is the only warm room.
  The conversation starts from that.
- The harbour wall. The morning.
  The view of the fjord before anyone else is watching.
  The Village Elder's usual spot.
- The trail end. The Tourist who finished the walk
  is not the same Tourist who started it.
  She has been carrying the border for 84 kilometres.
  She is ready to ask what she has been walking past.

## Withheld
- What Høruphav looked like when the herring
  were still running at volume.
  The Village Elder knows. He has not been asked
  by someone who arrived without already knowing
  the answer. The Tourist is the first person
  in a long time who does not know.
- The specific August when the fisherman
  understood the change was permanent.
  He has not said this to anyone.
  He might say it here, to the Tourist,
  over the food she ordered because
  she did not know the menu.
