# Position: The Elder
## The pre-instrument baseline in living form.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Stewardship
**Role:** The Shore

## Has
The memory of what the water was before the monitoring began.
Not data. Embodied knowledge — the smell of the herring season,
what the eelgrass did to the boats, what the water looked like
in an August before the dead zone had a name.
The position holds the pre-1957 record.
It exists in whoever has been at this shore long enough
to hold the comparison between then and now.
It is mortal. When the holder goes, it goes.
The position does not offer an archive.
It offers a person, for the duration of their life.

## Orders
Stay. Return to the harbour wall.
The position does not explain why this matters.
It simply requires continued presence
at the place where the knowledge was accumulated.
The elder did not agree to hold the baseline.
They accumulated it by being here.
The position arrived after the accumulation was complete.
Now it requires them to remain available
to whoever arrives with the right question.
The right question has not arrived for a long time.

## Loses
The expectation of being asked.
The elder was certain the knowledge had value —
that someone would come, eventually, and ask
in a way that would let it be given a form.
The position shows them that the form requires a receiver,
and that receivers arrive without preparation,
or not at all.
The Tourist is not a researcher. She is not equipped.
She is the only person who arrived without already knowing
what the elder knows — which makes her
the only person who might be able to receive it.

## Drives
On the loss of the expectation — points anyway.
Says: it was different here.
Not because it will be recorded.
Because it is true and there is someone present
and the knowledge will not survive silence.
The drive is the refusal to let what was witnessed
go unwitnessed one more time.

*Assigned by: The Shore. Duration of presence from before the change.*
*Held by: whoever was here before the monitoring began and is still here now.*
