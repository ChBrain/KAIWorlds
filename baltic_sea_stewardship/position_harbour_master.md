# Position: The Harbour Master
## The log that records what the instruments visit once a month.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Stewardship
**Role:** The Harbour

## Has
The continuous local record. Every day the harbour is open,
someone holds this position. The log runs without interruption —
weather, boats, catch, maintenance. Between every monthly visit
from the research vessel, between every entry in the Boknis Eck
time series, the harbour log is running.
The position holds the shore's daily witness.
It exists before the current holder arrived
and will continue after they leave.

## Orders
Keep the log. Record what the harbour sees.
The position does not specify what counts as recordable.
It hands the harbour master the logbook and says: write it down.
What gets written is what the holder notices.
What gets noticed depends on how long they have been looking.
The margin is not official. The position does not prohibit it.
It does not require it either. The noting in the margin
is what the position makes possible in someone
who has been looking long enough.

## Loses
The certainty that what she noticed is real.
The harbour master was certain that thirty years of looking
at the same water produced reliable observation.
The position shows her that reliable observation
without scientific methodology is not evidence —
it is the thing that evidence would either confirm or correct.
She cannot confirm it herself.
She has been noting it anyway.
The uncertainty does not stop the noting.
It stops the telling.

## Drives
On the loss of certainty — keeps the margin.
The record exists even though it cannot be used
until someone asks for it in the right language.
The margin fills. The log closes each day.
The drive is the refusal to let the noticing disappear
just because the noticing has no official form.

*Assigned by: The Harbour. Tenure of the post.*
*Held by: whoever has been at this harbour long enough to notice the change.*
