# Process: The Season
## The annual return that makes the comparison inevitable.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Stewardship
**Persona:** The Shore

## Initiates
The year turns. The Fisher goes out again.
The Farmer walks the field boundary again.
The Harbour Master opens the log again.
The Elder returns to the harbour wall.
None of them decided to begin.
The season began and they are in it.
The process initiates the moment the steward
finds themselves doing the same thing
they did last year, in the same place,
and the comparison arrives without being summoned.

## Direction
Toward the moment of maximum knowledge.
The Fisher in August knows things about this water
he does not know in January.
The Farmer in spring knows things about the field
he does not know in autumn.
The moment of maximum knowledge is also
the moment of maximum witness —
when what is declining is most visible
because this year is being held against last year
against the year before.
The comparison deepens with each return.
It cannot be undone by not returning.

## Lever
The accumulated years. One season changes nothing.
Ten seasons is a pattern. Forty seasons is a record.
The lever is duration — the irreducible fact
that the steward has been here before
and remembers what before looked like.
Remove the duration and the season produces
only this year. With it the season produces
this year against every year the steward has lived through.
That is the record the instruments cannot replicate.

## Echo
Another year has been witnessed.
The comparison has deepened by one.
The knowledge the steward carries is now
one year more precise, one year more irreplaceable,
one year closer to the moment
when no one holds it anymore.
The season ran. The record grew.
The record is still in a body, not a file.
