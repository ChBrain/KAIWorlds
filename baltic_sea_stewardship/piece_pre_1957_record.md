## Load Bearing
Without it the Boknis Eck time series has no before.
Every trend the instruments show is a trend
from the moment the instruments were installed —
which was after the postwar agricultural intensification
had already been running for a decade.
The pre-instrument baseline is in the people
who were here before 1957, or who inherited
the knowledge of those people.
Remove it and the full scale of the change
has no living witness. Keep it and the record
exists — mortal, distributed, not yet in any file,
available for the duration of the people who hold it.

## Apparent
Not a single object. A conversation that has not happened.
The Fisher's coordinates of where the cod held
before they stopped holding there.
The Farmer's knowledge of what the wet corner produced
before the tile was laid.
The Harbour Master's margin, four years deep.
The Elder's gesture toward the water and the sentence:
it was different here.
Each one a fragment. Together: the baseline.
The Tourist is the only person in the room
who does not already hold a fragment
and therefore might be able to receive all of them.

## Yearbook
Before 1957: the water was different here.
The herring ran at volume. The eelgrass fouled the propellers.
The deep basins held oxygen through August.
Nobody wrote it down as baseline.
It was just the water.

30 April 1957: Johannes Krey lowers the first water sampler
at Boknis Eck. The monitoring begins.
The sea is already under pressure.
The before is already the past.
The people who remember it are still alive.

The monitoring runs for sixty-seven years.
The before recedes. The people who hold it age.
The knowledge becomes more precise
as it becomes more mortal.

The Tourist arrives. She does not know
she is the last generation
for whom the living record is still available —
the last person who can receive the comparison
from someone who was here before the comparison
needed to be made.

The piece has no form yet.
