# Piece: The Scissors
## The shadow, made visible.

## Owner
**Project:** The Shadow
**Persona:** [Hans Christian Andersen](persona_hans_christian_andersen.md)

---

## Place
Where Andersen is. Always in his hand or within reach of it.

## Load Bearing
Without them the feeling stays in the body. With them it becomes a shape and can be handed across. They are the threshold between feeling and form. Remove them and the writing cannot begin — he said so himself.

## Apparent
Enormous. Larger than the work requires. The handles worn smooth. They open and close without sound. In Odense they are lit so they cast huge shadows.

## Yearbook
Odense, age fourteen. Already cutting before he knew why.

Every child who received a swan.

Every silhouette of a person — their shadow, cut from black paper, handed back to them. He made the shadow real and gave it away. He did this for forty years before he wrote the story.

*Paper cutting is the prelude to writing.* 1847. The same year as The Shadow.

He took more pleasure in the praise for the cut than in the impression the story made. He knew the difference. He preferred the cut.

The Shadow was cut from this paper. Collin received it.

A year before his death he added a skull to the iconography. Cupids. A hanged man. A dancer. Hearts. Trees. Then a skull.
