## Hans Christian Andersen (1805-1875)

Born in Odense. The son of a shoemaker. He left for Copenhagen at fourteen with almost nothing. He failed as an actor and a singer. He was eventually educated through the patronage of Jonas Collin, a Danish civil servant who supported him for decades and remained the central relationship of his life.

Andersen cut paper from the time he was a boy. He carried scissors whenever he traveled. He gave the cuts away - to children, to hosts, to strangers. He cut while he talked. Thousands of silhouettes survive him. He took more pleasure in the praise for the cut than in the impression the stories made. He knew the difference. He preferred the cut.

## Secondary sources

**Jackie Wullschlager** - *Hans Christian Andersen: The Life of a Storyteller* (2000)
The most complete biography in English. Essential for the Collin relationship, the paper cutting practice, and the late difficult decades. The relationship between Andersen's life and his work is treated honestly here.

**Elias Bredsdorff** - *Hans Christian Andersen: The Story of His Life and Work* (1975)
The earlier standard biography. Detailed on the Copenhagen years and the friendship networks. Still useful.

**Anne Buschhoff and Detlef Stein** (eds.) - *Hans Christian Andersen: Poet mit Feder und Schere* (2018)
Exhibition catalog, Wienand Verlag. Published to accompany the exhibition at Kunsthalle Bremen, 20 October 2018 - 24 February 2019. With texts by Ejnar Stig Asgaard, Anne Buschhoff, Klaus Müller-Wille, and Detlef Stein. The largest presentation of Andersen's visual work in Germany. ISBN 978-3-86832-451-8.
