## Place
[The Farm](place_farm.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it the Drift is speculation.
With it: the Drift is an obligation.
The Farmer is committed to a count by a date.
The Buyer is committed to a price on delivery.
Remove the Contracte: the road is a gamble.
With it: a supply chain.

## Apparent
A written agreement. Concluded the previous autumn.
Names: the seller, the buyer, the count of animals,
the price per head agreed, the delivery point - Wedel,
the delivery date - spring market.
Signed or sealed by both parties.
Carried by the Farmer through the winter.
The Buyer holds his copy in Flanders or Hamburg.

## Yearbook
**Attested directly:**
Contracts - Contracte - were often concluded the year before
the Drift, directly in Jutland and across the peninsula,
with price and delivery date agreed in advance.
(Source: ochsenweg.de, Der mittelalterliche Ochsenhandel)

**Structural confirmation:**
The same practice was standard across northern European
commodity trade in the same period and among the same
network of Flemish and Dutch buyers.
Advance contracts for agricultural commodities -
wool, cattle, grain - were normal commercial instruments
in the 13th–17th century North Sea trade network.

**The Contracte at Wedel:**
The [Handshake](piece_handshake.md) at Wedel is the fulfilment
of the Contracte - not a new negotiation,
but the closing of one already opened.
