# Piece: The Cattle

## Owner
- **Project:** Ochsenweg
- **Place:** [The Farm](place_farm.md)

## Place
[The Farm](place_farm.md).
Until the Farmer decides.
Then: where the Drover is.

## Load Bearing
Without the Cattle there is no Drift.
The road existed before them. It will exist after.
But the Drift - the mechanism that built the market at Wedel,
that named the road, that employed the Drover,
that required the Toll Keeper, that fed the Innkeeper,
that filled the Ferry Barge - runs on the Cattle.
Remove them: the road is a path. Not a system.

## Apparent
Lean. Wintered in Jutland on sparse feed.
Smaller than the fattened cattle they will become
in the Frisian marshes.
Horns marked before departure -
specific cuts or burns identifying the Farmer's ownership.
Walk at approximately 15–20 km per day.
Lose condition on the road.
Arrive at Wedel thinner than they left.

## Yearbook
**c. 1420 onward** - lean cattle assembled each spring.
**1485** - 2,491 oxen recorded at Gottorf toll station.
**1545–1547** - 39,000 head at Schleswig toll.
**1610–1620** - peak: 40,000–45,000 head per year.
**1618 onward** - numbers fall.
**1885** - 700 animals at Wedel market.
**2003** - 88 animals.
**2022** - the market cancelled. The Cattle do not walk south anymore.
