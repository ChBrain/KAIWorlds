# Piece: The Gate

## Owner
- **Project:** Ochsenweg
- **Place:** [The Toll Gate](place_toll_gate.md)

## Place
[The Toll Gate](place_toll_gate.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it the threshold has no body.
The Toll Keeper's authority requires a physical point
that the road passes through and cannot bypass.
Remove the Gate: the authority exists in principle. It cannot be enforced.

## Apparent
A barrier across the road.
In early centuries: a rope or bar.
Later: a wooden gate on a post.
Later still: a fortified structure at permanent toll stations.
At the Danevirke: the gap in the wall that the road passes through.
Always: the thing that is closed until the Toll Keeper opens it.

## Yearbook
**c. 500 CE onward** - the Danevirke crosses the road. First institutional Gate.
**737–968 CE** - Danevirke expanded. Gate at Hedeby controls
the most important crossing point between the two seas.
**16th–18th century** - permanent toll stations at Rendsburg,
Neumünster, Bramstedt, Ulzburg.
**Rinderpest years** - the Gate acquires quarantine function.
**1864** - Danevirke Gate used militarily for the last time.
Abandoned when Prussian troops bypass it across frozen marshland.
**19th century** - toll stations close. The Gates come down.
