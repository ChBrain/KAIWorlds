# Piece: The Pilgrim's Credential

## Owner
- **Project:** Ochsenweg

## Place
Where the Pilgrim is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the Pilgrim is a vagrant.
With it: someone with a sanctioned reason to be walking south.
The Toll Keeper reads it. The Innkeeper charges the pilgrim rate.
The hospice opens its door.

## Apparent
A folded document. Issued by a church authority before departure.
Names the pilgrim. Names the destination.
Accumulates stamps and marks at churches, hospices, and waypoints.
By Wedel it is worn, marked, and damp from the road.

## Yearbook
**c. 1200 onward** - pilgrims using the Hærvejen as the northern leg
of the route to Santiago de Compostela. The Via Jutlandica.
**c. 1500** - included in Erhard Etzlaub's Romwege map.
**Post-Reformation** - pilgrimage diminishes in Protestant Scandinavia.
**Present** - a modern Pilgrim Passport available at 45 stamp points.
Functionally identical to the medieval Credential.
