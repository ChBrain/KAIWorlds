# Piece: The Handshake

## Owner
- **Project:** Ochsenweg
- **Place:** [The Wedel Market](place_wedel_market.md)

## Place
[The Wedel Market](place_wedel_market.md).
Under the [Roland](piece_roland.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it the negotiation is a conversation.
The Handshake is the act that makes the transaction legally binding.
Under the authority of the Roland, in the presence of witnesses,
the palms meet. After that: the cattle belong to the Buyer.
Remove the Handshake: the market is a gathering. Not a court.

## Apparent
Two right hands meeting. Held for a moment. Released.
The entire transaction resolves into this.
Invisible afterward. No document records the gesture itself.
Only its consequences.

## Yearbook
**c. 1450 onward** - performed under the Roland as the legal
instrument of sale at the Wedel market.
**Peak, c. 1600** - performed thousands of times each spring.
Up to 30,000 cattle changing hands by this gesture.
**1885** - still performed. 700 animals.
The Handshake is the last functioning part of the original mechanism.
**1984** - a bronze sculpture installed on the Wedel market square.
Two hands in a handshake.
**2003** - 88 animals. Performed by the last dealers who came
for tradition, not profit.
**2022** - the market cancelled. The bronze sculpture remains.
