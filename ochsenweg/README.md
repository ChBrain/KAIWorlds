## What this world is

The Ochsenweg (Ox Road; Danish: Hærvejen) runs from Viborg in Denmark to Wedel on the Elbe. It follows the Jyske Højderyg - the Jutland Ridge - the only dry line north to south on the Cimbrian Peninsula. The road was not constructed. It was walked into being.

Ten personas. Ten pieces. Six places. Ten positions. Three processes.
The Yearbook of the Ridge Road spans 3,700 years. The Ochsenmarkt at Wedel was cancelled in March 2022.

## The processes

**Travel South** - movement along the ridge from Viborg to Wedel. The road is dry enough to walk. A position has a reason to move south. The season is spring. The resistance is everything between here and there.

**Cross the Threshold** - runs at every point the road meets authority. The toll gate, the ferry landing, the market entrance. The traveller has what the threshold requires, or does not.

**Work the Market** - the transaction. The cattle exchange hands or they do not. The Contracte is drawn or it is not. The Handshake seals what the Toll Register records.

## The personas

**Mads Eriksen** *(The Farmer, Jutland, 1581)* - owns the cattle. Does not walk south himself. The weight of the decision stays on the farm.

**Jens** *(The Drover, Holstein, c. 1600)* - hired to move the cattle from the farm to Wedel. The cattle are not his. The road knowledge is.

**Sigrid Magnusdóttir** *(The Pilgrim, Norwegian, c. 1347)* - walks south on the same road as the drovers. The Credential she carries is not for the market.

**Heinrich Brauer** *(The Trader, Lübeck, c. 1510)* - moves goods north and south. Knows the toll rates at every gate.

**Georg Pfeiffer** *(The Toll Keeper, Rendsburg, 1744)* - the road passes through his gate. The register records who and how many.

**Margarethe** *(The Innkeeper, Neumünster, c. 1560)* - knows which travellers in the room should not be trusted. Does not offer this freely.

**Pieter de Groot** *(The Ferryman, Wedel, c. 1595)* - the last threshold before the market. The Elbe does not negotiate.

**Niels Buch** *(The Cattle Dealer, Jutland, c. 1572)* - assembles the herd for the Farmer. His count is the Farmer's evidence.

**Adriaan van Hoorn** *(The Buyer, Antwerp, c. 1580)* - arrived from the south. The cattle fatten in the Frisian marshes. He knows what they will be worth.

**Inge Petersen** *(The Child of Wedel, born 1955)* - seven years old in 1962, watching the cattle pass the Roland at five in the morning. Certain the market will be here every April for the rest of her life.
