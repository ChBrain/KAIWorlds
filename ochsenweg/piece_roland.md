# Piece: The Roland

## Owner
- **Project:** Ochsenweg
- **Place:** [The Wedel Market](place_wedel_market.md)

## Place
[The Wedel Market](place_wedel_market.md).
North side of the Marktplatz, since 1950.
Previously: the centre of the Marktplatz.

## Load Bearing
Without it [Work the Market](process_work_the_market.md)
has no authority. The handshake under the Roland is legally binding.
The handshake in an open field is a conversation.
Remove the Roland: the market is a gathering. Not a court.

## Apparent
4.5 metres tall. 32 blocks of Obernkirchener sandstone.
A standing figure: crown, sword, imperial orb.
Faces south toward the Elbe.
Full colour - painted, parts gilt.
The lower quarter replaced in 1950 by sculptor Egon Lissow.
The brick pedestal: since 1950. The original stone pedestal: gone.

## Yearbook
**c. 1450** - first Roland erected. Probably wood.
**1558** - current statue commissioned. Sandstone. 32 blocks.
Stands in the centre of the Marktplatz. Faces the Elbe.
**1653** - severely damaged after Thirty Years' War. Extensively renewed.
**1950** - post-war relocation. Lower quarter rebuilt by Egon Lissow.
Moved to north side of Marktplatz.
**1984** - restored. Bronze handshake sculpture installed nearby.
**2012** - final restoration. 800th anniversary of Wedel.
**2022** - Ochsenmarkt cancelled indefinitely. The Roland still stands.
