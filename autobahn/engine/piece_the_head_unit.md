# The Head Unit

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
In the dashboard. Centre, within reach of the Driver.

## Load Bearing
Without it the cab is silent and sealed. The head unit is the interface between the Driver and what the car can receive or play. Remove it and the Driver has only the road and whatever is already in their head.

## Apparent
A unit in the dashboard. Buttons, knobs, a display — analogue or early digital. A slot for a cassette or a CD. A tuner for UKW. The volume knob turned once at the start and not touched again, or adjusted constantly, or turned down at certain moments without the Driver noticing they did it. No internet connection. No app. No account. What it plays is what is physically present or what is being broadcast.

## Yearbook
In a truck cab: a CD loaded before departure. Still playing. The Driver stopped hearing it before the halfway point. The head unit does not know this.

In a light commercial vehicle: UKW, the same regional station since departure. Traffic report at the half hour. The Driver registered the last one without turning it up.

In a compact car: a slot that still reads CDs in a car that is otherwise ordinary. Used occasionally. Mostly silent.
