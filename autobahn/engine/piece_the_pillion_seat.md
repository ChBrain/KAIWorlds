# The Pillion Seat

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
Behind the rider on a motorbike. Where the rider is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the motorbike carries one person. With it the journey is shared - but asymmetrically. The rider steers. The pillion holds on.

## Apparent
A seat behind the rider's seat. Narrower. Higher in some configurations. Footpegs on both sides. A grab rail or the rider's body - one or the other, and the choice between them is itself a statement. No windscreen. No roof. No door. The wind at 130 km/h a physical force against the chest and face. Rain arrives unfiltered. Cold is the temperature of the air at speed. The road surface transmitted directly through the seat and spine. The rider's back the only fixed point in the field of view.

## Yearbook
Occupied by whoever trusted the rider enough to get on. The pillion cannot steer, cannot brake, cannot see past the rider's shoulder. Can lean with the bike or against it - leaning against it is wrong, and the rider feels it immediately. The pillion learns to lean with, or the journey is short. Every passenger in every car is separated from the driver by a dashboard, a gearstick, a cupholder. The pillion is separated by nothing. This is either the point or the problem, depending on who is riding.
