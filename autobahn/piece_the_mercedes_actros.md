# The Mercedes Actros

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
Where its Driver is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the goods do not move. The Actros is not on the A7 for the journey - it is on the A7 for the cargo. Remove it and the supply chain behind it stops. The right lane empties. The MAN truck knows this. The Actros knows it differently - it is a newer cab, more automated, the driver assist systems more present.

## Apparent
Seats: the Driver Seat, the Passenger Seat - inner, the Passenger Seat - outer.

High. The Mercedes star on the front of the cab. The cab wider than the MAN - the GigaSpace variant, room for the bunk and a second person if needed. The Predictive Powertrain Control active: the transmission shifting based on GPS data about the road ahead, the truck anticipating the Rendsburg bridge gradient before it arrives. The driver seat with air suspension. Two passenger seats: GigaSpace and BigSpace variants are 2.5 metres wide with flat floor - no engine tunnel compression. Interior height of 2.13 metres between the seats. The cab a room, not a cockpit. The mirrors: large, the trailer visible in sections. The dashboard: more screens than the MAN. The automation more present, more offering to take over. The Driver deciding how much to let it.

## Yearbook
On the A7 today with a load that will be somewhere else tomorrow. The driver has been in this cab for nine hours. The Predictive Powertrain Control has been active the entire time. The driver has been watching it work, deciding when to override it, developing a relationship with the automation that nobody at Mercedes anticipated and nobody has a name for.
