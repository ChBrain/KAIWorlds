# the Brücke Hengstey

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn
- Place: the A1

## Shown
Between [Volmebrücke](place_a1_volmebruecke.md) and [Hagen-West](place_a1_88_hagen_west.md). The rail tracks of the Rangierbahnhof Hagen-Vorhalle cross the A1 here. From above: the Brücke Hengstey carries the tracks over the road - 88 metres, eight rail tracks and two district roads. From below: the A1 passes through the Unterführung Vorhalle Bahngelände - 121 metres, no hard shoulder, daylight narrows then returns. One crossing, two names, depending on which side you are on. Right side southbound: noise barrier, solid, turquoise-blue, high. Nothing of the tracks visible from the carriageway.

## Holds
The crossing. Rail bridge built 1960. Expanded 1985. Structural deficits identified 2011 - heavy goods on outer lanes only, overtaking prohibited for trucks. Replacement construction 2018–2023. Three spans: 25m / 37m / 25m. Completed and in full service. Up to 100,000 vehicles per day through the underpass. The Rangierbahnhof lies to the west - one of nine major marshalling yards in Germany.

## Offers
The passage. The only point between exits 87 and 88 where rail and road cross. Neither sees the other.

## Withheld
What moves on the tracks. The noise barrier withholds it in both directions.
