## Shown
The Vogelfluglinie. The A1 enters Schleswig-Holstein at Heiligenhafen-Ost and runs south through Ostholstein - past Oldenburg in Holstein, Neustadt, along the Lübecker Bucht coast, through Lübeck - to Kreuz Lübeck where the A20 branches east. Two lanes per direction. The landscape: the Wagrien peninsula, the Ostsee coast, the Holsteinische Schweiz, the approach to the Hanseatic city. The road follows the old Migratory Birds Line - the route that birds fly from Scandinavia south. The B207 north of Heiligenhafen continues to Puttgarden and the ferry to Rødby. The A1 is the German road of the Scandinavian connection.

## Offers
The Vogelfluglinie - the fastest land route between Hamburg and Scandinavia. The Baltic coast from Fehmarn to Lübeck. The transition between the Scandinavian world and the German interior.

## Withheld
The direction. The A1 runs both ways. North leads toward the ferry, toward Denmark, toward Scandinavia. South leads toward Hamburg and the rest of Germany. Which direction a Driver is travelling is not visible in the road - only in the car and the person in it.
