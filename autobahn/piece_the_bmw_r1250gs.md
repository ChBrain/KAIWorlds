# The BMW R1250GS

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn
- [Persona: Jonas](persona_jonas.md)

## Place
Where its Rider is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the Rider is not on the road in this way. The R1250GS is what makes the A7 a physical experience rather than a managed one. Remove it and the Rider is in a car - enclosed, insulated, separated from the road. On the GS the separation is gone. The road is at body distance.

## Apparent
Seats: the Driver Seat, the Pillion Seat.

Large for a motorbike - the adventure category, built for both Autobahn and gravel. The boxer engine: cylinders protruding either side, the engine visible, the vibration through the handlebars at idle. The seat high - the Rider above car rooflines, the A7 visible further ahead than from any car. No roof. No doors. The wind above 100 km/h a physical presence - not noise but pressure. The weather not filtered. The temperature of the road felt through the boots. The lane lines visible directly below. The truck passing at 90 km/h: a wall of turbulence that moves the GS sideways by half a metre before the Rider corrects without thinking.

## Yearbook
On the A7 today because someone chose the long way home. Or the only way home. Or because it is the kind of day that requires the road to be felt rather than crossed.

At the Stau south of Neumünster: the Rider filtered to the front between the lanes - legal in Germany if done carefully. Forty cars passed in four minutes. Each driver watched the GS go by. Some of them thought about it. Most did not.
