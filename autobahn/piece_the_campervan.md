# The Campervan

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
Where its Driver is. Slower than it looks like it should be.

## Load Bearing
Without it the journey is a car journey with accommodation at the other end. With it the journey is the accommodation - the bed, the kitchen, the table are all moving at 100 km/h on the A7. Remove it and two people need a hotel. With it they need only a parking place, and the A7 has a Raststätte every forty kilometres.

## Apparent
Seats: the Driver Seat, the Passenger Seat, the Caravan Berth.

High. The wind resistance above 110 km/h audible from inside - a pressure rather than a noise. The cab integrated into the living space: the driver seat forward, the living area behind, no partition. The kitchen directly behind the seats. At the Raststätte: the highest vehicle in the car park that is not a truck. The tinted rear windows: a privacy the car does not have. The fold-out bed over the cab: occupied or folded, visible from the outside when occupied, the shape of two people sleeping visible against the tinted glass.

## Yearbook
Purchased when someone decided the hotel years were over. On the A7 today going north or south depending on the season. June: going north toward Denmark. August: going south toward Bavaria or Austria. The Driver has done this route before. The route is the point - not the destination, the moving. The campervan is the argument that the journey and the destination are the same thing.
