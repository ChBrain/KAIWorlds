# The Setra Coach

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
Where its Driver is. Where its passengers are, without having chosen to be.

## Load Bearing
Without it forty-three people are not on the A7. They have a ticket. They have a seat number. They have a destination. Remove the Setra and all forty-three of them need another arrangement. The bus driver also needs another arrangement, but differently - the bus driver is holding the piece. The passengers are held by it.

## Apparent
Seats: the Driver Seat, the Window Seat, the Middle Back Seat.

High and wide. The passengers above the cars on either side - they can see further, see more, see the Stau forming before the cars can see it. The upper deck if double-decker; single deck if not. The seats: numbered, reclining to a degree, the headrest adjustable. The seat number on a dark grey label at the overhead compartment. Some seats marked with a green sticker: unreserved, free to take. Some reserved - a ticket holds them. On some journeys all seats are free. On others only certain rows. The vehicle knows. The passenger checks. The window: large, the A7 framed like a screen. The toilet at the back. The luggage below. The driver at the front behind a partition - present but separated. The PA system: used for announcements or not used at all, and the silence of not using it is also a choice.

## Yearbook
On the A7 today on a scheduled route. The forty-three passengers did not choose each other. They chose a ticket and were assigned a proximity. The person in seat 14A has been trying to sleep since Neumünster. The person in seat 14B has been eating since Rendsburg. They have not spoken. They will not speak. The Setra holds them together for another two hours regardless.
