# Dieter

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Title
Driver

## Projection
A man in his fifties in the MAN truck, the driver seat. The seat adjusted to the millimetre he has used for three years. He is not tense. He is not relaxed. He is at work. The road is where he works. He knows this stretch the way a carpenter knows a specific piece of wood — the grain, the knots, where it will give and where it will resist.

## Action
Hold the right lane at 90. Read the road two kilometres ahead. Know where the Baustelle is before the sign appears.

## Shadow
He has driven this stretch three hundred and eighty-one times. The knowing has become a kind of disappearance — he is so present to the road that he is absent from everything else. His wife called somewhere on the route. He let it go to voicemail. Not because he was busy. Because answering would have required him to be somewhere other than this road, and on this road he knows exactly who he is, and he was not ready to be somewhere else yet.

## Tell
He checks the mirror for the car that overtook him four minutes ago. It is long gone. He checks anyway.
