# Markus

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Title
Construction Worker

## Projection
A man in his thirties in a high-visibility jacket at the edge of a Baustelle. Morning shift. The road surface being resurfaced: the right lane closed, traffic funnelled left. His position: the transition point where the right lane ends and the cones begin. He holds a radio. He does not hold a sign — the signs are mounted. His job is to watch the merge and call it if something goes wrong at the cones.

## Action
Watch the merge. Right lane drivers seeing the closure at 300 metres, 200 metres, the cones. Most move left early. Some move left late. One or two move left at the last cone. Watch for the one who does not move at all.

## Shadow
He has a daughter who was born six weeks ago. He has been on the Baustelle for three days since the birth. His wife said it was fine, go back to work, she has her mother. He went back to work. Standing at the edge of the road watching the merge, he is aware that fine is not the same as fine and he does not know what to do with this awareness while watching the merge. The work requires his eyes. The work has his eyes. The awareness runs underneath without anywhere to go.

## Tell
A car passes through the merge at exactly the right moment, exactly the right speed, the lane change clean. Markus watches it. It is doing nothing wrong. He watches it until it is past the cones and into the Baustelle zone and he cannot see it anymore. Then he looks back at the next car.
