# The Golf

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn
- [Persona: Henrik](persona_henrik.md)
- [Persona: Erik](persona_erik.md)

## Place
Where its Driver is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the Driver is standing at the side of the road. The A7 is inaccessible on foot. Remove the Golf and Henrik cannot make the journey. The processes cannot run. The positions have no one to hold them and nowhere to be held.

## Apparent
Seats: the Driver Seat, the Passenger Seat, the Window Seat - left, the Window Seat - right, the Middle Back Seat.

Silver or grey or dark blue - the colour decided years ago and no longer noticed. Four doors. The driver seat on the left, worn on the left bolster. The passenger seat on the right, adjusted to the last person who sat in it. The back seat: three positions, the middle one narrower, the seatbelt buckle in the way. The dashboard ordinary: speed, fuel, warning lights. The cupholder between the front seats. The rear windows: small scratches from hands that have operated them ten thousand times. A child's drawing in the door pocket.

## Yearbook
Built in Wolfsburg. Owned by someone before Henrik. The passenger seat is two centimetres further back than Henrik set it - the last person to sit there was taller. He has not adjusted it back. He does not think about why.

Driven by Henrik from Hamburg to Denmark six years ago, then back south again today. In between: the ordinary journeys that do not get remembered - the supermarket, the airport, the three hours on the A1 in the rain that felt like nothing at the time.

Driven by Thomas with three children in the back seat and Andrea turned forty degrees toward them since before Rendsburg. The child's drawing in the door pocket: his youngest made it two years ago and he cannot bring himself to throw it away.

Driven by Lena and her partner in silence from Schleswig. The coffee cold in the cupholder. Neither of them speaking. The Golf moving south regardless.

Driven by Sabine with the music too loud when Kaltenkirchen came and went at 120 km/h.

The Golf does not know which journey it is on. It is always on one.
