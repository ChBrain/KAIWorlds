# The BMW R1250GS

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
Where its Rider is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the Rider is not on the road in this way. The R1250GS is what makes the Autobahn a physical experience rather than a managed one. Remove it and the Rider is in a car — enclosed, insulated, separated from the road.

## Apparent
Seats: the Driver Seat, the Pillion Seat.

Large for a motorbike - the adventure category, built for both Autobahn and gravel. The boxer engine: cylinders protruding either side, the engine visible, the vibration through the handlebars at idle. The seat high - the Rider above car rooflines, the road visible further ahead than from any car. No roof. No doors. The wind above 100 km/h a physical presence - not noise but pressure. The weather not filtered. The temperature of the road felt through the boots. The lane lines visible directly below. The truck passing at 90 km/h: a wall of turbulence that moves the GS sideways by half a metre before the Rider corrects without thinking.

## Yearbook
GS lineage begins 1980 with the R 80 G/S — the first large dual-sport motorcycle, invented the adventure-touring category. Won Paris-Dakar 1981, 1983, 1984. R 100 GS 1987. R 1100 GS 1994 — first four-valve enduro engine. R 1150 GS 1999. R 1200 GS 2004 — 30kg lighter than predecessor, created the modern adventure-touring market. R 1250 GS introduced 2019 — 1,254cc boxer engine with ShiftCam variable valve timing, 136hp, TFT display, LED headlight standard. One millionth boxer-engined GS produced Berlin-Spandau, June 2023. Replaced by R 1300 GS in 2024.

Built in Berlin since 1969. The most successful motorcycle in BMW Motorrad's lineup. On the road today because someone chose the long way home. Or the only way home. Or because it is the kind of day that requires the road to be felt rather than crossed.
