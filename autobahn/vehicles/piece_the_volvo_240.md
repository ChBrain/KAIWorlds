# The Volvo 240

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
Where its Driver is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the Driver is not on the road in this car. The 240 is what makes the journey possible and what makes it specific - remove it and anyone could be driving anything. The 240 does not announce itself. It is simply there, running.

## Apparent
Seats: [the Driver Seat](piece_the_driver_seat.md), [the Passenger Seat](piece_the_passenger_seat.md), [the Window Seat - left](piece_the_window_seat.md), [the Window Seat - right](piece_the_window_seat.md), [the Middle Back Seat](piece_the_middle_back_seat.md).

Grey. Four doors. Model year 1986. The body angular, rectangular headlights at the front, black bumpers. The steering wheel foam-wrapped. The dashboard flat and padded. The instruments analogue: speedometer, tachometer, fuel gauge. No screens.

The seats: velour. Driver and passenger seat individually adjustable. Rear bench without centre tunnel - rear-wheel drive, flat floor in the back. Three passengers if necessary.

The engine B230F, 2.3 litres, 114 hp. Quiet at cruising speed. The engine note low and steady.

## Yearbook
Built in Gothenburg. Model year 1986. Production run of the series: 1974 to 1993, nearly nineteen years. Over 2.8 million built.

In the GDR the Volvo 240 was the car of the privileged - factory directors, cooperative chairmen, occasionally Stasi. The car meant something before 1989. After 1989 thousands of them appeared on West German used car lots. The price fell. The car stayed the same.

The B230F engine is considered one of the most reliable four-cylinders of its generation. It runs until it doesn't. Usually it runs.
