# The Mercedes E-Class

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
Where its Driver is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the journey is less insulated. The E-Class is what separates the Autobahn from the Driver - the noise, the vibration, the gap between the road's reality and the cabin's version of it. Remove it and the Driver feels more of what the road is actually doing.

## Apparent
Seats: the Driver Seat, the Passenger Seat, the Window Seat - left, the Window Seat - right, the Middle Back Seat.

Large. The cabin quiet at 130 km/h - quieter than it has any right to be. The driver seat with more adjustment options than most drivers use. The MBUX screen: information available but not demanded. The suspension absorbing what the road surface offers. The back seat genuinely usable - wide, the legroom real. The star on the bonnet visible from the driver seat. At 200 km/h on the left lane: effortless. The car does not communicate effort. That is its character.

## Yearbook
E-Class name first used in 1993 for the W124, which had been in production since 1984. Predecessors trace to 1926. Six E-Class generations: W124 1984–1997, W210 1995–2003 (controversial oval headlights), W211 2002–2009, W212 2009–2016 (most successful generation), W213 2016–2023 (drag coefficient 0.23, semi-autonomous driving), W214 2024–present (all-wheel drive standard, mild-hybrid throughout). Built in Bremen and other plants. Europe's taxi of choice for decades — the car equally at home carrying a head of state and a fare from the airport.

On the Autobahn today in the hands of someone who is either very comfortable or performing comfort. The difference is in the grip on the wheel. Relaxed grip: comfortable. White-knuckle grip: performing.
