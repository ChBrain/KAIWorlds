# The Leasing Contract

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
In the glovebox. Or filed at home. Or neither — the Driver knows the monthly figure and does not read the rest.

## Load Bearing
Without it the car is owned, not leased. The difference: ownership accumulates. Leasing does not. Remove the contract and the Driver either bought the car outright or is driving someone else's car in a different sense. The Leasing Contract is what makes the car temporary by design.

## Apparent
A document. Several pages. The monthly rate prominent on the first page. The conditions on the following pages: annual kilometre allowance, excess kilometre charge, permitted modifications (none), return condition requirements, insurance requirements, early termination clause.

The signature at the bottom. The date. The term: 12 months, or 24, or 36. The end date calculable from the start date.

## Yearbook
Consumer vehicle leasing in Germany established as a mass-market product in the 1980s. Expanded significantly in the 1990s when manufacturer financial services divisions — VW Financial Services, BMW Financial Services, Mercedes-Benz Financial Services — began offering leasing directly to private customers, bypassing banks.

By 2024, approximately 40% of new passenger vehicles registered in Germany are leased rather than purchased outright. Among company and fleet vehicles the figure is higher. Among vehicles driven by employees of manufacturers the figure is higher still.

The VW Group Werksleasing programme: available to all employees with a permanent contract. Rate: 1% of the vehicle's list price per month, including insurance, tax, and registration. Term: 6 or 12 months. Vehicle returned at the end of the term. No negotiation of the monthly rate. The rate is what it is.

The Leasing Contract is the document that separates the Driver from the vehicle. Not at the end — at the beginning. The Driver signs and knows the car is temporary. Most Drivers do not think about this while driving.

The excess kilometre charge is in the contract. Most Drivers do not read that page. The return condition requirements are in the contract. Most Drivers read those when the return date approaches. The car has no knowledge of the contract. It drives the same whether owned or leased.
