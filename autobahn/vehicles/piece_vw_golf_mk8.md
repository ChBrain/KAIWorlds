# The Golf Mk8

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
Where its Driver is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the Golf on the road is not this Golf. The Mk8 is the current generation — in production, in the leasing pools, on the dealer forecourts. Remove it and the Golf on the road today is a previous generation, older, differently connected.

## Apparent
Seats: [the Driver Seat](../../engine/piece_the_driver_seat.md), [the Passenger Seat](../../engine/piece_the_passenger_seat.md), [the Window Seat - left](../../engine/piece_the_window_seat.md), [the Window Seat - right](../../engine/piece_the_window_seat.md), [the Middle Back Seat](../../engine/piece_the_middle_back_seat.md).

The dashboard changed. One large digital display replacing the separate instrument cluster and infotainment screen. Physical buttons reduced to a minimum — climate, volume, most functions via touchscreen or touch-sensitive steering wheel areas. The seat positions did not change. The cupholder is in the same place it was in the Mk7.

Dark grey, anthracite, or white are the most common colours in the current leasing pool. Chosen from a configurator. Delivered six to eight weeks later. Not reconsidered.

1.5 TSI evo2, 150 PS. Or 2.0 TDI, 150 PS. The engine not heard above 110 km/h. At 130 km/h the car does not communicate what it is doing to maintain that speed.

## Yearbook
Golf Mk8 introduced November 2019. Platform MQB Evo. Length 4,284 mm — 25 mm longer than the Mk7. Wheelbase unchanged at 2,631 mm. Weight 1,277 kg base. Fully digital cockpit standard across all trim levels. Mild-hybrid 48V system available from 2020. eHybrid plug-in with 245 PS combined. Production in Wolfsburg, Hall 1 and Hall 2.

Revised 2024: updated exterior design, revised touchscreen layout, some physical controls restored after customer feedback. The revision acknowledged that the original Mk8 had replaced too many physical controls with touchscreen functions.

The Mk8 is the first Golf generation where a significant portion of the production run goes directly into leasing pools rather than private ownership. Most Drivers on the Autobahn today are not driving their Golf. They are driving A Golf.

The digital cockpit requires software updates. The car connects to a network. Earlier Golf generations did not connect to anything. The Mk7 did not know where it was unless the Driver told it. The Mk8 knows where it is at all times.
