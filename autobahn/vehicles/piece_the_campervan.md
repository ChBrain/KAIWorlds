# The Campervan

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
Where its Driver is. Slower than it looks like it should be.

## Load Bearing
Without it the journey is a car journey with accommodation at the other end. With it the journey is the accommodation - the bed, the kitchen, the table are all moving at 100 km/h on the Autobahn. Remove it and two people need a hotel. With it they need only a parking place, and the Autobahn has a Raststätte every forty kilometres.

## Apparent
Seats: the Driver Seat, the Passenger Seat, the Caravan Berth.

High. The wind resistance above 110 km/h audible from inside - a pressure rather than a noise. The cab integrated into the living space: the driver seat forward, the living area behind, no partition. The kitchen directly behind the seats. At the Raststätte: the highest vehicle in the car park that is not a truck. The tinted rear windows: a privacy the car does not have. The fold-out bed over the cab: occupied or folded, visible from the outside when occupied, the shape of two people sleeping visible against the tinted glass.

## Yearbook
German motorhome industry roots in Bad Waldsee. Hymer's first motorhome 1961, the Caravano. Hymermobil presented at Caravan Salon 1971 — the first fully integrated recreational vehicle in series production. Dethleffs founded 1931. Knaus 1960. The German motorhome industry today the largest in Europe. Main chassis: Fiat Ducato, Mercedes Sprinter, Volkswagen Crafter. The Fiat Ducato underneath most of them, regardless of the badge on the cab.

Purchased when someone decided the hotel years were over. On the Autobahn today going north or south depending on the season. June: north. August: south. The driver has done this route before. The route is the point — not the destination, the moving. The campervan is the argument that the journey and the destination are the same thing.
