# The BMW 3 Series

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
Where its Driver is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the Driver is not on the road. The 3 Series is the car that wants to be driven - remove it and the Driver who chose it specifically for that quality is in a different car having a different experience of the Autobahn.

## Apparent
Seats: the Driver Seat, the Passenger Seat, the Window Seat - left, the Window Seat - right, the Middle Back Seat.

Compact. The driver seat low and centred, the wheel angled toward the driver rather than upright. The dashboard: the iDrive screen dominant, the instrument cluster behind the wheel. Sport mode available - when engaged the throttle sharpens, the exhaust note changes. The rear-wheel drive felt in corners: the car rotates, the back end alive. The back seat: present but secondary. This is not a family car pretending otherwise. The kidney grille visible from inside only in the way it is not visible - as an absence of wind noise.

## Yearbook
Seven generations since 1975: E21 1975–1983, E30 1982–1994 (first M3 1986, first Touring 1987), E36 1990–2000 (2.7 million units), E46 1997–2006 (first satellite navigation, xenon headlights), E90 2004–2013 (iDrive), F30 2012–2019, G20 2018–present. BMW's best-selling model line, around 30% of annual BMW sales. Built in Munich.

The car that wants to be driven. Rear-wheel drive as default — the handling balance felt immediately. The driver who chose it over the Golf is aware they chose it. That awareness is in the way they sit — slightly forward, slightly engaged. The road is the point. The destination is secondary.
