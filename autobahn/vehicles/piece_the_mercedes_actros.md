# The Mercedes Actros

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
Where its Driver is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the goods do not move. The Actros is not on the Autobahn for the journey - it is on the Autobahn for the cargo. Remove it and the supply chain behind it stops. The right lane empties. The MAN truck knows this. The Actros knows it differently - it is a newer cab, more automated, the driver assist systems more present.

## Apparent
Seats: the Driver Seat, the Passenger Seat - inner, the Passenger Seat - outer.

High. The Mercedes star on the front of the cab. The cab wider than the MAN - the GigaSpace variant, room for the bunk and a second person if needed. The Predictive Powertrain Control active: the transmission shifting based on GPS data about the road ahead, the truck anticipating bridge gradients before they arrive. The driver seat with air suspension. Two passenger seats: GigaSpace and BigSpace variants are 2.5 metres wide with flat floor - no engine tunnel compression. Interior height of 2.13 metres between the seats. The cab a room, not a cockpit. The mirrors: large, the trailer visible in sections. The dashboard: more screens than the MAN. The automation more present, more offering to take over. The Driver deciding how much to let it.

## Yearbook
Mercedes-Benz Actros introduced 1996, replacing the SK series. International Truck of the Year 1997. Three main generations: first 1996–2002, second 2002–2011, third (MP3) 2008–2018, fourth (MP4) 2011–2021, fifth (MP5) 2018–present. The MP4 introduced the GigaSpace cab — 2.5 metres wide, 2.13 metres interior height, flat floor throughout, the cab a room rather than a cockpit. Predictive Powertrain Control introduced MP4: GPS-based transmission shifting that anticipates gradients before they arrive. The MP5 added MirrorCam — digital cameras replacing mirrors, the image on screens inside the A-pillar. Built in Wörth am Rhein, the largest truck plant in the world.

On the Autobahn today with a load that will be somewhere else tomorrow. The driver has been in this cab for nine hours. The automation has been running the whole time. The driver watches it, decides when to override, develops a relationship with a system that has no name for what they have developed.
