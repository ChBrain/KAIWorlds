# The Golf

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
Where its Driver is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the Driver is not on the road in this configuration. Remove the Golf and the most common journey on this road — two people, front seats, destination known — loses its default vehicle.

## Apparent
Seats: the Driver Seat, the Passenger Seat, the Window Seat - left, the Window Seat - right, the Middle Back Seat.

Silver or grey or dark blue - the colour decided years ago and no longer noticed. Four doors. The driver seat on the left, worn on the left bolster. The passenger seat on the right, adjusted to the last person who sat in it. The back seat: three positions, the middle one narrower, the seatbelt buckle in the way. The dashboard ordinary: speed, fuel, warning lights. The cupholder between the front seats. The rear windows: small scratches from hands that have operated them ten thousand times. A child's drawing in the door pocket.

## Yearbook
First production Golf rolled off the line in Wolfsburg in March 1974 — a front-engined, front-wheel-drive replacement for the Beetle. Eight generations followed. Mk1 1974–1983, 6.99 million units. Mk2 1983–1991. Mk3 1991–1997, first TDI diesel and first Golf Variant estate. Mk4 1997–2003, first satellite navigation and xenon headlights, first fully galvanised body. Mk5 2003–2008. Mk6 2008–2012, five-star EuroNCAP, World Car of the Year 2009. Mk7 2012–2019. Mk8 2019–present, fully digital instrument cluster. Over 37 million units sold across all generations — the third best-selling automobile in history.

The driver seat worn on the left bolster on every used example. The passenger seat adjusted to whoever sat there last. The child's drawing in the door pocket on some of them.

The Golf does not know which journey it is on. It is always on one.
