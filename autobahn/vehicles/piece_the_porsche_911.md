# The Porsche 911

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
Where its Driver is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the Driver is not on the road in this way. The 911 is what makes the left lane a statement rather than a lane. Remove it and the driver arrives three minutes late without having driven at 210.

## Apparent
Seats: the Driver Seat, the Passenger Seat.

Low - lower than expected for a car of this reputation. Rear-engined: the weight behind the driver, the nose light, the front lifting at speed. The driver seat tight, bolstered, the wheel small and close. The dashboard: speed dominating, RPM beside it, nothing decorative. The engine behind the driver seat - heard differently from inside than from outside. The sound insulation: at 200 km/h the cabin is quieter than a Golf at 130. The road through the tyres rather than through the noise. Two seats in the front, two vestigial seats behind that nobody sits in. The colour: whatever it is, it is the wrong colour for the driver to be three minutes late.

## Yearbook
First shown Frankfurt 1963 as Type 901 — renamed 911 after Peugeot's objection to three-digit names with a zero. Rear-engined flat-six, air-cooled until 1998. Eight generations: F-Series 1963–1973, G-Series 1974–1989 (first Turbo 1975), 964 1988–1994 (first all-wheel drive), 993 1993–1998 (last air-cooled), 996 1997–2005 (first water-cooled, controversial), 997 2004–2012, 991 2011–2019 (233,540 units), 992 2018–present. Over one million 911s sold — the most successful sports car in the world.

The shape barely changed. The silhouette identifiable from 200 metres across sixty years of production. Built in Stuttgart-Zuffenhausen. The driver who chose it chose it specifically. That choice is in the way they sit — slightly forward, slightly committed. The road is the point. The destination is secondary.
