# The Ford Transit Custom

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
Where its Driver is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the last mile does not close. The Transit Custom is not on the road for the journey - it is on the road for the delivery. Remove it and the cargo stays where it is. The Transit Custom does not want to be driven. It wants to be loaded and unloaded. That is not the same thing as being a car.

## Apparent
Seats: the Driver Seat, the Passenger Seat - left. Two seats in the cab. The cargo area behind: separated by a panel. The panel has a sliding window.

The cab is high. The dashboard functional - no ambient lighting, no leather trim. The instrument cluster analogue. The steering wheel thick, the position upright. A cupholder between the seats. The Passenger Seat retrofitted with a mounting bracket at some point - the bracket still there, slightly bent. The gearstick manual. In sixth gear at 90 km/h the engine note settles and holds. It does not change unless the traffic changes.

Livery: white. Company logo on both doors. Six years old. 187,000 kilometres on the clock. The rear right tyre newer than the others.

## Yearbook
Ford Transit in production since 1965 — one of the longest-running commercial vehicle nameplates in Europe. Transit Custom introduced 2012 as a compact variant of the Transit range, built in Turkey. Second generation 2018–present. Europe's best-selling light commercial vehicle for most of its production history. The most common white van on German roads.

Built in Turkey. On the Autobahn today not for the journey — for the delivery. The cargo area is what matters. The cab is what the driver occupies while the cargo area moves. 187,000 kilometres. The rear right tyre newer than the others.
