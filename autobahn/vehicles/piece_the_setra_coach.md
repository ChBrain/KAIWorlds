# The Setra Coach

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
Where its Driver is. Where its passengers are, without having chosen to be.

## Load Bearing
Without it forty-three people are not on the Autobahn. They have a ticket. They have a seat number. They have a destination. Remove the Setra and all forty-three of them need another arrangement. The bus driver also needs another arrangement, but differently - the bus driver is holding the piece. The passengers are held by it.

## Apparent
Seats: the Driver Seat, the Window Seat, the Middle Back Seat.

High and wide. The passengers above the cars on either side - they can see further, see more, see the Stau forming before the cars can see it. The upper deck if double-decker; single deck if not. The seats: numbered, reclining to a degree, the headrest adjustable. The seat number on a dark grey label at the overhead compartment. Some seats marked with a green sticker: unreserved, free to take. Some reserved - a ticket holds them. On some journeys all seats are free. On others only certain rows. The vehicle knows. The passenger checks. The window: large, the road framed like a screen. The toilet at the back. The luggage below. The driver at the front behind a partition - present but separated. The PA system: used for announcements or not used at all, and the silence of not using it is also a choice.

## Yearbook
Setra name from "selbsttragend" — self-supporting body structure, introduced 1951 by Kässbohrer in Ulm. The S in Setra model names stands for the original company. Daimler acquired Kässbohrer's bus division 1995, merged with EvoBus. Setra now part of Daimler Buses alongside Mercedes-Benz. TopClass 500 series — the flagship coach range, in production since 2012. Built in Neu-Ulm.

On the Autobahn today on a scheduled route. Forty-three passengers who did not choose each other. They chose a ticket and were assigned a proximity. Two hours remain. The Setra holds them together regardless.
