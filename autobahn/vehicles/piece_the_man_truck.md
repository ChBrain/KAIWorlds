# The MAN Truck

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
Where its Driver is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the long-haul driver is without a vehicle. The MAN truck is what makes the right lane permanent, the speed capped at 90, the road visible from above. Remove it and the driver is in a car, anonymous, indistinguishable from the traffic he has been reading for thirty years.

## Apparent
Seats: the Driver Seat, the Passenger Seat - inner, the Passenger Seat - outer.

High - the cab above the rooflines of other vehicles. MAN TGX series: the dashboard a full wall of information, tachograph centre-left, the steering wheel large. The driver seat adjusted over years to one position: the driver's position. Two passenger seats to the right of the driver: beside each other, both forward-facing, both elevated above the road. The engine tunnel beneath the seats varies by cab configuration - in some cabs raised and warm, in others flat floor. The road visible from both seats at a height no car achieves. The bunk behind the cab seats, curtain currently open. The windscreen wide - the road visible at a different angle than from a car, the distances compressing differently. The trailer behind: 18 metres of it, felt in every turn, every lane change, every braking. Not seen from the mirrors completely. Known by its weight.

## Yearbook
MAN origins 1758, truck manufacturing since 1915 in Nuremberg. First diesel truck with direct fuel injection 1924. Turbocharged engines 1951. F90 series with new cab 1986. F2000 1994. TGA Trucknology Generation introduced 2000 — over 250,000 units before replacement. TGX and TGS launched 2007, replacing the TGA: Truck of the Year 2008. Second-generation TGX unveiled 2020 — fully digital instrument cluster, 10cm higher cab, improved aerodynamics. Volkswagen AG majority shareholder since 2011.

The TGX: flagship long-haul. D26 and D38 engines, 430 to 640 HP. TipMatic automated transmission. EfficientCruise, EfficientRoll. The right lane. Always the right lane. 90 km/h. The cars passing on the left have always been passing on the left.
