# The ADAC Truck

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
Where the breakdown is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the Driver on the hard shoulder is stranded. The ADAC truck is what makes the hard shoulder a temporary condition rather than a final one. Remove it and the broken-down car stays broken down. The Autobahn continues past it. Nobody stops.

## Apparent
Seats: the Driver Seat.

Yellow. The yellow visible from 500 metres in the right-hand mirror. The rotating amber light on the roof. The ADAC logo. Pulling in behind the broken-down vehicle: the truck larger than expected when it arrives, the Breakdown Driver already assessing before the door opens. The toolkit in the back: organised by a logic only the Breakdown Driver knows. The tow hook at the rear. The diagnostic laptop. The jump leads. The jerry can for the Driver who ran out of fuel and will not say so directly.

## Yearbook
ADAC — Allgemeiner Deutscher Automobil-Club — founded 1903 in Munich. Roadside assistance service "Gelber Engel" (Yellow Angel) established 1928. Fleet today: around 1,900 breakdown vehicles across Germany, responding to roughly 3.5 million calls per year. The yellow is RAL 1016 sulphur yellow, standardised across the entire fleet. Every vehicle identically equipped. The driver's first question on arrival is always about the vehicle, never the driver.

On the Autobahn today for the fourth time this week. A flat tyre near one exit, a dead battery at a Raststätte, an overheated engine at a bridge. The broken-down driver is always either very calm or very not calm. The ADAC driver has stopped noticing which.
