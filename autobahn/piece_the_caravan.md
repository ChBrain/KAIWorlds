# The Caravan

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
Behind the car that is towing it. Always behind. Never ahead.

## Load Bearing
Without it the family is in a car. With it the car is something else - wider, slower, differently balanced, the braking distance changed, the overtaking near-impossible. Remove the caravan and the Driver who chose it regains a car they have not driven in two weeks and have slightly forgotten how to drive without a trailer.

## Apparent
Seats: the Caravan Berth.

White or cream. Longer than expected when reversing. The coupling between car and caravan: a ball joint, the caravan following the car's line with a delay that is always slightly wrong in tight turns. The windows: closed, the inside dark when moving. The chassis swaying at speed above 100 km/h - felt in the towing car as a pendulum beginning at the back. The Caravan Driver knows the sway. Learned it the first time it started and they did not know yet what to do. The sway is managed by reducing speed. The Caravan Driver reduces speed and the cars behind pile up and cannot pass.

## Yearbook
Stored in a driveway or a storage facility for ten months. Taken out in June or July. The first hour of driving: remembering what the mirrors show, recalibrating the following distance, the braking. By Hamburg it will feel normal again. By next year it will need to be relearned.

On the A7 today: in the right lane at 90 km/h. The caravan swaying at 95 - the Driver knows exactly where the threshold is. The cars behind do not know about the threshold. They see a car going slowly. The caravan is the reason and the reason is not visible from behind.
