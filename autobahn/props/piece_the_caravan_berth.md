# The Caravan Berth

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
Inside the caravan. Along the walls or at the rear dinette. Not at the front - the front is the coupling and the hitch and the darkness.

## Load Bearing
Without it the caravan carries nobody. It is a box being pulled down the Autobahn. With it the box is occupied - someone is inside, being carried, facing sideways or backwards through the journey. Expected: the caravan is stationary. The Autobahn does not check.

## Apparent
A bench seat or a fixed berth along the interior wall. Facing sideways - perpendicular to the direction of travel. Or facing the rear - the road receding rather than arriving. The window beside it: small, level with the road surface rather than above it. The sway of the chassis felt directly - no suspension between the berth and the pendulum. At 90 km/h the caravan rocks slightly on its axis. At 95 km/h the rocking begins to find a rhythm. The occupant learns the rhythm or does not sleep.

## Yearbook
Occupied in June and July. By children who read until they felt sick. By adults who tried to sleep and felt every join in the road surface. By the person who chose to be in the caravan rather than the towing car - for the quiet, for the space, for the ability to lie down. The road is not visible from here in the direction of travel. The Autobahn appears in the rear window as it recedes. Everything on this road has already happened before the caravan berth sees it. In a campervan the same berth moves under its own engine - the driver reachable, the front window visible from the doorway. The road still arrives in the rear window. The berth is the same berth.
