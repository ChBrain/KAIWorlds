# The Mercedes E-Class

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
Where its Driver is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the journey is less insulated. The E-Class is what separates the A7 from the Driver - the noise, the vibration, the gap between the road's reality and the cabin's version of it. Remove it and the Driver feels more of what the road is actually doing.

## Apparent
Seats: the Driver Seat, the Passenger Seat, the Window Seat - left, the Window Seat - right, the Middle Back Seat.

Large. The cabin quiet at 130 km/h - quieter than it has any right to be. The driver seat with more adjustment options than most drivers use. The MBUX screen: information available but not demanded. The suspension absorbing what the road surface offers. The back seat genuinely usable - wide, the legroom real. The star on the bonnet visible from the driver seat. At 200 km/h on the left lane: effortless. The car does not communicate effort. That is its character.

## Yearbook
On the A7 today in the hands of someone who is either very comfortable or performing comfort. The difference is in the grip on the wheel. Relaxed grip: comfortable. White-knuckle grip: performing.

Used as a company car by someone whose company chose it. Or bought privately by someone who waited until they could. Either way the star on the bonnet means something to the person inside, or it means nothing, and both are visible from outside.
