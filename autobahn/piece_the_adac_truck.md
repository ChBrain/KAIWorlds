# The ADAC Truck

## Owner
- Project: Autobahn

## Place
Where the breakdown is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the Driver on the hard shoulder is stranded. The ADAC truck is what makes the hard shoulder a temporary condition rather than a final one. Remove it and the broken-down car stays broken down. The A7 continues past it. Nobody stops.

## Apparent
Seats: the Driver Seat.

Yellow. The yellow visible from 500 metres in the right-hand mirror. The rotating amber light on the roof. The ADAC logo. Pulling in behind the broken-down vehicle: the truck larger than expected when it arrives, the Breakdown Driver already assessing before the door opens. The toolkit in the back: organised by a logic only the Breakdown Driver knows. The tow hook at the rear. The diagnostic laptop. The jump leads. The jerry can for the Driver who ran out of fuel and will not say so directly.

## Yearbook
Dispatched from a depot near Neumünster or Quickborn. On the A7 today for the fourth time. The first three: a flat tyre near Bordesholm, a dead battery at the Raststätte Holmmoor, an overheated engine on the Rendsburg bridge - that one required the police as well, the lane closure, forty minutes of Stau behind it.

The broken-down Driver is always either very calm or very not calm. The ADAC Breakdown Driver has stopped noticing which. The vehicle is the variable. The person is the constant.
