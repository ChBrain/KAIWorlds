# Place: The Park
## The open place. Unfamiliar terrain. The Finder cannot use what they know about the Hider.

## Owner
- **Project:** Easter Egg World

## Shown
- Open ground. Grass, paths, trees, benches.
- Features neither position owns: a bandstand, a pond, a particular oak.
- Other people. Families, dogs, strangers. The world is not private here.
- No clear boundary. The search area must be agreed before [Hiding](process_hiding.md) begins.
- Seasonal texture: mud, dew, early spring growth, wind.
- [The Eggs](piece_the_egg.md), placed but invisible against unfamiliar ground.
- The [Hider](position_hider.md), somewhere in the open. Less able to disappear than in the House.

## Holds
- [The Egg](piece_the_egg.md)
- [The Unfound Egg](piece_the_unfound_egg.md)
- [The Agreement](piece_the_agreement.md)

## Offers
- Neutral ground. Neither position has prior claim.
  The [Finder](position_finder.md) cannot rely on knowing the [Hider](position_hider.md)'s habits here.
  Must read the terrain directly.
- Scale. More space than the Garden or the House.
  The search takes longer. The pressure builds differently.
- Public witness. Other people are present.
  [The Settlement](process_the_settlement.md) may have an audience.
  The gap between expectation and experience
  is observed by strangers who have no stake in it.
- Genuine difficulty. The [Hider](position_hider.md) can use features
  the [Finder](position_finder.md) has never thought about.
  The terrain does not give hints the way the House does.

## Withheld
- The boundary of the search area, if it was not clearly stated before the Signal.
  The Park does not announce its edges.
  A disputed boundary is a dispute [The Agreement](piece_the_agreement.md) must resolve —
  and often did not anticipate.
- The [Unfound Egg](piece_the_unfound_egg.md).
  In the Park, it may never surface.
  The ground keeps what was given to it.
  The [Hider](position_hider.md) knows it is there.
  Nobody else ever will.
