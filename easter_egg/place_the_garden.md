# Place: The Garden
## The original place. Bounded, textured, known by both.

## Owner
- **Project:** Easter Egg World

## Shown
- Grass. Some of it long, some cut short.
- Borders and flower beds. Plants at varying stages of spring growth.
- A path — stone, brick, or worn earth — running through the space.
- Specific features: pots, a shed, a tree, a fence line, a compost heap, a washing line.
- The boundary — hedge, wall, or fence. The edge of the world.
- Morning light. Possibly damp. Possibly cold.
- [The Eggs](piece_the_egg.md), placed but not yet visible.
- The [Hider](position_hider.md), watching from inside or from the boundary.
- The [Finders](position_finder.md), at the threshold. Not yet released.

## Holds
- [The Egg](piece_the_egg.md)
- [The Unfound Egg](piece_the_unfound_egg.md)
- [The Agreement](piece_the_agreement.md)

## Offers
- Terrain with texture. The [Finder](position_finder.md) can read the garden's features
  against their theory of the [Hider](position_hider.md).
- Familiar ground. Both positions have stood here before.
  The [Finder](position_finder.md) knows where things are usually kept.
  The [Hider](position_hider.md) knows the [Finder](position_finder.md) knows this.
- Difficulty through concealment. Long grass, deep pots, the underside of things.
  The terrain participates in the hiding.
- A bounded world. The [Finder](position_finder.md) knows the search has an edge.
  Nothing is hidden beyond the fence.

## Withheld
- Which features the [Hider](position_hider.md) used and which they ignored.
  The garden has more hiding places than were used.
  The [Finder](position_finder.md) cannot tell the difference until [The Settlement](process_the_settlement.md).
- The [Unfound Egg](piece_the_unfound_egg.md), if it exists.
  The garden does not announce it. It simply holds it.
- Whether the hiding was fair.
  The garden is neutral. It conceals equally for all [Finders](position_finder.md).
  The [Agreement](piece_the_agreement.md) is not the garden's concern.
