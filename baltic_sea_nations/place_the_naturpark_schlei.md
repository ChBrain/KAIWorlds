# Place: The Naturpark Schlei
## Location: Schleswig-Holstein, Germany. Shore of the Schlei from Schleimünde to Schleswig.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Nations
**Persona:** The Border

## Shown
- The shore of the Schlei. Both banks, managed as a protected landscape since 2008. 39 municipalities inside the Naturpark boundary.
- The reed beds, the hedgerow landscape, the sandy beaches, the sheltered bays. Breeding and resting habitat for endangered waterbirds. Walking paths, cycling routes, water access points throughout.
- The Naturpark boundary. Present in the signage, the management plans, the conservation surveys, the maps. The boundary runs along the shore.
- The waterline. Where the boundary ends. Not marked. Present in every governance document. The Schlei is the defining feature of the Naturpark. The Schlei is outside the Naturpark's remit.
- The RECOVER network. The Naturpark Schlei is a named project partner. RECOVER is building instruments to measure the water the Naturpark is named after. The Naturpark manages the shore the instruments will be deployed from.

## Holds
- The Naturpark management plan.

## Offers
- The conservation record. Decades of land-side ecological monitoring — bird populations, vegetation, landscape condition. The water condition is not in it.
- The 39 municipalities. Their institutional relationship with the Naturpark since 2008. The network of local engagement the RECOVER project is building on.
- The RECOVER connection. The Naturpark inside the project. The instrument being built to answer the question the Naturpark's own remit cannot ask.

## Withheld
- What the land-side conservation record would show if read against the water-side monitoring data. The eelgrass retreat in the water. The land-use history in the surveys. Together: a picture of the relationship between the shore and the inlet. The comparison has not been made inside any single institution.
