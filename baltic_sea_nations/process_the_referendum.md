# Process: The Referendum
## The vote that drew the line the water did not cross.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Nations
**Persona:** The Border

## Initiates
A region disputed for fifty-six years requires resolution. The Allied Powers establish a commission. Two zones are defined. Each zone votes separately. The outcome of each zone is binding. The moment the zones are fixed the line exists as a possibility. The vote determines only where it runs.

## Direction
Toward the boundary. Zone 1 votes Danish. Zone 2 votes German. The line is drawn between the results. It crosses the Schlei. It crosses the Flensburg Fjord. It runs through communities, farms, fishing grounds, families. The commission draws it where the votes divided. The commission has no instruction regarding the water.

## Lever
The democratic legitimacy. Remove it and the line is imposed — subject to revision, negotiation, revision again. Keep it and the line is the settled outcome of a free vote. Settled outcomes are not reopened. The legitimacy is what made this particular line permanent. Every governance structure that follows from it follows from that permanence, not from the geography.

## Echo
A permanent line across the Flensburg Fjord. The boundary established by the Allied Commission, drawn where the votes of 1920 divided. Every ministry, every mandate, every monitoring programme, every funding application that governs this water since that date inherits the line. The water has been crossing it continuously. The line has not moved.
