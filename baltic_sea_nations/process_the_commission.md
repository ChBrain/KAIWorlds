# Process: The Commission
## The boundary fixed before anyone voted.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Nations
**Persona:** The Border

## Initiates
The war is over. The disputed territory requires resolution. The Allied Powers establish the International Commission for the Schleswig Plebiscites. Its first task is not to run a vote. It is to fix the positions of the possible line — to determine, before the votes are cast, the geometry within which the democratic result will fall.

## Direction
Toward the fixed geometry. The Commission studies the region. It defines two zones. It determines that each zone will vote as a unit — not village by village, not farm by farm, but as blocks large enough to produce a clear administrative result. The blocks are drawn around population distributions, transport corridors, economic dependencies. The water is not a criterion.

## Lever
The zone boundary. Not the line itself — the boundary that determines which votes are counted together and which separately. Draw the zone boundary differently and the democratic result falls differently. The zone boundary was set before any ballot was cast. It was set by people who were trying to be fair. Fairness required a geometry. The geometry required a decision about what the territory was for. The territory was for people. Not for water.

## Echo
Two zones. Each will vote as one. The democratic result will be unambiguous. The line will fall between them with the legitimacy of popular will behind it. The Referendum runs inside this geometry. The Commission's work is done before the first ballot opens. The line it made possible is the line the Referendum made permanent. The water was not consulted at either stage.
