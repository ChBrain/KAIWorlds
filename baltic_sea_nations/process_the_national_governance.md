# Process: The National Governance
## The mandate that runs correctly on one shore.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Nations
**Persona:** The Administrator

## Initiates
The line is permanent. The mandate covers one side of it. The monitoring stations are installed, the targets are set, the budget cycle is open. The process runs continuously — not triggered by events, not waiting for recognition. It runs because the mandate exists and the water is there.

## Direction
Toward the national decision. Data gathered on one shore. Restoration targets set for one shore. Budget allocated for one shore. Report filed for one shore. Each step correct. Each step within mandate. Each step further from the condition of the whole.

## Lever
The mandate. The instrument of governance built for jurisdictions with boundaries. Remove it and the administrator has no authority. Keep it and the authority is precisely shaped for the land — and the land ends at the waterline. What the mandate cannot hold is not in the mandate. It is not a gap in the mandate. It is the shape of the mandate.

## Echo
A correct national decision. Compliant. On time. Filed. On the other side of the line: another correct national decision. Compliant. On time. Filed. The Flensburg Fjord receives both decisions simultaneously. It does not distinguish between them. It continues.
