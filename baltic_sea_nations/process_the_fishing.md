# Process: The Fishing
## The harvest that the Baltic-wide instrument cannot see at inshore scale.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Nations
**Persona:** The Border

## Initiates
The stock is in the water. The EU sets the Total Allowable Catch for Baltic cod, herring, and sprat through the Common Fisheries Policy. Denmark and Germany participate in the process through BALTFISH — the forum of Baltic member states — and the Baltic Sea Advisory Council. The season opens. The vessels leave the harbour on both sides of the line.

## Direction
Toward the catch. The same inshore stock, worked from both shores of the Flensburg Fjord and the Schlei. The Baltic-wide TAC governs the outer sea. The inshore fishery operates within it. The fish that spawn in the sheltered water of the Fjord, that feed on the food web stressed by the nutrient load from both shores, that move between Danish and German coastal waters without registering the border — these fish are inside the EU quota. The EU quota was not built to see them specifically.

## Lever
The scale of the management instrument. The TAC is set for the Baltic as a whole, based on Baltic-wide stock assessments. Remove the scale mismatch and one inshore-specific instrument governs the Fjord's fishery in relation to its specific ecological condition. Keep it and the most precise instrument available for this water is too large to capture what is happening in it.

## Echo
A compliant inshore fishery. Within the Baltic TAC. Nationally reported. The specific condition of the Flensburg Fjord stock — its relationship to the oxygen depletion, the nutrient load, the retreat of the eelgrass nursery habitat — is not in the TAC calculation. RECOVER's network includes fisheries managers from both shores. The inshore-specific assessment has not yet been made.
