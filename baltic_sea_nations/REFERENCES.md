## The 1920 Schleswig Plebiscite

The referendum was conducted in two zones.
Zone 1 (North Schleswig) voted 10 February 1920 — 75% Danish.
Zone 2 (Central Schleswig including Flensburg) voted 14 March 1920 — 80% German.
The border was drawn between the results.
The Allied Commission established the final boundary line on 7 February 1920 —
before the Zone 1 vote, fixing the possible positions of the line in advance.
The line was confirmed internationally and came into effect 15 June 1920.

## The RECOVER Project

management solutions in the Danish-German border Region.
Duration: October 2025 – September 2028.
Budget: €2.3 million.
Lead institution: GEOMAR Helmholtz Centre for Ocean Research Kiel.

## EU Nature Restoration Regulation

Regulation (EU) 2024/1991 of the European Parliament and of the Council
on nature restoration. Entered into force June 2024.
Includes binding targets for restoration of marine habitats including
seagrass beds.

## Baltic Sea Fisheries — EU Common Fisheries Policy

Baltic Sea fisheries are managed under the EU Common Fisheries Policy (CFP)
following dissolution of the International Baltic Sea Fishery Commission in 2007.
Total Allowable Catches are set by EU institutions with input from BALTFISH
(the forum of Baltic EU member states) and the Baltic Sea Advisory Council.
Member states with direct management interest include Denmark and Germany.
https://oceans-and-fisheries.ec.europa.eu/policy/common-fisheries-policy-cfp_en

## The Danish Shore — Natura 2000 and UNESCO Process

The southern shore of Als facing the Flensburg Fjord is designated
as a Natura 2000 protected area under EU habitat law.
Aabenraa Kommune and Sønderborg Kommune administer the Danish shore.

A cross-border UNESCO Biosphere designation process for the Flensburg Fjord
is underway involving Aabenraa Kommune, Sønderborg Kommune, Stadt Flensburg
and Kreis Schleswig-Flensburg. A report completed in early 2026 concluded
the fjord has significant potential for UNESCO status.
Source: Sydnyt.dk, 5 February 2026.

Naturpark Nordals — Danish nature park on the island of Als near the Flensburg Fjord.
Established 2020. 1,928 hectares. Managed by Sønderborg Kommune.
https://www.naturparknordals.dk
