# Process: The Monitoring
## The record that measures the water it cannot see whole.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Nations
**Persona:** The Border

## Initiates
The mandate requires evidence of its own compliance. A monitoring station on each shore. Instruments calibrated. Sampling schedule set. The first measurement taken. The process does not stop after that. It has been running on both sides of the line longer than any current governance structure has existed.

## Direction
Toward the national record. Temperature, salinity, nutrients, oxygen — measured, entered, analysed, reported. Each step precise. The data moves from the water into the record without losing accuracy. It enters a system built for one shore.

## Lever
The data standard. Each monitoring system was built independently to serve the mandate that funded it — different parameters, different sampling depths, different reporting formats, different institutional languages. Remove the difference and the two records join. Keep it and the most precise dataset of this water's condition exists in two systems that have never been read as one.

## Echo
Two national records. Each correct. Each complete within its mandate. The Flensburg Fjord is in both of them, measured from opposite shores, filed under different headings, in different languages, in different institutions. RECOVER's first task is to join them. The task has not yet been completed.
