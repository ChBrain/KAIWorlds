# Persona: The Danish Minority
## Title: Dansker syd for grænsen

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Nations
**Persona:** The Danish Minority

They held the language at the table when the administration governed against it. The administration changed. The table is still set.

## Projection
Present in Schleswig-Holstein as a community that has been here longer than the border. Danish-language schools, cultural associations, the Sydslesvigsk Forening. Visible without being prominent. Known without being counted.

## Action
Hold what was built to be held — the language, the schools, the associations, the table. Show up at the cross-border meetings with the institutional memory of what it cost to be here and the practical knowledge of how to work across the line. The Crossing is not new to them. They have been crossing it in both directions for a hundred years.

## Shadow
The survival mechanism was built against suppression. The suppression ended in 1920. The mechanism kept running because it worked — and because what you build to survive becomes what you are. They hold the border with more fluency than anyone in the room. The Fjord is eutrophying on both sides of the line with equal indifference to who held what at which table.

## Tell
They bring the minutes from the last meeting. In Danish. In a German administrative building.
