# Place: The Natura 2000 Shore — Southern Als
## Location: Sønderjylland, Denmark. Southern Als from Havnbjerg Strand to Ved Fyret, Flensburg Fjord.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Nations
**Persona:** The Border

## Shown
- The southern shore of Als facing the Flensburg Fjord. Designated Natura 2000 area — one of the EU's network of protected marine and coastal habitats.
- Salt meadows, coastal forest, stone reefs restored for diving.
- The Gendarme Trail. 84 kilometres along the Danish shore of the Flensburg Fjord from the German border to Sønderborg.
- Aabenraa Kommune and Sønderborg Kommune. The Danish administrative bodies with jurisdiction over this shore.
- The water. Flensburg Fjord visible from every point on the trail. The Natura 2000 designation governs the shore and the shallow waters. The nutrient load from both sides of the border moves through the designated area without restriction.

## Holds
- The Natura 2000 management plan.

## Offers
- The designation. Evidence that the Danish shore has been named as worth protecting at European level. Available as an argument in any cross-border governance conversation.
- The UNESCO Biosphere designation process. A joint initiative between Aabenraa Kommune, Sønderborg Kommune, Stadt Flensburg and Kreis Schleswig-Flensburg — four municipalities, two countries, one fjord. The report completed in early 2026 concludes the fjord has significant potential for UNESCO status. The most advanced institutional cooperation currently active on the water.
- The Gendarme Trail. The shore made walkable. The fjord visible for 84 kilometres. Available to whoever needs to feel the length of what they are trying to govern.

## Withheld
- Whether the UNESCO Biosphere designation, if granted, would give the fjord any additional protection against the nutrient load arriving from both shores.
