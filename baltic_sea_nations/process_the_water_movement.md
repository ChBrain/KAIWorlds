# Process: The Water Movement
## The distribution that the current runs without instruction.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Nations
**Persona:** The Border

## Initiates
Nutrients are in the water. The current is running. Both conditions are permanent from spring through autumn. The process does not wait for the farming season to end before it begins moving what the farming season produced.

## Direction
Bilateral. What entered the water on the German shore moves toward the Danish shore. What entered on the Danish shore moves toward the German shore. The exchange is continuous. Neither shore receives only what its own fields produced.

## Lever
The current. It has no administrative channel. Remove it and the nutrient load stays local — each shore managing the consequences of its own fields. Keep it and every nationally correct farming decision becomes a cross-border event before the season ends.

## Echo
A distributed nutrient load. The German monitoring station records conditions shaped partly by Danish agriculture. The Danish monitoring station records conditions shaped partly by German agriculture. Neither record says so. Neither mandate requires it to.
