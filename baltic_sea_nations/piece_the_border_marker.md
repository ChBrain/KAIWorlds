# Piece: The Border Marker
## The thing that holds its position while the water moves around it.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Nations
**Persona:** The Border

---

## Place
[The Flensburg Fjord](place_the_flensburg_fjord.md). On the shore.

## Load Bearing
Without it the world has a problem but no line. The border marker is what makes the administrative division legible. Remove it and the governance structures on both sides remain — but the gap between them becomes invisible. The marker makes the gap visible. Visible gaps can be crossed. Invisible gaps cannot.

## Apparent
Stone or metal. Weathered. The year 1920 is on some of them. They stand on the shore where the land is dry enough to hold them. Not in the water. The water is not divided.

## Yearbook
1920: drawn after the referendum. The region voted. The line was placed where the votes divided. Some families ended up on different sides of it.

In the same period, the Sønderjysk Kaffebord was already established in the homes of the border region. The marker held the line. The table held the language. Neither stopped the water.

RECOVER 2025: The marker is still there. The water is still moving. The research is being conducted on both sides of the line. The marker does not appear in any of the research protocols.
