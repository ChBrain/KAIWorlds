# Place: The Schlei
## Location: Schleswig-Holstein, Germany. Baltic mouth at Schleimünde, western end at Schleswig.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Nations
**Persona:** The Border

## Shown
- An inlet. Fifty kilometres long. Narrow at the mouth, widening into broad shallow basins inland. Brackish at the mouth, nearly fresh at the end.
- The reeds. Dense on the shallower banks. Migration corridor for waterbirds. The Naturpark Schlei manages the shore. The Naturpark's jurisdiction ends at the waterline.
- The eelgrass beds. Present in the shallower reaches. Reduced in depth and extent since 1970. The turbidity is higher than it was. The light reaching the bottom is less.
- The monitoring stations on the shore. German institutions. The data goes into the national record.
- The town of Schleswig at the western end. Cathedral. Gottorf Castle on the bank. The city that named the duchy. The duchy no longer exists as an administrative unit. The city is still here.
- The Baltic at the eastern end — the mouth at Schleimünde. [The Flensburg Fjord](place_the_flensburg_fjord.md) is connected through these open waters. What the Schlei carries, the Fjord receives.

## Holds
- [The Combined Dataset](piece_the_combined_dataset.md).
- [The Sediment Record](piece_the_sediment_record.md).

## Offers
- The gradient. Salt to fresh, fifty kilometres. The ecological zones change along it. Each zone responds differently to the nutrient load.
- The eelgrass beds. Their current extent. Their retreat since 1970. Available to whoever is measuring.
- The town of Schleswig. The western end of the inlet. The name of the territory the border divided.

## Withheld
- What the Schlei held before the postwar agricultural intensification. The eelgrass at its full extent. The water clarity before the nutrient load built up. The sediment record holds this. Reading it in the language the monitoring uses requires an instrument that does not yet exist.
