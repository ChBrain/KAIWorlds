# Piece: The Sediment Record
## The baseline that exists before the monitoring began.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Nations
**Persona:** The Border

---

## Place
[The Schlei](place_the_schlei.md). On the floor of the inlet.

## Load Bearing
Without it the monitoring record has no before. Every trend the instruments show is a trend from the moment the instruments were installed — which was after the agricultural intensification had already run for decades. With it the Schlei has a pre-disturbance baseline: what the eelgrass covered, what the water clarity was, what species composition looked like before the nutrient load arrived. The sediment holds this. It has not been asked to speak in the language the monitoring uses. RECOVER is building the instruments that could ask it.

## Apparent
Layers of sediment on the floor of the Schlei. Each layer a year. Pollen, shell fragments, organic matter, diatom assemblages — the biological record of the water above, compressed and held. The deeper the layer, the older the record. The layer from 1950 is there. The layer from 1900 is there. The layer from before the border was drawn is there.

## Yearbook
The Schlei has been accumulating its own record since the last ice age.

The monitoring stations on the shore began recording in the postwar period — after the agricultural intensification that elevated the nutrient load was already underway. The instruments have never measured the Schlei before the problem began.

The sediment has. It holds the pre-disturbance eelgrass distribution in the pollen record. It holds the pre-disturbance species composition in the diatom assemblages. It holds the shift — the layer where the record changes — in the organic matter content and the chemistry.

Nobody has read this record in the language the monitoring uses. The comparison between what the sediment holds and what the instruments record would name the full scale of the change. The full scale has not been named.
