## Place
[The Flensburg Fjord](place_the_flensburg_fjord.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it the Crossing has no instrument. Every conversation about the Fjord's condition is a conversation between two institutions that have each measured half of one system and cannot combine the result. With it the governance gap becomes a technical problem with a known solution. The solution has not yet been implemented.

## Apparent
Two sets of monitoring files. Temperature, salinity, oxygen, nutrients, chlorophyll — measured at stations on the German shore and stations on the Danish shore. The parameters overlap. The formats do not. The institutional languages do not. The data is precise on both sides. Combined it would describe one system. Separated it describes two monitoring programmes.

## Yearbook
Both monitoring programmes predate RECOVER by decades. The German record: nutrient data from the Schlei and the southern shore of the Flensburg Fjord, elevated nitrogen and phosphorus across the full record. The Danish record: parallel measurements from the northern shore, parallel trends, parallel conclusions. Filed separately. In different institutions. In different languages.

October 2025: RECOVER begins. Joining the datasets is among its first operational tasks. The formats are being reconciled. The shared standard is being built. The combined picture does not yet exist as a single file.
