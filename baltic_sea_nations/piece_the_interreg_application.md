# Piece: The Interreg Application
## The cross-border instrument that requires two national submissions.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Nations
**Persona:** Interreg Deutschland-Danmark

---

## Place
[The Flensburg Fjord](place_the_flensburg_fjord.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it RECOVER does not exist. The Interreg funding mechanism is the only instrument that can resource cross-border research because national funding cannot address cross-border problems. The mechanism that crosses the border is built on forms that do not.

## Apparent
A funding application. Two national submissions — one through German administrative channels, one through Danish administrative channels — for one project addressing one shared problem. The project number is shared. The budget is shared. The reporting is parallel. The lead institution is GEOMAR in Kiel. The Danish partner is SDU in Sønderborg. The form requires each to account for its own national share.

## Yearbook
The Interreg Deutschland-Danmark programme exists because the EU recognised that national funding cannot address cross-border problems. The programme is the institutional acknowledgement that the border is an obstacle to governing what it divides.

RECOVER: €2.3 million. October 2025 — September 2028. 21 network partners from industry, administration and civil society across the Danish-German border region. Submitted in two national parts. Funded as one cross-border project.
