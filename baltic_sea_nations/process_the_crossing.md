# Process: The Crossing
## The cooperation that the problem requires and the mandate does not provide.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Nations
**Persona:** The Border

## Initiates
The problem is on both sides. The mandate covers one. The administrator has recognised that governing their half alone is insufficient. The crossing begins before the meeting is scheduled — at the moment the recognition arrives.

## Direction
Toward the joint protocol. The shared dataset. The coordinated recommendation. The governance structure that the mandate does not contain but the problem requires. Direction is not guaranteed. The crossing can fail at any translation point between the two systems.

## Lever
The shared problem. Remove it and the crossing is goodwill without direction — a relationship between neighbours, pleasant, without consequence for the water. Keep it and the crossing has a reason that both sides can name independently. The shared problem is what makes the crossing load-bearing rather than ceremonial.

## Echo
A joint monitoring protocol. Combined datasets. A recommendation that addresses both sides of the line. Whether it gets implemented depends on two separate budget cycles, two separate electoral timelines, two separate definitions of political feasibility. The crossing produced the recommendation. What the recommendation produces is outside the process.
