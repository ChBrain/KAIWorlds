# Baltic Sea Nations

*The Danish-German border region. The water on both sides. 1920–2026.*

---

*For the person responsible for one side of a problem that has two.*

---

## What this world is

The border between Denmark and Germany was drawn in 1920.
The Schlei, the Flensburg Fjord, the Kiel Bight: water that existed
before the border and continues to exist after it,
moving between jurisdictions without asking permission.

The RECOVER project is cross-border by design.
GEOMAR and CAU Kiel on the German side. SDU on the Danish side.
Interreg funding because national funding cannot address
cross-border problems. The research has already crossed the border.
The governance structures that must implement the findings have not.

This world builds the gap between the waterline and the border line
and runs it on whoever has to govern across both.

## The personas

**The Border** — was drawn by people to govern people.
Cannot govern water. Holds its position while the problem moves around it.

**The Danish Minority** — held the language at the table when the
administration governed against it. The administration changed.
The table is still set. The water is still eutrophying.

**The German Minority** — stayed when the border moved north past them.
A century later they are cited as proof that the border can be crossed.
The Fjord has not read the citation.

## The places

**The Flensburg Fjord** — 36 kilometres. The border crosses it halfway.
The water does not change at the crossing.

**The Schlei** — fifty kilometres. Enters the Baltic at Schleimünde,
ends at Schleswig — the city that named the duchy.
The duchy no longer exists. The nutrient load does.

**The Natura 2000 Shore — Southern Als** — the Danish equivalent.
Named as worth protecting at European level. The UNESCO Biosphere
designation process already underway — four municipalities, two countries,
one fjord. The designation is a recognition of value. It is not an instrument
for governing the nutrient load that determines whether that value survives.

**The Naturpark Schlei** — named after the water outside its jurisdiction.
A RECOVER network partner. The instruments will be deployed from its shore.

## The pieces

**The First Joint Measurement** — the number that does not yet exist.
One row. Both shores. One file. It is coming.

**The Sediment Record** — the baseline that exists before the monitoring began.
The Schlei has been recording its own condition since before the border arrived.
Nobody has read it in the language the monitoring uses.

**The Border Marker** — holds its position on the shore
while the water moves around it.

**The Combined Dataset** — the complete picture that exists in two halves.
Never joined.

**The Interreg Application** — the cross-border instrument
that requires two national submissions.

**The Sønderjysk Kaffebord** — the table that held the language
when the administration governed against it.
The Crossing is trying to prove what the table already proved.

**The EU Nature Restoration Regulation** — the European obligation
that arrived after the condition it requires reversing.

## The processes

**The Commission** — fixed the zone geometry before anyone voted.
The line was possible before it was democratic.

**The Referendum** — drew the line the water did not cross. 1920.
Echo still running.

**The National Governance** — the mandate that runs correctly on one shore.

**The Monitoring** — the record that measures the water it cannot see whole.

**The Farming** — the application that feeds the field and the fjord.

**The Water Movement** — the distribution that the current runs without instruction.

**The Fishing** — the harvest that the Baltic-wide instrument cannot see at inshore scale.

**The Crossing** — the cooperation the problem requires and the mandate does not provide.
