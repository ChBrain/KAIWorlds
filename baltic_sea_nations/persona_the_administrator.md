# Role: The Administrator
## The person responsible for one side of a problem that has two.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Nations
**Role:** The Border

## Has
The mandate. For one side. The mandate is real, the authority is real, the accountability is real. It covers everything within the jurisdiction. The jurisdiction ends at the border. The problem does not.

## Orders
Govern the half you can reach. Report on the half you can reach. Be accountable for the half you can reach. The role does not provide authority for the other half. It does not explain why the other half matters to the half you are responsible for. It hands the mandate and says: this is your domain.

## Loses
The whole picture. They were certain that governing their half well was sufficient. The role shows them the half is connected to the other half by water. What happens on the other side arrives in their jurisdiction. What they decide arrives on the other side. The mandate covers the effects of this connection only after they have already occurred.

## Drives
On the loss of the whole picture — builds the relationship across the line that the mandate does not provide. Not because the role requires it. Because the problem does. The Interreg application. The shared monitoring protocol. The joint workshop. None of these are in the mandate.
