# Piece: The EU Nature Restoration Regulation
## The European obligation that arrived after the condition it requires reversing.

## Owner
**Project:** Baltic Sea Nations
**Persona:** The European Union

---

## Place
[The Flensburg Fjord](place_the_flensburg_fjord.md).

## Load Bearing
Without it the restoration of the Flensburg Fjord and the Schlei has no binding European target. With it both member states are legally obligated to improve the ecological condition of their coastal waters by set deadlines. The obligation is real. The condition of the water was shaped by decisions made before the obligation was written. Both member states are now required to reverse a condition their current governance structures were not designed to prevent.

## Apparent
Regulation (EU) 2024/1991. Adopted June 2024. Binding on all EU member states. Restoration targets for marine habitats including seagrass beds, which have retreated in both the Schlei and the Flensburg Fjord. Deadlines set. Compliance schedules required. The regulation is in force. The eelgrass is still retreating.

## Yearbook
The eelgrass in the Schlei and the Flensburg Fjord began retreating decades before the regulation was written. The monitoring documented the retreat. The documentation informed the scientific advice. The scientific advice shaped the regulation. The regulation now requires restoration of what the monitoring recorded disappearing.

June 2024: the regulation enters into force. Both member states begin compliance planning. The planning is national. The eelgrass bed whose restoration is required extends across both sides of the line. The compliance plans are being written separately.
