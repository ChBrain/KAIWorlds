# Place: The Press Conference
## Location: A harbour road. A lectern. Wind.
## The water always visible behind whoever is speaking.

## Owner
- **Project:** Baltic Sea — The Wrong Sea

## Shown
- A lectern on a road near the water.
  Not a podium. Not a stage.
  A lectern on a road.
- Cameras. Microphones pointed at whoever is speaking.
- The water behind them.
  Always visible. Always in frame.
  Never the subject of the shot.
- The people who came to listen.
  Some of them have questions.
  Most of the questions
  are the same question
  asked in different forms.
- The spokesperson's notes.
  Accurate. Prepared.
  Insufficient for the room.
- The road back to the car.
  Visible from where everyone is standing.
  Nobody moves toward it
  until the conference ends.

## Holds
The form.
The press conference is the container
the mandate provides
for a situation the mandate
cannot contain.
It holds what can be said accurately.
It does not hold
what the people came to hear.
The gap between those two things
is present in every pause.

## Does
Makes whoever stands at the lectern
aware that the words are correct
and not enough.
The data is accurate.
The condition is named precisely.
The form is followed.
The room is not satisfied.
Not because the words are wrong.
Because the room came
for something the words cannot give —
the sense that the weight of authority
is adequate to what is happening
in the water behind the speaker.
The press conference
cannot produce that sense.
It produces instead
the visible effort to produce it.
Which is not nothing.
Which is also not what was needed.

## Withheld
Whether returning accomplishes anything.
The press conference does not answer this.
It requires the return
without answering it.
The person at the lectern
holds both
without the place offering resolution.
