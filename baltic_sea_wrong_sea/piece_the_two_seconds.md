# Piece: The Two Seconds
## The tail. Once. Against everything.

## Owner
- **Project:** Baltic Sea — The Wrong Sea

## Place
The Sandbank. Poel.
April 9th, 2026.
Morning.

## Load Bearing
Without it the Witnessing has no pivot.
The animal is simply dying —
present, breathing at intervals,
sinking into the seabed.
With it: the animal moved.
Not enough to change anything.
Exactly enough to change everything
about what the Witnessing means.
Remove it: the shore is a vigil.
With it: the shore is something harder to name —
the place where the difference between
*trying*
and
*changing the outcome*
becomes visible
and stays visible
and does not resolve.

## Apparent
The tail beat.
One or two seconds.
Witnessed by the ministry spokesperson
who reported it neutrally:
*He moved slightly.
He beat his tail for one or two seconds.
But that changes nothing about the situation.
He is still stuck.*
The spokesperson was correct.
The spokesperson was also
describing something
the words do not fully hold.

## Yearbook
Once.
April 9th.
Morning.
Not repeated.
Not sufficient.
Not nothing.
