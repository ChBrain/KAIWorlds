# Piece: The Wrong Sea
## Not a metaphor. The actual water.

## Owner
- **Project:** Baltic Sea — The Wrong Sea

## Place
The Baltic.
All of it.
The Kiel Bight.
Wismar Bay.
The Kirchsee off Poel.
The straits between
here and the North Sea.
The 500 kilometres
the animal did not cover.

## Load Bearing
Without it nothing in this world
has its pressure.
The wrong sea is not the tragedy.
It is the condition.
The tragedy runs inside the condition.
Remove it: the animal is stranded
somewhere it was built for —
which happens,
which is sad,
which is not this world.
With it: every piece,
every position,
every process
runs inside the specific pressure
of a creature
in a place
that cannot sustain it —
not because the place is hostile
but because the place
is simply
not the right one.
The wrong sea does not want the animal dead.
It is indifferent.
That is worse.

## Apparent
One third less salt than the Atlantic.
No tides.
Average depth: 25 metres in the Kiel Bight.
Shallower toward the coast.
Shallower toward Poel.
The skin requires more salt
than the water contains.
The skin fails.
The sea continues.

## Yearbook
Here before the animal arrived.
Here after it leaves.
The wrong sea for this animal.
The right sea for the harbour porpoise,
the sprat, the herring,
the organisms
that have been here longer
than any of the decisions
about what to do with them.
The Baltic is not wrong.
The animal is not wrong.
The combination is wrong.
The combination keeps happening.
The sea does not change.
The animals keep coming.
