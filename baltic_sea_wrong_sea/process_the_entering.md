# Process: The Entering
## The mechanism that puts the animal in the wrong place.

## Owner
- **Project:** Baltic Sea — The Wrong Sea

## Initiated by
A large whale moves through the Danish straits
into the Baltic Sea.
The Baltic has been navigated by large whales before.
The record shows it.
It will happen again.
The net was already in the animal's mouth
before the straits were crossed —
or it was not,
and something else brought it here:
a shoal of herring that moved,
a migration route that shifted,
noise that redirected,
curiosity that did not calculate the cost.
The reason is not confirmed.
The initiation is.

## Direction
Into shallower water.
The animal is moving.
The sea is not getting deeper.

## Lever
The animal's body pressing against
a body of water it was not built for.
One third less salt than the Atlantic.
No tides to assist.
Sandbanks at depths that should be safe.
Fishing nets already in the water,
already in the record,
already the common condition
of 300,000 animals per year.
The carrying force: an animal that has survived
everything the Atlantic asked of it.
The resistance: a sea that asks something different.

## Echo
The animal is in the Baltic.
It is already weakened by what it entered with,
or by what the water is doing to its skin,
or both.
The Stranding can now begin.
