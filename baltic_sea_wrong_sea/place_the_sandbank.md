# Place: The Sandbank
## Location: The western Baltic coast — Timmendorfer Strand,
## Wismar Bay, the island of Poel. Wherever the water is wrong.

## Owner
- **Project:** Baltic Sea — The Wrong Sea

## Shown
- Shallow water. Paler than it should be this far out.
- Sand that holds weight it was not designed to hold.
- The animal. Partially visible above the surface.
- Sprinklers running. The firefighters keeping the skin wet.
- The exclusion zone line. Tape or boats or distance.
  It moves. The radius holds.
- The equipment on the shore: the excavator that came
  from Lower Saxony. The rubber dinghies.
  The drones above.
- The crowd behind the line.
  Phones raised. Not leaving.
- The seagulls on the animal's back.
  Present from the fourth day.
  Nobody mentions them after the first report.

## Holds
The animal.
Not by force.
By the specific combination of
wrong depth,
wrong salinity,
no tides —
and twelve tonnes of animal
that has already tried
three times
and does not have
a fourth try left in it.

## Does
Makes everyone who approaches it
aware that they are approaching something
that cannot be approached usefully.
The sandbank does not prevent approach.
It makes every approach
a question about what approach is for.
The expert with the plan
arrives at the exclusion zone
and the sandbank asks:
*what is the plan for?*
The minister arrives at the lectern
and the sandbank asks:
*what is the mandate for?*
The person who drove three hours
arrives at the tape
and the sandbank asks:
*what did you come for?*

## Withheld
Whether the plan would have worked.
What the animal was navigating toward
the three times it freed itself.
What it was navigating toward
when it returned.
The sandbank holds all of this
without distinguishing between them.
It will hold them after the animal is gone.
It held them before the animal arrived.
