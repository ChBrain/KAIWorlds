# Process: The Stranding
## The mechanism that holds the animal in place.

## Owner
- **Project:** Baltic Sea — The Wrong Sea

## Initiated by
The animal is in the Baltic.
It is weakened.
It is in shallow coastal water.
A sandbank is present.
This is not an unusual combination
in the western Baltic in March.
It has happened before.
It will happen again.

## Direction
Toward the seabed.
The animal's weight — twelve tonnes —
pressing into sand
that was not designed to hold it
and cannot release it
without assistance it cannot ask for.

## Lever
Twelve tonnes pressing into a sandbank
in water too shallow to lift it.
No tides.
Skin already tearing from wrong salinity.
Net remnants in the mouth
preventing eating,
possibly preventing navigation.
The carrying force: an animal that freed itself
three times.
Three times it found the depth.
Three times it returned to the shallows.
The resistance: a body of water
that offers no reliable path
to the 500 kilometres of
narrow straits, shipping lanes,
and wrong-depth passages
that stand between here
and the North Sea,
and then the Atlantic,
and then home.

## Echo
The animal is in the seabed.
Fifty to sixty centimetres into the sand
under its own weight.
It is alive.
It is singing.
The Witnessing can now begin.
