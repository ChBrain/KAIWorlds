## The Wrong Sea — Scientific Context

**Baltic Sea salinity**
Average salinity: 7–8 PSU in open water; 10–15 PSU near straits.
North Sea and Atlantic: 30–35 PSU.
Sperm whale skin requires salinities at or above North Sea levels.
At Baltic salinities, osmoregulatory stress begins within days.

**Baltic depth profile**
Average depth: 55 metres (full basin). Kiel Bight: 10–25 metres.
Sperm whales are deep-water animals; typical dive depth 300–1000 metres.
The Kiel Bight offers no functional diving depth.

**The channel**
From Poel island to the western approach of the Fehmarn Belt: approximately 80–100 km.
From the Fehmarn Belt to Skagerrak: approximately 400 km of progressively saltier water.
The Great Belt and the Sound are the primary exits.
Both involve shipping lanes, shallow passages, and acoustic interference.
