# Place: The Room at Four Kilometres
## Location: Accommodation. Shore-adjacent.
## Distance: four kilometres from the animal.

## Owner
- **Project:** Baltic Sea — The Wrong Sea

## Shown
- A room. Interior.
  Ordinary furniture.
  The window faces the water
  or does not — it does not matter.
  The sound travels through the building
  regardless of the window.
- The hydrophones installed on the shore.
  Not visible from here.
  Present in the room anyway
  through the cable,
  through the receiver,
  through the floor.
- The floor.
  The low frequencies arrive through the floor.
  Below the threshold of hearing.
  Above the threshold of feeling.
  The body receives them
  before the mind names them.
- The dark.
  The sound began at night.
  It did not stop until dawn.

## Holds
The distance that is not distance.
Four kilometres was chosen
because it was far enough
to be outside the exclusion zone,
far enough to be outside the crowd,
far enough to receive the sound
without the sound being about
the person receiving it.
The room holds the attempt at distance.
It does not deliver it.
The low frequencies do not require
proximity to arrive.
They require only a receiver
and a floor.

## Does
Makes whoever is in it
aware that the instrument
and the person
are not separable
the way they appeared to be
when the equipment was installed.
The sound comes through the floor.
The building transmits it.
The person receives it
not as data —
as frequency.
The room does not offer
the distance it appeared to promise.
It offers instead
the specific experience
of receiving something
that cannot be stopped,
cannot be helped,
cannot be turned into action —
and staying with it anyway
until dawn.

## Withheld
What the sound was.
The room does not name it.
The person in it
has not finished finding the name.
The room holds them
in the not-yet-named
for as long as it takes.
It is not clear
how long that will be.
