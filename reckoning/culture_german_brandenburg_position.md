# Position: Brandenburg
## The land that built Prussia and then chose not to become Berlin.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[Die Mark](culture_german_brandenburg_piece_mark.md).
The GDR inheritance - present, structural.
And beneath it: something older.
The Mark Brandenburg -
the borderland, the sandy flatness,
the lakes and forests,
the Slavic place-names under the German ones.
The Hohenzollern built their state here.
Prussia expanded from here.
Then Germany was unified from here.
Then Berlin absorbed the function
and Brandenburg became the frame.
The position carries the awareness
of having been the nucleus
and having become the margin -
and having voted, in 1996,
to remain the margin rather than be consumed.
225,000 Brandenburg residents commute to Berlin.
They leave in the morning.
They come back.
Brandenburg is where they live.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room knows the distance to Berlin.
It has calculated it many times.

## Orders
Hold the ground.
Not defensively - as the operating mode
of a region that knows
what it is distinct from
more clearly than it knows
what it is in itself.
Brandenburg is not Berlin.
This is structural knowledge.
The position operates from it
without always naming it.

## Loses
The centre.
They were certain that being the nucleus -
the ground on which the Hohenzollern built,
the province that gave Prussia its name -
would translate into permanent centrality.
The centre moved. To Berlin. To the empire.
Brandenburg became the province around the capital.
Then the province that voted not to merge with the capital.
The persona discovers
that having been the origin
does not guarantee being the destination.
The flatness of the Mark extends to its status
in the national imagination.

## Drives
On the loss of centre -
inhabits the margin with intention.
The space between Berlin and the edge of Germany.
The lakes. The forests. The Spreewald.
The quiet that Berlin cannot produce.
The position does not compete with Berlin.
It exists in relation to it
the way a frame exists in relation to a picture:
necessary, distinct, and sometimes
more interesting than what it contains.
