## Shown
- The Zeche Zollverein. Katernberg district.
  The headframe visible from the motorway.
  12 stories. Bauhaus design. 1932.
  Coal mined here until 1986.
  UNESCO World Heritage since 2001.
  Now: museum, design school, event space, skating rink.
  The machinery is preserved inside.
  The purpose has changed.
  The structure remains.
- The Villa Hügel. The Krupp family estate.
  269 rooms. Built 1873.
  The family that built an empire on Ruhr steel
  and supplied the Kaiser and the Wehrmacht.
  Open for exhibitions. Entrance fee.
  The history is in the building.
  The building is open.
- The Ruhr Museum. Inside the Zollverein.
  The history of the region from 300 million years ago.
  The coal seams that formed.
  The industry that extracted them.
  The transition that followed.
  All in one building that is a former coal washing plant.
- The Stadtgarten. The city park.
  Built on filled-in mining subsidence.
  The ground beneath it moved.
  The park was built anyway.
- The unemployment office. Innenstadt.
  Unremarkable building.
  Among the busiest in North Rhine-Westphalia
  through the 1980s and 1990s.
  Less busy now than it was.
  Not empty.
