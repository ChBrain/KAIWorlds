# Piece: The Hill at Ettersberg
## Goethe walked here. Buchenwald was built here. The hill did not choose between them.

## Owner
- **Place:** Weimar

## Place
Weimar. The Ettersberg.
Eight kilometres north of the city.
478 metres above sea level.
Goethe walked this hill regularly.
He called it one of the most beautiful places in Thuringia.
The concentration camp was built on the same hill in 1937.
The view from the camp toward Weimar: twelve minutes by car.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Thuringian position loses its defining pressure.
Thuringia is the state of Goethe and Schiller,
of Weimar Classicism, of the Bauhaus,
of the first German democracy named after its city.
It is also the state of Buchenwald,
built eight kilometres from Goethe's walking path.
The position cannot hold one without the other.
The hill is load-bearing precisely because
it does not resolve the contradiction.
The Goethe path and the camp road
run on the same hill.
The Thuringian position carries both.

## Apparent
The Buchenwald Memorial. Open every day.
Free entry.
56,000 prisoners passed through.
43,000 died.
The quarry where they worked:
one kilometre from the main gate.
Goethe's oak: the legend says
a tree on the hill that Goethe loved
was left standing when the camp was built.
The prisoners named it Goethe's oak.
When Allied bombing destroyed part of the camp in 1944,
the oak burned.
The stump was preserved as a memorial within the memorial.
The Weimar National Theatre:
Goethe directed it for 26 years.
Ten minutes from the main square.
The Goethe-Schiller monument outside it:
the most photographed statue in Thuringia.
Both men buried together
in the Fürstengruft, the ducal vault.
Two kilometres from the Theaterplatz.

## Yearbook
1775: Goethe arrives in Weimar.
He stays for the rest of his life.
1794: Goethe and Schiller meet.
Their friendship defines a cultural era.
Weimar Classicism.
1919: the Weimar Republic founded.
Germany's first democracy named after this city.
The Bauhaus founded here the same year.
1933: the Weimar Republic ends.
The Thuringian Nazi party had been the first
to enter a German state government, in 1930.
The ground was prepared here.
1937: Buchenwald opens.
On the Ettersberg.
On Goethe's hill.
1945: American troops liberate Buchenwald.
General Patton orders the citizens of Weimar
to walk through the camp.
They walk through.
What they knew beforehand
is disputed and has been disputed since.
1949: Thuringia becomes part of East Germany.
The GDR promotes the classical heritage.
Goethe and Schiller as cultural assets.
Buchenwald as antifascist memorial.
Both instrumentalised.
Both real.
1990: reunification. Thuringia reconstituted as a state.
The hill at Ettersberg is still there.
Both paths still run on it.
