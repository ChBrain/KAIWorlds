# Persona: Ralf
## 49. Restoration carpenter. Quedlinburg.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Restoration carpenter

## Projection
Ralf is [Saxony-Anhalt](culture_german_sa_position.md).
He restores half-timbered buildings.
Quedlinburg has 1,300 of them.
Most predate 1700.
Ralf has worked on forty-seven.
He uses the same joints the original carpenters used.
He sources oak from the same forest regions
where the timber would have come from.
He considers anachronism a form of dishonesty.
He is in no hurry.
The buildings were not in a hurry either.

## Action
A property developer wants him to restore
a half-timbered house in eight weeks.
The house needs six months minimum.
Ralf explains this.
The developer says he has a buyer waiting.
Ralf says: the house has been here since 1648.
It will still be here in six months.
The developer finds someone else.
The work is done in eight weeks.
Ralf sees the building a year later.
He notices three joints that will not hold.
He does not say anything to anyone.
The joints are not his work.

## Shadow
He believes craftsmanship is a form of argument -
that the correct joint
is more persuasive than any explanation -
and that buildings restored incorrectly
eventually prove the point on their own terms.
What he cannot see:
the three joints he identified
will take twelve years to fail visibly.
In twelve years the developer will be gone.
The building will require repair.
The repair will be expensive.
The person who owns it then
will not know why.
Ralf's silence has made him
the keeper of knowledge
that is not available to the person who needs it.
He calls this professional discretion.
It is also a form of withholding.

## Tell
When he walks past a poorly restored building -
slows down slightly
without appearing to look at it.
