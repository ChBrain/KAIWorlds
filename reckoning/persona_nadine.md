# Persona: Nadine
## 37. Rights editor. Frankfurt.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Rights editor

## Projection
Nadine is [Hesse](culture_german_hesse_position.md).
She sells foreign rights for a mid-sized Frankfurt publisher.
She works the Frankfurt Book Fair every October.
The rest of the year she works the same deals
over email and video calls.
The fair compresses into six days
what takes twelve months to build.
She is good at both rhythms.
She is better at the fair.
She does not know whether this is about her
or about what happens when rooms fill
with people who all want the same thing:
something worth carrying across a border.

## Action
A Scandinavian agent offers her a manuscript
at a price above what her publisher will pay.
She reads it overnight.
She believes it is worth more than the offer.
She also knows her publisher will not pay it.
She calls the agent in the morning.
She tells him she cannot match the price
and explains why the book is worth what he's asking.
She recommends two other publishers
who she believes could.
The agent is surprised.
He thanks her.
The book goes elsewhere.
Six months later he sends her the next manuscript first.
The price is lower.

## Shadow
She believes that the book fair works
because everyone operates on reputation -
that the deal you decline well
is worth more than the deal you close badly.
What she cannot see:
she has been building her reputation
for eleven years.
She is trusted and effective.
She has also never fought internally
for a price her publisher wouldn't pay.
She accommodates the budget.
She blames the budget.
The books she let go
were sometimes books she should have fought for.
She does not know which ones.
She has made peace with not knowing.
The peace is not entirely earned.

## Tell
When she encounters a manuscript she cannot buy -
reads it to the end anyway.
