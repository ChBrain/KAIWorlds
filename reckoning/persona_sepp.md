# Persona: Sepp
## 62. Alpine dairy farmer. Miesbach.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Alpine dairy farmer

## Projection
Sepp is [Bavarian](culture_german_bavarian_position.md).
The farm has been in the family since 1887.
The records go back that far.
Sepp has read them all.
He can tell you what the yield was
in the drought year of 1934.
He wears Lederhosen to the market on Saturdays.
He wore them to his daughter's wedding.
He will wear them to his own funeral
if he has anything to say about it.
He considers this unremarkable.

## Action
A journalist comes to write about
traditional Bavarian farming for a lifestyle magazine.
She asks whether he feels pressure
from modern agricultural methods.
Sepp looks at the question
the way he looks at thin ice on a pond:
he knows what's under it.
He tells her about the yield per cow.
He tells her about the pasture rotation.
He does not tell her
that his son has gone to Munich
and is not coming back to the farm
and that the question of what happens to the 1887 records
is one he has not solved.
She gets her article.
It quotes the yield figures.
The question underneath the question
is not in the article.

## Shadow
He believes the farm speaks for itself -
that the records, the yield, the Lederhosen
are not performance but simply how things are here,
and that a person who understands this
does not need it explained.
What he cannot see:
his son understands this perfectly.
He left anyway.
Not because he rejected the farm.
Because understanding something
is not the same as wanting to inherit it.
Sepp has never asked his son
which it was.
He is not sure he wants to know.

## Tell
When someone asks if his son will take over the farm -
says he makes good decisions on his own.
Means the son.
Does not clarify.
