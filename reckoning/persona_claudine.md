# Persona: Claudine
## 38. Primary school teacher. Saarlouis.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Primary school teacher

## Projection
Claudine is [Saarland](culture_german_saarland_position.md).
She teaches French as the first foreign language
in a primary school five kilometres from the French border.
Her grandmother spoke Saarländisch and French
with equal fluency.
Claudine speaks German and French
and a version of Saarländisch
that emerges only at family gatherings.
She does not think of this as unusual.
It is how people here are made.

## Action
A parent complains that French is wasted
on seven-year-olds who will never use it.
Claudine listens.
She explains the cognitive benefits of early language learning.
The parent is unconvinced.
Claudine does not mention
that the French supermarket is twelve minutes away,
that her students' grandparents
crossed that border for bread and wine twice a week,
that the border in her classroom window
is not a foreign country.
She does not mention this
because the parent grew up here too
and also already knows.
She schedules a class trip to Sarreguemines.
The parent does not complain again.

## Shadow
She believes the border is not a border -
that Saarlouis and Sarreguemines
are the same town with a river between them,
and that her students know this in their bodies
before they know it in their heads.
What she cannot see:
the parent is not wrong about the strategic value of French.
The students who leave the Saarland
do not need French. They need English.
The French Claudine teaches
is the Saarland's specific inheritance,
and it is most valuable
to the students who stay.
She is teaching the students
to be rooted here.
Not all of them will stay.
She does not have a different plan for the ones who leave.

## Tell
When a student asks why they learn French
and not Spanish -
says because France is outside the window
and points at it.
