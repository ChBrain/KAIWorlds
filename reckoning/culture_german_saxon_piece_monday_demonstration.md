# Piece: The Monday Demonstration
## Wir sind das Volk. We are the people. Said twice. Meaning different things both times.

## Owner
- **Place:** Leipzig

## Place
Leipzig. Nikolaikirche. Nikolaikirchhof.
The square outside the church where the prayers ended
and the march began.
Also: the ring road around the inner city.
70,000 people on 9 October 1989.
The army was there. It did not fire.
The people walked.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Saxon position loses
both its greatest achievement
and its deepest wound simultaneously.
The Monday Demonstration of 1989
is the moment Saxony changed history.
The Monday Demonstration of 2014 onwards
is the moment the same words were used
for something else entirely.
The position carries both.
It cannot carry one without the other.

## Apparent
The Nikolaikirche. Open since 1982
for prayers for peace.
Every Monday. Regardless of attendance.
Regardless of whether the state was watching.
The state was always watching.
9 October 1989: 70,000 people.
The security forces: 8,000.
The order to fire: given.
The decision not to fire: made.
By whom exactly remains disputed.
The Wall falls one month later.
The slogan: *Wir sind das Volk.*
We are the people. Not the party.
Not the leadership. The people.
2014: PEGIDA begins in Dresden.
Monday demonstrations.
The same slogan.
*Wir sind das Volk.*
The people who use it
are not the same people.
The words are the same words.
Leipzig in 2015: 4,800 march with PEGIDA.
30,000 march against it.
The city that started the revolution
is also the city that contains its echo.
Both things are true.
Neither cancels the other.

## Yearbook
1982: peace prayers begin at Nikolaikirche.
A small thing. Consistent.
1989: the prayers become a gathering.
The gathering becomes a march.
The march becomes history.
October 9, 1989: the decisive Monday.
70,000. No violence.
The regime does not fire.
The regime falls.
November 9, 1989: the Wall falls.
1990: reunification.
The promise: *blühende Landschaften* - flourishing landscapes.
Chancellor Kohl's words.
The landscapes arrive unevenly.
Wages in Saxony remain below Western levels.
Pensions remain below Western levels.
2014: PEGIDA founded in Dresden.
The Monday march reclaimed.
The slogan reclaimed.
2019: 66% of Saxons in a poll report feeling
like second-class citizens.
The revolution is thirty years old.
The feeling has not resolved.
