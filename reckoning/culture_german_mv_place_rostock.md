## Location: Mecklenburg-Vorpommern, Germany

## Shown
- The Kröpeliner Strasse. The pedestrian zone.
  The university at one end. Founded 1419.
  One of the oldest in Europe.
  Students in the cafés.
  The city feels younger here
  than most of the state.
- The Warnemünde beach. North of the city.
  White sand. The lighthouse.
  In July: crowded.
  The same beach in October: empty,
  wind off the Baltic,
  a few dog walkers.
  The seasonal contrast is the state in miniature.
- The Petrikirche. The oldest church in Rostock.
  The viewing platform at 117 metres.
  From up here: the port, the Warnow river,
  the city, the Baltic beyond.
  The largest port in the state.
  The ferry to Denmark, to Sweden.
  The world arrives here.
  Much of the state empties toward it.
- The Neptun shipyard. Still operating.
  Specialised in naval and offshore vessels
  after the container market went to Korea.
  The adaptation was painful and partial.
  It is still here.
- The Rostock-Lichtenhagen housing estate.
  1992: pogrom against Vietnamese residents.
  The worst racist attack in Germany
  since the Second World War.
  The building is still there.
  The sunflower planted there in 2012
  by victims and witnesses.
  The city does not always foreground this.
  It is there.
