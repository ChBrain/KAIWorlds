# Persona: Bernhard
## 63. Retired philosophy professor. Weimar.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Retired philosophy professor

## Projection
Bernhard is [Thuringian](culture_german_thuringian_position.md).
He taught at the Bauhaus University for thirty years.
Aesthetics and ethics.
He has written four books.
The fourth is about the proximity
of Weimar Classicism and Buchenwald.
He began it in 2015.
He has not finished it.
He is not sure he can finish it.
He walks to the Goethe monument every morning.
He has walked to it four thousand times.
He has not visited the memorial on the Ettersberg
since his mandatory school visit in 1978.

## Action
A graduate student asks him
to supervise a dissertation
on the ethics of proximity -
specifically, what it means
that the German Enlightenment's centre
and the German genocide's instruments
occupied the same eight kilometres.
Bernhard agrees.
He gives the student his outline for the fourth book.
He tells her to take what is useful.
He meets with her every three weeks.
He pushes her thinking on each section.
The dissertation is excellent.
He recommends it for publication.
He does not return to his own manuscript.

## Shadow
He believes the question is the most important
an ethicist in this place can ask -
that the proximity is the defining fact
of German intellectual culture
and that it has not been adequately answered.
What he cannot see:
he has been asking the question for forty-eight years.
He has given it to a graduate student to answer
because he cannot answer it himself.
He knows why he cannot answer it.
He has not walked to Ettersberg since 1978.
The eight kilometres
is the distance between asking the question
and inhabiting the ground it stands on.
He stands at one end.
He has supervised three dissertations
from the same position.

## Tell
When asked how the fourth book is going -
asks about the student's progress instead.
