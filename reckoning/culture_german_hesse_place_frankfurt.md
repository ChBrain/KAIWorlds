## Shown
- The Römerberg. The old town square.
  The medieval city hall.
  Reconstructed after WWII.
  Tourists photograph it.
  Frankfurt was 75% destroyed in the war.
  The reconstruction was pragmatic.
  The skyline that replaced what burned:
  the Bankenviertel, the towers.
  Frankfurt chose the future.
- The Zeil. The shopping street.
  Among the highest sales per square metre in Europe.
  The money that flows through Frankfurt
  is spent here by people who live here.
  The money is invisible.
  The Zeil is not.
- The Museumsufer. The museum embankment.
  Fifteen museums along the south bank of the Main.
  The Städel. The architecture museum.
  The film museum. The communications museum.
  Frankfurt's cultural infrastructure
  is disproportionate to its size
  because the city has money
  and spends some of it on culture.
- The Paulskirche. St Paul's Church.
  1848: the first freely elected German parliament met here.
  The German democratic tradition is sometimes dated
  from this building.
  Now a ceremony hall.
  The Peace Prize of the German Book Trade
  awarded here every October.
  During the Book Fair.
- The Main. The river.
  The skyline reflects in it.
  People jog along it.
  In summer: beach bars on the banks.
  The river is the only part of Frankfurt
  that everyone agrees is beautiful.
