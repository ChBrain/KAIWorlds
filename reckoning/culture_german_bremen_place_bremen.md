## Shown
- The Marktplatz. The Roland at its centre.
  The Town Hall beside him.
  Weser Renaissance facade.
  Both UNESCO World Heritage.
  The square has been the city's centre
  since Bremen was a city.
- The Schütting. The merchants' guildhall.
  Opposite the Town Hall.
  The merchants and the senate
  facing each other across the square.
  This arrangement is not accidental.
- The Schnoor. The medieval quarter.
  Half-timbered houses.
  The narrowest streets in the city.
  Preserved through the war
  because the bombing missed it.
  The bombing did not miss the port.
- The port. Second largest in Germany after Hamburg.
  Container ships on the Weser.
  Bremerhaven at the river's mouth,
  sixty kilometres away,
  technically part of the same state.
  The state is two cities
  that cannot see each other
  but govern together.
- The unemployment office. Bremerhaven.
  Among the highest unemployment rates
  in Germany for thirty years.
  The port that was the gateway
  for German emigrants to America
  has fewer ships now.
  The Roland cannot see Bremerhaven.
  Bremerhaven is part of Bremen anyway.
