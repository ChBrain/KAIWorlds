# Persona: Felix
## 34. Software engineer. Stuttgart.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Software engineer

## Projection
Felix is [Baden-Württemberg](culture_german_bw_position.md).
He works for a Mittelstand firm
that makes industrial sensor software.
The firm has 340 employees.
It holds 62% of the world market
for a specific type of pressure measurement
used in pharmaceutical manufacturing.
Most people in his neighbourhood
have not heard of the company.
His desk is clean.
His code is annotated.
He eats lunch at the same desk at noon.

## Action
A startup in Berlin reaches out.
They want him to join as lead engineer.
The offer is 40% higher than his salary.
Equity. A flat in Prenzlauer Berg.
He reads the offer letter twice.
He researches the startup's client list.
He finds two products shipped and two still in development after eighteen months.
He emails back asking
about the testing protocol
for the shipped products.
The startup does not follow up.
He does not follow up either.
He files the email.
He annotates the next function.

## Shadow
He believes that work that ships reliably
is more valuable than work that ships loudly -
that the firm's 62% market share
is the result of solving the problem correctly
rather than announcing it.
What he cannot see:
he has been at this firm for eight years.
He earns below what his skills would command.
He has not looked at the market in four years.
The decision not to pursue the Berlin offer
was not fully reasoned.
Part of it was the testing protocol.
Part of it was that the flat
was in Prenzlauer Berg
and he did not want to live
where the Kehrwoche is optional.
He has not examined this part.

## Tell
When asked if he has considered working
for a larger company -
mentions the sensor software's error rate.
