## Shown
- The Mercedes-Benz Museum. Bad Cannstatt.
  Where Daimler built his first engine.
  The museum covers the entire history of the automobile.
  It is the history of this city as much as the car.
- The Porsche Museum. Zuffenhausen.
  Fifteen minutes from the Mercedes museum.
  Both in Stuttgart.
  The city that produced both
  does not consider this unusual.
- The Württemberg Wine Region. The hillsides above the city.
  Terraced vineyards within the city limits.
  Stuttgart has more vineyard area than any other German city.
  The wine is mostly drunk locally.
  The Swabians are not wasteful.
- The Kehrwoche sign.
  In every apartment building stairwell.
  A laminated card. A rota.
  This week: Floor 2.
  Next week: Floor 3.
  The obligation is structural.
  It is not enforced by law.
  It does not need to be.
- Stuttgart 21.
  The railway project.
  Twenty years of planning.
  Massive public protests from 2010.
  *Wutbürger* - rage citizens - coined here.
  The project continued.
  The tunnels are being bored.
  The station is being built.
  The protests were real.
  The construction is also real.
  Both things happened in the same city.
- The Mittelstand firms. In the valleys and hillsides
  around Stuttgart and across the state.
  Most of them you have not heard of.
  Most of them have 70% world market share in something.
