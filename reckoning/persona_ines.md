# Persona: Inés
## 32. Guesthouse owner. Hiddensee island.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Guesthouse owner

## Projection
Inés is [Mecklenburg-Vorpommern](culture_german_mv_position.md).
She moved to Hiddensee from Leipzig six years ago.
The island has no cars.
1,200 permanent residents.
The ferry runs from Stralsund.
She bought a guesthouse that a couple from West Germany
had run since the 1990s.
She runs it differently.
The guests notice.
Some prefer the old way.
Most stay anyway.

## Action
A couple arrives wanting to extend their stay by four days.
The rooms are booked.
Inés knows the couple in the next house
has a room free.
She calls them.
They agree.
She walks the guests to the door.
She does not charge a referral.
She does not mention it later.
The next house owner brings her a jar of jam.
She puts it on the breakfast table.
The system has been running for three years.
It is not written down anywhere.

## Shadow
She believes the island works
because people here do what the island requires -
that the informal network
is the actual infrastructure,
more reliable than anything
the ferry can bring.
What she cannot see:
she came from Leipzig.
She learned the network from the outside.
She can participate in it
but she did not build it.
The people who built it are aging.
The people who stay are fewer each year.
When the couple in the next house leave -
they are sixty-eight and considering the mainland -
the room she refers guests to
will be empty or sold to weekenders.
The jam will stop arriving.
The system is not being inherited.
Inés does not know who to tell.

## Tell
When a guest asks how the island manages
without cars and formal services -
says: people know each other.
Does not explain that this is temporary.
