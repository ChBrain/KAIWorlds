## Shown
- Sanssouci. The park and palaces.
  Frederick the Great's retreat.
  Open every day. Entrance fee for the palaces.
  The park: free.
  In summer: full of visitors.
  Frederick's grave: beside the vineyard terrace.
  Beside his dogs.
  As he requested.
- The Cecilienhof. 1945.
  Where the Potsdam Conference took place.
  Churchill, Stalin, Truman.
  The round table still in the room.
  Now a hotel and museum.
  The decisions made here
  determined what Brandenburg would become
  for the next forty-five years.
- The Brandenburger Strasse. The pedestrian zone.
  The Dutch Quarter nearby:
  Frederick William I brought Dutch workers here
  in the 18th century to build.
  They built and stayed.
  The quarter is still there.
  Potsdam has been receiving immigrants
  who built things and stayed
  for three centuries.
- The Landtag. The state parliament.
  On the site of the old city palace.
  A modern reconstruction.
  The debate about whether to rebuild the palace
  went on for years.
  They rebuilt it.
  The government moved in.
- The Glienicker Brücke. The bridge.
  Potsdam to West Berlin.
  During the Cold War: the bridge where spies were exchanged.
  Gary Powers. Rudolf Abel.
  The bridge crosses the Havel.
  Now a cycling bridge.
  The exchange point is marked.
