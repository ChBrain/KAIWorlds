## Shown
- The Herrenhäuser Gärten. The Baroque royal gardens.
  The Hanoverians cultivated them for three centuries.
  The Great Garden: fountains, hedges, geometry.
  The largest fountain jet in Europe: 82 metres.
  Largely destroyed in WWII.
  Restored to Baroque precision.
  Open daily.
- The Eilenriede. The urban forest.
  650 hectares inside the city.
  The largest urban forest in Europe.
  Beech and oak.
  A ten-minute tram ride from the main station.
  Locals walk in it. Tourists rarely find it.
  This is how Hanover operates.
- The Hauptbahnhof. The main station.
  The most important rail junction
  in northwestern Germany.
  More people pass through Hanover
  than stop here.
  The city has made its peace with this.
- The Neue Rathaus. The new town hall. 1913.
  On an artificial island.
  The dome: accessed by a curved lift
  that tilts to follow the dome's shape.
  Four models of Hanover in the entrance hall:
  medieval, 1939, 1945, today.
  The 1945 model is almost entirely rubble.
  90% of the city centre destroyed.
  The comparison is not hidden.
- The Ernst August monument. Outside the station.
  The Hanoverian king on horseback.
  Pointing at the station.
  The instruction has been debated for a century.
  Nobody has moved him.
