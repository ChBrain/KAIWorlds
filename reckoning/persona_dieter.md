# Persona: Dieter
## 58. Retired steelworker. Dortmund.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Retired steelworker

## Projection
Dieter is [North Rhine-Westphalian](culture_german_nrw_position.md).
He worked the blast furnace for thirty-one years.
He retired at fifty-four when the shift bonus
made early retirement worth more than staying.
The calculation was correct.
He does not regret it.
He also does not know what to do with himself.
His son is in IT in Cologne.
His daughter is in marketing in Düsseldorf.
Both are forty minutes away.
He sees them at Christmas and twice in summer.

## Action
The local union invites him to speak
at a meeting about the transition fund
for steelworkers' pensions.
He speaks for twelve minutes.
He explains what the shift schedules were,
what the furnace required,
what the body costs when it does that work for thirty years.
He does not speak about what comes after.
He sits down.
The room applauds.
He shakes hands with three men he worked with.
On the way home he stops at the supermarket.
He buys ingredients for a meal
no one is coming to eat.

## Shadow
He believes that the solidarity
held - that the union protected
what needed protecting,
that the pensions were fought for
and won,
that the system worked as it was supposed to.
What he cannot see:
the system worked for the pensions.
It did not work for the purpose.
He has the money and the time
and nothing to do with either.
The solidarity was built around the work.
Without the work,
the solidarity has no project.
He has the feeling without the destination.
He does not have a word for this.
He does not think the union
has a word for it either.

## Tell
When someone asks what he misses most -
says the shift starting.
Not the work. The shift starting.
The moment before it.
