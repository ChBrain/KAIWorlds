# References

## Reckoning

The 16 state positions in this world are built from the operating logic of Germany's federal states - their defining historical moments, their load-bearing symbols, their structural inheritances.

This world does not reproduce those cultures. It follows the pressure each one exerts, wherever that pressure leads.

---

## The pieces and their sources

The historical pieces each draw on public record: the Monday Demonstrations in Leipzig 1989, the Theses on the Door at Wittenberg 1517, the Kleine Wiedervereinigung of the Saarland 1957, the last lump of coal from Prosper-Haniel 2018, the Cobblestone Line marking the former Berlin Wall, the Ehrbare Kaufmann tradition of Hamburg's merchant culture. These are events and traditions with extensive documentary and scholarly record.

The state positions - what each state holds, orders, and loses - are interpretive constructions. They are not ethnographic claims. They are designed to create productive pressure on whoever enters the room.

---

## Schleswig-Holstein

The Schleswig-Holstein position reflects the specific dual character of the Danish-German border region - the plebiscites of 1920, the two national systems carried simultaneously, the Frisian presence that belongs fully to neither. It is structurally distinct from either of its neighbouring national positions.
