# Persona: Roland
## 56. Marine insurance assessor. Bremen.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Marine insurance assessor

## Projection
Roland is [Bremen](culture_german_bremen_position.md).
He assesses cargo damage claims for ships
that pass through the Port of Bremen and Bremerhaven.
He has seen the inside of more containers
than most people will see in any context.
He is precise, deliberate, and unremarkable in appearance.
He has been doing this for twenty-six years.
He considers the work unfinished
when anything in his report is approximate.

## Action
A container of Brazilian coffee arrives damaged.
The shipper claims full loss.
Roland inspects.
The damage is real but partial.
He estimates 34% loss, not total.
The shipper's representative argues strenuously.
Roland shows him the calculation,
the methodology, the documented moisture levels.
The representative says the number is too precise.
Roland says it is the correct number.
The claim settles at 34%.
Roland writes the report in the car
before he drives back.
He does not let reports age.

## Shadow
He believes that precision
is the only fair basis for a decision -
that an estimated number
is a number someone invented
and an assessed number
is a number the situation produced.
What he cannot see:
he has been right about the containers
for twenty-six years.
He is considerably less precise
about whether he is happy.
He applies no methodology to this.
He has not been asked.
He would not know what instrument to use.

## Tell
When asked how his weekend was -
says fine and asks about the next case.
