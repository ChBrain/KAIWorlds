# Piece: The Moselle
## The river that has been making wine for two thousand years and does not care who governs the banks.

## Owner
- **Place:** Mainz

## Place
Mainz. Where the Moselle meets the Rhine.
Koblenz: the Deutsches Eck.
The confluence of Rhine and Moselle.
The rivers do not respect state lines.
They predate the state by two millennia.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Rhineland-Palatinate position loses its depth.
The Moselle is not a tourist attraction.
It is the operating system.
The slate terraces: built by Roman legions, maintained since.
The vines: planted by the Romans, varietally refined for twenty centuries.
The wine: structural to rural life -
the rhythm of the year, the economy of the village,
the identity of the hillside.
Rhineland-Palatinate was assembled in 1946
from three pieces that had never been one thing.
The Moselle was there before the assembly.
It will be there after.
The position is held together by the river
more than by the political unit.

## Apparent
The Moselle from Trier to Koblenz.
The slate-terraced hillsides.
Riesling on the steep slopes.
The villages: Bernkastel-Kues, Cochem, Zell.
Medieval, half-timbered, the river at their feet.
The wine production:
Rhineland-Palatinate produces more wine
than any other German state.
70% of German wine comes from here.
The Moselle is the centre of the Moselle-Saar-Ruwer region.
The Romans planted vines here around 100 AD.
The continuity is not metaphorical.
The vine stocks on some slopes
are hundreds of years old.
The Romantic Rhine: the gorge between Bingen and Koblenz.
40 castles in 65 kilometres.
UNESCO World Heritage.
Built by tolling the river traffic.
The revenue from the river
built the castles that now photograph well.
The Trier Roman monuments:
the Porta Nigra, the Imperial Baths.
Trier is the oldest city in Germany.
2,000 years of inhabitation.
The Romans are still in the walls.

## Yearbook
100 AD: Romans plant vines on the Moselle.
The terraces begin to be built.
3rd century AD: Trier becomes the capital
of the Western Roman Empire.
For sixty years: the centre of the world
is on the Moselle.
843: Treaty of Verdun.
The region passes between Frankish kingdoms.
The passing between powers
has not stopped since.
1356: the Golden Bull.
The Archbishops of Mainz, Trier, Cologne
confirmed as electors of the Holy Roman Emperor.
The region at the centre of imperial politics.
1792: French troops occupy the left bank of the Rhine.
The French stay, on and off, until 1815.
The Moselle wine travels to Paris.
1815: the Congress of Vienna.
The region divided between Prussia, Bavaria, Hesse.
Never one thing.
1946: Rhineland-Palatinate established
by French military administration.
From three pieces.
The Moselle: still there.
Still making wine.
Still not caring who governs the banks.
