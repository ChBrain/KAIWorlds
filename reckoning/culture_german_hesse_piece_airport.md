# Piece: The Airport
## The most connected point in Germany. The place nobody is from.

## Owner
- **Place:** Frankfurt

## Place
Frankfurt. Frankfurt Airport.
The third busiest airport in Europe.
The largest employer in Germany: 80,000 people.
A city within a city.
Terminal 1. Terminal 2.
The Transit Hotel: where you sleep
between one flight and another.
Nobody lives at the airport.
The airport is where you pass through
on the way to somewhere else.
Frankfurt is the state of Hesse.
The airport is Hesse's most visited address.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Hesse position loses its engine.
Frankfurt without the airport
is a significant European financial city.
Frankfurt with the airport
is the most globally connected city in Germany.
The European Central Bank. The Deutsche Börse.
The publishing industry. The book fair.
All of these are downstream of the airport.
The airport made Frankfurt the gateway.
The gateway made Frankfurt the hub.
The hub made Hesse the state
through which more international connections
flow than any other German state.
The position carries the weight of transit -
of being the place where the world
passes through.

## Apparent
Frankfurt Airport: 50 million passengers per year.
105 airlines. 320 destinations.
The cargo: 2.1 million tonnes per year.
The Frankfurt Book Fair: every October.
7,000 exhibitors. 100,000 visitors.
The most important publishing event in the world.
Also in Frankfurt.
The European Central Bank: Sonnemannstrasse.
The institution that manages the euro.
Also in Frankfurt.
The Commerzbank Tower: 259 metres.
The tallest building in Germany.
Frankfurt.
The apple wine: Ebbelwoi.
Local. Not exported.
The airport sends everything out.
The Ebbelwoi stays.

## Yearbook
1936: Frankfurt Airport opens.
1958: jet age begins at Frankfurt.
1972: Terminal 1 opens.
The airport becomes the hub.
1998: the European Central Bank established in Frankfurt.
The euro's home is here.
The decision was not accidental:
Frankfurt was already the financial hub.
2001: the Frankfurt Book Fair marks its 500th anniversary.
The first book fair was held here in 1454.
Gutenberg printed the Bible forty kilometres away.
The connection is not lost on the city.
2011: Terminal 2 expanded.
The airport is always expanding.
The transit continues.
2022: 50 million passengers.
Post-pandemic recovery.
The hub resumed.
