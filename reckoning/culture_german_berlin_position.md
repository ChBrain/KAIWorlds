# Position: Berlin
## The city whose identity is built on volatility.

## Owner
- *
## Has
[The Cobblestone Line](culture_german_berlin_piece_cobblestone_line.md).
The division - 28 years of wall,
producing two cities in one space:
East Berlin with its Stalinist architecture
and its Stasi and its specific GDR culture;
West Berlin with its subsidies,
its exemption from conscription,
its artistic experimentation,
its island mentality.
Both cities formed their people.
Both are inside the one city now.
The Mauer im Kopf: still present.
The volatility: named by the writer Rory MacLean -
*Berlin is all about volatility.
Its identity is based not on stability but on change.*
The city that has been the capital of Prussia,
the Weimar Republic, the Third Reich,
divided East and West,
and reunified Germany
in 180 years.
Each time: rebuilt.
Each time: different.
The position carries all of it
as operating context.
It is not settled.
It does not intend to settle.
It exists before the persona arrives.
The room is always being renovated.

## Orders
Build.
Not metaphorically - literally.
The cranes are always there.
The city is always finishing something.
The position does not preserve itself.
It reconstructs itself.
The Stadtschloss: rebuilt.
The Bauakademie: being rebuilt.
The wall's line: marked, not rebuilt.
The distinction is important.
Some things are preserved as record.
Most things are rebuilt as future.
The position navigates between them.

## Loses
The stability.
They were certain that the reunification -
the wall fallen, the capital restored,
the government returned from Bonn -
would produce a settled Berlin.
A city that knew what it was.
The city did not settle.
It continued to change.
The Ossi-Wessi divide: documented, persistent.
The gentrification: ongoing.
The Schwabenhass: real and named.
The affordable rent that made Berlin creative:
less affordable each year.
The position discovers
that volatility is not a phase.
It is the condition.
The city is defined by it
the way other cities are defined
by their rivers or their hills.

## Drives
On the loss of stability -
builds into the volatility.
The position does not seek settlement.
It produces the next version.
The cranes move.
The districts shift.
The people who came for the cheap rent
are replaced by the people
who came because the culture existed.
The culture exists because the rent was cheap.
The rent is no longer cheap.
The culture is still there.
For now.
The position does not know how long.
It builds while the building is possible.
That has always been the Berlin way.
