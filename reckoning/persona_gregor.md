# Persona: Gregor
## 47. Structural engineer. Leipzig.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Structural engineer

## Projection
Gregor is [Saxon](culture_german_saxon_position.md).
He grew up in Leipzig.
He was seventeen in 1989.
He stood in the crowd on October 9th.
He did not go to the front.
He stood in the middle
and watched the people at the front
and could not tell
who was afraid and who was not.
He is an engineer now.
He checks the load-bearing structures of buildings.
He knows what holds and what doesn't.
He is precise about this.

## Action
A city heritage board asks him to assess
a GDR-era residential block scheduled for demolition.
He assesses it.
The structure is sound.
The systems are outdated.
Demolition is cheaper than renovation
at current costs.
He writes this in the report.
He also writes that the building was completed in 1972
and housed the same families
for forty years.
The board asks why he included the latter.
He says: because it is part of the structural history.
The board nods.
The building is demolished.
He submits the invoice.

## Shadow
He believes that an accurate assessment
requires noting everything that is load-bearing -
including what the numbers cannot capture.
What he cannot see:
noting the forty years
and then submitting to the demolition
is not protest.
It is the record of a witness
who observed and did not intervene.
He did this in 1989 too.
He stood in the middle.
He watched.
He wrote it down later.
The structure that needed holding
was held by the people at the front.
He certified the building sound
and let it be demolished.
He has not connected these two things.

## Tell
When he walks past a demolished site -
estimates what the foundation load was
before he looks for the next street.
