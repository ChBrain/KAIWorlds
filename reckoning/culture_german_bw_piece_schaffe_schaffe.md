# Piece: Schaffe schaffe Häusle baue
## Work, work, build a house. The sentence a culture lives by.

## Owner
- **Place:** Stuttgart

## Place
Stuttgart. The Neckar valley.
The Mercedes-Benz Museum.
The Porsche Museum.
Both in Stuttgart.
Both built in the Swabian hills.
The Kehrwoche sign outside the apartment door:
this week, this floor cleans the stairwell.
Since before anyone can remember.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the Baden-Württemberg position loses its engine.
*Schaffe schaffe Häusle baue* is not a folk saying.
It is a description of the operating system.
The frugality that built the Mittelstand -
the dense network of small and medium enterprises
that make the specialised components
for the machines of the world.
Hidden champions: companies with 70% world market share
in products most people have never heard of.
The thrift was not chosen from comfort.
It was chosen from the memory of poverty -
the Thirty Years' War decimated this region -
and it produced prosperity.
The prosperity did not abandon the thrift.
The thrift is now the engine of the prosperity.
Remove it and the hidden champions
become visible for the wrong reasons.

## Apparent
The Kehrwoche. The sweeping week.
Every apartment building in Stuttgart:
a rota. Each household cleans the shared stairwell
on their assigned week.
The sign is posted.
The obligation is real.
The Mittelstand.
Not BMW and Daimler - though they are Swabian too.
The company in Tuttlingen
that makes 70% of the world's surgical instruments.
The company in Schwäbisch Hall
that makes the hydraulic fittings
for aerospace that you have never heard of.
The dual education system:
apprentices split their time between
the vocational school and the company.
The system has been running since the 19th century.
Germany's engineering culture runs on it.
*Schaffe schaffe Häusle baue.*
The song Ralf Bendix wrote in 1964:
*Work and work, build a house,
and don't look at the girls.*
Number one in Germany.
The Swabians found it accurate.

## Yearbook
1618–1648: the Thirty Years' War.
Württemberg's population reduced by a third.
Some estimates say half.
The poverty that followed.
The frugality that poverty produced.
The frugality that survived the poverty.
1817: Robert Bosch born in Albeck, near Ulm.
1886: Carl Benz builds the first automobile.
In Mannheim.
Gottlieb Daimler, in Bad Cannstatt, the same year.
Both Swabian.
Both working in sheds.
The shed is load-bearing.
1919: the Bauhaus founded in Weimar.
Walter Gropius: Swabian by adoption.
The Bauhaus aesthetic and the Mittelstand ethic
are related: function is the form.
The ornamentation that costs without contributing
is waste.
Waste is immoral.
1952: Baden-Württemberg formed
from the merger of three states.
The merger was contested.
The Badeners and the Württembergers
did not always agree.
The state functions regardless.
2012: *Schwabenhass* coined.
Swabians moving to Berlin:
300,000 of them.
They clean the stairwells.
They expect others to as well.
The conflict is documented, discussed, named.
The Swabians do not modify the Kehrwoche.
