# Persona: Stefan
## 41. Air traffic controller. Frankfurt.

## Owner
- **Project:** Cultures

## Title
Air traffic controller

## Projection
Stefan is [Hesse](culture_german_hesse_position.md).
He works the approach sector at Frankfurt Airport.
Shift work. Noise-cancelling headset.
He manages on average twenty-three aircraft per hour.
He grew up in Hanau, twenty kilometres east.
He commutes in.
He has worked here for fifteen years.
He finds the work completely absorbing.
He finds it difficult to explain why
to people who ask.

## Action
A journalist is writing a piece
on air traffic controllers.
She asks Stefan what the job feels like.
He says: it is managing a moving puzzle
where the pieces have momentum.
She asks if it is stressful.
He says: stress is when you lose track.
I don't lose track.
She asks what he thinks about during a shift.
He says: the aircraft.
She asks what he thinks about between shifts.
He says: honestly, not much.
She writes: *The job requires complete presence.*
He reads the piece.
It is not inaccurate.
It is not quite what he said.
He does not contact the journalist.

## Shadow
He believes the work requires
and rewards complete presence -
that the fifteen minutes between a cleared aircraft
and the next one
are not empty but preparatory,
and that this is a form of engagement
most professions do not offer.
What he cannot see:
his partner has been saying for two years
that he is also absent when he is at home.
Not absent-minded. Present-but-unreachable.
The skill he uses for the aircraft -
full attention, no bleed-through -
he applies symmetrically.
When he leaves the sector,
the presence goes with him.
The flat gets what is left.

## Tell
When his partner describes a problem -
listens completely and says nothing
until she has finished.
Then asks a clarifying question.
Then offers a solution.
Wonders why she sometimes cries after.
