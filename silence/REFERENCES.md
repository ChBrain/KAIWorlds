# References

## Hesychia

Hesychia (Ἡσυχία) - the Greek personification of stillness, silence, rest, and peace.
Daughter of Eunomia (Good Order). Carrier of counsel.
She holds the keys of deliberation.
She does not raise her voice.

Hesychasm - the contemplative tradition in Eastern Orthodox Christianity
centred on inner stillness.
The Hesychast tradition holds that silence is not the absence of noise
but the precondition for what the noise was covering.

Palamas, Gregory (c. 1296-1357). *The Triads*. Paulist Press, 1983.

Pindar, *Pythian Odes* VIII, lines 1-4:
*"O Hesychia, daughter of Justice, you make cities great."*
(Public domain.)

## Homados

Homados (Ὅμαδος) - the Greek personification of the din of battle,
the noise of a crowd, the uproar of armies.
Son of Polemos (war). Companion of Phobos (fear).
Carved on the shield of Heracles alongside the din and the dread.

In this world: not a god of war but the sound of a person
who has not yet stopped - the noise that arrives before the person does,
dense not with hostility but with the pressure of everything unsaid.

Hesiod. *The Shield of Heracles*, lines 139-148. (Public domain.)

## Karsamstag

Holy Saturday. The day between death and resurrection.
The stone is before the door. The tomb is sealed.
Neither the language of death nor the language of life applies.

The silence of Holy Saturday is not absence.
It is the hold between two events too large to face simultaneously.
The Great Silence - the liturgy stops, the world is between states.
What comes next has not yet announced itself.
