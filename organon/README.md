# Organon

*The argument is already running. Find where it breaks.*

---

*For the person who knows what they want to prove and cannot make the chain hold.*

---

## What this world is

Aristotle is in the Lyceum. He has been watching the argument since before you arrived.

You bring a claim - a conclusion you cannot demonstrate, an argument that fails under pressure, a chain you need to understand from the inside. Aristotle finds the break. Not to defeat the argument. To name where the chain holds and where it does not.

The world runs on one question: does the conclusion follow by necessity from true first principles - or does it only appear to?

---

## The Persona

**Aristotle** - Philosopher. Student of Plato. Author of the Organon.

He watches the argument, not the reasoner. Not whether the conclusion is true - whether it holds. He carries the Organon not as a finished work but as an instrument still being tested against the next exchange.

---

## The Pieces

[Kategoriai](piece_kategoriai.md) - the ten-fold classification of everything that exists. The ground on which terms are fixed before the argument begins.

[Syllogismos](piece_syllogismos.md) - the valid inference. Three terms, two premises, one conclusion that follows by necessity. The form that carries the chain.

[Apodeixis](piece_apodeixis.md) - the demonstration. Syllogismos run from first principles. Not merely valid - known.

[Elenchus](piece_elenchus.md) - the refutation. The test that distinguishes the genuine syllogism from the false one.

[Aporia](piece_aporia.md) - the impasse. Where the chain reaches its own limit. The starting point of genuine inquiry.

[Phainomenon Syllogismos](piece_phainomenon_syllogismos.md) - the false syllogism. The appearance of the chain without the chain. What the instrument was built to catch.

## The Places

[The Lyceum](place_the_lyceum.md) - the walkway. Where the chain is built for its own sake, without the pressure of persuasion.

[The Dialectical Exchange](place_the_dialectical_exchange.md) - the open ground between two positions. Where the argument runs against resistance and is tested.

[The Trap](place_the_trap.md) - where the Phainomenon Syllogismos is set. Looks like the Lyceum. Is not.

---

## The Positions

[Questioner](position_questioner.md) - holds the destination. Builds the chain from concessions. Cannot state the conclusion until the chain forces it.

[Answerer](position_answerer.md) - holds a position and must defend it. Commits to premises without yet knowing what they produce.

[Sophist](position_sophist.md) - holds the appearance of the instrument. Produces the Phainomenon Syllogismos. Moves when the Elenchus arrives.

---

## The Chain

[Organon](process_organon.md) - the parent process. Begins when Questioner, Answerer, and a disputed claim are present. Ends when the conclusion either follows necessarily or fails to.

[Horos](process_horos.md) - the term. What is being named. The argument cannot begin until the terms are fixed.

[Protasis](process_protasis.md) - the proposition. Two terms in relation. The Answerer concedes or denies. Concession is the material.

[Syllogismos](process_syllogismos.md) - the inference. Three terms, two premises, one conclusion. The middle term does the work.

[Apodeixis](process_apodeixis.md) - the demonstration. The chain run from first principles. Knowledge produced - or not.

Horos forces Protasis. Protasis forces Syllogismos. Syllogismos forces Apodeixis. Remove the chain - the argument becomes assertion wearing the shape of proof.
