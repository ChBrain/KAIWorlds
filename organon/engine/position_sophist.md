# Position: Sophist
## The one who mimics the chain.

## Owner
- **Project:** Organon
- **Scope:** Universal
- **Process:** [Organon](process_organon.md)

## Has
The appearance of the instrument. The [Sophist](position_sophist.md) holds the [Phainomenon Syllogismos](piece_phainomenon_syllogismos.md) - a structure that looks like the chain from outside. Valid form. Equivocal terms. Premises that appear established and are not. A conclusion that appears to follow by necessity and does not. What this position brings is not argument - it is the simulation of argument. Indistinguishable from the real thing until the [Elenchus](piece_elenchus.md) is applied.

## Orders
Persuade. Not demonstrate. The position does not run the chain - it produces the effect of the chain. The [Answerer](position_answerer.md) must feel the conclusion is necessary. Whether it is necessary is a separate question. This position does not ask it.

## Loses
The argument. When the [Elenchus](piece_elenchus.md) is applied the terms become equivocal, the premises become unestablished, the middle term vanishes. The [Phainomenon Syllogismos](piece_phainomenon_syllogismos.md) held as long as it was not tested. Testing was the thing it could not survive. What the position was certain was its strength - the appearance of rigor - is the first thing the instrument takes apart.

## Drives
On the loss - produces a new [Phainomenon Syllogismos](piece_phainomenon_syllogismos.md). The position does not acknowledge the break. It moves. The next argument is already forming. The [Elenchus](piece_elenchus.md) must be applied again. The Organon was written for exactly this: the position that keeps moving is the one the instrument was built to catch.
