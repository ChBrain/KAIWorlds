# Piece: Elenchus
## The refutation.

## Owner
- **Project:** Organon
- **Process:** [Syllogismos](process_syllogismos.md)

## Place
[The Dialectical Exchange](place_the_dialectical_exchange.md). Applied wherever a [Syllogismos](piece_syllogismos.md) is present.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the [Phainomenon Syllogismos](piece_phainomenon_syllogismos.md) cannot be distinguished from the real one. Both have the same shape from the outside. The [Elenchus](piece_elenchus.md) is the instrument that produces the distinction. Without it the chain cannot be tested - it can only be believed or doubted. The instrument exists because this piece exists.

## Apparent
A [Syllogismos](piece_syllogismos.md) whose conclusion is the contradictory of the [Answerer](position_answerer.md)'s position. Run from premises the [Answerer](position_answerer.md) has already conceded. If the [Elenchus](piece_elenchus.md) holds, the [Answerer](position_answerer.md) has contradicted themselves. Their position cannot stand alongside what they have already granted. One of the two must be released.

## Yearbook
Socrates used it without naming it - the method that produces [Aporia](piece_aporia.md) by showing the [Answerer](position_answerer.md) that they hold contradictory beliefs. He called it midwifery. Aristotle named the structure.
Aristotle distinguishes genuine [Elenchus](piece_elenchus.md) from the [Phainomenon Syllogismos](piece_phainomenon_syllogismos.md) - the apparent refutation that mimics it. On Sophistical Refutations is dedicated to this distinction alone.
The piece travels into every cross-examination, every adversarial proceeding, every debate where one position must be tested against what the holder has already committed to. When the cross-examiner holds up the earlier concession and places it against the current claim - the Elenchus is running.
