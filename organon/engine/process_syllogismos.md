# Process: Syllogismos

## Owner
- **Project:** Organon
- **Process:** [Organon](process_organon.md)

## Initiated by
[Protasis](process_protasis.md) has run. Premises have been conceded. Three terms are present: one shared between the two premises, two appearing once each. The [Questioner](position_questioner.md) now draws the conclusion. The [Answerer](position_answerer.md) has already granted what produces it.

## Direction
Toward a conclusion that follows by necessity - that cannot be otherwise given the premises. Not toward a true conclusion. Toward a necessary one. The [Syllogismos](piece_syllogismos.md) does not require the premises to be true. It requires the conclusion to follow. If it follows, the form holds. Whether it produces knowledge depends on [Apodeixis](process_apodeixis.md).

## Lever
Carrying force: the middle term - the term shared between the two premises, absent from the conclusion, doing the work of connecting subject to predicate. Every valid inference has one. Find the middle term and the structure of the argument becomes visible.

Resistance: the [Answerer](position_answerer.md)'s sense that the conclusion is wrong. They conceded the premises. The conclusion follows. But it conflicts with what they believed before the argument began. The resistance is not logical - it is the weight of prior conviction pressing against a necessary result. The [Elenchus](piece_elenchus.md) is the instrument for determining whether that resistance is valid or merely uncomfortable.

## Echo
[Apodeixis](process_apodeixis.md) inherits a conclusion that has been drawn. The [Answerer](position_answerer.md) is committed - they conceded the premises, the conclusion follows, they cannot deny it without denying what they already granted. What changed: the argument has a shape. The [Phainomenon Syllogismos](piece_phainomenon_syllogismos.md) has the same shape. They are not yet distinguishable.
