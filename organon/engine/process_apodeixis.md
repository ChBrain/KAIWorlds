# Process: Apodeixis

## Owner
- **Project:** Organon
- **Process:** [Organon](process_organon.md)

## Initiated by
[Syllogismos](process_syllogismos.md) has run. A conclusion has been drawn that follows by necessity from the premises. The question now is whether the premises themselves are true - not merely conceded, not merely probable, but known. [Apodeixis](piece_apodeixis.md) runs only when the premises are first principles: true, primary, immediate, better known than the conclusion, and causally prior to it.

## Direction
Toward knowledge - not opinion, not probability, not valid inference from uncertain premises, but necessary truth derived from what cannot be otherwise. If the premises are first principles, the conclusion is known. If they are not, what [Syllogismos](process_syllogismos.md) produced is valid but not demonstrative. Useful. Not knowledge.

## Lever
Carrying force: the first principles themselves - the premises that require no prior demonstration because they are the ground all demonstration stands on. They are not derived. They are grasped by nous - the faculty that sees them directly. Without them the chain produces infinite regress or circular proof.

Resistance: the [Aporia](piece_aporia.md) - the genuine impasse where two equally valid arguments point to opposite conclusions, or where the first principles themselves are in question. [Aporia](piece_aporia.md) does not break the instrument. It is the place where real inquiry begins. Aristotle calls it the starting point of philosophy. The chain stops here not from failure but from reaching the ground.

## Echo
[Organon](process_organon.md) is complete. The argument has been run from terms through propositions through valid inference to demonstration - or it has not. What changed: the conclusion is either known or it is not. The distinction is now visible. What the chain produced can be named precisely: demonstration, valid inference, dialectical probability, or the appearance of one of these.
