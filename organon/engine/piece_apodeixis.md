# Piece: Apodeixis
## The demonstration.

## Owner

## Place
[The Lyceum](place_the_lyceum.md). Produced only when first principles are present.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and [Syllogismos](piece_syllogismos.md) produces valid inference without knowledge. Valid inference is not knowledge. It is form without epistemic ground. [Apodeixis](piece_apodeixis.md) is what separates the chain that produces science from the chain that merely holds together. The Organon was built to produce this piece. Everything before it in the chain is preparation.

## Apparent
A [Syllogismos](piece_syllogismos.md) whose premises are first principles - true, primary, immediate, better known than the conclusion they produce, and causally prior to it. The conclusion does not merely follow. It cannot be otherwise. It is known with necessity. The one who grasps it cannot un-know it without abandoning the premises. The premises themselves are grasped by direct intellectual vision - not by argument, not by the chain, but by the faculty that sees the first principles as self-evidently true.

## Yearbook
Aristotle builds the entire Posterior Analytics around this piece. It is his account of what scientific knowledge is and what it requires. The work sets the standard so high that almost nothing meets it - Aristotle himself achieves it perhaps only in mathematics and formal logic. In biology, ethics, politics, rhetoric - he works with what is probable, not with what is necessary.
The standard did not stop being the standard. Newton's Principia is structured as demonstration from first principles. Euclid's Elements is the clearest instance the world produced. Spinoza's Ethics attempts it explicitly, derives ethics from definitions and axioms as if it were geometry.
The piece is what every structured argument is reaching for and almost never achieves. The gap between valid inference and genuine demonstration is where most of human knowledge lives.
