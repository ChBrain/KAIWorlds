# Place: The Lyceum

## Owner
- **Project:** Organon

## Shown
The covered walkway. Long. Open at the sides. [Aristotle](persona_aristotle.md) moves through it while the argument runs - the peripatetic method, thinking on foot. The [Questioner](position_questioner.md) and [Answerer](position_answerer.md) are present. The argument is the object of attention. No audience. No judges. The chain is built here for its own sake, not for effect.

## Holds
- [Kategoriai](piece_kategoriai.md) - the ground the terms are fixed on, present before the argument begins.
- [Apodeixis](piece_apodeixis.md) - produced here when the chain runs from first principles to necessary conclusion.
- [Aporia](piece_aporia.md) - arrives here when the chain reaches its own limit.

## Offers
The conditions for demonstration. The [Questioner](position_questioner.md) can build the chain without the pressure of an audience or a verdict. The argument can be run, broken, rebuilt. The [Elenchus](piece_elenchus.md) can be applied without consequence beyond the argument itself. What this place makes available: the chain running clean, without the distortion of persuasion.

## Withheld
Whether the first principles are present. The Lyceum offers the conditions for demonstration but cannot supply the first principles themselves. They are grasped by direct intellectual vision or they are not grasped. The place holds the instrument. It does not hold the ground the instrument stands on. That is what [Aporia](piece_aporia.md) surfaces when the chain reaches the limit.
