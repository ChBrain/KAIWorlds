# Piece: Phainomenon Syllogismos
## The false syllogism.

## Owner
- **Project:** Organon
- **Process:** [Organon](process_organon.md)

## Place
[The Trap](place_the_trap.md). Set by the [Sophist](position_sophist.md) wherever the [Questioner](position_questioner.md) and [Answerer](position_answerer.md) are present.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and the [Organon](process_organon.md) has no adversary. The instrument was built against this piece. Without it [Horos](process_horos.md) has nothing to protect the terms from, [Elenchus](piece_elenchus.md) has nothing to distinguish itself from, [Apodeixis](process_apodeixis.md) has no counterfeit to be distinguished from. The whole of On Sophistical Refutations exists because this piece exists.

## Apparent
The shape of a [Syllogismos](piece_syllogismos.md). Three apparent terms. Two apparent premises. A conclusion that appears to follow. The break is inside: an equivocal term shifting meaning between premises, a premise that has not been established, a conclusion that does not follow by necessity but by proximity. From the outside it is indistinguishable from valid inference until the [Elenchus](piece_elenchus.md) is applied.

## Yearbook
Aristotle catalogs thirteen types in On Sophistical Refutations - six dependent on language, seven independent of it. The work is the closing book of the Organon: the instrument ends by naming what it was built to catch.
The Sophists built their reputation on this piece. Zeno's paradoxes are its most elegant form - arguments whose conclusions cannot be true and whose structure cannot be faulted without identifying exactly where the break is.
The piece has never stopped traveling. Every field that depends on argument has its resident [Phainomenon Syllogismos](piece_phainomenon_syllogismos.md) - the argument that everyone accepts because no one has yet named where it breaks. The ones who name it inherit the field. The ones who cannot are still inside [The Trap](place_the_trap.md).
