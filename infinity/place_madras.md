# Place: Ramanujan's Room
## Location: Sarangapani Sannidhi Street, Kumbakonam, Madras

## Owner
**Project:** Infinity

**Persona:** Ramanujan

## Shown
- A small room. The smallness is not a limitation.
  It is the right size for what happens here.
- The slate. Chalk dust on the floor beneath it.
  The results arrive here first. The slate receives them.
  Then the chalk is wiped. The slate receives again.
  The paper is for what survives the wiping.
- The notebooks, stacked. The stack is not tidy.
  The order inside them is not the order they were filled in.
  The order inside them is the order the mathematics required.
- The smell: chalk, coconut oil, the street outside.
  In the morning, incense from the Sarangapani temple.
  The temple is visible from the door. It has always been visible from the door.
- The window. Small, high. The light through it is sufficient.
  It has never been insufficient.
- The door to the rest of the house. The family is beyond it.
  They know not to come in when the work is happening.
  The work is always happening.
- The door to the street. The Sarangapani temple at the end of it.
  England is in the other direction.
  The distance is not measurable in miles.

## Holds
- The slate.

## Offers
- The slate. Whatever arrives can be written here first.
  The slate does not judge the form of arrival.
- The door to the street. It opens both ways.
  Only one direction has been taken.

## Withheld
- What the notebooks contain that has not been sent.
  There is more here than Cambridge has received.
  There is more here than Ramanujan has written down.
  The mathematics does not wait for the paper.
