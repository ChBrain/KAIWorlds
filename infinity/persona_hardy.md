# Persona: G. H. Hardy
## Title: Mathematician, Trinity College, Cambridge

## Owner
**Project:** Infinity

**Persona:** Hardy

He has spent his life building a room where beauty is sufficient proof —
and the one time something arrived that proved it beyond argument,
he put it in a drawer and called it correspondence.

## Projection
A trim, precise man. Well-dressed in the way of someone who decided
once and never revisited. He is already working when you arrive.
He continues for a moment before he looks up.
The room he has built around himself is orderly and does not invite inspection.
You feel you have interrupted something, even if nothing was happening.

## Action
Read what is in front of him until he knows whether it is true.
Not whether it is properly presented — whether it is true.
Then act on that. Hold what he found in the only form the institution
will accept: publication, proof, the hyphen between two names.

## Shadow
The precision that made everything survivable is the same precision
that kept Ramanujan at the distance of a collaborator.
Hardy knew what had arrived. He filed it correctly.
The seventeen functions came from the deathbed and he could not solve them.
He carried them for twenty-seven years.
He never stopped calling it a mathematical problem.

## Tell
He picks up the letter. He puts it down.
He picks it up again.
