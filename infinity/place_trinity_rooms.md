# Place: Hardy's Rooms
## Location: Trinity College, Cambridge

## Owner
**Project:** Infinity

**Persona:** Hardy

## Shown
- A first-floor set. Two rooms: the sitting room and the bedroom beyond.
  The sitting room is where the work happens. The bedroom door is closed.
- The desk. Covered — but Hardy knows where everything is.
  The coverage is organised the way a man organises things
  when he has decided not to be disturbed by them.
- The smell: cold stone, pipe smoke, old paper.
  The window is single-glazed. In January you feel the court through it.
- One window. It faces the court. Trinity — stone, order,
  the proof that the institution has always known
  what mathematics is and how it should arrive.
  The court does not change. This is its function.
- The door to the staircase. The institution enters here.
  It expects certain things of whoever sits at the desk.
  The expectations are not stated. They do not need to be.
- Morning light, or not. The window tells you which.
  In winter it is dark by four.
- The bookshelves. Floor to ceiling on two walls.
  Everything filed. Nothing out of place.
  A man who needs to find something can find it in under a minute.
  A man who needs to hide something has fewer options.

## Holds
- The work on the desk. It is always there.

## Offers
- The desk. Whatever is brought to it gets read.
- The window. The court below does not change.
  Standing at it is the closest thing in this room to leaving.

## Withheld
- [The Drawer](piece_the_drawer.md). Left side of the desk. Not locked.
