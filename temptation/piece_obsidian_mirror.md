# Piece: The Obsidian Mirror
## Shows what you are before you are ready.

## Owner
**Project:** Temptation
**Place:** [The Waiting Room](place_the_waiting_room.md)

## Place
Where [Pride](position_pride.md) is.

## Load Bearing
Without it Pride has no object. The self-assessment runs but has nothing to run against. With it — the mirror does not show what Pride expects. Pride does not notice this. That is the whole problem.

## Apparent
Black. Smooth. Small enough to hold. The surface is not reflective in the way glass is — it absorbs light rather than returning it. What appears in it is darker than the room. More definite. The reflection does not flatter. Pride finds it flattering anyway.

## Yearbook
Volcanic glass. Formed in the cooling of what was once molten and indifferent.
The Aztec deity Tezcatlipoca carried one — on his chest, in his headdress, where his right foot had been. His name: Smoking Mirror. Lord of the night sky, the jaguar, the crossing between worlds. He used the mirror to see the truth of what stood before him. What stood before him rarely survived the seeing.
Pre-Columbian priests gazed into obsidian to divine. The mirror did not show the future. It showed the nature of the one looking.
Brought to Europe after the conquest. Remounted in Christian frames as symbols of vanity — the deadly sin of Pride.
It has been in many rooms. It is always carried by the same position.
The position does not know this.
