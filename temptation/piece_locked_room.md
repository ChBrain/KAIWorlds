# Piece: The Locked Room
## What is kept by staying closed.

## Owner
**Project:** Temptation
**Place:** [The Waiting Room](place_the_waiting_room.md)

## Place
Where [Chastity](position_chastity.md) is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the boundary is just a preference. With it — the closure has a history of what it has protected, and what it has cost, and the question of whether those are the same thing.

## Apparent
A key. Old. Iron. The room it opens is not here. The key is carried anyway. It is kept in a pocket, not displayed. Chastity touches it occasionally — not to check it is there, but from habit. The habit is old enough that its origin is gone.

## Yearbook
Bluebeard: the one room the wife must not open. The key given and then the test of whether it would be used. The room opened. What was in it had always been in it. The prohibition was never about the room.
Julian of Norwich: walled herself into a cell at thirty years old. Voluntarily. The door sealed from the outside at her request. She lived there for twenty years. Wrote the first book in English known to be written by a woman. The locked room was not a prison. It was a condition of seeing clearly.
The Vestal Virgins: the sacred fire tended for thirty years. The closure as civic duty. The city's safety held in the body's boundary.
The key is not about the door it locks. It is about what the holding makes possible.
Chastity does not think about Julian. Chastity just keeps a little distance.
