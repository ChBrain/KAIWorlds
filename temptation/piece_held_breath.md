# Piece: The Held Breath
## The body knowing the next thing requires this moment first.

## Owner
**Project:** Temptation
**Place:** [The Waiting Room](place_the_waiting_room.md)

## Place
Where [Patience](position_patience.md) is.

## Load Bearing
Without it patience is just endurance — passive, accumulating nothing. With it — the holding is purposeful. The breath is held not because breathing is wrong but because release is being saved for the right moment. Remove the piece and Patience becomes Wrath with better manners.

## Apparent
Not an object. A state. The chest slightly raised. The stillness that is not relaxation — it is readiness suspended. Someone who holds their breath in this way is not afraid. They are waiting for the signal that has not come yet. The signal will come. They are certain of this. The certainty is what makes the holding possible.

## Yearbook
The diver before descent: the last breath before going under is not the largest breath. It is the most considered one. Too much air and the body becomes buoyant and fights the depth. The right amount is the right amount.
The archer at full draw: the breath released halfway, then held. The arrow goes when the held breath says it should. Not before. The body knows the moment. The mind does not override it.
The surgeon's pause: before the first incision. The operating theatre goes very still. The pause is not hesitation. It is the last moment before the consequence of the action becomes permanent. Every surgeon knows this moment. The good ones honour it.
The vigil: the night watch beside the dying, the newborn, the fire. Hours of held attention. Not sleep. Not quite wakefulness. The breath shallow and regular and ready.
Patience breathes normally. The piece is invisible. Only Wrath, across the room, can feel it — that quality of readiness that looks like stillness and is something else entirely.
