# Piece: The Thank You Letter
## The act performed so the feeling does not evaporate.

## Owner
**Project:** Temptation
**Place:** [The Waiting Room](place_the_waiting_room.md)

## Place
Where [Gratitude](position_gratitude.md) is.

## Load Bearing
Without it gratitude is a mood — present, then gone, leaving no record that it was ever there. With it — the feeling became an act, and the act exists independently of whether the feeling persists.

## Apparent
Paper. Handwritten. Folded once. The envelope is sealed. The address is written. It is ready to send. It has been ready to send for some time. It is in a coat pocket or a bag, carried from the place it was written toward the place it needs to go. It has not arrived yet. This is not avoidance. It is simply that the journey is not finished.

## Yearbook
Roman: gratias agere — to perform thanks. A public act, not a private feeling. Done in front of witnesses. The Stoics distinguished between feeling grateful and being grateful — the first is involuntary, the second is a practice. The letter is the practice.
The medieval letter of thanks: formal, structured, witnessed. The recipient kept it. It was a legal document as much as an emotional one. Gratitude as binding record.
Seneca: benefits should be passed on, not hoarded. The letter that acknowledges a benefit puts it into circulation. The thanks that is not expressed dies with the feeling.
Gratitude writes the letter because not writing it would mean the feeling was not real. The feeling was real. The letter is proof.
Envy has never written one.
