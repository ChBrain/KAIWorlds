# Piece: The Unfinished Letter
## The intention intact. The act suspended.

## Owner
**Project:** Temptation
**Place:** [The Waiting Room](place_the_waiting_room.md)

## Place
Where [Sloth](position_sloth.md) is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the acedia has no form — it is a condition without an object. With it — the incompleteness is specific and testable. The letter exists. It has a beginning. It does not have an end. Remove the piece and Sloth is just tired. With it, Sloth is somewhere else entirely while appearing to be here.

## Apparent
Several pages. Handwritten. The first page is complete — the handwriting is clear, considered, the sentences finished. The second page ends mid-sentence. Not mid-word — the thought arrived at a natural pause and then did not continue. The pen is present. The next word is not difficult to imagine. It has not been written. The pages are not crumpled. They have been handled carefully. Set down carefully. Picked up again. Set down again.

## Yearbook
Acedia: the medieval sin that is not laziness. The monk who cannot pray — not from disbelief, not from exhaustion, but from a kind of spiritual distance that descends without warning and lifts without explanation. The cell is the right size. The hour is the correct hour. The prayer does not come.
Evagrius Ponticus named it in the fourth century: the noonday demon. The one that arrives at midday and makes the sun seem stationary. Time stops moving. The monk looks out the window. The window shows the same thing it always shows. This is unbearable.
The unfinished symphony. The unfinished novel. The unfinished letter. These are not always failures of will. Sometimes the person is simply — elsewhere. The work is waiting for them to come back. They intend to come back.
Sloth means to come back. Has meant to come back for some time.
The letter is for someone who would be glad to receive it. This does not help.
