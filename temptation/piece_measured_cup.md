# Piece: The Measured Cup
## The only thing standing between the right outcome and nothing.

## Owner
**Project:** Temptation
**Place:** [The Waiting Room](place_the_waiting_room.md)

## Place
Where [Temperance](position_temperance.md) is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the calibration has no instrument. Temperance holds. With it — the holding has a standard, and the standard has a history of what happens when it is ignored.

## Apparent
Graduated. Glass or clear plastic. The markings are precise — millilitres on one side, something older on the other. It has been used. The inside is clean. It sits in a bag or a pocket, carried not as a statement but as a tool. Temperance does not display it.

## Yearbook
The apothecary's measure: the wrong proportion in a compound does not produce less of the right effect. It produces a different effect entirely. The measure is what stands between medicine and poison. Between the dose that helps and the dose that harms. The line between them is not moral. It is exact.
The alchemist's graduated vessel: proportion as the work. The Great Work fails not from wrong ingredients but wrong measure. The transmutation requires precisely this much and no more.
The temperance movement's pledge cup: signed, witnessed, specific. Not a feeling. A quantity. A line below which the hand stays. The cup as the contract with oneself.
Temperance does not carry it as a trophy. Carries it because the alternative is to trust the feeling, and the feeling says the vending machine would be fine.
The feeling is not calibrated. The cup is.
