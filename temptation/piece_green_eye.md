# Piece: The Green Eye
## The gaze that harms what it falls on.

## Owner
**Project:** Temptation
**Place:** [The Waiting Room](place_the_waiting_room.md)

## Place
Where [Envy](position_envy.md) is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the comparison runs internally with no external form. With it — the looking has a history, and the history says that this kind of attention has always cost the one it lands on something, even when nothing was said.

## Apparent
An amulet. Glass. Concentric circles — white, then black, then the deep blue of the outer iris, then the green at the centre that gives it its name. It is worn or carried against the evil eye. Against exactly what it is. Envy does not see the irony. The irony is constitutive.

## Yearbook
Mal occhio — the evil eye. Among the oldest documented beliefs in human culture. Appears in Mesopotamian texts. In Homeric Greece. In Roman curse tablets. In the Islamic world as the nazar. The belief: that envy, transmitted through the gaze, curses what it falls on. The coveted thing spoils. The praised child sickens. The admired livestock dies.
Amulets against it are among the oldest surviving human objects. The protection against the evil eye is the image of the evil eye. The cure carries the disease.
Iago: I am not what I am. The envy that operates through language rather than sight, but the mechanism is identical — the gaze that transforms what it attends to.
Envy carries the amulet as protection. Against what others might do to them. Does not consider the other direction.
