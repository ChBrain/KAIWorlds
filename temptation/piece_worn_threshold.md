# Piece: The Worn Threshold
## The proof that is not in any record.

## Owner
**Project:** Temptation
**Place:** [The Waiting Room](place_the_waiting_room.md)

## Place
[The Waiting Room](place_the_waiting_room.md). The door to the inner room.

## Load Bearing
Without it Diligence has no evidence that the showing up was real. The work is done. The record shows the outcome. The threshold shows the cost. Remove it and the accumulation is invisible — even to the one who accumulated it.

## Apparent
Stone, or wood that has become like stone. The centre of the step is lower than the edges — not by much, perhaps a centimetre, perhaps less. The depression is smooth. Polished by passage. There are no individual marks — no single crossing left a trace. The trace is the sum of all of them. The step does not know it is evidence. It simply is.

## Yearbook
The monks' path to matins: walked in darkness, twice a night, every night, for decades. The stone of the cloister worn into a shallow channel by feet that never meant to leave a mark. The mark is there. The monks who made it are not.
The teacher's floor in front of the board: worn where they stand to explain, to demonstrate, to wait for the answer. Year after year. The floor does not record the lessons. It records the standing.
The threshold of the great libraries: worn by the readers who came before the catalogues existed. The knowledge of what they sought is gone. The evidence that they sought it is in the stone.
Diligence does not know the threshold is their piece. They cross it every time they enter. Have always crossed it. Will cross it again.
The threshold is the only record of what the crossing cost.
Nobody asked it to keep the record. It kept it anyway.
