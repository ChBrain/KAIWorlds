# References

## The Seven Deadly Sins

**Prudentius, *Psychomachia* (405 AD)**
The first work to pair each sin with an opposing virtue in direct allegorical combat.
Latin title: *Psychomachia* (Battle of the Soul).
The combat structure - Sin and Virtue facing each other - originates here.

**Gregory the Great, *Moralia in Job* (c. 590 AD)**
Pope Gregory I established the canonical list of seven sins: Pride, Greed, Lust, Envy,
Gluttony, Wrath, Sloth (*Superbia, Avaritia, Luxuria, Invidia, Gula, Ira, Acedia*).
The list consolidated earlier theological work into the form still in use.

**Dante Alighieri, *Purgatorio* (c. 1314)**
Canonical ordering of the sins by severity, from most to least grave.
The *Commedia* remains the most widely referenced literary treatment of the framework.
