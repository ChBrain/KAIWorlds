# Piece: The Apple
## The desire that preceded the object of it.

## Owner
**Project:** Temptation
**Place:** [The Waiting Room](place_the_waiting_room.md)

## Place
Where [Lust](position_lust.md) is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the wanting floats free — no weight, no history. With it — the appetite has a shape it has carried since before the garden. The apple does not cause the wanting. It is what the wanting looks like when it finds a form.

## Apparent
Ripe. The skin is unmarked. The colour is deep — red bleeding into gold at the shoulder. It smells of itself, which is to say it smells of ripeness and sweetness and the faint edge of fermentation that ripeness always contains. It has not been eaten. It will not be eaten here. It is simply present, and present is enough.

## Yearbook
Before Eden: the golden apple of the Hesperides. Atalanta, who could not be caught, slowed by three golden apples thrown across her path — desire made obstacle, desire made detour.
The apple of discord: thrown by Eris at the wedding feast — for the fairest. Three goddesses. One judgment. Ten years of war.
Eden: not introduced by Eve. Carried into the story by what the story was already about.
The Norse: Idunn's apples, which kept the gods young. Stolen, the gods aged. Returned, they were restored. The apple as what cannot be done without.
Lust does not think about any of this. Lust is just hungry.
