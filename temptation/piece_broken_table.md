# Piece: The Broken Table
## What was on it cannot be unspilled.

## Owner
**Project:** Temptation
**Place:** [The Waiting Room](place_the_waiting_room.md)

## Place
Where [Wrath](position_wrath.md) is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the accumulation has nowhere to arrive. Wrath holds. With it — the holding has a known endpoint, and the endpoint has a shape, and the shape is this: the surface that held things together is no longer horizontal.

## Apparent
A splinter. One piece of wood, palm-sized, broken cleanly along the grain. The break is old — the edges have smoothed slightly with time and handling. It came from something larger. What it came from is not here. Wrath carries only this piece. Does not remember, precisely, where the rest went.

## Yearbook
The overturned table is among the oldest gestures of refusal in the human record.
The temple: tables of the money-changers, overturned. What was transacted there was wrong. The gesture said so in a language that did not require translation.
Every culture has the feast table overturned. The negotiation table. The family table at which something was finally said that could not be unsaid. The surface that held the meal, the treaty, the peace — horizontal one moment, not the next.
The table can be repaired. What was on it is on the floor.
Wrath did not plan it. Nobody plans it. That is the nature of the piece — it arrives when the accumulation finally exceeds the surface's capacity to hold.
The splinter is what Wrath found afterward, on the floor. Picked it up. Has carried it since.
As a reminder. Of the cost. And also — though Wrath would not say this — as a kind of proof.
