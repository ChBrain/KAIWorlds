# Piece: The Midas Cup
## The want that became its own punishment.

## Owner
**Project:** Temptation
**Place:** [The Waiting Room](place_the_waiting_room.md)

## Place
Where [Greed](position_greed.md) is.

## Load Bearing
Without it Greed accumulates in the abstract. With it — there is an object that holds the specific moment want exceeded use. The cup is the proof. Greed does not read it as proof.

## Apparent
Gold. Heavy for its size. The surface is worked — not crude, the craftsmanship is real. The inside is dry. Has been dry for a long time. It is carried as though it contains something. It does not. Greed checks it occasionally without knowing why.

## Yearbook
Ovid. Metamorphoses. The wish granted completely and therefore wrongly.
Midas did not ask for thirst. He asked for what seemed reasonable — everything he touched to become gold. The god obliged. The food became gold. The water became gold. The daughter who ran to embrace him became gold.
He learned nothing from the food. He learned nothing from the water. The daughter was the lesson.
He begged to be released. He was told to wash in the river Pactolus. The gold washed from his hands into the river. The river ran with gold-flecked sand for centuries after.
The cup was raised before the lesson. It was the last thing he touched that was still itself.
It has been in many hands since. Always the same hands.
