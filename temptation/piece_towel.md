# Piece: The Towel
## The inversion made cloth.

## Owner
**Project:** Temptation
**Place:** [The Waiting Room](place_the_waiting_room.md)

## Place
Where [Humility](position_humility.md) is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the gesture of service has no object. Humility defers in the abstract. With it — the deferral has a form, a weight, a history of hands that held it before.

## Apparent
Plain. White, or once white. Worn at the edges from use. Folded. The fold is precise — not for display, but from habit. It has been used. It will be used again.

## Yearbook
Maundy Thursday. The mandate: as I have done, so you must do.
The Roman emperor washed the feet of twelve paupers annually. The English king washed the feet of as many poor men as he had years of age — kneeling on stone, with this cloth, before subjects who did not know what to do with their king on his knees.
The ritual is older than the ceremony. The guest's feet washed by the host. The lord who would not wash — and the one who broke that, and changed what lordship meant.
Pope Francis washed the feet of prisoners. Of refugees. Of women. Each time: the same cloth. The same inversion.
Humility has carried it without knowing its history.
The history is in the weave.
