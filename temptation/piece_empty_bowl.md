# Piece: The Empty Bowl
## Given before it is filled.

## Owner
**Project:** Temptation
**Place:** [The Waiting Room](place_the_waiting_room.md)

## Place
Where [Generosity](position_generosity.md) is.

## Load Bearing
Without it the giving is purely abstract — an impulse without form. With it — the gesture precedes the calculation. The bowl is empty and offered. What fills it, if anything, is not the point.

## Apparent
Ceramic. Plain. Wide at the mouth. The glaze is uneven — made by hand, not machine. It has been full before. The interior is stained faintly with what it once held. It is clean. It is empty. It is being held out.

## Yearbook
Zen: the monk's begging bowl. The teacher Bankei said — hold it out before you know what will be placed in it. The discipline is in the holding, not the receiving.
Medieval almsgiving: the bowl set at the monastery gate before dawn. Filled by those who had. Given to those who arrived. No accounting of who gave what. No record of who received.
The harvest offering: given from the store before the store is counted. The act of giving before certainty as a statement about what certainty is worth.
In some traditions the empty bowl is the prayer. Not the words — the open hands. The posture of receiving that is also the posture of offering.
Generosity carries it. Does not know they are making a theological statement. Thinks they are just being helpful.
