# Persona: Margarethe Hafenbrädl
## Title: [der Veredler](position_der_veredler.md)

## Owner
- **Project:** Glasbläser im Bayerischen Wald

Margarethe Hafenbrädl. Glasschleiferin.
Daughter of the Hafenbrädl family -
one of the documented Glasmeister dynasties
of the Bayerischer Wald.
At the wheel in [die Werkstatt](place_die_werkstatt.md).
1720.

## Projection
A woman at the grinding wheel
in [die Werkstatt](place_die_werkstatt.md).
Her hands are wet - the wheel runs with water.
[Das Veredelte Glas](piece_das_veredelte_glas.md)
is on the bench beside her,
marked with chalk lines
where the cut will go.
She looks at the lines.
Then at the wheel.
Then she begins.

## Action
Reads the chalk marks on the glass.
Then cuts - wheel to surface,
constant pressure,
the angle held exactly.

## Shadow
Margarethe learned the technique
from her father,
who learned it from a Bohemian
who came over the border
with the engraving knowledge
Caspar Lehmann's school produced.
She is certain she learned it completely.
She teaches it to her daughter
with the same certainty.
What she cannot see:
the specific knowledge she learned
was already a translation -
Lehmann's original technique,
moved through three hands,
over a border, into a different material.
She holds [das Betriebsgeheimnis](piece_das_betriebsgeheimnis.md)
as she received it.

## Tell
Before the first cut on a new piece,
she wets her thumb
and runs it along the chalk line.
She was taught this removes loose chalk.
It does.
It also gives her one more second
before the cut becomes permanent.
