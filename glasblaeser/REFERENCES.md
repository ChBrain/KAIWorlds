# References

## The Region

**Bayerischer Wald** - the most forest-rich mountain range in Germany.
Glass production documented from the 14th century.
The region sits on the Bavarian-Bohemian border.
Glastradition documented in Zwiesel since 1305.

## The Craft

**Waldglas** - forest glass. Green from iron oxides in the regional quarzsand.
Produced in Waldglashütten from approximately the 12th to 17th century.
The Wanderglashütte moved every 10–20 years as the surrounding forest was exhausted.
Primary sources: Theophilus Presbyter (1120), Georgius Agricola (1530).

**Hierarchy of the Waldglashütte:**
Documented in regional guild records and Wikipedia (Glasmacher, Waldglas, Waldglashütte):
Holzfäller, Schürer, Hirte, Wirker, Anfänger (Kaier), Kölbelmacher, Einbläser, Meister, Einträger.
The Einbläser blew the Vorform; the Meister finished the piece;
the Einträger carried the finished piece to the Kühlofen.
Women and daughters of the glassmakers (Fasserinnen/Einbinderinnen) packed the finished goods.
Children served as Einträger. Neither group is named in the primary sources.

**The Glasträger:** Carried glass on Buckelkraxen (pack frames) along the Handelswege.
Also known as Kraxenträger, Reffträger, or Kiepenkerl.
Source: Wikipedia (Waldglas).

## The Finishing Craft

**Caspar Lehmann** (c. 1570–1622): gem cutter to Emperor Rudolf II in Prague.
First modern glass engraver to adapt gem-cutting techniques to glass.
His technique crossed the Bohemian border into the Bayerischer Wald in the 17th century.
Sources: Britannica (Bohemian glass, Glassware - German); Wikipedia (Bohemian glass).

**The Glasschleifweg:** 78km walking trail passing 20 former finishing sites in the Oberpfälzer Wald.
Source: dieglasstrasse.de.

## The Glasfachschule Zwiesel

Founded: 18 September 1904.
First and largest glass school in Germany.
Location: Fachschulstraße 15–19, 94227 Zwiesel.
Three school types: Fachschule, Berufsfachschule, Berufsschule.
Fourteen Ausbildungsrichtungen.
Approximately 300 students.
The Lehr- und Versuchsglashütte opened 1957.
Sources: glasfachschule-zwiesel.com; arberland-bayerischer-wald.de; hogn.de (120 Jahre Glasfachschule).

Founding context: The Bohemian glass schools in Steinschönau (1856) and Haida (1869) preceded it.
The Zwiesel Magistrat first petitioned for a school in May 1894,
citing the technical inferiority of Bavarian craftspeople versus Bohemian.
Source: hogn.de (120 Jahre Glasfachschule Zwiesel, 2024).

## The Glasstraße

Founded 1997 by three men connected to the region.
250 kilometres from Waldsassen (north) to Passau (south).
Five routes through Oberpfälzer Wald and Bayerischer Wald.
Eight Landkreise.
Source: dieglasstrasse.de; glasregion-arberland.de.

## The Poschinger Family

Glashüttenherren in the Bayerischer Wald since 1568 (Joachim Poschinger acquires Zadlershütte).
The only historic glass family still active today.
Franz Poschinger (1637–1701): took over Frauenau 1662; rebuilt after the fire of 1669.
His operational records survive.
Fifteen generations documented.
Sources: deutsche-biographie.de; gutsverwaltung-frauenau.de; pressglas-korrespondenz.de.

The Poschinger persona (Franz Poschinger) in this world is built from documented
historical facts about a person who died in 1701.
No claim is made to the living Poschinger family's story, business, or property.

## The Gründerwerkstatt

A subsidised studio space for graduates of the Glasfachschule and related crafts.
Located in a former Glashütte in Zwiesel.
Ten workshop places. Low rent. Ministry funding and private donors.
Source: erlebe.bayern (Glas und Handwerk im Bayerischen Wald).
