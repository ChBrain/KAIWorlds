# Process: The Trade
## The glass moves out of the forest.

## Owner
- **Project:** Glasbläser im Bayerischen Wald
- **Position:** [der Glasträger](position_der_glastraeger.md)
- **Place:** [die Glasstraße](place_die_glasstrasse.md)
- **Piece:** [die Glasstraße](piece_die_glasstrasse.md)
- **Piece:** [die Kraxe](piece_die_kraxe.md)

## Initiated by
The piece is packed.
[Der Glasträger](position_der_glastraeger.md) is present -
a Glasträger with [die Kraxe](piece_die_kraxe.md) on his back,
or a cart on a forest track,
or a railway freight car,
or a wholesale distributor's catalogue,
or a link on a manufacturer's website.
The mechanism is the same across six centuries.
Someone carries the glass out of the forest
toward someone who wants it.

## Direction
Toward the buyer.
Along [die Glasstraße](place_die_glasstrasse.md) -
the road that was walked before it was named,
that was named in 1997,
that was always the distance between
where the glass is made
and where it is wanted.

## Lever
Carrying force: the glass has value
only when it reaches someone who did not make it.
Inside the forest it is inventory.
Outside the forest it is trade.
Resistance: [die Glasstraße](place_die_glasstrasse.md) itself -
the distance, the terrain, the breakage risk,
the market that may have changed
by the time [der Glasträger](position_der_glastraeger.md) arrives.
He moves toward a price
that was set at the other end
without him.

## Echo
The glass has left the forest.
[Die Glasstraße](piece_die_glasstrasse.md) holds the record
of everything that moved along it.
[The Market](process_the_market.md) can begin.
