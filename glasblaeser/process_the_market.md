# Process: The Market
## The glass meets the hand that will keep it.

## Owner
- **Project:** Glasbläser im Bayerischen Wald
- **Position:** [der Glashändler](position_der_glasshaendler.md)
- **Place:** [die Glasstraße](place_die_glasstrasse.md)
- **Piece:** [das Glas](piece_das_glas.md)
- **Piece:** [das Veredelte Glas](piece_das_veredelte_glas.md)

## Initiated by
The glass has arrived.
[Der Glashändler](position_der_glasshaendler.md) is present.
The market is open -
a city fair in the 15th century,
a nobleman's commission in the 17th,
a Werksverkauf on [die Glasstraße](place_die_glasstrasse.md) in 2024,
a hotel group's procurement meeting
where one supplier is from China.

## Direction
Toward the exchange.
The glass passes from carrier to buyer.
Money moves the other way.
The chain from forest to hand closes here.

## Lever
Carrying force: the buyer's need -
functional, aesthetic, or sentimental,
depending on the century and the buyer.
Resistance: the shelf.
On the shelf next to the handmade piece
is a piece that was not made in the forest,
was not blown by a Glasmeister,
was not finished by a Veredler
who trained at [die Glasfachschule](place_die_glasfachschule.md).
It costs less. It is also glass.
The buyer sees both. The buyer chooses.
The process does not determine the outcome.
The process only brings the piece
to the place where the choice is made.

## Echo
The glass has changed hands or it has not.
If it has: the chain is complete.
If it has not: the piece returns to the shelf,
or to the carrier's inventory,
where it waits.
The chain does not break. It waits.
