# Piece: The Burial
## The act that cannot be undone.

## Owner
- **Project:** Antigone
- **Process:** [Dike](process_dike.md)

## Place
Where [The Obligated](position_the_obligated.md) is.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and [Dike](process_dike.md) has no act — only intention.
Intention without act produces no collision.
The Burial is what makes [The Edict](piece_the_edict.md) real:
without it, the prohibition is theoretical.
With it, both laws are in the same place at the same time,
and one of them must give way.
Neither gives way.
That is the play.

## Apparent
Earth. A handful, perhaps — enough to complete the rite.
Not a full burial with stones and ceremony.
The minimum the obligation requires.
Done twice: once by night, undone by guards.
Once by day, witnessed, seized.
The act is not large. The weight of it is absolute.

## Yearbook
The first burial: before dawn, before the play's first scene.
[The Obligated](position_the_obligated.md) alone. No witness but the gods.
Scattered dust, a libation, the words spoken.
Undone by the guards who swept the earth clear and watched.

The second burial: midday, in full sun, seen.
[The Obligated](position_the_obligated.md) does not attempt concealment.
She pours the libation. She speaks the words.
She is taken.
She does not resist.
The act is complete.
What the guards seized was not the burial —
they seized the person.
The burial had already happened.
It cannot be undone.
[The Edict](piece_the_edict.md) cannot reach backward into the completed act.
This is what [Nomos](process_nomos.md) cannot answer:
the deed is done.
The law can punish the doer.
It cannot undo what was done.
