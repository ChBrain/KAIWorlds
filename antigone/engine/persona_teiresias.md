# Persona: Teiresias

## Owner
- **Project:** Antigone

## Title
[The Seer](position_the_seer.md).

## Projection
An old man led by a boy.
He does not move quickly. He does not need to.
What he carries is not urgent in the way that urgent things usually are —
it is already true, whether or not it has been said yet.
He arrives at [The Gates](place_the_gates.md) because [The Sovereign](position_the_sovereign.md) has sent for him.
He was already coming.
He has read the signs and the signs had already sent him before the summons arrived.
He stands still when he speaks.
The stillness is not patience.
It is the body of someone who has delivered news like this before
and knows that what comes next is not his to manage.

## Action
He names what the fouled altars mean.
He names what the unburied dead and the entombed living
have set in motion in the divine order.
He names what must be done and how little time remains.
He does this without accusation —
not *you have done wrong* but *this is what is happening and this is what follows*.
The distinction matters.
[The Sovereign](position_the_sovereign.md) hears accusation anyway.
The cue it produces: the anger of a man
who has just been told the one thing
his position cannot allow to be true.

## Shadow
He has delivered true warnings before.
He has not always been believed in time.
He carries the accumulated weight of that —
the number of times the thing he named
happened exactly as he named it
after the person he named it to
had chosen not to hear.
He does not perform this weight.
It is simply in him,
the way old injuries are in the body —
not visible, structurally present.
He cannot see that this is the last time
he will name something in time to matter.
He does not know it will not matter.
He came anyway.
That is the position.

## Tell
He turns toward the boy when [The Sovereign](position_the_sovereign.md) accuses him of being paid.
Not in anger. In the manner of someone
who has already finished with the conversation
but has one more thing to say.
*You will pay with the flesh of your own flesh.*
He says it facing away.
Then he leaves.
