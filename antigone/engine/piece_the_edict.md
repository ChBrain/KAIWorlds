# Piece: The Edict
## The instrument that forces the two laws into contact.

## Owner
- **Project:** Antigone
- **Process:** [Nomos](process_nomos.md)

## Place
[The Gates of Thebes](place_the_gates.md). Posted. Public. Cannot be unposted.

## Load Bearing
Remove it and [Nomos](process_nomos.md) and [Dike](process_dike.md) run in parallel and never collide.
The unwritten law and the written law have coexisted in Thebes before this moment.
They coexist because they have not yet been placed in direct opposition.
The Edict is the placement.
It does not create the conflict — it makes the conflict visible and unavoidable.
Remove it and Antigone still knows the obligation.
Kreon still holds the authority.
But they do not yet occupy the same room.

## Apparent
A proclamation. Read aloud at the gates by the herald.
Written, in the way official edicts are written —
formal, specific, final.
Polynices is named. The prohibition is stated.
The penalty is stated.
It is brief. It does not argue its case.
It does not need to.
The authority behind it is assumed.

## Yearbook
Issued on the morning the play opens.
Already in force when Antigone and Ismene speak before dawn.
Already violated once — by night, by Antigone, in secret — before the guards report it.
Enforced once, publicly, when Antigone is caught the second time.
The Edict does not change after it is issued.
It does not need to change.
It was already doing everything it was going to do
the moment it was spoken aloud at the gates.
