# Position: The Bystander
## The one who sees the obligation and does not enter it.

## Owner
- **Project:** Antigone
- **Process:** [Dike](process_dike.md)

## Has
The same knowledge [The Obligated](position_the_obligated.md) has.
The same blood. The same brother. The same unburied body.
The same unwritten law.
The position is not ignorant of the obligation.
It is afraid of the consequence.
Fear is not the same as not knowing.
The position carries both simultaneously —
the knowledge of what is owed
and the knowledge of what the city will do to the one who pays it.

## Orders
Survive. The living cannot help the dead if the living are dead.

## Loses
The claim that survival was wisdom.
[The Obligated](position_the_obligated.md) went in and did not come back.
The position remained and was not destroyed.
It was certain that remaining meant something —
that witness was a form of presence, that grief was a form of loyalty.
What the play leaves open:
whether the position was right to survive
or simply fortunate to have been afraid at the right moment.
The play does not answer.
The position lives with the question.

## Drives
On the loss —
offers to share the punishment.
[The Obligated](position_the_obligated.md) refuses.
The position is left with the offer unmade good —
the gesture of solidarity without the cost of it.
It stands at the end of the play.
It does not know what to do with that.
