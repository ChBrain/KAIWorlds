# Process: Dike
## The divine order. What the gods require of the living toward the dead.

## Owner
- **Project:** Antigone

## Initiated by
[The Obligated](position_the_obligated.md) is present.
[The Burial](piece_the_burial.md) has already happened — once, by night, undone by guards.
The process does not begin with the first burial.
It begins before the play opens, in the moment Antigone understood
that Polynices lay unburied and that she was the one who knew it.
That understanding is the initiation.
Everything after is consequence.

## Direction
Toward the completion of the rite — not toward survival, not toward justice, not toward recognition.
Toward the act itself, performed correctly, witnessed by the gods.
Whether the living see it is not the direction.
Whether it is done is the direction.
[The Obligated](position_the_obligated.md) does not move toward martyrdom.
She moves toward [The Burial](piece_the_burial.md).
The martyrdom is what [Nomos](process_nomos.md) makes of it.

## Lever
Carrying force: the unwritten law.
Older than Thebes. Older than Kreon's edict. Older than the city.
The obligation to bury the dead does not require a proclamation.
It does not require a reason.
It requires a body and someone who knows the obligation exists.
[The Obligated](position_the_obligated.md) knows.

Resistance: [Nomos](process_nomos.md).
The written law does not yield to the unwritten
any more than the unwritten yields to the written.
Each claims absolute authority.
Neither has a mechanism for resolving the claim.
The process runs against this wall at full force
and does not slow.

## Echo
[The Burial](piece_the_burial.md) is complete.
[The Tomb](place_the_tomb.md) holds [The Obligated](position_the_obligated.md).
The gods have received what was owed them.
The living have not.
What the next moment inherits:
a rite completed in a sealed room,
no witness but the dead,
and a city that will argue for centuries
about whether it was right.
