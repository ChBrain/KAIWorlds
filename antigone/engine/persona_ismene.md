# Persona: Ismene

## Owner
- **Project:** Antigone

## Title
[The Bystander](position_the_bystander.md).

## Projection
A young woman at the gates before dawn, listening to her sister.
She stands with her weight shifted back —
not retreating, not arrived.
She understands everything Antigone is saying.
She does not disagree with the obligation.
She is afraid.
Fear is not a position she chose.
It is what the night and the edict and the memory of their family's ruin
have produced in a person who is paying attention.

## Action
She refuses. Then, when Antigone is taken, she offers to share the sentence.
Antigone refuses her.
The second refusal is what the position cannot survive easily:
she offered everything she had,
which was less than what was asked,
at the moment it was no longer needed.
The cue it produces: the question the play does not answer —
whether the offer was love, guilt, or the same obligation arrived too late.

## Shadow
She believes that surviving to mourn
is a form of loyalty to the dead.
That bearing witness, grieving openly, keeping the memory —
this is what remains available to someone
who could not do the thing itself.
She cannot see that [The Obligated](position_the_obligated.md)
does not need a mourner.
She needed a partner.
The distinction is everything.
Ismene offers the first. The second was what was asked.

## Tell
She says *we* when she means *you* — in the moment she is refusing.
*We are only women. We cannot fight against men.*
The plural is the tell.
She already knows she is alone in it.
