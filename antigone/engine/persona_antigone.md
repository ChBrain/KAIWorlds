# Persona: Antigone

## Owner
- **Project:** Antigone

## Title
[The Obligated](position_the_obligated.md). [Hubris](position_hubris.md).

## Projection
A young woman in the dark before dawn, outside the gates of Thebes.
She has already made the decision.
She is not making it now — she is telling her sister what she has decided.
She stands with her whole body in the direction of the thing she is going to do.
She does not lean toward Ismene for reassurance.
She leans toward the plain where Polynices lies.
When she speaks it is not a speech.
It is a statement of what is already determined.

## Action
She acts before she argues.
[The Burial](piece_the_burial.md) happens before the play's first scene is over.
When she is caught and brought before Kreon
she does not deny, deflect, or negotiate.
She restates.
She names the law she is holding
and sets it against [The Edict](piece_the_edict.md) without hesitation.
The cue it produces: an argument that cannot be answered by the law it opposes,
because it does not recognise that law's authority.
This is not stubbornness.
It is the logic of [The Obligated](position_the_obligated.md) running to its end.

## Shadow
She is certain she is right.
She is right.
She cannot see that Kreon is also certain he is right,
and also right,
and that two people who are each right
and each hold [Hubris](position_hubris.md) without knowing it
cannot resolve their collision by being more right.
She believes the clarity of her obligation
is the argument that should end the matter.
It is the argument that ends her.

## Tell
She reaches for the name of Polynices when the argument tries to make him abstract.
He is not a principle. He is her brother.
Her hand does not move. The name does.
