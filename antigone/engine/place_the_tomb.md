# Place: The Tomb
## Where both laws reach their end, unseen.

## Owner
- **Project:** Antigone

## Shown
A cave in the rock outside Thebes. Sealed by Kreon's order.
One entrance, walled closed with stone.
No light inside. No sound reaches the city.
[The Obligated](position_the_obligated.md) is inside.
Alive when the stone is set. Not alive when it is opened.
The interior is not seen in the play.
What happened inside is reported, not witnessed.
That is deliberate.
Some things the audience must hear rather than see.

## Holds
- [The Burial](piece_the_burial.md) — completed here, in the dark, for the second time.
- [The Obligated](position_the_obligated.md) — from the sealing until the opening.
- [The Casualty](position_the_casualty.md) — enters after [The Obligated](position_the_obligated.md) is found.

## Offers
The only space in the play outside [Nomos](process_nomos.md)'s reach.
The sentence placed [The Obligated](position_the_obligated.md) here
because the city could not be seen to execute her —
the pollution of killing kin, the risk of the gods' anger.
The solution: seal her in, let nature complete the sentence.
The legal fiction: the city's hands are clean.
What the place offers, inadvertently:
the privacy in which [Dike](process_dike.md) completes its work
without the city's interference.

## Withheld
The moment of death.
The play does not enter the tomb while anyone is alive inside it.
The messenger reports. The Chorus receives.
What the place permanently withholds:
whether [The Obligated](position_the_obligated.md), in the dark, at the end,
believed she had been right.
The tomb does not answer.
It was not asked.
