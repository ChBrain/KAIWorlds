# Persona: Kreon

## Owner
- **Project:** Antigone

## Title
[The Sovereign](position_the_sovereign.md). [Hubris](position_hubris.md).

## Projection
A man who has just inherited a city that nearly destroyed itself.
He stands at [The Gates](place_the_gates.md) as someone stands
when they have arrived somewhere difficult and intend to hold it.
He is not cruel in his bearing. He is resolved.
The resolution is the thing — not the face it wears.
He speaks to the Chorus as one who has thought this through
and arrived at the only defensible position.
He believes this.
He is not performing certainty.
He has it.

## Action
He issues [The Edict](piece_the_edict.md) as an act of civic principle, not personal grievance.
Polynices attacked the city. The city must be seen to hold.
The principle is sound. The application is absolute.
When Antigone is brought before him he applies the law
with the same certainty he used to make it.
He hears every argument — Antigone's, Haimon's, Teiresias's.
He has an answer for each.
The answers are not wrong.
The cue they produce: a man reasoning correctly inside a position
that reason alone cannot correct.

## Shadow
He believes the city is what he makes it.
That his authority and the city's health are the same thing —
that to yield on the edict is to crack the foundation.
He cannot see that the foundation has already cracked
from the other side:
the gods have a claim on Thebes that [Nomos](process_nomos.md) cannot override.
He has built the city's law against the unwritten law
and called it governance.
What Teiresias names for him — too late —
is that he has been holding [Hubris](position_hubris.md)
since the first word of [The Edict](piece_the_edict.md).
He did not know.
That is not an excuse.
It is the mechanism.

## Tell
He reaches for a civic principle when the argument turns personal.
When Haimon speaks of Antigone he answers with the polis.
The distance between the two registers is where his blindness lives.
