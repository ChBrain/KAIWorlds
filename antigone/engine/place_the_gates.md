# Place: The Gates of Thebes
## Where the city's law is visible and enforced.

## Owner
- **Project:** Antigone

## Shown
The entrance to Thebes. Open ground between the walls and the plain.
The body of Polynices lies beyond the walls —
exposed, unshaded, watched by guards on the plain outside.
[The Edict](piece_the_edict.md) was proclaimed here and holds here.
[The Sovereign](position_the_sovereign.md) speaks from here.
[The Witness](position_the_witness.md) occupies the space between stage and audience —
present through every scene, visible to both the actors and the watching city.
Light: full. Public. Nothing that happens here is hidden.

## Holds
- [The Edict](piece_the_edict.md) — proclaimed here, in force here, enforced here.

## Offers
The authority of the public space.
What is said here is said to the city.
What is done here is witnessed by the city.
For [The Sovereign](position_the_sovereign.md): the demonstration that the law holds.
For [The Obligated](position_the_obligated.md): the act done in the open, not concealed.
For [The Witness](position_the_witness.md): everything. This is where the position sees from.
For [The Casualty](position_the_casualty.md): the space in which the argument
might still reach [The Sovereign](position_the_sovereign.md) before the sentence is carried out.

## Withheld
The ground of the plain beyond the walls, where Polynices lies.
The place belongs to the city. The body belongs to neither side.
[Nomos](process_nomos.md) claims it by proximity.
[Dike](process_dike.md) claims it by obligation.
The gates stand between them.
They do not resolve the claim.
They hold it open long enough for the collision to complete itself.
