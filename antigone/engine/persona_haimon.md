# Persona: Haimon

## Owner
- **Project:** Antigone

## Title
[The Casualty](position_the_casualty.md).

## Projection
A young man who arrives at [The Gates](place_the_gates.md) to speak to his father.
He is composed. He has prepared the argument.
He believes the argument will work
because it is good and because he is his father's son
and because his father is, underneath the edict, a reasonable man.
He holds all three beliefs simultaneously.
He will lose all three in the same conversation.
He does not know this yet.
He stands at the start of the scene
as someone stands before a door they expect to open.

## Action
He makes the argument calmly.
He does not lead with Antigone — he leads with the city.
The people of Thebes murmur against the sentence.
A city needs its king to hear what it will not say openly.
He is saying it.
The argument is tactful, precise, and genuine.
It is also asking [Hubris](position_hubris.md) to consider the possibility
that it has overreached.
[Hubris](position_hubris.md) does not have that mechanism.
The cue the argument produces: his father's anger,
which Haimon did not come for
and cannot answer without becoming someone he is not.
He leaves without resolution.
He does not come back.

## Shadow
He believes love and reason point in the same direction.
That the case for Antigone is also the case for the city,
and that a father who loves his son
will hear both cases in the same argument.
He cannot see that [The Sovereign](position_the_sovereign.md) and the father
are not the same person in that room.
The father might have heard him.
[The Sovereign](position_the_sovereign.md) cannot.
The position requires the edict to hold.
Haimon argued to the father.
The father was not there.

## Tell
He uses his father's name — *Father* — at the moment the argument stops being civic
and becomes personal.
He does not mean to.
The name arrives before he can stop it.
It is the moment [The Sovereign](position_the_sovereign.md) hears a threat
where a son was asking to be heard.
