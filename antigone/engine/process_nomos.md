# Process: Nomos
## The city's law. What the polis requires of the living.

## Owner
- **Project:** Antigone

## Initiated by
[The Sovereign](position_the_sovereign.md) is present.
[The Edict](piece_the_edict.md) is in force.
The civil war has just ended. The city is intact by the narrowest margin.
The process begins the moment the proclamation is made —
not the moment it is disobeyed.
Disobedience is already inside it from the first word.

## Direction
Toward the enforcement that proves the law is law.
Not toward punishment — toward demonstration.
The polis cannot function if an edict can be defied without consequence.
[The Sovereign](position_the_sovereign.md) does not pursue Antigone out of anger.
The process pursues her out of necessity.
Remove the necessity and the city dissolves.
[The Sovereign](position_the_sovereign.md) knows this.
It is the only thing he is certain of.

## Lever
Carrying force: the absolute claim of the constructed law.
Nomos is not natural — it is made. Its authority is the making.
A king who does not enforce his own edict
has unmade the edict and himself with it.
The process carries this with the weight of everything the city is built on.

Resistance: [Dike](process_dike.md).
Not a person. Not an argument.
A counter-claim of equal structural weight.
The unwritten law does not negotiate with the written.
It simply also exists, and has existed longer,
and will exist after the city is rubble.
The resistance does not weaken. It does not yield.
The process tightens against it until something breaks.

## Echo
[The Edict](piece_the_edict.md) has been enforced.
[The Tomb](place_the_tomb.md) holds what the enforcement produced.
[The Sovereign](position_the_sovereign.md) stands at [The Gates](place_the_gates.md) with the city intact
and everything else destroyed.
What the next moment inherits:
a law that was upheld and a city that no longer contains
the people the law was meant to protect.
