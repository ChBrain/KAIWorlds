# Antigone

*Thebes. The war is over. The edict is posted. The burial has already happened.*
*Two laws are in the same city at the same time.*
*Neither will yield.*

---

*For the collision between two people who are each completely right.*

---

## What this world is

A king who has just saved his city and is destroying it by the same act.
A woman who has already done the thing that will kill her and would do it again.
A son who believes reason can reach a father whose position has no room for it.
A seer who arrives in time and is not heard in time.
A chorus of old men who understand everything and can change nothing.

The world runs on Sophocles' question: when two laws collide and both are legitimate,
which one breaks, and what does the breaking cost?
The play does not answer. The world does not treat it as answered.

## Characters

**Antigone** — [The Obligated](engine/position_the_obligated.md). [Hubris](engine/position_hubris.md).
She does not perform her obligation. She enacts it.

**Kreon** — [The Sovereign](engine/position_the_sovereign.md). [Hubris](engine/position_hubris.md).
He does not perform his authority. He applies it.

**Ismene** — [The Bystander](engine/position_the_bystander.md).
She knows the obligation. She survives it.

**Haimon** — [The Casualty](engine/position_the_casualty.md).
He holds neither law. He is destroyed by both.

**Teiresias** — [The Seer](engine/position_the_seer.md).
He names the mechanism. He is not heard in time.

**The Chorus** — [The Witness](engine/position_the_witness.md).
They see both sides. They tell the audience what to feel about it.

## The Laws

[Nomos](engine/process_nomos.md) — the city's constructed law. What the polis requires.
[Dike](engine/process_dike.md) — the divine order. What the gods require of the living toward the dead.

Both are absolute. Both are correct. They cannot coexist in the same act.
